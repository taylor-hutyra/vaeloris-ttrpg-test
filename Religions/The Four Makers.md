# The Four Makers

**The Four Makers** is the common Human creation myth shared across all three major cultures of [[Vaeloris]] — the [[Varnathi]], the [[Mirennians]], and the [[Lothari]]. While each culture interprets the myth through its own lens, the core narrative is remarkably consistent: a primordial force called the **Great Smith** initiated creation, and from the resonance of that act came four divine Craftsmen who shaped the unfinished cosmos into the world mortals now inhabit.

## The Creation

The myth begins with the **Great Smith**, who roused the world from formless stillness with the ringing of the **First Flame** upon the **Foundation Bell** — a strike that awakened form, time, and possibility. This act is the Human parallel to [[Aenareth]]'s strumming of the Heartwood of Silence (Elven tradition) and [[Aen'r-Khadaz]]'s Pulse through the bedrock of the void (Dwarven tradition).

From the harmonic resonance of that original act came four divine Craftsmen:

### The Mason
Who raised the mountains, sculpted valleys, and gave **structure** to stone. The Mason is the builder of foundations, the principle of endurance and law. Corresponds to **Tharn'm** in [[The Four Pillars|Dwarven tradition]] and **N'rthelion** in [[The Four Harmonies|Elven tradition]].

### The Carpenter
Who coaxed trees from the dust and taught roots to weave the world. The Carpenter is the principle of **growth**, connection, and the living framework that holds civilization together. Corresponds to **Bronr'n** (Dwarven) and **C'rhanna** (Elven).

### The Jeweler
Who adorned the depths with beauty, refining metal and light. The Jeweler is the principle of **hidden worth**, the beauty that must be sought and earned through labor. Corresponds to **Farrazul** (Dwarven) and **Mirelaris** (Elven).

### The Painter
Who stirred color into sky and dream into silence. The Painter is the principle of **vision**, creativity, and the emotional depth that transforms mere existence into experience. Corresponds to **Elmura** (Dwarven) and **Lelyari'** (Elven).

## The Withdrawal

The Makers, once their work was done, **withdrew** — not out of anger or abandonment, but because the world had become their vessel. They reside now in the hills, winds, ores, and dreams. As the myth teaches: *"The divine did not vanish; it was woven into the world."*

After their departure, a residual divine spark remained — what some call the **Flame of Will**. From it came the division of mortal essence:
- From the light came Humanity — passionate, willful, and yearning for meaning
- From the shadow came the [[Orkin]] — resilient, instinctive, and misunderstood

Humans view themselves as **unfinished** — not flawed, but evolving. Where [[Elves]] see echoes and [[Dwarves]] see permanence, Humans see **potential**.

## Cultural Variants

### The Varnathi Interpretation
The [[Varnathi]] believe in a godlike force called **Valaneth**, who attempted to shape the world through balance and logic. The Paroxysm came when Valaneth's will fractured into mortals, each carrying a shard of purpose. They view the Four Makers as aspects of divine logic:
- The Mason as the **Lawgiver**
- The Carpenter as the **Worldbinder**
- The Jeweler as the **Crafter of Worth**
- The Painter as the **Spark of Ideal**

*"The hand is only as worthy as the plan it follows."*

### The Mirennian Interpretation
The [[Mirennians]] believe the world began with a **Celestial Flame** that danced alone. Loneliness made it split into many fires — stars, suns, and souls. Humans are said to be flames that chose flesh. They see the Four Makers as elemental muses:
- The Mason as the **Old Flame Beneath**
- The Carpenter as the **Laughing Grove**
- The Jeweler as the **Deep Spark**
- The Painter as the **Dancing One**

*"A fire that doesn't move dies. A fire that doesn't share goes mad."*

### The Lothari Interpretation
The [[Lothari]] speak of **The Breathless Dream** — a sleeping god whose thoughts became reality. Humans were the first waking thought, born from curiosity. They remember the Four Makers not by name but by recurring motifs in dreams and visions:
- The Mason as the **Pillar**
- The Carpenter as the **Wandering Root**
- The Jeweler as the **Hidden Glint**
- The Painter as the **Flickering Breath**

*"To remember is to resist. To dream is to defy."*

## The Creation Epic

The common version of the myth is preserved in an oral poem known as **The Flame and the Anvil**:

> *When the world was still ash and nothing,*
> *The Great Smith struck the anvil,*
> *And from that blow, the bones of the world were cast.*
> *The Mason laid the mountains and caves,*
> *The Carpenter wove the green in winding paths,*
> *The Jeweler placed light within stone,*
> *And the Painter gave the world color, sorrow, and dream.*
> *When they had shaped the world,*
> *The fire they left behind split,*
> *And we were born of the brighter half.*
> *Not perfect. Not complete. But striving.*

## Legacy

The Four Makers is the most widely shared religious tradition among [[Humans]], bridging the vast cultural divides between highland philosophers, coastal poets, and desert mystics. Its power lies in its humility: it does not claim Humans are the greatest of the world's peoples, only that they are the most **incomplete** — and that this incompleteness is not a curse but an invitation to keep building, keep striving, keep reaching toward the fire that the Makers left behind.
