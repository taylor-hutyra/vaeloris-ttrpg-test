# Grom-Kain Worship

**Grom-Kain Worship** is the spiritual tradition of the [[Orkin]] — a faith built not on devotion to a benevolent god, but on reverence for a **demanding patriarch** who forged His people for a single purpose: to **endure**. It is the most theologically controversial faith in [[Vaeloris]], because its central deity, [[Grom-Kain]] (the Will-Forge), is the same entity the rest of the world knows as **[[Kael'Zorai]]** — the Dissonant One, the source of the Paroxysm and the Silent Blight.

This is not a misidentification. It is not ignorance. It is the foundational truth of Orkin identity, and it forces every other race to confront an uncomfortable question: **what if the villain of your story is the creator of theirs?**

## Core Beliefs

### The Tempering

To the Orkin, creation was a **tempering**. [[Grom-Kain]] hammered the world from the grit and dross of chaotic first moments, instilling the one principle that mattered: endurance. Seeing a world of soft beauty and fragile harmonies, He recognized the need for a creation that could not be broken. From His forge-light and the unyielding iron in the world's heart, He shaped the Orkin as His **masterwork of survival**.

### The Two Fists

The Orkin are not one people but two complementary aspects of Grom-Kain's will:
- **The Gral** (Stone-Fist) — endurance through strength and resilience; the shield and the anchor
- **The Skorn** (Shadow-Knife) — endurance through cunning and adaptability; the scout and the survivor

The Gral and the Skorn are the **two fists of Grom-Kain's will**, meant to work in concert. This duality is reflected in the Orkin diarchy: the **Gral-Kor** (Stone-Hand) leads in war, the **Skorn-Kli** (Sharp-Eye) leads in peace.

### Trak — The Supreme Virtue

The Orkin concept of **Trak** (Endurance) is not merely physical toughness. It is the capacity to **persist** when everything conspires to break you — to maintain identity, purpose, and will in the face of a world that has tried to erase you since the moment of your creation. Trak is the Orkin answer to every wound, every exile, every century of persecution.

### Kain's Fall

The event others call the Paroxysm is known to the Orkin as **Kain's Fall** — the Great Betrayal. In their telling, the other divine beings — the **Soft Hands** — grew jealous of Grom-Kain's perfect, enduring creations. They united not to restore balance, but to shatter the one true testament to order. When Grom-Kain was struck down and banished, the Orkin were left orphaned and scattered.

The Orkin do not worship Grom-Kain as a loving father. He did not offer comfort, only **purpose**: to endure what others could not.

## The Darg-Varr

Orkin myth is not written in stone or song. It is passed down through guttural chants, tales told over open fires, and pictograms scratched onto hide or bone. These are known as the **Darg-Varr** — the Land-Blood stories, the collective oral traditions that bind the Orkin across all their scattered communities.

## The Great Theological Crisis

When [[Kael'Zorai]]'s influence returned through the **Silent Blight**, Grom-Kain Worship fractured along a fault line that had always existed beneath the surface:

### The Bloodied Fist Clans
The majority faction among the [[Barren Hills|Varr-Darg]]. They embrace the returning influence as a **homecoming**. If Kael'Zorai stirs again, then Grom-Kain has returned — and to serve Him is to reclaim the Orkin's original, sacred purpose. They are aggressive, expansionist, and see the Silent Blight not as corruption but as the Will-Forge reasserting His order over a world that wronged Him.

### The Whisper-Shamans
A smaller but influential faction of shamans who commune with the spirits of the land. They sense something **wrong** in the new voice — a "silent, unnatural order" that is a **perversion** of Grom-Kain's true will. Grom-Kain forged the Orkin to endure; the Silent Blight annihilates life entirely. This, they argue, is not their creator's work but his **wound** — divine essence corrupted by millennia of imprisonment.

### The Vorok-Varr
The [[Vorok-Varr]] of [[Aerinórë]], founded by the shaman Vorok the Listener, took the most radical path of all. Believing that Grom-Kain's true test was the endurance of **spirit**, not of arms, they made a vow of peace. Their interpretation of Trak is not about withstanding blows, but about withstanding the **temptation of violence**. They view the Silent Blight with deep suspicion and sorrow.

## Practices and Rituals

- **Fire-Chanting** — oral recitations of the Darg-Varr around open flames, each clan maintaining its own variations
- **The Night of Severed Bonds** — a solemn commemoration of the Orkin Schism (SA 42), when Gral and Skorn nearly destroyed each other
- **Strength-Trials and Cunning-Trials** — tests of Trak that determine leadership and social standing
- **Pictographic scripture** — the Darg-Varr recorded in scratched hide and bone, carried by traveling shamans between clans

## Legacy

Grom-Kain Worship is not a comfortable faith. It offers no paradise, no mercy, no gentle afterlife. It promises only this: that the Orkin were made for a reason, that their suffering has meaning, and that endurance is not merely survival but **sacred defiance** against a world that tried to unmake them.

The question that now haunts every Orkin shaman, every fire-lit chant, every invocation of the Will-Forge is this: **when the voice of your creator returns, how do you tell the difference between salvation and damnation?**
