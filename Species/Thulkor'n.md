# The Thulkor'n

The **Thulkor'n**, or **Broken-Crafted**, are the descendants of [[Dwarves]] exiled from [[Kharnzarak]] for common crimes -- theft, oath-breaking, violence. Banished from their clans and cast out from the deep halls, they sought a new beginning on the free continent of [[Novaterra]]. They are pragmatists and survivors, a scattered diaspora that has largely abandoned the rigid honor codes of their ancestors while retaining their innate mastery of stone and metal.

## Origin and Exile

The word **Thulkor'n** itself carries the weight of their history: *thul* means "breaking" or "shattering," and *kor* means "chisel" or "craft-mark." They are the Broken-Crafted -- those whose craft-marks were literally chiseled away from the rolls of [[Kharnzarak]] when they were cast out. To the [[N'nm-Khazal'n]], the Thulkor'n are a source of collective shame, common criminals who broke their sacred oaths.

The reality is more complex. While many Thulkor'n ancestors were indeed convicted of crimes, the rigid caste system of [[Kharnzarak]] defined "crime" broadly. Philosophical dissent, unauthorized innovation, and refusal to accept one's hereditary craft could all merit exile. Some, like [[Brenna]], were banished for philosophical rather than criminal reasons -- a [[Thulkor'n]] by law but an [[Elm-korin]] in spirit.

## Core Philosophy: Survival

Having had their clan identity stripped from them, the Thulkor'n's loyalty is to their **contracts**, their **comrades**, and their **coin**. The old oaths mean nothing to those who were cast out by oaths. What matters is the deal you can make today and the hand you can trust tomorrow.

This philosophy is not cynicism -- it is hard-won pragmatism. The Thulkor'n learned early that the world beyond [[Kharnzarak]]'s halls does not care about ancient lineage or carved law. It cares about what you can build, what you can fix, and whether your word holds weight.

## Society: A Scattered Diaspora

The Thulkor'n have no central authority, no king, no council of elders. They form loose-knit communities in the underbellies of [[Novaterra]]n cities or serve in mercenary companies. Their social structures are pragmatic and transient, built around necessity rather than tradition.

### Mercenary Charters

In the free cities of [[Novaterra]], many Thulkor'n form **mercenary companies** governed by a charter -- a legal document outlining pay, responsibilities, and the Captain's authority. Loyalty is to the contract, not a king or a god. Companies like **The Grit-Shields** are known for their reliability and their brutal efficiency.

### The Under-Guilds

On the mainland, many Thulkor'n have found a niche in the criminal underworlds of port cities like [[Aurumport]]. They operate as enforcers, arms-smiths, and engineers for thieves' guilds, their loyalty bought and paid for. Their craftsmanship makes them invaluable to any organization that needs weapons, locks, or tunnels.

### The Freeholds

In the untamed wilds of [[Novaterra]], some Thulkor'n have established small, independent, and heavily fortified settlements called **Freeholds**. These are governed by a single, powerful leader -- often the founder -- who rules by strength and reputation. Freeholds are isolationist and deeply suspicious of all outsiders.

## View of Other Dwarves

The Thulkor'n view the [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] with **bitter resentment** for casting them out. Centuries of exile have not dulled the sting. Every Thulkor'n knows the story of their family's banishment, passed down as a cautionary tale and a grudge.

They often see the [[Elm-korin]] as naive idealists, dreamers chasing perfection in a world that rewards practicality. But a Thulkor'n will gladly work for [[Gearhaven]] if the pay is right -- philosophy is a luxury the exile cannot afford.

They have little to no contact with or knowledge of [[Gnomes]].

## Skills and Trades

Despite their exile, the Thulkor'n retain the innate Dwarven mastery of stone and metal. Their work is rougher than [[Kharnzarak]]'s finest, born of necessity rather than tradition, but it is no less effective. Thulkor'n-forged weapons are prized in [[Novaterra]]'s markets for their durability and straightforward design -- no ornamentation, no wasted material, just function.

They are also skilled **tunnelers**, **siege engineers**, and **fortification builders**. A Thulkor'n engineer can assess a wall's weakness by pressing an ear to the stone -- an instinct that no amount of exile can erase.

## Common Expressions

The Thulkor'n have adapted Dwarvish sayings to their harsher reality:

- *"The oath broke us; the hammer rebuilt us."* -- A defiant statement of identity.
- *"Coin before kin -- kin left first."* -- Justification for mercenary loyalty.
- *"A Freehold stands because its people chose to stand."* -- The frontier creed.
- *"Don't carve promises in sand."* -- Only make commitments you can keep.

## Notable Thulkor'n

[[Brenna]] is perhaps the most prominent Thulkor'n in the current age -- a dwarf exiled for philosophical reasons whose beliefs align more closely with the [[Elm-korin]] than with the criminals and outcasts who form the bulk of the Thulkor'n diaspora. Her practical, no-nonsense attitude is a hallmark of the Thulkor'n survivalist mindset, even as her deeper convictions set her apart.
