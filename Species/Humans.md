# Humans

Humans are the most numerous and culturally diverse people of [[Vaeloris]]. They view themselves as unfinished — not flawed, but evolving. Where [[Elves]] see echoes and [[Dwarves]] see permanence, Humans see potential. Though divided by myth and dialect, they share core beliefs: choice is sacred, identity is shaped not given, and the world is still becoming.

## Origin

The most widespread Human creation myth tells of the **Great Smith**, who roused the world from formless stillness by striking the First Flame upon the Foundation Bell — a blow that awakened form, time, and possibility. From this harmonic resonance came the **Four Makers**:

- **The Mason**, who raised the mountains and gave structure to stone
- **The Carpenter**, who coaxed trees from the dust and taught roots to weave the world
- **The Jeweler**, who adorned the depths with beauty, refining metal and light
- **The Painter**, who stirred color into sky and dream into silence

It is believed the [[Elves]] and [[Dwarves]] were formed by these divine artisans. When the Makers withdrew, a residual divine spark remained — the **Flame of Will**. From the light came Humanity; from the shadow came the [[Orkin]]. The great calamity known as the **Paroxysm** (or The Sundering Flame) is described as the moment when the primal flame of creation burned too brightly and split itself — a trial of emergence where beings were shaped through pain and chaos.

## Physical Traits

Humans are physically versatile, ranging widely in build, complexion, and stature depending on their cultural lineage. They are neither the strongest nor the most graceful of the peoples of [[Vaeloris]], but their adaptability and short generational cycles make them engines of cultural and technological change.

## Culture

Though divided by myth and dialect, Humans share a hunger to discover both origin and destiny. Their sayings reflect old labor truths, frontier pragmatism, and inherited metaphors from farming, war, and craft:

- *"Don't sharpen blades in the rain."* — Don't prepare too late.
- *"The ember waits for breath."* — Even small ideas need action.
- *"What's carved, holds."* — Choices are lasting.
- *"Grain bends, stone remembers."* — People adapt, but the world holds history.

## Sub-Groups

### The Varnathi (Highland Philosopher-Warriors)

The [[Varnathi]] believe in a godlike force called **Valaneth**, who attempted to shape the world through balance and logic. Their society is scholarly and aristocratic, led by philosopher-guardians. They see the Four Makers as aspects of divine logic — the Mason as the Lawgiver, the Carpenter as the Worldbinder, the Jeweler as the Crafter of Worth, the Painter as the Spark of Ideal. They govern through the **Pentarchy of [[Vernath]]**, a republic of five co-equal Consuls. Common saying: *"Measure twice, live once."*

### The Mirennians (Coastal Traders and Poets)

The [[Mirennians]] believe the world began with a Celestial Flame that danced alone — loneliness made it split into many fires, and Humans are flames that chose flesh. Their cities are mercantile, cosmopolitan, and culturally vibrant. They see the Four Makers as elemental muses: the Old Flame Beneath, the Laughing Grove, the Deep Spark, and the Dancing One. They govern through the [[Mirennian League]], a maritime alliance of independent port-cities ruled by the **Admiralty Council**. Common saying: *"Sails full or none at all."*

### The Lothari (Desert-Bound Mystics)

The [[Lothari]] speak of **The Breathless Dream** — a sleeping god whose thoughts became reality. They are semi-nomadic, practicing ancient rituals and dream-interpretation. The Four Makers appear in their traditions as recurring dream motifs: the Pillar, the Wandering Root, the Hidden Glint, and the Flickering Breath. They are governed by the **Silent Network**, a council of mystics and visionaries. Common saying: *"Wind writes where we forget."*

## Shared Perspectives

Unlike [[Elves]] or [[Dwarves]], Humans rarely agree on origin or destiny — but they share these core beliefs:

- Choice is sacred
- Identity is shaped, not given
- The world is still becoming, and so are we

Their greatest virtue and flaw is the same: an insatiable hunger to build, discover, and define meaning in a world that offers none freely.
