# The Mirennians

The **Mirennians** are the coastal [[Humans]] of the maritime free ports, a people defined by commerce, expression, and an unshakable belief in personal freedom. Often called the **Coastal Traders and Poets**, their civilization is a cosmopolitan thalassocracy -- a Venetian and Phoenician analog where coin and charisma determine standing.

## Origin and Creation Belief

The Mirennians believe the world began with a **Celestial Flame** that danced alone. Loneliness made it split into many fires -- stars, suns, and souls. [[Humans]] are said to be flames that chose flesh, each one a spark of the original divine fire wrapped in mortal form. This belief imbues Mirennian culture with a restless, passionate energy and a deep respect for individual expression.

## View of the Four Makers

The [[Four Makers]] are seen as elemental muses or traveling spirits, each with shifting names and roles:

- **The Mason** is called **the Old Flame Beneath** -- the enduring warmth of the earth
- **The Carpenter** is **the Laughing Grove** -- the playful spirit of growth
- **The Jeweler** becomes **the Deep Spark** -- hidden brilliance waiting to be found
- **The Painter** is celebrated as **the Dancing One** -- patron of all art and expression

These figures are often invoked in poetry or art rather than formal worship, and their names shift with the telling.

## Cultural Themes

Mirennian society revolves around **freedom**, **commerce**, and **expression**. Their cities are mercantile, cosmopolitan, and in constant cultural flux. Status is determined not by blood but by wealth and influence -- there are no hereditary nobles, only **Merchant Princes** whose power lasts only as long as their fortunes.

Their speech is flowing and musical, laden with idioms about wind, light, and luck. A Mirennian negotiator speaks of sails and tides the way a Varnathi speaks of vaults and keystones.

## Government: The Mirennian League

The [[Mirennian League]] is not a nation but a powerful maritime alliance of independent port-cities, bound together by mutual profit and a shared distrust of land-based empires.

- **The Admiralty Council** -- composed of the heads of the ten most powerful Merchant Houses, with each House's vote weighted by the tonnage of their trade fleet
- **The Master of Tides** -- the elected head of the council, rotating annually, serving as chief diplomat and naval commander but with little direct power over member-cities

## Major Factions

Three currents of power flow through the League:

- **The [[Golden Compass Syndicate]]** -- the largest and most powerful house, focused on mainstream trade and maintaining stability. They view the [[Pirate Confederacy]] as a pest to be eradicated.
- **The [[Azure Hand]]** -- a secretive house that profits from smuggling, espionage, and the gray market trade of magical artifacts. They maintain a complex, often collaborative relationship with the Pirate Lords.
- **The [[Sea-Whisperers' Guild]]** -- controllers of the magical art of wind-shaping, making them a crucial power broker. No house can go to war or launch a major expedition without their blessing.

## Society: A Meritocracy of Wealth

In the League, status is determined entirely by one's wealth and influence. This creates a society of ruthless ambition, dazzling opportunity, and spectacular ruin. Fortunes rise and fall with the tides. A dockworker's child can become a Merchant Prince in a generation; a Prince can lose everything in a single storm.

The League's cities are vibrant melting pots where [[Elves]], [[Dwarves]], [[Gnomes]], and [[Humans]] mingle freely in the market squares. Cultural exchange is constant, and the Mirennians pride themselves on their openness -- though this openness is always transactional at its core.

## Relations with the Pirate Confederacy

The [[Pirate Confederacy]] of the Fractured Isles is the League's greatest irritant. This lawless confederation of pirate crews operates out of hidden coves in the southern isles, governed by the **Captains' Council** -- a loose gathering of the most feared captains who set the Code and coordinate raids. The pirates are united in their hatred of the Mirennian navy and the [[Varnathi]] fleet alike.

The [[Azure Hand]] faction maintains secret dealings with the pirates, using them to destabilize [[Golden Compass Syndicate]] rivals.

## Common Sayings

- *"Sails full or none at all."* -- Go all-in or not at all.
- *"Coins jingle loudest in empty hands."* -- Boasts often hide desperation.
- *"Every harbor forgets the last storm."* -- Time softens even hardship.

## Verses and Traditions

The **Song of the Breakwater** is a seafarer's chant known across every port: *"The tide remembers only wind, and we are sails on borrowed breath. Let the storm forget your name, and ride as if you never looked back."*

The **Lantern Festival Verse** is recited during the annual Festival of Lights: *"One flame for wish, two for thanks, three for someone you forgot to love."*

Mirennian merchants recite the **Coin's Shadow** as a riddle and a warning: *"Gold laughs in silver's ear, but copper walks the furthest. Ask the purse, not the hand."*
