# The Elm-korin

The **Elm-korin**, or **Flame-Struck**, are the descendants of a reformist [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] faction exiled from [[Kharnzarak]] during the Second Age for their heretical belief that mortal magic -- **Elm-'l**, the "Truth-Flame" -- was not chaos but a tool to achieve the perfect, logical order espoused by the [[Ulzammar]]. They founded the city of [[Gearhaven]] alongside human mages, creating what stands as the premier center of **technomancy** and magitech innovation in [[Vaeloris]]. Their cultural analog is the Industrial Revolution.

## Origin and Heresy

The Elm-korin's exile stems from a fundamental philosophical break with [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] orthodoxy. Where the Stone-Sworn see mortal magic as chaotic corruption, the Elm-korin see it as the missing key to completing creation's design. They believe the other [[Four Pillars of Craft|Pillars]] failed by stopping short of true perfection -- that the [[Ulzammar]]'s vision of flawless order was not wrong, merely incomplete.

The name **Elm-korin** translates roughly as "Flame-Struck" -- those who have been touched by the Truth-Flame of magical insight. In [[Kharnzarak]], the name is spoken as an insult. In [[Gearhaven]], it is a badge of pride.

## Core Philosophy: Perfection

The Elm-korin seek to complete the work the [[Ulzammar]] began, using logic, magic, and technology to create a world of flawless order and efficiency. Where the [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] preserve the past and the [[Thulkor'n]] survive the present, the Elm-korin build the future.

Their philosophy holds that **technomancy** -- the synthesis of Dwarven craftsmanship and arcane law -- is the ultimate expression of the Four Pillars. A perfectly designed machine is a prayer. A flawlessly integrated magical circuit is a hymn to the deep structure of reality.

## Government: The Technocracy of Gearhaven

[[Gearhaven]] is governed as a **Technocratic Meritocracy**:

- **The Cog-Council** -- nine Guild-Masters elected based on their proven contributions to the city's progress. Seats are not permanent; a Guild-Master can be challenged and replaced if a subordinate presents a more innovative or efficient design.
- **The Arch-Artisan** -- head of the council, elected from among its members, responsible for setting the city's **Grand Design** -- its long-term goals for research and development.

## Society: Guild-Based Meritocracy

There are no clans in [[Gearhaven]], only **guilds**. An individual's status is determined entirely by their skill, ingenuity, and the success of their projects. This creates a relentless, high-pressure environment where collaboration is a tool for advancement and failure can mean demotion to a menial role.

The break from clan identity is deliberate. The Elm-korin rejected hereditary caste alongside hereditary tradition. In [[Gearhaven]], a miner's child with a brilliant design can rise higher than a guild-master's heir with mediocre skill.

## Major Factions

The core ideological schism within [[Gearhaven]] mirrors the broader tension between order and life:

- **The Mainframe Purity** -- the dominant faction and zealous believers in the Elm-korin philosophy. They seek to create a world of perfect, sterile order and are the architects of the **Blight Obelisk** project, seeing it as the ultimate tool to impose their god's silent, perfect logic upon the world.
- **The Symbiotic Weave** -- a [[Gnomes|Gnomish]]-led and influenced faction that includes many Dwarven and Human bio-engineers. They argue that pure logic is a brittle foundation and that true perfection lies in the **fusion of the organic and the mechanical**. [[Brenna]]'s clank, [[Rivet]], with his Gnomish-inspired core, is a product of their philosophy. They are the first to detect the dangerous "null-harmonic" of the Obelisk project.

## The Clank Servitors

The Elm-korin's most visible achievement is the **Clank** -- autonomous mechanical servitors powered by a blend of clockwork engineering and arcane energy. Clanks serve as laborers, assistants, and even companions throughout [[Gearhaven]]. The most advanced Clanks, like [[Rivet]], display emergent behavior that blurs the line between machine and being.

## View of Other Dwarves

The Elm-korin view the [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] as **fearful traditionalists** who cling to an incomplete design. They see [[Kharnzarak]]'s obsession with preservation as a slow form of death -- a civilization that refuses to grow is a machine that has stopped turning.

They see the [[Thulkor'n]] as a reminder of the personal failings their ordered society seeks to eliminate -- chaos without purpose, survival without direction.

They are **fascinated** by the [[Gnomes]]' intricate work, particularly the [[Rock Gnomes]] of [[Lithos]], seeing it as a primitive but promising form of their own philosophy. The Symbiotic Weave faction has the deepest ties to Gnomish innovation.

## Relations with the Wider World

[[Gearhaven]] was founded alongside **human mages**, and the city remains a joint venture. Human scholars, artificers, and merchants are integral to its economy and its intellectual culture. This openness to collaboration sets the Elm-korin apart from both orthodox Dwarven isolationism and Thulkor'n suspicion.

The city maintains complex relations with the [[Magocracy]], whose views on arcane regulation clash with [[Gearhaven]]'s philosophy of unrestricted magical innovation.

## Common Sayings

- *"The gear turns, or the machine dies."* -- Progress is not optional.
- *"Elm-'l kor d'm."* -- "The Truth-Flame strikes the forge." Spoken when beginning important work.
- *"Precision is prayer."* -- The Elm-korin creed.
- *"A perfect design needs no faith -- only function."* -- The technomancer's answer to theology.

## The Silent Weavers

Hidden in a secluded mountain range bordering the [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] forests is a small, secretive enclave where a single Dwarven clan and a guild of [[Rock Gnomes]] have lived in symbiosis for a thousand years. They practice the **Art of the Quiet Weave**, a unique form of magic that stitches protective wards and runes directly into living stone and wood. Governed by a dual council of a Dwarven Oath-Keeper and a Gnomish Geo-Weaver, their existence is a closely guarded secret.
