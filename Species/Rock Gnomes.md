# Rock Gnomes

The **Rock Gnomes**, or **Deep-Weavers**, are a [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] offshoot that migrated into the deep root systems of the world's mountains, becoming tenders of stone, crystal, and geothermal patterns. They live in breathtaking, silent cities carved into subterranean caverns, lit by phosphorescent fungi and powered by volcanic vents. The [[Nûm-Khazalûn]] [[Dwarves]] know them by the respectful name **Nûm-kith** ("Stone-kin"), recognizing them as fellow children of a Stone-based Pillar.

## Origin and Migration

Born from the same creation as all [[Gnomes]] -- grown by the **Twin Roots**, [[Ceryn-Maia]] and [[Ilys-Tekne]] -- the Rock Gnomes felt the pull of the deep earth rather than the forest canopy. In the earliest days, when the Gnomish people were still hidden and unified, some families followed the deep aquifers and root networks downward, drawn by the hum of tectonic plates and the shimmer of uncut crystal.

Over centuries, these underground pioneers developed a unique culture adapted to the subterranean world. They became master **gem-cutters**, **metallurgists**, and makers of intricate, silent clockwork mechanisms. Their art is the art of precision -- every cut exact, every gear silent, every structure perfectly balanced.

## Core Philosophy: Precision

The Rock Gnomes see the world as a grand machine of stone and energy. Their sacred duty is to **listen for the grinding of the gears and keep the machine running smoothly**. Where the [[Forest Gnomes]] tend to living ecosystems, the Rock Gnomes tend to the geological and geothermal systems that form the world's deep infrastructure.

Precision is not merely a skill but a moral principle. A gear that does not turn smoothly is a flaw to be corrected. A crystal that is cut improperly is not just ugly -- it is a small wound in the world's deep pattern. The Rock Gnomes believe that perfection in craft is a form of worship.

## Lithos: The Silent City

[[Lithos]] is the greatest city of the Rock Gnomes -- a breathtaking subterranean metropolis hidden deep within the Dragon's Tooth mountains. Its caverns are vast, lit by cultivated phosphorescent fungi and warmed by carefully channeled geothermal vents. The silence of [[Lithos]] is legendary; its clockwork mechanisms are engineered to operate without a whisper, and its inhabitants speak in low, measured tones.

The city is a marvel of Gnomish engineering: bridges of spun crystal span underground chasms, water is distributed through gravity-fed systems of perfect hydraulic balance, and the temperature is maintained by a network of volcanic vent-valves that the Rock Gnomes have tended for millennia.

## Government: The Guild-Conclave of Lithos

[[Lithos]] is governed as a **Technocratic Guild-Conclave**:

- **The Guild-Conclave** -- a council of nine, where each member is the undisputed master of their respective craft (e.g., the Master Gem-Facer, the Prime Harmonist, the Chief Chronometrist). A seat is held for life or until a peer demonstrably surpasses the master's skill.
- **The Deep Warden** -- leader of the Conclave, chosen from among its members. Their role is not to rule but to "listen" to the great machine of the world -- the grinding of tectonic plates, the hum of leylines -- and to direct the Conclave's efforts to maintain stability.

## Society: A Meritocracy of Precision

Rock Gnome society is rigidly structured around **guilds**. An individual's worth is measured by the precision and quiet perfection of their work. The major guilds include:

- **Gem-Faceting** -- cutting and tuning crystals for both aesthetic and functional purposes
- **Metal-Tuning** -- working with rare alloys and resonant metals
- **Pressure-Gauging** -- monitoring and managing the geothermal systems
- **Chrono-Crafting** -- building the silent clockwork mechanisms for which [[Lithos]] is renowned

There is little room for sentiment in guild culture. Excellence is expected, mediocrity is corrected, and failure is treated as a flaw requiring diagnosis and repair -- not punishment, but recalibration.

## Major Factions

- **The Clockwork School (Orthodox)** -- the dominant philosophy, focused on the perfection of pure, silent mechanics. They see the universe as a vast, intricate timepiece and believe that understanding its mechanisms is the highest calling.
- **The Resonance School (Heretics)** -- a smaller school that studies the harmonic properties of crystals and metals. They believe true perfection lies not in silent mechanics but in creating perfectly tuned resonances that can influence magic and reality itself. The orthodox school considers their work dangerously close to the "chaotic" magic of the Loud-Walkers.

## The Secret Art: Aiding The Great Weaving

One of the Rock Gnomes' most closely guarded secrets is their role in the creation of [[The Great Weaving]] -- the vast magical ward that shields the [[Cirhannim]]'s homeland of [[Hannanórë]]. Rock Gnome artisans secretly provided the crystalline focusing matrices and deep-earth anchor-runes that give the Weaving its stability. This collaboration is known to only a handful of individuals in either culture.

## The Silent Weavers

Hidden in a secluded mountain range bordering the [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] forests is a small, secretive enclave where a single Dwarven clan and a guild of Rock Gnomes have lived in symbiosis for a thousand years. They practice the **Art of the Quiet Weave**, stitching protective wards and runes directly into living stone and wood. Governed by a dual council of a Dwarven Oath-Keeper and a Gnomish Geo-Weaver, they are creators of some of the most powerful defensive artifacts in the world.

## View of Other Gnomes

The Rock Gnomes see the [[Forest Gnomes]] as wonderfully creative but a bit too messy and chaotic -- their symbiotic, organic approach to craft lacks the satisfying precision of a perfectly cut gem or a silent gear. They respect the Forest Gnomes' warmth and diplomacy but would never trade their own orderly caverns for a fungal Hollow.

They have almost no concept of the [[Sand Gnomes]], whose desert existence is so far removed from the subterranean world that the two branches might as well inhabit different planets.

## View of the Dwarves

The Rock Gnomes respect the skill of the [[Dwarves]] but find them terribly loud. The [[Nûm-Khazalûn]]'s forges, with their ringing hammers and roaring bellows, are an assault on Rock Gnome sensibilities. Still, there is a quiet, deep kinship between the two peoples -- both understand the language of stone, even if they speak it at very different volumes.

The [[Elm-korin]] of [[Gearhaven]] fascinate the Rock Gnomes. The Symbiotic Weave faction within [[Gearhaven]] has the closest ties to Rock Gnome innovation, and the two groups exchange ideas through carefully guarded channels.

## Common Sayings

- *"Kelos on mori."* -- "Growth in silence." Shared with all Gnomes.
- *"The quietest gear turns the longest."* -- A Rock Gnome maxim.
- *"Listen to the stone before you cut it."* -- Patience before action.
- *"A crystal remembers every blow."* -- Precision matters; mistakes are permanent.

## The Deep Warden's Vigil

The most sacred duty in [[Lithos]] is the **Deep Warden's Vigil** -- a continuous, centuries-long monitoring of the world's tectonic and leyline patterns. The Deep Warden and their assistants maintain vast, intricate models of the earth's deep structure, tracking shifts in pressure, temperature, and magical resonance. When something moves in the deep earth, [[Lithos]] knows about it first.
