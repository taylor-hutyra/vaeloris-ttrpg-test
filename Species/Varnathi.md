# The Varnathi

The **Varnathi** are the highland [[Humans]] of [[Vernath]], a people defined by philosophy, discipline, and the conviction that they are the world's rightful guardians of order. Often called the **Highland Theorists**, their civilization draws upon a deep tradition of scholarship, aristocratic governance, and an unwavering belief in legacy and self-discipline. Their cultural analog is broadly Greco-Roman in character.

## Origin and Creation Belief

The Varnathi believe in a godlike force called **Valaneth**, who attempted to shape the world through balance and logic. The [[The Paroxysm]] came when Valaneth's will fractured into mortals, each one carrying a shard of divine purpose. In this telling, [[Humans]] are born with fragments of divine will, and the Varnathi consider it their sacred duty to refine and fulfill those fragments through disciplined action.

## View of the Four Makers

The Varnathi recognize the [[Four Makers]] as aspects of divine logic, assigning them semi-philosophical roles rather than treating them as nature spirits:

- **The Mason** as the **Lawgiver** -- the principle of structure and order
- **The Carpenter** as the **Worldbinder** -- the principle of interconnection
- **The Jeweler** as the **Crafter of Worth** -- the principle of value and refinement
- **The Painter** as the **Spark of Ideal** -- the principle of aspiration and vision

They are revered less as forces of nature and more as principles of perfection the mortal soul strives toward.

## Cultural Themes

Varnathi society revolves around three pillars: **honor**, **legacy**, and **self-discipline**. Every citizen is expected to contribute to the state's enduring greatness, and personal achievement is measured not by wealth alone but by the lasting mark one leaves on civilization.

Their speech is formal and archaic, heavy with metaphors drawn from crafting and architecture. A Varnathi politician speaks of "vaults" and "keystones" the way others speak of trust and friendship.

## Society and Caste

Varnathi society is highly stratified, based on one's perceived duty to the state:

- **The Pillar Houses** -- old aristocratic families who dominate the Senate and traditionally hold the Consulship of Pillars
- **The [[Crimson Guard]]** -- the elite military class, publicly revered but privately rife with corruption and entitlement
- **The Citizenry** -- common merchants, artisans, and farmers
- **The Assimilated** -- non-human citizens, primarily [[Orkin]], who are granted rights but face systemic prejudice

## Government: The Pentarchy

The [[Varnathi Pentarchy]] is a balanced republic led by five co-equal Consuls, each representing a core pillar of society:

1. **The Consul of Pillars** -- represents the old aristocratic families and internal affairs
2. **The Consul of Coin** -- represents the powerful merchant and artisan guilds
3. **The Consul of Scrolls** -- represents foreign relations and serves as liaison to the [[Magocracy]]
4. **The Consul of the People** -- represents the citizenry, managing civic works and public sentiment
5. **The Consul of Blades** -- supreme commander of the [[Crimson Guard]] and all defensive forces

## Major Factions

Three competing ideologies vie for control of [[Vernath]]'s future:

- **The Crimson Banner (The Hawks)** -- the political arm of the military, championing a strong, proactive force. Led by the popular General [[Valerius]].
- **The Marble Hand (The Traditionalists)** -- the old guard who believe stability and strict adherence to the [[Magocracy]]'s peace are paramount
- **The Open Palm Movement (The Reformists)** -- idealists who advocate for true equality for the Assimilated, arguing that [[Vernath]]'s strength lies in unity, not military might

## Current Political Climate

As of 412 T.A., the [[Varnathi Pentarchy]] is in a state of near-paralysis. General [[Valerius]], immensely popular after his celebrated campaigns, constantly pushes for increased military funding and more aggressive border security. He is consistently blocked by the [[Marble Hand]] faction, who fear provoking the [[Magocracy]]. The Consuls of Coin and People are caught in the middle.

## Common Sayings

- *"Measure twice, live once."* -- Care in action ensures survival.
- *"Stone breaks louder when ignored."* -- Do not ignore building problems or quiet people.
- *"A crooked vault falls silent."* -- Dishonor brings ruin.

## Verses and Traditions

The Varnathi preserve their wisdom in architectural recitations and meditation verses. The **Marble Law** is recited by architects and statesmen alike: *"Shape is meaning, and meaning is fate. The vault holds because the line holds. What the wall forgets, the floor remembers."*

Their meditation verse **Ash and Echo** captures the Varnathi spirit: *"We are not made, we are struck. Like bronze upon stone, sparked by will. In the halls of silence, purpose hums."*
