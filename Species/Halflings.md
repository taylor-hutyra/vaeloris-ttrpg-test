# Halflings

Halflings have no grand creation myth. The very idea strikes them as a bit presumptuous. When asked where they came from, a Halfling is likely to shrug and say, *"From good soil and sensible parents, same as a prize-winning pumpkin."* Their history does not begin with gods or divine wars, but with the founding of the first permanent settlement, [[Greenmeadow]], in the Second Age. Their story is not one of divine right, but of earned contentment.

## Origin

The Halfling worldview is rooted in the tangible: the warmth of a hearth, the fullness of a pantry, the reliability of a neighbor. They see the grand struggles of the "Tallfolk" — their general term for [[Humans]], [[Elves]], and [[Dwarves]] — as a dreadful amount of fuss over things that don't keep the rain out or make the stew taste any better.

They do not worship gods, finding the concept both abstract and alarming. Instead, they practice a form of neighborly superstition. One might leave a bit of honey on the windowsill for the "Bumble-Sprites" that help the flowers grow, or pour a splash of ale on the ground for "Old Man Root," the spirit of a good field. These are not acts of worship, but of simple, practical courtesy.

Their highest values are **community, comfort, and common sense**. A Halfling hero is not a king who conquers a nation, but the neighbor who organizes the bucket brigade that saves the town from a fire. Their epics are recipes, their sacred texts are town charters, and their greatest virtue is knowing when to mind one's own business.

## Physical Traits

Halflings stand between three and four feet tall, with round, expressive faces and curly hair. They are sturdy and surprisingly tough for their size, with large, slightly furred feet that they almost never cover with shoes. Their build tends toward the comfortable and well-fed, a point of pride in their culture. They possess an almost supernatural ability to move quietly and go unnoticed when they choose.

## Culture

Halfling lore is entirely oral, passed down as fireside stories, tavern toasts, and the kind of practical wisdom a grandparent mutters while weeding a garden. Their dialect of Common, called **Hearth-Speech** or **River-Tongue**, is characterized by colorful metaphors, practical phrasing, and a friendly, informal tone.

Common greetings and phrases:
- *"Well met! Is the kettle on?"*
- *"Mind the stones on your path."* (farewell)
- *"Well, butter my biscuits!"* (surprise)
- *"Straight as a dog's hind leg."* (describing dishonesty)
- *"That's a fox in the pantry."* (a serious, unexpected problem)

Granny Pip's Pocketful of Wisdom:
- *"A fast horse gets you to trouble quicker."*
- *"You can't eat gold, but you can always trade a good pie for it."*
- *"The tallest tree catches the most wind."* (a warning against ambition)
- *"Measure a person by the friends at their table, not the coins in their purse."*
- *"Worrying is like rocking in a chair. Gives you something to do, but gets you nowhere."*

## Sub-Groups

### The Hearthfolk (Homestead Halflings)

The [[Hearthfolk]] are the heart and soul of Halfling society — the farmers, brewers, artisans, and innkeepers who build and maintain the vibrant, comfortable communities known as **Burrows**. Their lives are governed by the seasons, town charters, and the unwritten laws of good neighborliness. Governance is through **Town Councils** of respected elders, with an elected **Mayor** serving a one-year term. Being Mayor is considered a burdensome civic duty, not a position of power — a common phrase is, *"Never trust a man who wants to be Mayor."*

Major families include:
- **The Thistlewicks** — A sprawling, influential trading family, worldly and a bit too ambitious by Halfling standards
- **The Appledowns** — The farming heart of the Commonwealth, deeply traditional and isolationist
- **The Rumblkins** — Hill-dwelling miners, gruff and pragmatic

### The Wayfinders (Wandering Halflings)

The [[Wayfinders]] are the exception to the Halfling rule, driven by a curiosity that outweighs their love of comfort — a trait their Hearthfolk kin call **"the Itchy-Foot."** They venture into the wider world as adventurers, traders, couriers, and rogues, thriving in the bustling cities of the Tallfolk. They use their natural stealth, cleverness, and disarming charm to navigate the "Big Scramble." In cities like [[Aurumport]] and [[M'ren'r']], they operate through the **Spicers' Guild** — on the surface a legitimate merchant guild, in reality a vast intelligence network and mutual aid society led by the shadowy **Whisper-Master**.

## Notable Figures

- **Milo Thistlewick** — A classic Wayfinder with deep connections across the [[Mirennian League]]
- **The Whisper-Master** — Shadowy leader of the [[Spicers' Guild]], the Wayfinder intelligence network
- **The River-Folk Commodore** — Leader of the semi-nomadic Halflings who live on flotillas along the great rivers

## Other Enclaves

- **The River-Folk of the Silkenflow** — Semi-nomadic Halflings on flotillas of interconnected river barges, led by a Flotilla-Council where each barge captain has an equal vote. Their Commodore is elected for their knowledge of the river's currents and skill in trade negotiations. They are masters of smuggling and fiercely independent
- **The Brinepipers of the Salty Coast** — Hardy coastal Halflings on [[Novaterra]], renowned fishers, salt-miners, and brewers of famously potent seaweed grog. Their Mayor is chosen not by vote but by who brings in the largest fish during the annual Brine-Tide Festival. They have a tense but profitable relationship with the [[Pirate Confederacy]], acting as primary food suppliers in exchange for protection from [[Mirennian League]] taxes
- **The Hollowkin Settlement of [[Aerin'r']]** — An integrated community with local [[Gnomes]], governed by a Joint-Hollow Council, a testament to the historic **Declaration of Hollowkin Rights**
