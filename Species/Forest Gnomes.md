# Forest Gnomes

The **Forest Gnomes**, or **Root-Tenders**, are the original and most numerous of the [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] people. They live in hidden **Hollows** beneath the great forests of the mainland, most notably in the hills surrounding [[The Dreaming Grove]]. Their culture is a perfect reflection of their creation story: a synthesis of naturalism and intricate craft, where tending to living systems and building delicate mechanisms are seen as two expressions of the same sacred work.

## Origin and Creation

The Forest Gnomes trace their creation to the **Twin Roots** -- the two divine mothers who shaped them with patience and purpose:

- **[[Ceryn-Maia]]** (The Green Mother, Ceridara) -- the nurturing force of living systems and growth
- **[[Ilys-Tekne]]** (The Bright Artisan, Illysia) -- the refining force of craft, design, and precision

In the Gnomish telling, the world was a vast, beautiful, but slightly imperfect garden. While other gods raised mountains and forged stars, the Twin Roots saw beauty in the small, essential things -- the way a root system holds a hillside, the way moss breaks down stone into soil, the way a single gear can turn a great machine. They grew the [[Gnomes]] from the deep magic of the forest floor and imbued them with an artisan's soul.

## Core Philosophy: Balance

The Forest Gnomes believe the work of the Twin Roots is a delicate dance between nature and artifice. Their sacred duty -- the **Mori-Syna** (the Quiet Work) -- is to maintain that balance, serving as the unseen mechanics, gardeners, and harmonizers who mend the world's intricate patterns. Their most sacred proverb captures this perfectly: *"The deepest roots make no sound."*

The cataclysm others call [[The Paroxysm]] is known to the Gnomes as **Syn-Thalos** -- the Unraveling of the Pattern. They view [[Kael'Zorai]] not as an evil entity but as a flawed design, a gear that ground against the others until the entire machine threatened to shatter.

## Mycohollow: The Fungal City

[[Mycohollow]] is the largest and most influential Gnomish settlement -- a city woven into a gargantuan, semi-sentient fungal grove. The city is a living organism as much as a settlement, its structures grown rather than built, its corridors lit by bioluminescent fungi, its communications carried through the mycelial network that connects every Hollow.

The relationship between the Forest Gnomes and their fungal home is not metaphorical but literal. Through the practice of [[Myco-Whispering]], Gnomish mystics commune directly with the grove, sensing its needs, reading its warnings, and guiding its growth. The grove, in turn, provides shelter, food, medicine, and a communication network that spans the entire settlement.

## The Practice of Myco-Whispering

[[Myco-Whispering]] is the Forest Gnomes' unique bio-magical discipline -- the art of communing with fungal networks. A skilled Myco-Whisperer can:

- Sense disturbances in the forest through the mycelial web
- Encourage specific fungal growths for food, medicine, or building material
- Send messages through the network to distant Hollows
- Detect the health of the broader ecosystem by reading the grove's chemical signals

Myco-Whispering is not a spell but a relationship -- a deep, patient attunement that takes decades to develop. The most accomplished Whisperers are said to dream in the grove's rhythms, their sleep filled with visions of root-networks and spore-clouds.

## Government: The Mycelial Collective

[[Mycohollow]] is governed as a **Symbiotic Council**:

- **The Mycelial Council** -- composed not of elected officials but of Gnomes who have achieved the deepest symbiotic link with the grove. Membership is fluid and earned through communion, not votes. They do not issue commands but "resonate intentions" through the city's network.
- **The Twin Speakers** -- two co-equal leaders representing the Gnomes' dual heritage:
  - **The Myco-Whisperer** -- spiritual leader responsible for the grove's health (representing [[Ceryn-Maia]])
  - **The Geo-Weaver** -- lead artisan responsible for physical structures and the integration of craft and nature (representing [[Ilys-Tekne]])

## Society: Guilds within Hollows

Forest Gnome society is organized into communal **Hollows** (neighborhoods) and specialized **Guilds** (professions). A Gnome belongs to both, creating a cross-woven social fabric. One's standing is determined by the health of their Hollow and the quality of their Guild's work.

For centuries, the Forest Gnomes remained hidden, tending to the world's secret places. It was these Gnomes who eventually emerged to sign the **Declaration of Hollowkin Rights** with the [[Halflings]], establishing formal relations with the wider world.

## Major Factions

The central political conflict in [[Mycohollow]] is not about power but about the correct method for performing the Mori-Syna:

- **The Balancers (The Majority)** -- the current ruling philosophy. They believe the **Syn-Koinos** (the Shared Pattern) is a living system that must be gently guided, mended, and balanced. They favor diplomacy, patience, and subtle intervention.
- **The Purifiers (Scions of the Unbroken Pattern)** -- a zealous faction who believe the world's pattern is actively corrupted by the chaotic actions of the "Loud-Walkers." Their sacred duty is to excise such flaws to restore the original pattern.
- **The Integrators (The New-Growth School)** -- innovators who argue that the Syn-Koinos must evolve. They believe new elements, including the magic of other races and foreign technologies, can be woven into the pattern to make it stronger.

## View of Other Gnomes

The Forest Gnomes are the most diplomatic of the Gnomish peoples. They view the [[Rock Gnomes]] as endearingly stubborn -- brilliant craftsmen whose precision is admirable, if a bit cold and mechanical. They see the [[Sand Gnomes]] as tragically isolated from the vibrant "Green," though they respect the Dune-Whisperers' extraordinary ability to find life in the harshest of places.

## Relations with the Wider World

The Forest Gnomes maintain careful diplomatic relations with several peoples:

- They brokered peace with the [[Halflings]] and signed a treaty with the [[Magocracy]]
- A small, wealthy enclave of **Crystal-Singers** operates within [[Mórenórë]], crafting vital focusing crystals and arcane conduits for [[Aen'valar]] scholars
- They refer to the larger races as "Loud-Walkers" or "Sky-Folk" -- neutral, descriptive terms without malice

## Common Sayings

- *"Kelos on mori."* -- "Growth in silence." The core tenet of their philosophy.
- *"Syn-belos fynora."* -- "May your path have dappled light." A common blessing.
- *"Tekne lia-ni."* -- "Craft with love." A reminder to put soul into one's work.
- *"Thalos arkaios."* -- "The flaw is ancient." A phrase of weary resignation when dealing with others' shortsightedness.

## Root-Rhymes

Forest Gnome wisdom is preserved in **Root-Rhymes** -- instructional proverbs passed between generations:

- *"A tangled root makes a weak tree."*
- *"Measure a gear by its quietest turn."*
- *"The brightest color is hidden in the moss."*
- *"To understand the forest, ask the beetle, not the bear."*
