# Simiah

The **Simiah** are the clever problem-solvers and tool-makers of the [[Waldkyn]], embodying the balance of **Inspiration** ([[Elaraieth]]) and **Stability** ([[Thamédran]]). They combine creative vision with practical know-how, producing a variant that is endlessly inventive but always grounded in what actually works. Their characteristic phrase, *"A better branch can always be built,"* encapsulates their optimistic belief that every problem has a solution waiting to be crafted.

## Role Among the Waldkyn

Simiah serve as the engineers, artisans, and innovators of their communities. In the [[Whispering Vales]], they are the ones who design clever irrigation systems, build bridges that work with the landscape rather than against it, and craft the tools that make daily life easier for all variants. They are natural collaborators, constantly seeking input from others to refine their ideas — a Simiah rarely works alone, preferring to think out loud in the company of others.

## Temperament and Culture

The Simiah are the most socially energetic of the Waldkyn variants, rivaled only by the [[Faun]] in their love of company. But where Faun seek connection through celebration and music, Simiah seek it through collaboration and shared problem-solving. They are the ones who gather around a broken cart, a collapsed bridge, or a puzzle, and immediately begin proposing solutions, debating approaches, and building prototypes from whatever materials are at hand.

Their combination of Inspiration and Stability makes them uniquely suited to the Waldkyn ideal of the Synthesis: they dream big but build solid. A Simiah invention may be ingenious, but it will also be practical, durable, and designed to work in harmony with its environment rather than against it. They view waste and over-engineering as equal forms of Song-Wrong.

## Among the Kith

The Simiah have found their greatest expression among the [[Chartered Kith]] of [[Nexus]], where the city's philosophy of **The Great Weaving** — integrating nature and technology — aligns perfectly with their natural inclinations. Simiah artisans and engineers are among the most prominent figures in Nexus, driving the innovations in bio-architecture and technomancy that define the city. Many serve on or aspire to the **Weaver's Synod**, their masterworks of synthesis earning them seats of influence.

In the [[Whispering Vales]], the Simiah occupy an ambiguous position. The [[Quiet Vales Kith]]'s philosophy of Naturalism sits uneasily with the Simiah drive to build and improve, and more than a few Simiah have departed the Vales for Nexus over the centuries, drawn by the promise of a place where their talents are celebrated rather than restrained. Those who remain in the Vales channel their inventiveness into more naturalistic pursuits — clever agricultural techniques, living-wood construction, and tools crafted from found materials.

## The Great Weaving Debate

The Simiah are at the heart of the philosophical divide between the [[Quiet Vales Kith]] and the [[Chartered Kith]]. The traditionalists argue that Simiah inventiveness, left unchecked, leads inevitably to the kind of technological excess that is a Song-Wrong. The progressives counter that the Simiah embody exactly the kind of active, evolving balance that the Synthesis was always meant to be. This debate plays out in miniature within Simiah communities everywhere, making them perhaps the most philosophically conflicted of the eight variants.
