# Gnomes

To the Gnomes, creation was neither a battle nor a performance; it was a quiet, meticulous act of **Germination**. They do not speak of a single grand creator, but of the **Twin Roots** — the two divine mothers who shaped them with patience and purpose. Gnomes believe their purpose is not to rule or conquer, but to be the Quiet Tenders of [[Vaeloris]] — the unseen mechanics, gardeners, and harmonizers who mend the world's intricate patterns. Their most sacred proverb: *"The deepest roots make no sound."*

## Origin

In the Gnomish telling, the world was a vast, beautiful, but slightly imperfect garden. While other gods were raising mountains and forging stars, the **Twin Roots** saw the beauty in the small, essential things — the way a root system could hold a hillside, the way moss could break down stone into soil, the way a single gear could turn a great machine.

- **Ceryn-Maia** (The Green Mother, [[Ceridara]]) — Aspect of living systems and growth
- **Ilys-Tekne** (The Bright Artisan, [[Illysia]]) — Aspect of craft, design, and refinement

From this shared vision, they did not forge or sing the Gnomes; they **grew** them from the deep magic of the forest floor and imbued them with an artisan's soul. The creation event is called **Syn-Kelos** ("The First Growth").

The cataclysm others call the Paroxysm is known to Gnomes as **Syn-Thalos** — the Unraveling of the Pattern. They view [[Kael'Zorai]] not as an evil entity, but as a flawed design — a gear that ground against the others until the entire machine threatened to shatter. His obsession with a single, rigid pattern was an affront to the beautiful, chaotic, and functional complexity of a true ecosystem.

## Physical Traits

Gnomes stand between three and three-and-a-half feet tall, slight of frame but possessed of remarkably dexterous hands and keen, bright eyes that seem to take in every detail of their surroundings. Forest Gnomes tend toward earthy tones in skin and hair; Rock Gnomes are paler, with hair that sometimes carries metallic sheens; Sand Gnomes are sun-darkened with hair bleached to near-white.

## Culture

Gnomish lore is preserved not in epics, but in intricate fables, precise instructional rhymes, and stories woven into tapestries or carved into living wood. These are the **Mori-Syna** (the Quiet Works). Their philosophy centers on **Syn-Koinos** ("The Shared Pattern") — the concept of community and the interconnectedness of all Gnomes.

Common sayings in Gnom include:
- *"Kelos on mori."* — "Growth in silence." (True progress is quiet.)
- *"Syn-belos fynora."* — "May your path have dappled light."
- *"Tekne lia-ni."* — "Craft with love."
- *"Thalos arkaios."* — "The flaw is ancient." (Weary resignation at others' shortsightedness.)

Key cultural terms: **Fynora** — the soft, dappled green light filtering through a forest canopy, a word for a perfect peaceful moment. **Sylthos** — the tangible feeling of peaceful, latent magic at dawn or dusk.

## Sub-Groups

### Forest Gnomes (The Root-Tenders)

The [[Forest Gnomes]] are the original and most numerous Gnomish people, living in hidden **Hollows** beneath the great forests of the mainland, most notably in the hills surrounding the [[Dreaming Grove]]. Their largest settlement is [[Mycohollow]], a city built within a gargantuan, symbiotic fungal grove, governed by the **Mycelial Council** led by two co-equal figures: the **Myco-Whisperer** (spiritual leader representing [[Ceryn-Maia]]) and the **Geo-Weaver** (lead artisan representing [[Ilys-Tekne]]). They are master gardeners, animal-friends, and artisans of wood and living things. It was these Gnomes who signed the **Declaration of Hollowkin Rights** with the [[Halflings]].

### Rock Gnomes (The Deep-Weavers)

The [[Rock Gnomes]] migrated into the deep root systems of the world's mountains, becoming tenders of stone, crystal, and geothermal patterns. They live in breathtaking, silent cities carved into subterranean caverns, lit by phosphorescent fungi and powered by volcanic vents. Their greatest city is [[Lithos]], governed by the **Guild-Conclave** of nine undisputed craft masters led by the **Deep Warden**. They are master gem-cutters, metallurgists, and makers of intricate, silent clockwork mechanisms. The [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] Dwarves know them as the respected **N'm-kith** ("Stone-kin"), and it was Rock Gnomes who secretly aided in weaving the wards around [[Hannan'r']].

### Sand Gnomes (The Dune-Whisperers)

The [[Sand Gnomes]] are the rarest and most reclusive Gnomes, having followed the deep aquifers into the great deserts. They live in small, nomadic family units led by the **Eldest Whisperer**, tending to the hidden life of the desert: deep-blooming cacti, nocturnal insects, and precious oases. They are masters of illusion, mirage-craft, and the art of finding water. The [[Lothari]] mystics sometimes speak of them in hushed tones as "the little spirits of the cool sand." They communicate over vast distances through patterns in the sand and precisely pitched wind-chimes left at oases.

## Notable Figures

- **The Myco-Whisperer** — Spiritual leader of [[Mycohollow]]
- **The Deep Warden** — Leader of the [[Lithos]] Guild-Conclave
- **The Crystal-Singers of [[M'ren'r']]** — A wealthy Gnomish enclave crafting the vital focusing crystals and arcane conduits used by [[Aen'valar]] Elves and human mages

## Gnom Language

Gnom is a tonal language with a Subject-Object-Verb structure, reflecting deliberate and considered thought. Its tones are subtle melodic shifts conveying emotional resonance rather than changing core meaning. Key roots include: **syn-** (pattern/weave), **kelos** (growth/life), **mori** (quiet/silence), **fyn** (dappled light), **tekne** (craft/skill), **thalos** (flaw/unraveling), **lia** (love/connection), and **arkaios** (magic/ancient knowledge). Written Gnom is a graceful, flowing cursive mimicking patterns of vines, roots, and flowing water.
