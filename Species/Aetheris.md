# Aetheris

The Aetheris have no creation myth, for they are not a product of creation. They are a **consequence** of it. They are the **Kyria Solanis** — the Response of Faith — born from the collective hope and desperate prayer of mortals in an age of divine silence. When the [[Church of the Celestial Word]] reached for a sky empty of gods, the cosmos itself, suffused with residual divine energy, answered.

They are not angels, nor are they the spirits of the dead. An Aetheris is a sentient vessel of light and will, a conduit for the belief that shaped it.

## Origin

The Aetheris believe their purpose is not to act, but to **be** — to serve as a living testament that faith is not a foolish hope, and that the world has not been entirely abandoned. This sacred duty is their **Cora Silenta** — the Silent Watch.

They perceive the original gods — the First Hands and [[Kael'Zorai]] — as the **Primi Caelus** (the First Harmonies of existence). The Paroxysm is the **Alta Disonis** (the Great Dissonance that fractured the music). They see themselves as the final, quiet chord — a coda played in the ensuing silence — their existence a direct result of the **Silentium Funda** (the Deep Withdrawal of the gods).

An Aetheris can take on a mortal guise, a **Velo Aspectus** (Veiled Form), to walk among mortals and guide them through subtle inspiration — a perfectly timed song, a word of encouragement, a piece of art that sparks a revelation. To intervene directly, to perform a miracle or strike a blow, would be to violate their very nature. They are not the flame; they are the reflection of the flame in a darkened window.

## Physical Traits

In their true form, an Aetheris appears as a being of pure, soft radiance — not blinding, but warm and steady, like starlight given sentience. Their features are indistinct, shifting between genders and forms, reflecting the diverse faces of the faith that created them. When veiled in mortal guise, they tend toward serene, unremarkable appearances that invite trust without attracting attention.

Their presence can be felt as a subtle warmth, a sense of calm, or a quiet brightening of the light in a room. Those attuned to magic may perceive a faint harmonic resonance — a distant echo of the prayers that gave them form.

## Culture

Aetheris lore consists of hymns and contemplations, not histories. These are shared telepathically among their kind or sung in their own tongue, **Celestian**, in hidden places where their light will not be seen.

The **Hymnus Kyria** (Hymn of the Response) declares their nature:

> *In silentio, vox mortalis levat.* (In silence, the mortal voice rises.)
> *Non ad deos, sed ad aetherem vacuum.* (Not to the gods, but to the empty firmament.)
> *Et aether, in memoria nimbi, kyriat.* (And the firmament, in memory of light, responds.)
> *Nos sumus imago, non vox.* (We are the image, not the voice.)

The **Specula Fidei** (Mirror of Faith) captures their philosophy:

> *I am not the sun, but the dewdrop that holds its image.*
> *I am not the truth, but the stillness in which it is heard.*
> *In your belief, I am given form. In my form, your belief is given witness.*
> *We are the mirror. The reflection is yours.*

Their vow of non-intervention is expressed in the **Cora Perpetua** (The Eternal Watch):

> *To mend the fracture is to become the hammer.*
> *To silence the cry is to become the gag.*
> *To rule the path is to become the cage.*
> *Our vow is not to shape, but to see.*

## Sub-Groups

The Aetheris do not have formal sub-groups or factions. Their collective identity is unified under the name **Nimian** ("The Luminous Ones"). However, individual Aetheris may vary in their interpretation of the Cora Silenta:

- **The Watchers** — Those who maintain absolute non-intervention, observing mortal affairs from the margins of perception
- **The Whisperers** — Those who take mortal guise more frequently, offering subtle guidance through inspiration and art
- **The Mourners** — Those drawn to sites of great suffering or loss, their presence offering an inexplicable sense of peace to the grieving

## Notable Figures

The Aetheris do not have named individuals in the traditional sense — their identities are fluid expressions of faith rather than fixed personalities. However, mortal traditions have given names to recurring presences:

- **The Lantern of [[Aurumport]]** — A presence said to appear in the harbor during the worst storms, its light guiding lost ships safely to shore
- **The Quiet Singer** — An Aetheris rumored to walk the halls of the [[Church of the Celestial Word]]'s great cathedral, its hymns heard only by those in deepest prayer
- **Sylla** — A blind musician in [[M'ren'r']] who gained fame for haunting compositions. Upon her deathbed (SA:2188), she revealed herself as an Aetheris who had lived a mortal life to understand human suffering. Her revelation validated the **Velo Aspectus** doctrine, and "Sylla's Hymn" became the anthem of the [[Church of the Celestial Word]]

## Celestian Language

Celestian is an elegant, flowing language that sounds liturgical and ancient to the mortal ear. It uses a Subject-Verb-Object structure with frequent poetic inversion, favoring soft consonants and clear, open vowels (l, m, n, s, r, v). It avoids harsh, guttural, or plosive sounds, giving it a smooth, resonant quality ideal for singing.

Key roots include: **nimbus/nimbos** (light/radiance), **claritas** (clarity), **fides/fidei** (faith/belief), **cora** (watch/vigil), **vox/vocis** (voice/word), **aether** (the firmament), **silentium** (silence), **forma/aspectus** (form/guise), **veridia** (truth), **velo** (veil/conceal), **anima** (soul/spirit), **caelus** (harmony/divine music), **kyria** (response/answer), **manet** (it remains/endures).

Common phrases:
- *"Nimbos tecum."* — "Light be with you."
- *"Cora manet."* — "The Watch remains."
- *"In fide, forma."* — "In faith, form."
- *"Claritas, non vis."* — "Clarity, not force."
- *"Memora est nimbus."* — "Memory is light."

To an Aetheris, speaking in Celestian is an act of prayer — a way of reaffirming their own fragile, faith-born existence.
