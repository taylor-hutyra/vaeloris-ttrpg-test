# Orkin

To the Orkin, creation was neither a song nor a structure, but a **tempering**. The world was forged in the mind of a single, purposeful entity they remember as [[Grom-Kain]] — the Will-Forge, the Shaper of What Must Be. He did not sing the world into being; He hammered it from the grit and dross left behind by the chaotic first moments, instilling in it the one principle that mattered: **endurance**. The Orkin were not an afterthought, but His masterwork of survival.

## Origin

[[Grom-Kain]], seeing a world of soft beauty and fragile harmonies, recognized the need for a creation that could not be broken. From the shadow of His forge-light and the unyielding iron in the world's heart, He forged the Orkin. From this **First Tempering** came two aspects of a single, unbreakable will:

- **The Gral** ("Stone-Fist") — The larger of the Orkin peoples, forged to embody endurance through strength and resilience. They are the shield, the anchor, the unwavering line. Other races call them **Orcs**.
- **The Skorn** ("Shadow-Knife") — The smaller and more numerous Orkin, forged to embody endurance through cunning and adaptability. They are the scout, the trapper, the survivors who thrive in the world's cracks. Other races call them **Goblins**.

The Gral and Skorn are not two races, but the **two fists** of Grom-Kain's will, meant to work in concert.

The event others call the Paroxysm is known to the Orkin as **Kain's Fall**, or **The Great Betrayal**. In their history, the other divine beings — the "**Soft Hands**" — grew jealous of Grom-Kain's perfect, enduring creations. They united not to restore balance, but to shatter the one true testament to order. When Grom-Kain was struck down and banished, the Orkin were left orphaned and scattered.

This is why, when [[Kael'Zorai]]'s influence returns with the [[Silent Blight]], many Orkin tribes do not see it as a foreign corruption — they see it as a homecoming. Their true maker has returned. [[Grom-Kain]] is identified with [[Kael'Zorai]].

## Physical Traits

The **Gral** are powerfully built, standing six to seven feet tall with broad frames, prominent tusks, and skin ranging from deep green to grey-brown. Their bodies are engines of resilience — dense muscle, thick skin, and a remarkable capacity to heal from wounds that would fell other races.

The **Skorn** are small and wiry, standing three to four feet tall with sharp features, large pointed ears, and quick, darting eyes. What they lack in size they make up for in speed, dexterity, and an almost preternatural cunning. Their skin tones range from sickly yellow-green to deep ochre.

## Culture

Orkin myth is passed down through guttural chants, tales told over open fires, and pictograms scratched onto hide or bone — known as the **Darg-Varr** (The Land-Blood stories). The supreme virtue is **Trak** — endurance, the will to survive at any cost. The **Varr** (clan) is the absolute center of Orkin life.

Common sayings in Grokh:
- *"Darg is Darg."* — "Land is land." (What's yours is what you can hold.)
- *"Varr trak, or varr grak."* — "Clan endures, or clan dies."
- *"Skorn-nar, Gral-tak."* — "Cunning first, then strike."
- *"The sky watches. The mud remembers."* (Actions have consequences.)

Most clans are led by a **diarchy**: the **Gral-Kor** (Stone-Hand, the warlord) and the **Skorn-Kli** (Sharp-Eye, the strategic leader). A clan with a weak Gral-Kor may be strong but foolish; a clan without a clever Skorn-Kli may be strong but starves. The balance is essential.

## Sub-Groups

### Varr-Darg (The Land-Blood Clans)

The [[Varr-Darg]] are the traditionalist, often aggressive Orkin clans of the mainland, concentrated in the harsh [[Barren Hills]]. Centuries of conflict with expanding [[Varnathi]] nations have forged them into a hardened and pragmatic people. They hold the most fervent belief in the return of [[Grom-Kain]] and are most susceptible to the call of the [[Silent Blight]].

Key factions:
- **The Bloodied Fist Clans** — Majority faction, believing the only path to reclamation is through war
- **The Whisper-Shamans** — Influential faction of shamans who commune with land spirits, wary of [[Kael'Zorai]]'s "silent, unnatural order" as a perversion of Grom-Kain's true will

### Novaterran Holds (The Free Holds)

The [[Novaterran Holds]] are descendants of Orkin who took the "Mirennian Passage" to [[Novaterra]], seeking escape from the mainland's endless wars. They are pragmatists and opportunists, organized into powerful **Holds** that function like mercenary corporations or industrial syndicates. Leadership is won through business acumen and strategic alliances.

Major Holds:
- **The Iron-Grit Syndicate** — The largest Hold, controlling vast mining operations supplying [[Gearhaven]]
- **The Shattered-Spear Company** — A legendary mercenary Hold known for discipline
- **The Dust-Diggers** — Loose confederation of prospecting Holds roaming Novaterra's deserts

### Vorok-Varr (The Listener's Clan)

The [[Vorok-Varr]] took the most radical path. Founded by the shaman **Vorok the Listener**, they made a vow of peace and settled on [[Aerinórë]], integrating with local [[Waldkyn]] and [[Mirennians]]. Their interpretation of Trak is not about withstanding blows, but withstanding the temptation of violence. Their central philosophy is **Vorok-Kal** ("Listener's Peace"). Governed by a **Council of Listeners** — the tribe's wisest shamans — and protected by the **Peace-Warden**, whose role is entirely defensive.

## Notable Figures

- **Malgrimm** — An Orkin whose old tribe belongs to the Whisper-Shaman faction
- **Vorok the Listener** — Legendary shaman who founded the pacifist [[Vorok-Varr]]
- **The Peace-Warden** — Designated protector of the [[Vorok-Varr]], strongest warrior sworn to defense alone

## Grokh Language

Grokh is guttural, direct, and lacks tenses — meaning is derived from context and tone, spoken almost always in the present active tense. Key roots: **gral** (stone/strength), **skorn** (shadow/cunning), **grom** (will/forge), **kain** (shaper/maker), **trak** (endure/survive), **varr** (blood/clan), **darg** (land/territory), **kor** (hand/fist), **kli** (eye/sight), **grak** (to die/fail), **nak** (no/negative).
