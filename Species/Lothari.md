# The Lothari

The **Lothari** are the desert-dwelling [[Humans]] of the [[Great Desert]], a semi-nomadic people bound by ancient rituals, a shared mystical tradition, and a network of hidden oases and secret routes. Known as the **Desert-Bound Mystics**, they are keepers of memories the rest of the world has forgotten.

## Origin and Creation Belief

The Lothari speak of **The Breathless Dream** -- a sleeping god whose thoughts became reality. They believe [[Humans]] were the first waking thought, born from curiosity itself. The [[The Paroxysm]] was a failed dream trying to reshape the world again, but was forgotten -- an echo of divine restlessness that still ripples through the sands.

This belief gives the Lothari a unique relationship with memory and forgetting. They see themselves as the guardians of what the world dreams and discards, collecting lost knowledge in their oral traditions and dream-rituals.

## View of the Four Makers

The [[Four Makers]] are remembered by the Lothari not by name but by recurring motifs in dreams and visions:

- **The Mason** as **the Pillar** -- the enduring structure that holds up the sky
- **The Carpenter** as **the Wandering Root** -- the hidden connections beneath the sand
- **The Jeweler** as **the Hidden Glint** -- truth buried beneath the surface
- **The Painter** as **the Flickering Breath** -- the ephemeral beauty of mirage and vision

These figures appear more often in Lothari dream-rituals than in formal doctrine. They are felt rather than worshipped, encountered in trance rather than in temples.

## Cultural Themes

Lothari society is defined by three interlocking values: **memory**, **secrecy**, and **vision**. They believe that what is forgotten by others must be preserved, that the deepest truths require protection, and that the waking world is only a thin surface over a vast ocean of meaning.

Their speech is sparse and symbolic, often mixing silence and deliberate pauses for spiritual effect. A Lothari elder might say nothing for several minutes before delivering a single, devastating insight. Words are treated as precious -- to speak carelessly is to waste the breath of the Sleeping God.

## Government: The Silent Network

The [[Lothari Silent Network]] is a council of the most respected mystics and visionaries from each of the major clans. They have no state and no formal borders. The Lothari do not rule by decree but by interpreting dreams and celestial patterns to guide their people's movements and decisions.

Their leadership is a matter of **spiritual authority**, not political power. A member of the Silent Network earns their place through demonstrated prophetic ability, the depth of their dream-walking, and the trust of their clan. Decisions are made by consensus, often after lengthy communal dream-sessions where the entire clan enters a shared trance.

## Society and Way of Life

The Lothari are semi-nomadic, following ancient routes between hidden oases that they guard with fierce secrecy. Their camps are temporary but intricately organized, with every tent and fire-pit placed according to celestial alignment.

Lothari clans are extended family units, each with its own oral history stretching back to the earliest days of the [[Great Desert]]. Children are taught to read the sands, the stars, and the patterns of their own dreams from an early age. By adolescence, every Lothari has undergone the **Dream-Walk** -- a solitary journey into the deep desert to receive a vision that will guide their life.

## Relations with Other Peoples

The Lothari maintain an enigmatic relationship with the wider world. They trade in rare desert herbs, dream-incense, and prophetic counsel, but they never reveal the locations of their oases or the nature of their deepest rituals.

They have a quiet, almost invisible kinship with the [[Sand Gnomes]], whom they call "the little spirits of the cool sand." The two peoples share the desert in mutual respect, occasionally meeting at remote oases to exchange stories and warnings.

The [[Varnathi]] and [[Mirennians]] regard the Lothari with a mixture of fascination and unease. The Lothari, for their part, view the highland philosophers as trapped in rigid thought and the coastal traders as too loud to hear the wind.

## Common Sayings

- *"Wind writes where we forget."* -- The world remembers what we do not.
- *"Drink the shade before the sun drinks you."* -- Take rest or pay for it.
- *"Even silence has a pattern."* -- Listen deeper; there is always meaning.

## Verses and Traditions

The **Veil of Sand** is a fire-circle tale told on the longest nights: *"The first man rose when a thought burned too bright. He walked into the sun's mouth and asked for shade. The sun replied, 'Make your own.' So he did -- every step a shadow, every breath a memory."*

The **Whispering Jug** is a nomadic parable passed between clans: *"The jug you carry holds only what you give it. Empty? That was your choice. Cracked? That was your path. Singing? That was your soul, once."*

The **Dustsong Blessing** is spoken at partings: *"May your footprints fade behind you, but not your shadow. May the wind forget your name, but not your walk."*
