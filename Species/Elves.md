# Elves

The Elves of [[Vaeloris]] do not speak of creation as a single act, but as an unfolding song — a resonance that shaped the world from formlessness into beauty. They trace their essence to the harmonious intertwining of **C'rhanna** (Forest) and **Lelyari'** (Color/Dream), whom they honor as the **Evendari'** — the Archetypal Parents. Their union formed a resonance that awoke awareness in the natural world, and from this song, Elves emerged — not made or placed, but attuned to the music of existence.

## Origin

The world-song was born from the **Lainor'el** — the Four Harmonies, each a divine essence that brought balance and depth to reality:

- **N'rthelion** — Stone, resonance of endurance and structure
- **C'rhanna** — Forest, breath of growth and natural order
- **Mirelaris** — Gem, shimmer of hidden beauty and inner worth
- **Lelyari'** — Color/Dream, light of emotion, vision, and expression

The Great Smith is understood not as a craftsman in form, but as [[Aenareth]] — the Prime Voice who strummed the Heartwood of Silence, setting the resonance in motion like wind through the first hollow tree.

In time, the Elves recall a sorrowful distortion known as the **Alatirn'** — the Dissonance. A Fifth Tone, **Elaru'n**, emerged not by harmony but by assertion, seeking to reshape the music rather than join it. It reverberated with overwhelming precision, attempting to draw all music into its structure. When it failed, it left behind silences where sound once flowed. The Elves do not speak of banishment, but of resting dissonance — a tone now stilled, but not unmourned.

## Physical Traits

Elves are slender and graceful, typically taller than Humans, with sharp, angular features and ears that taper to points. Their movements carry an unconscious fluidity, as if their bodies are still responding to the rhythm of the world-song. Eye colors span an extraordinary range, often reflecting the dominant Harmony of their lineage — greens and golds among the [[Cirhannim]], silvers and blues among the [[Aen'valar]], and pale violets among the [[Sael'anir]].

## Culture

Elven culture is steeped in music, seasonal rite, and the preservation of memory. They view the rise of other mortals as diverging tones born from different intersections of harmony and discord, holding no ill will toward other races but viewing them as the world's attempt to express itself through varied cadences. Their language, **Lainor'el**, is a reflection of their harmonic worldview.

Common phrases include:
- *"L'ren i'laiv'"* — "Sing in light" (a universal blessing)
- *"M'ress' n'r'"* — "Silent path" (contemplation)
- *"Thel an i'aen"* — "Seek in the future" (an Aen'valar farewell)
- *"Sael lenenya"* — "May the wind be your rhythm" (a Sael'anir greeting)

## Sub-Groups

### Cirhannim (The Wood-Spirit People)

The [[Cirhannim]] are the most traditionalist and isolationist of the Elven peoples, residing in the magically concealed forest of [[Hannan'r']]. To them, the world-song is a sacred, finite composition that must be preserved exactly as it was first played. They see change as a form of corruption. Governed by a theocratic monarchy under Queen Elara III and the powerful **Talanvar' Council** of mystics, their lives revolve around the Seasonal Harmonies. Their society is stratified by proximity to the "original harmony" — from Mystics to Silent Sentinels to Lore-Weavers to Green-Tenders.

### Aen'valar (The Forward-Singers)

The [[Aen'valar]] represent the Elven diaspora on [[Novaterra]]. They believe the world-song is not finished but is an ongoing composition. Their sacred duty, the **Aen'valan** ("The Unwritten Song"), is to seek new knowledge and add new verses to creation's epic. They founded the great library-observatory of [[Aethelas]] and were the first to theorize about the Arcane Fraying. Their renowned Archmage **Elaruniel** collaborated with scholars to publish *The Frayed Weave*, the foundational document for the Theory of Arcane Scarcity.

### Sael'anir (The Sky-Wanderers)

The [[Sael'anir]] are a nomadic and contemplative people living in small traveling bands on the pristine continent of [[Aerin'r']]. Their philosophy, the **Sael'mora** ("The Sky-Path"), holds that the world-song is not meant to be preserved or changed, but simply experienced. True wisdom comes from constant movement, allowing one to hear the song from all possible angles. They have no permanent cities, gathering every five years at the **Star-Moot** where decisions require unanimous consensus among all band elders.

## Notable Figures

- **Queen Elara III** — Monarch of the Verdant Throne of [[Hannan'r']]
- **Archmage Elaruniel** — Renowned [[Aen'valar]] scholar, author of *The Frayed Weave*, former Loremaster Prime of [[Aethelas]]
- **Lelyariel** — "Radiant Dreamer," legendary Elven queen of the First Age

## Lainor'el Language

Elvish follows a Subject-Verb-Object structure with frequent poetic inversion. Key roots include: **aen-** (forward/future), **cir-** (forest), **sael-** (sky/wind), **thel-** (to seek/to know), **mel-** (to love), **lelya-** (dream/flow), and **-iel** (harmony/melody). Pronouns: **enya** (I), **es'** (you), **elen** (they), **an'** (we). The language preserves its harmonic and spiritual character through soft, flowing phonetics.
