# The N'nm-Khazal'n

The **N'nm-Khazal'n**, or **Stone-Sworn**, are the keepers of [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] tradition -- the deeply orthodox clans who reside in the great mountain kingdom of [[Kharnzarak]]. They believe that Dwarven craft reached its apex in the First Age and that their sacred duty is to preserve those ancient techniques and societal structures without deviation. Their cultural analog is Norse/Germanic in character.

## Origin and Core Philosophy

The N'nm-Khazal'n trace their creation to the enduring alliance of [[Tharn'm]] and [[Farrazul]], two of the [[Four Pillars of Craft]]. They hold that [[Dwarves]] were shaped from stone and soul-deep ore, carved beneath the world's crust, and imbued with rhythm, purpose, and permanence. The Pillars are not worshipped as gods but honored as **Fundamentals** -- primal truths reflected in every aspect of Dwarven life.

Their highest virtue is **Thral'k** (Endurance) -- the ability to maintain cultural and structural integrity against the pressures of time and change. They are deeply suspicious of mortal magic, which they view as a chaotic and unreliable force that defies the structured, rhythmic nature of true creation.

## The Fracture of Law

Of the great cataclysm others call the [[Paroxysm]], the N'nm-Khazal'n speak cautiously. They call it **Dur'k Thul** (the Fracture of Law), a time when the pattern cracked. Ancient stone records speak of an additional force, the [[Ulzammar]] -- not a being but a concept that sought to refine creation into stillness. It attempted to seal the fires, stop the winds, and bind the stars in numerical lattice. The name Ulzammar has been chipped away in many vaults, and elder runes are lost. To speak of it aloud is discouraged in the deep halls.

## Government: The Stone-Sworn Hegemony

[[Kharnzarak]] is ruled by a hereditary monarchy bound by sacred law:

- **The High King** -- absolute monarch ruling from the Adamant Throne. The current ruler is **High King [[Thorgar VII]]** of the [[Barazkor Clan]].
- **The Stone-Lay Council** -- nine clan-lords serving as advisors, high court, and legislative body. Their unanimous opposition can delay a royal decree, forcing the king to justify his decision before the ancestral tombs.

## Societal Structure: The Rigid Caste of Craft and Clan

N'nm-Khazal'n society is a pyramid:

- **The High Clans** -- noble families whose lineage ties directly to legendary First Age artisans and warriors
- **The Guild Clans** -- families whose identity and standing are defined by hereditary craft
- **The Gatestone Clans** -- those who perform the most menial and dangerous labor

Upward mobility is virtually nonexistent. Clan and craft are inextricably linked, and one's birth determines one's path.

## Major Clans and Factions

- **The [[Barazkor Clan]] (The Iron-Gavels)** -- the Royal Clan. Fiercely traditionalist and militant, they are wardens of [[Kharnzarak]]'s military and its laws. Lord [[Durin Stone-Fist]] is a ranking member and the king's most trusted enforcer.
- **The [[Farraz'l Clan]] (The Gem-Cutters)** -- the wealthiest of the High Clans, controlling the kingdom's vast gem and precious metal mines. Outwardly traditional, inwardly opportunistic.
- **The [[Gulgrym Clan]] (The Deep-Miners)** -- the most powerful Guild Clan, representing the miners who form [[Kharnzarak]]'s economic backbone. A downtrodden but proud people, harboring deep resentments toward the High Clans.
- **The [[Bronr'n Legacy]] (The Secret Reformists)** -- a clandestine society of artisans and scholars who secretly revere the Pillar of Growth. They believe [[Kharnzarak]]'s stagnation will be its doom, and they study forbidden [[Elm-korin]] schematics and [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] designs in hidden workshops.

## View of Other Dwarves

The N'nm-Khazal'n see the [[Thulkor'n]] as common criminals who broke their oaths -- a source of shame best forgotten. They view the [[Elm-korin]] with a mixture of pity and contempt, as dangerous idealists who abandoned the purity of the stone for the fleeting chaos of magic.

They maintain a quiet, almost invisible respect for [[Gnomes]], particularly the [[Rock Gnomes]], whom they call the **N'mm-kith** ("Stone-kin"), recognizing them as fellow children of a Stone-based Pillar.

## Stone-Lays and Tradition

Dwarven myth is preserved in **Stone-Lays** -- verses etched into cavern walls, gates, and great forgehalls. They are spoken, sung, or hammered in rhythm. The most sacred Stone-Lays are carved into the **D'm-G'l**, the deep halls where the first kings ruled. To alter a Stone-Lay is an act of supreme sacrilege.

## Common Sayings

- *"N'm zalg'l."* -- "Stone does not forget." Said when recording events or delivering justice.
- *"Kor zul elm."* -- "Hammer before flame." Prioritize structure before beauty or emotion.
- *"D'm baraz."* -- "May your forge hold." A blessing for stability, family, and strength.
- *"Khaz'n dur."* -- "We chisel in silence." A phrase signaling reverence, secrecy, or sacred duty.
- *"Barak-n'm."* -- "Stone be with you." A formal greeting.
- *"Zulg'l-d'm."* -- "Until the echo returns." A farewell expressing hope to meet again.

## Other Notable Enclaves

Beyond [[Kharnzarak]], two smaller N'nm-Khazal'n groups persist:

- **The [[Zelg'l-Kharn]] (Echo-Clan)** of [[Bjalmund's Spire]] -- a fortress-monastery high in the Dragon's Tooth mountains, cut off from [[Kharnzarak]] for centuries. They blend traditional ancestor-worship with reverence for the mountain's fury, led by a **Runespeaker**.
- **The Bronze-Crest Clans** of the southern coasts -- coastal city-states founded by N'nm-Khazal'n who rejected isolationism. They are master shipwrights whose enchanted "stone-ships" are plated with bronze, governed by a Council of Captain-Thanes.
