# The Drakona

The **Drakona** are a proud and ancient people of draconic lineage — humanoid in form but unmistakably dragonkind. Once the unchallenged rulers of the sky, they commanded a vast empire of floating Cloud Cities sustained by aeromancy, descending upon the surface world in SA:2341 to subjugate the nations of [[Vaeloris]]. Their empire was shattered in the cataclysm of the **Earth-Anchor Ritual** (SA:2688), and they have since endured centuries of occupation, segregation, and slow cultural erosion — a fallen people whose pride has never broken.

Individual Drakona are called **Drakon** (masculine) or **Drakona** (feminine). Their language carries Slavic-inspired phonetics — heavy consonant clusters, rolling vowels, and a rhythm that rises like smoke from embers.

## Physical Traits

Drakona are humanoid with pronounced draconic features. Their skin is covered in fine, interlocking scales that range from obsidian-black to burnished copper, pale silver, or deep crimson depending on bloodline. Their eyes are slitted and luminous, often amber or molten gold, and glow faintly in darkness.

Most Drakona possess vestigial wings — leathery membranes folded against the back, too small for true flight but capable of gliding short distances. Before the [[Earth-Anchor Ritual]], the noble bloodlines bore full, functional wings. Since the Skyfall, natural flight has been magically stripped from the species entirely.

All Drakona are innately heat-resistant, able to withstand temperatures that would kill other mortals. Members of the elder bloodlines — the **Ognevein** (Fire-Blooded) — can produce gouts of flame from their throats, though this ability has diminished across generations. They are physically powerful, with dense bone structure and clawed hands, standing slightly taller than [[Humans]] on average.

The most culturally significant physical trait is the presence or absence of wings. Full-winged Drakona were the aristocracy of the Cloud Cities. **Flightless Drakona** — those born with withered or absent wings — have been treated as second-class citizens throughout their history, a prejudice that fueled the [[Broken Wing Insurgency]].

## Homeland: Drakenvast

The ancestral territory of the Drakona is **[[Drakenvast]]** — a vast, mountainous region of volcanic highlands, frozen tundra, and geothermal valleys in the far north-east of [[Vaeloris]]. Before the Skyfall, their true cities floated above these peaks in the permanent cloud layer, sustained by ancient aeromantic rituals. The greatest of these was **[[Aethelgard]]**, the primary Cloud City.

Their ancestral ground-city, **[[Drakograd]]** (the "Hearth of Scales"), served as a mining and forging center carved into a dormant volcano. After the Skyfall, the ruins of the fallen Cloud Cities were designated the **[[District of Scales]]** — a walled reservation where surviving Drakona were confined by the [[Magocracy]].

Drakenvast is rich in volcanic minerals, fire-forged metals, and **Ember Crystal** — a naturally occurring substance that stores thermal energy. Control of these resources drove much of the Drakona's imperial ambition.

## Government: The Vozhdate

The Drakona are governed by an autocratic system centered on the **Vozhd** (Supreme Leader), who claims to be the living vessel of the Eternal Flame. The Vozhd holds absolute authority over military, religious, and civil matters.

The **Council of Scales** is a nominally advisory body of twelve elder Drakona drawn from the great bloodlines. In practice, it rubber-stamps the Vozhd's decrees. Dissent within the Council is rare and punished swiftly — members who object too loudly tend to suffer "accidents" in the volcanic forges.

The cult of personality around the Vozhd is central to Drakona governance. The current political structure emerged after the Skyfall, when the surviving military commanders consolidated power around a single leader to prevent total collapse. What began as wartime necessity calcified into permanent autocracy.

## The Three Cultural Branches

### Khor'drak — The Imperial Loyalists

The **Khor'drak** ("Heart of the Dragon") are the old-blood aristocracy — the descendants of the full-winged noble houses who ruled the Cloud Cities. They control the Drakona heartland and dominate the military hierarchy. Their ideology is one of Drakona supremacy: the Eternal Flame forged them to rule, and the Skyfall was not defeat but a temporary exile.

They are the cultural backbone of the Vozhdate, producing its generals, its propagandists, and its secret police — the **Zhar'kov** ("Flame-Watchers"), who root out dissent and monitor the loyalty of Drakona abroad. The Khor'drak maintain an elaborate code of military honor, patronize the traditional arts, and sponsor fire-forging academies. Their architecture is monumental — basalt spires, flame-lit halls, and sculptures carved from cooling lava.

### Vyr'skalii — The Protectorate Administrators

The **Vyr'skalii** ("Scale-Beyond") are the Drakona stationed in foreign lands, ostensibly to "protect Drakona minorities" living outside the homeland. In reality, they serve as occupation forces, intelligence operatives, and cultural enforcers in territories the Vozhdate claims as spheres of influence.

Some Vyr'skalii genuinely believe in their protective mission — they see scattered Drakona communities suffering discrimination in [[Humans|Human]] and [[Elves|Elven]] cities, and view themselves as their only shield. Others are cynical occupiers who use the protection narrative to extract resources and project power. The Vyr'skalii maintain garrison-towns in border regions, staffed by soldiers who may spend decades away from Drakenvast. Their children grow up between cultures, often speaking the local tongue better than Drakonii.

### Zhel'nov — The Reformists and Exiles

The **Zhel'nov** ("Those Who Turn") are the Drakona intelligentsia — artists, scholars, merchants, and dissidents who reject the Vozhdate's authoritarianism and seek integration with other races. Many Zhel'nov have fled the homeland entirely, forming exile communities in [[M'ren'r']], [[Novaterra]], and cosmopolitan port cities.

Zhel'nov writers and composers produce some of the most haunting art in [[Vaeloris]] — music that blends Drakona fire-chants with Mirennian sea-songs, literature that grapples with collective guilt over the invasion era. They are loathed by the Khor'drak as traitors and watched by the Zhar'kov even in exile. Despite this, the Zhel'nov maintain underground networks that smuggle dissidents out of Drakenvast and funnel forbidden texts back in.

## Religion: The Eternal Flame

The Drakona revere the **Eternal Flame** — a creation myth holding that before the world existed, there was only fire. Not destructive fire, but *generative* fire — the heat that forges metal, the warmth that hatches eggs, the light that banishes void. The Drakona believe they were shaped directly from this primordial flame, making them the world's eldest and most essential people.

The Vozhd claims to be the Eternal Flame's living vessel — a divine mandate that elevates political loyalty to religious obligation. Temples to the Flame are found in every Drakona settlement: great braziers of never-dying fire tended by the **Zharnitsy** (Flame-Priests), who also serve as the regime's ideological enforcers.

The Zhel'nov reformists have developed a counter-theology: that the Eternal Flame belongs to all peoples, not the Vozhd alone, and that hoarding its light is the truest heresy.

## The Broken Wing Insurgency (SA:2950)

The deepest wound in Drakona society is the treatment of the **flightless** — those born without wings or with vestigial stubs too withered for even gliding. In the Cloud Cities, the flightless were laborers and servants. After the Skyfall stripped all Drakona of flight, the distinction should have dissolved — but the old prejudice calcified. Full-winged Drakona (even with useless wings) retained social status; the flightless remained underclass.

In SA:2950, the **[[Broken Wing Insurgency]]** erupted — a decades-long campaign of sabotage, assassination, and magical terrorism led by flightless Drakona demanding equal status. They bombed Mana-Pylons, assassinated [[Magocracy]] Magisters, and attacked Khor'drak symbols of authority.

The Vozhdate's response was brutal and cynical. While publicly condemning the insurgents, the regime used the violence to justify crackdowns on *all* dissent — including the peaceful Zhel'nov. The Broken Wing became both a genuine civil rights movement and a convenient pretext for authoritarian control. The insurgency's moral complexity — victims becoming perpetrators, oppression breeding radicalism — remains one of the defining tensions of modern Drakona identity.

## Key Themes

The Drakona embody the tragedy of imperial collapse: genuine cultural greatness — magnificent architecture, haunting music, disciplined military tradition, and fire-forging arts unmatched in [[Vaeloris]] — existing alongside authoritarian brutality, paranoid nationalism, and the exploitation of their own people.

Their defining paradox is the rhetoric of protection. The Vozhdate genuinely believes (or claims to believe) that Drakona abroad are persecuted and need the homeland's shield. This narrative justifies expansion, occupation, and militarism — even as the regime crushes freedom at home.

They are a people shaped by deep paranoia about encirclement: surrounded by the [[Varnathi Pentarchy]], watched by the [[Magocracy]], and haunted by the memory of the Skyfall. Every policy is filtered through the question: *will this prevent another Fall?*
