# Aethelas

**Aethelas** (Elvish: "Star-Sight") is a city-sized library and observatory on [[Novaterra]], founded by the [[Aen'valar]] Elves in SA:2175. Dedicated to the pursuit of the **Aen'valan** ("The Unwritten Song"), it is the global center for magical research and the intellectual heart of the Elven progressive tradition.

## Government

Aethelas is a sovereign city-state governed by the **[[Conclave of Masters]]**, a scholastic meritocracy formed by the heads of the university's most prestigious schools of thought -- Aetherial Physics, Arcane Theory, Historical Resonance, and others. Leadership is earned through groundbreaking research and peer-reviewed discovery.

The **Loremaster Prime** is the elected head of the Conclave, serving a twenty-year term. Their role is to guide the institution's academic focus rather than to rule. The renowned Archmage [[Elaruniel]] held this position for many years, having won a landmark election in SA:3010 against Archmage [[Voronwe]], a [[Magocracy]] loyalist.

## History

Aethelas was founded to serve as a beacon of free inquiry, attracting the most brilliant minds from all races and draining the mainland of its intellectual capital. It became the site of some of the most consequential scholarship in [[Vaeloris]]:

- **SA:2319** -- [[Elaruniel]] published *On the Efficacy of Dissonance*, arguing that harmonic magic was too slow for warfare. She was censured by academics but secretly recruited by the Coalition of Mages.
- **SA:2360** -- Elaruniel applied her Dissonance Theory to create the **Mana-Combustion Drive**, enabling the Allied Air Fleet.
- **SA:2715** -- From seclusion in Aethelas, Elaruniel published *The Frayed Weave*, arguing that the [[Earth-Anchor Ritual]] had broken the immune system of the universe. The [[Magocracy]] banned the text, but it became the bible of the Aen'valar.
- **SA:3010** -- Elaruniel's election as Loremaster Prime marked Aethelas's formal drift away from Magocracy influence.

## Character

Aethelas is a city of spires and observatories, where crystal lenses the size of houses track the movements of stars and the subtle currents of arcane energy. Its libraries contain the most comprehensive collection of knowledge in the known world. Scholars from every race walk its halls, though the [[Aen'valar]] remain the dominant cultural force. The city has a reputation for intellectual courage -- a willingness to pursue knowledge wherever it leads, regardless of political consequence.
