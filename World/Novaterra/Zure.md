# Zure

**Zure** was a floating market built on a massive chunk of land ripped skyward by raw magic during [[The Paroxysm]] of the First Age. Suspended in the high atmosphere, it became the most notorious neutral zone of the [[Dragonfall]] era -- a place where [[Allied Command]] spies, [[Drakona]] defectors, [[Cloudborn Corsairs]], and [[Shadewalkers]] met to trade intelligence and goods far above the war raging below.

## History

Zure emerged as a trading post around SA:2500, during the long aerial war between the Allied nations and the [[Drakona]] Empire. Its elevation and lack of allegiance made it the only place where all factions could conduct business. The market operated under an unwritten code: no weapons drawn, no questions asked, payment in any currency accepted.

For nearly two centuries, Zure was the "Casablanca" of [[Vaeloris]] -- a hub of espionage, black-market arms dealing, and desperate diplomacy. Allied agents purchased intelligence on [[Drakona]] fleet movements. Drakona defectors traded secrets for passage to neutral territory. [[Cloudborn Corsairs]] fenced stolen cargo. [[Shadewalkers]] sold information to anyone who could pay.

## Destruction

On the winter solstice of SA:2688, the [[Allied Command]] cast the **[[Earth-Anchor Ritual]]**, a metaphysical hook that violently grounded the concept of "Flight" within the Drakona's aeromantic matrix. The ritual was too broad -- it destabilized all flight magic. Zure, sustained by the same Paroxysm-era levitation that held it aloft, plummeted along with the [[Drakona]] Cloud Cities, Allied airships, and every floating island in [[Vaeloris]].

## Legacy

Zure's remains now lie as a massive underwater ruin, rumored to contain the lost treasury of the [[Shadewalkers]] and caches of pre-Skyfall technology. Treasure hunters and salvage crews have attempted to locate "Zure's Grave," but the site is deep, treacherous, and said to still crackle with residual arcane energy. The name has become a byword for a place of lost opportunity and the collateral cost of war.
