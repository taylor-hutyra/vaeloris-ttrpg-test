# Vorok-Varr

**Vorok-Varr** is a peaceful [[Orkin]] community on the continent of [[Aerin'r']], founded by **[[Vorok the Listener]]** -- a visionary Orkin leader who rejected the cycles of violence and subjugation that defined Orkin existence on the mainland. The settlement stands as living proof that the Orkin, when freed from the pressures of persecution and scarcity, are capable of building something gentle.

## Government

Vorok-Varr is governed by the **Council of Listeners** -- elders chosen for their wisdom, empathy, and ability to hear all sides of a dispute before speaking. The name honors the settlement's founder and reflects the community's core value: that true strength lies in understanding, not in force.

Decisions are reached through patient deliberation. There is no hierarchy of command; the Council's authority rests on the trust of the community rather than on law or tradition. This governance model stands in stark contrast to the Varr (Clan) structure and warlord hierarchies that dominate mainland [[Orkin]] societies.

## History

Vorok the Listener led a band of [[Orkin]] across the sea to [[Aerin'r']] long before the [[Mirennian League|Mirennians]] arrived. The exact date is debated, but the community was well established by the time a Mirennian expedition discovered [[Aerin'r']] in SA:761. The captains, weary of the corruption of the old world, swore a **Pact of Non-Interference** with the settlement.

Vorok-Varr has endured for centuries by remaining small, self-sufficient, and aligned with its neighbors. The community lives alongside [[Sael'anir]] Elves and [[Waldkyn]] of the [[Aerin're Kith]], participating in shared cultural events like the **Star-Tide Festival** (first held SA:1890 at [[Lanthir Haven]]).

When a [[Varnathi]] timber syndicate attempted to exploit [[Aerin'r']] in SA:1845, the continent's diverse peoples -- [[Sael'anir]] wind-shapers, [[Waldkyn]] druids, and the Orkin of Vorok-Varr -- defended their home through non-violent resistance, bankrupting the expedition without drawing a single sword.

## Character

Vorok-Varr is a modest settlement of earthen longhouses, communal gardens, and open-air gathering circles. The community practices subsistence farming, herbal medicine, and oral storytelling. Children are taught that every voice deserves to be heard and that patience is the sharpest weapon. Visitors from the mainland often find it disorienting -- a place where [[Orkin]] greet strangers with food rather than steel.
