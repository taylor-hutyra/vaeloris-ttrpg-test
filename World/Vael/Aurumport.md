# Aurumport

Aurumport is a major port city on [[Vael]]'s eastern coast, a key member of the [[Mirennian League]]. The city is notable for its significant [[Orkin]] population, its Halfling [[Wayfinder]] networks, and its role as the site of the [[Convention of Accord]] that ended the Second Age.

## History

Aurumport grew from one of the earliest Mirennian trade posts, established during the Rise of the Merchant Houses in SA:275. Its name reflects the wealth that flowed through its docks -- gold from the interior, trade goods from across the seas, and the profits of a mercantile culture that valued coin above all else.

The city's character shifted with the [[Labor Integration Act]] of SA:2130, which brought Orkin workers into human cities. Aurumport's [[Orkin Quarter]] developed into one of the more established urban Orkin communities, with its own internal governance through clan elders, the [[Butchers' Guild]], and the [[Masons' Guild]].

## The Convention of Accord

In SA:3280, the [[Magocracy]] summoned all world leaders to Aurumport for the [[Convention of Accord]] -- the treaty that codified the status quo into permanent international law. The [[Laws of Accord]] fixed national borders, declared [[Aerin'r']] a Sanctuary of Nature, retained Magocracy jurisdiction over magical crimes, and guaranteed free non-magical air travel. The signing marked the official transition from the Second Age to the Third Age.

## Culture

Aurumport embodies the Mirennian ethos: wealth determines status, fortunes rise and fall with the tide, and every harbor forgets the last storm. The [[Spicers' Guild]] -- a Halfling [[Wayfinder]] network that is ostensibly a legitimate merchant guild but in reality a vast intelligence network and mutual aid society -- operates prominently here, making the city a hub for information brokering and discreet services.

## Present Day

Aurumport continues to serve as one of the League's most important commercial hubs. The diverse population of humans, Orkin, and Halfling Wayfinders creates a cosmopolitan, if occasionally volatile, social landscape. Its historical role as the birthplace of the Laws of Accord gives it symbolic weight in international diplomacy.
