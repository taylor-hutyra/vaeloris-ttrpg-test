# Hannan'r'

Hannan'r' ("Spirit-Haven" in Elvish) is the sacred homeland forest of the [[Cirhannim]] Elves, concealed from the mortal world behind [[The Great Weaving]] -- a permanent illusionary barrier of impenetrable mist. It is the most ancient continuously inhabited settlement on [[Vael]] and the spiritual heart of Elven civilization.

## Geography

To outsiders, Hannan'r' appears as an impassable bank of mist. Only those carrying a specific harmonic token can see the path through. Beyond the barrier lies an ancient, magically preserved old-growth forest of towering trees, luminous glades, and living architecture shaped over millennia. [[Mist-Lanterns]] -- crystalline beacons placed along the border -- project and anchor the Great Weaving.

Beneath the great-trees, hidden [[Gnomish Hollows]] house a small but vital population of [[Rock Gnomes]] who have maintained a symbiotic relationship with the Elves since SA:89, when [[Tinbrel Quillroot]] wove bronze lattice-work into the roots to stabilize the forest's magical defenses against iron ore interference.

## Government

Hannan'r' is a theocratic monarchy. The hereditary ruler of the [[Verdant Throne]], currently [[Queen Elara III]], holds immense ceremonial power. True authority rests with the [[Talanvar' Council]], composed of the eldest mystics from the three great houses: [[House Valerion]] (isolationists), [[House Silvanus]] (cautious reformists), and [[House Lyrandor]] (pragmatic wardens). The Council can veto any royal decree by declaring it a "Song-Wrong."

## The Great Weaving

Completed in SA:55 by [[Queen Lelyariel]] and the Mages of the Sun-Leaf Conclave, the Great Weaving draws magic from moonlight to sustain a month-long ritual effect that has persisted for millennia. The barrier effectively erased Hannan'r' from mortal maps, beginning the "Long Silence" between Elves and the outside world.

## Culture

Cirhannim society is stratified by proximity to the "original harmony": the Mystics (rulers), the [[Silent Sentinels]] (warriors), the Lore-Weavers (artisans), and the Green-Tenders (commoners). Their speech is archaic and highly poetic, and their lives revolve around the [[Talanvar']] -- the Seasonal Harmonies. They view change as corruption and outside influence as dissonance.

## Present Tensions

[[House Valerion]] dominates the Talanvar' Council with staunch isolationism. However, [[House Silvanus]] secretly believes the forest's perfect isolation has become a slow decay, and they maintain forbidden contact with scholars in [[M'ren'r']].
