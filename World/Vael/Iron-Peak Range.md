# Iron-Peak Range

The Iron-Peak Range is a treacherous mountain chain running north-to-south across eastern [[Vael]], forming a natural wall between the [[Varnathi Highlands]] and the [[Coastal Regions]]. This geographic barrier was the single most important factor in the cultural divergence between the Varnathi and Mirennian human peoples.

## Geography

The range is characterized by steep, jagged peaks and narrow, dangerous passes. The iron ore deposits that give the mountains their name made them valuable to early prospectors, but the terrain proved too hostile for permanent settlement. High-altitude winds and heavy snowfall render many passes impassable for months at a time.

## Historical Significance

When the human tribes scattered after the [[Great Withdrawal]] in SA:1, those who could not cross the Iron-Peak Range turned to the sea for survival. This single geographic fact created the fundamental divide between "Highlander" (Varnathi) and "Coaster" (Mirennian) human cultures that persists to the present day.

The Varnathi Stone-Road Projects of SA:110 improved passage through the range, and by SA:440 the Empire had mustered enough engineering capability to march an army through to found [[Port Farrick]]. Nevertheless, the range remained a logistical challenge that limited the Empire's ability to project power onto the coast, contributing to the centuries-long "Cold War" between land and sea.

## Strategic Value

Control of the mountain passes has been a persistent strategic concern. During the Salt War (SA:654-665), the Codex of the Road (SA:425) routes through the range became critical military supply lines. The range continues to function as a natural buffer that prevents either the [[Varnathi Imperial Democracy]] or the [[Mirennian League]] from easily dominating the other.
