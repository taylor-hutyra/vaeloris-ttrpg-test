# Kharnzarak

Kharnzarak is the great Dwarven mountain kingdom within the [[Central Mountains]] of [[Vael]], home to the orthodox [[N'm-Khazal'n]] ("The Stone-Sworn"). Once the most powerful subterranean civilization in the world, it was sealed behind the Great Adamant Gates in SA:1400 by the paranoid [[High King Thorgar VI]] and has existed in near-total isolation ever since.

## Founding

The Dwarven clans began reclaiming their ancestral halls from [[Skitter-Gloom]] infestations in SA:12, fighting a brutal room-by-room tunnel war in total darkness. The rediscovery of the [[First Forge]] was a pivotal moment. In SA:91, Thane [[Korin Barazkor]] of the [[Iron-Hand]] clan was declared the first High King, achieving unity by controlling the supply of "Star-Metal" (meteorite iron). The [[Crown of the Mountain]] -- a geometric crown forged from seven metals representing the founding clans, said to heat up if the wearer speaks a lie -- symbolized the new order.

## The Caste System

Kharnzarak's society is rigidly hierarchical, governed by ancient laws carved into the [[Stone-Lays]]. Clan and craft are inextricably linked, organized into High Clans, Guild Clans, and Gatestone Clans. The highest virtue is [[Thral'k]] (Endurance) -- the ability to maintain cultural and structural integrity against the pressures of time and change.

## History of Exile

Kharnzarak's rigid perfectionism produced two waves of exile. The [[Thulkor'n]] ("The Broken-Crafted") were criminals and oath-breakers publicly stripped of clan names through the [[Ritual of Shattering]] (SA:365). The [[Elm-korin]] ("The Flame-Struck") were visionary reformists exiled in SA:690 for the heretical belief that mortal magic could achieve perfect order. Both groups enriched human civilization with Dwarven skill.

## The Sealing

The catastrophic Earthwake of SA:1200 -- a structural failure caused by centuries of architectural stagnation -- shattered major clan halls. The paranoid [[Thorgar VI]] blamed "surface taint," purged any Dwarf with outside contact, and in SA:1400 ordered the Great Adamant Gates sealed. The runes were hammered shut, and for centuries the kingdom was a tomb of silence.

## Present Day

Circa SA:3050, the N'm-Khazal'n allowed a single trade gate to open. They sell high-quality steel and gold but refuse to buy anything, fearing surface contamination. The influx of Dwarven steel fueled the industrial revolution elsewhere. Within, the kingdom remains deeply orthodox, suspicious of change, and haunted by the echoes of the Earthwake.
