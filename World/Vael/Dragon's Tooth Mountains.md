# Dragon's Tooth Mountains

The Dragon's Tooth Mountains are the northernmost peaks of [[Vael]], a range of towering, frost-bitten summits that once served as the territory of the Giants. Now abandoned, the mountains hold the ruins of cyclopean fortresses, the monastery of [[Bjalmund's Spire]], and untold treasures of Titan-Steel.

## Geography

The range sits at the extreme northern edge of Vael, with peaks reaching above the cloud line into perpetual ice. The valleys between the peaks are vast, high-altitude expanses once tended by the Giants and their massive herds of woolly beasts. The terrain is treacherous, with sheer cliffs, avalanche-prone slopes, and weather that can turn lethal without warning.

## The Giants

When a Dwarven mining expedition broke into a high-altitude valley in SA:75, they recorded the first official contact with the Giants. These colossal beings tended cyclopean ruins and ignored the smaller races unless provoked. The High King of [[Kharnzarak]] decreed a policy of "Wide Berth," marking Giant territory as forbidden. Scholars theorized the Giants were the "gardeners" of sanctuaries abandoned by the [[First Hands]].

In SA:1775, a [[Gearhaven]] survey team ascending the highest peak found the massive cyclopean fortress completely abandoned. Meals were left on tables; forges were cold but unweathered. No bodies were found. The Giants had simply ceased to exist on the material plane. The cause of their disappearance remains one of the great mysteries of Vaeloris.

## The Scavenger Rush

Following the Giants' disappearance, nations and adventurers scrambled to loot the strongholds for "Titan-Steel" and ancient artifacts. The rush brought temporary wealth but also danger, as the Giant ruins contained defenses and architectural hazards scaled for beings many times the size of mortals.

## Bjalmund's Legacy

The monastery of [[Bjalmund's Spire]] was established at the site where the minor deity [[Bjalmund]] the Shieldbearer sacrificed his physical avatar to defeat the [[Echo-Lord]] in SA:380. It remains a pilgrimage point for the [[Wardens of the Wild]] and a center of contemplative martial tradition.
