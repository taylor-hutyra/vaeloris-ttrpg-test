# Barren Hills

The Barren Hills are the harsh, arid badlands that serve as the ancestral homeland of the [[Varr-Darg]] Orkin. Pushed into this inhospitable territory by centuries of human and Dwarven expansion, the Orkin forged a culture of perpetual siege mentality, viewing all other races as existential threats.

## Geography

The Barren Hills are a broken landscape of rocky scrubland, dry ravines, and wind-scoured plateaus. Rainfall is scarce and the soil is poor, supporting only the hardiest of vegetation. What the land lacks in fertility it offers in mineral wealth -- iron and coal deposits that have drawn the covetous eyes of human empires for centuries. The terrain is ideal for guerrilla warfare, with countless caves, narrow defiles, and defensible high ground.

## History

The Orkin were not always denizens of the Barrens. Following the [[Orkin Schism]] of SA:42 and the codification of the [[Varr]] clan structure, growing human and Dwarven expansion pushed the Orkin from their ancestral hunting grounds. After the coordinated raids of the [[Season of Breaking]] (SA:125) failed to halt the expansion, the combined might of the [[Wardens of the Wild]] and Varnathi armies forced the Orkin into the Long March of SA:210.

The Barren Hills became their permanent, fortified homeland. Orkin culture shifted entirely to a "siege mentality." Emperor [[Caelius I]]'s March of Order (SA:410) shattered the disorganized warbands with steel legions, pushing the Orkin even further into the badlands and establishing [[Castellum]] on the conquered frontier.

The most devastating assault came during the War of the Barren Hills (SA:1210), when the Varnathi Republic used scorched earth tactics to seize iron and coal deposits, burning the Orkin's limited food sources. Thousands of desperate Orkin paid Mirennian smugglers to ferry them to Novaterra, reducing the mainland population to scattered guerrilla bands.

## The Orkin Shield

In SA:2450, during the Drakona War, the Varr-Darg earned unexpected recognition. When the Drakona launched a ground invasion through the Barren Hills to flank the Alliance, the Orkin refused to retreat. Using tunnel warfare and desperate tactics, they held the pass for a decade. The [[Allied Command]] was forced to acknowledge their contribution, sending supplies and advisors -- laying groundwork for future integration.

## Government

The Varr-Darg operate under a [[Diarchy of Strength and Cunning]]: the [[Gral-Kor]] (warlord, always a Gral/Orc) commands in war, while the [[Skorn-Kli]] (strategist, usually a Skorn/Goblin) chooses targets and manages resources. The dominant [[Bloodied Fist Clans]] favor reclamation through war, while the [[Whisper-Shamans]] counsel spiritual endurance.
