# Lothari Desert

The Lothari Desert is a vast arid expanse in the southern reaches of the mainland continent of [[Vael]], home to the semi-nomadic Lothari people and their enigmatic [[Silent Network]]. Known also as the Southern Sands or the Great Desert, this harsh landscape of shifting dunes, hidden oases, and ancient ruins has shaped a culture of endurance, mysticism, and deep memory.

The Lothari believe in "The Breathless Dream" — a sleeping god whose thoughts became reality — and their mystics practice ancient dream-rituals and celestial interpretation that predate the great Human empires. Their culture values memory, secrecy, and vision above all else, and their sparse, symbolic speech patterns reflect a people who find meaning in silence as readily as in words.

In SA:1300, the nomadic tribes united under the [[Desertborn Confederacy]], seizing control of the overland Silk Road and imposing the "Toll of Sand" on any trade moving between East and West by land. This made the Confederacy a wealthy, neutral buffer state — a role it has maintained into the Third Age. The desert's strategic position and the Lothari's fierce independence have kept even the [[Magocracy]] from exerting direct control over the region.

The [[Silent Network]], formally organized in SA:1350, operates from hidden vaults scattered throughout the desert. These archives preserve knowledge of the First Age, the Paroxysm, and countless other historical records that would otherwise have been lost to the secular revisionism of the expanding empires. The Lothari believe the "Age of Noise" will eventually collapse, and the world will need its memory back.
