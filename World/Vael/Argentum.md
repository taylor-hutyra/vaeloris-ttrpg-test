# Argentum

Argentum is a silver mining metropolis in the [[Central Mountains]] of [[Vael]], founded in SA:485. What began as a mining camp exploded into a city of stone and coin, becoming the financial and industrial heart of the Varnathi world -- and eventually a political rival to [[Vernath]] itself.

## Founding

The story of Argentum begins with the discovery of massive silver veins in the Central Mountains in SA:230. The [[Treaty of Coin]] between Vernath and [[Kharnzarak]] established mining rights and standardized the "Vernathi Silver Mark." But the city's true birth came in SA:485, when a [[Thulkorûn]] prospector named [[Grogor the Eye]] discovered the "Deep-Vein" -- a massive deposit of nearly pure silver. The Empire annexed the region immediately, and the mining camp became a metropolis overnight.

## The Silver Lords

Argentum's wealth created the "Silver Lords," a new class of merchant-nobility whose economic power eventually rivaled the Emperor's. Their influence was decisive in the Bloodless Coup of SA:581: when [[Titus II]] attempted to seize their assets, the Silver Lords of Argentum joined with the Governors of Castellum and Port Farrick to cut off resources. The Emperor abdicated, and the Republic was born.

## The Arcane Revolution

Argentum was the epicenter of the Arcane Revolution. In SA:1401, a [[Shadewalkers]]-funded archaeological team excavated the Whispering Tomb near the city, breaching a sealed vault of First Age texts. The lead scholar, [[Melek the Grey]], was exposed to raw arcane residue. Six years later, in SA:1407, he appeared in Argentum's central market, levitating the city's stone obelisk and transmuting lead into silver, proclaiming "The gods have left the keys in the lock." The age of mortal magic had begun.

## Culture

Argentum embodies ambition and industry. Its identity as "Finance and Industry -- the New Money" contrasts sharply with Vernath's "Philosophy and Law -- the Old Guard." The city's architecture is a blend of Dwarven-influenced stonework and the ostentatious displays of the Silver Lords. Mining drives the economy, but banking, minting, and trade have long surpassed raw extraction in importance.

## Present Day

Argentum remains a financial powerhouse within the [[Varnathi Imperial Democracy]], its merchant class wielding enormous influence in the Senate through the [[Consul of Coin]]. The city's pragmatic, profit-driven culture makes it a natural counterweight to Vernath's idealism and Castellum's militarism.
