# Gear-Hollow

Gear-Hollow is a mixed [[Halfling]]/[[Gnome]] settlement on [[Vael]], best known as the birthplace of the movable type printing press. The town embodies the unique "Burrows and Hollows" society created by the [[Declaration of Hollowkin Rights]] -- a civilization that baffled human tax collectors with its lack of hierarchy.

## History

Gear-Hollow grew from the integration of Halfling and Gnome communities following the historic [[Declaration of Hollowkin Rights]] in SA:980. When human expansion pushed into the fertile western river valleys, the Halfling clans settled the region while the Forest Gnomes, rather than fighting, revealed themselves. The resulting treaty established a shared society where Halfling practicality met Gnomish ingenuity.

## The Printing Press

In SA:1360, a Gnome tinkerer named [[Bimble Pockets]] invented the movable type printing press in Gear-Hollow. This seemingly modest invention changed the world. The explosion of literacy and pamphlets that followed threatened the monopoly of the [[Shadewalkers]] and artisan guilds, as the "Secrets of the Guilds" began to leak to the common public. Information, once the currency of the powerful, became accessible to anyone who could read.

## Culture

Gear-Hollow is a place where Halfling warmth meets Gnomish precision. The town's structures are a hybrid of cozy Halfling burrows and intricate Gnomish workshops, connected by tidy pathways lined with both flower gardens and mechanical curiosities. Governance follows the informal "sensible arrangement" model: when a decision affecting both peoples needs to be made, the Halfling family elders and the Gnomish artisans meet, and solutions are found that keep Halfling pantries full and Gnomish projects undisturbed.

## Economy

The settlement thrives on a combination of Halfling agriculture (grain, vegetables, ale) and Gnomish craftsmanship (clockwork, tools, printed materials). The printing press industry has made Gear-Hollow disproportionately influential for its small size, as pamphlets, books, and broadsides flow outward to every corner of the continent.

## Present Day

Gear-Hollow remains a peaceful, productive settlement that punches far above its weight through the power of the press. Its citizens prefer to be left alone by the "Big Scramble" of Tallfolk politics, but the information flowing from their printing houses ensures they are never truly irrelevant.
