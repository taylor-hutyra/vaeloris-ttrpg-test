# The Fens

The Fens are a vast swampland on the mainland of [[Vael]], home to the reclusive [[Murk-Tenders]] -- a culture of primarily [[Ribbet]] and [[Fungril]] [[Waldkyn]] who have adapted to a life of secrecy and subtle influence in the marshes.

## Geography

The Fens are a sprawling wetland of mist-shrouded bogs, slow-moving waterways, tangled root systems, and stagnant pools. Visibility is poor, the footing treacherous, and the air thick with moisture and the smell of peat. To outsiders, the swamp appears hostile and impassable. To the Murk-Tenders, it is a rich ecosystem of rare herbs, medicinal fungi, and alchemical ingredients found nowhere else on the continent.

## The Murk-Tenders

The Murk-Tenders are a highly superstitious culture of Waldkyn governed by a [[Shamanic Circle]] led by a single [[Bog-Seer]] -- a powerful mystic who divines the future by reading patterns in mist and marsh bubbles. They are deeply territorial and view all outsiders, including other Waldkyn, with distrust.

Despite their isolation, the Murk-Tenders possess ancient knowledge of herbal remedies and poisons unmatched elsewhere in Vaeloris. Those who earn their trust -- a rare feat -- gain access to cures and concoctions of extraordinary potency.

## History

The Ribbet and Fungril Waldkyn settled the Fens shortly after the Root-Taking of the Vales in SA:20, drawn to the swamp's rich ecosystem and isolation from the expanding settlements of the [[Single-Forms]]. When [[Caelius I]] launched the March of Order in SA:410, some Orkin warbands fled into the western marshes. The Murk-Tenders tolerated their presence only so long as they remained at the margins and respected the swamp.

## Strategic Significance

The Fens serve as a natural barrier on the western flank of the continent, discouraging military incursion from any direction. The Murk-Tenders' knowledge of poison and herbalism makes them dangerous enemies and invaluable, if reluctant, allies. Their Bog-Seers are rumored to possess genuine prophetic ability, though their cryptic utterances are notoriously difficult to interpret.
