# Whispering Vales

The Whispering Vales are the ancestral homeland of the [[Waldkyn]], a network of sheltered, heavily forested valleys on the mainland of [[Vael]]. Protected by ancient Song-Wards and governed by the [[Great Council of the Vales]], the region has remained a sovereign sanctuary of naturalism for over three thousand years.

## Geography

The Vales occupy a series of interconnected valleys rich with old-growth forest, moss-covered stone, and crystal-clear streams. The landscape is shaped not by axes and plows but by Waldkyn rapid-growth rituals, which formed living trees into habitations during the Great Planting of SA:20. Song-Wards -- magical defenses woven into the land itself -- repel unnatural creatures and discourage unwelcome visitors.

## History

After the [[Great Withdrawal]], the Waldkyn sought to heal the land scarred by the Paroxysm. In SA:20, they formally settled the Vales, rejecting stone cities in favor of living architecture grown from the forest itself. This marked their transition from nomadic war-bands to a sedentary, custodial society.

The Waldkyn played a crucial diplomatic role in SA:195, mediating the [[First Council of Races]] at the Stone-Circle of Oth, which led to the [[Pact of the Wardens]] and the creation of the [[Wardens of the Wild]]. Their reputation as neutral mediators persisted for centuries.

The first major cultural schism occurred in SA:925 when the [[Chartered Kith]] departed the Vales, drawn to the cosmopolitan success of [[M'ren'r']]. Those who remained became the [[Quiet Vales Kith]], traditionalists who view civilization as a Song-Wrong.

## Government

The Vales are governed by the [[Great Council of the Vales]], composed of eight Speakers representing each Waldkyn variant (Faerie, Faun, Firbolg, Fungril, Galapa, Katari, Ribbet, Simiah). Executive authority rests with the [[Triumvirate of Seasons]]: the Root-Speaker (past/tradition), the Trunk-Speaker (present/defense), and the Branch-Speaker (future/diplomacy).

## Present Tensions

Three factions vie for the Vales' future: the [[Stillwood Circle]] isolationists, the [[Flowing River School]] interventionists, and the militant [[Kyn-Clans]] who believe balance must be imposed by force. The Kyn-Clans' terrorist campaigns against Varnathi border towns in SA:2315 destabilized the region and drew unwanted attention to the Vales.
