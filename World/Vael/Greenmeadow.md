# Greenmeadow

Greenmeadow is the founding settlement of the [[Halfling]] people, established in SA:980 in the fertile western river valleys of [[Vael]]. Protected by the [[Treaty of the Hearth]], it enjoys permanent neutrality -- a status earned not through military might but through the devastating soft power of controlling the continent's food supply.

## Founding

Halfling clans, migrating away from the militarized [[Varnathi Republic]], settled the region they named Greenmeadow. Their arrival triggered the "Gnomish Unveiling" -- rather than fight, the [[Forest Gnomes]] who already inhabited the area revealed themselves. The historic [[Declaration of Hollowkin Rights]] established a shared society of "Burrows and Hollows," a community without hierarchy that baffled human administrators.

## The Green-Thumb Strike

In SA:1280, during the War of the Barren Hills, Varnathi legions trampled Greenmeadow's harvest fields to create supply roads. The Halflings did not fight back with weapons. They simply stopped exporting grain and ale. Within three months, the Imperial Army faced mutiny from starvation and plummeting morale. The Republic was forced to sign the humiliating [[Treaty of the Hearth]], granting Greenmeadow permanent neutrality and forbidding military boots on their soil.

This established the Halflings as a "Sleeping Giant" of logistics whom no army dares anger. The saying holds: harming a Halfling village became taboo even for ruthless generals.

## Government

Greenmeadow is governed by direct democracy through a [[Town Council]] of elected elders -- typically the heads of the most respected families or guild-masters. The Mayor serves a one-year term and is considered a burdensome civic duty, not a position of power. "Never trust a man who wants to be Mayor."

The major families include the [[Thistlewicks]] (traders), the [[Appledowns]] (farmers, deeply traditional), and the [[Rumblkins]] (miners, perpetually underappreciated).

## Culture

Halfling culture is rooted in the tangible: warmth of a hearth, fullness of a pantry, reliability of a neighbor. They have no grand creation myth and find the concept presumptuous. Their highest values are community, comfort, and common sense. A Halfling hero is the neighbor who organizes the bucket brigade, not the king who conquers a nation. Their epics are recipes, their sacred texts are town charters, and their greatest virtue is knowing when to mind one's own business.

## Present Day

Greenmeadow remains the agricultural heart of [[Vael]], its surpluses feeding cities and armies across the continent. The [[Hearthfolk Commonwealth]] -- the loose confederation of Halfling Burrows -- operates from here, its politics a tapestry of family ties, town charters, and the pragmatic understanding that a happy neighbor is a quiet neighbor.
