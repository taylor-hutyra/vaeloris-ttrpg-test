# Sunken Coast

The Sunken Coast is a fog-shrouded, crumbling stretch of coastline on [[Vael]], home to the [[Shadow-Singers]] -- a heretical and secretive cult of Elves descended from [[Aen'valar]] who became obsessed with the "perfect, logical rhythm" of [[Elaru'n]], the Fifth Tone.

## Geography

The Sunken Coast is a desolate, eroding shoreline where the land appears to be slowly surrendering to the sea. Fog blankets the coast almost perpetually, muffling sound and distorting perception. Half-submerged ruins of unknown origin dot the shallows, and the tide pools glow with an unnatural bioluminescence. The atmosphere is one of profound, unsettling silence -- fitting for a people who worship the music of absence.

## The Shadow-Singers

The Shadow-Singers are a coven of Elves who believe the other [[Lainor'el|Four Harmonies]] are chaotic and sentimental, and that true power lies in mastering the "music of silence." They practice forbidden magic that manipulates order, time, and nullity -- disciplines that other Elven cultures consider an abomination.

They are governed by a [[Matriarch of Silence]] who claims to hear the whispers of the erased god ([[Kael'Zorai]], known to the Elves as [[Elaru'n]]). Their knowledge of the Fifth Tone's nature is terrifyingly extensive, making them a potential source of invaluable information -- or a devastating weapon in the wrong hands.

## Political Significance

The Shadow-Singers are shunned by every major Elven faction. The [[Cirhannim]] consider them the ultimate Song-Wrong. The [[Aen'valar]] view them as a cautionary tale of intellectual hubris taken to its darkest conclusion. Yet their deep understanding of [[Kael'Zorai]]'s resonance patterns means they could be manipulated into becoming reluctant allies against his returning influence -- or devoted servants to his cause.

Their isolation on the Sunken Coast is as much self-imposed as enforced. They prefer the margins of the world, where the Harmonies are thinnest and the silence loudest.
