# Vol

Vol is a volcanic island in the eastern seas of [[Vaeloris]], a territory of the [[Mirennian League]] that has been both a strategic outpost and a source of catastrophic disaster. The island is dominated by [[Mount Cinder]], whose eruption in 635 S.A. reshaped the geopolitics of the entire world.

## Geography

The island rises steeply from the ocean, its slopes carved by ancient lava flows into terraced ridges of black basalt and obsidian. [[Mount Cinder]] dominates the northern half, its caldera still venting sulfurous steam into the heavy air. The southern coast is more hospitable, where the original Mirennian settlement clings to sheltered coves between cliffs of volcanic glass. Hot springs and geothermal vents dot the landscape, providing natural heating and, in later ages, a source of industrial energy.

The soil, enriched by centuries of volcanic ash, supports lush subtropical vegetation where it has not been scorched bare. Rare alchemical reagents grow in the mineral-rich fumarole fields, drawing scholars and merchants willing to brave the island's unpredictable temper.

## The Sundering

In 635 S.A., [[Mount Cinder]] erupted with a force that shook the foundations of civilization. The explosion was not natural — later scholars theorize that a Mirennian [[Wind-Shaper]] attempted to tap into the geothermal vents to enhance their elemental communion and failed catastrophically. The consequences were devastating:

- **The Gloom Famine**: Volcanic ash choked the skies across [[Vael]] for three years, blocking sunlight and destroying harvests. The famine that followed killed tens of thousands across every nation on the mainland.
- **The Tsunami of Tears**: Massive waves radiating from the eruption destroyed forty percent of the [[Mirennian League]]'s fleet and harbor infrastructure, crippling their naval supremacy and leaving them vulnerable to the [[Varnathi]] Republic.
- **The Salt War**: The resulting food scarcity made salt — essential for preservation — more valuable than silver, directly triggering the devastating [[Salt War]] of 654 S.A.

## Modern Vol

The island was gradually resettled as the volcano calmed, though [[Mount Cinder]] has never fully gone dormant. The Mirennians maintain a garrison and a small port, and the island serves as a waypoint for ships crossing the eastern seas. Alchemists and geomancers study the volcanic phenomena, and the [[Elm-korin]] Dwarves have expressed interest in harnessing the geothermal energy for technomantic applications.

Vol stands as a reminder of the dangers of tampering with forces beyond mortal comprehension — a lesson the world has learned and forgotten many times over.
