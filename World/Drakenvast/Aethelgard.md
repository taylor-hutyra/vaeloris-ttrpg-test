# Aethelgard

**Aethelgard** was the primary Cloud City of the [[Drakona]] Empire -- a colossal floating city sustained above the peaks of [[Drakenvast]] by ancient aeromantic rituals. It served as the seat of [[Empress Valkiss]] and the supreme symbol of Drakona power, pride, and civilization. Only accessible by flight, Aethelgard embodied the fundamental divide in Drakona society: the winged aristocracy who lived above, and the flightless who labored below.

## Architecture

Aethelgard floated in the permanent cloud layer above the volcanic highlands of [[Drakenvast]], sustained by continuous, localized rituals of Air Magic. Its architecture was a testament to Drakona engineering and artistry -- vast platforms of Sky-Stone connected by aerial bridges, monumental basalt towers adorned with flame-lit carvings, and sweeping galleries open to the wind. The city contained nurseries, schools, temples of the [[Eternal Flame]], military academies, and the imperial palace complex.

The requirement of flight for access was not merely practical but deeply ideological. It ensured that only full-winged [[Drakona]] -- the aristocratic bloodlines -- could reach the seat of power. Flightless Drakona, regardless of talent or achievement, were physically excluded from the heart of their own civilization. This architectural bigotry fueled generations of resentment that eventually erupted in the **[[Broken Wing Insurgency]]**.

## The Fall

In SA:2680, the [[Allied Command]] authorized **Operation Icarus** -- a suicide reconnaissance mission. An elite team, disguised with high-level illusions, infiltrated Aethelgard. They discovered that the Cloud Cities were held aloft by a continuous aeromantic ritual, and they returned with the knowledge to destroy them. They also returned with the knowledge that Aethelgard held civilians, schools, and nurseries -- making the coming strike an act of genocide as well as military necessity.

On the winter solstice of SA:2688, the [[Earth-Anchor Ritual]] was cast. The metaphysical hook violently grounded the concept of "Flight" within the Drakona's aeromantic matrix. Aethelgard and the other Cloud Cities plummeted. Millions of [[Drakona]] -- soldiers, civilians, children -- died in the impact.

## Legacy

The ruins of Aethelgard were designated as part of the **[[District of Scales]]** by the [[Magocracy]] in SA:2725 -- a walled reservation where surviving Drakona were confined and used as living batteries to power anti-Rot wards. The memory of Aethelgard's fall -- "the day the sky itself was murdered" -- remains the central trauma of Drakona identity, cited by the [[Khor'drak]] as justification for militarism, by the [[Zhel'nov]] as a reason for reform, and by the [[Broken Wing Insurgency|Broken Wing]] as proof that their people's rulers abandoned them long before the enemy struck.
