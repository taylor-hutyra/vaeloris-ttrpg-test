# Drakenvast

Drakenvast is the ancestral homeland of the [[Drakona]], a vast expanse of volcanic highlands, jagged mountain ranges, and geothermal valleys in the far north of [[Vaeloris]]. For millennia it was hidden above the clouds, its people ruling from floating cities sustained by ancient aeromancy. Since the catastrophe of the Skyfall in 2688 S.A., it is a land of ruins and bitter endurance — the grounded heart of a once-skyborne empire.

## Geography

The terrain of Drakenvast is brutally inhospitable to non-Drakona life. Active volcanoes punctuate a landscape of basalt plateaus, sulfurous hot springs, and glacial valleys scoured by winds that never cease. The geothermal energy that powers the region's few remaining settlements also makes the land unstable — earthquakes are common, and new vents open without warning.

Before the Skyfall, the surface of Drakenvast was sparsely inhabited. The Drakona civilization existed primarily in the Cloud Cities — massive floating fortresses sustained by continuous aeromantic ritual — that drifted above the volcanic highlands. The largest and most magnificent of these was [[Aethelgard]], the seat of the Drakona Empress, which housed nurseries, schools, libraries, and the great command chambers of the aerial armada.

## The Vozhdate

The Drakona are governed by the [[Vozhdate]], a ruling council that blends military hierarchy with a deep reverence for ancestral tradition. The culture of Drakenvast draws from values of discipline, endurance, and collective sacrifice — every Drakona is born into service, and the concept of individual ambition divorced from the needs of the people is considered a profound moral failure. The Vozhdate's authority was absolute during the age of the Cloud Cities; since the Skyfall, it has become the organizing principle of survival.

## The Cloud Cities

The floating fortresses of the Drakona were among the greatest achievements in the history of [[Vaeloris]]. Held aloft by continuous, localized rituals of Air Magic — aeromancy passed down through generations of Drakona sky-priests — the Cloud Cities were invisible from below, hidden by their own altitude and by ancient concealment wards. From these bastions, the Drakona observed the surface world for centuries, gathering intelligence and resources through proxies like the [[Pirate Confederacy]] before launching their invasion in 2341 S.A.

[[Aethelgard]], the capital, was infiltrated during Operation Icarus in 2680 S.A. The Allied reconnaissance team discovered not a fortress of monsters, but a civilization — complete with nurseries, academies, and civilian districts. This knowledge haunted the [[Allied Command]] when they authorized the Earth-Anchor Ritual that brought the cities crashing down.

## The Skyfall and After

The Earth-Anchor Ritual of 2688 S.A. was the most devastating single act of magical warfare in the history of the world. It seized the concept of "flight" within the Drakona aeromancy matrix and violently grounded it. The Cloud Cities — including [[Aethelgard]] and the fortress of [[Drakograd]] — plummeted from the sky. Millions of Drakona died in the impacts. The craters where the cities fell became zones of thinned reality where the [[Grey Rot]] would later fester.

The survivors crawled from the wreckage, stripped of flight and dignity. The Treaty of the Broken Peaks in 2700 S.A. dissolved the Drakona Empire and confined its people to the "District of Scales" in the grounded ruins of their former capital. The [[Magocracy]] classified them as "Wards of the State" and used them as living batteries to power anti-Rot wards, draining their life force in service of the very powers that destroyed their civilization.

## The Broken Wing

Resistance never died in Drakenvast. The [[Broken Wing]] insurgency launched decades of magical terrorism against the [[Magocracy]] — bombing Mana-Pylons and assassinating Magisters to protest the confinement and exploitation of the Drakona people. These attacks were used by the Magocracy to justify further repression, creating a cycle of violence and subjugation that continues into the Third Age.

The Drakona remember what was taken from them. In the volcanic highlands of Drakenvast, the old songs are still sung in secret — songs of the sky, of [[Aethelgard]]'s spires catching the sunrise above the clouds, of a time when the Drakona were the lords of the air. The Vozhdate bides its time. The embers of empire are not yet cold.
