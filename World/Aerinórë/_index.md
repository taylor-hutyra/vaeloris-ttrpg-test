# Aerinórë

Aerinórë is the world's last unspoiled continent — a pristine expanse of ancient forests, crystal rivers, and untouched coastline lying to the north of [[Vael]]. It is a place where the land itself seems to resist the intrusion of civilization, where fog swallows unwelcome visitors and forests dismantle logging camps overnight. To the peoples of the Old World, it is a myth whispered by sailors: a paradise that cannot be mapped, a sanctuary that cannot be conquered.

## Geography

The continent is smaller than [[Vael]] or [[Novaterra]], its coastline wreathed in perpetual mist generated by the wind-shaping of [[Sael'anir]] guardians. Inland, towering old-growth forests cover rolling hills and gentle river valleys. There are no cities in the conventional sense — no walls, no paved roads, no smokestacks. The landscape is dotted with [[Sael'anir]] camps that follow the star-paths, [[Vorok-Varr]] settlements built into hillsides and caves, and [[Waldkyn]] communities woven into the living canopy.

## The Peoples

Aerinórë is home to those who chose a different path:

- **[[Sael'anir]]** — The nomadic Elves who left [[Hannanórë]] in 80 S.A., believing the "Song of the World" could not be heard from behind a wall. Led by the Star-Gazer [[Ilphara]], they mapped the stars and winds, and their descendants now serve as the continent's invisible guardians, redirecting fog and weather to confuse intruders.
- **[[Vorok-Varr]]** — Peaceful [[Orkin]] who rejected the endless cycle of war and exile. They live simply, tending the land rather than fighting over it, their settlements a living rebuke to the prejudice of the Old World.
- **[[Waldkyn]]** — Communities of various Waldkyn variants who maintain the continent's ecological balance, their nature-magic flowing in concert with the rhythms of the forest.

## The Silent Repulsion

Aerinórë does not need armies. When a [[Varnathi]] timber syndicate backed by mercenaries attempted to clear-cut the coastal forests in 1845 S.A., the continent defended itself. [[Sael'anir]] wind-shapers redirected the fog to trap the loggers in a labyrinth of mist, while [[Waldkyn]] druids accelerated plant growth to dismantle camps and entangle equipment. The expedition went bankrupt without a single blade being drawn. This event cemented Aerinórë's reputation as a "haunted" land in the popular imagination — a useful fiction the inhabitants do nothing to dispel.

## The Star-Tide Festival

Every year since 1890 S.A., the diverse peoples of Aerinórë gather at [[Lanthir Haven]] for the Star-Tide Festival — a celebration of music, art, and shared existence that transcends racial boundaries. The art and music produced here spread via [[Mirennian League|Mirennian]] traders, subtly influencing the youth of the mainland to question their rigid, segregated societies. The Festival is perhaps the strongest argument that different peoples can live together without conquest or hierarchy.

## Protected Status

The [[Convention of Accord]] of 3280 S.A. formally declared Aerinórë a "Sanctuary of Nature," forbidding industrial development or military presence. This legal protection merely codified what the continent's inhabitants had enforced for millennia through subtler means. The [[Magocracy]] patrols the surrounding waters, though whether they do so to protect the sanctuary or to prevent others from learning the secrets of peaceful coexistence remains a matter of cynical debate.
