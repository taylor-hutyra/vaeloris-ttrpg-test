# Deep-Vein Silver

Deep-Vein Silver is a massive deposit of nearly pure silver discovered in SA:485 in the [[Central Mountains]] by the [[Thulkor'an]] prospector [[Grogor the Eye]]. Its discovery triggered the Silver Boom that reshaped the economy and politics of the Second Age, birthing the city of [[Argentum]] and establishing silver as the standard currency of [[Vaeloris]].

## Discovery

The Silver Boom began when Grogor the Eye -- a [[Thulkor'an]] Dwarf, exiled from [[Kharnzarak]] and working as an itinerant prospector in the Central Mountains -- struck an enormous vein of silver ore of extraordinary purity. Unlike typical silver deposits, which require extensive smelting and refinement, the Deep-Vein silver was nearly pure in its raw state, reducing processing costs dramatically.

The [[Varnathi Empire]] annexed the region immediately, recognizing the strategic and economic potential of the find.

## The Treaty of Coin

The Central Mountains lay in territory nominally claimed by the Dwarves of [[Kharnzarak]] but left unoccupied. The discovery triggered a tense standoff between the Empire and the Dwarven High King. The resulting [[Treaty of Coin]] established a pragmatic compromise: Humans could mine the silver, but Dwarves -- with their superior metallurgical expertise -- would refine it and take a percentage.

The Treaty also standardized the weight and purity of the "Vernathi Silver Mark," creating the first reliable currency to replace the barter system in the region. This standardization facilitated trade across cultural and national boundaries, accelerating the economic integration of the continent.

## Argentum

The mining camp that sprang up around the Deep-Vein exploded into a metropolis within a generation. [[Argentum]] -- named for the silver that was its lifeblood -- became a city of stone and coin, attracting prospectors, merchants, artisans, and opportunists from across the continent.

The city gave rise to the "Silver Lords," a new class of merchant-nobility whose wealth rivaled and eventually surpassed that of the traditional aristocracy. Their growing power became a destabilizing force in Varnathi politics, contributing to the tensions that would eventually topple the monarchy in the Bloodless Coup of SA:581.

## Properties

Deep-Vein Silver is prized for its exceptional purity and workability:

- **Coinage**: The standard for reliable currency across the continent
- **Jewelry and art**: Favored by silversmiths for its luster and malleability
- **Magical applications**: High-purity silver is an effective conductor of certain types of arcane energy, making it valuable for warding inscriptions and enchanted tools
- **Trade standard**: Even after the rise of [[Mana-Crystals]] as an alternative currency, silver remains the baseline unit of value in most economies

## Legacy

The Deep-Vein has been mined continuously for over two thousand years, and though the richest veins are long exhausted, secondary deposits and deeper shafts continue to produce. [[Argentum]] endures as a financial capital, its economy having long since diversified beyond mining into banking, commodity trading, and arcane industry. The city's motto -- carved above the gates of its Exchange Hall -- reads: "From the stone, wealth. From wealth, power. From power, duty."

The story of [[Grogor the Eye]] is told differently depending on the audience. To the Varnathi, he is a fortunate prospector whose discovery enriched the Empire. To the Thulkor'an, he is a reminder that even the exiled and broken can change the world.
