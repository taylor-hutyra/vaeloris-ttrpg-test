# Mana-Crystals

Mana-Crystals are naturally occurring or artificially grown crystals that store concentrated magical energy. They serve a dual role in the Third Age economy of [[Vaeloris]]: as fuel for arcane devices, [[Technomancy|technomantic]] systems, and magical infrastructure, and as a universally accepted medium of exchange -- a currency backed not by a government's promise, but by measurable magical energy.

## Formation

Mana-Crystals form naturally at locations where ambient magical energy concentrates to the point of physical crystallization. These sites include:

- **Ley-line intersections**: Points where underground channels of magical energy cross and pool
- **Geothermal vents**: Volcanic activity seems to catalyze mana crystallization
- **Ancient ritual sites**: Locations where powerful magic was performed repeatedly over centuries
- **Scars of Dissonance**: Paradoxically, the sealed wounds from [[The Paroxysm]] sometimes produce mana crystals as residual energy slowly solidifies

Natural crystals vary widely in quality. Common-grade crystals are cloudy, irregular, and hold modest energy charges. Rare "clear" crystals are more efficient conductors. The finest specimens -- flawless, deeply saturated gems -- are produced by the [[Crystal-Singers of Mórenórë]], who use harmonic resonance to grow perfect crystals in controlled environments.

## As Currency

The adoption of Mana-Crystals as currency emerged organically from the arcane economy of the Third Age. Unlike silver coins, whose value depends on the issuing authority, a Mana-Crystal's worth is intrinsic and verifiable: any mage can assess the energy content of a crystal with a simple diagnostic spell. This made crystals a trusted medium of exchange across national borders, political allegiances, and cultural divides.

Standard denominations are measured by energy content:

- **Spark**: A small, cloudy crystal sufficient to power a simple spell or light a lantern for a week
- **Glow**: A mid-grade crystal equivalent in value to several silver marks
- **Blaze**: A high-grade crystal worth a small fortune, used for major transactions
- **Singer-Grade**: Flawless crystals of exceptional purity, used only for critical infrastructure and effectively priceless

## As Fuel

Mana-Crystals are the energy source that makes the Third Age possible. They power:

- **[[Mana-Pylon Systems]]**: The arcane energy grid that lights cities and powers wards
- **[[Clank Servitors]]**: Autonomous constructs fueled by crystal cores
- **[[Arcane Conduits]]**: Refined crystals shaped into the components that channel magical energy
- **Military systems**: Arcane cannons, defensive wards, and battle-mage amplifiers
- **Communication**: Long-range messaging systems that transmit encoded pulses through crystal relays

## Stockpiling and Strategy

Control of Mana-Crystal deposits is a matter of strategic importance. The [[Magocracy]] regulates crystal mining on the mainland. [[Gearhaven]] maintains strategic reserves and invests in synthetic crystal growth. The [[Coalition of Mages]], in the lead-up to the [[Drakona]] conflicts, secretly stockpiled crystals to prepare for war.

The [[Shadewalkers]] control the black market for unregulated crystals, and [[Ashfall]] has developed techniques for extracting trace mana from industrial waste -- a process that produces low-quality but abundant crystals at a fraction of the cost.

## The Fraying Question

[[Archmage Elaruniel]]'s [[The Frayed Weave]] raised alarming questions about the sustainability of crystal harvesting. If natural crystal formation depends on ambient mana concentration, and civilization is draining that concentration faster than it regenerates, then the crystal supply is not renewable -- it is finite and shrinking. This theory remains controversial, but reports of declining crystal yields from the oldest mining sites have given it uncomfortable weight.
