# Rune-Boxes

Rune-Boxes are lead-lined containers developed in SA:1530 by the [[Wandering Kin]] — the loose confederation of exiled Dwarven caravans (Thulkorûn and Elm-korin) that roamed the mainland during the Arcane Revolution. Designed to hide magical artifacts from the detection methods employed by the Varnathi Republic's [[Legio Null]] Inquisitors, the Rune-Boxes became the essential tool of the magical underground.

During the Mage-Purge Wars (SA:1450-1700), the practice, study, or possession of arcane materials was punishable by death throughout the [[Varnathi Democracy|Varnathi Republic]]. The Wandering Kin, already serving as intermediaries and traders across the continent, recognized both the moral imperative and the immense profit potential in smuggling magical contraband. Their Dwarven craftsmanship was uniquely suited to the task — the lead lining blocked magical emanations, while the exterior was designed to pass as ordinary trade goods.

The Rune-Boxes transformed the Wandering Kin from itinerant merchants into the primary smugglers of magical contraband on the mainland. A sealed Rune-Box could carry anything from translated copies of Melek's forbidden texts to enchanted tools and alchemical components, all invisible to the "Cold-Iron" detection methods the Inquisitors relied upon. The technology represented a quiet but significant act of resistance against the Republic's persecution of magic users.

Despite the eventual legalization of magic under the Treaty of the Silver Quill (SA:1700), Rune-Boxes remained in use well into later ages — adapted for smuggling any cargo that powerful institutions preferred to keep out of circulation. Their legacy endures as a symbol of the Wandering Kin's role as a "State without Borders," loyal to their own code rather than the laws of any nation.
