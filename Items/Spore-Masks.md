# Spore-Masks

Spore-Masks are alchemical protective devices developed in SA:2710 by the Gnomes of the [[Mycelial Collective]] during the catastrophic outbreak of the Grey Rot that followed the Skyfall. While Human mages faltered — their conventional healing magic actually accelerating the Rot — the Gnomes discovered that the plague was a fungal-magical hybrid and applied their deep understanding of mycology to create a practical countermeasure.

The Spore-Masks function as protective filtration gear, preventing the wearer from inhaling the Grey Rot's spore-like magical particles. Combined with alchemical sprays that could slow the spread of the Rot on contact, the masks transformed the Gnomes into the frontline medics of the crisis — one of the few forces capable of entering infected zones without succumbing to the plague.

The significance of the Spore-Masks extended far beyond their medical utility. They proved that the [[Magocracy]]'s scorched-earth approach to containment — burning entire cities to prevent the Rot's spread — was not the only viable strategy. When the Thulkorûn Dwarves of Iron-Beach refused to let the Magocracy "cleanse" their city in SA:2780, it was Gnomish medics equipped with Spore-Masks and alchemical treatments who cured the outbreak, demonstrating that practical ingenuity could succeed where raw magical power had failed.

The development of the Spore-Masks elevated Gnomish prestige across Vaeloris and deepened the alliance between the Gnomes and the "Free Peoples" who opposed the Magocracy's monopoly on crisis response. Many Gnomes who worked with the Rot during this period began incorporating it into their study of the "Shared Pattern," sowing the seeds for the Integrator faction that would emerge in later generations.
