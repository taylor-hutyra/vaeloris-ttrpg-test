# Brimstone

Brimstone is a high-grade mineral fuel essential to the industrial economies of the Third Age, found primarily in the volcanic regions of [[Novaterra]]. Its exceptional energy density makes it the preferred fuel for technomantic furnaces, steam-turbine systems, and heavy industrial processes. Control of Brimstone deposits has been a flashpoint for geopolitical conflict since the industrialization of the Second Age.

## Properties

Brimstone is a dense, sulfurous mineral compound extracted from volcanic deposits and geothermal vents. It burns at significantly higher temperatures than conventional coal or charcoal, and its combustion produces a sustained, even heat ideal for metalworking, glass-making, and the operation of steam-powered machinery.

Key characteristics include:

- **Energy density**: Approximately three times the caloric output of equivalent-weight charcoal
- **Burn duration**: A single block of refined Brimstone can sustain a forge for hours
- **Temperature ceiling**: Capable of reaching the extreme temperatures needed to work advanced alloys and power high-pressure steam systems
- **Byproducts**: Combustion produces sulfurous gases that require ventilation; prolonged exposure is harmful to living workers

## Industrial Importance

The rise of [[Technomancy]] and the industrialization of [[Novaterra]] created an insatiable demand for energy. While [[Mana-Crystals]] power arcane systems, the mechanical side of technomantic civilization -- the forges, the foundries, the steam turbines, the docks -- runs on Brimstone. [[Gearhaven]]'s vertical city of brass and steel consumes vast quantities, and the Technocracy's factories operate around the clock.

On the mainland, the [[Federation of City-States]] controls the most significant non-Novaterran deposits, giving the Federation a strategic leverage that its military alone could not provide.

## The Technocratic Blockade

In SA:1880, [[Gearhaven]]'s Brimstone supplies ran critically short. The Cog-Council ordered a naval blockade of the [[Federation of City-States]]' ports to force a favorable trade deal. In a stunning reversal of historical enmity, the [[Varnathi Empire]] -- traditionally the Federation's rival -- formed the [[Iron Pact]], a temporary military alliance with the Federation to break the blockade.

The resulting conflict lasted thirty years and reshaped the military doctrines of both the mainland and Novaterra. The Federation emerged with a modernized army that blended Imperial discipline with Federalist innovation. Gearhaven was forced to diversify its energy sources, accelerating research into alternative fuels and synthetic Mana-Crystal production.

## Supply and Trade

Brimstone deposits are unevenly distributed across Vaeloris:

- **Novaterra (volcanic regions)**: The largest and highest-quality deposits, controlled by [[Gearhaven]] and the [[Free Holds]]
- **Federation territories (mainland)**: Significant deposits in the southern mountains, a critical strategic asset
- **Ashfall**: The barony extracts Brimstone as a byproduct of its necro-industrial processes, though the quality is inconsistent

The Brimstone trade is one of the most heavily regulated and contested commodity markets in the world. Embargoes, tariffs, and blockades related to Brimstone supply have triggered multiple conflicts, and control of supply routes remains a central concern of every major power's foreign policy.

## Legacy

Brimstone represents the unglamorous foundation of Third Age civilization. While scholars debate the Theory of Arcane Scarcity and generals plan for mana-powered warfare, the forges and factories that produce their tools and weapons run on this sulfurous stone. Without Brimstone, the gears of the world would grind to a halt.
