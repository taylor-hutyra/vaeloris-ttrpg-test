# Aether-Glider

The Aether-Glider is a non-magical flying vessel that represents one of the most consequential inventions in the history of [[Vaeloris]]. Unveiled in SA:3134 by engineers in [[Gearhaven]] collaborating with Mirennian architects, the Aether-Glider achieved sustained flight using alchemically refined "Lift-Gas" and steam-powered propellers — entirely without the Aeromancy that had been banned by the [[Magocracy]]'s First Decree.

The invention exploited a critical legal loophole. The First Decree (SA:2866) specifically forbade "Flight Magic" — the study or use of Aeromancy, punishable by "Stilling" (lobotomy of the magical center of the brain). But the Aether-Glider used no magic whatsoever. The Magocracy, bound by its own rigid laws, could not legally ban the vessels without inciting a global revolt. Their attempt to impose a "Sky-Tax" on "Atmospheric Displacement" was met with united resistance from the [[Mirennian League]] and the Federation of City-States, and the Magocracy was forced to back down — the first time the regime blinked in the face of opposition.

The Aether-Glider triggered the "Sky-Rush" — an explosion of commercial and military air travel that reshaped global commerce and geopolitics. The Mirennian League poured its fortune into building massive fleets of airships, reclaiming their ancient title as masters of the sky. For the first time since the Skyfall (SA:2688) grounded every flying vessel on the continent, the skies were open again.

By the current era (TA:412), Aether-Glider technology has evolved into a diverse range of airships, from armored military cruisers to sleek commercial transports, forming the backbone of long-distance travel and trade across Vaeloris.
