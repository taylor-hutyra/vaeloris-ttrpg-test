# Star-Metal

Star-Metal is an exceptionally rare meteorite iron found in the upper peaks of the Dragon's Tooth mountain range, within the territory of [[Kharnzarak]]. Prized for its extraordinary hardness, durability, and unmatched capacity to hold magical enchantments, it is the most coveted metal in [[Vaeloris]] -- and its control has shaped the course of Dwarven history.

## Origins

Star-Metal is not of this world. It arrives in meteorites that strike the highest peaks of the Dragon's Tooth range, where the thin atmosphere and extreme altitude allow fragments to survive their descent relatively intact. The Dwarves believe these fragments are gifts from the Four Pillars of Craft -- remnants of the divine forging that shaped the world, still falling from the heavens like sparks from an eternal anvil.

The deposits are small, scattered, and found only at extreme elevations where conditions are lethal to all but the hardiest climbers. Mining Star-Metal is as much a feat of endurance as it is of craft, and the quantity recovered in any given year can often be measured in pounds rather than tons.

## Properties

Star-Metal possesses several qualities that set it apart from all terrestrial metals:

- **Hardness**: Significantly harder than the finest Dwarven steel, capable of holding an edge indefinitely
- **Durability**: Resistant to corrosion, heat, and physical stress far beyond conventional alloys
- **Mana affinity**: The crystalline microstructure of meteorite iron naturally aligns with arcane energy flows, allowing Star-Metal to hold enchantments with exceptional stability and duration
- **Weight**: Surprisingly light for its strength, approximately two-thirds the weight of equivalent steel
- **Distinctive appearance**: A subtle, dark luster with visible crystalline patterns (Widmanstatten lines) that mark it as otherworldly

## Working Star-Metal

Star-Metal is notoriously difficult to forge. Its melting point exceeds that of conventional forges, and it resists the shaping techniques that work on ordinary iron and steel. Only the most skilled Dwarven smiths -- masters of the ancient forge-techniques preserved in the Stone-Lays -- possess the knowledge to work it. The process requires specialized furnaces, rare flux compounds, and weeks of patient, incremental heating and hammering.

A single Star-Metal blade can take months to forge and represents a lifetime's worth of a master smith's skill. Such weapons are never sold; they are gifted, inherited, or earned through deeds of extraordinary merit.

## Political Significance

Star-Metal's rarity and value made it a decisive political tool. In SA:91, Thane [[Korin Barazkor]] of the Iron-Hand clan leveraged his control of the Star-Metal deposits in the upper peaks to unite the warring Dwarven clans. By controlling the supply of the most precious metal in the mountain, he compelled the other clans to acknowledge his authority, ending the inter-clan Tunnel Wars and establishing himself as the first High King of a unified Kharnzarak.

The [[Crown of the Mountain]] -- the regalia of the Dwarven High King -- is forged from seven distinct metals representing the seven founding clans, with Star-Metal forming the crown's central band. It is said to heat up if the wearer speaks a lie while sitting on the Adamant Throne.

## Scarcity

Star-Metal cannot be mined like conventional ore; it can only be gathered from impact sites. New meteorites fall rarely and unpredictably. The total quantity of Star-Metal in Dwarven vaults is estimated to be less than the weight of a single Deep-Hull ship. This extreme scarcity has made it effectively priceless -- a single ingot of Star-Metal is worth more than a prosperous farm, and a finished Star-Metal weapon is worth more than a small city.

The sealing of [[Kharnzarak]] in SA:1400 removed the world's only known source from circulation entirely, making existing Star-Metal artifacts among the most sought-after treasures in Vaeloris.
