# First Compact

The First Compact is the founding legal code of [[Vernath]], drafted by the philosopher-warrior [[Vardas the Elder]] in SA:28. Part constitution, part moral philosophy, and part military doctrine, it established the principles of duty, order, and stoicism that would define [[Varnathi Empire|Varnathi]] civilization for millennia. It remains the most influential legal document in the history of [[Vaeloris]].

## Creation

In the windswept northern highlands, seven warring human tribes struggled to survive in the aftermath of [[The Paroxysm]] and [[The Great Withdrawal]]. Tribal superstition held that the gods had abandoned humanity as punishment for sin. Vardas the Elder rejected this interpretation. He preached that the gods had left because mortals were ready to stand on their own -- that divine absence was not punishment, but a graduation.

Vardas united the seven tribes not through conquest alone, but through a revolutionary idea: that survival required collective obligation. He laid the foundation stone of [[Vernath]] on a high plateau, utilizing the ruins of a First Age fortification, and carved the First Compact into the wall of the city's central hall.

## Core Principles

The First Compact is built on three pillars:

### Duty
Every citizen must contribute to the defense and maintenance of the city-state. The Compact mandates specific forms of service: military training for the young, infrastructure maintenance for the able-bodied, counsel and education for the elderly. Idleness was classified as a crime against the state -- not out of cruelty, but from the pragmatic recognition that a frontier settlement could not afford passengers.

### Order
The Compact establishes a structured hierarchy of governance: a Council of Elders drawn from each of the seven founding tribes, a system of codified laws (rather than tribal custom), and standardized penalties for transgressions. Justice was to be public, consistent, and impersonal -- applied equally regardless of tribal affiliation.

### Stoicism
The Compact's philosophical core teaches that suffering is inevitable, but weakness is a choice. Citizens are expected to face hardship with discipline and resolve. The Varnathi saying "Stone breaks louder when ignored" -- a warning against neglecting problems -- originates from the Compact's preamble.

## The Amended Compact

When [[Caelius I]] unified the highland city-states and declared the birth of the [[Varnathi Empire]] in SA:381, he amended the First Compact to include loyalty to the Imperial Throne alongside loyalty to the state. This amendment transformed a civic document into an instrument of imperial authority, binding citizens not just to their community but to the Emperor personally.

The amendment was controversial. Purists in Vernath argued that Vardas's vision was civic, not monarchic -- that the Compact demanded service to the collective, not submission to an individual. This tension between the Compact's original intent and its imperial interpretation has fueled political debate in Varnathi society for over two thousand years.

## Influence

The First Compact shaped virtually every aspect of Varnathi culture:

- **Military**: The Compact's emphasis on duty and service created the disciplined legionary tradition that made the Empire a continental power
- **Architecture**: Vernath's grid-planned streets, fortified waystations, and elevated roads reflect the Compact's emphasis on order and infrastructure
- **Education**: The scholar-aristocrat class -- the philosopher-guardians of Varnathi society -- trace their mandate to the Compact's requirement for educated governance
- **Law**: The codified legal tradition, standardized weights and measures, and public justice system all descend from the Compact's principles

## Physical Form

The original First Compact is carved into a granite slab set into the wall of Vernath's Council Hall. Copies were engraved in every major Varnathi city, carved into mile-markers along the Imperial road network, and eventually printed and distributed after the invention of the [[Printing Press]]. Every Varnathi citizen is expected to have read the Compact by their sixteenth year.

## Legacy

The First Compact endures as the moral foundation of Varnathi identity -- even as the civilization it created has fractured, reformed, and fractured again. When the monarchy fell in the Bloodless Coup (SA:581), the Republic invoked the Compact's civic principles. When the Republic became a military hegemony, critics invoked the Compact's egalitarian intent. The document has been claimed by every faction of Varnathi politics, each reading their own values into Vardas's ancient words.

The Compact's most famous line -- "Stone breaks louder when ignored" -- has passed into common usage across Vaeloris, spoken by people who have never read the document and may never have heard of Vardas the Elder. It is perhaps the greatest compliment to his work: his words outlived their context and became universal truth.
