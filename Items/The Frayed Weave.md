# The Frayed Weave

*The Frayed Weave* is a theoretical treatise authored by [[Archmage Elaruniel]] of the [[Aen'valar]] Elves, published in SA:2715 from the library-observatory of [[Aethelas]]. It argues that the unchecked use of arcane magic -- particularly the [[Magocracy]]'s [[Mana-Pylon Systems|Mana-Pylon networks]] and industrial-scale wards -- is depleting the ambient magical field of [[Vaeloris]], a process Elaruniel called the "Arcane Fraying." The text is the foundational document for what the wider world would come to call the Theory of Arcane Scarcity.

## Context

Elaruniel was a celebrated member of the [[Aen'valar]] -- the progressive Elven faction that believed the "world-song" was an ongoing composition, not a finished work. As an Archmage, she possessed both the theoretical knowledge and the observational acuity to perceive patterns that others missed.

Her research began after the [[Skyfall]] -- a catastrophic event involving the [[Drakona]] that devastated much of the world. Haunted by her role in the conflict, Elaruniel withdrew to [[Aethelas]] and spent years analyzing the magical aftermath. What she found alarmed her: the Skyfall had not merely caused physical destruction. It had damaged the "immune system of the universe" -- the ambient mana field that sustained all magical life and practice.

## Central Argument

The Frayed Weave presents three core claims:

1. **The mana field is finite.** The ambient magical energy of Vaeloris is not an inexhaustible resource. It was deposited by the departed gods and regenerates slowly, if at all. Every spell cast, every ward maintained, every Mana-Pylon operated draws from this finite reservoir.

2. **Consumption exceeds regeneration.** The industrialization of magic -- particularly the continental Mana-Pylon networks and the Magocracy's extensive ward systems -- is draining the field faster than it can replenish. Elaruniel presented data showing declining mana concentrations at monitoring sites across the continent.

3. **The consequences are catastrophic.** If the Fraying continues unchecked, the mana field will eventually thin to the point of collapse. Magic will cease to function. Every technomantic system, every ward, every enchantment will fail simultaneously. Civilization, built on the assumption of abundant magic, will collapse with it.

## Reception

The [[Magocracy]] immediately denounced *The Frayed Weave* as alarmist propaganda. They banned its publication in human lands, suppressed Elaruniel's research, and pressured the [[Grand University]] to distance itself from her conclusions. The Magocracy's own mages argued that the mana field was self-sustaining and that Elaruniel's data reflected localized anomalies, not a systemic crisis.

Among the Aen'valar and the intellectual communities of [[Novaterra]], the response was very different. *The Frayed Weave* became required reading in the universities of [[Gearhaven]] and the underground academies that operated beyond the Magocracy's reach. It was called "the bible of the academic underground" -- a text that illuminated a truth the powerful did not want acknowledged.

## Legacy

In the decades following its publication, evidence has slowly accumulated in support of Elaruniel's theory. Reports of declining [[Mana-Crystals|Mana-Crystal]] yields from the oldest mining sites. Localized "dead zones" around heavily used Mana-Pylon networks where magic functions erratically or not at all. The increasing frequency of arcane system failures in the most developed regions of the mainland.

The Magocracy continues to dismiss these reports. But *The Frayed Weave* endures -- passed hand to hand, copied in secret, debated in whispered conversations in the back rooms of universities and taverns alike. It asks the question that no one in power wants to answer: what happens when the magic runs out?
