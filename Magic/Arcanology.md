# Arcanology

Arcanology is the formal study and practice of arcane magic -- the manipulation of the ambient mana field left behind by the departed gods. It is the dominant magical tradition of the Third Age, practiced across [[Vaeloris]] by trained mages, battle-casters, and academic scholars. Unlike the elder magics of the Elves or the elemental attunements of the Mirennians, Arcanology treats magic as a science: measurable, reproducible, and teachable.

## Discovery

For over a thousand years after [[The Great Withdrawal]], magic was the exclusive domain of gods, spirits, and the elder races. Humanity's relationship with the arcane began in SA:1401, when a Varnathi archaeological team excavated a pre-Paroxysm ruin near [[Argentum]] and breached a sealed vault containing texts from the First Age "Divine Workshop."

The lead scholar, Melek the Grey, was exposed to raw arcane residue. His mind was forcibly opened to the ambient field, and after six years of seclusion, he demonstrated his abilities publicly in Argentum's central market -- levitating the city's massive stone obelisk and transmuting lead into silver. His proclamation, "The gods have left the keys in the lock," marked the birth of mortal arcane practice.

## The Wild Years and the Purge

Melek's death three days later -- his brain burned out from channeling too much energy -- foreshadowed the dangers of untrained magic. The "Wild Years" (SA:1410) saw chaos as New Mages accidentally boiled harbors, set libraries on fire, and terrified the populace. The [[Varnathi Republic]] responded with the Decree of Prohibition (SA:1425) and the brutal [[Mage-Purge Wars]], driving mages into exile.

## Codification

The persecution ended only after the Battle of Crimson Field (SA:1695), where [[Gearhaven]]'s battle-mages annihilated a Republican army fifty times their number. The [[Treaty of the Silver Quill]] (SA:1700) legalized magic, and the [[Grand University]] was founded in [[Mórenórë]] in SA:1750 to standardize magical education and prevent another catastrophic "Wild Years."

Arcanology was organized into formal schools: Elemental Manipulation, Transmutation, Kinetics, Divination, Warding, and Enhancement. Students progressed through rigorous examinations, and the [[Magister's Council]] oversaw state-sanctioned practitioners in the former Republic.

## The Theory of Arcane Scarcity

By the late Second Age, the [[Aen'valar]] scholar [[Archmage Elaruniel]] published [[The Frayed Weave]] (SA:2715), arguing that the unchecked use of arcane magic was depleting the ambient mana field -- the very "immune system of the universe." She theorized that the [[Magocracy]]'s wards and industrial-scale magic were causing instability that would eventually lead to catastrophic failure.

The Magocracy denounced and suppressed her research, but her Theory of Arcane Scarcity became foundational text in the academic underground of [[Novaterra]] and among the [[Aen'valar]].

## Practice in the Third Age

In the modern era, Arcanology is widespread but unevenly distributed. The [[Magocracy]] controls magical education and licensing on the mainland. [[Gearhaven]] integrates arcane theory into [[Technomancy]]. The [[Federation of City-States]] trains battle-mages embedded in infantry squads. And independent practitioners -- hedge-wizards, itinerant enchanters, and rogue scholars -- operate in the margins of regulated society.

[[Mana-Crystals]] serve as both fuel and currency in arcane practice, their value reflecting the growing anxiety over magical resource depletion.

## The Fraying

The greatest danger of Arcanology remains the Fraying -- the catastrophic mental and physical breakdown caused by channeling more arcane energy than the mortal mind can sustain. Melek the Grey was its first victim. Countless others have followed. The Grand University's primary mandate, above all else, is to ensure that no student meets the same fate.
