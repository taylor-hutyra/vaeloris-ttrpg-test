# Wind-Shaping

Wind-Shaping is the signature elemental practice of the [[Mirennian League]], a ritualistic communion with the residual elemental essence of [[Gwynfaer]] (The Windweaver) that allows practitioners to command wind and weather. It is not considered true arcane magic in the academic sense, but rather a deep attunement to the divine remnants woven into the natural world after [[The Great Withdrawal]].

## Origins

When the gods withdrew from the mortal plane at the start of the Second Age, the elemental forces they once commanded did not vanish entirely. The Mirennians, coastal traders and poets who viewed the Four Makers as elemental muses, discovered that the wind still carried echoes of Gwynfaer's will. Early practitioners -- fisherfolk and sailors -- learned to listen to these echoes and, in time, to speak back.

The first recorded combat use of Wind-Shaping occurred in SA:520, during the Incident of the Burning Mast. An Imperial warship attempted to blockade a Mirennian port to enforce tariffs, and a Mirennian Sea-Whisperer summoned a localized gale that drove the ship onto the rocks. This demonstration shocked the [[Varnathi Empire]] and proved that the League could not be challenged on open water.

## The Guild of Tides

By SA:550, the [[Guild of Tides]] had codified the art into a formal discipline. Apprentices spend years learning to read the subtle shifts in pressure, temperature, and humidity that signal the presence of elemental residue. Masters can redirect storm fronts, becalm entire fleets, or conjure tailwinds that allow Mirennian ships to sail against prevailing currents.

The [[Sea-Whisperers' Guild]], a related order, focuses on the intersection of wind and wave, specializing in naval combat applications and deep-ocean navigation.

## Practice and Technique

Wind-Shaping is performed through a combination of rhythmic chanting, precise hand gestures, and the use of "wind-harps" -- stringed instruments mounted on ship masts that resonate with elemental frequencies. The practitioner enters a meditative state, attuning their breath to the rhythm of the surrounding air, and then guides the wind through force of will.

The art is deeply tied to Mirennian culture and its saying: "Sails full or none at all." Practitioners are respected as essential crew members, and no Mirennian trade vessel sails without at least one Wind-Shaper aboard.

## Limitations

Wind-Shaping cannot create wind from nothing; it amplifies and redirects existing currents. It is ineffective underground or in tightly enclosed spaces. Prolonged use causes physical exhaustion, and overextension has been known to kill practitioners -- the catastrophic eruption of Mount Cinder in SA:635 is theorized to have been caused by a Wind-Shaper who attempted to tap geothermal vents and lost control.

## Legacy

Wind-Shaping granted the [[Mirennian League]] absolute naval superiority throughout the Second Age, enabling them to dominate coastal trade, discover [[Novaterra]], and resist the military might of the [[Varnathi Empire]]. Even after the rise of true arcane magic in the Fourth Epoch, Wind-Shaping remained a valued and distinct tradition, its practitioners proudly separate from the academies and universities that trained conventional mages.
