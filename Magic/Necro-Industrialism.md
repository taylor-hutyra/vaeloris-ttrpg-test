# Necro-Industrialism

Necro-Industrialism is the fusion of necromantic magic with industrial processes, practiced exclusively in the sovereign barony of [[Ashfall]]. It is the most reviled magical discipline in [[Vaeloris]] -- a system that animates the dead as tireless factory workers and soldiers, powering an economy that never sleeps and an army that grows stronger with every casualty.

## Origins

The roots of Necro-Industrialism lie in the ethical gray zones that emerged during the rapid industrialization of the Third Age. As the [[Magocracy]] consolidated control over mainstream arcane practice, certain researchers pushed into forbidden territory. Necromancy -- the manipulation of death energy -- had been practiced in secret for millennia, but it had always been the province of individual practitioners: lone liches, rogue scholars, and desperate healers.

The innovators of [[Ashfall]] asked a different question: what if death could be industrialized?

## The Ashfall Secession

In SA:3100, the industrial barony of Ashfall declared independence from the [[Federation of City-States]]. When the [[Magocracy]] sent armies to crush the rebellion, Ashfall unveiled its terrible weapon: factories and armies powered by the undead. Forges burned day and night, tended by corpses that required neither food, nor rest, nor wages. Soldiers fell on the battlefield and rose again to fight for Ashfall.

The Magocracy invaded three times. Each time, the dead rose to fight again. A war of attrition against an enemy that replenished its losses from its own casualties was, as the Magisters reluctantly concluded, "mathematically impossible."

## The Ashfall Compact

In SA:3250, the Magocracy formally recognized Ashfall's sovereignty through the [[Ashfall Compact]]. The treaty was not born of respect but of pragmatic necessity. The Magocracy needed a source of "unethical" research that it could deny involvement with -- a dark laboratory to supply forbidden weapons for use against threats like the [[Rot]] and the [[Broken Wing]] insurgency.

Ashfall became the Gray Market of the world, supplying forbidden or unethical technology to any buyer with sufficient coin and discretion.

## Mechanics

Necro-Industrial processes rely on the harvesting of necromantic energy from death and decay. Corpses are prepared in "Animation Mills," where preserved remains are inscribed with binding runes and infused with a sustained flow of death magic. The resulting undead are not sentient; they are automata of flesh and bone, capable of following simple instructions and performing repetitive physical labor.

More advanced applications include:
- **Death-Forges**: Smelters fueled by the thermal energy released during magical decomposition
- **Bone-Looms**: Factories where undead workers produce textiles, ammunition, and processed goods around the clock
- **Risen Legions**: Military formations of undead soldiers, commanded by living necromantic officers

## Ethical Opposition

Every major power in Vaeloris officially condemns Necro-Industrialism. The [[Grand University]] classifies it as a violation of the "Principle of Willing Participation" -- the ethical foundation of arcane practice. Religious orders view it as a desecration of the dead. The [[Waldkyn]] call it a profound "Song-Wrong," the ultimate violation of natural balance.

Yet the products of Ashfall's factories flow quietly through back channels into every nation on the continent. The world condemns the practice in public and purchases its output in private -- a hypocrisy that defines the moral compromises of the Third Age.

## The Human Cost

Living workers in Ashfall suffer from prolonged exposure to necromantic energy: pallid skin, chronic fatigue, premature aging, and a condition known as "Gray-Lung" caused by inhaling particulate matter saturated with death magic. Ashfall's population is sustained not by natural growth but by a steady influx of the desperate, the criminal, and the exiled -- those with nowhere else to go.
