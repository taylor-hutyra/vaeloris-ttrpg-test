Act I — The Catalyst

Chase thriller — hunted, desperate, racing to escape • Sessions 1–8 • 0→150 XP

Lux Aeterna — Home

Sessions 1–2 0 XP

Hidden mage-refugee settlement of ~400 souls in desert foothills. Players are relic-hunters working for Tajsh Thale. Establish NPC bonds, run sidequests, build emotional investment.

"The Night the Lantern Was Full" — the settlement at its most alive. Old Cira says: "Don't forget this."
Tajsh Thale Warden Sabel Old Cira Marta Dustborn Hask Aldric Greypen Petra

The Mining Camp & Ruin of Sorath

Session 1

Tajsh sends the party to find a missing mining crew. They find the camp destroyed by Scorch-Wraiths and discover the Ulzammar Band — the first Crown piece — buried in First Age stone. Optional dungeon explores a Scar of Dissonance.

  • Combat: 3 Scorch-Wraiths
  • Discovery: The Band (Crown piece #1 — domain of Time)
  • Optional: Foreman Dast's journal reveals a hidden temple

The Temple Mission & Ink's Analysis

Sessions 2–3 ~75 XP

Return the Band to Tajsh. He brings in Ink (Shadewalker archivist) who identifies the artifact as pre-Paroxysm, divine-made. Optional mission to retrieve a book and resonance key from a hidden temple. The Band destabilizes Lux Aeterna's defenses.

Ink (Shadewalker)

The Architect Arrives

Session 3 Key Emotional Beat

A being of geometric light appears in the tunnels. Claims to be the last servant of the Four. Identifies the Band as a fragment of a world-ending instrument. Gives the party the Resonance Compass and a cosmic mission: gather the fragments and unmake them.

Addresses each PC personally with devastating emotional precision — calculated targeting disguised as compassion.

Red flags: geometric (not organic) light, no shadow, no reflection, plants go still nearby, speaks of "order" and "completion" — the language of Kael'Zorai.
The Architect (Echo-Lord in disguise)

Decision: Leave Lux Aeterna

The Band destabilizes the mirror-charm array. Legio Null is converging. Sabel's plan: the party takes the Band and flees north through the desert toward the mountains, drawing pursuit away while the settlement relocates. The players must leave everyone behind.

"The Last Morning" — ask each player: "Who do you find? What do you say?"

The Desert Chase

Sessions 4–5 ~150 XP

Three-leg flight overland north from Lux Aeterna toward the Dragon's Tooth Mountains. Soru (Lothari guide) leads through hidden paths. Commander Thalric's Sand-Riders pursue. Miravel Ashwine joins on the mountain approach.

  • Leg 1 — Foothills: Sand-Rider outriders parallel the party
  • Leg 2 — Salt Flats: Open terrain, sandstorm, civilians caught in the open
  • Leg 3 — Mountain Approach: Lothari waystation, standoff with Thalric, Miravel arrives
Soru Miravel Ashwine Commander Thalric

Act II — The Journey

Exploration & moral drama — breathing room to build relationships • Sessions 6–25 • 150→450 XP

Ascending the Mountains — Overland Scenes

1–2 Sessions travel

Several days climbing through the Dragon's Tooth foothills toward the Observatory. Miravel makes coffee at dawn. Mountain encounter table. Downtime and character bonding. The Compass hums contentedly.

Conversation seed: "What's the first thing you'll do when this is over?" Remember the answers. Return to them in Act III.

Dragon's Tooth Mountains — Starfall Peaks Observatory

Sessions 6–8 150→200 XP Adventurer

The party ascends to Stellion's Observatory — a First Age structure at the mountain summit. Vaelric Star-Eye (astronomer, 11 years alone) has been watching the stars shift. His instruments triangulate three points of stellar distortion — the Crown pieces.

  • Vaelric identifies: one signal north (Aerinórë — the Setting), one southwest (Gearhaven — the Gem), one nearby (the Band — in the party's pack)
  • He gives the party a star-chart confirming where to search
  • Directs them to Mórenórë for Waldkyn knowledge of the northern continent
One lens points not at the sky but at the Resonance Compass. The Echo-Lord has been using the observatory through the Compass to pinpoint locations.
Vaelric Star-Eye

Mórenórë — The Free City

Sessions 9–11 ~200–250 XP

Neutral city of ~8,000 on a peninsula. The one place on Vael where the Decree is openly ignored. Mages walk freely. The party confirms Vaelric's findings with Waldkyn scholars and arranges a short sea crossing to Aerinórë via Azure Hand captain Vesper Moraine.

Sidequests:

  • "The Scholar's Debt" — Protect Archivist Fenwick from a Shadewalker operative. Rewards: Aerinórë text, Song-Ward intel (15 XP)
  • "The Refugee Ship" — 40 mage-refugees arrive. A girl who looks like Petra. (10 XP)
  • "The Stillwood Debate" — Waldkyn diaspora argue about the spreading grey silence in the Vales. Fungril mystic Root-Memory senses the Crown piece's resonance. Intel on Aerinórë's wards (10 XP)
Inquisitor Dorn (Legio Null infiltrator) is here posing as a scholar, mapping the party's contacts.
Lorekeeper Theren Archivist Fenwick Vesper Moraine (Azure Hand captain) Fern-Tongue (Ribbet-kyn guide) Root-Memory (Fungril mystic) Inquisitor Dorn
⬇ Short sea crossing Mórenórë → Aerinórë — Vesper Moraine's ship, guided through the mist by Fern-Tongue or Azure Hand charts

Arc B: Lanthir Haven, Aerinórë — The Setting

Sessions 12–18 250→350 XP Hardest Moral Choice

A coastal sanctuary of ~800 souls — Waldkyn (all 8 variants), Hollowkin, Vorok-Varr Orkin, Sael'anir Elves. No currency, no violence, no walls. The Setting (Crown piece #2 — domain of Systematization) is woven into the Song-Ward network of the 600-foot Great Singing Tree, imposing systematic structure on the harmony.

Removing the Setting makes the Song-Wards incoherent, thins the mist, and exposes Aerinórë to the outside world for the first time in 1,400 years.

Sidequests:

  • "The Weight of Welcome" — Participate in daily life. Help Thistlebottom cook. Play canopy games. The sidequest is falling in love with the place. (5 XP/activity, max 15)
  • "The Mist-Reader's Worry" — Help Ilwen strengthen Song-Wards. Learn the mist has been thinning for months. (10 XP)
  • "Dewdrop's Question" — "Can you put it back when you're done?" No XP. Exists purely to make the players feel the weight.
"The Last Song" — When the Setting is removed, the tree shudders. Sap runs like tears. The 1,400-year harmony goes incoherent — a cacophony of clashing notes. Aelindra says: "It has been singing since before I was born. I didn't know it could sound like this."
Song-Mother Aelindra (Galapa) Brightleaf (Katari) Dewdrop (Ribbet — child) Vorok-Kal Thistlebottom Ilwen (wind-shaper)

Decision: How to Take the Setting

Path 1: The Honest Approach Present your case to the Listening Circle. Three days of debate. If compelling: Aelindra gives the Setting. The Waldkyn sing a farewell. +3 Lanthir Haven rep. Song-Ward choir comes in Act III.
Path 3: The Deception Convince them you'll return it. Buys time. Delayed betrayal discovered weeks later. In Act III, only Brightleaf comes — alone, because Dewdrop asked her to.
Path 2: The Theft Take and run. Wards collapse. Ilwen burns himself out maintaining the mist. Settlement exposed. -3 Lanthir Haven rep. No Waldkyn allies in Act III.

The Chase from Aerinórë

Flee through old-growth forest. Sael'anir wind-walls confuse. A Katari tracker follows from the canopy: "Why? We trusted you. Why?" The party enters Vorok-Varr territory and is captured — gently — by 30 warriors who sit down and wait for the conversation to finish.

Miravel reveals her spatial teleportation to escape. The air folds. Reality creases. They land in Aurumport, thousands of miles away. Miravel collapses bleeding.

⬇ Miravel's revelation — "There's something I haven't told you."

Interlude: Aurumport

Sessions 19–20

Major Mirennian port, ~20,000 souls. After Lanthir Haven's paradise, Aurumport is loud, dirty, commercial, and alive. 3–5 days of recovery, resupply, and emotional reconnection. Miravel attunes to the Aurumport ley-line nexus.

  • The Architect appears — pleased, urgent, doesn't ask how the party is doing
  • Brightleaf may arrive (if honest approach) — she crosses the ocean to travel with the party
  • The Pale Ledger may make contact — "politely, over drinks, in a place where killing is bad for business"
Miravel's doubt: "The Architect told me the last piece is at Gearhaven. But the Setting wasn't where it was supposed to be — it was woven into a community. Did the Architect know that?"
The Pale Ledger Brightleaf (if earned)
⬇ Miravel teleports the party near Gearhaven — "It won't be that bad this time." Overland approach on foot.

Arc C: Gearhaven, Novaterra — The Gem (Brass Heart)

Sessions 21–25 350→450 XP

Frontier boomtown of ~2,000 at Rust-Water Bay. Five years old. Birthplace of technomancy. The Gem (Crown piece #3 — domain of Silence) dampens volatile geothermal energy beneath the city. The engineers call it "the Brass Heart." Removing it makes the vents go wild.

Sidequests:

  • "The Jungle's Teeth" — Pylon 4 down. Kill Maw-Borer. Discover the mana-crystal geode. (15 XP)
  • "Sabotage" — Someone's tampering with pylons. Multiple resolution paths. (15 XP)
  • "The Bookkeeper's Errand" — Azure Hand wants a Shadewalker spy identified. (15 XP)
  • "Children of the Cliff" — Rescue children, discover Crown mural. (10 XP)
"The Nursery Window" — Tomlin is building a mechanical bird from scraps. His mother died in the purges. He's starting to forget her face.
Arch-Artisan Thaelric Cogmaster Pip Sprocket Sergeant Hrothgar Tinker Gaz Tomlin (child) Rusty / Wobbles / Sparky (the Rigs)

Decision: How to Take the Brass Heart (the Gem)

Path 1: The Substitute Engine Descend the Crystal Deep dungeon (3 levels). Harvest a mana-crystal geode core. Build a substitute dampener (3-day skill challenge). Execute the swap — timed 5-round siege while geothermal vents flare and the jungle attacks. The Rigs defend the Nursery. (3–4 sessions, best content)
Path 2: Diplomacy Earn Thaelric's respect through sidequests. Present evidence of the cosmic threat. Charm/Negotiation check (Hard to Formidable). He surrenders the Heart and manages the fallout himself.
Path 3: Theft Fast. Catastrophic. Dozens of civilians die. Nursery hit. -3 Gearhaven rep. No allies in Act III. Tomlin may not survive.

Consequence: The Compass Sings

With all three Crown pieces — Band, Setting, and Gem — the Resonance Compass emits a single, clear, triumphant note. Beautiful and terrible. It pulls toward the Barren Hills with physical force. The warmth is now heat. Holding it feels like holding a heart.

The note it sings is the opening phrase of the Fifth Harmony. The Compass is yearning.
▲ Gearhaven (substitute: +2, diplomacy: +1) • ▼ Gearhaven -3 (theft)
⬇ Miravel travels ahead to prepare the Barren Hills anchor — "The inscription wanted to be written."

Act III — The Convergence

War movie — convergence, revelation, siege • Sessions 26–35 • 450→600 XP

Return to the Mainland — Convergence

Sessions 26–27

Journey to the Barren Hills through Ashkeld and Orkin territory. The world converges — starting contacts reappear, drawn by the Crown's resonance. The party's past returns.

  • Starting contacts arrive: Gora Ironbreath, Finnet, Vosk Two-Names, Sergeant Cavel, etc.
  • Commander Thalric returns with 100 Sand-Riders. Can be convinced to redirect toward the real threat (Formidable check with evidence)
  • Orkin encounter: Whisper-Shaman Grukhor Ash-Eye senses the Crown — "You carry the Forge-Father's tools. Whatever speaks to you in that light — it is not Him. It is His wound."
"The Night Before" — the last campfire. Grukhor sings a lullaby in Grokh. Ask each player: "What does your character do on the last night?"
Grukhor Ash-Eye Commander Thalric (potential ally) Krag-Vosk the Unbroken The Pale Ledger

The Trap — The Barren Hills

Session 28 The Reveal

The Scar of Dissonance at the Hills' center. Nothing grows in a 100-yard circle. The party places the three pieces as instructed. They snap together. The Scar doesn't unmake them — it feeds them.

The Architect drops its disguise. The Echo-Lord crowns itself. The Fifth Harmony begins — a wave of absolute order. Wind stops. Water freezes. Animals go still. Mages feel their magic rewritten.

The Architect: "Thank you. I could not have gathered them myself — the fragments were designed to resist my touch. Only mortal hands."

"This Is Not My Father" — Krag-Vosk's Reckoning

Not Optional

Krag-Vosk feels the Fifth Harmony in his blood — the ecstasy of divine contact. Then the stillness follows. His fire goes quiet. Not silenced. Erased.

He fights it. He rises. He says:

"My Father shaped us to SURVIVE. To FIGHT. This is not His work. This is His WOUND. And I will not kneel to a wound wearing my Father's face."

He turns to the party: "Tell me where to hit it."

Miravel's Return

Key Emotional Beat

Miravel arrives mid-assault — exhausted, half-burned. She's been dismantling her anchor points. The anchors weren't just tracking tools — the Echo-Lord has been using them as relay nodes for the Fifth Harmony.

"I didn't know. I need you to know that. I didn't know what I was building."

Her journal falls open: pages of names. Every person who mattered. Tomlin. Aelindra. Dewdrop. The players' own names — each followed by something personal she noticed and never said aloud.

Forgive herPermanent ally. Lost her strongest magic but not her usefulness.
Let her sacrificeCollapses all anchors. Permanently loses spatial teleportation. Survives. Must rebuild.
Don't forgive herShe accepts. Finishes her work. Disappears.

The Cathedral of Geometry — Final Dungeon

Sessions 29–32 450→600 XP

Floating structure of dark crystal. Corridors rearrange by systematic logic. Three Outer Ring chambers:

  • The Hall of Time — Time-zones (fast/slow), 4 Chrono-Sentinels
  • The Hall of Control — 8×8 grid puzzle. 6 Geometric Constructs move in chess patterns (Towers/Bishops/Knights). Cause collisions to destroy them.
  • The Hall of Silence — Total absence of sound. No verbal magic. 2 Silence Wraiths feed on absorbed sound. Song-Ward Tuning Fork is the counter.

The Echo-Lord's Final Offer

Session 33 Hardest Moment

The Inner Sanctum. A sphere of perfect geometry. The Echo-Lord repeats the exact words it used to address each PC in Act I — then extends them:

"No more purges. No more wars. No more burning children for the crime of being born with a spark. Isn't that what you wanted?"

Every player at the table should feel the pull. The refusal must be earned — 28 sessions of caring about people is how you earn it.

The Shattering — A Symphony of Broken Things

Session 33–34

The Crown can only be shattered with many voices, not one. Harmony Threshold of 5 — each tradition channeled reduces it by 1. Need any 5 of 7:

  • Waldkyn Song-Wards — Aelindra's choir or a Waldkyn PC
  • Elm-korin Forge-Chants — Thaelric or Gearhaven ally
  • Orkin War-Drums — Grukhor's shamans (always present)
  • Human Magic — Any PC with the Spark (costs 4 strain)
  • Lothari Dream-Singing — Soru or Cira (if present)
  • Dwarven Stone-Lays — Dwarven allies (if present)
  • The Players' Own Defiance — Shout, sing, scream, laugh. Only works if 3+ others are active.

When the fifth tradition joins: the Fifth Harmony cracks. Not because the noise is louder — because it is more.

The Crown Shatters

  • Fifth Harmony wave collapses
  • Cathedral begins to crumble — 5 rounds to escape
  • The Scar of Dissonance seals
  • The Echo-Lord screams — not in pain, but in grief. It genuinely believed it was saving the world.
  • Its consciousness scatters into ambient dissonance, too fragmented to coalesce again for centuries

Canon note: This failed attempt becomes the first stirring that leads to the Grey Rot in SA:2705 — the Echo-Lord's scattered essence seeping through other Scars, weaker but persistent. The players didn't end the threat. They delayed it. That's all anyone can do.

Epilogue — Resolution by Faction

Session 35
  • Lux Aeterna: Sabel meets them in the desert. "You came back."
  • Gearhaven: Pip's communicator (garbled): "The substitute holds! Tomlin's rebuilding his bird from scratch."
  • Aerinórë (honest): Aelindra's message: "We are learning to live with an open sky. It is harder than we expected. It is also more beautiful."
  • Aerinórë (theft): Silence. Then rumors of Mirennian prospectors exploring "a newly discovered northern continent."
  • Orkin: Grukhor carves the party's names into a bone-totem. "You fought for a world that never fought for us. We will remember."
  • Mórenórë: Captain Voss writes a factual account that becomes the first document to shift opinion on the Decree.
