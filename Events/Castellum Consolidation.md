# Castellum Consolidation

While the Republic was nominally a union of equals, the militaristic city-state of [[Castellum]] began to dominate the Senate. Consul [[Marius the Iron-Hand]] used the threat of Orkin raids to garrison Castellum troops in other cities.

## Background

The [[Secular Decrees]] had removed religious authority as a counterbalance to military power. [[Castellum]], the Empire's southern fortress-city, had the largest standing army in the Republic.

## What Happened

Marius the Iron-Hand leveraged ongoing Orkin threats to justify stationing Castellum troops across the Republic. This effectively turned the democratic federation into a military hegemony, with increased military funding enabling the annexation of the independent "River Kingdoms" on the western border.

## Consequences

Democratic norms within the Republic eroded significantly. Castellum's military dominance foreshadowed the increasing authoritarianism that would characterize the Republic throughout Epoch III.
