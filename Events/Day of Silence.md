# Day of Silence

The Day of Silence marks the precise moment the [[The Twelve Minor Deities|twelve minor deities]] receded from the physical plane, recorded by all races though interpreted differently. It is the first day of the Second Age.

## Context — The Great Withdrawal

The Day of Silence was preceded by the [[The Great Withdrawal|Great Withdrawal]], in which the four First Hands — [[Tham'dran]], [[Virandros]], [[Myrasael]], and [[Elaraieth]] — receded into the metaphysical structure of reality. The twelve minor deities initially assumed expanded responsibilities as the primary sources of divine guidance. The Day of Silence marks the subsequent moment when even these minor deities departed the physical plane.

## The Withdrawal

The localized avatars that had led armies against [[Kael'Zorai]] simply dissolved into light, stone, or wind. In Human settlements of the Highlands, panic ensued as protective barriers faded. In the Dwarven halls, the forges briefly went cold. The psychological impact was devastating — for the first time, mortals felt truly alone.

## What Remained

Though direct divine intervention ended, mortals retained access to divine influence through:

- **Moments of inspiration** — divine sparks accessible to worthy individuals
- **Natural harmony** — fundamental balance underlying all existence
- **Sacred sites** — locations where divine influence remained concentrated (e.g., [[The First Forge]], [[The Resonance Halls]], [[The Crystal Gardens]])
- **Sacred artifacts** — the First Hammer, the Eternal Brazier, the Singer's Harp, the [[Harmony Crystals]]

## Consequences

The immediate aftermath brought collapse of the temporary military coalitions formed during the Divine War. Human tribes migrated en masse toward defensible high ground. A decade of magically induced twilight followed — the "Years of Grey" — caused by the lingering atmospheric debris of divine combat.

The Day of Silence set the tone for the entire Second Age: the Age of Mortals.
