# Silent Coup of the Free Marches

[[Gruk the Breaker]] was assassinated by a Republican agent, but his death did not end the movement. His lieutenants established a Council and formally declared the Republic of the Free Marches.

## Background

The [[Siege of the Free Marches]] had proven that the territory could endure military pressure. With Gruk's charismatic leadership gone, the movement needed to institutionalize to survive.

## What Happened

The "Silent Coup" was not a violent overthrow but a quiet transformation — from a rebellion led by a single champion to a governed republic. Though short-lived, it was the first nation in history founded on anti-slavery principles.

## Consequences

The Free Marches inspired slave revolts across the continent. More practically, the Republic's need to contain this breakaway state forced the [[Varnathi Republic]] to keep its armies close to home, effectively halting their colonial expansion for generations.
