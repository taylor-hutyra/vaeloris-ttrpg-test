# Season of Breaking

Pushed out of their ancestral hunting grounds by Human agricultural expansion, loose coalitions of [[Orkin]] clans launched a coordinated assault on the outlying settlements of [[Vernath]] and [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] surface outposts. Unlike previous raids, these were strategic, targeting granaries and water supplies.

## Background

The [[Stone-Road Projects]] and growing Varnathi population drove rapid colonization of lands the Orkin had used since the [[Orkin Schism]] pushed them to the margins. With no seat at the table of the emerging civilizations, the Orkin turned to coordinated violence.

## What Happened

Loose coalitions of Orkin clans launched simultaneous strikes against frontier settlements, demonstrating a level of strategic coordination that shocked the "Civilized Races." They targeted supply infrastructure rather than military positions, destroying three major human frontier towns.

## Consequences

The Season of Breaking became a yearly occurrence, forcing the realization among Humans, Elves, and Dwarves that the Orkin were a strategic threat rather than a mere nuisance. This directly prompted the [[First Council of Races]] and the formation of the [[Wardens of the Wild]].
