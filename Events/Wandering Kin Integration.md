# Wandering Kin Integration

On the mainland, the [[Wandering Kin]] — Dwarven caravans that traveled between cities — began acting as intermediaries for the hidden mages still living under the [[Mage-Purge Wars|Purge]].

## Background

The Dwarven caravan network provided natural cover for smuggling. Their wagons crossed borders freely, and their reputation as traders gave them access to markets and settlements where mages hid.

## What Happened

The Wandering Kin developed "Rune-Boxes" — lead-lined containers that could hide magical artifacts from Republican Inquisitors. These innovations made them the primary smugglers of magical contraband on the mainland.

## Consequences

The Kin's smuggling network kept magical knowledge alive on the mainland despite the Purge and directly enabled the growth of the [[Rise of the Arcane Black Market|Arcane Black Market]]. Their dual role as legitimate traders and covert smugglers would define their culture for centuries.
