# Red-Ink Crisis

The [[Shadewalkers]], realizing they could not defeat the [[Magocracy]] militarily, chose a different battlefield entirely: the economy. They manipulated the exchange rate of Mana-Crystals — the global currency standard — causing hyperinflation that threatened to collapse the [[Magocracy]]'s government.

## The Financial Attack

The crisis was elegant in its simplicity. By flooding certain markets with counterfeit crystal certificates while hoarding real crystals elsewhere, the [[Shadewalkers]] created cascading bank failures across the continent. Governments that had weathered wars and plagues found themselves helpless before a ledger full of red ink.

## The Devil's Bargain

The [[Magocracy]] was forced to hire the [[Shadewalkers]] themselves to stabilize the banks — the only people who understood the damage because they had caused it. By the Third Age, the "Thieves' Guild" effectively runs the global banking system, making them untouchable by the law. The Red-Ink Crisis proved that in the modern world, the pen — and the ledger — were mightier than the spell.
