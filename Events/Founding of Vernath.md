# Founding of Vernath

In the windswept northern highlands, the philosopher-warrior [[Vardas the Elder]] united seven warring tribes under a single banner. Rejecting the tribal superstition that the gods had abandoned them due to sin, Vardas preached that the gods had left because mortals were ready to stand on their own.

## The Foundation

He laid the foundation stone of [[Vernath]] on a high plateau, utilizing the ruins of a First Age fortification. The city was built not merely as shelter, but as a statement of mortal self-determination.

## The First Compact

Vardas established the [[First Compact]], a constitution emphasizing duty, order, and stoicism. Its core principle — "Stone breaks louder when ignored" — mandated that all citizens must contribute to the defense and maintenance of the city-state. Idleness was classified as a crime against the state.

## Legacy

The founding of [[Vernath]] gave birth to the [[Varnathi]] cultural identity, distinct from the scattering lowland tribes. This city-state would eventually grow into the most powerful human polity on the continent, spawning an empire that shaped the course of the Second Age.
