# Halfling Hearthsong Era

While the "Tallfolk" fought over colonies and resources, [[Halflings|Halfling]] culture entered a golden age. Their agricultural surpluses from [[Greenmeadow]] fed the warring armies of the Republic, making them indispensable to every faction.

## The Hearthsongs

Traveling Halfling minstrels spread "Hearthsongs" — ballads of simple life, hearth-warmth, and peace — that became popular even among soldiers on distant battlefields. These songs transcended national and racial boundaries, creating a shared cultural experience among the common people of all nations.

## Diplomatic Immunity

Harming a Halfling village became taboo even for the most ruthless generals. The combination of economic leverage (from the [[Green-Thumb Strike]]) and cultural goodwill (from the Hearthsongs) gave the [[Halflings]] a unique form of protection that no army or treaty could match. The Hearthsong Era demonstrated that soft power could be more enduring than steel.
