# Charting of the Broken Coast

[[Mirennians|Mirennian]] pathfinders completed the first accurate maps of the treacherous eastern coastline. Captain [[Hanno the Wave-Reader]] discovered the "Safe-Harbor Currents," a series of reliable water flows that allowed ships to navigate the jagged reefs safely.

## Background

Since the [[Keel-Laying of First Wave]], the Mirennians had turned to the sea as their primary means of survival, separated from the highlands by the Iron-Peak Range. Accurate charts of the dangerous coastline were essential for expanding beyond subsistence fishing.

## What Happened

Captain Hanno led an expedition that mapped the entire eastern coast, identifying safe passages through the jagged reefs using predictable current patterns. The Elvish loan-word "Sarinth" (Safe Harbor) entered common sailor vocabulary during this period.

## Consequences

The charting enabled the establishment of the first permanent Mirennian trade posts, which would later grow into the great Port Cities. This marked the true beginning of Mirennian maritime dominance.
