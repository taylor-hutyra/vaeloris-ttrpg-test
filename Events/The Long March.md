# The Long March

Facing the combined might of the [[Wardens of the Wild]] and the organized armies of [[Vernath]] and [[Kharnzarak]], the major [[Orkin]] clans realized they could not hold the fertile valleys. The Gral-Kor (Warlords) ordered a mass retreat into the inhospitable [[Barren Hills]].

## Background

The [[First Council of Races]] had produced a unified military response to the [[Season of Breaking]]. For the first time, Humans, Elves, and Dwarves coordinated their forces against the Orkin.

## What Happened

Outnumbered and outmatched by the Wardens and the standing armies, the Gral-Kor ordered a strategic withdrawal. The entire Orkin population migrated into the Barren Hills, a harsh but defensible wasteland.

## Consequences

The Barren Hills became the permanent, fortified homeland of the Varr-Darg. Orkin culture shifted entirely to a "siege mentality," viewing all other races as existential threats. This isolation and resentment would persist for centuries.
