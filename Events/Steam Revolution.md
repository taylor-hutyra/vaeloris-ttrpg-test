# Steam Revolution

## Background

Barred from researching powerful magic by the [[First Decree]], the engineers of [[Novaterra]] turned to physics and chemistry instead. The [[Technocratic Schism]]'s guarantee of machine autonomy meant the [[Magocracy]] had no legal basis to stop them.

## What Happened

High-pressure steam engines, rifled muskets, and clockwork automation began to replace magical equivalents. For the first time, non-magical technology could match or exceed what mages could do.

## Consequences

The Steam Revolution was "The Great Leveller" -- a peasant with a rifle could kill a mage. The balance of power began to shift imperceptibly back to the common man, and the seeds were planted for the [[Second Age of Flight]] that would break the Magocracy's control of the skies.
