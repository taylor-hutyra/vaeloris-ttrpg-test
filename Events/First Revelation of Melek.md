# First Revelation of Melek

After six years of seclusion following the [[Excavation of the Whispering Tomb]], [[Melek the Grey]] appeared in the central market of [[Argentum]]. Before a crowd of thousands, he levitated the city's massive stone obelisk and transmuted a merchant's lead weights into pure silver. He proclaimed, "The gods have left the keys in the lock."

## Background

Melek had spent six years studying and experimenting with the arcane residue he was exposed to during the excavation. His seclusion ended with a dramatic public demonstration that proved human magic was possible.

## What Happened

Melek distributed copies of his translated notes from the First Age texts. Within months, hundreds of intellectuals, artists, and desperate commoners began unlocking their own latent abilities. The "Spark" had been lit.

## Consequences

Melek was found dead three days after his revelation, his brain burned out from channeling too much energy. His death foreshadowed the dangers of the "Fraying" — the madness that would plague untrained mages. Despite his death, the knowledge could not be suppressed, and the world would never be the same.
