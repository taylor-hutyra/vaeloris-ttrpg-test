# The Great Withdrawal

The **Great Withdrawal** was the systematic recession of the four First Hands from the physical plane of [[Vaeloris]], marking the end of direct divine involvement in mortal affairs at the cosmic level.

## The Recognition

The Divine War had demonstrated the dangers of excessive cosmic-level involvement in mortal affairs. The First Hands recognized that their continued direct presence risked creating dependency rather than fostering independence, and that mortal races needed freedom to grow and define their own destinies.

## How They Withdrew

The First Hands chose not to die, but to recede into metaphysical realms, becoming part of the world's fundamental structure:

- **[[Tham'dran]]** — withdrew into foundational reality itself. Presence felt in enduring structures, stability, permanence.
- **[[Virandros]]** — withdrew into the cycles of existence. Presence felt in growth, change, renewal, adaptation.
- **[[Myrasael]]** — withdrew into the realm of perfection. Presence felt in beauty, refinement, the pursuit of excellence.
- **[[Elaraieth]]** — withdrew into consciousness itself. Presence felt in inspiration, awareness, transcendent experience.

## Consequences

The twelve minor deities assumed greater roles as the primary sources of divine guidance. The great divine artifacts — the First Hammer, the Eternal Brazier, the Singer's Harp, the [[Harmony Crystals]] — were embedded in reality to continue serving mortals.

The Great Withdrawal was distinct from the later [[Day of Silence]], when the minor deities themselves departed the physical plane entirely at the start of the Second Age.
