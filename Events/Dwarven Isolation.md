# Dwarven Isolation

Disturbed by the political chaos of the Humans and the rise of the [[Silver Lords]], the High King of [[Kharnzarak]] ordered the "Lower Deep" gates sealed. Trade continued on the surface, but non-Dwarves were no longer permitted into the cities proper.

## The Closing

This was the first major step in Dwarven withdrawal from the wider world. While not as absolute as the later [[Sealing of Kharnzarak]], it represented a fundamental philosophical shift: the Dwarves no longer trusted the surface world to be a stable partner.

## Cultural Stagnation

With external influences cut off, Dwarven culture turned inward. The rigid caste system established during the [[Unification of Dwarven Clans]] calcified without the challenge of new ideas. Innovation slowed. Tradition became dogma. The seeds of future crises — the Thulkorûn exile, the Bronrûn Exodus, and ultimately the [[Sealing of Kharnzarak]] — were planted in this era of proud isolation.
