# Formalization of the Thulkorun

To deal with the "Thul" crisis, the High King of [[Kharnzarak]] instituted the Ritual of Shattering. Rather than execution or imprisonment, criminals and oath-breakers were publicly stripped of their clan name, their hammer shattered, and exiled to the surface.

## Background

The [[Rise of the Thul]] had overwhelmed the prison-mines and created a growing underclass of outcasts. The existing judicial system could not cope with the scale of the problem.

## What Happened

The Ritual of Shattering became the formal judicial punishment: public breaking of a Dwarf's forging hammer, rendering them "spiritually dead" to the Stone. The exiled were designated [[Thulkorun]] ("The Broken-Crafted") and cast out to the surface world.

## Consequences

The Thulkorun became a distinct demographic across the continent. Despite their disgrace, they were highly skilled Dwarven laborers, and their influx into Human cities accelerated Human technological development -- most notably contributing to the [[Standardization of Steel]].
