# Rise of the Merchant Houses

In the coastal settlements, the most successful ship captains began to consolidate power, forming the first Merchant Houses. They built fortified warehouses and private militias, effectively becoming the governing bodies of the coast.

## Background

The [[Charting of the Broken Coast]] had established permanent trade posts along the eastern coastline. Over generations, the most successful captains accumulated vast wealth from maritime trade.

## What Happened

The transition was gradual but transformative. Ship captains pooled resources into formal Houses, replacing tribal and village leadership with an oligarchy of wealth. The [[Golden Compass Syndicate]] was founded as the first major trade guild.

## Consequences

This shift from tribal governance to mercantile oligarchy defined Mirennian culture for the rest of the Second Age. The Merchant Houses would eventually evolve into the hereditary aristocracy of the [[Rise of the Merchant Princes|Merchant Princes]].
