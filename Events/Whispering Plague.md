# Whispering Plague

A minor magical echo from a sealed [[Scars of Dissonance|Scar of Dissonance]] leaked into the water table near the Human/Dwarven border. Those who drank the contaminated water fell into comas, muttering in the tongue of the Abyss. It was not a disease but a magical contamination.

## Background

The [[The Paroxysm|Paroxysm]] left behind Scars of Dissonance across the landscape -- sealed wounds in reality that occasionally leaked dangerous magical residue. The growing settlements near the border were unknowingly built over contaminated water sources.

## What Happened

Victims who drank the tainted water fell into deep comas, speaking in abyssal tongues. The crisis prompted the first collaboration between Dwarven Runesmiths and Human hedge-healers, who worked together to purify the water source using combined techniques.

## Consequences

The Whispering Plague led directly to the establishment of the "Warding Corps" in [[Vernath]], the precursors to professional monster hunters. It also demonstrated the value of inter-racial cooperation against magical threats.
