# Discovery of the Silver Veins

Imperial prospectors discovered massive veins of silver in the [[Central Mountains]], territory nominally claimed by [[Dwarves]] but unoccupied. A tense standoff ensued until the Treaty of Coin was signed.

## Background

The growing economy of [[Vernath]] and expanding trade networks created demand for a reliable currency. Human prospectors ranged further into the mountains, encountering unclaimed but nominally Dwarven territory.

## What Happened

After the discovery, a tense standoff between Human miners and Dwarven claimants was resolved through diplomacy. The Treaty of Coin established that Humans could mine the silver, but Dwarves would refine it and take a percentage. This created the standardized "Vernathi Silver Mark."

## Consequences

The Treaty of Coin replaced the barter system in the region with standardized silver currency. The mining camp that grew around the deposits would eventually become the metropolis of [[Argentum]], one of the most powerful city-states of the Second Age.
