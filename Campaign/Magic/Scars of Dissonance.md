# Scars of Dissonance

*The class of phenomenon. For the specific Scar the party encounters in Chapter 1, see [[The Sorath Scar]].*

## What They Are

**Scars of Dissonance** are places where reality cracked during the First Age and was never healed. Most of them are remnants of [[The Ulzammar|Ulzammar]]-era harmonic work — music the ancients used to shape matter and time at a scale no mortal throat should have been capable of. The music stopped. Some of the cracks stayed open.

A Scar is not a portal. It is a **thinning** — a place where the membrane between ordinary reality and *what lies behind* has worn thin enough that things leak through in both directions.

## How They Behave

- **Site-bound.** A Scar does not move. It widens or narrows over time, but stays where it opened.
- **Mana-reactive.** Scars grow when nearby mana is disturbed, especially by First Age artifacts (e.g., [[Ulzammar Band|Crown fragments]]) or by concentrated magical practice. They shrink, slightly, when left entirely alone.
- **Time-dilated.** Inside a Scar, time does not flow cleanly. Travelers who enter for hours may emerge days later; travelers who spend what feels like years inside may emerge after only months.
- **Connected.** Scars are not isolated incidents — they are all cracks in the same underlying membrane, which means a traveler who survives long enough inside one may find their way to others.

## What Comes Out

Scars in different terrains produce different aberrations:

| Terrain | Common Spawn |
|---|---|
| Desert | [[Scorch-Wraiths]] — glass-and-sand aberrations |
| Forest | Shade-Briar fiends (not yet encountered) |
| Stone / cave | Silent Ones (not yet encountered) |
| Coastal | Salt-Hollow things (not yet encountered) |

Lesser fiends are native; greater fiends — the things that anchor a Scar — rarely leave.

## What Goes In

Mortals occasionally fall into Scars by accident (see [[Vorn]]'s seam-fall in [[01-The-Desert|Chapter 1]]) or enter them deliberately to close them (see [[Valeth]]'s lost crew).

Most do not return.

## The Song-Ward Tradition

A Waldkyn Song-Ward apprentice taught [[Valeth]] that aether-quartz, sung into at the right frequencies, creates a small field of tonal dissonance that lesser Scar-spawn cannot cross. This is the practical magic the Waldkyn use to keep forest Scars contained. It is a dying tradition.

[[Valeth]]'s Anchor is a specialized, cracked, battle-worn version of a Song-Ward device.

## Known Scars in the Campaign

- **[[The Sorath Scar]]** — desert foothills east of [[Lux Æterna]]. Under the First Age ruin of Sorath. Site of Chapter 1.
- Others exist across Vaeloris. The party may encounter more.

## The [[Silent Network]]

The Silent Network (a Lothari dream-mystic organization) has been quietly monitoring known Scars for centuries. [[Old Cira]] was placed in [[Lux Æterna]] specifically to watch [[The Sorath Scar]]. See her file for details.
