# Lux Ætterna — Map Definitions

Self-contained map specs and AI prompts. Each prompt is copy-paste ready.

**What is Lux Ætterna?** A hidden refugee settlement of ~400 people built into a deep, narrow box canyon in arid desert foothills. The canyon is naturally difficult to find — a narrow dogleg entrance, high inward-leaning walls, deep permanent shade — and is further concealed by carved ward-glyphs (the "Stitching") that create an aversion field, making anyone approaching without knowledge of the settlement feel compelled to look elsewhere and keep walking. Dwellings are carved into the cliff faces at multiple levels, connected by ladders and rope-and-plank walkways. The canyon floor holds the market. Cook-fire smoke vents through natural chimney cracks widened with picks.

## Map 1: City Overview

**Coverage:** The full box canyon plus ~500 feet of surrounding desert.
**Scale:** 1 inch ≈ 100 feet. The canyon is roughly 300 feet long, 80–120 feet wide.
**Orientation:** North at top. Canyon runs north-south, open at the south end via a narrow dogleg, closed at the north with a sheer cliff face.

### Spatial Layout (South to North)

**South — The Approach:**
Canyon mouth is a narrow crack (~10 feet wide) between two rock faces. The path doglegs — turns east then north — so there's no direct line of sight from outside. From the exterior, it looks like every other dry wash in the foothills. Desert scrub, rocky ground, nothing inviting.

**Center — The Veiled Market (widest section, ~120 feet across):**
The canyon widens here. The west wall has a broad natural overhang sheltering half the market floor. The east wall is more vertical, with dwellings carved into the rock at two and three levels — doorways glowing amber with lantern-light, connected by ladders and rope-walkways.

1. **The Central Well** — dead center of the widest point. Stone ring, 8 feet diameter.
2. **Tajsh's Vault** — west wall at ground level, under the overhang. Lean-to structure built against the canyon wall with a visible doorway and barred window.
3. **The Dusty Lantern** — east wall at ground level, slightly south of the well. The tallest freestanding structure: two stories, stone-and-timber, canvas awning.
4. **The Grey Flame** — east wall, near the south entrance to the market. A small carved niche shrine in the canyon wall, candle glow visible.

Surrounding the keyed locations: unnamed stalls, tent canopies, lean-tos, and carts on the canyon floor. Organic layout — no grid, no straight alleys. Above the ground floor, carved cliff-dwellings at two levels on both walls, with ladders and rope-and-plank walkways connecting them.

**North-Center — Transition Zone:**
Canyon narrows from 120 feet back to ~60 feet. Smaller shops (Needle & Thread, Crystal Counter, Iron Shelf) shown as building silhouettes, not individually keyed at this zoom. The path narrows between the walls toward the Warrens.

**North End — The Warrens:**
5. **Cave Entrances** — 3–4 arch-shaped openings at the base of the north cliff face, the largest ~15 feet wide. Deep shadow inside. These lead to the residential cave network. Label: "THE WARRENS"

**Canyon Rim — The Outlook:**
6. **Signal Rock** — east rim, the highest accessible point. Small flag/marker symbol.
Two camouflaged observation shelters on east and west rims (subtle, terrain-matching).
On the canyon walls: faint carved glyph patterns visible — the Stitching. These run in interlocking lines along the upper cliff faces. Suggest them as faint etched patterns in the rock, not glowing.

**Outside the Canyon (south):**
Dotted path from south canyon wall (west side) curving southeast to a dry riverbed labeled "Lira's Run" — the concealed escape tunnel.
Desert terrain: dune lines, scrub, rocky outcrops.
Small ruin icon ~1/4 mile east labeled "Ruin of Sorath."

## Map 2: The Veiled Market (Borough)

**Coverage:** The widest section of the canyon, from the south entrance dogleg to the narrowing north neck. Roughly 180 feet long, 120 feet wide.
**Scale:** 1 inch ≈ 30 feet

### Spatial Layout (South to North)

**South Entrance Area:**
1. **The Grey Flame** — east canyon wall, just past the entrance dogleg. Small carved niche with a charcoal portrait and a candle. Alcove symbol cut into cliff face.
2. **Cira's Corner** — west canyon wall, south end, slightly recessed under a jutting rock shelf. Woven-mat shape under overhang with small awning.

**Central Market:**
3. **The Central Well** — dead center of the open space. Stone ring, 8 feet across, rope-and-bucket crossbar. ~15 feet of open ground around it.
4. **Tajsh's Vault** — west wall, under the broad natural overhang, north of Cira's Corner. Lean-to against canyon wall: front door, barred window, locked back room partially carved into rock. Cluttered silhouette.
5. **The Dusty Lantern** — east wall, across from the well. Two-story building (the only two-story freestanding structure). Ground floor tavern, canvas awning over outdoor seats, three upstairs windows.

**North Market Area:**
6. **The Crystal Counter** — east wall, north of the Lantern. Counter-front shop with jars and bottles on shelves. Awning over counter.
7. **Needle & Thread** — west wall, north of Tajsh's Vault. Cramped rectangular shop against cliff, interior extends into a shallow cave.
8. **Iron Shelf** — west wall, further north near the neck. Small forge — chimney, anvil symbol, open-front smithy.

**Vertical Elements:**
Above the ground-floor shops on both walls: carved cliff-dwellings at second and third levels. Ladders leaning against the rock face. Rope-and-plank walkways spanning the canyon at the second level. These are residential — not keyed at this zoom, but shown as doorways and window openings in the cliff face.

**Between keyed locations:** 15–20 unnamed market stalls as tent-canopy and table shapes. Winding narrow paths, no straight alleys.

## Map 3: The Warrens (Borough)

**Coverage:** The cave network behind the north cliff face. Approximately 200 feet deep into the rock, 150 feet wide.
**Scale:** 1 inch ≈ 25 feet
**View:** Top-down cave plan. Small cross-section inset in one corner showing 30-foot elevation drop from entrances to Deep Well.

### Spatial Layout

**South Edge — Entrances:**
Three cave arch openings in the cliff face, spaced 20–30 feet apart. Central one is largest at ~15 feet wide.

**Just Inside (10–30 feet in):**
1. **The Schoolroom** — largest natural chamber, ~40 x 30 feet, accessed through the central entrance. Rough desk shapes in rows facing a flat teaching wall. Open doorway arch to main corridor.

**Central Corridor:**
Main passage runs north, 8–12 feet wide, branching east and west with natural curves. 15–20 small rounded family chambers (10–15 feet each) branch off corridors. Each has a short dashed curtain-line across its opening. Not individually keyed.

**East Branch:**
2. **The Sick-Bay** — natural grotto, ~25 x 20 feet. Cold spring pool at center (small irregular blue shape). Supply alcove with shelf shapes. Set apart from residential chambers.

**North Edge — Deepest Point:**
3. **The Deep Well** — lowest chamber, accessed by descending passage. Natural shaft (circle with depth hatching) into aquifer. Two guard positions flanking it.
4. **The Children's Tunnel** — narrow crack (2–3 feet wide) in rock wall behind the Deep Well. Dotted line trailing off the north edge of the map into darkness. Small "?" at the map edge.

**Cross-section inset:** Elevation profile — entrances at top, Schoolroom near entrance level, Sick-Bay midway, Deep Well at bottom. 30-foot total drop.

## Map 4: The Outlook (Borough)

**Coverage:** The canyon rim and surrounding hilltops within ~500 feet of the canyon edge.
**Scale:** 1 inch ≈ 75 feet
**View:** Top-down. The canyon itself is a void in the center (interior is on other maps). This map shows the defensive perimeter.

### Spatial Layout

**Center — The Canyon Void:**
Elongated north-south void with cliff edge hatching inward. Faint amber glow from below suggesting lantern-light in the depths. Not detailed here.

**East Rim:**
1. **Signal Rock** — prominent rock outcropping on east rim near north end. Signal-glass/flag symbol. Highest point with clear eastern sightline.
2. **Observation Post East** — camouflaged shelter midway along east rim. Small rectangular silhouette, sand-colored canvas, barely visible against terrain. Slit window facing east.

**West Rim:**
3. **Observation Post West** — camouflaged shelter on west rim, south of center. Matches east post treatment. Slit window facing west and south.

**South Rim:**
4. **Warden's Post** — exposed rock outcropping on south rim overlooking the canyon entrance dogleg and desert approach. No structure — just a marked rock with lookout symbol.
5. **Bolt Hole Entrance** — south canyon wall interior, west side. Concealed tunnel opening as dotted arch. Dotted path curves southeast down the hillside to a dry riverbed labeled "Lira's Run."

**Canyon Walls — The Stitching:**
Faint carved glyph patterns visible on the upper canyon walls — suggested as subtle etched decorative lines following the natural rock strata. Not glowing. They look ancient and easy to miss, which is the point.

**Surroundings:**
Desert hills, scrub, rocky outcrops, dry wash channels. Terrain should show this canyon is one of many similar geological features in rugged foothills — indistinguishable from its neighbors.
