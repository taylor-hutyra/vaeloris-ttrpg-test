# SCENE 2 — THE RIFT OPENS

**Budget: 10 minutes**
**Cast on stage:** [[Yldroth]], [[Norseman]], [[Arden]], and — by the end — [[Valeth]] and [[Vorn]]
**Location:** [[Mining Camp]], eastern edge of the outcrop

---

## The Interrupt

Mid-combat — after one or two rounds of active fighting — **all three [[Scorch-Wraiths]] freeze at the same moment.** Their hollow geometric eyes rotate, focusing on something none of the PCs can see. Then they begin to **screech.**

### Read-Aloud — The Screech

> *The wraith closest to you stops moving. All three of them do, in the same half-second, like puppets whose strings were pulled taut at the same instant. The fire-pit wraith lifts its head. They open their mouths, which are not mouths — hollow geometric cavities where faces should be. And they SCREECH.*
>
> *It is not a sound. It is the wrongness of a sound. Glass scraping against itself. A choir trying to sing underwater. The note passes through your teeth before it reaches your ears. Every lantern in the camp flares, then dims. The burning pitch in the sand flickers backwards for a heartbeat — flames shrinking toward the fuel instead of up.*
>
> *The ground trembles.*

## Cascade — The Fissure Opens

Describe each beat with a pause between. This is a sequence, not a description.

### Beat 1 — The Sand Collapses

> *Fifteen feet to the east of the fire-pit, the sand sinks. Not like a footstep — like a lung exhaling. A shallow depression forms. Then a deeper one. Then the sand pours downward, into a hole that wasn't there a second ago.*

### Beat 2 — The Quartz is Exposed

> *As the sand falls away, something catches the lantern-light. Pale blue crystal — aether-quartz — running in a vein down the exposed rock wall of the new pit. Rich. Rawer than the seam the crew was cutting. The vein is humming.*

### Beat 3 — The Fissure

> *The quartz vein splits. Not cracks — SPLITS. A hairline tear runs vertically down the center of the crystal, and through the tear you can see something that is not the inside of a rock. Darkness that has weight. A wind that smells like burning tin.*

### Beat 4 — Emergence

> *Something comes out of the tear.*
>
> *An elf, tall, lean, covered in dust and old blood. He is dragging a dwarf across his shoulders — unconscious, breathing shallow, one boot missing. The elf is singing. A single sustained note, low and wrong, delivered with the concentration of a man who has been doing it for a very long time. He has a shard of aether-quartz cupped in one hand. The quartz is glowing.*
>
> *As he clears the tear, he cuts the note — three sharp notes, falling, breaking the pattern. The fissure shudders. Then sand pours in from every direction at once, flooding the hole. The tear closes. The ground settles. Where the fissure was, there is now only disturbed sand and an exposed lip of aether-quartz.*
>
> *The elf sets the dwarf down, drops to one knee, and looks up at you. His pupils are too dilated. He does not look surprised to see you.*
>
> *He says, in a voice that has not spoken aloud in a very long time:*
>
> *"Is this your camp? I'm sorry. We needed somewhere to come through."*

## GM Notes — What Just Happened

- [[Valeth]] has spent years learning that his aether-quartz shard, sung into at the right frequencies, creates fields of tonal dissonance that lesser fiends cannot cross. He learned this from a dead Waldkyn Song-Ward apprentice in his rift-crew. See [[Valeth]] for his full history.
- He saved [[Vorn]] (who fell through a seam three days ago while mining) by humming a protective ring. They were then surrounded and thought they were going to die.
- As a last-ditch pattern, [[Valeth]] sang a new dissonance — **three sharp descending notes against the sustained tone** — and it tore a hole back to the real world. He has never done that before. He doesn't know if he can do it again. The quartz shard cracked badly on the way through.
- The three [[Scorch-Wraiths]] screeched because they felt a sibling-wound to their own Scar opening a dozen feet away. Their home reality, briefly, reached out.
- The fissure **cannot be reopened.** The seam is sealed. The exposed aether-quartz vein, however, is real and harvestable (**200 Sparks of raw quartz** if the party brings tools and time).

## The Wraiths Re-Engage

The wraiths' screech ends. They refocus. They are angrier than before, though the party won't know why. The fight resumes — see [Scene 3 — The Fight Concludes](03-scene-the-fight-concludes.md).

## The Anchor

[[Valeth]]'s aether-quartz shard is now visibly cracked. Tell the player privately: **he has 4 uses of the Anchor remaining** (not 7). The new dissonance-song cost three. It has also given him a new option: if he ever sings the three-note pattern again, he MIGHT be able to open a seam. He does not know. He is afraid to try.
