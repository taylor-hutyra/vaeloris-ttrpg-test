# SCENE 5 — THE RIDE BACK

**Budget: 5–10 minutes**
**Cast:** all five PCs
**Location:** scrub desert between [[Mining Camp]] and [[Lux Æterna]]

---

## Purpose

A breather. The party has been in combat and in wonder and in grief, all in one hour. Give them space. This is where new-party-dynamics start to form.

**Full strain recovery, no wound recovery.**

## Offer One Prompt

Pick one of these and offer it to any player who seems ready. **Do not force it.** If the table wants to skip to arrival, cut.

- *"[[Vorn]] — you're riding at the back, alone. Someone from this party is going to ride up next to you at some point. Which of them, and what do you say, or do you let them speak first?"*
- *"[[Valeth]] — you haven't slept in two nights. The [[Ulzammar Band|Band]] is in someone's pack, and the song you've been following has gone silent. Do you try to sleep tonight? What do you do instead?"*
- *"[[Yldroth]] — you're the other elf here. [[Valeth]] is Aen'valar, and you can see what that costs him. Is there something you want to say to him tonight, or do you let him find you first?"*
- *"[[Norseman]] — you're the mage of this group in a world that burns mages. Does [[Valeth]]'s strange magic make you feel safer, or less safe?"*
- *"[[Arden]] — what you sensed on [[Valeth]] isn't fading. He has been somewhere that does not let go of people cleanly. Is there something you want to find out? Or do you keep that to yourself?"*

## Mid-Ride Optional Encounter (skip unless the table wants it)

**A Lothari waystation** — a cairn of white stones, an underground cistern. The door-stone is warm. A dream-catcher of woven wire hangs from the ceiling. Anyone who sleeps here receives a single vivid dream (GM's choice — foreshadow [[The Architect]], hint at a PC's backstory).

**Or nothing at all.** A ride through cold desert. Stars overhead. Camels are reliable. Night is quiet.

## Read-Aloud — Arrival at [[Lux Æterna]]

> *The sun has been down an hour by the time you reach the slot canyon. You know the way in. Two sandstone pillars, identical to anyone who doesn't know what they're looking at. A narrow slot. A quarter-mile of stone walls. And then the lanterns bloom out of the dark, and [[Lux Æterna]] is there, waiting for you.*
>
> *Except it isn't, exactly. The lanterns are flickering wrong. One, steady-steady, flicker, steady, flicker-flicker. The market is quieter than it should be. Someone is humming a single sustained note from the mouth of the Warrens. A low, sustained, wrong note.*
>
> *[[Valeth]] stops his camel. He listens. He looks at you.*
>
> *He says: "That's the song. It's here too. It's been here the whole time."*
