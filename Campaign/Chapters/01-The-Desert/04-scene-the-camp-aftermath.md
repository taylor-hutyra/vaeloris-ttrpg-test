# SCENE 4 — THE CAMP AFTERMATH

**Budget: 15–20 minutes** (much of it reading the journal aloud)
**Cast:** all five PCs
**Location:** [[Mining Camp]] — the mining pit and the bodies

---

This scene covers everything the party does in the camp after the fight: descending into the pit, finding the **[[Ulzammar Band]]**, returning to the surface, finding **[[Torvin Dast|Torvin]]'s journal**, puzzling out the three-ring symbol, searching the bodies, and deciding whether to bury them.

Let the party move through these in whatever order they choose. The journal is the emotional anchor of the scene — save it for last if you can.

---

## PART A — THE PIT AND THE BAND

### Descent

[[Valeth]] walks to the mining pit without prompting. The ladder is where the crew left it. At the bottom of the pit, a shaft drops fifteen more feet into black crystalline stone.

### Read-Aloud — Climbing Down

> *The air changes as you climb down. It gets thicker — not warmer, not colder, just MORE. Your lantern flame slows until it's moving like it's underwater. Your own breath sounds far away. When you drop the last step of the ladder, the rope swings twice, stops mid-arc, and hangs there.*
>
> *In the wall of the shaft: a circlet of dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins. Half-embedded, like the stone grew up around it. Looking at it is like looking at something you're not supposed to see — your eyes want to slide off.*

### The [[Ulzammar Band]] — Inline Reference

**Artifact · First Age · Crown Fragment (1 of 3).** See [[Ulzammar Band]] for full properties.

- **Short range:** objects fall ~1.5× slow; flames slow; heartbeats lag behind breath.
- **Long range:** enchantments stutter; ward-glyphs waver at their edges; alchemy delays; water tastes metallic.
- **Pull:** if left, reappears in the nearest PC's pack by morning.

### The Touching Check

Any PC touching the Band: **Discipline (Willpower), Average ◆◆.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Success | External sensation. *Certainty. Gears meshing. Single thought: "Incomplete."* Can set it down. |
| Failure | Feels like their own thought. Wants to hold it. Second ◆◆ to put it down. |
| Triumph | Success + glimpse of three interlocking crystal shapes. |
| Despair | Failure + Staggered until end of next turn. |

### Who Should Touch It? Flavor Options

- **[[Vorn]]** — recognition more than contact. Offer **Knowledge (Lore), Hard ◆◆◆**. See [Part C](#part-c--the-symbol) for the grandmother memory.
- **[[Arden]]** — can *sense* it without touching. **Knowledge (Arcana), Hard ◆◆◆**. On success: *this object does not belong to this world; it was made, then forgotten, then found, and is not happy about the last.* Triumph: *it is part of something larger. Three parts. All missing.*
- **[[Valeth]]** — should NOT touch it. Tell his player privately: *the song you have been following goes silent when the Band is held up. You don't want to know what happens if you touch it yourself.*

### [[Valeth]]'s Private Moment

When the Band is held up to light — whoever holds it — **the song in [[Valeth]]'s head stops.** First silence in four days.

Pass to his player privately:
> *The sound has stopped. The silence is louder than the song was. You are not sure if it is relief or grief.*

### Other Finds in the Pit

- **Exposed aether-quartz vein** from Scene 2 — **200 Sparks** of raw crystal if harvested (tools + time). [[Valeth]] can craft a replacement Anchor from it. See [[Valeth]].
- **[[Torvin Dast|Torvin]]'s pick** — at the top of the shaft. Nicked. A serviceable weapon. If [[Vorn]] wants it: yes.
- **Dark stone:** the shaft walls are not naturally black — they are *stained.* **Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆** suggests something was here. Something left.

### Carrying the Band

- **Wrapped in cloth in a pack** (default) — field leaks but manageable.
- **Carried openly** — strong field; bad idea for travel.
- **Left behind** — it follows them.

If wrapped, narrate: *"Your blanket goes over it. The pack closes. Almost immediately, you feel a little lighter — like stepping back from a fire."*

## PART B — THE JOURNAL

### Finding It

A PC investigates [[Torvin Dast|the foreman]]'s body. (If no one volunteers, prompt [[Vorn]] — he will want to.)

### Read-Aloud — The Foreman

> *[[Torvin Dast|The foreman]] is older than the others. Fifty-something. Weathered. He died trying to cover someone smaller — a man collapsed underneath him, legs twisted, ankle wrapped in a dirty bandage. That's Rozen. [[Torvin Dast|Torvin's]] vest has been partly burned. A leather corner pokes out of an inner pocket. You pull it free. A journal, sand-stained, brass clasp still closed.*
>
> *His right hand is still clenched. When you open it, there's a small brass compass inside. Engraved, very small, on the back: "Come home."*

See [[Torvin Dast]] for the compass's background (Esha, his wife). Let whoever found it decide to keep it, give it to [[Vorn]], or set it aside.

### Reading the Journal Aloud

**Hand the player the printed handout OR read aloud.**

**Do not summarize.** The cadence is doing emotional work. Read straight through, Day 1 to Day 13, roughly four minutes. Let the table sit in silence.

### [[Vorn]] Hears His Own Story

Watch his player's face when you read Day 7 (*"[[Vorn]] cut the first shaft. He's humming today. He never hums."*), Day 11 (*"He says it is not his humming. He said that twice in a row and didn't notice he said it twice."*), and Day 12 (*"He is not anywhere."*).

**Let the silence after you finish reading be his to break.**

### Pause on the Tenderness

Don't rush past these lines:
- Day 1: *"Rozen is already complaining about his boots."*
- Day 3: *"Good crew. I like this crew."*
- Day 9: *"I owe him a drink. He says two. We are agreed on one."*
- Day 12 late: *"We are not miners anymore. We are witnesses."*

## PART C — THE SYMBOL

### Place the Handout

When you reach **Day 5**, pull out the Symbol handout and put it on the table. Say:

> *"[[Torvin Dast|Torvin]] tried to draw the symbol. He couldn't get it right. Here's his attempt. This is what he saw on the pedestal."*

Any PC who studies it: **Discipline (Willpower), Easy ◆.** On failure, mildly dizzy, loses their place mid-sentence.

### [[Vorn]]'s Lore Check (if not yet rolled in Part A)

**Knowledge (Lore), Hard ◆◆◆.**

- **Success:** His grandmother once sketched this in ash on the hearthstone, then wiped it clean with her hand. *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."* She was afraid. She would not explain. She died when he was twelve.
- **Triumph:** He also remembers her whispering one word under her breath: ***"Ulzammar."*** First time he has thought of it in forty years.

### [[Arden]]'s Reading

**Knowledge (Arcana), Hard ◆◆◆.** Success: the mark is a **notation**, meant to be *read* or *performed.* Triumph: it may be meant to be **sung.**

### Full Symbol Description (read aloud to any PC leaning in)

> *Three rings, interlocked — but not like chain-links. Each ring passes through both of the others simultaneously, which is the sort of thing that seems simple until you try to draw it yourself. The rings aren't perfect circles. They're slightly flattened, like they've been pulled at from three directions and held there. Where the three cross at the center, the carver went back with something finer and gouged out a single deep point.*
>
> *If you follow one ring with your eye, your eye wants to keep going. Around, and under, and around. It takes a small effort to stop.*

## PART D — OTHER ITEMS AND THE CHOICE

### Finds in the Camp

Let the party search freely. Available:

- **Tomas's half-carved wooden bat** — sentimental. Pocketable.
- **Rozen's unfinished letter** — to someone named Peret. Addressed but never finished.
- **Maret's knife** — Dwarven, clan-marked. ◼ Boost on [[Vorn]]'s first attack with it, next combat.
- **Crew pay-pouch** — 40 Sparks. Enough for lodging and two meals in [[Lux Æterna]].
- **Three camels** — tethered on the far side of the outcrop. Calm. Thirsty. Serviceable.

### Bury or Leave?

- **Burying the crew** — takes an hour. [[Vorn]] will want this. Let him decide.
- **Leaving them** — earns a quiet grief beat on the ride back.

No mechanical penalty either way. Both have emotional weight.
