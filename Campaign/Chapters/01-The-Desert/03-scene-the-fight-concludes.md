# SCENE 3 — THE FIGHT CONCLUDES

**Budget: 10 minutes**
**Cast on stage:** [[Yldroth]], [[Norseman]], [[Arden]], [[Valeth]], [[Vorn]] (unconscious)
**Location:** [[Mining Camp]]

---

## The Combined Combat

The three [[Scorch-Wraiths]] re-engage, screech over. [[Valeth]] is on one knee, spent from the rift-song. [[Vorn]] is unconscious on the sand next to him. The party now has a fifth body to protect, and a mysterious stranger who clearly has some kind of magic they don't understand.

### Tactical Situation

- **[[Valeth]]** is at **2 strain, 0 wounds** — exhausted from the song. He can defend himself but not much more this combat. He has **0 uses of his Anchor available this scene** (it's cracked, recovering).
- **[[Vorn]]** is unconscious, prone, on the ground beside the sealed fissure. Still breathing. Can be woken with a **Medicine (Intellect), Average ◆◆** or by damage (a wraith targeting him will wake him immediately — and give him one wound).
- The **three wraiths** re-engage. Push them harder this round — they are more aggressive after the screech.

### Opportunity for [[Yldroth]]

If [[Yldroth]] wants to spend an action reviving [[Vorn]] rather than attacking: he walks to the dwarf, strikes him sharply in the sternum, and [[Vorn]] wakes up gasping. Ki-healing. No check required. Narrate it:

> *Your fist lands against [[Vorn|his]] sternum, not hard — exactly hard enough. The blow pushes back through flesh into something that is less flesh. [[Vorn]] sucks in a breath like a drowned man surfacing. His eyes open. He says, coughing: "Who are you. Who are ANY of you."*

This is a good first scene for [[Yldroth]] and [[Vorn]] to meet.

## Push to a Finish — Two Rounds, No More

**Round 1 of renewed combat:**
- All three wraiths act. Full aggression. Geometric Eyes used freely.
- [[Valeth]] can take a defense-only action — describe him sitting with his back to a crate, eyes closed, aether-quartz in his lap, breathing slowly.
- The party should feel the pressure.

**Round 2 / Finish:**
- If a wraith dies, push the **Shatter** hard — describe the glass spraying across the camp, a burning patch of pitch nearby vitrifying into a sheet of fused sand-glass.
- When the last wraith dies, narrate it theatrically. Let a PC have the kill line. Ask: *"You have the wraith at your mercy. How do you finish it?"*

### Creative Play Rewards

- Any PC who uses cold (including [[Norseman]] if he has a cold attack) — auto-success, and the disabled wraith's beam-eye goes dark for the rest of the fight.
- Any PC who shoves a wraith into another wraith's line-of-fire — the beam hits the wraith and wounds it; describe one wraith *shattering* the other in a slow-motion explosion of glass.
- [[Arden]] using subtle mysticism (a misdirection, a flicker of illusion) — add ◼◼ to a group ally's next attack as the wraith hesitates.

## Post-Combat — The Silence

When the last wraith dies:

> *Silence. Real silence. The burning pitch is still there, but the flames have gone quiet. Your own breathing is loud. The aether-quartz vein in the sand is still humming — softly, low, the kind of sound you feel in your teeth rather than hear.*
>
> *[[Valeth]] opens his eyes. [[Vorn]] sits up slowly, one hand on the sand for balance. He looks at [[Valeth]]. He says: "You pulled me out."*
>
> *[[Valeth]] says: "Yes."*
>
> *[[Vorn]] says: "Thank you." And then, because he is a dwarf and he has just looked around the camp and recognized one of the bodies: "Oh, no. No, no."*

## Introductions

Give each new player **2–3 minutes of spotlight.** Do not rush this.

### Ask the [[Valeth]] player, in order:

1. *"What's the last thing you remember, before you started dragging [[Vorn]] toward the seam?"*
2. *"What's the worst thing that happened to you in the rift?"* (Optional — allow a pass.)
3. *"What do you call the song you've been following the last four days?"*

### Ask the [[Vorn]] player:

1. *"You remember striking the deep seam. What was going through your head, right before the stone opened?"*
2. *"You remember darkness that had weight. Was there a shape in it, or only the dark?"*
3. *"You recognize one of the bodies in this camp. Which one, and what did they mean to you?"* *(The third question anchors him to the dead crew emotionally. It matters in [Scene 5](05-scene-the-journal.md).)*

### Don't Force Trust From the Other PCs

If [[Yldroth]], [[Norseman]], or [[Arden]] are suspicious — lean in. [[Valeth]] looks wrong. His pupils are too dilated for the dim light. The blood on his tunic is three days old but the cut on his palm is fresh. [[Vorn]]'s boots are clan-marked — not standard Tajsh-issue.

**Optional:** Perception (Cunning) ◆◆ catches any of the above.

## Who is [[Arden]] Watching?

If the [[Arden]] player wants a private note from the GM: he senses something *layered* on [[Valeth]]. Not wrong, exactly. **Older than he should be.** The elf has been in a place where time does not flow cleanly. [[Arden]] can feel it the way one can feel an old building.

Do not share this with the whole table. Pass it on a note.

## Transition

The sun is fully down. The aether-quartz in the sand is still humming. [[Valeth]] looks at the mining pit. He has not looked at it before. He knows where it is.

He says: *"The song is over there. Under the stone. That's what I've been following."*
