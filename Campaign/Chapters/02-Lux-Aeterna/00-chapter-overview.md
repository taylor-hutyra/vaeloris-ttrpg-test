# CHAPTER 2 — LUX ÆTTERNA

*From the slot-canyon lanterns to the Architect's pocket-space, with an evening of wards gone wrong in between*

---

## Chapter Summary

The party arrives at [[Lux Æterna]] after dark with the **[[Ulzammar Band]]** in their pack. The settlement is wrong — the Stitching wavering at its edges, lanterns out of rhythm, water tasting metallic, [[Old Cira]] humming a single low note at the mouth of the Warrens. They'll hand the Band to [[Tajsh Thale]], spend an evening and a morning among the settlement's NPCs and shops, then meet Tajsh again for the revelation of the three-ring symbol.

Moments after Tajsh speaks, the Stitching will drop for one heartbeat — the canyon's cloak of aversion failing long enough for any observer watching the foothills to lock attention onto it. [[Old Cira]] will summon them to the Warrens. They will descend. They will meet [[The Architect]].

The chapter ends on Cira's question: *"How long were you gone?"*

## Chapter Budget

~**60–75 minutes** total. Scene 2 and Scene 3 can expand or contract depending on player interest. Scene 5 is non-negotiable — preserve at least 25 minutes for it.

## What the Party Takes with Them Out of This Chapter

- The **[[Resonance Compass]]** (gift of [[The Architect]])
- An unverified mission: go north, to the observatory in the Dragon's Tooth, and find what the stars know.
- A first layer of doubt about the light that offered to help them.
- Reputation +1 with [[Lux Æterna]] if the Petra confrontation goes well (see Scene 2).

## GM Reminders

- **[[The Architect]] is warm, not ominous.** Play relief first. Wrongness is a secondary signal for the perceptive.
- **Don't rush Scene 2.** The settlement's ordinary life is load-bearing. If it doesn't feel precious tonight, its later loss won't register.
- **End on Cira's question.** Each player, one sentence. Then stop.
