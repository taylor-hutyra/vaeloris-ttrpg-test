# SCENE 5 — THE ARCHITECT

**Budget: 25–30 minutes · the session's centerpiece**
**Cast:** all five PCs, [[The Architect]] (central), [[Old Cira]] (bookending)
**Location:** [[Deep Chamber]], via the Warrens

**Prep notes before running:** re-read [[The Architect]] for GM-only truth (Echo-Lord, lies-vs-truths audit). The scene has **four phases** — Entry, the Naming, the Message, the Questions.

---

## Phase 1 — Entry (5 min)

### Descent

The party moves past [[Old Cira|Cira]] into the Warrens. They walk through the [[The Warrens|residential tunnels]], past the Schoolroom, past the Sick-Bay, past the Deep Well. At the deepest bend — the **Children's Tunnel** — they find the crack behind the Deep Well that three children recently widened enough to squeeze through.

(This is what the children were going quiet about. They had found the edge of [[Deep Chamber|something]]. They did not go in.)

### Read-Aloud — Descent

> *The tunnel narrows as you go. The walls start to feel older than the rest of the settlement — because they are. [[Lux Æterna]] was built over someone else's ruin. You've always known that. You just haven't had a reason to care, until tonight.*
>
> *The air cools. The torch flame flickers slower. Slower. Then it stops flickering entirely. It is still burning. It just isn't moving.*
>
> *You reach the chamber. Round, empty, stone. There's nothing here. You've walked to the end of the tunnel system.*
>
> *And then the last of you crosses the threshold.*

### Read-Aloud — The Transition (slow; pauses are doing work)

> *The stone behind you is no longer there. Not dark. Not hidden. Not THERE. You don't turn around to check. You know, the way you know where your own hand is, that if you turned around there would be nothing to turn toward.*
>
> *Your torch is still. The flame has not gone out. It is simply holding its shape, like someone pressed pause on it.*
>
> *The air is warm and has no smell. Your breathing is slower than it should be. You aren't running out of air. You aren't getting any, either.*
>
> *Around you — not close, not far, distance isn't the right word here — rings of geometric light rotate in planes that don't intersect the way your eyes expect them to. Symbols you don't know the names of drift past and through each other. Some of them are versions of the three-ring mark. Some of them are things that mark could grow into if you let it keep going.*
>
> *There is no door. There is no floor, but you are not falling. You are, in every sense that matters, standing somewhere that is not a place.*
>
> *And at the center of all of it — not a figure, exactly. A CENTER. A point where the geometry converges and something is looking back.*

**Pause. Let the silence sit.** Let a player be the one who speaks first. When ready, deliver the Architect's first line, quiet:

> *"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."*

### Pocket-Space Properties (GM Reference)

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside. Players will not notice.
- **No door.** No way out. They wait to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons do nothing. Discipline ◆◆ to recognize. Failure: they try anyway, feel foolish.
- **Echoless voices.** Like speaking inside a glass sphere.

## Phase 2 — The Naming (7–10 min)

The Architect addresses each PC by name. Deliver slowly. **This is relief, not threat.** Every word is tuned to lower their defenses. Let it land.

### [[Valeth]] (Aen'valar Elf)

> *"Child of the long silence. You have walked closer to the truth of things than any of your companions. What did you find, there, in the rooms that are not rooms? You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. That is why the song found you first."*

### [[Vorn]] (Dwarf)

> *"Son of the stone-line. You have a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You know what it is. You felt it when you struck the seam — not fear of falling, but recognition. You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

### [[Yldroth]] (Elf Monk)

> *"Child of the Aen'tir line. Your fists have remembered how to hold grief and how to release it both. You heal what you strike because you learned, long before you had a teacher, that the body is a text and the hand is the pen. Your brother in silence — [Valeth] — does not know yet how much you will need to teach him. You will know when."*

### [[Norseman]] (Human Battle Mage)

> *"Son of the northern cold. You carry magic in a world that burns people for carrying it. You have made peace with being both the sword and the hand that wields it — most mages never learn that. The purge will not stop because we wish it to stop. It will stop because someone decides it will. You have been deciding things your whole life that others were too afraid to decide. I came to you because you are already the person this task requires."*

### [[Arden]] (Drakona Mystic — Hidden Identity)

*(The Architect pauses. Looks at him. When it speaks, its voice softens — noticeably. A register only [[Arden]] feels the intimacy of.)*

> *"Son of the Sleeping Wings. Do not flinch. I will not say the name of what you are aloud — not here, not where your companions have not yet earned the weight of it. But I know. Your scales are beneath your skin because they have to be, and I will not ask you to bare them. What your people once were is not a weight you chose. What your people become is. You are already choosing well."*

**For the [[Arden]] player:** this is a devastating moment. The Architect has SEEN him and chosen DISCRETION. It is intimate, protective, knowing. Exactly the gesture that a wise mentor would make. It is also exactly the gesture that a skilled manipulator would make. The player gets to decide which one it was.

## Phase 3 — The Message (5 min)

Run as continuous dialogue, not a list:

1. *"The object you carry — what you call the Band — is a fragment of an instrument. Once whole, it was used to reshape the world. It nearly ended. The fragments were scattered to prevent that ending. Two more remain."*
2. *"I do not know where they are. Fifteen hundred years of watching, and even I cannot see everything. The fragments were hidden by gods. They are hidden from me too."*
3. *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left behind to watch. To wait. To speak when there was finally someone to speak to."*
4. *"I have prepared something for you."*

> *A plinth of folded light rises in front of the party. On it, revealed: a palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal, slotted in the center. It hums. [[Valeth]] hears the note he has been following — except now it is in harmony with itself, and before it was alone.*

5. *"This is a Compass. It will sing when a fragment is near. But it cannot tell you where to begin. You must first learn where to look. Go north. The Dragon's Tooth pierces the sky. At its peak, there stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the stars as mortals read letters. Fragments disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark."*
6. *"Once all three are gathered, they must be brought to a wound in the world, and there they will be unmade. Only there. Nowhere else. That is the task."*

The plinth of light remains. The [[Resonance Compass]] is on it. A PC may take it at any time — it will not resist.

## Phase 4 — The Questions (10–15 min)

**Tell your players in your GM voice:** *"It has said what it came to say. It is waiting. You can ask it anything."*

### Prepared Answers

**"What are you?"**
> *"A servant. When the Four turned their hands from the world, some were left behind to watch the places that could not be left unwatched. I am what remains of that watching. I am not a god. I do not want worship. I want the world to continue."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because the Band has answered your hand. Because those of you who carry wounds know how to walk with them. And because you are HERE. Gods plan. Mortals arrive. I have learned to work with what arrives."*

**"Are you good?"**
> *"I am useful. Goodness is a mortal accounting, and the accounts are kept by those who survive to keep them. I was left here to prevent a catastrophe. That is the only measure I am prepared to answer for. When this is done, you will decide whether what I did was good. That is your right, not mine."*

**"What is the thing that will come if we fail?"**
> *"A song that the world cannot survive being sung. You would not understand the name of it. The best of my language can describe it only as HUNGER GIVEN FORM. It sleeps. The fragments, gathered and wrongly used, would wake it. Gathered and rightly unmade, they would silence it forever."*

**"Why can't you just take the Band yourself?"**
> *"Because I am not here. What you see is a shape my attention is taking, in a place my attention has reached. I cannot lift a stone. I cannot kill your enemies. I can watch, and speak, and prepare the ground. The rest is for hands that bleed."*

**"How do we know you're telling the truth?"**
> *"You do not. You never will, not entirely. Any test I suggest is a test I have prepared for. This is the oldest problem. I will offer what I can: I have told you nothing tonight that you can verify immediately. Go north. Find the observatory. See whether the stars say what I say they say. If they do not, come back and name me a liar. I will still be waiting."*

**To [[Valeth]]: "Have you been in the rifts?"**
> *"I know what is in them. I know what you did not let them take from you. That knowledge is not the same as the living of it. You have something I do not. It is why I can ask this of you and not of myself."*

**To [[Vorn]]: "Did you kill my crew?"** *(pause — let the table feel it)*
> *"No. What killed your crew was already walking the thin places long before I knew your name. I could not reach them. I could not reach you, until the seam opened. If I had reached further, sooner — perhaps. But regret is a door I cannot afford to open. Yours is a grief I do not know how to carry for you. I am sorry."*

**To [[Arden]]: "Are there others like me?"** *(if he asks privately or out loud)*
> *"Fewer than there once were. More than you believe. Some are hiding. Some have forgotten what they are. One or two have chosen the fire. You will meet them, in time. They will know you."*

**"What about the ones hunting us?"**
> *"The [[Legio Null]] will come. Soon. Your settlement was visible from the sky for one heartbeat. That is enough. You must leave this place, and soon. I cannot stop the hunt. I can only tell you to RUN."*

**"Will we see you again?"**
> *"When the Compass sings, I will be near. Not always in a form your eyes can hold. But near."*

**Coercion / threat (calm, slightly amused):**
> *"You are holding a Band you cannot control in a settlement your enemies will find within the week. I am the hand offered to you in the dark. If you prefer another, seek it. I will not chase you. I will be here if you return."*

### Skill Checks Available

Any player may ask to roll. Offer these if a player states an intent:

| Intent | Check | Difficulty | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Notice something wrong | Perception | ◆◆ | The Architect casts no *reflection* in the floating crystal symbols. Nothing else in the space has this problem. |
| Read beneath composure | Perception | ◆◆◆ | Anticipation when it speaks of "unmaking." Not relief. Triumph: *hunger.* |
| Magical signature | Knowledge — Arcana | ◆◆◆ | Divine on surface, **abyssal** underneath. Perfume over rot. Overlay is skilled. Wrongness is not. |
| Environment | Knowledge — Nature | ◆◆ | A moth drifted in with you. It is paused mid-flight. Not dead. *Paused.* |
| Floating symbols | Knowledge — Lore | ◆◆◆◆ | Compositional notation — music for frequencies no mortal throat can produce. The three-ring mark is a **chord.** Triumph: [[Torvin Dast|Torvin]]'s sketch was a degraded version. |
| Resist emotional pull | Discipline | ◆◆ | Recognize the Naming as *too* tailored. A therapist would call it skill. A spy would call it targeting. |
| Coax out more | Charm or Negotiation | ◆◆◆ | Reveals one detail beyond plan. GM picks: (a) **Brenn Hallowveil** — the observatory's last living custodian. (b) A Waldkyn Song-Ward master who could train [[Valeth]]'s anchor. |
| Mislead | Deception vs | ◆◆◆◆ | Appears to believe. Half a beat later, one sentence betrays that it did not. Only the rolling player notices. |
| Threaten, demand, coerce | Coercion | ◆◆◆◆◆ | Fails. Amused. *"You are welcome to try. I have been threatened by better than you and was not moved. Sit down. Or do not. I am not in a hurry."* |

## Phase 5 — Exit

When the questions run out:

> *"Go north. Take the Band. Take the Compass. Trust the people who love you. Do not trust me further than you have to. This is the only honest thing I will say tonight."*

### Read-Aloud — Collapse

> *The geometry folds inward. The planes rotate, then dim. The center-point you have been speaking to contracts to a single point of light, and then that point is gone. You blink.*
>
> *The torch is flickering again. The stone behind you is stone. The floor is under your boots. The [[Resonance Compass|Compass]] is warm in someone's hand.*
>
> *You turn. [[Old Cira|Cira]] is at the mouth of the tunnel. She looks at you. She says:*
>
> *"How long were you gone?"*
>
> *One of you answers, "A few minutes."*
>
> *She says, "It has been three hours. I could not see you. I was not sure you were coming back. How do you feel?"*

---

## Ending the Chapter (and the Session)

Ask each player, **in character, one sentence only**: *How do you feel?*

Go around the table. Each player, one sentence. No discussion. When the fifth has spoken:

> *That's where we leave it for tonight. Good game, everyone.*

**End.**
