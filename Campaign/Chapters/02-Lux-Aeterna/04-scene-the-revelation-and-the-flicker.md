# SCENE 4 — THE REVELATION AND THE FLICKER

**Budget: 10–15 minutes**
**Cast:** all five PCs, [[Tajsh Thale]], [[Old Cira]] (closing)
**Location:** [[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh's office]], then the [[Veiled Market|market]], then the mouth of the Warrens

---

## Setup

[[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]] has read the journal. He has not slept. The [[Ulzammar Band|Band]] is on his desk, wrapped in dark wool. The candle is across the room, behind him.

## Read-Aloud — Opening

> *[[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]]'s office is small. Desk. Two chairs. A kettle that is whistling and being ignored. The [[Ulzammar Band|Band]] is on the desk, wrapped in dark wool. He's not looking at it.*
>
> *He gestures at the chairs. Only two, for five of you. He waits to see who sits down. You notice he is watching [[Valeth]] and [[Vorn]] with a particular flat attention. He does not ask them their names. He will, eventually. Not yet.*
>
> *He picks up the journal. Sets it down. Picks it up again. Says, without looking up:*
>
> *"I've seen that symbol twice in my life."*

## The Monologue — Deliver Slowly. Pause on the Pauses.

> *"Once, when I was twenty-two. [[Shadewalkers|Shadewalker]] archive. Sealed crate. We weren't allowed to touch it. Which meant I spent three hours memorizing everything I could see through the slats of the lid. There was a symbol burned into the lid. Three rings, interlocked. I tried to draw it later. I couldn't get it right. Neither could [[Torvin Dast|Torvin]]."*
>
> *(He taps the journal.)*
>
> *"Second time — seventeen years ago. A ruin in the Barren Hills. I was paid a great deal of money to forget I had been there. The symbol was carved into the wall of a room my employer did not want anyone to enter. He did not enter it either. He paid me to carve it shut and walk away. I did."*
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> *"Both times, everyone in the room treated it the way you treat a cobra. Very still. Very careful. Nobody wanted to be the one who reached for it."*
>
> *(He looks up. At the returning PCs. Then at [[Valeth]] and [[Vorn]].)*
>
> *"That book — whatever's on the pedestal [[Torvin Dast|Torvin]] found — I need it. I need you to go back to that temple and bring it to me. And before any of you asks why: I don't know yet. I know that the kind of people who use that symbol don't leave things in abandoned mines by accident. They leave them there for someone to find. I'd like to know who they were hoping to find. And I'd like to know before whoever they were hoping to find gets here first."*

## Dialogue — Prepared Answers

**"What were the [[Shadewalkers]] so afraid of?"**
> *"I never found out. The crate was gone by the next month. The briefing file I tried to pull after was missing. I stopped asking."*

**"Who was your employer at the Barren Hills?"**
> *"A name I don't say out loud. Sorry. Not today. Ask me again in a year."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because you found it. Because whatever this is has decided you exist. And because I trust you more than I trust anyone else in this settlement right now. That's not a compliment. It's arithmetic."*

**"What about the Band?"**
> *"Stays here. In this room. In this wool. I don't want it on the road. Whoever's hunting things like this will find it faster if it's moving. Get me the book. I'll get us someone who can read it."*

**"The dwarf and the elf — what about them?"** *(Looks at them.)*
> *"You came out of a hole in the world. Your friend walked out of a rift that shouldn't be there. You're either part of this or you're worse than part of this. Either way, you go with them. I want you where I can see you. Welcome to [[Lux Æterna]]."*

## The Ledger

[[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]] asks [[Valeth]] and [[Vorn]] their names. Writes them in his small leather ledger.

> *"Next of kin? Anyone we should notify if something happens out there?"*

He's not asking because he wants to know. He's asking because **someone has to write the name down.** (This ledger reappears much later. It matters.)

## THE FLICKER — The Interrupt

Just as the party is about to leave his office — or just after they've agreed to the mission:

### Read-Aloud

> *A sound you had not noticed — a low, constant hum you'd registered so long ago it had become the canyon's silence — stops.*
>
> *Every carved glyph on the upper canyon walls goes dark. Hundreds of them, all at once. The faint amber glow they give off — the glow you've never thought about, because it's just always there — is gone. For one heartbeat, the canyon is only stone and shadow and the ordinary lanterns burning at the market stalls.*
>
> *Then the glyphs come back. Brighter than they should be. They pulse once, twice, then settle into their usual faint amber hum. The background sound returns.*
>
> *One heartbeat. The Stitching dropped.*
>
> *[[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]] is already on his feet. He is looking at [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] through the window, and [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] is running toward the outlook. [[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]] says, quietly: "That was bad. That was very bad. Stay here."*
>
> *From the mouth of the Warrens, you can hear [[Old Cira|Cira]] humming — louder than she was yesterday, louder than she has ever been heard to hum — a single sustained note that is climbing, slowly, in pitch.*

## What Just Happened (GM note)

For one heartbeat, the **aversion field** that normally pushes away anyone observing the foothills was completely absent. Any observer in that instant — a [[Legio Null]] scout on the high road, a patrol on the mesa rim, a [[Silent Network]] dream-reader tracking resonance, a [[Shadewalkers|Shadewalker]] with a tracking-glass — had their attention pulled to the canyon and **locked there.** The Stitching snapped back, but the damage was done. Someone saw. This is a catastrophic event — not because of lasting physical damage, but because **the canyon is no longer unknown.** [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] is already preparing evacuation protocols. The Legio Null will not be here tomorrow. They will be here in three days. That is Session 2's pressure.

**More immediately:** the flicker also coincides with [[The Architect]]'s attention reaching the Deep Chamber. Its arrival caused it.

## Cira's Summons

The party leaves [[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh's]] office and moves through the [[Veiled Market|market]]. People have stopped. Looking up. Not saying anything. [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] is on the outlook wall, shouting orders quietly.

[[Old Cira|Cira]] is at the mouth of the Warrens. She has stopped humming. She is waiting for them. When the party is close enough to hear her, she says:

> *"I cannot follow you where this goes. Listen carefully. Doubt generously. Come back."*

She steps aside. The tunnel is open.

## If the Party Hesitates

They might. Let them. [[Old Cira|Cira]] waits. After a moment:

> *"It will not come to you if you stay up here. It will wait. It has waited fifteen hundred years. But while it waits, what is above will find what is below. Go. Now. Please."*

## Transition
