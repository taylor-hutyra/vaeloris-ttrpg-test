# SCENE 2 — THE EVENING

**Budget: 15–20 minutes**
**Cast:** all five PCs; vignette NPCs as chosen
**Location:** [[Lux Æterna]] — [[Veiled Market]], [[Dusty Lantern]], Hask's Workshop, canyon ladder, the Well

---

## How to Run This Scene

This is a **menu**, not a script. The party will split up or stay together — let them. Run **2–3 vignettes**, chosen by where the PCs go. **The Well is the one to hit** if you can.

Each vignette is self-contained. Run them in any order. Skip freely.

---

## VIGNETTE — [[Marta Dustborn|Marta]] at the [[Dusty Lantern]]

Human woman, 40s. Burn scars on her arms. Always cooking. Warm and dry at the same time.

Behind the bar. The lanterns are out of rhythm tonight — one flickers, two steady, one flickers, all four flicker, three steady. She is pretending not to notice.

If a PC orders food or drink, she serves — [[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]]'s tab if they remember to mention it, her own tab if they don't.

If they mention the lanterns:
> *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why."*

### The Elf-Recognition Beat

**If [[Valeth]] enters her tavern:** Marta takes one long look at him, then serves him the same bowl she serves everyone else. She does not ask where he came from. She has been where he has been, in her own way. (She was burned once, badly. She survived. She knows what someone looks like who survived something.)

### Rumor on Tap

If a PC strikes up conversation: **Charm (Presence), Easy ◆.** On success, roll 1d4 on the rumor table — see [Scene 3](03-scene-downtime.md).

## VIGNETTE — [[Hask]] in the Alchemy Workshop

Orkin, mid-30s. Broad. Hands stained. Goggles pushed up like a second set of eyes. Slow, considered speech.

He is sitting on a stool, watching a potion simmer. The potion has been simmering for an hour. It has neither boiled nor cooled. He is watching it the way a farmer watches weather.

If a PC enters, he hands them a cup of something warm without asking:

> *"Whatever you brought back — it's in the walls now. Your hands are probably hungry."*

### The Elf Beat

**If [[Valeth]] passes by:** Hask looks at him for three full seconds. Says: *"You've been somewhere my people used to go. Before."* Doesn't elaborate. Doesn't have to.

### What Hask Knows

- The alchemy is subtly broken tonight. Reactions that should take seconds are taking minutes. He's cataloguing it.
- He sells common potions. See [Scene 3 — Shopping](03-scene-downtime.md#the-shops).

## VIGNETTE — [[Selin Voss|Selin]] on the Ladder

Gnomish woman, 50s. Nervous, twitchy. Fingers stained blue. Talks fast, interrupts herself.

She is twenty feet up a wooden ladder against the upper canyon wall, retouching the deep-cut lines of a **Stitching glyph** with a graver's chisel and a small pot of enchanted ink. The glyph has been flickering for an hour — the amber glow it should hold steadily dips every few seconds, then recovers. She does not like that. When a PC walks past, she grabs their sleeve (or shouts down):

> *"Does this look right to you? Does it? I've been staring at it for so long I can't tell anymore. Tell me it looks right. No, actually tell me if it looks wrong. Don't lie to me. Does it look wrong."*

### What She Knows

- The Stitching is **stuttering** — not failing. Never happened in her six years of tending it. The glyph-pattern is sound. The cut-depth is sound. The ink is sound. What is wrong is *the ambient mana the glyphs draw on.*
- She is the only person alive who understands the full Stitching pattern. If she dies, the wards fail within weeks. She knows this. It exhausts her.

### If a PC Tries to Help

**Knowledge — Arcana (Intellect), Hard ◆◆◆.** On success, they reinforce the failing patch for the rest of the evening. [[Selin Voss|Selin]] will remember it, and so will [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]].

**Reward:** 10 Sparks, a Boost die on one future social check with any [[Lux Æterna]] resident.

## VIGNETTE — THE WELL (RUN THIS ONE)

> *You hear raised voices at the well. Not arguing — one voice, loud, shouting at a small girl. "She's been doing this. I've seen the sparks on her fingers. Whatever's in the water, it's HER." A handful of people are standing around, not stopping him, not helping. [[Petra]] — ten years old, soot on her hands — is not crying. She is watching the man who is yelling at her. She looks very small.*

### Who's Who

- **Oris Hallow**, the accuser. Frightened, not villainous. His wife is sick. He thinks the water is making her sicker. He is wrong. He has chosen [[Petra]] because he needs to blame something.
- **[[Petra]]**, 10 years old. Manifested sparks two weeks ago. Hasn't been inside the schoolroom since. Done crying where anyone can see. Wants to know if it gets better.

### Options the Party Has

| Approach | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| De-escalate with words | Charm or Negotiation ◆◆ | Oris is shamed, leaves. |
| Authority | Coercion ◆◆◆ | Crowd disperses; resentment lingers. |
| Remove [[Petra]] | no roll | Simple. [[Petra]] goes with them for a moment. |
| Explain (the Band) | Knowledge — Arcana ◆◆ | Risky but works. Some people believe. |
| Do nothing | — | [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] arrives in 2 minutes and disperses the crowd. Tension stays. |

### Consequence

**If the party defends [[Petra]], she remembers** which PC did so. That memory matters later in Act I.

### If [[Petra]] is Approached Directly

She does not want reassurance. She wants to know if it **gets better** — if people learn to live with it. The PC can answer honestly, lie, or say nothing. Whatever they say, she nods like she expected it, and goes back inside.

### [[Norseman]] Specifically

If [[Norseman]] is present and recognizes [[Petra]] as a fellow mage — there's a beat here for him. He was her once. He can tell her so. If he does, she looks at him for a long moment, then nods, then runs. She will find him tomorrow morning and ask him a single question. (GM's choice. Recommend: *"Have you ever burned someone by accident?"*)

---

## What to Drop Regardless of Where They Go

**[[Warden Sabel|Sabel]] sighting.** A PC sees him at some point tonight — doing weapons checks on the militia crossbows, one by one, approving each. He has checked them all twice already. **This is how he sits still.** If a PC approaches and asks if everything is okay: *"Everything is exactly what it is. I'm checking the bows."* (Meaning: *no, and I'm managing it.*)

He saw [[Tajsh Thale|Tajsh]]'s candle freeze. He has not mentioned it to anyone.

---

## Transition

When the evening feels complete — or when 20 minutes have passed — the party beds down for the night. Cut to [Scene 3 — Downtime](03-scene-downtime.md) for the morning.
