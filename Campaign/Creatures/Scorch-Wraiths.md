# Scorch-Wraith

*Aberration. Spawn of [[Scars of Dissonance|Scars]]. Humanoid in silhouette, architectural in geometry, hungry for mana.*

## Description

Humanoid shapes made of **superheated sand and glass**, with hollow geometric cavities where faces should be. They move with uncanny precision — the angles of their limbs are too regular. When they strike, their claws cut lines that are suspiciously straight, as if drawn with a ruler. They do not speak. They sometimes screech, in response to cosmic disturbances near their home Scar.

They feed on residual mana. They cluster around First Age ruins, aether-quartz deposits, and anywhere that magic has concentrated and been abandoned.

### Abilities

- **Immune to Fire.** No damage from fire sources.
- **Vulnerable to Cold.** +2 damage from cold. Cold attacks also disable *Geometric Eyes* for 1 round.
- **Blindsight.** No penalties from darkness, smoke, or visual obscurement.
- **Glass-Touch.** On Brawl hit, target's armor partially vitrifies. Add ◼ (Boost) to next attack against that target (until end of target's next turn).
- **Geometric Eyes (maneuver).** Projects a beam of focused heat at Short range in a line. Coordination (Agility) ◆◆. Fail: 8 fire damage. Triumph: set target's gear on fire.
- **Shatter.** When defeated, explodes in a shower of glass shards. Characters at Engaged range: Coordination ◆ or 3 piercing damage.

## Tactical Notes

- They are tethered to residual mana. If the party retreats beyond Long range, Scorch-Wraiths return to feeding rather than pursue.
- They are not smart. They are, however, *coordinated* — three of them will screech and freeze simultaneously when a sibling Scar-wound opens nearby. See [[02-scene-the-rift-opens|Chapter 1 Scene 2]].
- Cold is the correct answer. Water is the second-correct answer.

## Lothari Lore

The Lothari call them *Sand-Ghosts* and avoid the areas where they are known to cluster. The wider Silent Network has been quietly rerouting caravans around the desert foothills east of [[Lux Æterna]] for the last year.
