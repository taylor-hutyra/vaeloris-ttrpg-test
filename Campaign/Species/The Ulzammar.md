# The Ulzammar

*The pre-First Age people who sang shape into matter and then broke the world doing it. The source of everything the campaign is about.*

## Summary

The Ulzammar were a people of Vaeloris before the First Age. They practiced **harmonic magic** — a form of sorcery in which sustained, precise musical tones could shape matter, alter time, or reach into adjacent realities. They built places no one alive remembers. They made artifacts that still work. They carved symbols (see [[The Three-Ring Symbol]] — forthcoming stub) that are chords rather than runes, meant to be performed rather than read.

They also **caused the Paroxysm** — the cataclysm at the close of the pre-First Age — when they attempted a harmonic working large enough to reach back to [[The Four]] after the Four withdrew from the world. The working did not reach the Four. It cracked the membrane of reality in dozens of places, created the first [[Scars of Dissonance]], and killed most of the Ulzammar in an instant.

The survivors scattered the **Crown of the Ulzammar** — three fragments they had forged as part of the working — and disappeared. Tradition holds that they are extinct.

Tradition is, in this case, convenient rather than correct.

## The Crown of the Ulzammar

Three pieces. The Ulzammar called them:

- **The Measure** — the Band. Time. *([[Ulzammar Band]] — recovered by the party in Chapter 1.)*
- **The Setting** — the Gem's mount. Systematization. *(Not yet recovered.)*
- **The Stone** — the Gem itself. Stillness. *(Not yet recovered.)*

And a secret fourth, unnamed element — *the silence at the chord's center.* What unmakes what the Three have made. [[Vorn]]'s grandmother knew the first three words. She would not speak the fourth.

## Relevance to the Campaign

- The [[Ulzammar Band]] is Ulzammar work.
- The three-ring symbol on the pedestal is Ulzammar notation.
- The ruin of Sorath is an Ulzammar site.
- [[The Sorath Scar]] is an Ulzammar wound.
- [[The Architect]] is **an Ulzammar construct** — or claims to be; the truth is more complicated. See [[The Architect]] for the GM-only version.

Every cosmological mystery in this campaign traces back to the Ulzammar.

## GM Notes

- **The Ulzammar are not fully extinct.** Survivors exist — deep hiding, reality-adjacent, profoundly not-mortal. They may become a factor in Act III. For now, treat them as historical.
- **Harmonic notation** recurs throughout the campaign. The party will not learn to read it without significant study and a Waldkyn Song-Ward master. The Architect will offer to teach them. Whether they accept is a choice.
