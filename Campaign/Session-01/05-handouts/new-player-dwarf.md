# PLAYER HANDOUT — The Dwarf

*For the dwarf player. Read before Session 1. Bring questions.*

---

## Who You Are

You are a dwarf of Vaeloris. Your people are pre-mortal — long-lived, older than most kingdoms, descended from the age of the Gods. Stone is your language. You remember your clan-lines the way other peoples remember songs.

Dwarves and elves are not enemies in this world. Your peoples took different paths — your people stayed, built, recorded; their people walked — but you understand each other. An elf whose boots have seen the same road as yours is, in a meaningful sense, a cousin.

You are a tank. You carry the weight at the front. Your shoulders know the shape of a pickaxe and a shield in equal measure.

## Why You Were in the Desert

You took work with a relic-hunting crew out of **Lux Æterna** — a hidden settlement in the Westward Sands, a refuge for people the Mage-Purge Wars have no use for. The foreman's name is **Torvin Dast.** He is a fair man. He hired you because he needed a pick-man who could cut straight. You liked him.

You had been with the crew for six days when it happened.

You hit the deep seam. The aether-quartz was richer than anything you had ever seen. Underneath it, black stone. Stone that made hands go numb. You took the deep cut. Your third strike did not crack the stone. It **opened** it.

You fell. The hole was no deeper than your waist. You fell farther than that.

## What You Remember from the Other Side

You remember darkness that had weight. You remember the air being wrong — thicker, then thinner, then wrong in a way that does not have a word. You remember shapes moving toward you. You remember being certain you were about to die.

And you remember a song. A single sustained note, closer than you expected, from behind you. And then a hand, pulling you up.

When you woke, you were on the sand. The sun was on your face. An elf was kneeling beside you, dressing a wound you did not remember taking. He said: *"You're alive. I'm sorry about your crew."*

You have been walking with him since. You do not remember deciding to. He seems to know where he is going.

## What You Feel

**Grief, mostly.** Your crewmates — Torvin, Rozen, Maret, Tomas, Gennik — were not close friends. You worked with them six days. But you liked them. You liked Torvin. He kept a brass compass in his vest that his wife gave him, engraved *"Come home."* He showed it to you once. He was proud of her.

You have not spoken about any of this out loud. You do not know how.

**A song, faintly.** For the last day, you have been hearing something the others (the elf) call a song. You are not sure you are hearing the same one. Yours is quieter. But it is there. It is under your breath when you are not paying attention. You find yourself humming, and you didn't know you were doing it.

## Something Your Grandmother Said

When you were very small, your grandmother once sketched a symbol in ash on the hearthstone — three interlocking rings with a dark point at the center. She looked at it for a long time. Then she wiped it clean with her hand. She said, under her breath: *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."* She would not explain. She died when you were twelve. You have not thought about her in a long time.

If you see a symbol that looks like the one she drew — the GM will tell you. You will remember her.

## Your Starting Items

- A pickaxe from the mining crew (still usable as a weapon)
- Clan-marked boots (the elf noticed them — they are not standard Lux Æterna-issue)
- A small stone carved with your clan sigil, which you wear on a cord
- Your grandmother's name, which is yours to say or not say

## In the First Session

You will find your crew. They will be dead. This will hurt. Take the time you need.

You will go back to Lux Æterna. You will meet people who will become important to you, whether you want them to or not. You will meet something — someone — that will tell you your grief was not wasted. Listen carefully to how that makes you feel. Both parts of it.

## Things to Decide Before Play

- What is your name?
- What is your clan's name?
- What was your grandmother's name? Are you named after anyone?
- Why did you take this job? (Money? Running from something? Running toward something?)
- Of Torvin's crew, who did you like most? You do not need to share this with the party unless you want to.
