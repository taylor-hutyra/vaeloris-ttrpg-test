# PLAYER HANDOUT — The Aen'valar Wanderer

*For the elf player. Read before Session 1. Bring questions.*

---

## Who You Are

You are **Aen'valar** — one of the Wandering Elves of Vaeloris. Your people are pre-mortal, long-lived, and older than nations. You do not belong to any city. You have never built one. When your people speak of home, they mean a direction, not a place.

Dwarves and elves are not enemies in this world. Your people share the long memory of the time of the Gods. You understand each other's philosophies, even when you disagree about how to live. A dwarf whose boots are in the same dust as yours is, in a meaningful sense, a cousin.

## What Happened to You

Years ago — five, you think, though time has become strange — you joined a crew of rift-walkers. Their task was to enter a Scar of Dissonance and kill the fiend anchoring it. Your crew was good. Your crew was not good enough.

You were the only one who came back, and you did not come back clean.

You spent what felt like **six to ten years** inside the rift. Outside, only three or four passed. You survived because you learned to listen. The rift has its own rules; you learned them one at a time. You lost a companion to every rule you learned.

One of your crew, a Waldkyn Song-Ward apprentice, taught you the first trick that kept you alive: a specific sustained note, hummed into a shard of aether-quartz, that creates a small field of tonal dissonance the lesser fiends cannot cross. She taught you the note and then died in the next ambush. You have been using the trick ever since. Your quartz is cracking. You have **seven uses left.**

When you finally emerged — two weeks ago, into the eastern desert — you were not the person who entered. You are darker now. More careful. Slower to trust. You do not sleep without waking twice a night. Your pupils are dilated. Sunlight hurts for the first hour of every morning. You are not a villain. You are a man who has seen things and found a way to keep walking.

## Why You Are Here Now

For the last four days, you have been hearing a **song.** A single tone, sustained, threading through the ambient noise of the world. It is not a song you know. It is a song you have been walking toward because it is the only thing in your life right now that feels like it has a shape.

Two days ago, the song got loud. You were near a thin-place — a seam between realities — and you heard someone on the other side. A dwarf, falling. You reached him. You sang your anchor-note. You bought four heartbeats and used them to throw you both through the same seam.

You came out in the desert with a half-dead dwarf across your shoulders. You have been walking west since. The song is loudest in the direction of a camp you can smell burning.

## Your Starting Items

- Your anchor (7 uses remaining)
- Travel gear — weathered, incomplete
- A small knife that is much older than you look
- A pouch containing a single braided strand of hair you do not talk about

## The Dwarf

He was the dwarf you rescued. He owes you his life. You owe him nothing, but you carried him out, and that means something. You have been watching him since. He is quiet. He is grieving something he has not named yet. You respect that.

## The Singing

You are the only person who can hear the song. Tell your GM privately, at any time, if the song changes — gets louder, quieter, changes pitch, stops. The song has its own rules. You are still learning them.

## In the First Session

You will arrive at a camp at a significant moment. You will lead the party to what is singing. You will meet someone — something — that feels familiar and wrong at the same time. Pay attention. You know what the rift felt like. Compare.

## Things to Decide Before Play

- What is your name?
- What is the last thing you remember from before the rift?
- What is one thing you lost in there that was not a person?
- What do you call the song you are following?
