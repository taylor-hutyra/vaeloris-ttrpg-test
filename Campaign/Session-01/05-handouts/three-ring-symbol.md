# HANDOUT — The Three-Ring Symbol

**Context:** Carved into the stone pedestal Torvin Dast's crew found on Day 4. Branded (invisibly, but the party will feel it) into the metal of the Ulzammar Band. Sketched — badly — in the margin of Torvin's journal, Day 5.

---

## Description (read aloud when a PC examines it)

> *Three rings, interlocked — but not like chain-links. Each ring passes through both of the others simultaneously, which is the sort of thing that seems simple until you try to draw it yourself.*
>
> *The rings aren't perfect circles. They're slightly flattened, like they've been pulled at from three directions and held there. Where the three cross at the center, the carver went back with something finer and gouged out a single deep point — or perhaps that point was always there and the rings were drawn around it.*
>
> *If you follow one ring with your eye, your eye wants to keep going. Around, and under, and around. It takes a small effort to stop.*

---

## Torvin's Sketch (what the players see on paper)

*(Draw, or describe: three overlapping rings in the shape of three Olympic rings — simple interlock, not the true Borromean topology. Underneath, in Torvin's hand:)*

> *"This is not right. They go THROUGH each other. I can't make my hand do it."*

---

## Visual Reference for the GM

If you want to draw or print the real symbol for the table:

- Search "Borromean rings" as a starting reference. The true symbol is this — three rings where each passes through both others in a way that cannot be separated without cutting.
- Modify: slightly flatten the rings (they should look tensioned, not perfect).
- Add: a deep gouged point at the center where all three cross.
- The carved version on the pedestal is perfect. Torvin's attempt is not.

---

## Mechanical Note

Any PC who studies the real symbol for more than a few seconds: **Discipline (Willpower), Easy ◆.** On failure, they feel mildly dizzy and lose their place in whatever they were saying.

Dwarven PCs who roll **Knowledge (Lore), Hard ◆◆◆**: recall a grandmother or clan-elder who once sketched this symbol in ash, then wiped it clean and said *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."* She never said what the fourth was.

---

## What It Actually Is (GM ONLY)

The symbol is a **chord**, not a rune. It represents three specific tonal frequencies that, when produced simultaneously, are the "name" of the Crown of the Ulzammar. Each ring corresponds to one Crown piece — Band (Time), Setting (Systematization), Gem (Stillness). The central gouged point is **the silence at the chord's center** — the Fourth, which unmakes what the Three have made. The Ulzammar carved this symbol at every site touching their work. It is not a warning. It is a signature.

The Architect knows this. Tajsh does not. Nobody in the party does, yet.
