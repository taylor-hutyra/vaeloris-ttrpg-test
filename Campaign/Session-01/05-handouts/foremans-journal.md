# HANDOUT — The Foreman's Journal

> *A leather-bound book, sand-stained, brass clasp. Found in the vest pocket of Torvin Dast, foreman of the mining crew. His handwriting is cramped and practical. Read in order. Some entries are the mundane complaints of a working man. Some are not.*

---

**Day 1** — Left Lux Æterna at first light. Six of us. Tajsh wants the vein scouted in ten days or we don't get paid the bonus. Rozen is already complaining about his boots.

**Day 2** — Good ground. Water at a rock cistern two hours before camp. Rozen complaining about hardtack again. Told him he can eat sand. Maret laughed. She never laughs.

**Day 3** — [Dwarf] says his back is tighter than usual. Says it's the air. Tomas says it's because [Dwarf] is old. [Dwarf] threatened to show him how old. Good crew. I like this crew.

**Day 4** — Detoured east around a rockfall. Lost half a day. Hit a dust wall around midday and took shelter in an old mine entrance cut into the canyon wall — pre-collapse construction, could be Varr stonework, hard to tell. Waited inside about an hour.

**Day 4 (continued)** — While sheltering, Rozen found a crack in the back wall. Cave-in had widened it at some point. Squeezed through with him and Maret out of curiosity. What we found wasn't a mine shaft.

Long corridor — cut stone, very old, not natural. Opened into a large chamber. High ceiling, mostly rubble. In the center: a chasm. Fifteen feet across, maybe more. Dark all the way down. Across it: a stone pedestal. On the pedestal, a book, open. Something else in the center of the book. Couldn't make it out. Then the bats.

Not small bats. Torso-sized. We ran. Rozen twisted his ankle. Got out of there.

**Day 5** — Rozen limping. Slowing us. Symbol on that pedestal keeps coming back to me. Three rings. Interlocking. I tried to draw it. Couldn't get it right. The rings won't sit still on the page.

*(Margin sketch — three overlapping rings, drawn badly. Underneath: "This is not right. They go THROUGH each other. I can't make my hand do it.")*

**Day 6** — Quicksand stretch. Underground river surfacing. Maret to the knee. Rope-and-post, everyone across. Sand was warm. Didn't like that.

**Day 7** — Arrived at the site. Crystal is real money. Sent the bird back to Tajsh with the tag. [Dwarf] cut the first shaft. He's humming today. He never hums. Asked what the tune was. He said he didn't realize he was doing it.

**Day 8** — Strange feeling. Like someone standing behind you. Turned around twice at dinner — nothing there. Rozen said the same. Maret says it's the bat business.

**Day 9** — Tomas broke a tooth on hardtack. I owe him a drink in Lux Æterna if we make it back in time for the Lantern's Wednesday stew. He says I owe him two drinks. I said one. We are agreed on one.

**Day 10** — Rich vein. Purest I've seen. Something under it, though — dark stone that doesn't feel right. Tomas touched it. His hand went numb for an hour. Keeping that out of the official report until we're back.

**Day 11** — Nobody's sleeping. Rozen hears footsteps. I hear them too. [Dwarf] says the humming in his head is louder now. He says it is not his humming. He said that twice in a row and didn't notice he said it twice.

**Day 12** — [Dwarf] went down the shaft this morning. Took the deep cut like he asked to. Heard him shout. Heard the stone break. Dust settled and the hole he made is no deeper than his waist. He isn't in it. He is not anywhere. I put my arm into the hole up to the shoulder. There is nothing in the hole. The hole is not a hole.

**Day 12 (later)** — Rozen won't stop crying. Maret won't speak. Tomas is sitting on a crate with his knife out. I told them we leave at first light. We are not miners anymore. We are witnesses.
