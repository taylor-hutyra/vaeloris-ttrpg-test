## Scorch-Wraith (Rival, run as group of 3)

**Type:** Aberration
**Wounds:** 8 | **Strain:** 6 | **Soak:** 3 | **Defense:** 0/0

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 1 |

**Skills:** Brawl 2, Perception 1
**Equipment:** Glass claws — Brawl; Damage 6; Crit 3; Range Engaged; **Burn 1**

### Abilities

- **Immune to Fire.** No damage from fire sources.
- **Vulnerable to Cold.** +2 damage from cold. Also disables *Geometric Eyes* for 1 round.
- **Blindsight.** No penalties from darkness, smoke, or visual obscurement.
- **Glass-Touch.** On a successful Brawl hit, target's armor partially vitrifies — add ◼ (Boost) to the next attack against that target (lasts until end of target's next turn).
- **Geometric Eyes (maneuver).** Projects a beam of focused heat at Short range in a line. Coordination (Agility) check, ◆◆. On failure: 8 fire damage. On Triumph: set target's gear on fire.
- **Shatter.** When defeated, explodes in glass shards. Characters at Engaged range: Coordination ◆, fail = 3 piercing damage.

## The Ulzammar Band

**Type:** Artifact, First Age, Crown Fragment (1 of 3)

### Appearance

Circlet of dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins like lightning frozen mid-strike. Cool to the touch. Weighs almost nothing. Eyes want to slide off.

### Passive Effects (near the Band)

- **Temporal thickening.** Within Short range: dropped objects fall ~1.5× slower. Flames flicker in slow motion. Liquids pour reluctantly. Hearts seem to lag half a beat behind breath.
- **Stutter field.** Within Long range: enchantments behave unpredictably. Mage-Weave stutters. Mirror-charms flicker. Alchemy reactions delay or accelerate. Water near it tastes metallic.
- **Pull.** If left behind, the Band appears in the nearest PC's pack by the next morning. It wants to be carried.

### Touch Check (first time touching)

**Discipline (Willpower), ◆◆.** See [beat-3-band-and-journal.md](../03-scenes/beat-3-band-and-journal.md) for results table.

### Properties the Party Will Learn Later

- Slows aging nearby. (Not known Session 1.)
- Is the **Time** piece of the Crown of the Ulzammar. (Not known Session 1.)

## The Aether-Quartz Anchor (Elf's Item)

**Type:** Personal, semi-magical, expendable.

### Description

Palm-sized shard of raw aether-quartz wrapped in sinew. Pale blue, cracked, warm. The elf wears it on a cord around his neck or in a hand-pouch at his belt.

### Use

**Action (1/scene):** The elf hums a specific sustained note into the quartz. For **3 rounds**, fiends and Scar-touched entities within Short range treat him and one adjacent ally as if behind a Waldkyn Song-Ward:

- Cannot cross to Engage.
- Ranged attacks against protected targets add ◆◆ (Setback).

After use, the quartz cracks visibly.

### Charges

**7 uses remaining** at session start. When the last use is spent, the shard shatters permanently. He will need to find or craft another.

### Narrative Notes

- The note he sings is something he learned from a dead Waldkyn Song-Ward apprentice in his rift-crew.
- Aether-quartz from this session's mining pit would be usable to craft a new anchor. A **Knowledge (Arcana) or Mechanics, Hard ◆◆◆** plus a week's work. He would need the raw crystal and the right tuning device.
- Tajsh would pay good Sparks for a crate of raw aether-quartz. Decision point for the party later in Act I.

## The Resonance Compass

**Type:** Artifact, gifted by the Architect in Beat 7.

### Appearance

Palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal. A slot in the center — the size of a thumb-tip — is empty. The slot has smooth, precise edges; it was made for a specific object.

### Known Properties (Session 1)

- **Hums** when the party is within 10 miles of a Crown fragment. Pitch climbs as they get closer.
- **Heats** (gently) in the hand of whoever is carrying it when a fragment is within Long range of them personally.
- **Synchronizes** with the Band when both are in the same room — subtle, but the elf's anchor-note will ring in sympathy with both.

### Unknown Properties (revealed later)

- **Vector.** At close range, begins to pull — physically — toward the nearest fragment.
- **Sings.** When all three fragments are present, it sings one clear triumphant note.
- **The Architect manifests through it.** This is the secret. Any perceptive check near a later Architect appearance — ◆◆◆◆ Perception — reveals the light originates from the Compass.
- **The slot** fits a **Resonance Disc.** The disc is in the sealed chamber of the Ruin of Sorath, on the pedestal Torvin described. Retrieving it is Session 2's A-plot.

## The Foreman's Compass (Brass)

**Type:** Sentimental item.

Small brass pocket-compass. Engraved on the back: *"Come home."* Gift from Torvin's wife Esha. Still works. Points roughly north.

**Mechanical benefit if carried:** ◼ Boost on any Survival check to navigate in the desert. *"Torvin taught me to read it"* — whoever carries it is implicitly remembering him.
