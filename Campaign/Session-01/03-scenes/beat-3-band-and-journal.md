# Beat 3 — The Band and the Journal

**Budget: 15 minutes**
**Location:** The Mining Camp — the mining pit, and Torvin's body
**Characters present:** All 5 PCs

---

## Two Parallel Discoveries

Run these in whatever order the party chooses. Most parties will investigate the pit first (because the elf walks to it), find the Band, then return to the camp and find the journal. That's ideal pacing.

---

## The Pit and the Band

The elf leads the party to the mining pit without prompting. The ladder goes down into a roughly hewn excavation. At the bottom, a shaft continues another fifteen feet down, cut straight into black crystalline stone.

### Read-Aloud — Descent into the Shaft

> *The air changes as you climb down. It gets thicker — not warmer, not colder, just MORE. Your lantern flame slows until it's moving like it's underwater. Your own breath sounds far away. When you drop the last step of the ladder, the rope swings twice and then stops mid-arc and hangs there.*
>
> *In the wall of the shaft: a circlet of dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins. Half-embedded, like the stone grew up around it. Looking at it is like looking at something you're not supposed to see — your eyes want to slide off.*

### The Touching Check

If a PC touches the Band: **Discipline (Willpower), Average ◆◆.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Success** | They feel the sensation but identify it as external. *Absolute certainty. The feeling of gears meshing. A single coherent thought: "Incomplete."* They can set it down. |
| **Failure** | The thought feels like their own. They want to keep holding it. A second check, ◆◆, to voluntarily put it down. |
| **Triumph** | Success + they glimpse three interlocking crystal shapes for a heartbeat. |
| **Despair** | Failure + Staggered until end of their next turn. |

The Band is cool to the touch and weighs almost nothing. It does not resist being picked up. It does not want to be put down.

### The Elf's Reaction

When the Band is held up to the light, the elf **stops hearing the song.** It goes silent for the first time in four days. Describe it to the player privately: *"The sound that has been in your head since you came out of the rift — it stops. The silence is louder than the song was."*

He does not know what this means. Let the player decide what to do with that.

## The Journal (in Torvin's vest)

When the party returns to camp or a PC investigates the bodies:

### Read-Aloud — Finding It

> *The foreman is older than the others. Fifty-something. Weathered. He died trying to cover someone smaller — a man collapsed underneath him, legs twisted, ankle wrapped in a dirty bandage. The foreman's vest has been partly burned. A leather corner pokes out of an inner pocket. You pull it free. A journal, sand-stained, with the brass clasp still closed.*
>
> *His right hand is still clenched. When you open it, there's a small brass compass inside. Engraved, very small, on the back: "Come home."*

### Running the Journal

**Give the players the printed journal handout** ([05-handouts/foremans-journal.md](../05-handouts/foremans-journal.md)) OR read it aloud. Do not summarize. The cadence of the entries is doing emotional work.

Read straight through, Day 1 to Day 13. It takes about four minutes. Let the table sit in silence while you do it.

**If the dwarf PC is present while this is read:** he hears his crewmates' names. He hears *himself* in Day 7 (*"He's humming today. He never hums"*) and Day 11 (*"He said it is not his humming"*) and Day 12 (*"He is not anywhere"*). Watch the player's face. That's what the beat is for.

### The Symbol in the Margin

When you reach Day 5, pull out the Symbol handout ([05-handouts/three-ring-symbol.md](../05-handouts/three-ring-symbol.md)) and put it on the table. Say:

> *"Torvin tried to draw the symbol. He couldn't get it right. Here's his attempt. This is what he saw on the pedestal."*

Any PC who studies the symbol: **Discipline (Willpower), Easy ◆.** On failure, they feel mildly dizzy and lose their place in whatever they were saying.

---

## The Dwarf's Lore Check

If the dwarf PC wants to roll on whether he recognizes anything (the Band, the symbol, the stone): **Knowledge (Lore), Hard ◆◆◆.**

**On success:** His grandmother once sketched this symbol in ash, then wiped it clean and said *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."* She never said what the fourth was. She was afraid.

**On Triumph:** He also remembers a word she said under her breath — *"Ulzammar."* He doesn't know what it means. It is the first time he has thought of it in forty years.

---

## The Choice

At the end of Beat 3, the party has:
- The Band
- The journal
- The brass compass (small personal item — let the dwarf carry it if he wants)
- Five bodies to bury or leave

**Bury or leave?** Let them decide. Burying takes an hour and adds to the session clock. Leaving them earns a quiet grief beat on the ride back. Both are correct.
