# Beat 6 — Tajsh's Revelation

**Budget: 10–15 minutes**
**Location:** Tajsh's Office, Lux Aeterna
**Characters present:** All 5 PCs + Tajsh

---

## Setup

Morning. Tajsh has read the journal. He has not slept. He has the Band on his desk, wrapped in dark cloth. The candle is across the room, behind him. He is waiting for the party.

## Read-Aloud — Opening

> *Tajsh's office is small. Desk. Two chairs. A kettle that is whistling and being ignored. The Band is on the desk, wrapped in dark wool. He's not looking at it.*
>
> *He gestures at the chairs. Only two, for five of you. He waits to see who sits down. You notice he is watching the new ones, the elf and the dwarf, with a particular flat attention. He does not ask them their names. He will, eventually. Not yet.*
>
> *He picks up the journal. Sets it down. Picks it up again. Says, without looking up:*
>
> *"I've seen that symbol twice in my life."*

## The Monologue — Deliver Slowly

Read this aloud. Do not paraphrase. Slow down on the pauses.

> *"Once, when I was twenty-two. Shadewalker archive. Sealed crate. We weren't allowed to touch it. Which meant I spent three hours memorizing everything I could see through the slats of the lid. There was a symbol burned into the lid. Three rings, interlocked. I tried to draw it later. I couldn't get it right. Neither could Torvin."*
>
> *(He taps the journal.)*
>
> *"Second time — seventeen years ago. Ruin in the Barren Hills. I was paid a great deal of money to forget I had been there. The symbol was carved into the wall of a room my employer did not want anyone to enter. He did not enter it either. He paid me to carve it shut and walk away. I did."*
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> *"Both times, everyone in the room treated it the way you treat a cobra. Very still. Very careful. Nobody wanted to be the one who reached for it."*
>
> *(He looks up. At the returning PCs. Then at the new ones.)*
>
> *"That book — whatever's on the pedestal Torvin found — I need it. I need you to go back to that temple and bring it to me. And before any of you asks why: I don't know yet. I know that the kind of people who use that symbol don't leave things in abandoned mines by accident. They leave them there for someone to find. I'd like to know who they were hoping to find. And I'd like to know before whoever they were hoping to find gets here first."*

## What Tajsh Says If Asked

**"What were the Shadewalkers so afraid of?"**
> *"I never found out. The crate was gone by the next month. The briefing file I tried to pull after was missing. I stopped asking."*

**"Who was your employer at the Barren Hills?"**
> *"A name I don't say out loud. Sorry. Not today. Ask me again in a year."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because you found it. Because whatever this is has decided you exist. And because I trust you more than I trust anyone else in this settlement right now, and that's not a compliment, it's arithmetic."*

**"What about the Band?"**
> *"Stays here. In this room. In this wool. I don't want it on the road. Whoever is hunting things like this will find it faster if it's moving. Get me the book. I'll get us someone who can read it."*

**"The dwarf and the elf — what about them?"**
> *(He looks at them.)* *"You came out of a hole in the world. Your friend walked out of a rift that shouldn't be there. You're either part of this or you're worse than part of this. Either way, you go with them. I want you where I can see you. Welcome to Lux Aeterna."*

## The New Arrivals — Formal Ish

Tajsh asks the new PCs for their names. Writes them in his ledger. Asks: *"Next of kin? Anyone we should notify if something happens out there?"*

**This is the Sabel ledger beat displaced onto Tajsh.** Play it the same way. He's not asking because he wants to know. He's asking because someone has to write the name down.

## Interruption — The Flicker

Just as the party is about to leave Tajsh's office:

> *The lantern in the corner — the one you hadn't been paying attention to — goes out. Not dims. OUT. At the same time, the lantern over the door goes out. And outside, through the window, you see every lantern in the canyon go out at the exact same moment. And then, one second later, come back on, brighter than they should be, and settle.*
>
> *One heartbeat. The mirror-charms dropped.*
>
> *Tajsh is already on his feet. He is looking at Sabel through the window, and Sabel is running toward the outlook. Tajsh says, quietly: "That was bad. That was very bad. Stay here."*
>
> *From the mouth of the Warrens, you can hear Cira humming — louder than she was yesterday, louder than she has ever been heard to hum — a single sustained note that is climbing, slowly, in pitch.*

---

## Transition to Beat 7

The party will go to Cira. Or they will stay. Either way, Cira comes to find them. She stops at the Warrens mouth. She says the line.

> *"I cannot follow you where this goes. Listen carefully. Doubt generously. Come back."*

**Descend.** Cut to Beat 7.
