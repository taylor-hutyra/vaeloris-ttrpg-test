# Beat 7 — The Architect

**Budget: 25–30 minutes** (the session's centerpiece)
**Location:** The Deep Chamber, via the Warrens
**Characters present:** All 5 PCs + the Architect (+ Cira bookending)

**Prep notes before running:** re-read [the-architect.md](../01-npcs/the-architect.md) and [deep-chamber.md](../02-locations/deep-chamber.md). The scene has **four phases** — Entry, the Naming, the Message, the Questions.

---

## Phase 1 — Entry (5 min)

The party descends the Warrens. Use the Descent read-aloud from [deep-chamber.md](../02-locations/deep-chamber.md).

When **the last PC** crosses the threshold of the chamber, read the Transition block from [deep-chamber.md](../02-locations/deep-chamber.md) — the *"the stone behind you is no longer there"* passage.

**Then pause.** Let the silence sit. Let the players speak first, or don't. When you are ready, deliver the Architect's first line:

> *"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."*

Its voice is not loud. It does not need to be. It is the only sound there is.

---

## Phase 2 — The Naming (5–7 min)

The Architect addresses each PC in turn. It names them. It says something true and specific about them. **Adapt to each PC's background.** Use the table in `_meta/the-broken-harmonies.md` (lines 1242–1258) for returning PCs.

### For the Aen'valar Elf

> *"Child of the long silence. You have walked closer to the truth of things than any of your companions. What did you find, there, in the rooms that are not rooms? You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. That is why the song found you first."*

### For the Dwarf

> *"Son of the stone-line. You have a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You know what it is. You felt it when you struck the seam — not fear of falling, but recognition. You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

### GM Reminder

Deliver these as **relief, not threat.** The Architect is seeing these people clearly. Every word is tuned to lower their defenses. Let that land. The wrongness is for other beats. This beat is for being seen.

After the Naming, pause. Let the players react. Some will push back. Some will sit in it. Both are correct.

---

## Phase 3 — The Message (5 min)

The Architect delivers its six beats. Run them as continuous dialogue, not a list:

1. *"The object you carry — what you call the Band — is a fragment of an instrument. Once whole, it was used to reshape the world. It nearly ended. The fragments were scattered to prevent that ending. Two more remain."*
2. *"I do not know where they are. Fifteen hundred years of watching, and even I cannot see everything. The fragments were hidden by gods. They are hidden from me too."*
3. *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left behind to watch. To wait. To speak when there was finally someone to speak to."*
4. *"I have prepared something for you."*

> *A plinth of folded light rises in front of the party. On it, revealed: a palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal, slotted in the center. It hums. The elf hears the note he has been following — except now it is in harmony with itself, and before it was alone.*

5. *"This is a Compass. It will sing when a fragment is near. But it cannot tell you where to begin. You must first learn where to look. Go north. The Dragon's Tooth pierces the sky. At its peak, there stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the stars as mortals read letters. Fragments disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark."*
6. *"Once all three are gathered, they must be brought to a wound in the world, and there they will be unmade. Only there. Nowhere else. That is the task."*

Do not hurry. Let the plinth of light stay in the space. The Compass is on it. A PC can take it at any time — it will not resist.

---

## Phase 4 — The Questions (10–15 min)

**Tell your players, in your GM voice:** *"It has said what it came to say. It is waiting. You can ask it anything."*

Below are prepared answers. If the players ask something not here, follow two principles:
1. Answers sound true and are *mostly* true.
2. The Architect never answers the one question they didn't think to ask.

### Dialog — Prepared Answers

**"What are you?"**
> *"A servant. When the Four turned their hands from the world, some were left behind to watch the places that could not be left unwatched. I am what remains of that watching. I am not a god. I do not want worship. I want the world to continue."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because the Band has answered your hand. Because those of you who carry wounds know how to walk with them — the unwounded do not know how to carry things that ought not be carried. And because you are HERE. Gods plan. Mortals arrive. I have learned to work with what arrives."*

**"Are you good?"**
> *"I am useful. Goodness is a mortal accounting, and the accounts are kept by those who survive to keep them. I was left here to prevent a catastrophe. That is the only measure I am prepared to answer for. When this is done, you will decide whether what I did was good. That is your right, not mine."*

**"What is the thing that will come if we fail?"**
> *"A song that the world cannot survive being sung. You would not understand the name of it. The best of my language can describe it only as HUNGER GIVEN FORM. It sleeps. The fragments, gathered and wrongly used, would wake it. Gathered and rightly unmade, they would silence it forever."*

**"Why can't you just take the Band yourself?"**
> *"Because I am not here. What you see is a shape my attention is taking, in a place my attention has reached. I cannot lift a stone. I cannot kill your enemies. I can watch, and speak, and prepare the ground. The rest is for hands that bleed."*

**"How do we know you're telling the truth?"**
> *"You do not. You never will, not entirely. Any test I suggest is a test I have prepared for. This is the oldest problem. I will offer what I can: I have told you nothing tonight that you can verify immediately. Go north. Find the observatory. See whether the stars say what I say they say. If they do not, come back and name me a liar. I will still be waiting."*

**To the Elf: "Have you been in the rifts?"**
> *"I know what is in them. I know what you did not let them take from you. That knowledge is not the same as the living of it. You have something I do not. It is why I can ask this of you and not of myself."*

**To the Dwarf: "Did you kill my crew?"**
*(Pause. Let the table feel it.)*
> *"No. What killed your crew was already walking the thin places long before I knew your name. I could not reach them. I could not reach you, until the seam opened. If I had reached further, sooner — perhaps. But regret is a door I cannot afford to open. Yours is a grief I do not know how to carry for you. I am sorry."*

**"What about the ones hunting us?"**
> *"The Legio Null will come. Soon. Your settlement was visible from the sky for one heartbeat. That is enough. You must leave this place, and soon. I cannot stop the hunt. I can only tell you to RUN."*

**"Will we see you again?"**
> *"When the Compass sings, I will be near. Not always in a form your eyes can hold. But near."*

**Coercion or threat of any kind:**
*(Calm. Slightly amused.)*
> *"You are holding a Band you cannot control in a settlement your enemies will find within the week. I am the hand offered to you in the dark. If you prefer another, seek it. I will not chase you. I will be here if you return."*

### Dialog — Skill Checks Available

Any player can ask to roll. Offer these if a player tells you what they're trying to do:

| Intent | Check | Difficulty | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Notice something wrong | **Perception (Cunning)** | ◆◆ | No reflection of the Architect in the floating crystal symbols. Nothing else in the space has this problem. |
| Read beneath its composure | **Perception (Cunning)** | ◆◆◆ | Anticipation when it speaks of "unmaking" — not relief. *Triumph:* also hunger. |
| Read its magical signature | **Knowledge (Arcana)** | ◆◆◆ | Divine on surface, ABYSSAL underneath. Perfume over rot. Overlay is skilled. Wrongness is not. |
| Observe the environment | **Knowledge (Nature)** | ◆◆ | A moth drifted into the space with you. It is paused mid-flight. Not dead. Paused. |
| Read the floating symbols | **Knowledge (Lore)** | ◆◆◆◆ | A compositional notation — music written for frequencies no mortal throat can produce. The three-ring mark is a CHORD, not a rune. *Triumph:* Torvin's sketch was a degraded version of one of these. |
| Resist the emotional pull | **Discipline (Willpower)** | ◆◆ | Recognize the Naming as *too* tailored. A therapist would call this skill. A spy would call it targeting. |
| Coax out more information | **Charm or Negotiation (Presence)** | ◆◆◆ | The Architect reveals one concrete detail beyond its plan. GM picks: (a) the name of the observatory's last living custodian — **Brenn Hallowveil** — or (b) a Waldkyn Song-Ward master in the mountains who could train the elf's anchor. |
| Mislead the Architect | **Deception (Cunning)** vs | ◆◆◆◆ | It appears to believe. A half-beat later, one sentence betrays that it did not. Only the rolling player notices. |
| Threaten, demand, coerce | **Coercion (Willpower)** | ◆◆◆◆◆ | Fails. Architect is amused, not moved. *"You are welcome to try. I have been threatened by better than you and was not moved. Sit down. Or do not. I am not in a hurry."* |

## Phase 5 — Exit (2 min)

When the questions run out, or when the tension has crested, the Architect says:

> *"Go north. Take the Band. Take the Compass. Trust the people who love you. Do not trust me further than you have to. This is the only honest thing I will say tonight."*

Then describe the collapse — read slowly:

> *The geometry folds inward. The planes rotate, then dim. The center-point you have been speaking to contracts to a single point of light, and then that point is gone. You blink.*
>
> *The torch is flickering again. The stone behind you is stone. The floor is under your boots. The Compass is warm in someone's hand.*
>
> *You turn. Cira is at the mouth of the tunnel. She looks at you. She says:*
>
> *"How long were you gone?"*
>
> *One of you answers, 'A few minutes.'*
>
> *She says, 'It has been three hours. I could not see you. I was not sure you were coming back. How do you feel?'*

---

## End the Session

Ask each player, **in character, one sentence only**: *How do you feel?*

Go around the table. Each player, one sentence. No discussion, no follow-ups. When the fifth player has spoken:

> *That's where we leave it for tonight. Good game, everyone.*

End.
