# Beat 1 — The Scorch-Wraith Combat

**Budget: 15–20 minutes**
**Location:** The Mining Camp, mid-combat
**Characters present:** The 3 returning PCs only. New PCs arrive in Beat 2.

---

## Where We Pick Up

The party's mini-session ended mid-combat. Three Scorch-Wraiths (Rival) are still standing. The PCs have taken some strain, little damage. One tent is burning. The smell of superheated glass is everywhere.

## Run It Fast

**Do not play out every round.** This is the opening beat of a long session. Push to a finish in three rounds, maximum.

## Round 1 — The Push

- Wraith A uses **Geometric Eyes** on the highest-Defense PC. Force the Coordination ◆◆. On failure: 8 fire damage.
- Wraith B engages the nearest PC. Glass claws (Brawl, damage 6, crit 3, Burn 1).
- Wraith C moves into the fire-pit and **feeds** (recovers 2 strain). Position it as a target the party will want to take out before it recovers further.

## Round 2 — The Turn

- Whichever PC lands a significant hit (6+ damage) triggers **Shatter** on the dying wraith — shards fly, 3 piercing damage at Engaged range, Coordination ◆ to avoid.
- Narrate the death theatrically: *"The wraith folds inward, once, and then comes apart in a cloud of glass the size of your torso. You duck. Someone doesn't."*
- If a PC is still at full wounds, consider having one wraith's *Geometric Eyes* score a Triumph — set their pack on fire. Cost: 10 Sparks of gear, a rations loss, or a bandolier of alchemy vials ruptures (creates one of the burning pitch pools).

## Round 3 — The Finish

- Narrate the final wraith's destruction regardless of mechanical outcome if the fight is dragging. Let a PC have the kill line. Ask them: *"You have the wraith at your mercy. How do you finish it?"*

**If the PCs are creative** (cold water, ice, shoving one wraith into another to trigger Shatter, etc.) — reward it. Add a Boost die. Grant an automatic success.

## Tactical Reminders

- **Immune to fire.** Fire attacks and burning pitch don't hurt them.
- **Vulnerable to cold.** Cold damage deals +2, AND disables their eye-beam for 1 round.
- **Blindsight.** Smoke, darkness, illusion — no penalty.
- **Burn 1 on claws.** Track burn — it deals 1 additional damage at start of target's next turn unless extinguished.

## Post-Combat Narration

When the last wraith shatters:

> *Silence. Real silence. The kind the desert usually makes — wind, sand, distant stone — is gone for a moment. The pools of pitch are still burning. Your breathing is loud.*
>
> *Then, a quarter-mile east, something under the ruin pulses. A single sub-audible thump you feel in your teeth. And the crack in the rock at the eastern edge of the outcrop — the one you didn't notice before — widens.*

**That is the cue for Beat 2.** Pause. Let the players register it. Then cut to Beat 2.

## Wounds & Strain Between Beats

Do not heal anyone. Let them feel the combat's cost into Beats 2 and 3. Strain recovery begins at the start of Beat 4 (the travel back).

## XP for this Beat

**5 XP per PC** — award at end of session, not now.
