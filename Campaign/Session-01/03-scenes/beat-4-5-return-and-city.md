# Beats 4 & 5 — The Return and the City That Is Off

**Budget: 30–35 minutes combined (5–10 for travel, 20–25 for city)**
**Location:** Desert road → Lux Aeterna
**Characters present:** All 5 PCs

---

## Beat 4 — Travel Back (5–10 min)

This is a short, quiet beat. Use it to:

- Let the new PCs and old PCs exchange one or two lines of backstory.
- Let the elf or dwarf ask something personal of a returning PC, or vice versa.
- Recover strain. Full heal on strain, no wound recovery.

### Optional Short Prompt

Pick one of these and offer it to any player who seems ready:

- *"You're riding near the dwarf. He hasn't said anything in an hour. What do you say to him, or do you let him ride?"*
- *"The elf isn't sleeping. Second night in a row. Do you leave him alone, or do you go sit with him?"*
- *"Someone's going to have to explain to Tajsh what happened. Who's planning what to say?"*

Do not force roleplay. If the table wants to skip to arrival, cut to the city.

### Read-Aloud — Arrival

> *The sun has been down an hour by the time you reach the slot canyon. You know the way in. The two sandstone pillars, the narrow walls, the quarter-mile of stone — and then the lanterns bloom out of the dark and Lux Aeterna is there, waiting for you.*
>
> *Except it isn't, exactly. The lanterns are flickering wrong. One, steady-steady, flicker, steady, flicker-flicker. The market is quieter than it should be. Someone is humming a single sustained note from the mouth of the Warrens.*

---

## Beat 5 — The City Is Off (20–25 min)

### The Tajsh Drop

The first stop is Tajsh's office. He takes the Band. Candle freezes mid-flicker. He sees it, moves the candle across the room, returns. Says:

> *"Give me tonight with this. Come back in the morning."*

If the party tries to tell him about the new arrivals: he looks at them. Says: *"They're with you? Fine. They eat with you. They sleep where you sleep. If they're what they say, we're lucky. If they're not — you're my trap."*

He does not mention the symbol yet. He has not read the journal yet. **Hold that for Beat 6.**

### The Vignette Menu

Offer the party the city. They have an evening and a morning before Tajsh calls them back. Let them roam. Run **two or three** of these, not all. Pick based on which PCs gravitate where.

See [01-npcs/vignette-npcs.md](../01-npcs/vignette-npcs.md) for full NPC write-ups.

| Location | NPC | The Wrongness | Length |
|---|---|---|---|
| **The Dusty Lantern** | Marta | Lanterns stuttering out of rhythm. Half-empty room. | 3–5 min |
| **Hask's Workshop** | Hask | Potion simmering for an hour, neither boils nor cools. | 3–5 min |
| **Ladder in the canyon** | Selin Voss | Mage-Weave stutters. Begs for reassurance. | 3–5 min |
| **The Well** | The crowd, Petra | Metallic water. Crowd forming. Petra accused. | 5–7 min |
| **Vault entrance** | Old Cira | Humming one note. *"Two of you are walking. Only one came back."* (to the elf) | 2–3 min |

### The Well Confrontation — Run This One

Even if the party skips the others, try to hit this one. It's the session's emotional pressure-test.

> *You hear raised voices at the well. Not arguing — one voice, loud, shouting at a small girl. "She's been doing this. I've seen the sparks on her fingers. Whatever's in the water, it's HER." A handful of people are standing around, not stopping him, not helping. Petra — ten years old, soot on her hands — is not crying. She is watching the man who is yelling at her. She looks very small.*

**The accuser is a frightened man, not a villain.** His name is **Oris Hallow.** His wife is sick. He thinks the water is what's making her sicker. He is wrong. He needs someone to blame. He has chosen Petra.

Give the party the room to:
- De-escalate with words (Charm ◆◆ or Negotiation ◆◆)
- Shut it down with authority (Coercion ◆◆◆)
- Remove Petra from the scene
- Explain the actual cause (the Band) — risky, but it works (**Knowledge — Arcana ◆◆** to articulate it convincingly)
- Do nothing and let Sabel arrive in two minutes (Sabel disperses the crowd, Petra goes inside, the tension stays)

**Consequence:** Petra remembers whichever PC helped her. If they did. That memory matters in two sessions.

### The Cira Beat (for the Elf)

If the elf passes Cira at the Vault entrance, she stops humming. Looks up. Says:

> *"Two of you are walking. Only one came back."*

She returns to humming. No clarification.

Let the player sit with it. This is a gift. Do not explain it.

### Ending Beat 5

When the vignettes feel complete — or when 25 minutes are up — cut to morning. Tajsh sends word. He wants them in his office.

---

## Transition to Beat 6

> *The morning is gray. The lanterns have stabilized. The settlement looks almost normal, except you can tell by the way people aren't quite looking at each other that last night wasn't normal either. Tajsh's door is closed. When he opens it, he looks like he hasn't slept.*

Cut to Beat 6.
