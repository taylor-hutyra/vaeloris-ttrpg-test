# Beat 2 — The Elf and the Dwarf Arrive

**Budget: 15–20 minutes**
**Location:** The Mining Camp — the eastern crack in the outcrop
**Characters present:** The 3 returning PCs, plus both new PCs arriving

---

## What the Players See

After the post-combat silence, the crack at the eastern edge of the outcrop widens. A figure stumbles out, dragging an unconscious dwarf.

## Read-Aloud — Emergence

> *The air near the crack ripples. Not heat-shimmer — something else. Like a reflection in water that hasn't finished settling.*
>
> *A figure stumbles through. Tall. Lean. Long black hair streaked pale at the temples. He is covered in dust and old blood. He is carrying another figure across his shoulders — a dwarf, unconscious, breathing shallow. One of the dwarf's boots is missing. The elf's tunic is singed along the left side. He is bleeding from his left palm where something has cut him recently.*
>
> *He takes three steps into the open air, sets the dwarf down, and drops to one knee. He looks up at you. His pupils are too dilated. He does not look surprised to see you.*
>
> *He says, in a voice that has not spoken aloud in a long time: 'Is this your camp? I'm sorry. We needed somewhere to come through.'*

---

## Running the Introductions

**Give each new player 2–3 minutes of spotlight.**

### The Elf's Player — Three Questions

Ask, in order:

1. *"What's the last thing you remember, before you emerged?"*
2. *"What's the worst thing that happened to you in the rift?"* (Optional — let them decline.)
3. *"What do you call the song you've been following?"*

These are prompts, not interrogations. The player's answers become canon for the character.

### The Dwarf's Player — Wake Up

He regains consciousness mid-scene. Ask:

1. *"You remember striking the stone. What was going through your head, right before it broke?"*
2. *"You remember a darkness. Was there a shape in it, or only the dark?"*
3. *"You recognize one of the bodies. Which one, and what did that person mean to you?"*

The third question is important — it ties the dwarf into the dead crew emotionally.

### The Returning PCs — Reactions

Let the existing players respond however they want. **Do not force trust.** If they're suspicious, lean into the suspicion — the elf looks wrong. If they're welcoming, let that be a choice they make deliberately.

Optional check: any returning PC can roll **Perception (Cunning), Average ◆◆** to notice —
- The elf's pupils are too dilated for the dim light.
- The blood on his tunic is at least three days old, but the cut on his palm is fresh.
- The dwarf's boots don't match the other crew members' — these are older, clan-marked, not standard Tajsh-issue.

## The Elf's Pitch — Why He's Here

When the returning PCs ask what he's doing here, run this beat as dialogue (paraphrase, don't read):

> *"I've been hearing a song for four days. I've been following it. It's been getting louder as I came west. When your friend" — he gestures at the dwarf — "broke the seam from the other side, I was close enough to hear him. I got him out. The song is in this camp. It's under the stone, over there."*
>
> *He points at the mining pit. He has not looked at the pit. He has not seen it. He just knows.*

This moment should be slightly eerie and also obviously sincere. **He is not lying.** He really can hear it. Let the returning PCs decide how to feel about that.

## The Dwarf's Recognition

Once conscious, the dwarf PC looks around the camp. He recognizes the bodies. Some of these were his crew.

- **Let him have a moment with Torvin.** Describe the brass compass in Torvin's hand. Let the player do what they want with it.
- **Let him find the journal.** He sees the leather edge in Torvin's vest. If he takes it, he brings it to the rest of the party. If he doesn't, another PC finds it in Beat 3.

## The Question of Trust

The returning PCs may want to know whether to let the strangers come with them. Offer a path: **Tajsh's call.** The elf and dwarf need to come to Lux Æterna either way — the elf is following the song, and the dwarf has nowhere else to go. Whether they're PARTY or PRISONERS is Tajsh's decision tomorrow. Tonight, they travel together.

---

## Transition to Beat 3

When the introductions feel done, say:

> *The sun is fully down. Whatever's under that pit is still humming. Whoever wants to investigate — the ladder's right there.*

Cut to Beat 3.
