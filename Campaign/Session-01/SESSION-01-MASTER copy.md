# SESSION 1 — THE DESERT GIVES NOTHING FOR FREE

*GM Master Document · Broken Harmonies · Act I · TA 412*

---

## SESSION AT A GLANCE

**Duration:** 2 to 2.5 hours
**Players:** 5 (3 returning, 2 new — Aen'valar elf, dwarf tank)
**XP target:** ~20 XP per PC

### The One-Sentence Pitch

*A relic-hunt goes wrong. Something in the sand is older than the gods. And the light that offers to help you is the thing you should be afraid of.*

### The Seven Beats

| # | Beat | Budget | Part |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | The Scorch-Wraith combat (finish what's running) | 15–20 min | I — Desert |
| 2 | The elf and dwarf arrive through the Scar seam | 15–20 min | I — Desert |
| 3 | The Band, the journal, the dead crew | 15 min | I — Desert |
| 4 | Travel back to Lux Aeterna | 5–10 min | II — City |
| 5 | The city is off — vignettes, contacts, wards | 20–25 min | II — City |
| 6 | Tajsh reads the journal — the symbol revelation | 10–15 min | II — City |
| 7 | The Architect — supernatural encounter + Compass | 25–30 min | III — Architect |

**Hard-cut point if time runs short:** end of Beat 6. Beat 7 becomes next session's cold open.

### The Emotional Arc

The session moves from **violence → wonder → unease → awe.**

- **Beats 1–3** — adrenaline and confusion. The world is physical and dangerous.
- **Beat 4** — deflation. Travel. Quiet.
- **Beat 5** — the settlement the players are supposed to love starts showing cracks. **This is the session's secret heart.** The thing that will be lost later must be *felt* first.
- **Beats 6–7** — the plot reveals its scale. The Architect does not arrive as an enemy. It arrives as a *relief.* That is the point.

### Five GM Reminders

1. **Read the journal entries aloud.** Don't summarize. The cadence does emotional work.
2. **Let the elf lead the party to the Band.** Give him narrative authority in Beat 3. He heard it first.
3. **Let Petra matter.** Every player who spends 30 seconds with her will remember her when she dies later.
4. **The Architect is warm.** Do not play it ominous. Ominous is the secondary signal. Primary signal: *relief, recognition, being-seen.*
5. **End on Cira's question.** Not a monologue, not a recap. Her question. Each player answers in one sentence. Stop.

# PART I — THE DESERT

**Runtime: ~50 minutes.** Beats 1–3. Location: the mining camp, three days east of Lux Aeterna.

---

## BEAT 1 — THE SCORCH-WRAITH COMBAT

**Budget: 15–20 minutes · 3 returning PCs only**

### Where We Pick Up

The party's mini-session ended mid-combat. Three Scorch-Wraiths are still standing. The PCs have taken some strain, little damage. One tent is burning. The smell of superheated glass is everywhere.

### Scene-Setting — The Mining Camp

A scarred half-circle of tents and equipment in the lee of a rocky outcrop. Six tents, fire-pit in the center, mining tools stacked neatly (Torvin was that kind of foreman). The aether-quartz pit is at the base of the outcrop. Everything has been done right. Everything is also on fire, or was, until the fire ran out of things to burn.

*Fuller layout in Appendix B. For combat, you need: the tent half-circle (cover everywhere), the fire-pit (center — wraiths feeding here), the mining pit (out of line-of-sight from fire-pit).*

### Scorch-Wraith — Combat Reference

**Rival · Aberration · run as group of 3**

| Brawn | Agility | Intellect | Cunning | Willpower | Presence |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 3 | 1 | 2 | 2 | 1 |

**Wounds:** 8 · **Strain:** 6 · **Soak:** 3 · **Defense:** 0/0
**Skills:** Brawl 2, Perception 1
**Glass claws:** Brawl · Damage 6 · Crit 3 · Engaged · **Burn 1**

**Abilities**

- **Immune to Fire.** No damage from fire sources.
- **Vulnerable to Cold.** +2 damage. Also disables *Geometric Eyes* for 1 round.
- **Blindsight.** No penalty from darkness, smoke, visual obscurement.
- **Glass-Touch.** On Brawl hit, target's armor partially vitrifies — add ◼ to next attack against them.
- **Geometric Eyes (maneuver).** Focused heat beam, Short range, line. Coordination ◆◆. Fail: 8 fire damage. Triumph: set target's gear on fire.
- **Shatter.** When defeated, explodes in shards. Engaged range: Coordination ◆ or take 3 piercing.

### Running the Combat — Push to a Finish

**Three rounds, maximum.** Do not play out every move. This is the opening of a long session.

**Round 1 — The Push.**
- Wraith A: *Geometric Eyes* on highest-Defense PC.
- Wraith B: engages nearest PC, claws.
- Wraith C: moves to fire-pit, feeds (recovers 2 strain). Make it an obvious target.

**Round 2 — The Turn.**
- When a wraith dies, narrate **Shatter** theatrically: *"The wraith folds inward, once, and comes apart in a cloud of glass the size of your torso. You duck. Someone doesn't."*
- If the party is fresh, consider a Triumph on *Geometric Eyes* — set a pack on fire, or rupture an alchemy vial (creating a pitch-fire pool: 3 damage/round, lasts 4 rounds).

**Round 3 — The Finish.**
- If it's dragging, narrate the last wraith's death and give a PC the kill line. Ask: *"You have the wraith at your mercy. How do you finish it?"*
- Creative play (ice, water, shoving wraith-into-wraith to trigger Shatter) earns Boost dice or auto-success.

### Post-Combat Narration — Read Aloud

> *Silence. Real silence. The kind the desert usually makes — wind, sand, distant stone — is gone for a moment. The pools of pitch are still burning. Your breathing is loud.*
>
> *Then, a quarter-mile east, something under the ruin pulses. A single sub-audible thump you feel in your teeth. And the crack in the rock at the eastern edge of the outcrop — the one you didn't notice before — widens.*

**Pause. Let the players register it. Then cut to Beat 2.**

No strain recovery yet. Let the cost of combat carry into the next scenes.

## BEAT 2 — THE ELF AND THE DWARF ARRIVE

**Budget: 15–20 minutes · all 5 PCs**

### Read-Aloud — Emergence

> *The air near the crack ripples. Not heat-shimmer — something else. Like a reflection in water that hasn't finished settling.*
>
> *A figure stumbles through. Tall. Lean. Long black hair streaked pale at the temples. He is covered in dust and old blood. He is carrying another figure across his shoulders — a dwarf, unconscious, breathing shallow. One of the dwarf's boots is missing. The elf's tunic is singed along the left side. He is bleeding from his left palm where something has cut him recently.*
>
> *He takes three steps into the open air, sets the dwarf down, and drops to one knee. He looks up at you. His pupils are too dilated. He does not look surprised to see you.*
>
> *He says, in a voice that has not spoken aloud in a long time:*
>
> *"Is this your camp? I'm sorry. We needed somewhere to come through."*

### Running the Introductions

**Give each new player 2–3 minutes of spotlight.** Do not rush this.

**Ask the elf's player, in order:**

1. *"What's the last thing you remember, before you emerged?"*
2. *"What's the worst thing that happened to you in the rift?"* *(Optional — allow a pass.)*
3. *"What do you call the song you've been following?"*

**The dwarf wakes mid-scene. Ask:**

1. *"You remember striking the stone. What was going through your head, right before it broke?"*
2. *"You remember a darkness. Was there a shape in it, or only the dark?"*
3. *"You recognize one of the bodies. Which one, and what did that person mean to you?"*

The third question anchors the dwarf to the dead crew emotionally. It matters later.

### The Returning PCs — Don't Force Trust

If suspicion: lean in. The elf *looks* wrong. If welcome: let that be a chosen generosity.

**Optional — Perception (Cunning) ◆◆** for any returning PC:
- Elf's pupils too dilated for the dim light.
- Blood on tunic is 3+ days old; palm cut is fresh.
- Dwarf's boots are clan-marked — not standard Tajsh-issue.

### The Elf's Pitch — Paraphrase, Don't Read

> *"I've been hearing a song for four days. Following it. It's been getting louder as I came west. When your friend" — he gestures at the dwarf — "broke the seam from the other side, I was close enough to hear him. I got him out. The song is in this camp. It's under the stone, over there."*
>
> *(He points at the mining pit. He has not looked at the pit. He has not seen it. He just knows.)*

**He is not lying.** He really can hear it. Let the players decide how to feel about that.

### The Dwarf's Recognition

Once conscious, the dwarf looks around. He recognizes the bodies. These were his crew.

- **A moment with Torvin.** The foreman is collapsed over another body. His right hand is clenched. Describe the brass pocket-compass when the dwarf opens it — engraved *"Come home."* Let the player do what they want with it.
- **The journal.** A leather corner pokes from Torvin's vest. If the dwarf takes it, he brings it to the group. If he doesn't, another PC finds it in Beat 3.

### The Question of Trust

If the returning players ask whether to let the strangers come with them, offer **Tajsh's call.** The elf is following the song; the dwarf has nowhere else to go. Whether they're PARTY or PRISONERS is Tajsh's decision tomorrow. Tonight, they travel together.

**Transition:**
> *The sun is fully down. Whatever's under that pit is still humming. Whoever wants to investigate — the ladder's right there.*

## BEAT 3 — THE BAND AND THE JOURNAL

**Budget: 15 minutes · all 5 PCs**

Two parallel discoveries. Most parties will investigate the pit first (the elf walks to it), find the Band, then return to the camp and find the journal. That's ideal pacing.

### The Pit and the Band

The elf leads the party to the mining pit without prompting. The ladder drops into a rough excavation. At the bottom, a shaft continues another fifteen feet, cut straight into black crystalline stone.

### Read-Aloud — Descent into the Shaft

> *The air changes as you climb down. It gets thicker — not warmer, not colder, just MORE. Your lantern flame slows until it's moving like it's underwater. Your own breath sounds far away. When you drop the last step of the ladder, the rope swings twice, stops mid-arc, and hangs there.*
>
> *In the wall of the shaft: a circlet of dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins. Half-embedded, like the stone grew up around it. Looking at it is like looking at something you're not supposed to see — your eyes want to slide off.*

### The Band — Inline Reference

**Ulzammar Band** · First Age Artifact · Crown Fragment (1 of 3)

- **Passive (Short range):** Dropped objects fall ~1.5× slower. Flames flicker in slow motion. Heartbeats seem to lag behind breath.
- **Passive (Long range):** Enchantments stutter. Mirror-charms flicker. Alchemy reactions delay. Water tastes metallic.
- **Pull:** If left behind, appears in the nearest PC's pack by morning.

### The Touching Check

Any PC touching the Band: **Discipline (Willpower), Average ◆◆.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Success** | Feels the sensation, identifies it as external. *Absolute certainty. Gears meshing. A single thought: "Incomplete."* Can set it down. |
| **Failure** | Thought feels like their own. Wants to keep holding it. Second check ◆◆ to voluntarily put it down. |
| **Triumph** | Success + glimpse of three interlocking crystal shapes for a heartbeat. |
| **Despair** | Failure + Staggered until end of next turn. |

The Band is cool, weighs almost nothing. Does not resist being picked up. Does not want to be put down.

### The Elf's Private Moment

When the Band is held up to the light, the elf's song **stops.** For the first time in four days. Describe it to the player privately:

> *"The sound that has been in your head since you came out of the rift — it stops. The silence is louder than the song was."*

He does not know what this means. Let the player decide what to do with that.

---

### The Journal — Found in Torvin's Vest

### Read-Aloud — Finding It

> *The foreman is older than the others. Fifty-something. Weathered. He died trying to cover someone smaller — a man collapsed underneath him, legs twisted, ankle wrapped in a dirty bandage. The foreman's vest has been partly burned. A leather corner pokes out of an inner pocket. You pull it free. A journal, sand-stained, with the brass clasp still closed.*

**Hand the player the printed Journal handout (Appendix D) OR read aloud — do not summarize.** Read straight through, Day 1 to Day 13, roughly four minutes. Let the table sit in silence while you do.

### If Reading Aloud

> **Day 1** — Left Lux Aeterna at first light. Six of us. Tajsh wants the vein scouted in ten days or we don't get paid the bonus. Rozen is already complaining about his boots.
>
> **Day 2** — Good ground. Water at a rock cistern two hours before camp. Rozen complaining about hardtack again. Told him he can eat sand. Maret laughed. She never laughs.
>
> **Day 3** — Vorn says his back is tighter than usual. Says it's the air. Tomas says it's because Vorn is old. Vorn threatened to show him how old. Good crew. I like this crew.
>
> **Day 4** — Detoured east around a rockfall. Lost half a day. Hit a dust wall around midday and took shelter in an old mine entrance cut into the canyon wall — pre-collapse construction, could be Varr stonework, hard to tell. Waited inside about an hour.
>
> **Day 4 (continued)** — While sheltering, Rozen found a crack in the back wall. Cave-in had widened it at some point. Squeezed through with him and Maret out of curiosity. What we found wasn't a mine shaft. Long corridor — cut stone, very old, not natural. Opened into a large chamber. High ceiling, mostly rubble. In the center: a chasm. Fifteen feet across, maybe more. Dark all the way down. Across it: a stone pedestal. On the pedestal, a book, open. Something else in the center of the book. Couldn't make it out. Then the bats.
>
> Not small bats. Torso-sized. We ran. Rozen twisted his ankle. Got out of there.
>
> **Day 5** — Rozen limping. Slowing us. Symbol on that pedestal keeps coming back to me. Three rings. Interlocking. I tried to draw it. Couldn't get it right. The rings won't sit still on the page.
>
> *(Margin sketch — three overlapping rings, drawn badly. Underneath: "This is not right. They go THROUGH each other. I can't make my hand do it.")*
>
> **Day 6** — Quicksand stretch. Underground river surfacing. Maret to the knee. Rope-and-post, everyone across. Sand was warm. Didn't like that.
>
> **Day 7** — Arrived at the site. Crystal is real money. Sent the bird back to Tajsh with the tag. Vorn cut the first shaft. He's humming today. He never hums. Asked what the tune was. He said he didn't realize he was doing it.
>
> **Day 8** — Strange feeling. Like someone standing behind you. Turned around twice at dinner — nothing there. Rozen said the same. Maret says it's the bat business.
>
> **Day 9** — Tomas broke a tooth on hardtack. I owe him a drink in Lux Aeterna if we make it back in time for the Lantern's Wednesday stew. He says I owe him two drinks. I said one. We are agreed on one.
>
> **Day 10** — Rich vein. Purest I've seen. Something under it, though — dark stone that doesn't feel right. Tomas touched it. His hand went numb for an hour. Keeping that out of the official report until we're back.
>
> **Day 11** — Nobody's sleeping. Rozen hears footsteps. I hear them too. Vorn says the humming in his head is louder now. He says it is not his humming. He said that twice in a row and didn't notice he said it twice.
>
> **Day 12** — Vorn went down the shaft this morning. Took the deep cut like he asked to. Heard him shout. Heard the stone break. Dust settled and the hole he made is no deeper than his waist. He isn't in it. He is not anywhere. I put my arm into the hole up to the shoulder. There is nothing in the hole. The hole is not a hole.
>
> **Day 12 (later)** — Rozen won't stop crying. Maret won't speak. Tomas is sitting on a crate with his knife out. I told them we leave at first light. We are not miners anymore. We are witnesses.
>
> *(Last entry — written in a shaking hand:)*
>
> **Day 13** — There is something in the dark near the stones. I don't think it followed us from
>
> *(The entry ends mid-sentence. The line below is blank. The next page is soaked dark.)*

### The Symbol — Place the Handout on the Table

When you reach Day 5, pull out the Symbol handout (Appendix D) and put it on the table. Say:

> *"Torvin tried to draw the symbol. He couldn't get it right. Here's his attempt. This is what he saw on the pedestal."*

Any PC who studies it: **Discipline (Willpower), Easy ◆.** On failure, mildly dizzy, loses their place.

**Full description (read aloud to a PC who leans in):**

> *Three rings, interlocked — but not like chain-links. Each ring passes through both of the others simultaneously, which is the sort of thing that seems simple until you try to draw it yourself. The rings aren't perfect circles. They're slightly flattened, like they've been pulled at from three directions and held there. Where the three cross at the center, the carver went back with something finer and gouged out a single deep point — or perhaps that point was always there and the rings were drawn around it.*
>
> *If you follow one ring with your eye, your eye wants to keep going. Around, and under, and around. It takes a small effort to stop.*

### The Dwarf's Lore Check

If the dwarf wants to roll — **Knowledge (Lore), Hard ◆◆◆.**

- **Success:** His grandmother once sketched this in ash, then wiped it clean: *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."* She was afraid. She would not explain.
- **Triumph:** He also remembers a word she said under her breath — ***"Ulzammar."*** First time he has thought of it in forty years.

### The Choice — Bury or Leave

Let them decide. Burying takes an hour. Leaving them earns a quiet grief beat on the ride back. Both are correct.

# PART II — THE CITY

**Runtime: ~40 minutes.** Beats 4–6. Location: return road → Lux Aeterna.

---

## BEAT 4 — THE RETURN

**Budget: 5–10 minutes**

Quiet beat. Use it to let new and returning PCs exchange a line or two of backstory. Full strain recovery, no wound recovery.

### Optional Prompt — Offer One

- *"You're riding near the dwarf. He hasn't said anything in an hour. What do you say to him, or do you let him ride?"*
- *"The elf isn't sleeping. Second night in a row. Do you leave him alone, or do you go sit with him?"*
- *"Someone's going to have to explain to Tajsh what happened. Who's planning what to say?"*

**Do not force roleplay.** If the table wants to skip, cut to arrival.

### Read-Aloud — Arrival

> *The sun has been down an hour by the time you reach the slot canyon. You know the way in. Two sandstone pillars, narrow walls, a quarter-mile of stone — and then the lanterns bloom out of the dark and Lux Aeterna is there, waiting for you.*
>
> *Except it isn't, exactly. The lanterns are flickering wrong. One, steady-steady, flicker, steady, flicker-flicker. The market is quieter than it should be. Someone is humming a single sustained note from the mouth of the Warrens.*

## BEAT 5 — THE CITY IS OFF

**Budget: 20–25 minutes**

### The Tajsh Drop — First Stop

Tajsh takes the Band and sets it on his desk. A candle on the desk **freezes mid-flicker.** He sees it. Says nothing. Picks up the candle, carries it across the room, returns. Says:

> *"Give me tonight with this. Come back in the morning."*

If the party mentions the new arrivals: *"They're with you? Fine. They eat with you. They sleep where you sleep. If they're what they say, we're lucky. If they're not — you're my trap."*

**He does not mention the symbol yet.** He has not read the journal yet. Hold that for Beat 6.

### The Vignette Menu — Run 2 or 3, Not All

Pick based on which PCs gravitate where. The Well is the one to try to hit.

---

### Vignette — Marta at the Dusty Lantern

**Human woman, 40s. Burn scars on her arms. Always cooking. Warm and dry at the same time.**

Marta is wiping down the bar. The lanterns are out of rhythm tonight — one flickers, two steady, one flickers, all four flicker, three steady. She is pretending not to notice.

If a PC orders: she serves. If they mention the lanterns:

> *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why."*

**If the elf enters:** she takes one long look at him, then serves him the same bowl she serves everyone else. She does not ask where he came from. She has been where he has been, in her own way.

---

### Vignette — Hask in the Alchemy Workshop

**Orkin, mid-30s. Broad. Hands stained. Goggles pushed up like a second set of eyes. Slow, considered speech.**

Hask is sitting on a stool watching a potion simmer. The potion has been simmering for an hour. It has neither boiled nor cooled. He is watching it the way a farmer watches weather.

If a PC enters, he hands them a cup of something warm without asking:

> *"Whatever you brought back — it's in the walls now. Your hands are probably hungry."*

**If the elf passes:** Hask looks at him for three seconds. Says: *"You've been somewhere my people used to go. Before."* Doesn't elaborate. Doesn't have to.

---

### Vignette — Selin Voss on the Ladder

**Gnomish woman, 50s. Twitchy, fingers stained blue. Talks fast and interrupts herself.**

Selin is patching the Mage-Weave by lantern light. When a PC walks past, she grabs their sleeve:

> *"Does this look right to you? Does it? I've been staring at it for so long I can't tell anymore. Tell me it looks right. No, actually tell me if it looks wrong. Don't lie to me. Does it look wrong."*

She is the settlement's single most valuable non-combatant. She knows it. It exhausts her. The Weave is stuttering — not failing. Never happened in her six years maintaining it.

---

### Vignette — The Well Confrontation (Run This One)

> *You hear raised voices at the well. Not arguing — one voice, loud, shouting at a small girl. "She's been doing this. I've seen the sparks on her fingers. Whatever's in the water, it's HER." A handful of people are standing around, not stopping him, not helping. Petra — ten years old, soot on her hands — is not crying. She is watching the man who is yelling at her. She looks very small.*

**The accuser is Oris Hallow** — a frightened man, not a villain. His wife is sick. He thinks the water is making her sicker. He is wrong. He has chosen Petra because he needs to blame something.

**Petra manifested sparks two weeks ago.** She has not been back inside the schoolroom since. She is afraid of what she might do.

**Options the party has:**

| Approach | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| De-escalate with words | Charm or Negotiation ◆◆ | Oris is shamed, leaves. |
| Authority | Coercion ◆◆◆ | Crowd disperses but resentment lingers. |
| Remove Petra | no roll | Simple. Petra goes with them for a moment. |
| Explain (the Band) | Knowledge — Arcana ◆◆ | Risky but works. Some people believe. |
| Do nothing | — | Sabel arrives in 2 minutes, disperses the crowd. Tension stays. |

**Consequence:** If the party defends Petra, **she remembers** which PC did so. That memory matters in Session 3.

**If Petra is approached directly:** she does not want reassurance. She wants to know if it **gets better** — if people learn to live with it. The PC can answer honestly, lie, or say nothing. Whatever they say, she nods like she expected it, and goes back inside.

---

### Vignette — Cira at the Vault (For the Elf)

**Lothari, age unknown. Fortunes for food. Says devastatingly correct things unbidden.**

Cira is sitting outside the Vault where the Band was briefly stored. Humming one sustained note, louder than her usual tuneless hum.

**If the elf passes near her**, she stops. Looks up.

> *"Two of you are walking. Only one came back."*

She returns to humming. Will not clarify.

**Let the player sit with it.** This is a gift. Do not explain it.

---

### Tonight-State — Drop These Details Anywhere

Lace these into any scene the party walks into tonight. All happening simultaneously:

- Lanterns stutter asymmetrically.
- Well water tastes metallic.
- Mage-Weave stutters (Selin knows).
- Three of four working dogs refuse to come inside.
- Candles near the Vault freeze mid-flicker unpredictably.
- Cira hums one sustained note facing the Warrens.

The settlement is **holding its breath.**

### Ending Beat 5

Cut to morning when the vignettes feel complete or 25 minutes are up. Tajsh sends word. He wants them in his office.

> *The morning is gray. The lanterns have stabilized. The settlement looks almost normal, except you can tell by the way people aren't quite looking at each other that last night wasn't normal either. Tajsh's door is closed. When he opens it, he looks like he hasn't slept.*

## BEAT 6 — TAJSH'S REVELATION

**Budget: 10–15 minutes · all 5 PCs + Tajsh**

### Setup

Tajsh has read the journal. He has not slept. The Band is on his desk, wrapped in dark wool. The candle is across the room, behind him.

### Read-Aloud — Opening

> *Tajsh's office is small. Desk. Two chairs. A kettle that is whistling and being ignored. The Band is on the desk, wrapped in dark wool. He's not looking at it.*
>
> *He gestures at the chairs. Only two, for five of you. He waits to see who sits down. You notice he is watching the new ones, the elf and the dwarf, with a particular flat attention. He does not ask them their names. He will, eventually. Not yet.*
>
> *He picks up the journal. Sets it down. Picks it up again. Says, without looking up:*
>
> *"I've seen that symbol twice in my life."*

### The Monologue — Deliver Slowly. Pause on the Pauses.

> *"Once, when I was twenty-two. Shadewalker archive. Sealed crate. We weren't allowed to touch it. Which meant I spent three hours memorizing everything I could see through the slats of the lid. There was a symbol burned into the lid. Three rings, interlocked. I tried to draw it later. I couldn't get it right. Neither could Torvin."*
>
> *(He taps the journal.)*
>
> *"Second time — seventeen years ago. Ruin in the Barren Hills. I was paid a great deal of money to forget I had been there. The symbol was carved into the wall of a room my employer did not want anyone to enter. He did not enter it either. He paid me to carve it shut and walk away. I did."*
>
> *(Pause.)*
>
> *"Both times, everyone in the room treated it the way you treat a cobra. Very still. Very careful. Nobody wanted to be the one who reached for it."*
>
> *(He looks up. At the returning PCs. Then at the new ones.)*
>
> *"That book — whatever's on the pedestal Torvin found — I need it. I need you to go back to that temple and bring it to me. And before any of you asks why: I don't know yet. I know that the kind of people who use that symbol don't leave things in abandoned mines by accident. They leave them there for someone to find. I'd like to know who they were hoping to find. And I'd like to know before whoever they were hoping to find gets here first."*

### Dialogue — Prepared Answers

**"What were the Shadewalkers so afraid of?"**
> *"I never found out. The crate was gone by the next month. The briefing file I tried to pull after was missing. I stopped asking."*

**"Who was your employer at the Barren Hills?"**
> *"A name I don't say out loud. Sorry. Not today. Ask me again in a year."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because you found it. Because whatever this is has decided you exist. And because I trust you more than I trust anyone else in this settlement right now, and that's not a compliment, it's arithmetic."*

**"What about the Band?"**
> *"Stays here. In this room. In this wool. I don't want it on the road. Whoever is hunting things like this will find it faster if it's moving. Get me the book. I'll get us someone who can read it."*

**"The dwarf and the elf — what about them?"**
> *(He looks at them.) "You came out of a hole in the world. Your friend walked out of a rift that shouldn't be there. You're either part of this or you're worse than part of this. Either way, you go with them. I want you where I can see you. Welcome to Lux Aeterna."*

### The Ledger

Tajsh asks the new PCs for their names. Writes them in his small leather ledger.

> *"Next of kin? Anyone we should notify if something happens out there?"*

He's not asking because he wants to know. He's asking because someone has to write the name down.

### The Flicker — Interruption

Just as the party is about to leave:

> *The lantern in the corner — the one you hadn't been paying attention to — goes out. Not dims. OUT. At the same time, the lantern over the door goes out. And outside, through the window, you see every lantern in the canyon go out at the exact same moment. And then, one second later, come back on, brighter than they should be, and settle.*
>
> *One heartbeat. The mirror-charms dropped.*
>
> *Tajsh is already on his feet. He is looking at Sabel through the window, and Sabel is running toward the outlook. Tajsh says, quietly: "That was bad. That was very bad. Stay here."*
>
> *From the mouth of the Warrens, you can hear Cira humming — louder than she was yesterday, louder than she has ever been heard to hum — a single sustained note that is climbing, slowly, in pitch.*

The party will go to Cira. Or Cira comes to find them. She stops at the Warrens mouth and says:

> *"I cannot follow you where this goes. Listen carefully. Doubt generously. Come back."*

**Descend.**

# PART III — THE ARCHITECT

**Runtime: ~25–30 minutes.** Beat 7. The session's centerpiece.

## BEAT 7 — THE ARCHITECT

**Four phases, plus exit and ending.** The Architect is **warm, not ominous.** Relief first. Wrongness is a secondary signal for the perceptive.

---

### Phase 1 — Entry (5 min)

### The Deep Chamber — Before the Scene

At the deepest, oldest bend of the Warrens — a roughly circular stone room, twenty feet across. Pre-settlement, possibly pre-Paroxysm. The walls are cut stone in the First Age style: too-regular, geometric. Nothing in the room. A faint circular indentation in the center — as if something once stood there and was removed very long ago.

### Read-Aloud — Descent

> *The tunnel narrows as you go. The walls start to feel older than the rest of the settlement — because they are. Lux Aeterna was built over someone else's ruin. You've always known that. You just haven't had a reason to care, until tonight.*
>
> *The air cools. The torch flame flickers slower. Slower. Then it stops flickering entirely. It is still burning. It just isn't moving.*
>
> *You reach the chamber. Round, empty, stone. There's nothing here. You've walked to the end of the tunnel system.*
>
> *And then the last of you crosses the threshold.*

### Read-Aloud — The Transition (slow; the pauses are doing work)

> *The stone behind you is no longer there. Not dark. Not hidden. Not THERE. You don't turn around to check. You know, the way you know where your own hand is, that if you turned around there would be nothing to turn toward.*
>
> *Your torch is still. The flame has not gone out. It is simply holding its shape, like someone pressed pause on it.*
>
> *The air is warm and has no smell. Your breathing is slower than it should be. You aren't running out of air. You aren't getting any, either.*
>
> *Around you — not close, not far, distance isn't the right word here — rings of geometric light rotate in planes that don't intersect the way your eyes expect them to. Symbols you don't know the names of drift past and through each other. Some of them are versions of the three-ring mark. Some of them are things that mark could grow into if you let it keep going.*
>
> *There is no door. There is no floor, but you are not falling. You are, in every sense that matters, standing somewhere that is not a place.*
>
> *And at the center of all of it — not a figure, exactly. A CENTER. A point where the geometry converges and something is looking back.*

**Pause. Let the silence sit. Let a player be the one who speaks first.** When ready, deliver the Architect's first line, quiet:

> *"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."*

### Pocket-Space Properties (GM reference)

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside. Players won't notice.
- **No door.** Trying to find the exit fails. They wait to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons do nothing. **Discipline ◆◆** to recognize violence is not a tool. Failure: they try anyway, feel foolish.
- **Echoless voices.** Their own words sound like they're speaking inside a glass sphere.

---

### Phase 2 — The Naming (5–7 min)

The Architect addresses each PC by name and says something specifically, devastatingly true about them.

**Use the table in `_meta/the-broken-harmonies.md` lines 1242–1258 for returning PCs.** For the two new PCs, deliver these verbatim:

### To the Aen'valar Elf

> *"Child of the long silence. You have walked closer to the truth of things than any of your companions. What did you find, there, in the rooms that are not rooms? You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. That is why the song found you first."*

### To the Dwarf

> *"Son of the stone-line. You have a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You know what it is. You felt it when you struck the seam — not fear of falling, but recognition. You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

**Deliver as relief, not threat.** The Architect is seeing these people clearly. Every word is tuned to lower their defenses. Let that land. Pause after the Naming. Let the players react. Some will push back. Some will sit in it. Both are correct.

---

### Phase 3 — The Message (5 min)

Run as continuous dialogue, not a list:

1. *"The object you carry — what you call the Band — is a fragment of an instrument. Once whole, it was used to reshape the world. It nearly ended. The fragments were scattered to prevent that ending. Two more remain."*
2. *"I do not know where they are. Fifteen hundred years of watching, and even I cannot see everything. The fragments were hidden by gods. They are hidden from me too."*
3. *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left behind to watch. To wait. To speak when there was finally someone to speak to."*
4. *"I have prepared something for you."*

> *A plinth of folded light rises in front of the party. On it, revealed: a palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal, slotted in the center. It hums. The elf hears the note he has been following — except now it is in harmony with itself, and before it was alone.*

5. *"This is a Compass. It will sing when a fragment is near. But it cannot tell you where to begin. You must first learn where to look. Go north. The Dragon's Tooth pierces the sky. At its peak, there stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the stars as mortals read letters. Fragments disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark."*
6. *"Once all three are gathered, they must be brought to a wound in the world, and there they will be unmade. Only there. Nowhere else. That is the task."*

Do not hurry. The plinth of light remains. The Compass is on it. A PC may take it at any time — it will not resist.

---

### Phase 4 — The Questions (10–15 min)

**Tell your players, in your GM voice:** *"It has said what it came to say. It is waiting. You can ask it anything."*

### Prepared Answers

**"What are you?"**
> *"A servant. When the Four turned their hands from the world, some were left behind to watch the places that could not be left unwatched. I am what remains of that watching. I am not a god. I do not want worship. I want the world to continue."*

**"Why us?"**
> *"Because the Band has answered your hand. Because those of you who carry wounds know how to walk with them — the unwounded do not know how to carry things that ought not be carried. And because you are HERE. Gods plan. Mortals arrive. I have learned to work with what arrives."*

**"Are you good?"**
> *"I am useful. Goodness is a mortal accounting, and the accounts are kept by those who survive to keep them. I was left here to prevent a catastrophe. That is the only measure I am prepared to answer for. When this is done, you will decide whether what I did was good. That is your right, not mine."*

**"What is the thing that will come if we fail?"**
> *"A song that the world cannot survive being sung. You would not understand the name of it. The best of my language can describe it only as HUNGER GIVEN FORM. It sleeps. The fragments, gathered and wrongly used, would wake it. Gathered and rightly unmade, they would silence it forever."*

**"Why can't you just take the Band yourself?"**
> *"Because I am not here. What you see is a shape my attention is taking, in a place my attention has reached. I cannot lift a stone. I cannot kill your enemies. I can watch, and speak, and prepare the ground. The rest is for hands that bleed."*

**"How do we know you're telling the truth?"**
> *"You do not. You never will, not entirely. Any test I suggest is a test I have prepared for. This is the oldest problem. I will offer what I can: I have told you nothing tonight that you can verify immediately. Go north. Find the observatory. See whether the stars say what I say they say. If they do not, come back and name me a liar. I will still be waiting."*

**To the Elf: "Have you been in the rifts?"**
> *"I know what is in them. I know what you did not let them take from you. That knowledge is not the same as the living of it. You have something I do not. It is why I can ask this of you and not of myself."*

**To the Dwarf: "Did you kill my crew?"**
*(Pause. Let the table feel it.)*
> *"No. What killed your crew was already walking the thin places long before I knew your name. I could not reach them. I could not reach you, until the seam opened. If I had reached further, sooner — perhaps. But regret is a door I cannot afford to open. Yours is a grief I do not know how to carry for you. I am sorry."*

**"What about the ones hunting us?"**
> *"The Legio Null will come. Soon. Your settlement was visible from the sky for one heartbeat. That is enough. You must leave this place, and soon. I cannot stop the hunt. I can only tell you to RUN."*

**"Will we see you again?"**
> *"When the Compass sings, I will be near. Not always in a form your eyes can hold. But near."*

**Any coercion / threat (calm, slightly amused):**
> *"You are holding a Band you cannot control in a settlement your enemies will find within the week. I am the hand offered to you in the dark. If you prefer another, seek it. I will not chase you. I will be here if you return."*

### Skill Checks Available

Any player may ask to roll. Offer these if a player states their intent:

| Intent | Check | Difficulty | Result |
|---|---|---|---|
| Notice something wrong | Perception (Cunning) | ◆◆ | No reflection of the Architect in the floating crystal symbols. Nothing else in the space has this problem. |
| Read beneath composure | Perception (Cunning) | ◆◆◆ | Anticipation when it speaks of "unmaking" — not relief. Triumph: also hunger. |
| Read magical signature | Knowledge (Arcana) | ◆◆◆ | Divine on surface, ABYSSAL underneath. Perfume over rot. Overlay is skilled. Wrongness is not. |

| Resist emotional pull | Discipline (Willpower) | ◆◆ | The Naming is *too* tailored. A therapist would call this skill. A spy would call it targeting. |
| Coax more from it | Charm or Negotiation (Presence) | ◆◆◆ | Reveals one detail beyond plan. GM picks: (a) **Brenn Hallowveil** — the observatory's last living custodian. (b) A Waldkyn Song-Ward master in the mountains who could train the elf's anchor. |
| Mislead the Architect | Deception (Cunning) vs | ◆◆◆◆ | Appears to believe. A half-beat later, one sentence betrays that it did not. Only the rolling player notices. |
| Threaten, demand, coerce | Coercion (Willpower) | ◆◆◆◆◆ | Fails. Amused, not moved. *"You are welcome to try. I have been threatened by better than you and was not moved. Sit down. Or do not. I am not in a hurry."* |

### Phase 5 — Exit (2 min)

When the questions run out:

> *"Go north. Take the Band. Take the Compass. Trust the people who love you. Do not trust me further than you have to. This is the only honest thing I will say tonight."*

### Read-Aloud — Collapse

> *The geometry folds inward. The planes rotate, then dim. The center-point you have been speaking to contracts to a single point of light, and then that point is gone. You blink.*
>
> *The torch is flickering again. The stone behind you is stone. The floor is under your boots. The Compass is warm in someone's hand.*
>
> *You turn. Cira is at the mouth of the tunnel. She looks at you. She says:*
>
> *"How long were you gone?"*
>
> *One of you answers, "A few minutes."*
>
> *She says, "It has been three hours. I could not see you. I was not sure you were coming back. How do you feel?"*

## ENDING THE SESSION

Ask each player, **in character, one sentence only**: *How do you feel?*

Go around the table. Each player, one sentence. No discussion, no follow-ups. When the fifth has spoken:

> *That's where we leave it for tonight. Good game, everyone.*

**End.**

**Day 1** — Left Lux Aeterna at first light. Six of us. Tajsh wants the vein scouted in ten days or we don't get paid the bonus. Rozen is already complaining about his boots.

**Day 2** — Good ground. Water at a rock cistern two hours before camp. Rozen complaining about hardtack again. Told him he can eat sand. Maret laughed. She never laughs.

**Day 3** — Vorn says his back is tighter than usual. Says it's the air. Tomas says it's because Vorn is old. Vorn threatened to show him how old. Good crew. I like this crew.

**Day 4** — Detoured east around a rockfall. Lost half a day. Hit a dust wall around midday and took shelter in an old mine entrance cut into the canyon wall — pre-collapse construction, could be Varr stonework, hard to tell. Waited inside about an hour.

**Day 4 (continued)** — While sheltering, Rozen found a crack in the back wall. Cave-in had widened it at some point. Squeezed through with him and Maret out of curiosity. What we found wasn't a mine shaft. Long corridor — cut stone, very old, not natural. Opened into a large chamber. High ceiling, mostly rubble. In the center: a chasm. Fifteen feet across, maybe more. Dark all the way down. Across it: a stone pedestal. On the pedestal, a book, open. Something else in the center of the book. Couldn't make it out. Then the bats. Not small bats. Torso-sized. We ran. Rozen twisted his ankle. Got out of there.

**Day 5** — Rozen limping. Slowing us. Symbol on that pedestal keeps coming back to me. Three rings. Interlocking. I tried to draw it. Couldn't get it right. The rings won't sit still on the page.

*(Margin sketch — three overlapping rings, drawn badly. Underneath: "This is not right. They go THROUGH each other. I can't make my hand do it.")*

**Day 6** — Quicksand stretch. Underground river surfacing. Maret to the knee. Rope-and-post, everyone across. Sand was warm. Didn't like that.

**Day 7** — Arrived at the site. Crystal is real money. Sent the bird back to Tajsh with the tag. Vorn cut the first shaft. He's humming today. He never hums. Asked what the tune was. He said he didn't realize he was doing it.

**Day 8** — Strange feeling. Like someone standing behind you. Turned around twice at dinner — nothing there. Rozen said the same. Maret says it's the bat business.

**Day 9** — Tomas broke a tooth on hardtack. I owe him a drink in Lux Aeterna if we make it back in time for the Lantern's Wednesday stew. He says I owe him two drinks. I said one. We are agreed on one.

**Day 10** — Rich vein. Purest I've seen. Something under it, though — dark stone that doesn't feel right. Tomas touched it. His hand went numb for an hour. Keeping that out of the official report until we're back.

**Day 11** — Nobody's sleeping. Rozen hears footsteps. I hear them too. Vorn says the humming in his head is louder now. He says it is not his humming. He said that twice in a row and didn't notice he said it twice.

**Day 12** — Vorn went down the shaft this morning. Took the deep cut like he asked to. Heard him shout. Heard the stone break. Dust settled and the hole he made is no deeper than his waist. He isn't in it. He is not anywhere. I put my arm into the hole up to the shoulder. There is nothing in the hole. The hole is not a hole.

## HANDOUT — THE THREE-RING SYMBOL

**Context:** carved into the stone pedestal Torvin's crew found on Day 4. Branded (invisibly, but the party will feel it) into the metal of the Ulzammar Band. Sketched — badly — in the margin of Torvin's journal, Day 5.

**Description:**

> *Three rings, interlocked — but not like chain-links. Each ring passes through both of the others simultaneously, which is the sort of thing that seems simple until you try to draw it yourself.*
>
> *The rings aren't perfect circles. They're slightly flattened, like they've been pulled at from three directions and held there. Where the three cross at the center, the carver went back with something finer and gouged out a single deep point — or perhaps that point was always there and the rings were drawn around it.*
>
> *If you follow one ring with your eye, your eye wants to keep going. Around, and under, and around. It takes a small effort to stop.*

**Torvin's sketch (what the players see on paper):** three overlapping rings in the shape of the Olympic rings — simple interlock, not the true topology. Underneath, in Torvin's hand:

> *"This is not right. They go THROUGH each other. I can't make my hand do it."*

**For the GM drawing the real symbol:** search "Borromean rings" as a reference. Modify — slightly flatten the rings (tensioned, not perfect). Add a deep gouged point at the center.

## HANDOUT — PLAYER HOOK · THE AEN'VALAR WANDERER (ELF)

*For the elf player. Read before Session 1. Bring questions.*

### Who You Are

You are **Aen'valar** — one of the Wandering Elves of Vaeloris. Your people are pre-mortal, long-lived, older than nations. You do not belong to any city. You have never built one. When your people speak of home, they mean a direction, not a place.

Dwarves and elves are not enemies in this world. You share the long memory of the time of the Gods. You understand each other's philosophies, even when you disagree about how to live.

### What Happened to You

Years ago — five, you think, though time has become strange — you joined a crew of rift-walkers tasked with entering a Scar of Dissonance and killing the fiend anchoring it. Your crew was good. Your crew was not good enough.

You were the only one who came back, and you did not come back clean.

You spent what felt like **six to ten years** inside the rift. Outside, only three or four passed. You survived because you learned to listen. The rift has its own rules; you learned them one at a time. You lost a companion to every rule you learned.

One of your crew, a Waldkyn Song-Ward apprentice, taught you the first trick that kept you alive: a specific sustained note, hummed into a shard of aether-quartz, that creates a small field of tonal dissonance the lesser fiends cannot cross. She taught you the note and then died in the next ambush. You have been using the trick ever since. Your quartz is cracking. You have **seven uses left.**

When you finally emerged — two weeks ago, into the eastern desert — you were not the person who entered. You are darker now. More careful. Slower to trust. Sunlight hurts for the first hour of every morning. You are not a villain. You are a man who has seen things and found a way to keep walking.

### Why You Are Here Now

For the last four days, you have been hearing a **song.** A single tone, sustained, threading through the ambient noise of the world. You have been walking toward it because it is the only thing in your life right now that feels like it has a shape.

Two days ago, the song got loud. You were near a thin-place and you heard someone on the other side — a dwarf, falling. You reached him. You sang your anchor-note. You bought four heartbeats and threw you both through the same seam.

You came out in the desert carrying a half-dead dwarf. You have been walking west since. The song is loudest in the direction of a camp you can smell burning.

### The Singing

You are the only person who can hear the song. Tell your GM privately, at any time, if the song changes — gets louder, quieter, changes pitch, stops. The song has its own rules. You are still learning them.

### Things to Decide Before Play

- What is your name?
- What is the last thing you remember from before the rift?
- What is one thing you lost in there that was not a person?
- What do you call the song you are following?

## HANDOUT — PLAYER HOOK · THE DWARF

*For the dwarf player. Read before Session 1. Bring questions.*

### Who You Are

You are a dwarf of Vaeloris. Your people are pre-mortal — long-lived, older than most kingdoms, descended from the age of the Gods. Stone is your language. You remember your clan-lines the way other peoples remember songs.

Dwarves and elves are not enemies in this world. Your peoples took different paths — your people stayed, built, recorded; their people walked — but you understand each other.

You are a tank. You carry the weight at the front. Your shoulders know the shape of a pickaxe and a shield in equal measure.

### Why You Were in the Desert

You took work with a relic-hunting crew out of **Lux Aeterna** — a hidden settlement in the Westward Sands, a refuge for people the Mage-Purge Wars have no use for. The foreman's name is **Torvin Dast.** He is a fair man. He hired you because he needed a pick-man who could cut straight. You liked him.

You had been with the crew for six days when it happened.

You hit the deep seam. The aether-quartz was richer than anything you had ever seen. Underneath it, black stone. Stone that made hands go numb. You took the deep cut. Your third strike did not crack the stone. It **opened** it.

You fell. The hole was no deeper than your waist. You fell farther than that.

### What You Remember from the Other Side

Darkness that had weight. Air that was wrong — thicker, then thinner, then wrong in a way that does not have a word. Shapes moving toward you. Certainty you were about to die.

And a song. A single sustained note, closer than you expected, from behind you. And then a hand, pulling you up.

When you woke, you were on the sand. An elf was kneeling beside you, dressing a wound you did not remember taking. He said: *"You're alive. I'm sorry about your crew."*

You have been walking with him since. You do not remember deciding to. He seems to know where he is going.

### What You Feel

**Grief, mostly.** Your crewmates were not close friends. You worked with them six days. But you liked them. You liked Torvin. He kept a brass compass in his vest that his wife gave him, engraved *"Come home."* He showed it to you once. He was proud of her.

You have not spoken about any of this out loud. You do not know how.

**A song, faintly.** For the last day, you have been hearing something. You are not sure it is the same one the elf hears. Yours is quieter. But it is there. You find yourself humming, and you didn't know you were doing it.

### Something Your Grandmother Said

When you were very small, your grandmother once sketched a symbol in ash on the hearthstone — three interlocking rings with a dark point at the center. She looked at it for a long time. Then she wiped it clean with her hand. She said, under her breath:

> *"The Measure, the Setting, the Stone. Do not speak the fourth."*

She would not explain. She died when you were twelve. You have not thought about her in a long time.

If you see a symbol that looks like the one she drew — the GM will tell you. You will remember her.

### Things to Decide Before Play

- What is your name?
- What is your clan's name?
- What was your grandmother's name? Are you named after anyone?
- Why did you take this job? (Money? Running from something? Running toward something?)
- Of Torvin's crew, who did you like most? You do not need to share this with the party unless you want to.
