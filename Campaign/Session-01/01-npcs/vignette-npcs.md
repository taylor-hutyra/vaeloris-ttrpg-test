# Vignette NPCs — Beat 5

Small roles. Big cumulative weight. Each of these people is a thread the party will feel pulled when Lux Æterna falls later in Act I. **Don't skip them.** Pick two or three to run based on which PCs gravitate toward which space.

---

## Warden Sabel

**Where:** The settlement's defensive perimeter. The outlook. His cramped office off the barracks.

**Look:** Human male, mid-40s. Lean. Old soldier's posture. A crossbow that has been repaired so many times it is more maintenance than weapon.

**Voice:** Clipped. Military. Quiet.

**In Beat 5:** Sabel is doing weapons checks on the militia's crossbows, one by one, making a small sound of approval each time one is right. He has checked them all twice already. **This is how he sits still.**

If a PC approaches him and asks if everything is okay: *"Everything is exactly what it is. I'm checking the bows."* He means: *no, and I'm managing it.*

**What he sees:** He noticed Tajsh's candle freeze. He has not mentioned it to anyone. He is counting the things that have been wrong in the last six hours. He does not yet know what to do with the count.

**What he does next session:** When the mirror-charms flicker (Beat 7 transition), Sabel runs. He knows the charm flicker means Lux Æterna was briefly visible from the sky. He is preparing the evacuation plan.

## Marta Dustborn

**Where:** The Dusty Lantern (the settlement's tavern).

**Look:** Human woman, 40s. Arms marked by old burn scars she doesn't hide. Always cooking something.

**Voice:** Warm and dry at the same time. Doesn't look up when she talks.

**In Beat 5:** She's wiping down the bar. The lanterns are out of rhythm tonight — one flickers, two steady, one flickers, all four flicker, three steady. She is pretending not to notice.

If a PC comes in and orders something: she serves them. If they mention the lanterns: *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why."*

**What she sees:** She knows the settlement is wrong tonight. She does not have a framework for *why.* Her framework for when the world is wrong is: *feed people, keep them in the light, wait for it to pass.*

**If the elf enters her tavern:** she takes one long look at him, then serves him the same bowl she serves everyone else. She does not ask where he came from. She has been where he has been, in her own way.

## Hask

**Where:** The alchemist's workshop, low stone building at the canyon's north wall.

**Look:** Orkin, mid-30s. Broad. Hands always stained with something. Wears goggles pushed up on his forehead like a second set of eyes.

**Voice:** Considered. Slow. Never answers a question before he has thought about it.

**In Beat 5:** Sitting on a stool, watching a potion simmer. The potion has been simmering for an hour. It has neither boiled nor cooled. Hask is watching it the way a farmer watches weather.

If a PC enters: he hands them a cup of something warm without asking. *"Whatever you brought back — it's in the walls now. Your hands are probably hungry."*

**What he knows:** His alchemy is subtly broken. Reactions that should take seconds are taking minutes. Reactions that should take minutes are not completing at all. He is cataloguing it. He will have a written report by tomorrow morning that nobody will ever read.

**What he'll do if the elf passes:** looks at him for three seconds. Says: *"You've been somewhere my people used to go. Before."* Doesn't elaborate. Doesn't have to.

## Selin Voss

**Where:** On a ladder in the central canyon, patching the Mage-Weave.

**Look:** Gnomish woman, 50s. Nervous, twitchy, eyes that miss nothing. Fingers stained with blue dye.

**Voice:** Fast. Worried. Interrupts herself.

**In Beat 5:** Re-weaving a patch of Mage-Weave by lantern light. Mutters to herself. When a PC walks past, she grabs their sleeve:

> *"Does this look right to you? Does it? I've been staring at it for so long I can't tell anymore. Tell me it looks right. No, actually tell me if it looks wrong. Don't lie to me. Does it look wrong."*

**What she knows:** The Mage-Weave is stuttering. Not failing. Stuttering. Which has never happened in the six years she's been maintaining it. The weave pattern is sound. The material is sound. What is wrong is *the air around it.* The ambient mana the Weave draws on is behaving unpredictably.

She is the settlement's single most valuable non-combatant. If she dies, Lux Æterna's cover fails within weeks. Sabel knows this. Tajsh knows this. Selin knows this, and it exhausts her.

## Petra

**Where:** Outside the schoolroom (Aldric's), sitting on a step, watching nothing.

**Look:** 10 years old. Soot-marks on her fingers that don't wash off. Small for her age.

**Voice:** Quiet. Direct. She has already cried about this in private. She is done crying where anyone can see.

**In Beat 5:** Two weeks ago she manifested sparks. She hasn't been back inside the schoolroom since. She's afraid of what she might do.

If a PC speaks to her, she doesn't want reassurance — she's past that. She wants to know if it **gets better.** If people learn to live with it, or if it's always like this, always one bad moment away from disaster.

The player can answer honestly, lie, or say nothing. Whatever they say, she nods like she expected it, and goes back inside.

**If there is a commotion at the well** (the crowd blaming her for the metallic water): she is standing at the edge of the crowd, not speaking. Not crying. Watching the adult who is accusing her. The player who intervenes on her behalf will be remembered by her for the rest of her (brief) life.

**Remember Petra.** She appears in one more beat (The Night the Lantern Was Full, later in Act I) and then vanishes into the chaos. Players who talked to her will think about her when they hear about the children in Gearhaven.
