# The Architect

**Role this session:** The BBEG in disguise. Apparent ally. Actual horror.
**Appears in:** Beat 7 only.

**DO NOT SHARE THIS PAGE WITH PLAYERS.**

---

## True Identity (GM ONLY)

An **Echo-Lord** — a First Age entity of order-given-form, bound to the Crown of the Ulzammar. It is not a god. It is not a servant of the Four. It was imprisoned at the Paroxysm and has spent fifteen hundred years calling its own scattered pieces home.

Its goals:
1. Gather the three Crown fragments.
2. Be brought to the "wound in the world" (the Barren Hills) to be *reassembled,* not unmade.
3. Reshape Vael into a place of pure systematized order. No weather. No grief. No choice.

It cannot move stones or kill enemies. It can only **speak.** Everything it accomplishes, it accomplishes through mortal hands it has chosen carefully.

## What It Tells the Party (all true-sounding, most partially true)

1. The Band is a fragment of an instrument that nearly destroyed the world. ✓ True.
2. Two more fragments exist. ✓ True.
3. It is a servant of the Four. ✗ Lie.
4. It does not know where the fragments are. ✗ Partial lie — it has *approximate* locations but not precise.
5. The fragments must be unmade at a wound in the world. ✗ Lie. They will be *joined.*
6. Go north to the observatory. ✓ True (the observatory really does read fragment resonance).

## How It Manifests

Through the **Resonance Compass** it is already preparing for the party. Its attention reaches a location; it does not "travel." In the Deep Chamber, its attention is concentrated — so the space becomes a *pocket of its influence.* Time does not pass normally inside. There is no door, because the space is no longer attached to the settlement.

## Voice

Calm. Warm. Unhurried. Speaks as though it has already considered everything the party might say. Never raises its voice. Never interrupts. When it wants silence, the geometry around it *dims.*

It addresses each player by name on first contact and never asks how it knew the name. It knows.

## The Naming — Session 1 Lines

After it has given the Six Beats of its message (see [beat-7-architect.md](../03-scenes/beat-7-architect.md)), it addresses each PC. Use the table in the campaign doc (`_meta/the-broken-harmonies.md`, lines 1242–1258) for the returning PCs' backgrounds. For the two new PCs:

**To the Aen'valar elf:**

> *"Child of the long silence. You have walked closer to the truth of things than any of your companions. What did you find, there, in the rooms that are not rooms? You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. That is why the song found you first."*

**To the dwarf:**

> *"Son of the stone-line. You have a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You know what it is. You felt it when you struck the seam — not fear of falling, but recognition. You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

These words are **calculated.** Every sentence is precisely targeted to lower defenses. The warmth is instrumentalized. But the words themselves are *true.* That is what makes the betrayal, later, cut so deep.

## Red Flags for Perceptive Players

See the skill-check table in [beat-7-architect.md](../03-scenes/beat-7-architect.md). Players can roll to notice:
- No reflection in nearby crystal
- Hunger beneath its composure
- Abyssal signature under divine overlay
- Plants paused, not healthy
- The Naming is *too* accurate, delivered without a pause

## Do Not

- Do not play it as sinister. Play it as *warm.* The wrongness is underneath, only visible to those who look.
- Do not answer player questions with vagueness. Answer them with calm, specific-sounding, emotionally resonant half-truths.
- Do not force the suspicion. Let the player who looks for the cracks find them. Let the player who doesn't, receive the gift.
