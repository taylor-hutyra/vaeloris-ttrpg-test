# Session 1 — "The Desert Gives Nothing for Free"

**Duration:** 2 to 2.5 hours
**Players:** 5 (3 returning, 2 new — Aen'valar elf, dwarf tank)
**Setting date:** TA 412, Lux Æterna, Vael
**XP target:** ~20 XP per PC on completion

---

## The One-Sentence Pitch

*A relic-hunt goes wrong. Something in the sand is older than the gods. And the light that offers to help you is the thing you should be afraid of.*

---

## Session Objectives

By the end of this session, the party must:

1. Survive the Scorch-Wraiths at the mining camp.
2. Absorb the two new PCs into the group.
3. Recover the **Ulzammar Band** and **Torvin Dast's journal.**
4. Return to Lux Æterna and feel the settlement starting to strain.
5. Meet **the Architect.** Receive the **Resonance Compass.**
6. End on Cira's question: *"How long were you gone?"*

---

## The Seven Beats

| # | Beat | Budget | Location |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Scorch-Wraith combat — finish what's running | 20 min | Mining Camp |
| 2 | Elf & dwarf arrive through the Scar seam | 15–20 min | Mining Camp pit |
| 3 | The Band, the journal, the dead crew | 15 min | Mining Camp |
| 4 | Travel back to Lux Æterna | 5–10 min | Desert |
| 5 | The city is off — vignettes, contacts, wards | 20–25 min | Lux Æterna |
| 6 | Tajsh reads the journal — the symbol revelation | 10–15 min | Tajsh's office |
| 7 | The Architect — supernatural encounter + Compass | 25–30 min | The Deep Chamber |

**If time runs short:** hard-cut after Beat 6. End on the mirror-charm flicker and Cira humming at the Warrens' mouth. Beat 7 becomes next session's cold open.

## Emotional Arc

The session moves from **violence → wonder → unease → awe.**

- **Beats 1–3:** adrenaline and confusion. The world is physical and dangerous.
- **Beat 4:** deflation. Travel. Quiet.
- **Beat 5:** the settlement the players are supposed to love starts showing cracks. This is the session's secret heart — the thing that will be lost later must be *felt* first.
- **Beats 6–7:** the plot reveals its scale. The Architect does not arrive as an enemy. It arrives as a **relief.** That is the point.

---

## GM Reminders

- **Read the journal entries aloud.** Don't summarize. The cadence is doing work.
- **Let the elf lead the party to the Band.** Give him narrative authority in Beat 3. He heard it first.
- **Let Petra matter.** She appears in Beat 5. Every player who spends 30 seconds with her will remember her when she dies later in Act I.
- **The Architect is warm.** Do not play it ominous. Ominous is the secondary signal. Primary signal: *relief, recognition, being-seen.*
- **End on Cira's question.** Not a monologue, not a recap. Her question. Each player answers in one sentence. Stop.
