# The Mining Camp

**A scarred half-circle of tents and equipment in the lee of a rocky outcrop, a mile east of a low, dark hill that contains the First Age ruin of Sorath. Three days' ride from Lux Æterna. Torvin Dast's crew worked here for six days before the seam opened.**

---

## Read-Aloud — Approach (if needed)

> *The smell hits before the sight does. Burned canvas, burned hair, and under that something sharper — the way lightning smells, a second before it strikes.*
>
> *The camp was pitched well. Six tents in a half-circle, backs to the outcrop. The fire-pit is in the middle. The mining tools are stacked, because Torvin was that kind of foreman. Everything has been done right.*
>
> *Everything is also on fire, or was, until the fire ran out of things to burn.*
>
> *And in the center of it, where the fire-pit is: three figures of superheated sand and glass, humanoid, hollow-eyed, still feeding on whatever they are feeding on.*

---

## Layout

Four zones matter. Use them for combat positioning and investigation:

### 1. The Tent Half-Circle

Six tents. Two fully collapsed, three partially standing, one untouched. Burn patterns are **geometric** — perfectly straight lines, precise angles, where the wraiths' *Geometric Eyes* beams passed.

**Loot here:** personal effects of the crew (names on bedrolls — let the dwarf PC see these), sand-worn boots, a wooden carving Tomas had been whittling (a small bat, half-finished), a letter Rozen had been writing and never finished.

### 2. The Fire-Pit

The wraiths' feeding center. Residual mana pools here — the wraiths are drawn to it. **Bodies here:** three of five. Use this zone for the final round of combat.

### 3. The Mining Pit

A quarter-circle excavation cut into the base of the outcrop. Scaffolding, pick-marks in the stone, a winch and rope. The aether-quartz seam is visible in the wall — pale, almost blue, catches lantern-light wrong.

**At the bottom of the pit:** a shaft, roughly four feet across, cut straight down. The walls are black crystalline stone. At the bottom of the shaft (about fifteen feet down): **the Ulzammar Band,** half-embedded in the wall where the dwarf's pick struck.

**The hole the dwarf fell through** is here — a crack no deeper than a hand's-width into the black stone. Anyone who touches it feels nothing. It is not a physical hole anymore. It closed when the dwarf came out the other side.

### 4. The Torvin Find

Torvin's body is at the edge of the tent half-circle, collapsed over another smaller body (Rozen). In his vest pocket: **the journal.** In his right hand, still clenched: **a small brass compass** engraved *"Come home."*

## The Band — In the Pit

At the bottom of the shaft, the Band is visible in the wall. Describe it:

> *A circlet of dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins like lightning frozen mid-strike. It is half-embedded in the black stone, as if the stone grew up around it over centuries. The air near it is thick. A drop of sweat falls off your brow and takes a heartbeat longer than it should to hit the ground. Your lantern flame is a candle underwater — slow, dreamy, not flickering right.*

See [04-rules/key-items.md](../04-rules/key-items.md) for the Band's mechanical effects and the Discipline check on touching it.

## Tactical Notes — Combat (Beat 1)

- **The tent half-circle** is the combat zone. Cover is everywhere (collapsed tents, scattered crates, the outcrop). Let PCs move through it.
- **Terrain hazard:** three small pools of burning pitch from a ruptured alchemy vial. 3 damage per round to anyone who enters or starts their turn in the pool. Lasts 4 rounds.
- **The mining pit** is out of line-of-sight from the fire-pit zone. A PC who wants to investigate it during combat is safe from wraith eye-beams but also unable to help allies.
- **The wraiths do not leave the camp.** They are tethered to the residual mana. If the party retreats beyond Long range, the wraiths return to feeding and the fight ends. The campsite just won't be safe to return to.

---

## Post-Combat — The Silence

When the last wraith shatters, describe:

> *Silence. Real silence. The kind the desert usually makes — wind, sand, distant stone — is gone for a moment. The pools of pitch are still burning. Your breathing is loud.*
>
> *Then, a quarter-mile east, something under the ruin pulses. A single sub-audible thump you feel in your teeth. And the crack in the rock at the eastern edge of the outcrop — the one you didn't notice before — widens.*

That is the cue for Beat 2. The elf is about to arrive.
