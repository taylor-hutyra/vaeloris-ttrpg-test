# The Four

*The elder pantheon of [[Vaeloris]]. Withdrew from the world at or before the Paroxysm. Most mortals do not worship them. Most scholars disagree about whether they ever existed.*

## The Four

- **The Singer** — associated with the shaping of form, music, language.
- **The Builder** — associated with stone, growth, and what endures.
- **The Watcher** — associated with observation, patience, the long view.
- **The Silence** — associated with what cannot be spoken, rest, and endings.

No active temples exist in any settlement the party will visit. The Four are referenced in Lothari dream-tradition as *the ones who did not stay,* in Waldkyn song as *the four chords that stopped being played,* and in Dwarven clan-lore as *the hands that turned away.* They are acknowledged as real, historically, by every long-memoried tradition — and treated, by all of them, as absent.

## Why They Are Relevant

**[[The Architect]] claims to be a servant of the Four.** This is a lie — the Architect is an Echo-Lord, not a divine servant — but the claim is **plausible** because no living person can contradict it, and the Four's long silence has left a gap that anything with sufficient gravitas can fill.

Perceptive players who push on this claim (a successful **Knowledge — Lore ◆◆◆** during Chapter 2 Scene 5) can recall that the Four have not been recorded as *acting* in the world since the Paroxysm. The Architect's claim to have been *"left behind to watch"* fits the theology. It also fits a number of competing theories. It cannot be proven.
