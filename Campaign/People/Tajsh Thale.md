# Tajsh Thale

Human male, mid-40s. Lean, weathered, brown skin worn dark by desert sun. Eyes that assess everything before the rest of his face catches up.

## Personality

Tajsh speaks fast and thinks faster. He uses humor the way other people use armor — deflection, misdirection, buying time. When he's nervous he polishes whatever he's holding: a coin, a cup, a fragment of artifact. If you notice the polishing, he knows he's been read.

He is not cruel. He is pragmatic to a degree that, in another life, might have made him ruthless. In this one, it makes him the person who keeps 400 people alive through supply chain management, black-market relationships, and an instinct for which compromises actually matter. He cares about Lux Æterna deeply and shows it by making sure the water stays clean and the relic prices stay fair.

He does not tell people he cares about them. He tells them that the rations this week are better because he found a new Lothari contact. He means the same thing.

## Background

Born in Port Farrick. Recruited by the Shadewalkers at nineteen, trained as an archivist specializing in pre-Paroxysm artifacts. Left at thirty-three after a disagreement about an object he believed the Guild had no right to hold. The Shadewalkers call it stealing. He calls it a matter of principle. He still won't say what the object was or where it went.

Arrived in Lux Æterna in SA:1483, three years after the original refugee camp was established. Found a struggling cluster of tents and no economy. Recognized the value of what the surrounding ruins could provide. Within two years he had a relic brokerage, a Lothari supply route, and something resembling a market. He didn't become the settlement's leader. He just became the person the settlement needed, and the two things turned out to be the same.

## The Evacuation Fund

He skims 10% off every relic sale — not out of greed but into a hidden cache two miles south of the canyon, stocked with travel supplies, emergency Mana-Crystal denominations, and a set of route maps to three safe destinations. He has never told Sabel. He has never told anyone. The logic: if the settlement has to be abandoned suddenly, he'll need this, and he cannot afford to have a conversation about it first.

The logic terrifies him. He sleeps badly.

## The Shadewalker Connection

His contacts in the Shadewalkers are specific: one archivist (Ink), one logistics handler, one courier. He uses them for artifact identification, intelligence on outside threats, and the occasional supply acquisition that the Lothari route can't provide. They use him for early warning on interesting artifacts passing through the desert. It is a business relationship with no affection on either side and professional respect on both.

When Ink identifies the Ulzammar Band, the relationship becomes complicated.

## GM Notes

Tajsh should feel like the most competent adult in any room — not because he's more powerful, but because he's been at this longer. Let him be right about the small things, often, so that when he's wrong about the big thing (trusting the Architect longer than he should), it lands harder.

His relationship with the party should develop naturally from employer → trusted employer → something like family. He will not use that word. He may, once, late in the campaign, use their first names.
