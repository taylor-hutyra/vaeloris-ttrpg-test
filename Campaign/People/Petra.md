# Petra

*Ten years old. Soot on her fingers that doesn't wash off. Afraid of what she might do.*

## Summary

Petra manifested sparks two weeks before the party's arrival. She was seven when [[Aldric Greypen]] started teaching her to read. She was ten when her fingers started scorching paper she meant only to turn. She has not gone back to the schoolroom. She sits on the steps outside it, most mornings, watching nothing.

She is already past wanting reassurance. She has cried about this in private. She is done crying where anyone can see. What she wants to know is whether it **gets better** — whether people learn to live with this, or whether it is always like this, always one bad moment away from disaster.

## Chapter 2, Scene 2 — The Well

Oris Hallow — a frightened man whose wife is sick — accuses Petra of fouling the well water. He is wrong (the water tastes metallic because of the [[Ulzammar Band]]'s field). He has chosen her because he needs to blame something.

**The PCs have options** — de-escalate, remove her, explain, authority, do nothing. **See [[02-scene-the-evening|Chapter 2 Scene 2]] for the full beat.**

## Memory

**Petra remembers who helped her.** That memory matters when she appears again later in Act I — in the scene called *The Night the Lantern Was Full* — and again, more painfully, when the settlement falls.

## [[Norseman]] Beat

If Norseman intervenes and identifies himself as a fellow mage, Petra will find him the next morning and ask a single question. Default: *"Have you ever burned someone by accident?"*

## Appearances

- The Night the Lantern Was Full: later in Act I.
- The fall of [[Lux Æterna]]: end of Act I.

## Current State (Session 2)

**She has been practicing in the Warrens at night** — alone, when she can't sleep, in a side-passage that nobody uses. She burned her hand again two days ago. [[Aldric Greypen]] found her, healed it without making her cry, and did not tell her parents. He should. He won't. See [Tajsh Intel](../Session-02/Tajsh%20Intel.md) #12.

**Why she practices alone:** because every time she practices in front of someone, the *someone* flinches, even people who try not to. She is ten years old and she has correctly read every involuntary microexpression in her life. The Warrens at night is the only place she can practice without flinching at herself.

**Norseman hook (continued from her main scene):** if Norseman engages with her at the Well and she trusts him, she may quietly ask if he'd practice with her at night. Not for instruction. For the company of someone who won't flinch. That request — if accepted — becomes the load-bearing relationship that pays off when the settlement falls.
