# Old Cira

Lothari female. Small, sun-bleached, wrapped in linen that was once white. Completely blind — milky-pale eyes that track sound rather than light. Age indeterminate: she could be sixty or ninety or older. She eats olives out of a clay jar she carries everywhere and hums tunelessly at intervals that have no apparent relationship to anything happening around her.

## Personality

Old Cira speaks in riddles that are annoying until you realize, two days later, that she was precisely correct. This is not a communication style she has chosen — it is the nature of dream-reading. She sees fragments: images, feelings, patterns. She reports what she sees as clearly as she can. The problem is that clarity, in the Lothari tradition, means precision about the vision, not interpretation of it. She doesn't say "the artifact will destroy the settlement." She says "the clock's hand is buried — don't wind it." Both statements are equally accurate.

She does not explain herself after the fact. She does not say "I told you so." She just opens another olive.

She has been places, done things, survived events that she does not discuss. Occasionally a reference slips through — a detail about a person or a city or a battle from thirty years ago that she handles with the familiarity of firsthand experience. Nobody in Lux Ætterna has put the full picture together. Sabel has the most fragments, because he pays attention to details. He hasn't asked.

## The Silent Network Role

Old Cira is a ranking member of the Silent Network. The Council of Dreamers assigned her to the foothills in SA:1487 specifically because the Scar of Dissonance near the First Age ruin had been showing anomalous resonance for decades — slowly, then more quickly. The dream-network had been tracking it.

When she "wandered into" Lux Ætterna and said the sand told her to stop, she was technically correct. The sand humming near that canyon is the Scar's mana resonance interacting with the desert's natural mana scarcity. She felt it the same way any trained Lothari mystic would. She set up her mat and waited.

She communicates with the Silent Network council through the Lothari dream-relay system: a meditative state that sends impressions along the mycelial and mana-thread network that connects Lothari mystics across the desert. It takes three days for a message to reach the Council and three days for a reply. She does this while appearing to sleep.

## On the Architect

Old Cira feels the wrongness of the Architect before she understands it. Every Lothari dream-tradition trains mystics to feel the difference between natural patterns and imposed ones — between the organic harmonics of the living world and the cold perfection of Kael'Zorai's ordered structures. When the Architect manifests, she goes very still. She says nothing that night.

The next morning she says: *"The light that walks with you casts no shadow."*

She will not elaborate. She is not being coy. The vision does not elaborate. She is reporting what she saw.

## GM Notes

Old Cira is the campaign's truth-teller — always accurate, never actionable in the moment, achingly clear in retrospect. Use her sparingly so her appearances carry weight. She should appear three or four times in Act I, once or twice between Act II arcs, and be visibly *missing* from Act III until a critical moment.

Her reveal as a Silent Network operative should come organically, when the party needs it — when they're in Lothari territory in Act II and she provides an introduction that nobody except a Network ranking member could give. Let them do the math.
