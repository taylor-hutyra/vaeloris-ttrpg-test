# Selin Voss

Gnomish female, late 20s. Small even by Gnome standards. Hair perpetually escaping whatever she's done to it. Chalk dust and alchemical residue on her hands at all times — the chalk is from the glyph-work, the residue from the binding agents she mixes into the ward-ink. Moves through spaces like she's always on her way somewhere, then stops completely when she's thinking, which can be unnerving if you're standing next to her when it happens.

## Personality

Selin speaks fast under stress — which is to say she almost always speaks fast. She punctuates sentences with "obviously" in a way that is not condescending but genuinely confident: she believes the things she knows to be obvious, and is confused and slightly apologetic when they aren't. She has been known to explain glyph resonance theory to people who asked a much simpler question, notice halfway through, apologize, and continue anyway.

She is not nervous about most things — she is nervous about the Stitching, specifically, and everything else is fine. The wards thinning is the one outcome she cannot accept, which means the current degradation rate is a constant low-level horror she is managing while doing everything else.

She is genuinely funny, occasionally, when she's relaxed enough to be. She has been relaxed twice in six months.

## The Stitching

Selin calls the Veil-Ward network "the Stitching" because each glyph connects to its neighbors like stitches in fabric — pull one thread and the tension redistributes across the whole pattern. She didn't invent the original wards (three mages who left years ago carved the first generation), but she redesigned the glyph-linkage system, doubled the coverage area, and created the maintenance protocol that keeps the aversion field stable.

She is the only person in the settlement who understands the full pattern. The ward-tenders know how to refresh individual glyphs — trace the lines with chalk-and-blood ink, feed mana into the carved channels — but only Selin knows how the glyphs interconnect, which sequences must be refreshed in which order, and what happens when one section weakens faster than its neighbors.

Her day job is still seamstress. Everyone in Lux Ætterna has a survival job and a day job. The needle-and-thread metaphor is not a coincidence — she thinks in patterns, connections, tension, and seams. The shop is still called Needle & Thread. She actually does mend clothes. People just also come in to report ward anomalies between hem adjustments.

## The Current Problem

The Stitching requires constant refreshing because the desert environment degrades the glyphs. Wind abrades the chalk. Sun bleaches the binding agents. The mana that powers the aversion field bleeds away into the surrounding rock. Eleven years ago, when the first wards were carved, a single mage could maintain the whole perimeter. Now the settlement has expanded, the glyph network has tripled in size, and the desert has been slowly winning the erosion race.

Current status:
- Six ward-tenders in three shifts (needs eight to run safely)
- The outer ring glyphs are refreshed every 48 hours (should be every 36)
- Two sections on the eastern wall are degrading faster than the rest — Selin suspects something in the nearby foothills is actively pulling mana from the rock
- At current staffing, the outer ring becomes unreliable in approximately six months

## The Self-Sustaining Design

Selin has designed a new glyph pattern that draws mana from the aquifer beneath the Deep Well — a renewable source that would eliminate the need for constant magical refreshing. The glyphs would still need physical maintenance (re-chalking from weather erosion), but that's labor, not magic. The ward-tenders could go from six exhausted mages to two people with chalk and a steady hand.

The problem: implementing it requires scraping off every existing glyph and re-carving the entire network from scratch. During the re-carving, the aversion field drops completely. The process takes approximately 48 hours. For two full days, the canyon is just a canyon — and any scout who flies over or walks past will see it for what it is.

Selin has been sitting on this for four months.

## GM Notes

Selin is the settlement's technical conscience — the person who sees the engineering problem clearly and is slowed by the human problem of communicating it. Her secret is an Act I optional objective that can give players real agency over the settlement's survival trajectory.

Let her expertise show in small ways: she notices things nobody else would, corrects small assumptions about how magic works, occasionally touches a canyon wall with a focused expression and makes a note. She should feel like someone who is always quietly monitoring three problems simultaneously, only one of which is visible at any moment.
