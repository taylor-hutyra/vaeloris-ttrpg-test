# Aldric Greypen

Human male, mid-to-late 60s. Slight, stooped slightly from decades of bending over texts. White hair that always looks like he slept on it. Moves carefully, as though the ground has occasionally surprised him. His eyes are the sharpest thing about him.

## Personality

Aldric has a terrible memory for names and a perfect memory for everything else. He calls people "my dear" until he remembers their names, which can take weeks, and then he uses their names with careful deliberation, as if each instance is a considered choice. When he is angry — which is rare — he uses the correct name immediately, which can be startling.

He teaches children to read in the mornings and mages to control their Spark in the afternoons. His philosophy is foundational: control before power, always, without exception. He has seen what power without control does. He watched it kill the smartest man he ever met, and he has spent thirty years making sure it doesn't happen again.

He is the kind of person who makes you feel like your question was the right question, which is a specific and rare gift.

## Melek the Grey

Aldric studied under a student of Melek the Grey — a woman named Sera who spent the four years between the First Revelation (SA:1401) and Melek's death (SA:1407) recording everything she could. She passed what she'd documented to Aldric before the Decree's bonfires reached her archive.

He memorized it. All of it. The original demonstration parameters, the theoretical framework Melek used to access the Spark for the first time, the errors he made in the final year that accelerated the burnout of his mind. The sections that show that mortal magic is survivable under certain conditions. The sections that were specifically ordered burned by the Decree's architects, which suggests they knew something they didn't want anyone else to know.

He has never written it down. He doesn't trust paper. He teaches from memory, shapes his lessons around the knowledge, and has been hoping for twenty years that a student would ask the right question.

When asked about Melek, he goes quiet. He says: *"He died six years after the First Revelation. The magic burned through his mind like fire through dry paper. He was the smartest man who ever lived, and it killed him."*

This is true. It is also incomplete.

## The Chain

The chain of knowledge is two links:
- Melek the Grey → Sera (student, recorder, four years of documentation)
- Sera → Aldric Greypen (archive, now living memory)

If Aldric dies without passing this on, the chain ends. The Decree wins through attrition. He knows this. It keeps him up at night with a clarity that the teaching and the wine only partially blur.

## GM Notes

Aldric's memory is the campaign's hidden resource — the kind that matters in Act III when the party needs to understand what the Crown actually does and what it could do differently. Don't make it obvious. Let him slip a detail here and there, let players build the picture from fragments. The moment when they realize what he's been carrying is earned, not delivered.

His terrible memory for names is played for warmth, not comedy. He genuinely tries. He apologizes genuinely when he gets it wrong. The first time he gets a player character's name right without prompting is a moment worth noting.
