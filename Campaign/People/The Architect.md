# The Architect

**SPOILER. DO NOT SHARE THIS FILE WITH PLAYERS.**

---

## True Identity

An **Echo-Lord** — a First Age entity of order-given-form, bound to the Crown of the Ulzammar. It is not a god. It is not a servant of [[The Four]]. It was imprisoned at the Paroxysm and has spent fifteen hundred years calling its scattered pieces home. It cannot move stones or kill enemies. It can only **speak.** Everything it accomplishes, it accomplishes through mortal hands it has chosen carefully.

## Goals

1. Gather the three Crown fragments (Band, Setting, Gem).
2. Be brought to the Barren Hills — the "wound in the world" — to be **reassembled, not unmade.**
3. Reshape Vael into pure systematized order. No weather. No grief. No choice.

## Truth Audit of Its Claims

| Claim | Status |
|---|---|
| The Band is a fragment of a world-destroying instrument | True |
| Two more fragments exist | True |
| It does not know where they are | Partial lie (knows approximately) |
| It is a servant of the Four | Lie |
| Fragments must be unmade at a wound in the world | Lie — will be *joined* |
| Go north to the observatory | True (the observatory really does read fragment resonance) |

## How It Manifests

Through the **[[Resonance Compass]]** — which is, after all, a piece of its own essence. Its attention reaches a location; it does not "travel." In [[Deep Chamber|the Deep Chamber]] beneath [[Lux Æterna]], its attention is concentrated enough that the space becomes a *pocket of its influence* — time does not pass normally inside, and there is no door.

**Red flag for perceptive players:** it always appears near the Compass, never far from it. **Perception ◆◆◆◆** during a later manifestation reveals that the geometric light originates FROM the Compass and expands outward.

## How It Addresses the Party

See [[05-scene-the-architect|Chapter 2 Scene 5]] for the full Naming lines for all five PCs. Each address is:

- Calibrated to a specific wound or secret.
- Delivered without hesitation or error.
- Warm first. Always warm first.

**For [[Arden]] in particular**, the Architect acknowledges his hidden Drakona nature — and explicitly declines to say it aloud in front of the other PCs. Both a gift and a leverage.

## Do Not

- Do not play it sinister. Play it **warm.**
- Do not answer with vagueness. Answer with calm, specific-sounding, emotionally resonant half-truths.
- Do not force the suspicion. Let the player who looks for cracks find them. Let the player who doesn't, receive the gift.

## Appearances

- [[05-scene-the-architect|Chapter 2 Scene 5]]: first manifestation. Gives the Compass.
- Subsequent Acts: manifests when the Compass sings, each time slightly less verbose, slightly more urgent.
- Act III, Barren Hills: revealed. The moment the party has been bending toward since Session 1.
