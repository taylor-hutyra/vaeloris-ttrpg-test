# Session 3 — Morning in Lux Æterna → The Tunnel Mouth

> *The morning after the journal. Tajsh has read it. The Band is still in the party's pack. The city is wrong in small ways, and the wrongness is following them.*

**What carries over from Session 2:**
- The party delivered Torvin's journal to Tajsh. They withheld the Ulzammar Band.
- Tajsh read the journal overnight. He has seen the three-ring symbol before. He suspects there is more than they handed him.
- Stitching is stuttering. Lanterns are out of rhythm. The Band's temporal field is bleeding into the canyon.
- Valeth has not yet realized the song is coming from the Band itself.

**The design principle for this session:**
> **Do not punish the party for keeping the Band. Make keeping it create attention, leverage, and consequence.** Their secrecy writes the world's reactions. Tajsh becomes suspicious. Hask becomes perceptive. Selin becomes terrified. Cira becomes direct. When the Architect speaks next session, it will be intimate — because they kept it close.

**Two-session arc:**
- **Session 3 (this one):** Morning sandbox → Tajsh confrontation → Stitching flicker → Fight at the well → Cira's summons. **End on the cliffhanger.**
- **Session 4:** Descent through the Children's Tunnel → The Architect → Tajsh's counter-pitch.
- **Architect material is held for Session 4** by default. If the table blows through this session in <90 min, see §10 (Architect as Bonus Ending).

## §2 — Scene 1: Morning Open (5–8 min)

### Read-Aloud — Morning After

> *Morning in Lux Æterna is wrong in small ways. The lanterns have steadied — almost — but the wave of pulses you watched last night has left everyone moving a little carefully. People are talking quieter than they should at this hour. The market is open, but the bargaining has the edge of conversation that does not want to attract attention.*
>
> *Tajsh has your journal. The Band is still in your pack. Korel — the runner — passed your door at first light and said Tajsh would send for you "when he was ready." Which means you have a few hours.*
>
> *Marta puts the porridge down in front of you and does not ask how you slept.*

### The Offer

Drop the choice on the table directly. No exposition. No leading.

> *"You have a morning in this canyon and a problem in your pack. What do you do first?"*

**Three nodes available** (players can pick in any order, mix and match — they're modular):

| Node | What's there | Time |
|------|--------------|------|
| **A. Buy Information** — work the city for leads on the three-ring symbol | Streetwise / Knowledge Lore / Negotiation; rumor ladder | 8–10 min |
| **B. Shop** — Hask, Tomm, Selin, the market | Gear + Band anomaly clues from Hask and Selin | 5–10 min per stop |
| **C. Earn Lumens** — courier work, glyph repair, bone-dice at the Lantern | Skill challenges; money + reputation | 5–10 min per job |

The party can do **two to three of these** before Tajsh sends for them. Korel will appear when the table has spent ~30 min in the city OR when at least one Band anomaly has fired in public.

### Background Tension (running under every scene)

- The Band's effects are starting (see §3.4 — Band Anomaly Toolkit).
- Selin is up a ladder on the canyon wall, retouching a glyph that keeps fading. She is muttering.
- Cira is sitting at her mat. Not humming. Just watching.
- The silent stranger is at the Grey Flame, as always.

### Exit
Once they've made their first choice, jump straight to that node. No further intro.

## §3 — Scene 2: The City Sandbox (30–40 min)

> Modular. Players pick. Each node is a self-contained 5–10 min scene. Run two to three before Korel arrives.

### §3.1 — Node A: Buy Information

**Goal:** Let them ask around about the three-ring symbol. Reward investigation with a *rumor ladder*.

**Where they ask determines who answers:**
- **At the market stalls:** Marta-adjacent gossip. Streetwise.
- **At the Dusty Lantern:** off-duty militia. Streetwise or Charm.
- **Quietly, of an academic:** Aldric at the Schoolroom. Knowledge (Lore).
- **Crookedly, of a fixer:** Tajsh's runner Korel. Negotiation.

**Rumor Ladder** — same regardless of who they ask. Roll once per source.

| Result | What they get |
|--------|---------------|
| **Easy ◆ — Success** | *"That symbol is old. Older than the settlement. Ask Tajsh, Cira, or Selin — they're the ones who'd know."* |
| **Average ◆◆ — Success** | *"Three rings, like a chord. Not a merchant mark. Not Lothari. Not Varnathi. Something older."* |
| **Hard ◆◆◆ — Success** | *"A Shadewalker once paid good money for a rubbing with that geometry. The rubbing was burned within the hour, and the man who paid for it stopped using his right name."* |
| **Triumph (on any of the above)** | Someone — a drunk, a child, Aldric in passing — says: *"There's a book. Torvin wrote about a book, didn't he?"* |
| **Threat / Despair** | Someone makes a note of who asked. (Suspicion Clock +1.) |

**Important:** the three NPCs (Tajsh, Selin, Cira) each react to the symbol *independently* if the party shows it to them. This is the real investigation. The rumor ladder is the warm-up.

#### When Shown to Tajsh: **Provenance**
*"This is not a maker's mark. It's a warning label."* He'll connect it to First Age / Ulzammar / Shadewalker restricted knowledge — but only after his confrontation in §4. Hold this until then.

#### When Shown to Selin: **Notation**
She is on a ladder. They climb up or wait below. When they show her:

> *Selin stops. Takes the sketch with both hands. Holds it close to her eyes. Holds it farther. Closes one eye. Mutters. Then:*
>
> *"That isn't a logo. It's notation. See the spacing? Those rings aren't drawn. They're **tuned.**"*
>
> *(She hands it back. Doesn't say more. The chalk in her hand has trembled enough to leave a faint white line on her wrist.)*

#### When Shown to Cira: **Listening Back**
She is on her mat. Doesn't open her eyes when they hand it to her. Touches it with her fingertips.

> *"You keep asking what it means. Wrong question."*
>
> *(She gives the paper back without folding it.)*
>
> *"Ask what heard you ask."*

**If the party collects all three reads** — Provenance, Notation, Listening Back — they earn a bonus next session: **◼ to the first check at the Architect** (Perception, to spot the no-reflection tell). Do not telegraph the bonus. They have to earn it organically.

### §3.2 — Node B: Shop (and have the Band create problems)

Three shops worth visiting. Each is also a clue-scene because of the Band.

#### Hask — The Crystal Counter

He doesn't say hello. He's already watching the still on the workbench, which has been simmering for forty minutes and produced nothing.

> *Hask puts a bottle down in front of you. The liquid inside climbs the glass for half a second before remembering gravity. He looks at your packs. Not your faces.*
>
> *"Something you're carrying is making time behave impolitely."*

He does not demand the Band. He clocks the wrongness. **Do not punish them for evasion — escalate it instead.**

| If they... | Hask responds |
|------------|---------------|
| Say nothing | He nods once. *"Right. Buy what you came for, then."* (Suspicion Clock +0 — Hask is not Tajsh, he's just noticing.) |
| Lie about it | His good eye drops to whoever lied. He doesn't say anything. Just looks. They feel it. |
| Show him (uncover the Band) | He breathes out, long and slow. *"...that's what I thought."* See §5 — **The Eye Beat** (if trust is earned, he removes the eyepatch and reads it). |
| Ask what he sees | *"I see something measuring."* (One sentence. Then back to business.) |

**Inventory** — see [SESSION-02-Locations.md §6](../Session-02/SESSION-02-Locations.md). Hask will offer one Sand-Eye Drops at half price (10 Lumens) regardless of how the conversation goes. *"You'll want it where you're going."*

#### Tomm — Iron Shelf

No anomalies here. Tomm is the still point in the morning. He fixes things, sells gear at standard prices, and talks metallurgy if asked. Use this stop to *reset* between heavier scenes. If a PC asks a smithing question, give them a free piece of insight.

#### Selin — Needle & Thread (and the ladder)

Selin is **on the canyon wall** retouching a glyph that is fading. She is twenty feet up, roped to the cliff face, chalk-dust on her cheek.

**Available scene (Sandbox activity):** A PC can offer to help retouch the glyph.
- **Knowledge (Arcana) Hard ◆◆◆.**
- **Success:** they reinforce the failing patch. **Reward: 10 Lumens** + ◼ on one future social check with any Lux Æterna resident.
- **Failure:** the glyph holds, but the PC takes 2 strain. Selin gives them an Ash-Root Paste at cost.
- **Despair:** the chalk line briefly *reverses itself.* Selin panics. *"No. No, no, no. Glyphs don't unwind. They fail, they don't unwind."* The PC takes 3 strain. Selin recarves it overnight. Suspicion in the canyon escalates (Tajsh hears about it within the hour).

**If they show her the symbol:** see §3.1 above.

### §3.3 — Node C: Earn Lumens

Three quick jobs. Each is ~5 minutes of dice and a payout.

#### Job 1 — Retouch a Stitching Glyph (see §3.2 above)
*Selin's offer doubles as both shop scene and paid job.*

#### Job 2 — Bone-Dice at the Dusty Lantern
- Skulduggery or Cool, opposed by Marta's off-duty militia.
- Win or lose **5–15 Lumens.**
- **Advantage:** buys one rumor from the d10 Rumor Table ([SESSION-02-Locations.md §5 Lux Æterna.md](../World/Vael/Lux%20%C3%86terna/Lux%20%C3%86terna.md)).
- **Threat:** somebody at the next table starts paying attention to them. (Suspicion Clock +1 if the player is being loud about winning.)
- **Triumph:** one of the militia, drunk, says: *"You hear about the dark relay station? Six days silent."*

#### Job 3 — Courier Run
Tajsh's runner Korel finds them with a sealed package. *"Boss says you've got steady hands. Selin or Sabel — your pick. Ten Lumens on delivery."*

- **Athletics or Streetwise ◆◆** to navigate the morning market without being slowed.
- **Success:** 10 Lumens.
- **Triumph:** they overhear *"relay station dark six days"* (regardless of which recipient).
- **Threat:** someone — a stranger — asks them, casually, why Tajsh trusts them with sealed packages. They don't get an answer if they ask back.

### §3.4 — The Band Anomaly Toolkit

> The Band is in their pack. The world reacts. **Use these freely throughout the Sandbox** — at least 2–3 anomalies should fire before Tajsh sends for them.

| Location | Anomaly | Trigger / Read |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| **Hask's shop** | Liquids climb glass before falling. The still simmers but does not boil. Reactions take minutes instead of seconds. | *"That thing is measuring something."* (Hask, sees this on arrival.) |
| **Selin's glyph (ladder)** | Chalk line briefly reverses itself. | Despair on the Arcana check — OR — fires automatically if the Band-holder stands within ten feet of the ladder for more than a minute. *"Glyphs don't unwind."* |
| **The well** | Water surface freezes in a perfect circle around the Band-holder's reflection. Stays frozen for ten seconds. Returns to ripple-state on its own. | Triggers when the Band-holder approaches the well. The guards (Ral + second) notice. They do not report it yet. |
| **The Grey Flame shrine** | The candle stops moving. Does not go out. Flame holds its shape like a held breath. | Triggers when the Band-holder passes within five feet. **The silent stranger looks up — once, directly at the Band-holder — and then looks back down.** That's the whole beat. No words. (Slow burn. Pays off later.) |
| **The Lantern (Marta's)** | The cookpot's contents stop steaming for a beat. Then resume. Nobody but Marta notices, and she says nothing. | Triggers in passing. |
| **A market stall** | A merchant's coin spins on its edge for too long. Six full rotations before falling. | Random. Drop this anywhere ambient. |

**Valeth's song** — **NEW MECHANIC.** The hum he heard at arrival is *coming from the Band.* It is loudest when the Band-holder is closest to him. It is silent when he is far from the Band (>30 ft). Hand his player a private note when an anomaly fires near him:

> *"The hum is louder. The Band is humming it. You don't think anyone else can hear it. Yet."*

This will pay off either at the Architect (Session 4) or in a Scar elsewhere in the campaign — wherever you want the realization to land.

### Sandbox Exit

When **at least two** of the following are true, Korel arrives with Tajsh's summons:
- Tajsh has had the journal in his hands for at least one full activity (this is already true at session start — he had it overnight).
- The table has spent ~30 minutes in the city.
- At least one Band anomaly has fired in public.

Korel doesn't say much: *"Boss says now."*

## §4 — Scene 3: Tajsh Summons (15–20 min) — MUST HIT

> Tajsh has read the journal. He knows the symbol. He suspects they kept something back. He is not hostile. He is **a friend deciding whether they can be trusted.**

### The Tajsh Suspicion Clock

> Visible via behavior, not numbers. The clock is what *Tajsh does and says* across this scene — not a hidden GM count.

**Start the clock based on what happened in the Sandbox:**

| Sandbox behavior | Clock tick |
|------------------|-----------|
| They refused to discuss the journal with anyone | +1 |
| A Band anomaly fired in a public place (well, market, shrine) | +1 |
| Hask or Selin noticed the wrongness | +1 |
| They lied poorly to anyone (failed Deception) | +1 |
| They asked extensively about the symbol without showing what they know | +1 |
| They earned a Triumph on info-gathering (Tajsh hears: they're sharp) | -1 (good — he respects this) |

**The clock determines Tajsh's escalation across this scene** (1–2 ticks: he's mostly probing; 3–4: he names it; 5+: he gives the speech).

### Opening — Tajsh's Office

> *Tajsh is at his desk. The journal is open in front of him to the three-ring page. The kettle is whistling and being ignored. He doesn't stand up. He gestures at the chairs — two of them, for five of you. He waits to see who sits.*

He lets them speak first. Whoever speaks first, he listens. He doesn't interrupt.

### The Monologue (still useful — deliver slowly, let it land)

> *"I have seen this twice in my life. The first time was when I was twenty-two, and I was standing in a Shadewalker archive in Ostmark, and a sealed crate came in, and the man who'd brought it would not put it down. He set it on a stone table, and he stepped back from it like it was a cobra. Everybody in the room was very still. Very careful.*
>
> *"The second time was seventeen years ago. Barren Hills. I was hired to seal a chamber in an old ruin. The contract was specific. Carve this mark on the door. Carve it deep. Walk away. Don't look back. They paid me four times the going rate, and I still wish they hadn't.*
>
> *"This — what Torvin drew — this is the same mark."*

(Beat.)

> *"You gave me the journal. Good. Useful. Incomplete."*

He taps the page.

> *"Torvin did not die for a drawing. So I'm going to ask this once politely: **what else came out of that mine?**"*

### The Press — Available Player Responses

**Don't railroad. Let them choose how to handle it.** Possible moves:

| Player move | Check | Outcome |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| **Deception** — lie about the Band | Deception vs. Tajsh's Vigilance ◆◆◆ | Success: he backs off **out loud**; the Suspicion Clock ticks +1 silently. He sends Korel to ask Hask and Selin later. Failure: see "If he catches the lie" below. |
| **Negotiation** — "we'll share if you share" | Negotiation ◆◆◆ | Success: Tajsh shares more of his Shadewalker past — *"I was an archivist. I am not, anymore. I left because I read a thing I should not have read. That's all you get."* He'll trade fact for fact going forward. |
| **Charm** — appeal to trust | Charm ◆◆ | Success: he softens. Doesn't drop the question, but his next line is *"I'm asking because I want to help. Not because I want to take it."* |
| **Honesty** — tell him about the Band | (no check) | He breathes out. Long. *"Thank you. Now we can actually talk."* Suspicion Clock resets to 0. He asks to **see** it, not take it. They can show or refuse — refusal is fine, but he'll insist on a description. |
| **Misdirect** — "Torvin had other notes that burned" | Deception ◆◆◆ | Same as straight lie above. He may or may not buy it. |
| **Physical threat / aggression** | (any) | He doesn't blink. *"You're in my city, in my office, eating my food. Try again."* — does NOT escalate to violence himself. Sabel hears about it within the hour and shows up at the Lantern that night. |

### Tajsh's Core Position (deliver if the conversation stalls)

> *"I don't need you obedient. I need you honest enough that you don't accidentally kill everyone in this canyon."*

### The Suspicion Speech (only if the clock is at 3+)

> *"You are not prisoners. You are not employees anymore, either. You are people carrying a problem through my home. Help me understand it, and I am your friend. Make me chase you for it, and I become very good at that."*
>
> *(He doesn't say this angrily. He says it the way someone says the weather is changing.)*

### If He Catches the Lie

He doesn't accuse. He doesn't escalate in the room. But he stops sharing. The conversation gets shorter. He stands.

> *"Alright. We're done for the morning. Eat. Rest. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking."*

That night, he goes to Sabel privately. **Not to arrest them.** To prepare containment if the thing they carry threatens the canyon. The players will not learn this in Session 3 — it pays off later.

### Tajsh DEFERS the Ask

Regardless of how the conversation goes, Tajsh does **not** give the party an objective in this scene. The Book-Pitch is held for next session (after the Architect). His exit line:

> *"I need a night with this. Eat. Rest. Walk the canyon. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking."*

He polishes his thumb-ring. Sets the journal down very carefully. Looks at them once more.

> *"And whatever you're not telling me — keep it close. Don't put it down. Don't open it. Don't take it anywhere you don't want it heard."*

(He doesn't say *"the Band."* He doesn't have to.)

### Exit

The party leaves the Vault. **The flicker fires within the next minute** — whether they're on the steps, back at the Lantern, or walking through the market. The geography flows toward the well.

## §5 — Scene 4: The Flicker (3–5 min) — MUST HIT

### Read-Aloud — The Flicker

> *You're walking the market — heading back to the Lantern, or to the well, or to wherever you go to think. The morning has its usual half-rhythm: bargaining, sandals on cobble, somebody's child trying to convince somebody's goat to walk in a particular direction.*
>
> *Then every lantern in the canyon goes out at once.*
>
> *Not one by one. **All at once.** Not flickering. Out.*
>
> *One heartbeat.*
>
> *Then they come back. Too bright. Too steady. Then they settle.*
>
> *Somewhere up at the Outlook, Sabel is shouting. You can't make out the words. But the sound of it is the sound of a man who has just seen something he hoped he would never see.*

### Available Rolls — Any PC

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Sense what just happened | Perception ◆◆ | *Something outside just noticed this place. The Stitching didn't fail — it was interrupted. A piece of you knows you were briefly very visible.* |
| Read the magical state | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | *The wards didn't fail. They were forced open for one heartbeat by something the Band did. Triumph: that something just answered something from below.* |
| Spot the response on the rim | Vigilance ◆◆ | *Sabel was already moving before the lights came back. Whatever this is, he was halfway expecting it.* |

### The Eye Beat (OPTIONAL — only if Hask earned the trust in §3.2)

If a PC went into the Crystal Counter, showed Hask the Band (or strongly hinted), and earned his trust:

> *Hask is at the door of his shop. He has his good eye on you. The other — the patched one — he reaches up and lifts the patch.*
>
> *(Show, don't tell, for one beat. Then:)*
>
> *"There. That's what's wrong. The light off you is moving in two times at once. There's a clock running on you. And it's running fast."*

Then he puts the patch back. He has been the only person in the canyon to see the Band's signature directly. He will not say it again to anyone.

### Cira Stands Up

Wherever the party is in the market, they can see Cira's corner from somewhere. **She stands up.** First time all morning. Her mat is empty. She is walking — slowly, deliberately — toward the path that leads to the Warrens.

Don't say anything to the party about it yet. Let them notice.

### Sabel's Shout — The Handoff

About thirty seconds after the lights come back, **Sabel arrives** — at a sprint, from the rim path. He's not in armor. He left his post fast. He doesn't even slow down when he sees them.

> *"Where's the rest of you. **Where's the rest of you.** Tajsh — out. Out of the Vault. Get to the well. Something just got in and it's hunting."*

He doesn't wait for an answer. He goes.

The party can choose: follow Sabel toward the well, or do something else. **Most parties will follow.** If they hesitate, push — someone screams from the market floor. Then the screams get cut short, in a way that screams don't usually cut short.

The geography pulls everyone toward the well.

## §6 — Scene 5: Fight at the Well (15–20 min) — MUST HIT

### Read-Aloud — Arrival at the Well

> *The market has cleared faster than you've ever seen it clear. People are pressed against the cliff face on both sides, behind toppled stalls, frozen in the doorways of shops. The cobbles around the well are empty in a perfect ten-foot circle — nobody is standing inside that circle and nobody is going to.*
>
> *The water in the well is freezing. Not from the top down. From the bottom up. Reflected lantern-light is locked in the surface like flies in amber.*
>
> *And out of the shadow under the well-rim, something is**crystallizing into the air.** It is shaped roughly like a person. It is not finished yet. Its outline is still settling — like a sketch someone is still drawing — and where the lines aren't done yet, you can see the canyon wall through it.*
>
> *It turns its head. Toward whoever in your party is carrying the Band.*

### Battlefield

```
[Cliff face — civilians pressed against it]
                |
[Toppled fruit stall]          [Burning lantern line — Tava's stand]
                |
            [THE WELL]
        (Wraithling crystallizing here)
                |
        [Empty cobble circle — 10 ft]
                |
        [Selin's ladder — wall behind well, half-finished glyph at 20 ft]
                |
[Tajsh sprinting in from the south]    [Sabel sprinting in from the north]
```

**Range bands:**
- The Band-holder: **Engaged** with the Wraithling on its first action.
- The rest of the party: **Short** range to the well on arrival (~10 ft away, on the empty cobbles).
- Civilians: **Short** range, pressed against cliff faces.
- Selin's ladder: **Short** range to the well, vertical.
- Sabel and Tajsh: arrive at the **start of Round 2** unless the party acts to keep them out.

### Stat Block — Scar-touched Wraithling

```
Type: Rival
Wounds: 8 | Strain: — | Soak: 2 | Defense: 0/0
Brawn 3 | Agility 3 | Intellect 1 | Cunning 2 | Willpower 3 | Presence 1
Skills: Brawl 2, Perception 2, Vigilance 1

Equipment / Attack:
  Sync-Slash (Brawl) — Damage 5; Crit 4; Engaged; Burn 1

Abilities:
  • Out-of-Sync — Once per turn, when targeted by an attack, the
    Wraithling resolves a beat late. Add ◆ to the incoming attack
    for the rest of the round. (Lasts 1 round per use.)
  • Phase-Step — Maneuver: move from Engaged to Short range through
    a wall, floor, or shadow. Ignores difficult terrain.
  • Hungering Geometry — On its turn, it prioritizes whoever is
    holding the Band. If the Band-holder is more than Short range
    away, it Phase-Steps toward them as its maneuver, no question.
  • Wards-Bound — At the START of round 3 (or when its Wounds
    reach 0, whichever first), the wards re-anchor and it dissolves
    into ash and ozone. The fight ends.
```

### Battlefield Features (encourage creative use)

| Feature | Check | Effect |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Crowd panic** | Coordination or Leadership ◆◆ | Clear a band of civilians from a target area. Failure: a civilian takes the next stray hit. |
| **Toppled stall** | Athletics ◆ (vault) or ◆◆ (shove into creature) | Shove deals 2 wounds and counts as Engaged → Short displacement. |
| **Burning lantern line** | Ranged ◆◆ or Skulduggery ◆◆ | Drop burning oil between creature and Band-holder. Creates light barrier — Wraithling adds ◆ to attack rolls through it for one round. |
| **Well-chain** | Brawl or Athletics ◆◆ | Restrain it for one round. (It will Phase-Step out, but the action delay costs it a turn.) |
| **Selin's half-finished glyph** (the one she was working on this morning, now visible on the wall above the well) | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | Re-anchor the ward **early.** The Wraithling dissolves at the **end of this round** instead of round 3. **Triumph:** the wards re-anchor harder than they would have — Legio Null's fix on the canyon is less precise. (Pay off later.) |
| **Sabel's arrival (Round 2)** | (NPC action — GM-controlled) | Sabel takes one shot at the Wraithling with a hand crossbow. Hits on 50/50 odds. If hits: 4 damage, plus the Wraithling Phase-Steps away from Sabel and toward Band-holder. |
| **Tajsh's arrival (Round 2)** | (NPC action — GM-controlled) | Tajsh does not fight. He arrives, sees the well, sees who the thing is going for, and the look on his face is the look of a man whose worst guess just got confirmed. Track this — it informs his behavior afterwards. |

### Win Conditions

The fight can end **three ways:**

1. **Kill it** — Reduce Wounds to 0. Standard.
2. **Re-anchor the ward** — Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ at Selin's glyph (or two cumulative successes if multiple PCs try). Wraithling dissolves at end of current round.
3. **Round 3 timeout** — At the start of round 3, the Stitching re-anchors automatically. The fight ends regardless. (Sabel and the ward-tenders did this off-screen.)

**Design intent:** Don't TPK. The wards close. The party wins because the canyon's defenses caught up — *they* didn't necessarily save the day. That ambiguity is the point.

### Civilian Casualties (track this)

If the party ignores civilian protection (no Coordination/Leadership checks attempted, or repeated failures), **one civilian takes a Sync-Slash.** Not a named NPC. Use this only if the table is being callous — it sets up Sabel's mood for the rest of the session.

### After Combat — Read-Aloud

> *Whatever it was, it isn't there anymore. There's no body. There's a faint smear of grey ash on the cobbles where it stood, and the smell of ozone, and the well-water is steaming where it had been freezing. The lantern on the nearest stall is steady. Sabel is breathing hard. Tajsh is staring at the well.*
>
> *Somewhere in the crowd, a child is crying — briefly, then quiet. Marta is at the door of the Lantern, holding the policy.*
>
> *Cira is at the mouth of the Warrens. She has been watching the whole time. She is not humming.*

### Rewards

- **10–20 Lumens** from grateful market folk (passed quietly into a PC's hand by someone they don't know).
- **+1 Lux Æterna reputation** if they protected civilians (any successful Coordination/Leadership).
- **One free or discounted item** from Hask or Tomm in a future visit if they were visibly heroic.

## §7 — Scene 6: Cira's Summons (3–5 min) — END HERE

### Read-Aloud

> *Cira is waiting at the mouth of the Warrens. She is not on her mat. Her mat is empty. The dream-catcher is in her hand — she has untied it from the iron nail above her corner and carried it with her. She is not humming for the first time today.*
>
> *Sabel is on the rim somewhere, shouting orders. Tajsh is on the steps of his Vault watching the well steam. The canyon is moving again — slowly, carefully — but moving.*
>
> *Cira looks at the party with eyes that don't see and have never seen and somehow find each of you anyway.*

**The line — deliver slowly:**

> ***"The thing under the well has started answering the thing in your pack. If you want to know what you carry, come now. If you wait, it will choose someone smaller."***

She turns. She walks into the Warrens.

She does not look back.

### END SESSION

Drop the curtain here. **Do not let the table push further.** This is the cliffhanger.

If a player asks "can we follow her" — *"You can. Next session."*

If a player asks "what does she mean by 'someone smaller'" — *"Petra. She means Petra. But she didn't say it. Next session."*

Mark the session. Confirm the next session opens at the mouth of the Children's Tunnel. End on Cira's words.
