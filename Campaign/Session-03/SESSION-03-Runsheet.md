> *Morning in Lux Æterna is wrong in small ways. The lanterns have steadied — almost — but the wave of pulses you watched last night has left everyone moving a little carefully. ### The Three Independent Reads (the real investigation)

If shown the symbol directly. **Track which the party collects.**

**Tajsh — Provenance** *(during §3 confrontation)*
> *"This is not a maker's mark. It's a warning label."*
Connects to First Age / Ulzammar / Shadewalker restricted knowledge.

**Selin — Notation** *(on ladder; she'll come down)*
> *"That isn't a logo. It's notation. See the spacing? Those rings aren't drawn. They're tuned."*
She hands it back. Doesn't say more. Chalk on her wrist trembles.

**Cira — Listening Back** *(on mat; doesn't open eyes)*
> *"You keep asking what it means. Wrong question. Ask what heard you ask."*

**Bonus for collecting all three:** ◼ to the first check at the Architect next session (no announcement).

## §3 — Tajsh Summons (15–20 min) — PROTECT

> Tajsh suspects they kept something back. He is **not hostile.** He is a friend deciding whether they can be trusted.

### Suspicion Clock (visible-via-behavior, not numeric)

| Sandbox behavior | Tick |
|------------------|------|
| Refused to discuss the journal with anyone | +1 |
| A Band anomaly fired in public (well, market, shrine) | +1 |
| Hask or Selin noticed the wrongness | +1 |
| They lied poorly (failed Deception) | +1 |
| Asked extensively about the symbol without showing what they know | +1 |
| Earned a Triumph on info-gathering (Tajsh respects this) | −1 |

**Behavior bands:**
- **0–2:** Probing. He asks. He doesn't press.
- **3–4:** Names it. He says the speech (below).
- **5+:** He stands. He stops sharing. The conversation gets short. (See "If he catches the lie.")

### Opening

> *Tajsh is at his desk. The journal is open in front of him to the three-ring page. The kettle is whistling and being ignored. He doesn't stand up. He gestures at the chairs — two of them, for five of you. He waits to see who sits.*

### The Monologue (deliver slowly)

> *"I have seen this twice in my life. The first time was when I was twenty-two, and I was standing in a Shadewalker archive in Ostmark, and a sealed crate came in, and the man who'd brought it would not put it down. He set it on a stone table and stepped back from it like it was a cobra. Everybody in the room was very still. Very careful.*
>
> *"The second time was seventeen years ago. Barren Hills. I was hired to seal a chamber in an old ruin. The contract was specific. Carve this mark on the door. Carve it deep. Walk away. Don't look back. They paid me four times the going rate, and I still wish they hadn't.*
>
> *"This — what Torvin drew — this is the same mark."*

*(Beat.)*

> *"You gave me the journal. Good. Useful. Incomplete."*

He taps the page.

> *"Torvin did not die for a drawing. So I'm going to ask this once politely: **what else came out of that mine?**"*

### Player Responses — Available Moves

| Player move | Check | Outcome |
|-------------|-------|---------|
| **Lie** about the Band | Deception vs. Tajsh Vigilance ◆◆◆ | Success: he backs off out loud; Clock +1 silently. Sends Korel to question Hask/Selin later. Failure: see "Caught" below. |
| **Negotiate** — fact for fact | Negotiation ◆◆◆ | Success: he shares — *"I was an archivist. I am not, anymore. I left because I read a thing I should not have read. That's all you get."* From here, fact-trade is on. |
| **Charm** — appeal to trust | Charm ◆◆ | Success: *"I'm asking because I want to help. Not because I want to take it."* Doesn't drop the question. |
| **Honesty** — tell him about the Band | (no check) | Long breath. *"Thank you. Now we can actually talk."* Clock resets to 0. He asks to **see** — not to take. They can show or refuse. |
| **Misdirect** — "Torvin had other notes that burned" | Deception ◆◆◆ | Same as straight lie. He may or may not buy it. |
| **Physical threat** | (any) | *"You're in my city, in my office, eating my food. Try again."* Does not escalate to violence himself. Sabel hears within the hour. |

### Tajsh's Core Position (use if stalled)

> *"I don't need you obedient. I need you honest enough that you don't accidentally kill everyone in this canyon."*

### The Suspicion Speech (only if Clock ≥ 3)

> *"You are not prisoners. You are not employees anymore, either. You are people carrying a problem through my home. Help me understand it, and I am your friend. Make me chase you for it, and I become very good at that."*

*(He doesn't say this angrily. He says it the way someone says the weather is changing.)*

### If He Catches the Lie

He doesn't accuse in the room. He stops sharing. Conversation gets short. He stands.

> *"Alright. We're done for the morning. Eat. Rest. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking."*

That night, he goes to Sabel privately. **Not to arrest.** To prepare containment. Pays off later.

### Exit — Tajsh Defers the Ask

Regardless of clock state, he does **not** give them an objective. Book-Pitch is held for Session 4.

> *"I need a night with this. Eat. Rest. Walk the canyon. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking."*

*(Polishes thumb-ring. Sets the journal down very carefully.)*

> *"And whatever you're not telling me — keep it close. Don't put it down. Don't open it. Don't take it anywhere you don't want it heard."*

*(He doesn't say "the Band." He doesn't have to.)*

**The flicker fires within the next minute.**

## §4 — The Flicker (3–5 min)

### Read-Aloud

> *You're walking the market — heading back to the Lantern, or to the well, or to wherever you go to think. The morning has its usual half-rhythm: bargaining, sandals on cobble, somebody's child trying to convince somebody's goat to walk in a particular direction.*
>
> *Then every lantern in the canyon goes out at once.*
>
> *Not one by one. **All at once.** Not flickering. Out.*
>
> *One heartbeat.*
>
> *Then they come back. Too bright. Too steady. Then they settle.*
>
> *Somewhere up at the Outlook, Sabel is shouting. You can't make out the words. But the sound of it is the sound of a man who has just seen something he hoped he would never see.*

### Rolls Available (any PC)

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Sense what happened | Perception ◆◆ | *Something outside just noticed this place. The Stitching didn't fail — it was interrupted.* |
| Read the magical state | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | *The wards didn't fail. They were forced open for one heartbeat by something the Band did. Triumph: that something just answered something from below.* |
| Spot Sabel's response | Vigilance ◆◆ | *Sabel was already moving before the lights came back. Whatever this is, he was halfway expecting it.* |

### The Eye Beat (OPTIONAL — only if Hask trust earned in §2.2)

> *Hask is at the door of his shop. He has his good eye on you. The other — the patched one — he reaches up and lifts the patch.*

*(Show, don't tell, for one beat. Then:)*

> *"There. That's what's wrong. The light off you is moving in two times at once. There's a clock running on you. And it's running fast."*

He puts the patch back. He has been the only person in the canyon to see the Band's signature directly. He will not say it again to anyone.

### Cira Stands

She rises from her mat. First time all morning. Walks slowly, deliberately, toward the path leading to the Warrens. **Don't announce. Let them notice.**

### Sabel's Shout — The Handoff

~30 sec after lights return, **Sabel arrives at a sprint** from the rim path. No armor. Left his post fast.

> *"Where's the rest of you. **Where's the rest of you.** Tajsh — out. Out of the Vault. Get to the well. Something just got in and it's hunting."*

He doesn't wait. He goes. The geography pulls toward the well.

**If they hesitate:** someone screams from the market floor. The screams get cut short in a way screams don't usually cut short.

## §5 — Fight at the Well (15–20 min)

### Arrival at the Well

> *The market has cleared faster than you've ever seen it clear. People are pressed against the cliff face on both sides, behind toppled stalls, frozen in shop doorways. The cobbles around the well are empty in a perfect ten-foot circle — nobody is standing inside that circle and nobody is going to.*
>
> *The water in the well is freezing. Not from the top down. From the bottom up. Reflected lantern-light is locked in the surface like flies in amber.*
>
> *And out of the shadow under the well-rim, something is **crystallizing into the air.** It is shaped roughly like a person. It is not finished yet. Its outline is still settling — like a sketch someone is still drawing — and where the lines aren't done yet, you can see the canyon wall through it.*
>
> *It turns its head. Toward whoever in your party is carrying the Band.*

### Battlefield

```
        [Cliff face — civilians pressed]
              |
[Toppled stall]      [Burning lantern line — Tava's stand]
              |
          [THE WELL]
       (Wraithling crystallizing)
              |
       [Empty cobble circle — 10 ft]
              |
       [Selin's ladder — half-finished glyph at 20 ft]
              |
[Tajsh: south]                    [Sabel: north]
```

**Range bands:**
- Band-holder: **Engaged** with Wraithling on its first action.
- Rest of party: **Short** to the well on arrival.
- Civilians: **Short**, pressed against cliffs.
- Selin's ladder: **Short** to the well, vertical.
- Sabel + Tajsh: arrive at **start of Round 2**.

### Stat Block — Scar-touched Wraithling

```
Type: Rival
Wounds: 8 | Strain: — | Soak: 2 | Defense: 0/0
Brawn 3 | Agility 3 | Intellect 1 | Cunning 2 | Willpower 3 | Presence 1
Skills: Brawl 2, Perception 2, Vigilance 1

Sync-Slash (Brawl) — Damage 5; Crit 4; Engaged; Burn 1

Abilities:
• Out-of-Sync — Once per turn, when targeted by an attack, the
  Wraithling resolves a beat late. Add ◆ to incoming attack for
  the rest of the round.
• Phase-Step — Maneuver: move from Engaged to Short through wall,
  floor, or shadow. Ignores difficult terrain.
• Hungering Geometry — On its turn it prioritizes whoever holds
  the Band. If Band-holder is >Short, Phase-Steps toward them as
  maneuver — no choice.
• Wards-Bound — At the START of round 3 (or when Wounds reach 0,
  whichever first), wards re-anchor and it dissolves into ash and
  ozone. The fight ends.
```

### Battlefield Features

| Feature | Check | Effect |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Crowd panic | Coordination or Leadership ◆◆ | Clear civilians from a target area. Failure: civilian takes the next stray hit. |
| Toppled stall | Athletics ◆ (vault) or ◆◆ (shove into creature) | Shove: 2 wounds + Engaged → Short displacement. |
| Burning lantern line | Ranged ◆◆ or Skulduggery ◆◆ | Drop burning oil between creature and Band-holder. Wraithling adds ◆ to attacks through it for 1 round. |
| Well-chain | Brawl or Athletics ◆◆ | Restrain it 1 round. (It will Phase-Step out — but the action delay costs it a turn.) |
| Selin's half-finished glyph (wall above well) | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | **Re-anchor the ward early.** Wraithling dissolves at end of current round. **Triumph:** wards re-anchor harder; Legio Null's future fix on the canyon less precise (pays off later). |
| Sabel arrives (Rd 2) | NPC action | One crossbow shot, 50/50 hit. If hits: 4 dmg + Wraithling Phase-Steps away from Sabel toward Band-holder. |
| Tajsh arrives (Rd 2) | NPC action | Does not fight. Sees who the thing is going for. The look on his face is the look of a man whose worst guess just got confirmed. |

### Win Conditions

1. **Kill it** — Wounds to 0.
2. **Re-anchor the ward** — Arcana ◆◆◆ at Selin's glyph (or two cumulative successes if multiple PCs try). Dissolves end of round.
3. **Round 3 timeout** — Stitching auto-re-anchors. Fight ends regardless.

**Design intent:** the wards close. The party survives because the canyon caught up — *they* didn't necessarily save the day. Ambiguity is the point.

### Civilian Casualty (track)

If party ignores civilian protection (no Coord/Leadership attempted or repeated failures), **one unnamed civilian takes a Sync-Slash.** Sets up Sabel's mood. **Do not kill a named NPC for callousness this session** — save that for moments the table earns.

### After Combat

> *Whatever it was, it isn't there anymore. There's no body. There's a faint smear of grey ash on the cobbles where it stood, and the smell of ozone, and the well-water is steaming where it had been freezing. The lantern on the nearest stall is steady. Sabel is breathing hard. Tajsh is staring at the well.*
>
> *Somewhere in the crowd, a child is crying — briefly, then quiet. Marta is at the door of the Lantern, holding the policy.*
>
> *Cira is at the mouth of the Warrens. She has been watching the whole time. She is not humming.*

### Rewards

- **10–20 Lumens** from grateful market folk (passed quietly into a PC's hand by someone they don't know).
- **+1 Lux Æterna reputation** if civilians protected (any successful Coord/Leadership).
- **One free or discounted item** from Hask or Tomm in a future visit if visibly heroic.

## §6 — Cira's Summons (3–5 min) — END HERE

### Read-Aloud

> *Cira is waiting at the mouth of the Warrens. She is not on her mat. Her mat is empty. The dream-catcher is in her hand — she has untied it from the iron nail above her corner and carried it with her. She is not humming for the first time today.*
>
> *Sabel is on the rim somewhere, shouting orders. Tajsh is on the steps of his Vault watching the well steam. The canyon is moving again — slowly, carefully — but moving.*
>
> *Cira looks at the party with eyes that don't see and have never seen and somehow find each of you anyway.*

**The line — deliver slowly:**

> ***"The thing under the well has started answering the thing in your pack. If you want to know what you carry, come now. If you wait, it will choose someone smaller."***

She turns. She walks into the Warrens. **She does not look back.**

### END SESSION

Do not let the table push further. This is the cliffhanger.

- *"Can we follow her?"* → *"You can. Next session."*
- *"What does she mean by 'someone smaller'?"* → *"Petra. She means Petra. But she didn't say it. Next session."*

Confirm Session 4 opens at the mouth of the Children's Tunnel.
