# Species-Specific Backgrounds

## 1. Lothari Dreamer *(Lothari)*

**Starting Contact.** Elder Soren — Lothari caravan master, runs the coastal desert route, maintains Silent Network dream-relay points. Passes through Lux Æterna's supply chain regularly.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Perception.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"Your vision was real. You saw the pattern forming before anyone else — that is why you came here. You were always going to find this Band. Something called you because something knew you would answer."*

**Through-Line.** *"Was my vision a warning or an invitation?"*
**Peaks at:** the moment the Compass is revealed to be the Echo-Lord's eye.

---

## 2. Thulkorûn Exile *(Thulkorûn Dwarf)*

**Starting Contact.** Gora Ironbreath — old company sergeant, runs an "escort company" out of Irongate Crossing that fronts for the Wandering Kin (Star-Veiled Passage). Provides cover identities, intel on mountain routes, access to safehouses and supply caches.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Resilience.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"Your clan did not fail you. The world failed your clan. There is a difference, and you have been punishing yourself for a wound someone else inflicted. That ends now."*

**Through-Line.** *"Am I my clan, or am I my choices?"*
**Peaks at:** the sealed Dragon's Tooth trade-gate — hearing forge-hammers through stone, knowing their people are inside and they can't reach them.

**Embedded DM Notes.**
- **Crown of the Mountain (free narration when the Ulzammar Crown is first described, no roll):** *"Your people already have a famous crown — the Crown of the Mountain, forged by Thane Korin Barazkor in SA:91 to unify the warring clans. Seven metals, one for each founding clan, with a band of Star-Metal at its center… The Dwarven crown succeeded — and its people sealed themselves away from the world for 90 years. The Ulzammar crown failed — and its echo wants to try again at a cosmic scale."*
- **Act III convergence:** Gora Ironbreath rides up on the road from Irongate Crossing with a six-warrior escort: *"I don't know what you're carrying, but half the continent seems to want it."*

---

## 3. Mirennian Deserter *(Human or Mirennian)*

**Starting Contact.** Corvin Halstow — Azure Hand mid-level logistics officer in Saltwick. Doesn't yet know about the desertion. Window closes the moment status propagates up the chain.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Deception.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"The order you refused was wrong. You knew it the moment you heard it. Trust that knowing — it is the only faculty you possess that has never lied to you. It is why I came to you."*

**Through-Line.** *"I refused one order. Why do I keep following others?"*
**Peaks at:** Miravel's revelation — another person who served without asking.

---

## 4. Waldkyn Wanderer *(Waldkyn)*

**Starting Contact.** Finnet — Waldkyn herbalist who left the Vales years earlier; runs a trading circuit through Thornback. Has heard the same Stillwood rumors that drove the PC out.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Survival.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"The Vales are suffering. You knew it before you left — that is why you left. The answers you needed were never there. They were here. They were always going to be here."*

**Through-Line.** *"I left to find answers. Every answer shows a deeper question."*
**Peaks at:** the Vales — finding the home they left is dying.

**Embedded sensory tell (Waldkyn-flavored Architect red flag).**
Knowledge–Nature ◆◆ near the Architect: *"the plants near it are not healthier — they're stiller. A vine has stopped growing. A moth has stopped beating its wings."* Onset of Still-Water Dread.

---

## 5. Orkin Outcast *(Orkin)*

**Starting Contact.** Vosk Two-Names — Varr-Darg fixer in Ashkeld. Knows clan-territory passage, who's hiring, how to move people who don't want to be found. Professionally useful, not friendly.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Streetwise.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"They called it integration. They meant submission. You were right to refuse. You were right about all of it, and you were right before you had words for why."*

**Through-Line.** *"Integration or integrity — can I have both?"*
**Peaks at:** Krag-Vosk — the Orkin who never compromised and paid every day for it.

**Embedded Act III payoff.** Before the Bloodied Fist charge, Krag-Vosk locks eyes on this PC and says their Orkin name aloud — the one they may have tried to leave behind — then:
> *"I see you, kin. Whatever they told you in the Quarters — you are not small. You were never small."*

Then he goes. **Do not rush past this.**

## 6. Halfling Wayfinder *(Halfling)*

**Starting Contact.** Pell Thistlewick — senior family elder managing eastern desert routes. Grants access to family route maps, safe-house network, and courier chains. Two demands: don't embarrass the family name, send word before trouble becomes someone else's problem.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Geography.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"The road your family travels is a circle. It returns to comfort, to safety, to the same stories told in the same taverns. You are the first of your line who wanted something the road couldn't give you."*

**Through-Line.** *"My family deals in other people's stories. When does mine start?"*
**Peaks at:** the final scene — *this story is theirs.*

## 7. Gnomish Integrator *(Gnome)*

**Starting Contact.** Whisper — Forest Gnome Integrator based in Gearhaven, tracking First Age ruin anomalies through the mycelial relay network. Months of correspondence; never met in person.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Mechanics.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"The disturbance you came to study was not anomalous. It was a summons. The pattern has been waiting for minds like yours to find it. You did not choose this work. It chose you."*

**Through-Line.** *"I came to study the pattern. The pattern studied me back."*
**Peaks at:** the moment the Compass reveals its true nature — they have been observed the whole time.

# Human-Specific Background

## 8. Varnathi Ex-Inquisitor *(Human)*

**Starting Contact.** Sergeant Cavel — Varnathi Legion regular (NOT Legio Null), old friend who watched the PC's trajectory with discomfort. Hasn't reported the desertion. Every interaction risks him.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Vigilance.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You know better than anyone in this room what the Decree costs. You counted those costs on behalf of others — and then you discovered the cost firsthand. That accounting will not leave you. But what you know — about the enemy, about the system, about yourself — is exactly why you are here. What you were does not have to be what you are."*

**Through-Line.** *"I built the cage. How do I live inside it — and how do I tear it down?"*
**Peaks at:** Commander Thalric — facing the person who still believes everything they used to believe.

# Species-Agnostic Backgrounds

## 9. Persecuted Mage *(any mage-capable species)*

**Starting Contact.** Maren Saltbrook — retired midwife in Cove Hallow; runs a 20+ year mage-touched safe-house. Azure Hand-adjacent, never formally affiliated. Provides forged transit papers, safe harbor, and intel on who is being hunted.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Discipline.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You have been running since [the year they fled]. I know what that costs. I know you sometimes wonder whether the running is worth more than what you left behind. It is. You are worth more than what they tried to burn out of you."*

**Through-Line.** *"If the persecution ended tomorrow, who would I be?"*
**Peaks at:** the Echo-Lord's final offer — *"No more purges."*

---

## 10. Underworld Operative *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** The Quill — Shadewalker-adjacent information broker. Dead-drop correspondence only. Independent (sells to Shadewalkers and others). Never met in person; species, age, and location unknown.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Skullduggery.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You have spent your life working around systems that were designed to exclude you. You understand power better than the people who wield it. The job that brought you here was not the job you agreed to — it never is, with the jobs that matter. What you are about to do is not so different. Except this time, what you protect is worth the risk."*

**Through-Line.** *"I've always known the price of everything. What does it cost to stop calculating?"*
**Peaks at:** the Pale Ledger — a mirror of who they were before the job that changed them.

---

## 11. Survivalist *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** Yara — Lothari trader, desert circuit. Shared camps, traded water-source intel, covered for each other when Legio Null patrols got close. Not formally Silent Network, but moves messages on paths that aren't on any official map.

**Starting Bonus.** **Forager** (Tier 1 talent). Remove up to ■■ from any check to find food, water, or shelter; checks to forage or search take half the normal time.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You have never needed anyone's permission to exist. The world has tried to make that a weakness. It is not. The people with you now need someone who knows how to keep them alive when every structure around them is failing. That is what you are. That is what this moment requires."*

**Through-Line.** *"I know how to survive alone. Can I survive belonging somewhere?"*
**Peaks at:** Lux Æterna's destruction and loss — the first place they ever stayed.

**Embedded Act III convergence.** Yara rides up on a camel, alone, and says simply: *"I dreamed about you."* She joins the caravan without further explanation. She knows every water source in the eastern foothills.

## 12. Scholar/Archivist *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** Archivist Fenwick — former colleague at the Vernath Imperial Archive. Helped the PC disappear when the questions started. Cautious to the point of paralysis. Most useful contact — and most fragile.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Forbidden.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"Everything you have read, every risk taken to read it — it brought you here. The notation on this artifact is in the texts you found. You recognized it before anyone explained it to you. There are no coincidences in a pattern this old. You were always the one who would find this."*

**Through-Line.** *"I know what knowledge costs. Do I have the right to act on what I know?"*
**Peaks at:** the Starfall Peaks Observatory — Vaelric's instruments show a truth the world isn't ready for, and the question of whether revealing it saves people or destroys them.

---

## 13. Disgraced Soldier *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** Corporal Aeric — former unit-mate who voted to acquit at the court-martial; left service shortly after. Now a caravan guard on the Dragon's Tooth mountain road. No connections, no resources beyond his own — but unconditional loyalty in a world where that is rarer than gold.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 in any one combat skill of the player's choice (Melee, Ranged, Brawl, or a magic combat skill if applicable).

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You know the difference between discipline and obedience — you proved it the moment you said no. That distinction is rarer than you know. Most people cannot hold it when the pressure comes. You held it. That is why I came to you and not to the ones who followed orders."*

**Through-Line.** *"I followed orders until I couldn't. How do I know which ones are worth following now?"*
**Peaks at:** the Architect itself — a voice that always has a reason, always sounds right, always knows what to say.

---

## 14. Healer *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** Surgeon Haldan Dast — physician trained alongside the PC; recently relocated to Gearhaven. Works for whoever pays, including occasionally the Azure Hand. Provides medical supplies, intel from his patients, and discreet assistance.

**Starting Bonus.** **Surgeon** rank 1 (Tier 1 talent). On a Medicine check to heal wounds, the target heals one additional wound.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You have been spending your gifts quietly, hoping not to be noticed. Hoping to do enough good in the spaces the purge hasn't reached. You have saved more people than you will ever know. The world is larger than those spaces. The work I am asking of you is the same work — at a scale you have never imagined."*

**Through-Line.** *"I save people because I can. What do I do when saving one costs another?"*
**Peaks at:** the Brass Heart — the Gem's silence keeps Gearhaven's vents stable, but its power was never meant for this. Every treatment has a cost.

---

## 15. Ruined Merchant *(any species)*

**Starting Contact.** Iskar Weld — shipping factor in Gearhaven, relocated after the same wave of seizures swept the shared trade network. Doesn't know the full story. Will eventually ask.

**Starting Bonus.** +1 Negotiation.

**Architect's Personal Address.**
> *"You understand networks — how things connect, how value moves, how systems sustain themselves or collapse. What was taken from you was real. What you learned while you had it is also real, and it cannot be seized. The task before us requires exactly that understanding. What you built before was practice."*

**Through-Line.** *"I built something real and it was taken. What am I when I'm not what I built?"*
**Peaks at:** Lanthir Haven — a community that built paradise, and the party took the thing it was built around.

# Cross-Cutting Hooks (Apply Regardless of Background)

**The Architect's Personal Address Scene** (Ch.2 Sc.5).
Every PC gets a tailored line. The over-tailoring *is* the warning.

| Probe | Skill / Difficulty | Crack Revealed |
|---|---|---|
| Resist the warmth of the Naming | Discipline ◆◆ | The Naming is too tailored. Skill, not mentorship. Targeting, not therapy. |
| Notice something visually wrong | Perception ◆◆ | The Architect casts no reflection in the floating crystal symbols. |
| Read its emotional state | Perception ◆◆◆ | When it says "unmade," its tone shifts toward *anticipation*. **Triumph:** *hunger.* |
| Sense its magical signature | Knowledge–Arcana ◆◆◆ | Divine on the surface. *Abyssal* underneath. Perfume over rot. |
| Read the floating symbols | Knowledge–Lore ◆◆◆◆ | Compositional notation — music for frequencies no mortal throat can produce. The three-ring mark is a *chord.* |

**Cira's "It's lighter than you think."**
GM picks one player — whoever seems most guarded. Cira walks past their mat and says, without looking up:
> *"You've been carrying that since you were twelve. It's lighter than you think."*

She will not clarify. She will not acknowledge she said it. **Pays off** when that PC finally understands the answer to their through-line question — probably during or after the final confrontation.

**Cira's Architect dream-vision.** Near the Architect, Cira's visions converge on:
> *"A tower of perfect crystal, and inside it, nothing breathed."*

**"The Last Morning"** (Act I, Sess 5–6).
Before the chase begins, ask each player: *"Before you go — who do you find? What do you say?"* Two minutes per character. Three sessions later, a letter arrives — three caught in the Null sweep. **At least one name should be someone a player said goodbye to.**

**Act III convergence.** Pick 2–3 starting contacts to reappear in the Barren Hills based on which backgrounds are in play. Examples:
- **Gora Ironbreath** (Thulkorûn Exile) — six-warrior escort, Irongate Crossing road.
- **Yara** (Survivalist) — alone on a camel: *"I dreamed about you."*
- Others: GM picks based on table composition. The point is: the world remembers. Convergence, not arrival.

# GM Notes for the Personal Address

These words should feel like **relief.** Like being seen after years of invisibility.

This is calculated. Every sentence is precisely targeted to lower defenses — the Architect has been watching through Miravel's network, through the Resonance Compass it already prepared, through 1,500 years of learning to read mortal fear. The warmth is instrumentalized. **But the words themselves are true.** That is what makes the moment dangerous — and what makes the betrayal, later, cut so deep.

**NPC Reactions to the address:**
- **Tajsh:** Awed and skeptical in equal measure. *"That's either a god or the best con I've ever seen. Either way, I'd listen."* Privately, by the morning after, becomes the party's grounding skeptic — pulls them aside with the **Two-Sources Pitch** (see `full-campaign.md` *The Morning After*). He does not call the Architect a liar; he calls it *unverified.*
- **Sabel:** Silent. Watching. He doesn't trust anything that radiates certainty.
- **Cira:** Refuses to enter the room where it appeared. Later: *"It spoke truth. It spoke lies. I cannot tell which words were which. That is more frightening than a liar."*
- **Ink:** Leaves Lux Æterna immediately after — *unless* she is waiting for the pedestal book, in which case she stays the morning to translate (see `full-campaign.md` *Ink's Translation — The Splinter*). Either way, ends up reporting to her superiors. Most valuable intelligence asset in a generation.
