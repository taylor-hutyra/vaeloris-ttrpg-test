Tajsh Intel

Table-ready gossip Tajsh Thale can dispense. Every name and thread pulls from existing Lux Æterna canon (Campaign/World/Vael/Lux Æterna/, the d10 shop table, Tension Hooks, and the Session-02 NPC roster). Drop into any conversation where the party asks Tajsh "what's going on?" or hangs around the Vault long enough to draw him out.

Companion: SESSION-02-NPCs.md — Tajsh's voice rules, deferred-ask reframe, prepared answers.

How Tajsh Talks Gossip

He talks fast, drops names like a man who knows everyone in a 400-soul town does (because he does), and frames everything as a supply-chain problem first. He won't gossip about Sabel (mutual respect) or Cira (privately unsettled by her). The good gossip is delivered while he's polishing his thumb-ring, between sips of tea, half-distracted by the Band on his desk.

Pacing rule: dispense gossip in clusters of 2–3, not in a monologue. Tajsh interrupts himself with the next item only if a player shows interest in the previous one. If they don't engage, he moves on — "anyway" — and saves the rest.

Three Opener Lines (pick one)

"You want gossip? Sit. I have to talk to someone who isn't already inside it."

"Marta keeps a register, Sabel keeps a perimeter, and I keep a ledger. You ask the right person to find the right shape of trouble. Today, that's me. What do you want to know about?"

(Polishing thumb-ring.) "Most of this is benign. Two pieces aren't. I'll save those for last so you take them seriously when I get there."

Feuds & Tensions

# What he says Truth / Hook
1 "Joram and Tomm haven't spoken in eight days. Tomm took a forge order Joram had a verbal claim on. Tomm offered a discount — Joram said the discount was the insult, not the order. Both of them are wrong and neither will admit it. The settlement is enjoying it more than they should." Joram (Two-Axle Repairs, Thulkorûn) vs. Tomm Broadback (Iron Shelf, Halfling). PC mediation = +1 settlement reputation with both shops.
2 "Dennica accused Yussef of selling her route maps to outside buyers. Yussef says she's paranoid. Yussef is right that she's paranoid. He is also lying about the maps. I'm not getting in the middle of it." Dennica (Salted Rope, Mirennian deserter; jumpy for legitimate reasons) vs. Yussef (Sand-Map Stall, Lothari). Yussef's been selling old route data — not active routes — but it's compromised someone's anonymity. Hook for an underworld-flavored sidequest.
3 "The Koss brothers are feuding with each other again. Same fight as last spring. Don't ask what about. Nobody knows. They'll sort it out by the end of the week and pretend it never happened." Koss & Koss (Orkin scrap-metal brothers) — recurring, harmless, atmosphere only.
4 "Marta and Selin had words last week. Selin asked Marta to close the Lantern for two nights so she could try a glyph re-seat without drunk mages making the canyon hum. Marta said the day she closes the Lantern is the day she stops loving anyone. Selin hasn't been back in for a meal since. Marta is leaving bread at her shop door. It's an apology. Neither of them will say so." Foreshadows the 48-hour ward shutdown Selin is planning. Soft tension worth the table noticing. Pays off later if PCs pursue Selin's plan.

Schedules

Don't be in their way when they unload — they're proud and they're fast." Tuesday = next session ±1, GM's call. Yara is a Survivalist PC's starting contact (per Background-Reference.md). The substitute is probing for the settlement. Investigation lead — Streetwise or Perception checks at the market.
7 "Ward-tending shifts run dawn-to-dusk in three rotations. Selin works the noon rotation because nobody else can hold the heavy glyphs that long. She is at six tenders for an eight-tender weave. She has been at six for two months. She will tell you it's fine. It is not fine." Established canon. Foreshadow Stitching collapse.
8 "My Shadewalker contact — Ink — is two days out by Lothari express. She'll arrive whenever the caravan does. I told her it was urgent. She'll know what that means. Don't be in Tajsh's Vault when she gets here. Or be there. She's hard to read. I find her useful." Establishes Ink's arrival timing. Players choose presence or absence. Note: Ink's role shifts based on whether the party retrieves the pedestal book — see Ink's Translation — The Splinter in full-campaign.md.
9 "Tomm has the forge for crossbow-bolt re-tipping every fourth day. Sabel ordered three hundred bolts last month and Tomm is two-thirds through. The tap of his hammer is the canyon's clock. If you wake up and don't hear it, something's wrong." Atmospheric + sensory tell for ward-failure session. If the hammer stops, players who've heard this gossip will feel the silence.

Who's Healthy, Who Isn't

# What he says Hook
10 "Hask hasn't eaten in two days that I know of. Don't ask him about it. Marta's been leaving stew at his shop door. He hasn't touched it. I don't know what's wrong. He'll say when he says." Foreshadows whatever Hask reveal you've planned. Players who push get nothing. Players who feed him quietly — leaving food without comment — earn rep.
11 "Brother Oram has the cough back. The dry one. He had it last winter, beat it, said he wouldn't get it again. He has it again. He's still working. He'll stop when he falls over and not before. If a PC has Medicine, I'd take it as a personal favor if you spent ten minutes with him." Sick-Bay sidequest. Medicine ◆◆ → +1 Warrens reputation, Oram remembers.
12 "Petra burned her hand again. Practicing in the Warrens at night when nobody could see. Aldric found her, fixed it without making her cry. He didn't tell her parents. He should. He won't." Soft Petra hook — particularly for Norseman or any healer/mage-mentor PC.
13 "Sabel is on day five of not sleeping. He is functional. He is not okay. I have nothing useful to do with this information except give it to you." Foreshadow. PCs can intervene with a Discipline roll or a quiet tea — Sabel doesn't acknowledge it but remembers.
14 "Three families are sleeping in the market because someone's nightmare-burst scorched two chambers in the Warrens last week and nobody reported it to Sabel. The mage who did it is hiding in the Schoolroom. Aldric knows who. Aldric is not telling me. I am letting that be his decision." Tension Hook #3 (Lux Æterna). Available if PCs want to investigate or smooth it over.

The Two He Saves for Last

These are the load-bearing ones. Tajsh delivers them last because he wants the players to take them seriously.

#15 — The Children's Tunnel

(Polishes thumb-ring. Doesn't make eye contact.)

"Three children have been crawling into a tunnel behind the Deep Well that nobody knew was there. They come back smelling of ozone. They've gone quiet. I have not told their parents. I have not told Sabel. I don't know what to tell them. I think I'm hoping you'll tell me."

Truth: Tension Hook #4 (Lux Æterna). The Children's Tunnel is the access point to the Deep Chamber where the Architect manifests. The ozone smell is the leak from its pocket-space. Critically load-bearing for Scene 5 — if PCs investigate the tunnel during the Sandbox, they may meet the Architect earlier than scripted.

GM note: Tajsh genuinely doesn't know what's down there. He's hoping the party will. He has chosen, deliberately, not to involve Sabel — Sabel's response would be to seal the tunnel, and Tajsh isn't sure that's right yet. That hesitation is the gift he's giving the party.

#16 — The Mark at the Bolt Hole

(Quieter.)

"Sabel found someone scratched a Legio Null mark — the gear and chain — into the stone near the Bolt Hole. The emergency tunnel out. The one nobody is supposed to know exists. Not freshly carved. Maybe a week. He hasn't told me. I know because I check the Bolt Hole every morning, and I noticed it before he reported it. He will tell me, if I'm patient. If I'm not patient he'll close down on me. I'm being patient. Please don't be the people who break that."

Truth: Tension Hook #5 (Lux Æterna). Either an outside warning (someone hostile knows about the Bolt Hole) or an inside threat (an informant is signaling to outside contacts). Tajsh won't move until Sabel speaks. PCs can independently investigate — Perception ◆◆◆ at the Bolt Hole reveals the carve depth, the angle, and the calluses worn into the stone around it (the carver was right-handed and worked slowly, in daylight — not someone hiding).

GM note: This is also an early seed for the Lux Æterna will fall Act I climax. The Bolt Hole becomes load-bearing when the Legio Null arrives and the settlement evacuates. Whoever scratched that mark is either a friend trying to help or an enemy mapping the exit — and the answer is the kind of thing the party can determine if they investigate before the canyon goes hot.

Pacing Cheat-Sheet

If the party asks "anything else?" multiple times, deliver in this order. Each cluster ≈ 2 minutes of table time.

Cluster Items Mood
Opener Pick a #0 line + items #1, #3 Light. Tajsh enjoying himself.
Mid #5, #7, #9 Practical. Supply-chain Tajsh.
Soften #11, #12 Quiet. Tajsh-as-father-of-the-settlement.
Real #2, #6, #14 Tension rises. Things are wrong.
Land it #15, then #16 He stops polishing the thumb-ring. He's asking for help.

If the party engages on any item, deepen it before moving to the next cluster. If they don't engage, Tajsh smiles thinly and changes the subject.

What Tajsh Will NOT Gossip About

Mark these for the GM. If a player asks, he deflects.

  • Cira. "She came in on her own feet. Whatever else is true is hers to say. I don't pry." (Privately he is unsettled by her accuracy.)
  • Sabel personally. "Sabel keeps the wall and I keep the books. We don't tell each other's stories." Mutual respect.
  • His Shadewalker exit. "That's a story I don't tell on the day the Band shows up. Ask me again in a year."
  • The evacuation fund. Won't volunteer. Won't acknowledge if asked. Skulduggery ◆◆◆◆ to glimpse the entry in his ledger.
  • Anything about Vorn or Valeth's arrival from the rift. "You came out of a hole in the world. I'm watching. That's what you need to know about that."
