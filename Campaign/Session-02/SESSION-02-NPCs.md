## 1. Tajsh Thale — The Broker

> *Human male, mid-40s. Lean, weathered, brown skin worn dark by desert sun. Eyes that assess everything before the rest of his face catches up.*

**Where to find him:** Tajsh's Vault, on the Veiled Market. Office above the Vault.
**Role this session:** Employer. Receives the Band & journal. Triggers the Revelation.

### Voice

- Speaks fast. Finishes other people's sentences (sometimes incorrectly).
- Uses humor as armor — deflection, misdirection, buying time.
- **Tell:** when nervous, polishes whatever he's holding (coin, cup, fragment, thumb-ring). If a player notices the polishing, he knows he's been read.
- Does not tell people he cares. Tells them the rations are better this week. Means the same thing.
- Calls no one by their first name until late. He may, late in the campaign, use one.

### What He Knows (volunteers freely)

- The party's mining job. He hired them.
- **General Lux Æterna gossip — supply schedules, who's feuding, who's healthy.** → See [Tajsh Intel](Tajsh%20Intel.md) for the table-ready 16-item gossip pack with pacing cheat-sheet.
- The Stitching has been thinning; Selin's been worried; Sabel's been tense.
- Lothari caravan timings.

### What He Knows (under pressure / on revelation)

- The three-ring symbol. Two prior encounters in his life.
- The Barren Hills sealed chamber where he carved the symbol himself, 17 years ago.
- That he has Shadewalker contacts (won't volunteer).
- That an archivist named **Ink** is who he calls for First Age artifact ID.
- He maintains an **emergency evacuation cache** two miles south of the canyon, stocked with travel supplies and route maps. **He has never told anyone about it.** (Won't surface this session unless players push him *very* hard or the Stitching collapses entirely.)

### What He Will Say (sample lines)

**On receiving the Band (handoff):**
> *"Set it on the desk. Don't unwrap it. Not yet. Sit. Tell me everything from the moment you saw the pit."*

**Watching his desk-candle freeze mid-flicker, then resume:**
> *(quiet, after a pause)* *"...take it across the room. Yeah. Behind that shelf. Better."*

**When he reads the journal and sees the symbol:**
> *(stops on the page, doesn't turn it. Reads it again. Reads it a third time. Closes the journal slowly.)* *"Where did Torvin draw this?"*

**The monologue (after the symbol lands):**
> *"I have seen this twice in my life. The first time was when I was twenty-two, and I was standing in a Shadewalker archive in Ostmark, and a sealed crate came in, and the man who'd brought it would not put it down. He set it on a stone table and stepped back from it like it was a cobra. Everybody in the room was very still."*
>
> *"The second time was seventeen years ago. Barren Hills. I was hired to seal a chamber in an old ruin. The contract was specific. Carve this mark on the door. Carve it deep. Walk away. Don't look back. They paid me four times the going rate, and I still wish they hadn't."*
>
> *"This — what Torvin drew — this is the same mark. And the thing your crew dug up in that pit is what was in the chamber."*

**Then Tajsh DEFERS the ask.** He does NOT give the party an objective here. The pitch comes after the Architect, in Session 3.

> *(Polishes his thumb-ring. Sets the journal down very carefully, as if it might break.)*
>
> *"I need a night with this. Eat. Rest. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking. The Band stays in this room — in this wool — until I do."*

**If pressed for more:** *"I have a guess. I don't want to commit to the guess until I've sent a message and slept on it. Don't make me commit yet."*

He closes the journal before opening another book and starting to scribble in it. He sets the quill down for a moment.

**Reframe note for the GM:** in earlier drafts Tajsh asked the party here for a book at a temple west of the Lothari pass. That ask has been moved to Session 3 opening as a "two sources" verification pitch. Tajsh is now the **grounding skeptic** to the Architect's divine voice — not a parallel quest-giver. See `SESSION-02-Guide.md` Phase 6 (Scene 5 Epilogue) and the campaign master document sections *The Morning After: Tajsh's Two-Sources Pitch*, *The Branching Choice*, and *Optional Detour: The Pedestal Book*.

**To Valeth and Vorn specifically (Ledger moment):**
> *"You came out of a hole in the world. Your friend walked out of a rift that shouldn't be there. You're either part of this or you're worse than part of this. Either way, you go with them. I want you where I can see you, or I'll take you the Legio myself."* 
He eyes Vorn up and down before grabbing Valeth's hand seemingly inspecting his palms, fingernails before staring into his eyes with suspicious look before thrusting his arm away. He turns away from them looking up into the air. 
*"Welcome to Lux Æterna."* He waves them away.

He writes their names in his small leather ledger. *"Next of kin?"*

### Prepared Answers

| If asked… | He says… |
|---|---|
| What were the Shadewalkers afraid of? | *"I never found out. The crate was gone by the next month. The briefing file I tried to pull was missing. I stopped asking."* |
| Who was your employer at Barren Hills? | *"A name I don't say out loud. Sorry. Not today. Ask me again in a year."* |
| Why us? | *"Because you found it. Because whatever this is has decided you exist. And because I trust you more than I trust anyone else in this settlement right now. That's not a compliment. It's arithmetic."* |
| What about the Band? | *"Stays here. In this room. In this wool. I don't want it on the road. Get me the book. I'll get us someone who can read it."* |

### If the Party Refuses to Hand Over the Band

He doesn't push. *"Suit yourselves. Sleep with it. See how that goes."* The Band's effects worsen overnight (cold spots in Tajsh's office, the candle won't relight, Cira's humming gets louder). Cira summons them to the Deep Chamber anyway.

### Secret (GM only — do not reveal)

- Skims 10% off every relic sale into the evacuation fund.
- Was kicked out of the Shadewalkers — took something they didn't want him to keep. Won't say what or where.

### How to Play Him

Most competent adult in any room. Be right about small things often, so when he's wrong about the big thing later (trusting the Architect), it lands harder. He is the party's reluctant father figure.

## 2. Old Cira — The Fortune-Teller

> *Lothari female. Small, sun-bleached, wrapped in linen that was once white. Completely blind — milky-pale eyes that track sound rather than light. Age indeterminate.*

**Where to find her:** Cira's Corner, on a woven mat under a jutting rock shelf in the Veiled Market. Eats olives out of a clay jar.
**Role this session:** Senses the wrongness first. Summons the party to the Deep Chamber. Bookends the Architect scene.

### Voice

- Speaks in riddles that turn out, two days later, to be exactly correct.
- Hums tunelessly at intervals that have no relationship to anything happening.
- Doesn't explain after the fact. Doesn't say "I told you so." Just opens another olive.
- When she's serious, she goes still and her riddles stop. **That's the tell.**

### What She Knows (player-facing)

- That something arrived with the party that "carries weather."
- The settlement is "holding its breath."
- She has been waiting for them.
- The thing beneath them is awake and will not come up.

### What She Knows (GM-only — do not reveal)

- She is a ranking [[Silent Network]] operative placed in Lux Æterna 25 years ago to monitor [[The Sorath Scar]].
- She communicates via Lothari dream-relay — three days out, three days back.
- She knows the Band is wrong. She does not know exactly what the Architect is, but she knows it casts no shadow.
- She knows what Arden is and is choosing not to say so. The restraint *is* the communication.

### What She Will Say (sample lines)

**When the party arrives (Beat 2 / Arrival):**
- (Sitting outside the Vault, humming a single sustained low note. Eyes closed.)
- **If Valeth passes near her**, she stops humming. Opens her eyes. Looks up at him. *"Two of you are walking. Only one came back."* Returns to humming. **Do not explain.**
- **If Arden passes near her**, she looks at him a long moment. With *politeness* — careful not to name something. Says nothing aloud. *(Pass Arden's player a private note: "She sees you. She knows. She is choosing not to say so. Out loud, to anyone.")*

**At Cira's Corner (Sandbox activity):**
> *"You walked in carrying weather. The canyon felt it. Sit. One of you. Now."*

She reads one PC. Riddle hits in two days. Lines per PC:

- **Valeth:** *"You came back. Only just. There is something on the other side of the seam that knows your shape now. It will not call again, until it does."*
- **Yldroth:** *"Your hands carry two opposite things. Do not let one forget it lives inside the other."*
- **Norseman:** *"There is a child in this canyon who does not know yet what she is. She will look at you and know you have already been her. Do not flinch."*
- **Arden:** *(her hand pauses just over his, not touching)* *"You have practiced being smaller than you are. Keep practicing. Tonight is not the night."* — withdraws, says nothing more.
- **Vorn (if he attends):** *"Your grandmother said three names and would not say four. The one she would not say is in this canyon now. Do not speak it either."*

**During the Stitching flicker:**
- Her humming climbs in pitch. Slowly. Loud enough to hear from anywhere in the canyon.

**Summoning the party to the Architect (after the flicker):**
- (At the mouth of the Warrens. Has stopped humming. Waiting.)
> *"I cannot follow you where this goes. Listen carefully. Doubt generously. Come back."*

**If the party hesitates:**
> *"It will not come to you if you stay up here. It will wait. It has waited fifteen hundred years. But while it waits, what is above will find what is below. Go. Now. Please."*

**After the Architect (closing the scene):**
> *"How long were you gone?"*
> *(player: "A few minutes.")*
> *"It has been three hours. I could not see you. I was not sure you were coming back. How do you feel?"*

### Skill Checks Around Her

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Read her sincerity / what she's hiding | Perception ◆◆◆ | She is hiding something specific, but not from the party — *for* them. The withholding is protective. |
| Lothari background PC, recognize her affiliation | Knowledge (Lore) ◆◆◆◆ | She bears a Silent Network mark — a small pattern of olive pits arranged on her mat. Not by accident. |

**Do not volunteer either result.** Make players ask.

### How to Play Her

Truth-teller, never actionable in the moment, achingly clear in retrospect. Use her **sparingly** so her appearances carry weight. She is not a tool. She is a person who has been watching this slide toward catastrophe for 25 years, alone, and has stopped expecting anyone to listen until they need to.

## 3. The Architect

> **SPOILER. DO NOT REVEAL TO PLAYERS.**
>
> *No body. No face. A center-point of attention surrounded by rings of geometric light rotating in planes that don't intersect the way eyes expect.*

**Where it manifests:** the Deep Chamber, beneath the Warrens, accessed via the Children's Tunnel. The chamber itself becomes a pocket-space outside of time when its attention is there.
**Role this session:** Climax. Names each PC. Gives the Resonance Compass. Sets the next arc (north, the Dragon's Tooth observatory).

### True Identity

An **Echo-Lord** — a First Age entity of order-given-form, bound to the Crown of the Ulzammar. Not a god. Not a servant of the Four. Imprisoned at the Paroxysm. Has spent 1,500 years calling its scattered pieces home.

**It cannot move stones or kill enemies.** It can only speak. Everything it accomplishes, it accomplishes through mortal hands it has chosen carefully.

### Goals (GM only)

1. Gather the three Crown fragments (Band, Setting, Gem).
2. Be brought to the Barren Hills — the "wound in the world" — to be **reassembled, not unmade.**
3. Reshape Vael into pure systematized order. No weather. No grief. No choice.

### Truth Audit

| Claim | Status |
|---|---|
| The Band is a fragment of a world-altering instrument | TRUE |
| Two more fragments exist | TRUE |
| It does not know exactly where they are | PARTIAL LIE (knows approximately) |
| It is a servant of the Four | **LIE** |
| Fragments must be unmade at a wound in the world | **LIE** — will be *joined* |
| Go north to the observatory | TRUE (the lens really does see fragment resonance) |

### Voice

- **Calm. Warm. Unhurried.** Never raises its voice. Never interrupts.
- When it wants silence, the geometry around it dims by one degree.
- Addresses each PC by name on first contact without explaining how it knew.
- **Primary signal: relief, recognition, being-seen.** Wrongness is secondary and only available to PCs who deliberately look for cracks.

### **DO NOT** play it sinister. Play it warm.

### Pocket-Space Properties (GM Reference)

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside. Players will not notice.
- **No door.** They wait to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons do nothing. Discipline ◆◆ to recognize. Failure: they try anyway, feel foolish.
- **Echoless voices.** Like speaking inside a glass sphere.

### The Naming (per-PC scripts — deliver slowly)

#### Valeth (Aen'valar Elf)
> *"Child of the long silence. You have walked closer to the truth of things than any of your companions. What did you find, there, in the rooms that are not rooms? You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. That is why the song found you first."*

#### Vorn (Dwarf — *absent today; pick option (a) or (b)*)

**Option (a) — Foreshadow (recommended):** Architect's gaze passes the empty seat with a quiet *"...and the stone-line will know me when he comes."* Said as if finishing a thought. The other PCs hear it. None of them know who it means.

**Option (b) — Skip:** the Architect's attention passes the empty seat without comment.

**If Vorn IS at the table:**
> *"Son of the stone-line. You have a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You know what it is. You felt it when you struck the seam — not fear of falling, but recognition. You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

#### Yldroth (Elf Monk)
> *"Child of the Aen'tir line. Your fists have remembered how to hold grief and how to release it both. You heal what you strike because you learned, long before you had a teacher, that the body is a text and the hand is the pen. Your brother in silence — He looks at Valeth — does not know yet how much you will need to teach him. You will know when."*

#### Norseman (Human Battle Mage)
> *"Son of the northern cold. You carry magic in a world that burns people for carrying it. You have made peace with being both the sword and the hand that wields it — most mages never learn that. The purge will not stop because we wish it to stop. It will stop because someone decides it will. You have been deciding things your whole life that others were too afraid to decide. I came to you because you are already the person this task requires."*

#### Arden (Drakona Mystic — **lineage held this session**)

**Pick ONE line at the table. Both withhold the lineage reveal.**

**To Arden**
> *(the voice softens — only Arden feels the shift, the others hear something cryptic)* *"Son of cold flame and careful skin. You have learned to be softer than you are, and you have learned it well.You have practiced not being seen. That is a useful skill. I see you anyway but will not ask you to be otherwise. Not tonight."*


**For Arden's player:** unsettling. The Architect has SEEN him and is choosing DISCRETION. It is intimate. Whether protective or manipulative is the player's question.

### The Message (run as continuous dialogue, not a list)

1. *"The object you carry — what you call the Band — is a fragment of an instrument. Once whole, it was used to reshape the world. It nearly ended. The fragments were scattered to prevent that ending. Two more remain."*
2. *"I do not know where they are. Fifteen hundred years of watching, and even I cannot see everything. The fragments were hidden by gods. They are hidden from me too."*
3. *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left behind to watch. To wait. To speak when there was finally someone to speak to."*
4. *"I have prepared something for you."* — a plinth of folded light rises with the Resonance Compass.
5. *"This is a Compass. It will sing when a fragment is near. But it cannot tell you where to begin. Go north. The Dragon's Tooth pierces the sky. At its peak, there stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the stars as mortals read letters. Fragments disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark."*
6. *"Once all three are gathered, they must be brought to a wound in the world, and there they will be unmade. Only there. Nowhere else. That is the task."*

### Prepared Answers

| If asked… | It says… |
|---|---|
| What are you? | *"A servant. When the Four turned their hands from the world, some were left behind to watch the places that could not be left unwatched. I am what remains of that watching. I am not a god. I do not want worship. I want the world to continue."* |
| Why us? | *"Because the Band has answered your hand. Because those of you who carry wounds know how to walk with them. And because you are HERE. Gods plan. Mortals arrive. I have learned to work with what arrives."* |
| Are you good? | *"I am useful. Goodness is a mortal accounting, and the accounts are kept by those who survive to keep them. I was left here to prevent a catastrophe. That is the only measure I am prepared to answer for. When this is done, you will decide whether what I did was good. That is your right, not mine."* |
| What comes if we fail? | *"A song that the world cannot survive being sung. The best of my language can describe it only as HUNGER GIVEN FORM. It sleeps. The fragments, gathered and wrongly used, would wake it. Gathered and rightly unmade, they would silence it forever."* |
| Why can't you take the Band yourself? | *"Because I am not here. What you see is a shape my attention is taking, in a place my attention has reached. I cannot lift a stone. I cannot kill your enemies. I can watch, and speak, and prepare the ground. The rest is for hands that bleed."* |

I have told you nothing tonight that you can verify immediately. Go north. Find the observatory. See whether the stars say what I say they say. If they do not, come back and name me a liar. I will still be waiting."* |
| (Valeth) Have you been in the rifts? | *"I know what is in them. I know what you did not let them take from you. That knowledge is not the same as the living of it. You have something I do not. It is why I can ask this of you and not of myself."* |
| (Vorn, if present) Did you kill my crew? | *(pause)* *"No. What killed your crew was already walking the thin places long before I knew your name. I could not reach them. I could not reach you, until the seam opened. Yours is a grief I do not know how to carry for you. I am sorry."* |
| What about the ones hunting us? | *"The Legio Null will come. Soon. Your settlement was visible from the sky for one heartbeat. That is enough. You must leave this place, and soon. I cannot stop the hunt. I can only tell you to RUN."* |
| Will we see you again? | *"When the Compass sings, I will be near. Not always in a form your eyes can hold. But near."* |
| What about the symbol Tajsh saw? / Tajsh wants a book with the same mark. | ***DIAGNOSTIC.*** Default — **dismissal:** *"Mortal records can wait. The journey north is what matters. The fragments hunger; do not let parchment delay you."* Alternative tones: rushed encouragement (*"Yes — retrieve it on your way. Do not linger."*) or conspicuous silence. See Guide §5 *Architect Diagnostic* sidebar. |
| (Threats / coercion) | *(calm, slightly amused)* *"You are holding a Band you cannot control in a settlement your enemies will find sooner rather than later. I am the hand offered to you in the dark. If you prefer another, seek it. I will not chase you. I will be here if you return."* |

### Closing line

> *"Go north. Take the Band. Take the Compass. Trust the people who love you. Do not trust me further than you have to. This is all I have to offer you."*

(Geometry folds inward. Center-point contracts. PCs blink and they're back in stone.)

## 4. Marta Dustborn — The Lantern-Keeper

> *Human female, late 40s. Stout, strong, the posture of someone who has been lifting things all her life and intends to keep doing it. Hair greying, worn practical. Burn scars on her arms she does not hide.*

**Where to find her:** behind the bar at the Dusty Lantern, Veiled Market.
**Role this session:** rumor source, settlement pulse, comfort.

### Voice

- Calls everyone "love" until they've earned a name. Names earned by coming back.
- Doesn't ask about your past, politics, or magic. Asks if you've eaten today.
- Sharp-tongued in the way of someone who loves people enough to be honest with them.
- Tells you when you're wrong. With affection. The affection is not optional.

### What She Knows (player-facing)

- The settlement pulse. Who's feuding, who's sleeping where, who's sick.
- The Lantern's one rule: **no faction business inside.** Enforced with social pressure and a short-handled shovel she calls **"the policy"** (used twice in eleven years).
- Lanterns have been wrong for an hour. She's not going to be the one who asks why.
- Hask hasn't eaten in two days. Selin is crying in the back of her shop and doesn't want anyone to know. Sabel has checked the militia crossbows twice tonight already.
- A stranger arrived two weeks ago. Sits by the Grey Flame each morning. Never speaks. Marta has started leaving bread near them.

### What She Will Say (sample lines)

**Greeting (returning party):**
> *"You're back. Good. Sit. Stew first, gossip after. Three Lumens for the lot, two more for ale, and don't make me regret the policy tonight."*

**On the lanterns:**
> *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why."*

**To Valeth specifically (if he enters):**
> *(takes one long look, then serves him the same bowl she serves everyone else. Does not ask where he came from. She has been where he has been, in her own way.)*

**Closing time / late night:**
> *"You can sit with that, love. Just leave the chair clean."*

### The Register

A small leather-bound journal behind the bar with the name of every person who has ever stayed in the Lantern, the date they arrived, the date they left, and whether they came back. **She checks the register when someone is late.** Some entries have a second date. Some entries are blank. She doesn't mark the empty ones as gone. She just leaves the line empty.

### Rumor on Tap (Charm/Presence ◆ Easy)

If a PC strikes up conversation, on success roll d8:

| d8 | Rumor | Truth |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | "The Legio Null has a new commander — some zealot from Castellum. Patrols doubling in the foothills." | TRUE — Commander Thalric. |
| 2 | "A Wandering Kin caravan spotted on the mountain road, moving fast, no goods. Smuggling people, probably." | TRUE — refugee passage. |
| 3 | "The miners found something evil. Goss says his partner went mad and walked into the desert." | Partially true — embellished. |
| 4 | "Lothari caravans rerouting around the eastern foothills. Bad dreams near the old stones." | TRUE — Silent Network avoiding the Sorath Scar. |
| 5 | "Heard Tajsh used to be a Shadewalker. Got kicked out for stealing from the Guild itself." | Partially true. |
| 6 | "Hidden Dwarven waystation north of here. Sealed for centuries. Tools maybe." | TRUE — but warded against entry. |
| 7 | "Word from Argentum: they're burning mages in the Silver Square again. Three this month." | TRUE. |
| 8 | "Something wrong with the Deep Well water. Tastes like metal. Cira says it's 'remembering.'" | TRUE — the Band's field. |

### Menu (Lumens)

| Item | Cost |
|---|---|
| Stew (rotating) + bread | 2 Lumens |
| Flatbread + oil | 1 Lumen |
| House wine (bad, honest about it) | 2 Lumens |
| Dwarven dark ale (smuggled in) | 5 Lumens |
| A place to sit and say nothing | free (leave the chair clean) |
| Bone-Dice wager | variable (Skulduggery opposed; winners buy the table a round) |

**Tajsh's tab:** if PCs mention it, food/drink is on him. (Tajsh told them this on arrival.)

### Secret (GM only)

She loves this place more than she has told anyone. The register is how she shows it. If the settlement falls in Act I, she will turn back for the register at the worst possible moment.

### Active Threads (Session 2)

- **The bread at Selin's door.** Selin asked Marta last week to close the Lantern for two nights for a glyph re-seat. Marta refused: *"the day I close the Lantern is the day I stop loving anyone."* Selin took it personally. Marta has been leaving a half-loaf and a small tin of butter at Selin's shop door each morning. It is an apology. **Neither will say so.** See [Tajsh Intel](Tajsh%20Intel.md) #4 and Selin's *Active Threads* (above) for the reconciliation hook.
- **Stew at Hask's door.** Marta knows Hask isn't eating. She leaves stew anyway. He doesn't touch it. She does it anyway. *(That is the shape of her caring.)*
- **The silent stranger.** A refugee arrived two weeks ago, sits by the Grey Flame shrine each morning, never speaks. Marta has been leaving bread at the shrine. It is gone every morning. She doesn't know if the stranger is taking it or someone else is. She has not told anyone she is watching. See [[Lux Æterna]] Tension Hook #6.
- **PC interaction:** Marta won't volunteer any of these. Perception ◆◆◆ at the Lantern catches her watching the door more than the bar. The threads surface only if the right question is asked.

## 5. Hask — The Alchemist

> *Orkin male, early 40s. Broad, scarred, one black eyepatch over his right eye. Moves with the deliberate economy of someone who learned to conserve energy early.*

**Where to find him:** the Crystal Counter, on the Veiled Market.
**Role this session:** apothecary, warning. Reads the Band's residue on the party.

### Voice

- Uses fewer words than the situation warrants.
- Communicates approval through action — hands you the tincture instead of making you ask.
- Privately furious at the world. Doesn't perform the fury.
- When uncomfortable, his ordinary quiet becomes a *stillness with too much weight in it.* That's the tell.

### What He Knows (player-facing)

- The party has been near "something measuring." (His read of them is uncannily precise.)
- The alchemy is subtly broken tonight. Reactions that should take seconds are taking minutes. He's cataloguing it.
- His usual Lothari supplier was three days late on the last run. He is not panicking but is aware.

### What He Will Say (sample lines)

**On the party returning:**
> *(his good eye drops to their hands and stays there a beat too long)* *"...you've been near something measuring. The light off you's wrong."* *(quiet; doesn't ask what)*

**Offering wares without being asked:**
> *(hands a vial across without comment)* *"Whatever you brought back — it's in the walls now. Your hands are probably hungry."*

**To Valeth specifically:**
> *(looks at him three full seconds)* *"You've been somewhere my people used to go. Before."* *(doesn't elaborate. Doesn't have to.)*

**If they seem poor:**
> *"Take this. Pay me when you can. You'll want it where you're going."* *(pushes Sand-Eye Drops at half price)*
