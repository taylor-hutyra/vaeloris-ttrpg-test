# Lux Æterna — Read-Alouds

> Two GM-table read-aloud scripts. Each lands in **~1.5–2 minutes** at a careful, paced read. Tonight-specific wrongness (lanterns out of rhythm, the held hum, the dog refusing to enter, metallic water) is preserved in the **Arrival** piece; the **Essence** piece is timeless and can be reused at any session opening while the party is in the canyon.

---

## I. Arrival

*Read when the party crests the last ridge and rounds the second dogleg of the slot canyon. Beat 2 of Session 2.*

> *You round the second dogleg, and the slot opens, and Lux Æterna takes shape inside the cliffs.*
>
> *Walls of sandstone climb sixty feet and lean inward at the top, leaving a thin slash of bruised purple sky. The cliff faces are honeycombed — dwellings carved at two and three levels, doorways glowing amber with lantern-light, ladders and rope-and-plank walkways stitching them together. Smoke from cookfires threads up through natural chimney cracks and dissipates long before the rim. Below, the canyon floor: salvaged-stone cobbles, the Veiled Market lit at its edges by oil lamps shaded down to amber pinpricks. Far above, two ward-tenders roped to the rock face, chalk-lamps swinging at their hips.*
>
> *The smells hit all at once — cookfire smoke, goat stew, the mineral tang of well-water, cactus-root ale, the sweet ghost of incense from the Grey Flame. Your shoulders drop without asking. The camels slow without being told. You're inside the wards. You're home.*
>
> *Except.*
>
> *The market is too quiet. Nobody is arguing — and in Lux Æterna, somebody is always arguing. A low sustained hum carries from the mouth of the Warrens, pitched lower than a human throat should comfortably hold, and it has been going for a while.*
>
> *The lanterns aren't keeping rhythm. One steady. Two steady. All four flicker in unison. The whole left wall pulses once as a single organism, then settles.*
>
> *A working dog crosses your path. Stops. Looks back the way you came in. Backs up two steps. Then turns, and runs. Out. Outside. **Out.***
>
> *And the water in the basin near the gate, when one of you stoops to splash your face — tastes faintly of iron.*

**Handoff to Valeth (private, after the read-aloud lands):**

> *"And the song you followed for four days — the one that went silent the moment you left the foothills — is here. Faint. Underneath the hum. As if someone is playing it back from inside the rock."*

## II. The Essence of Lux Æterna

*A "what your characters know" piece. Read at the table once players are seated at the Veiled Market, after Arrival but before the Sandbox opens. Reusable across sessions while the party is in the canyon — split it across two sessions if needed; each section header is a natural pause.*

> *You know this place the way you know the inside of your own wrists.*
>
> ***Lux Æterna.** Eternal Light — a name given in defiance, not in description. The canyon itself is dark; the walls lean inward until the sky is a pale slash, and the floor lives in permanent twilight. The light is what the people make.*
>
> *Founded SA:1479 — eleven years ago — by mages fleeing the third decade of the Purge Wars. They carved the first glyphs of the Stitching by hand into the rock face: blood, chalk, and an old book none of them had a complete copy of. Outside, the canyon registers as unremarkable. Inside, you are invisible to the Legio Null. That was the deal the founders cut with the rock, and it has not been renegotiated since.*
>
> ***Tajsh** arrived four years after founding and turned a refugee camp into an economy — the Vault, the Veiled Market, the supply lines. **Sabel** came three years after that, a Varnathi deserter who took the Outlook and the perimeter. **Old Cira** drifted in a year later — Lothari, blind, age uncertain. Her riddles come true two days late. **Marta** has not turned anyone away since.*
>
> *Four hundred souls now. Mages of every stripe. Refugees from a dozen kingdoms. Halfling Wayfinders who scout and vanish. Lothari mystics who sit still and watch the light change. A handful of Gnomes, Orkin, Thulkorûn dwarves. Everyone here has come from somewhere worse.*
>
> *At the south end of the market, a niche in the canyon wall holds a single candle behind smoky glass, and above it a charcoal sketch of a gaunt man with wild eyes. **Melek the Grey** — the first mortal in living memory to demonstrate controlled mage-craft. Dead six years later, mind burned through. Name outlawed by the Decree, every record of him ordered erased.*
>
> *The candle has burned for eleven years without going out. Every mage in this canyon has, at one time or another, struck flint and lit it again.*
>
> *That candle is the closest thing this settlement has to a flag. A flag the wind cannot blow out.*
>
> *You have walls of rock, walls of glyph-magic, walls of practiced silence.*
>
> *You have home. For a given value of home.*

## GM Notes

- **Pacing.** Each read-aloud is ~280–330 words. Practice once aloud beforehand — the rhythm depends on the small pauses (every period, every line break). Don't rush.
- **Arrival ends on a beat, not a question.** After "tastes faintly of iron," **stop.** Let the silence hang. Don't prompt. Let a player be the one who breaks it.
- **Essence has natural pause points** at every section break. If you feel the table getting restless, stop after the founders paragraph or after the candle, pick up the rest at a later moment in the same session or the next.
- **Tonight-specific cues** (the dog, the metallic water, the hum, the lanterns) live only in the Arrival read-aloud. The Essence piece is timeless — reuse it freely.
- **Valeth's handoff** at the end of Arrival is a private beat for his player. Don't broadcast. Pass it as a quiet aside or a slipped note.
