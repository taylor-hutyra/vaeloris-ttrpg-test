## 1. Lux Æterna — Overview

> *"Eternal Light" — a name given in defiance, not description. The canyon is dark. The people in it refuse to be.*

### What It Is

A hidden refugee settlement of approximately **400 souls** concealed in a deep box canyon in the desert foothills between the Lothari Desert and the Varnathi Highlands. Founded SA:1479 — eleven years before the campaign. Shelters persecuted mages, deserters, refugees — anyone the Decree of Prohibition has marked for death.

### How It Survives

Three overlapping systems:
- **Geography** — narrow naturally concealed canyon with a dogleg entrance (no line-of-sight from outside).
- **Discipline** — strict light management, smoke vented through natural chimney cracks, no external signage.
- **Magic** — the **Stitching**: a network of carved glyphs radiating an aversion field. Anyone approaching without prior knowledge feels uneasy and looks elsewhere.

### Geography

- Canyon runs north–south. ~300 ft long, 80–120 ft wide (widest at center), 60–80 ft tall walls leaning inward at the top.
- Entrance: a narrow ~10 ft crack at the south end, doglegging east then north. From outside, looks like any other dry wash in the foothills.
- North end (deepest, narrowest): cliff face holds the cave openings into the Warrens.

### How the Stitching Works

Not an illusion. Works on **perception and attention.**

- **1+ miles out:** the canyon registers as unremarkable. Eyes slide over it.
- **500 ft – 1 mile:** growing unease. A gut feeling that this is a bad route.
- **Under 500 ft:** active disorientation. Difficulty remembering why you came this way. Low-grade headache. Sense of being watched.

The wards do **not** affect anyone who has been inside before. Once you've been here, you can find it again. (This is why every departure is a security risk and why Sabel tracks every one personally.)

### Failure Mode

The wards thin gradually. Outer ring weakens first → scouts start filing the canyon as "possible water source." Middle ring weakens → patrols start approaching. Inner ring fails → someone stands at the canyon mouth wondering why they feel uneasy.

Full degradation cycle once tenders stop refreshing: ~2 weeks.

### Currently (TA:412 / Session 2)

- Stitching is **stuttering** (per Selin) — never failed before but currently unstable. The Band's field is interfering.
- Six ward-tenders for an eight-tender weave. Outer ring runs unreliable in ~6 months at current staffing.
- **The flicker:** during the Revelation, the Stitching drops for one heartbeat. Anyone outside scanning the foothills in that instant locks attention onto the canyon. **Legio Null gets a fix.** The players don't need to know this yet.

### History — One-Paragraph

Founded SA:1479 by mage-refugees fleeing the Mage-Purge Wars. Tajsh Thale arrived SA:1483, established the Veiled Market. Sabel arrived SA:1486 after deserting the Varnathi Legion. Old Cira arrived SA:1487. Population reached 400 by SA:1488 — the canyon's practical capacity. Campaign begins SA:1490 / TA:412 with the discovery of the Ulzammar Band.

### Districts

- **Veiled Market** — commercial heart, safe zone.
- **The Warrens** — residential cave network.
- **The Outlook** — defensive perimeter on the canyon rim.
- **Deep Chamber** — pre-settlement First Age ruin beneath everything (only accessible via the Children's Tunnel).

### Power Structure

No formal government.
- **Tajsh** holds economy, information, external contacts.
- **Sabel** holds security, ward-tending schedule, patrols.
- They cooperate by mutual respect and necessity.
- **Old Cira** holds no authority and considerable influence.

### Read-Aloud — The Essence of Lux Æterna

*A "what your characters know" piece. Read at the table once players are seated at the Veiled Market, after Arrival but before the Sandbox opens. Lands in ~1.5–2 minutes. Reusable across sessions while the party is in the canyon — split it across two sessions if needed; each section break is a natural pause. Standalone copy with GM notes: [Read-Alouds-Lux-Aeterna.md](Read-Alouds-Lux-Aeterna.md).*

> *You know this place the way you know the inside of your own wrists.*
>
> ***Lux Æterna.** Eternal Light — a name given in defiance, not in description. The canyon itself is dark; the walls lean inward until the sky is a pale slash, and the floor lives in permanent twilight. The light is what the people make.*
>
> *Founded SA:1479 — eleven years ago — by mages fleeing the third decade of the Purge Wars. They carved the first glyphs of the Stitching by hand into the rock face: blood, chalk, and an old book none of them had a complete copy of. Outside, the canyon registers as unremarkable. Inside, you are invisible to the Legio Null. That was the deal the founders cut with the rock, and it has not been renegotiated since.*
>
> ***Tajsh** arrived four years after founding and turned a refugee camp into an economy — the Vault, the Veiled Market, the supply lines. **Sabel** came three years after that, a Varnathi deserter who took the Outlook and the perimeter. **Old Cira** drifted in a year later — Lothari, blind, age uncertain. Her riddles come true two days late. **Marta** has not turned anyone away since.*
>
> *Four hundred souls now. Mages of every stripe. Refugees from a dozen kingdoms. Halfling Wayfinders who scout and vanish. Lothari mystics who sit still and watch the light change. A handful of Gnomes, Orkin, Thulkorûn dwarves. Everyone here has come from somewhere worse.*
>
> *At the south end of the market, a niche in the canyon wall holds a single candle behind smoky glass, and above it a charcoal sketch of a gaunt man with wild eyes. **Melek the Grey** — the first mortal in living memory to demonstrate controlled mage-craft. Dead six years later, mind burned through. Name outlawed by the Decree, every record of him ordered erased.*
>
> *The candle has burned for eleven years without going out. Every mage in this canyon has, at one time or another, struck flint and lit it again.*
>
> *That candle is the closest thing this settlement has to a flag. A flag the wind cannot blow out.*
>
> *You have walls of rock, walls of glyph-magic, walls of practiced silence.*
>
> *You have home. For a given value of home.*

## 2. The Slot Canyon Approach

*The entrance. The party's first sight on returning.*

### Read-Aloud (when they arrive)

> *The sun has been down an hour by the time you reach the slot canyon. Two sandstone pillars — identical to anyone who doesn't know what they're looking at. A narrow slot. A quarter-mile of stone walls. And then the lanterns bloom out of the dark, and Lux Æterna is there, waiting for you.*
>
> *Except it isn't, exactly. The lanterns are flickering wrong. One steady, one steady, flicker, flicker-flicker, steady. The market is quieter than it should be — not silent, just careful. People aren't arguing tonight. That isn't normal.*
>
> *From the mouth of the Warrens, someone is humming a single sustained note. Low. Long. Not quite a pitch a human throat should be able to hold. The note has been going for a while.*

**Valeth recognizes the song.** *(prompt his player)* *"That's the song. It's here too. It's been here the whole time."*

### Layout

- **Two sandstone pillars** flank the entrance — natural, unremarkable from any approach.
- **The Slot:** ~10 ft wide, ~30 ft tall walls. Doglegs east, then north. ~250 ft of stone before opening.
- **The Inner Mouth:** widens into the canyon proper. Lanterns visible in the cliff-face dwellings above.

### Sentries

Two ward-tenders are roped to the cliff face above the inner mouth, refreshing glyphs by chalk-lamp. They will nod at returning faces but do not greet strangers.

### What Players Sense Without Rolling

- Lanterns out of rhythm (lanterns visible from the inner mouth).
- Market quieter than usual.
- Low sustained hum from the Warrens.
- Smell of cookfires, cactus-root ale, goat stew, faint metal in the well water.

### Available Rolls

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Read the wrongness | Perception ◆◆ | Three of four working dogs they pass are refusing to come inside the canyon. |
| Sense the mana state | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆ | The Stitching above is wavering — not failing, but stuttering. Something's interfering with the ambient mana. |
| Notice anything off-pattern | Vigilance (Willpower) ◆◆◆ | The signal-glass on the upper rim has a different colored panel set than usual — Sabel's emergency alert preset. |

### Exit / Transition

Drop them at the **Veiled Market.** Beat 3 (Sandbox) opens.

## 3. The Veiled Market — Overview

*The settlement's commercial and social heart. The safest place in Lux Æterna.*

### Read-Aloud

> *The Veiled Market fills the canyon at its widest — roughly 120 feet across — where the cliffs step back and a broad natural overhang on the west wall shelters half the market floor. The sky is a white-hot slash far above, but down here the canyon walls cast deep permanent shade. Stalls press against each other in no particular order: a spice seller wedged between an armorer and a fortune-teller. The cobbles are salvaged stone and packed dirt, worn smooth by 400 people walking the same paths over eleven years.*
>
> *The central well is the anchor — a wide stone ring worn to smoothness, perpetually surrounded by someone fetching water, someone sitting on its edge thinking, and at least one child using it as a stage. In the cliff faces, dwellings and shops at two levels glow amber with lantern-light from carved doorways. By full evening the floor is lit by shaded oil lamps — kept low, angled inward, never bright enough to cast a glow visible from the rim.*

### Tonight (Session 2 Specific)

- **Quieter than normal.** People aren't arguing. That's not normal.
- **Lanterns are out of rhythm** — one flickers, two steady, all four flicker, three steady.
- **Well water tastes metallic** (the Band's field).
- **Cira is sitting outside the Vault** humming a single sustained note, facing the Warrens.
- **Selin is up a ladder against the canyon wall** retouching a wavering glyph, muttering.
- **Three of four working dogs** refuse to come inside.

### Social Rules

- The market is governed by **reputation and debt**, not authority.
- Information flows: **rumors → Marta** at the Lantern; **relic intel → Tajsh** at the Vault; **dream-warnings → Cira**; **medical → Hask**; **ward anomalies → Selin**.
- Tomm Broadback at the Iron Shelf is the only stall that doesn't mark up.

### Pricing Convention

- **Common goods:** 150% standard.
- **Magical / enchanted:** 200% standard.
- **Tomm at Iron Shelf:** standard prices. Don't make a thing of it.

## 4. Tajsh's Vault

*Tajsh Thale's relic brokerage. Where the campaign began, and where the Revelation will fire.*

### Read-Aloud

> *The Vault is wedged between a weaver's stall and a Lothari spice-merchant — easy to miss if you don't know it's there. A heavy wooden door with iron banding, no signage, and a small brass bell wired to ring inside when the door opens. The front room is cluttered: shelves of catalogued artifacts (boxed and labelled), a long workbench, a rolled-up Lothari rug pinned with notes, a kettle on a small brazier. The back wall has a locked door. Tajsh's office is upstairs, accessed by a narrow staircase tucked behind the workbench.*
>
> *The office is small. Desk. Two chairs. A kettle that is whistling and being ignored. A window onto the canyon rim — Tajsh can see the Outlook from his chair.*

### What's Here

- **Front room:** catalogued First Age artifacts, prospecting maps, Tajsh's working inventory. Not for sale unless he says so.
- **Locked back room:** **Items Tajsh shouldn't have but does.** GM only — do not open this session unless players push hard. Inside (sample): three sealed Shadewalker briefing cylinders, a Wandering Kin charter signed in blood, two First Age glyph-rubbings he won't explain.
- **Upstairs office:** desk, two chairs (only two, for five PCs — *let this register*), kettle, ledger. **The Band sits on the desk wrapped in dark wool** during this session.

### People Here

- **Tajsh Thale** (always).
- **A runner** — a teenage boy named **Korel** who fetches messages between Tajsh and the rest of the settlement. He'll appear if Tajsh sends for the party.

### Tonight Specifically (Sandbox / Revelation)

When the party arrives:
- Tajsh gestures at the chairs. Only two for five of you. He waits to see who sits.
- He does not ask the new ones (Valeth, Vorn) their names. Yet.
- The party sets the wrapped Band on his desk. Tajsh unwraps a corner. Sees the dark crystal and pale metal veining. Doesn't touch it.
- **The candle on his desk stops flickering.** The flame is still lit. It is not moving. He watches it for a long second. Then he picks it up, carries it across the room, sets it on a shelf near the door. Comes back.
- *"Give me tonight with this. Come back in the morning."*

### Shop Goods (rare — only if asked)

Tajsh doesn't sell common gear. If pressed for relic-hunting gear:
- **Pry bars / chisels** — ~20 Lumens
- **Glyph-rubbing kit** — ~30 Lumens (charcoal, vellum, fixative)
- **Lothari-marked travel maps** — 40–80 Lumens depending on destination

## 16. The Deep Chamber

*The oldest room in the Warrens. Pre-settlement. Possibly pre-Paroxysm. The Architect's pocket-space.*

### How to Get There

From the Warrens: past the Schoolroom, past the Sick-Bay, past the Deep Well, through the **Children's Tunnel** behind the well. Beyond the tunnel, a narrow passage descends into older stone. The walls become *cut* rather than natural. Cuts are First Age style — too-regular, geometric, precise beyond mortal patience.

### Read-Aloud — Descent

> *The tunnel narrows as you go. The walls start to feel older than the rest of the settlement — because they are. Lux Æterna was built over someone else's ruin. You've always known that. You just haven't had a reason to care, until tonight.*
>
> *The air cools. The torch flame flickers slower. Slower. Then it stops flickering entirely. It is still burning. It just isn't moving.*
>
> *You reach the chamber. Round, empty, stone. There's nothing here. You've walked to the end of the tunnel system.*
>
> *And then the last of you crosses the threshold.*

### Physical Description (Before the Architect)

A roughly circular stone room, about twenty feet across. Cut stone walls. No exits other than the tunnel. Nothing else in the room. A faint circular indentation in the floor at the center — as if something once stood there and was removed a very long time ago.

The stone is cold. The air is still. Torches flicker less and less the deeper one descends. By the time visitors reach the chamber, the flame holds its shape like a held breath.

### What Actually Happens — Read-Aloud (the Transition)

> *The stone behind you is no longer there. Not dark. Not hidden. Not THERE. You don't turn around to check. You know, the way you know where your own hand is, that if you turned around there would be nothing to turn toward.*
>
> *Your torch is still. The flame has not gone out. It is simply holding its shape, like someone pressed pause on it.*
>
> *The air is warm and has no smell. Your breathing is slower than it should be. You aren't running out of air. You aren't getting any, either.*
>
> *Around you — not close, not far, distance isn't the right word here — rings of geometric light rotate in planes that don't intersect the way your eyes expect them to. Symbols you don't know the names of drift past and through each other. Some of them are versions of the three-ring mark. Some of them are things that mark could grow into if you let it keep going.*
>
> *There is no door. There is no floor, but you are not falling. You are, in every sense that matters, standing somewhere that is not a place.*
>
> *And at the center of all of it — not a figure, exactly. A CENTER. A point where the geometry converges and something is looking back.*

**Pause. Let the silence sit. Let a player be the one who speaks first. When ready:**

> ***"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."***

### Pocket-Space Properties

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside. Players will not notice.
- **No door.** They wait to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons do nothing. **Discipline ◆◆** to recognize. Failure: they try anyway, feel foolish.
- **Echoless voices.** Like speaking inside a glass sphere.

### What Players See if They Look Carefully

| Intent | Check | Outcome |

| Notice something visually wrong | Perception ◆◆ | The Architect casts no reflection in the floating crystal symbols. Nothing else here has this problem. |
| Read beneath its composure | Perception ◆◆◆ | When it speaks of "unmaking," the tone shifts toward *anticipation,* not relief. **Triumph:** that anticipation reads as *hunger.* |
| Magical signature | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | Divine on surface, **abyssal** underneath. Perfume over rot. |
| Environment | Knowledge (Nature) ◆◆ | A moth drifted in with the party. It is *paused* mid-flight. Not dead. Paused. |
| Floating symbols | Knowledge (Lore) ◆◆◆◆ | Compositional notation — music for frequencies no mortal throat can produce. The three-ring mark is a **chord.** Triumph: Torvin's sketch was a degraded version. |
