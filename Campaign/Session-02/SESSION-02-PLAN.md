## §2 — Beat 1: The Ride Back (8–10 min, no combat)

### Opening flavor

> *The mining camp falls behind you. The wind picks up after sundown — fine grit against the lips, the camels' steady plod, the wrapped Band weighing in someone's pack heavier than its size should allow. Valeth, the song you followed for four days is gone; the desert is just the desert again.*

### Burial flavor (default: buried)

- **If buried:** *"You left them under stone-cairns at dusk. Vorn rode quiet for an hour after, hands tight on the reins. The camels move easier than they did. The Band weighs heavier."*
- **If NOT buried:** *"You left them where they lay. By the time you crested the first dune, the buzzards had already stopped circling — they'd settled in. Nobody looks back. Norseman stops glancing east after the second hour."*

### Narrative dice menu

Offer these to the table. Players take none, one, or several. Each = ~1–2 min.

| # | Opportunity | Skill / Difficulty | Payoff |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | An old Lothari trade-cairn off the road, half-buried | **Perception ◆◆** | Cached water-skin + 1 day's rations × party. *Triumph:* +10 Lumens in oilcloth. |
| 2 | Recognize the cairn's clan-mark | **Knowledge Lore ◆◆◆** | This stretch was a *Lothari Silent Network* relay route a generation ago; PC may ask one follow-up question. |
| 3 | Read the dunes for shortcuts | **Survival ◆◆** | Shave ~30 min off the ride; arrive in better light; ◼ to the next overland Survival check. |
| 4 | Resist the Band's whisper through the wrap | **Discipline ◆◆** | *Success:* the murmur quiets. *Failure:* 1 strain; the Band's temporal field flickers (a flame at the next campfire will lag for a heartbeat). |
| 5 | Mage PCs (Norseman, Arden, Valeth) sense the wider weave | **Knowledge Arcana ◆◆◆** | The *Stitching* on the western horizon is pulsing wrong. Foreshadows Lux Æterna trouble. |

### Personal-thread opening (NOT scripted)

> *"The camels are walking close enough that any of you can ride alongside any other for a quiet word."*

That's the whole prompt. Do not direct PCs toward each other. If they take the opening, run with it. If they don't, push to Arrival.

### Exit
Canyon mouth in sight at dusk.

### Time guardrail
If past **15 min** total session time, push to Arrival immediately. Drop unused dice menu options.

## §3 — Beat 2: Arrival (5–7 min)

### Read-aloud anchor

> *The dogleg crack in the cliffside opens, and Lux Æterna takes shape inside it: amber lanterns climbing the canyon walls in tiered carved dwellings, smoke from cookfires, the smell of cactus-root ale and goat stew. But something is wrong. The lanterns aren't keeping rhythm — one flickers a half-beat behind the others. A low hum drifts up from the Warrens mouth. The water in the basin near the gate tastes faintly of iron.*

### Recap module — who they already know

Read whichever apply based on Session 1 contact:

- **[[Tajsh Thale]]** — your employer. Relic broker. Office above his Vault on the market. Sharp, warm, never stops moving.
- **[[Marta Dustborn]]** — runs the **Dusty Lantern**. Tavern keeper, original settler. Keeps a register of every guest. The shovel behind the bar is called "the policy."
- **[[Hask]]** — alchemist at the **Crystal Counter**. Orkin. One eye, scarred, quiet. Tests every draught on himself.
- **[[Old Cira]]** — fortune-teller at her woven mat in the market. Lothari, blind, age uncertain. Her riddles come true two days late.
- **[[Warden Sabel]]** — head of defense. Patrols the Outlook above the canyon rim.

### Brief history (2–3 sentences max)

> *Lux Æterna is a refugee settlement of about four hundred souls — mages, deserters, anyone the Decree of Prohibition wants dead. Hidden by the **Stitching**, a glyph-ward that makes outsiders' eyes slide off the canyon. Eleven years standing. Holding its breath.*

### What players sense without rolling

- The market is quieter than it should be at dusk.
- Lanterns flicker out of rhythm.
- The water tastes metallic.
- A low hum drifts from the Warrens.

**No checks. No combat.** Drop them at the Veiled Market and open the Sandbox.

If past **22 min** total session time, you've spent too long here. Cut to Sandbox even mid-description.

## §4 — Beat 3: Lux Æterna Sandbox (35–45 min)

### Format

The city is open. Players choose where to go. Each activity is self-contained, 5–10 min, with a clear in/out.

### Convergence rule

Advance to the **Revelation** when **BOTH** are true:
- (a) Tajsh has had the journal in his hands for at least one full activity.
- (b) The table has had ~30 minutes of city play.

Whichever comes later. Until then, keep the city open.

### Activities at-the-ready (all prepped — players pick)

#### A. Tajsh's Vault — deliver Band & journal (8–12 min)

**Anchor:** [[Tajsh Thale]]
**Hook:** the handoff. Triggers Revelation when Tajsh reads the journal.

**Tajsh's first reaction to the Band:**
> *Tajsh sets the wrapped bundle on the desk like he's setting down a bird with a broken wing. He doesn't unwrap it. Just looks at it. Polishes his thumb-ring without realizing. "...you brought it here. Good. Good. We don't open this in the market. Not until I've sent for Ink. Sit down. Tell me everything."*

He'll read the journal here or take it to a back room. **The Revelation fires the moment he gets to the three-ring symbol page** — but you can stretch the moment if the table needs more city time first ("Let me sit with this. Get yourselves fed. I'll send for you.").

**If they hold the Band back:** Tajsh doesn't push. Asks what they're keeping back, drops it. The Band's effects worsen overnight. Cira summons them anyway later.

#### B. The Dusty Lantern — drink, eat, gossip (5–8 min)

**Anchor:** [[Marta Dustborn]]
**Hook:** rumors and stew.

**Marta's greeting:**
> *Marta sees them come through the door, and the line between her brows softens for half a heartbeat. "You're back. Good. Sit. Stew first, gossip after. Three Lumens for the lot, two more for ale, and don't make me regret the policy tonight."*

**Rumors on offer (drip them in over the meal):**
- *"Lanterns been strange three days. Sabel's tearing his hair out."*
- *"Hask's been short with everyone. Nervous. Won't say why."*
- *"Stranger sat by the Grey Flame yesterday morning. Stared at Melek's sketch for an hour. Said nothing. I don't like it."*
- *"Selin hasn't been sleeping. Heard her crying in the back of her shop. Don't tell her I said."*

**Prices:** stew + bread 2 Lumens; flatbread + oil 1 Lumen; house wine 2 Lumens; dark ale 5 Lumens.

#### C. The Crystal Counter — apothecary (5–8 min)

**Anchor:** [[Hask]]
**Hook:** Hask sees the Band's residue on them; one-eye-flash; warning.

**Hask's read on them:**
> *Hask's good eye drops to their hands and stays there a beat too long. "...you've been near something measuring. The light off you's wrong." He says it quiet. He doesn't ask what. He asks if they want anything to keep them upright a few days longer.*

**Apothecary list (Lumens):**
| Item | Cost | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| Ash-Root Paste | 5 | ◼ to Resilience vs heat/dehydration, 8 hrs |
| Scorpion-Sting Tincture | 15 | Halves poison damage, 1 hr |
| Sand-Eye Drops | 10 | Removes darkness penalties, 4 hrs (stings) |
| Cira's Dreamcatch | 25 | One prophetic dream |
| Standard Healing Draught | 20 | Heals 3 Wounds |

**If the party seems poor:** he flags down whichever PC he's read most clearly. *"Take this. Pay me when you can. You'll want it where you're going."* — pushes Sand-Eye Drops at half price.

#### D. Cira's Corner — fortunes (5–8 min)

**Anchor:** [[Old Cira]]
**Hook:** she's been waiting. Reads one PC. Riddle hits in 2 days.

**Cira's opening:**
> *Cira is on her mat, palms upturned, eyes filmed white. She doesn't look up when they approach. "You walked in carrying weather. The canyon felt it. Sit. One of you. Now."*

**Reading prompts (pick by which PC sits):**
- **Valeth:** *"You came back. Only just. There is something on the other side of the seam that knows your shape now. It will not call again, until it does."*
- **Yldroth:** *"Your hands carry two opposite things. Do not let one forget it lives inside the other."*
- **Norseman:** *"There is a child in this canyon who does not know yet what she is. She will look at you and know you have already been her. Do not flinch."*
- **Arden:** *(her hand pauses just over his, not touching)* *"You have practiced being smaller than you are. Keep practicing. Tonight is not the night."* — she withdraws, says nothing more.
- **Vorn (if present):** *"Your grandmother said three names and would not say four. The one she would not say is in this canyon now. Do not speak it either."*

**The riddle hits in two days** — track it for next session.

#### E. Iron Shelf — gear (3–5 min)

**Anchor:** Tomm Broadback (Halfling, blacksmith)
**Hook:** low-friction shopping. Standard prices, no markup. GM resolves at the table.

Tomm talks metallurgy like religion. If a PC asks a smithing-craft question, give them a free bit of insight. No checks needed.

#### F. Needle & Thread — ward-scribe (5–8 min)

**Anchor:** [[Selin Voss]]
**Hook:** if a PC asks about the wards, she'll talk. Foreshadows the Stitching.

Selin is chalk-dusted, shaking slightly, hasn't slept. **If a PC pushes:**
> *"The Stitching's been thinning for months. We're at six tenders for an eight-tender weave. I have a design — a self-sustaining glyph pattern — but it needs a forty-eight-hour shutdown to seat. Forty-eight hours of no wards. If anyone outside is looking..." (she trails off, shakes her head, won't finish).*

Foreshadow only. No payoff this session.

#### G. The Warrens — Petra at the well (5–10 min)

**Anchor:** [[Petra]] (10 years old, newly-manifested mage)
**Hook:** **emotional beat for Norseman in particular.** Optional — only fires if a PC wanders the Warrens.

**The scene:**
> *A girl, maybe ten, sits on the steps outside the schoolroom. Her fingers are sooty — the kind of soot that doesn't wash. She's looking at her hands like they belong to someone else.*

**If a PC engages:**
- She'll show them. *"It happened two weeks ago. Nobody knows what to do with me. Aldric says it gets easier. I don't believe him."*
- If asked her name: *"Petra. I was born here. I've never been outside."*
- **If Norseman engages:** he sees what she is. He was her once. The plan offers no script — only the recognition.
- If a PC says it gets better, she asks: *"Have you ever burned someone by accident?"*

**She remembers who helps her.** Carries forward.

#### H. The Outlook — Sabel on the rim (5–8 min)

**Anchor:** [[Warden Sabel]]
**Hook:** tactical/security info. He's tired. Suspicious of strangers.

He won't call any PC by their first name yet. If they push for information:
- The Stitching is failing. Six tenders for an eight-tender ward.
- Patrols closer than they were last month. He's seen Sand-Rider tracks at the third waystation, a half-day's ride out.
- He's worried about an informant. Won't say more.

He'll thank them for getting back. He won't trust them yet. That's earned.

#### I. The Grey Flame shrine — the silent stranger (3–5 min)

The candle for **Melek the Grey** has burned for eleven years. Beneath it, a charcoal sketch.

A stranger is sitting on the bench across from the shrine. Cloaked, hood up. Watching the sketch. Hasn't moved in an hour. Won't speak if approached. Won't look up. Won't leave.

**Slow burn — nothing happens this session.** Plant the seed.

ALL of these:

## §5 — Beat 4: Revelation & Flicker + Combat (18–22 min)

### Trigger
Tajsh reads the journal and reaches the page where the three-ring symbol is sketched.

### Read-aloud anchor

> *Tajsh's hand stops on the page. He doesn't turn it. He reads it again. He reads it a third time. Then he closes the journal slowly and looks up at you, and for one full breath, he is not the man who hired you.*
>
> *"...where did Torvin draw this?"*

### Tajsh's monologue (delivered slowly — let it land)

> *"I have seen this twice in my life. The first time was when I was twenty-two, and I was standing in a Shadewalker archive in Ostmark, and a sealed crate came in, and the man who'd brought it would not put it down. He set it on a stone table, and he stepped back from it like it was a cobra. Everybody in the room was very still. Very careful.*
>
> *"The second time was seventeen years ago. Barren Hills. I was hired to seal a chamber in an old ruin. The contract was specific. Carve this mark on the door. Carve it deep. Walk away. Don't look back. They paid me four times the going rate, and I still wish they hadn't.*
>
> *"This — what Torvin drew — this is the same mark. And the thing your crew dug up in that pit is what was in the chamber. And I need you to understand what that means before I ask anything more of you."*

### Tajsh DEFERS the ask

He does **not** give the party an objective here. The ask comes after the Architect, reframed in Session 3 as a "two sources" pitch.

> *(Polishes thumb-ring. Sets the journal down very carefully.)*
>
> *"I need a night with this. Eat. Rest. I'll send for you when I know what I'm asking. The Band stays in this room — in this wool — until I do."*

If pressed: *"I have a guess. I don't want to commit to the guess until I've sent a message and slept on it. Don't make me commit yet."*

**Why deferred:** Tajsh's pitch lands harder *after* the Architect speaks, not before. Two sources is a Shadewalker discipline. Asking for verification on the heels of a divine encounter has different weight than asking for an errand before one.

### The Stitching drops — one heartbeat

> *Tajsh has just finished speaking. The lantern on his desk goes out. Then it comes back on, exactly as it was. The candle at the Grey Flame shrine outside, visible through the window — same. Out, then back.*
>
> *Outside, somewhere up at the Outlook, Sabel is shouting.*

**Perception ◆◆** for any PC who wants to act: *something just looked at the canyon from outside, and now it knows where you are.*

(Mechanically: this is the moment Legio Null gets a fix on Lux Æterna. The players don't need to know that yet. Pay it off in a future session.)

### Combat: Scar-touched intruder

A *thing* slipped through during the heartbeat the wards dropped. It comes through a wall, a floor, a shadow that wasn't there before.

**Battlefield:** Tajsh's office (cramped — engaged range to all on entry) OR the alley outside if the Sandbox handed off there.

#### Stat block — Scar-touched Wraithling
*(tuned-down Scorch-Wraith — single rival enemy)*

```
Type: Rival
Wounds: 8 | Strain: — | Soak: 2 | Defense: 0/0

Brawn 3 | Agility 3 | Intellect 1 | Cunning 2 | Willpower 3 | Presence 1

Skills: Brawl 2, Perception 2, Vigilance 1

Equipment / Attack:
  Sync-Slash (Brawl) — Damage 5; Crit 4; Engaged; Burn 1

Abilities:
  • Out-of-Sync — Once per turn, when targeted by an attack, the
    Wraithling resolves a beat late. Add ◆ to the incoming attack
    for the rest of the round. (Lasts 1 round per use.)
  • Phase-Step — Maneuver: move from Engaged to Short range through
    a wall, floor, or shadow. Ignores difficult terrain.
  • Wards-Bound — at the START of round 3 (or when its Wounds
    reach 0, whichever first), the wards re-anchor and it dissolves
    into ash and ozone. The fight ends.
```

**Design intent:** quick, punchy, foreshadows what slips through Scars. Not lethal. Don't TPK; the wards close.

### After combat

> *Whatever it was, it isn't there anymore. The lantern on Tajsh's desk is steady. Sabel's shouting has stopped. Somewhere outside, a child is crying — briefly, then quiet.*
>
> *A knock at the door. A boy from the Warrens. Out of breath.*
>
> *"Cira sent me. She says — she says the Children's Tunnel is open, and it won't wait."*

### Exit
The party heads to the Warrens, to the Deep Well, to the Children's Tunnel. The Architect is waiting.

### Time guardrail
If past **95 min** total session time, **truncate the combat to 1 round of skirmish + the wards close**. Architect time is sacred.

## §6 — Beat 5: The Architect (30–40 min) — CLIMAX

### The descent (read-aloud)

> *The Children's Tunnel is behind the Deep Well, and it is named because once it was small enough for only children to crawl through. Now it is big enough for you, but only just.*
>
> *Cira is at the mouth, palms up, eyes white. She does not speak. She steps aside.*
>
> *You go down. The tunnel turns. The air gets colder. The torchlight hesitates — not flickers, hesitates, as though it is uncertain whether to keep being light.*
>
> *And then there is a threshold. None of you decided to cross it. You are across it.*

### The chamber

> *There is no door behind you. There is no floor — but you are not falling. The torch in your hand has frozen, the flame held mid-flicker, a small orange knife stopped in air.*
>
> *Around you, geometry. Rings of light, intersecting at angles your eyes refuse to follow, rotating in planes that do not exist in the world you came from. At the center, a convergence — a point of attention.*
>
> *Then a voice. Calm. Warm. The kind of voice that tells you the door is unlocked because it has been waiting for you to arrive.*
>
> ***"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."***

### The Naming

Address the PCs **in this order** (skip Vorn's if absent — see options below).

#### Vorn (absent — see options)

If present:
> *"Son of the stone-line. There is a word your grandmother would not speak twice. You have carried it for a long time, thinking you were the only one. You were not lost in the desert. You were called. The hand that came for you came because mine could not."*

**If absent — pick one:**
- **(a) Foreshadow option:** *"...and the stone-line will know me when he comes."* — said quietly, like the Architect is finishing a thought. The other PCs hear it. None of them know who it means.
- **(b) Skip option:** the Architect's gaze passes the empty seat without comment. The lever stays in your pocket.

**Recommended: (a).** Plants the seed.

#### Valeth

> *"Child of the long silence. You walked closer to truth than the others ever will. You doubted whether to come back. You came back anyway. That — that is why you heard me."*

#### Yldroth

> *"Child of the Aen'tir line. Your fists remember grief, and they remember release, and you have learned to do both at once. What you strike, you also mend. Most do not learn that in three lifetimes."*

#### Norseman

> *"Son of the cold north. You carry magic in a world that burns it, and you have made your peace with being the sword and the hand that wields it both. The Purge does not stop because you wish it to. It stops when someone decides it will."*

#### Arden — TWO candidate lines, GM picks at the table

**Line A (recommended — "soft watcher"):**
> *(the voice softens — only Arden feels the shift, the others hear something cryptic) "Son of cold flame and careful skin. You have learned to be softer than you are, and you have learned it well. I will not ask you to be otherwise. Not tonight."*

**Line B ("seen and unsaid"):**
> *(a pause that is too long; the geometry around Arden dims by one degree) "You have practiced not being seen. That is a useful skill. I see you anyway. I will not say what I see. Not here. Not yet."*

**Either line withholds the Drakona reveal.** Both unsettle Arden. The other PCs hear something cryptic and intimate. The lever stays in your pocket for a future session.

### The message

After the Naming, the Architect speaks plainly:

> *"The thing you carry — the Band — is one fragment of an instrument. Three fragments. Two more remain.*
>
> *"I do not know exactly where the others are. I know they exist. I know they call to each other when they are near.*
>
> *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left here to watch, and to wait, and to guide whoever came when the Band was found. Tonight, that is you.*
>
> *"Go north. The Dragon's Tooth. At its peak stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the heavens as you read letters on a page. Fragments of the kind you carry disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark.*
>
> *"When you have all three, bring them to the wound in the world. They must be unmade together, or not at all."*

### The gift — Resonance Compass

> *In your hand — you do not know how it got there — there is a palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal. It is warm. It is humming, very faintly, in the same key as the geometry around you.*
>
> *"The Compass will sing when you near a fragment. It will guide you. When it sings strongly enough, I will be able to speak to you again."*

See [[Resonance Compass]] for full mechanics.

### Truth audit / cracks-in-the-armor

**Available to any PC who actively probes.** Don't volunteer — make them roll.

| PC intent | Skill / Difficulty | Crack revealed |
|---|---|---|
| Notice something visually wrong | **Perception ◆◆** | The Architect casts no reflection in the floating crystal symbols. |
| Read its emotional state | **Perception ◆◆◆** | When it says "unmade," its tone shifts toward *anticipation*, not relief. **Triumph:** that anticipation reads as *hunger*. |
| Sense its magical signature | **Knowledge Arcana ◆◆◆** | Divine on the surface. *Abyssal* underneath. Perfume over rot. |
| Read the floating symbols | **Knowledge Lore ◆◆◆◆** | The geometry is compositional notation — music for frequencies no mortal throat can produce. The three-ring mark is a **chord**. |
| Resist the warmth of the Naming | **Discipline ◆◆** | Recognize that the Naming is too tailored. This is skilled targeting, not mentorship. |
| Coax extra detail | **Charm or Negotiation ◆◆◆** | The Architect mentions, almost in passing — *"There is a man at the observatory. Brenn Hallowveil. He has spent his life reading what no one else looks at. He will know more than I can say from here."* |

**Truth-audit cheat sheet for the GM:**
| Architect's claim | Truth |
|---|---|
| Band is a fragment of a world-altering instrument | TRUE |
| Two more fragments exist | TRUE |
| It does not know exactly where they are | PARTIAL (knows approximately) |
| It is a servant of the Four | **LIE** |
| Fragments must be unmade at the wound | **LIE** (will be *joined*) |
| Go north to the observatory | TRUE (the lens really does see fragments) |

### Exit

> *The geometry releases you. The torch in your hand resumes its flicker — a single uninterrupted flame, as though it had never stopped.*
>
> *You are at the mouth of the Children's Tunnel again. Cira is gone from her mat. Above ground, the canyon is dark — deep middle of the night. The Compass is warm in someone's pocket.*

## §6.5 — Beat 6: Scene 5 Epilogue (3 min) — Tajsh, Waiting

> *No checks. No questions answered. This hooks the next session.*

Tajsh is on the steps of his Vault, lantern in hand. He has not slept. Sabel is on the rim above. The market is empty.

He looks at them. Looks at the Compass. Looks back at them.

> *"In the morning, I'm asking you for something. Not tonight. Tonight you sleep. But in the morning, I'm asking — and I want you to come hear me out before you decide whether the god of light gets the next word."*

He doesn't elaborate. **If pressed:** *"It's about a book Torvin saw. And a symbol. And whether the only voice you trust tonight is the one that knew your names before you walked in the room. That's all I'm saying tonight. Get some sleep."*

**End the session on this hook.** The Two-Sources Pitch and Branching Choice play out in **Session 3 opening** — see `full-campaign.md` *The Morning After: Tajsh's Two-Sources Pitch* and *The Branching Choice*.
