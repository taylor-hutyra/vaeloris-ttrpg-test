# Scene 1: The Ride Back

> **Budget:** 8–10 min · **Cast:** all PCs · **Location:** scrub desert between Mining Camp and Lux Æterna · **No combat.**

## Goal

A breather and a bridge. New-party-dynamics start to form. Set up the wrongness that will be waiting at Lux Æterna by giving Valeth a quiet moment to register that the song has gone silent.

## Setup — Read-Aloud (montage)

> *The mining camp falls behind you. The wind picks up after sundown — fine grit against the lips, the camels' steady plod, the wrapped Band weighing in someone's pack heavier than its size should allow. Valeth, the song you followed for four days is gone; the desert is just the desert again.*

### Burial Flavor — Default: BURIED

> *"You left them under stone-cairns at dusk. Vorn rode quiet for an hour after, hands tight on the reins. The camels move easier than they did. The Band weighs heavier."*

### Burial Flavor — IF NOT BURIED

> *"You left them where they lay. By the time you crested the first dune, the buzzards had already stopped circling — they'd settled in. Nobody looks back. Norseman stops glancing east after the second hour."*

## The Narrative Dice Menu

Offer these to the table. **Players take none, one, or several.** Each is one roll, ~1–2 min, and produces a small concrete payoff. Do **not** force any of them.

### Opportunity 1 — The Lothari Trade-Cairn

> *"Off the road, half-buried in the lee of a rock outcropping, a small pile of pale stones — an old Lothari cairn. Easy to ride past."*

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Perception ◆◆** | A small cache: a sealed water-skin (1 day's water for the party) and a wrapped bundle of dried meat (1 day's rations for the party). |
| **Triumph** | Also a small pouch of **10 Lumens** tucked in oilcloth at the bottom. |
| **Failure** | They notice the cairn but find nothing useful in it. |
| **Despair** | The cairn collapses when disturbed. A camel spooks; ride on, no payoff. |

### Opportunity 2 — Recognize the Cairn's Clan-Mark (chains off Opportunity 1)

> *"There's a small mark scratched into the side of one stone — a six-pointed star with a curve through the center."*

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Knowledge (Lore) ◆◆◆** | This stretch was a **Lothari Silent Network relay route** a generation ago. The cairns were drop-points for letters and supplies passed along by dream-mystics. The PC may ask one follow-up question of the GM about Lothari history — answered honestly. |
| **Triumph** | The PC can read three additional marks: this cairn was active 38 years ago, abandoned during the Mage-Purge Wars. Worth knowing. |

### Opportunity 3 — Read the Dunes

> *"The wind has shifted the dune-shapes since you came this way. There's a route through the high gully you didn't take on the way out — might cut time."*

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Survival ◆◆** | Shave ~30 minutes off the ride; arrive in better light; **◼ to the next overland Survival check** in this region. |
| **Triumph** | Also: spot a Sand-Rider's old camp site below the ridge — extinguished a week ago, judging by the ash. Patrols closer than a month back. (Foreshadow.) |
| **Failure** | The shortcut is harder than expected. No bonus, no penalty — just a longer ride. |
| **Despair** | They get briefly lost. Add ~30 min to the ride. Tighten the time budget. |

### Opportunity 4 — Resist the Band's Whisper

> *"The Band is in your pack, wrapped in wool. As the night gets colder you can almost feel it — not the weight, the pressure. A pulse just below thought. Like something humming inside your skull, asking to be looked at."*

**Offer this to whichever PC is carrying the Band, OR any PC who voluntarily reaches for it.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Discipline ◆◆** | The pressure quiets. The Band is just an object in a pack again. |
| **Triumph** | The PC briefly *understands* the pressure: the Band is not malicious. It is *waiting.* (No mechanical effect; flavor for later when the Architect speaks.) |
| **Failure** | 1 strain. The PC feels the temporal field flicker — a flame at the next campfire will lag for a heartbeat. |
| **Despair** | 2 strain. The PC dreams of a circular room with a missing center-point. They will recognize the Deep Chamber when they see it. (Don't tell them yet.) |

### Opportunity 5 — Mage PCs: Sense the Wider Weave

> *"You've felt it the whole ride — a thread of magic on the western horizon. The Stitching, you realize. The aversion field around Lux Æterna. From this far out it feels like a chord, steady and complicated."*

**Offer to Norseman, Valeth, or Arden.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| **Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆** | The chord is *pulsing wrong.* Not failing. Not yet. But stuttering in a way the PC has never sensed before. (Foreshadow Lux Æterna's troubles.) |
| **Triumph** | They can briefly hear the whole pattern. They feel a second harmonic threading through it — something *underneath* the canyon, much older, that has woken up. Don't elaborate. (This is the Architect.) |
| **Failure** | They feel the chord but can't read it. Frustration. 1 strain. |

## The Personal Opening (NOT scripted)

After 1–2 dice opportunities, deliver this once and let it lie:

> *"The camels are walking close enough that any of you can ride alongside any other for a quiet word."*

That's the whole prompt. **Do not direct any PC toward another.** If they take the opening, run with it. If they don't, push to Arrival.

## Exit Condition

Canyon mouth in sight at dusk. **Cut to Beat 2.**

## Time Guardrail

If past **15 min total session time**, push to Arrival immediately. Drop unused dice menu options.

# Scene 2: Arrival

> **Budget:** 5–7 min · **Cast:** all PCs · **Location:** Slot Canyon Approach → Veiled Market · **No combat. No checks (unless players push).**

## Goal

Drop the party into the city. Recap what they know. Open the Sandbox.

## Read-Aloud — Arrival

*Full script: [Read-Alouds-Lux-Aeterna.md](Read-Alouds-Lux-Aeterna.md). Lands in ~1.5–2 minutes at a careful read. After the final line, **stop** — let the silence hang.*

> *You round the second dogleg, and the slot opens, and Lux Æterna takes shape inside the cliffs.*
>
> *Walls of sandstone climb sixty feet and lean inward at the top, leaving a thin slash of bruised purple sky. The cliff faces are honeycombed — dwellings carved at two and three levels, doorways glowing amber with lantern-light, ladders and rope-and-plank walkways stitching them together. Smoke from cookfires threads up through natural chimney cracks and dissipates long before the rim. Below, the canyon floor: salvaged-stone cobbles, the Veiled Market lit at its edges by oil lamps shaded down to amber pinpricks. Far above, two ward-tenders roped to the rock face, chalk-lamps swinging at their hips.*
>
> *The smells hit all at once — cookfire smoke, goat stew, the mineral tang of well-water, cactus-root ale, the sweet ghost of incense from the Grey Flame. Your shoulders drop without asking. The camels slow without being told. You're inside the wards. You're home.*
>
> *Except.*
>
> *The market is too quiet. Nobody is arguing — and in Lux Æterna, somebody is always arguing. A low sustained hum carries from the mouth of the Warrens, pitched lower than a human throat should comfortably hold, and it has been going for a while.*
>
> *The lanterns aren't keeping rhythm. One steady. Two steady. All four flicker in unison. The whole left wall pulses once as a single organism, then settles.*
>
> *A working dog crosses your path. Stops. Looks back the way you came in. Backs up two steps. Then turns, and runs. Out. Outside. **Out.***
>
> *And the water in the basin near the gate, when one of you stoops to splash your face — tastes faintly of iron.*

### Valeth Beat (NOT optional — hand to Valeth's player)

After the read-aloud lands, deliver privately to Valeth:

> *"And the song you followed for four days — the one that went silent the moment you left the foothills — is here. Faint. Underneath the hum. As if someone is playing it back from inside the rock."*

> *"Valeth — you stop your camel. You listen. You look at the others. What do you say?"*

Let the player deliver. If they freeze, prompt: *"That's the song. It's here too. It's been here the whole time."*

## NPC Recap (read whichever apply)

Match what the party already knows from Session 1:

- **Tajsh Thale** — your employer. Relic broker. Office above his Vault on the market. Sharp, warm, never stops moving.
- **Marta Dustborn** — runs the **Dusty Lantern**. Tavern keeper, original settler. Keeps a register of every guest. The shovel behind the bar is called "the policy."
- **Hask** — alchemist at the **Crystal Counter**. Orkin. One eye, scarred, quiet. Tests every draught on himself.
- **Old Cira** — fortune-teller at her woven mat in the market. Lothari, blind, age uncertain. Her riddles come true two days late.
- **Warden Sabel** — head of defense. Patrols the Outlook above the canyon rim.

## Brief History (2–3 sentences)

> *"Lux Æterna is a refugee settlement of about four hundred souls — mages, deserters, anyone the Decree of Prohibition wants dead. Hidden by the Stitching, a glyph-ward that makes outsiders' eyes slide off the canyon. Eleven years standing. Holding its breath."*

## What Players Sense Without Rolling

- The market is quieter than it should be.
- Lanterns flicker out of rhythm.
- Well water tastes metallic.
- A low hum drifts from the Warrens.
- **Cira is sitting outside the Vault** humming a single sustained low note.
- **Selin is up a ladder** retouching a wavering glyph.
- Three of four working dogs refuse to come inside.

## Optional — One Roll Available

If a player asks specifically what's wrong:

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Read the wrongness | Perception ◆◆ | The party can feel the temporal field of the Band, faintly — that's what's making the lanterns and water wrong. Whoever carries the Band feels it strongest. |

## Cira's Line (only if Valeth or Arden passes near)

**Valeth:** Cira opens her eyes, looks up at him, says softly: *"Two of you are walking. Only one came back."* Returns to humming. **Do not explain.**

**Arden:** Cira looks at him. With *politeness* — not recognition. Says nothing aloud. *(Pass Arden's player a private note: "She sees you. She knows. She is choosing not to say so. Out loud, to anyone.")*

## Exit Condition

Drop them at the Veiled Market. **Open the Sandbox.**

## Time Guardrail

If past **22 min total session time**, you've spent too long here. Cut to Sandbox even mid-description.

# Scene 3: The Lux Æterna Sandbox

> **Budget:** 35–45 min · **Cast:** all PCs + Sandbox NPCs · **Location:** Veiled Market and adjacent

## Format

The city is open. **Players choose.** Each activity is self-contained, 5–10 min, with a clear in/out.

## Convergence Rule

Advance to the **Revelation** (Scene 4) when **BOTH** are true:
- (a) Tajsh has had the journal in his hands for at least one full activity.
- (b) The table has had ~30 minutes of city play.

Whichever comes later. Until then, keep the city open.

## Activities At-The-Ready

Full prep on each — the user can't pre-pick, so they're all ready. **Player files reference NPCs §1–11 and Locations §3–10 for fuller content.**

### A. Tajsh's Vault — Deliver Band & Journal

**Time:** 8–12 min · **Anchor:** Tajsh Thale (NPCs §1) · **Location:** Tajsh's Vault (Locations §4)

The handoff. **Triggers Revelation when Tajsh reads the journal.** You can stretch the moment if the table needs more city time first — *"Let me sit with this. Get yourselves fed. I'll send for you."*

**Tajsh's first reaction (read aloud):**
> *"Set it on the desk. Don't unwrap it. Not yet. Sit. Tell me everything from the moment you saw the pit."*

**He examines the bundle.** Unwraps a corner. Sees the dark crystal and pale metal. Doesn't touch it.

> *(the candle on his desk stops flickering — the flame is still lit but not moving — Tajsh watches it for a long second, picks it up, carries it across the room, sets it on a shelf near the door)* *"Better."*

If they hand over the journal in the same conversation: skip ahead — Tajsh reads it. Read the Revelation when ready (Scene 4).

If they hold the journal: Tajsh asks for it explicitly: *"What else did Torvin leave behind? You said there was a journal. I need it. Tonight."*

**Tajsh writes Valeth's and Vorn's names in his ledger.** *"Next of kin?"* (If Vorn is absent: he writes the name and asks if there's anyone Vorn would want notified. The party answers as best they can.)

#### Available Rolls at the Vault

| Intent | Check | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| Read what Tajsh isn't saying | Perception (Cunning) ◆◆◆ | He recognized the symbol the moment it came in. He's already deciding what to tell you and what to hold. |
| Spot the back-room door's wards | Knowledge (Arcana) ◆◆◆ | The lock is mundane. The frame is bound by recent glyph-work — Selin's hand. |
| Surreptitiously read the upstairs ledger | Skulduggery ◆◆◆◆ | Hard. He's watching. **Triumph:** glimpse a recent entry — *"Ink — write next caravan. Urgent."* |

#### If the Party Refuses to Hand Over the Band

Tajsh doesn't push. *"Suit yourselves. Sleep with it. See how that goes."* The Band's effects worsen overnight (cold spots, the candle won't relight, Cira's humming gets louder). Cira summons them anyway.

# Scene 4: The Revelation & Flicker (+ Combat)

## Read-Aloud — The Trigger

## The Monologue (deliver slowly — pause on the pauses)

> *"I have seen this twice in my life. The first time was when I was twenty-two, and I was standing in a Shadewalker archive in Ostmark, and a sealed crate came in, and the man who'd brought it would not put it down. >
> *"This — what Torvin drew — this is the same mark. And the thing your crew dug up in that pit is what was in the chamber. And I need you to understand what that means before I ask anything more of you."*

### Tajsh DEFERS the ask

He does **not** give them the next arc objective here. That ask comes after the Architect, reframed as a "two sources" verification — see *Scene 5 Epilogue* and the Session 3 hook. For now:

> *(He polishes his thumb-ring without realizing. Sets the journal down very carefully, as if it might break.)*

**Why deferred:** Tajsh's pitch lands harder *after* the Architect speaks, not before. The party has just been shown a being of light that knows their names. Tajsh — the human voice, the Shadewalker instinct — comes next. *Two sources is a Shadewalker discipline.* Asking for verification on the heels of a divine encounter is a different thing than asking for an errand before one.

### Battlefield

|   3   |    3    |     1     |    2    |     2     |     1    |

### Tactical Notes for the GM

- **Round 1:** It Phase-Steps to the desk and Sync-Slashes whoever is between it and the Band (or the journal). It will attack the Band-carrier preferentially.
- **Round 2:** If wounded, it Phase-Steps away to Short range (through a wall) and re-engages a different target. Uses Out-of-Sync the moment a heavy hitter targets it.
- **Round 3:** **Wards-Bound dissolves it at the start of the round, regardless of Wounds.** Even if the PCs haven't dropped it, the wards close and it's gone. **No TPK risk.** Don't telegraph this — let the moment land.
- **If a PC uses cold:** Out-of-Sync is disabled for 1 round + extra damage. Norseman with a cold spell, Valeth with aether-quartz channeled cold, anyone with a Frost-Touched weapon — give them the moment.

### Quick-Reference Combat Rules

- **Initiative:** Cool or Vigilance vs. Cool or Vigilance, opposed.
- **Engaged → Short:** one maneuver to disengage (Athletics ◆◆ if pursued).
- **Maneuver examples:** move one range band, draw weapon, aim, recover.
- **Action examples:** attack, cast magic, skill check.
- **Strain costs:** spending a second maneuver in a turn = 2 strain.

### Damage & Soak

Damage − Soak = Wounds dealt. **Burn 1** = 1 wound bleed at start of next turn unless the PC takes a maneuver to extinguish.

# Scene 5: The Architect — Climax

> **Budget:** 30–40 min · **Cast:** all PCs, the Architect, Cira (bookending) · **Location:** Deep Chamber (pocket-space) · **MUST HIT — protect this time.**

**Phase structure:** Entry → Naming → Message → Questions → Exit.

## Phase 1 — Entry (5 min)

### Descent — Read-Aloud

> *The tunnel narrows as you go. The walls start to feel older than the rest of the settlement — because they are. Lux Æterna was built over someone else's ruin. You've always known that. You just haven't had a reason to care, until tonight.*
>
> *The air cools. The torch flame flickers slower. Slower. Then it stops flickering entirely. It is still burning. It just isn't moving.*
>
> *You reach the chamber. Round, empty, stone. There's nothing here. You've walked to the end of the tunnel system.*
>
> *And then the last of you crosses the threshold.*

### The Transition — Read-Aloud (slow; pauses are doing work)

> *The stone behind you is no longer there. Not dark. Not hidden. Not THERE. You don't turn around to check. You know, the way you know where your own hand is, that if you turned around there would be nothing to turn toward.*
>
> *Your torch is still. The flame has not gone out. It is simply holding its shape, like someone pressed pause on it.*
>
> *The air is warm and has no smell. Your breathing is slower than it should be. You aren't running out of air. You aren't getting any, either.*
>
> *Around you — not close, not far, distance isn't the right word here — rings of geometric light rotate in planes that don't intersect the way your eyes expect them to. Symbols you don't know the names of drift past and through each other. Some of them are versions of the three-ring mark. Some of them are things that mark could grow into if you let it keep going.*
>
> *There is no door. There is no floor, but you are not falling. You are, in every sense that matters, standing somewhere that is not a place.*
>
> *And at the center of all of it — not a figure, exactly. A CENTER. A point where the geometry converges and something is looking back.*

**Pause. Let the silence sit. Let a player be the one who speaks first.** When ready, deliver, quiet:

> ***"I have been waiting a long time to meet you."***

### Pocket-Space Properties (GM Reference)

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside. Players will not notice.
- **No door.** They wait to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons do nothing. **Discipline ◆◆** to recognize. Failure: they try anyway, feel foolish.
- **Echoless voices.** Like speaking inside a glass sphere.

## Phase 2 — The Naming (7–10 min)

The Architect addresses each PC by name. **Deliver slowly. This is relief, not threat.** Every word is tuned to lower their defenses. Let it land.

> *(quiet, like finishing a thought)* *"...and the stone-line will know me when he comes."*

You were not lost in the dark. You were CALLED there. The hand that reached for you was sent because I could not send my own. What you survived, you survived so that we could stand here now. Nothing is wasted. Not even grief."*

You saw something that made you doubt whether you should come back. You came back. That choice is the one I needed. That is why you could hear me from across the veil. **For Arden's player:** unsettling. The Architect has SEEN him and is choosing DISCRETION. Whether protective or manipulative is the player's question.

## Phase 3 — The Message (5 min)

Run as continuous dialogue, not a list:

1. *"The object you carry — what you call the Band — is a fragment of an instrument. Once whole, it was used to reshape the world. It nearly ended. The fragments were scattered to prevent that ending. Two more remain."*
2. *"I do not know where they are. Fifteen hundred years of watching, and even I cannot see everything. The fragments were hidden by gods. They are hidden from me too."*
3. *"I am a servant of the Four. I was left behind to watch. To wait. To speak when there was finally someone to speak to."*
4. *"I have prepared something for you."*

> *A plinth of folded light rises in front of the party. On it, revealed: a palm-sized circle of dark crystal wrapped in pale metal, slotted in the center. It hums. Valeth hears the note he has been following — except now it is in harmony with itself, and before it was alone.*

5. *"This is a Compass. It will sing when a fragment is near. But it cannot tell you where to begin. You must first learn where to look. Go north. The Dragon's Tooth pierces the sky. At its peak, there stands an observatory built in the age of gods. Its instruments read the stars as mortals read letters. Fragments disturb the heavens. Any lens trained on the sky will see their mark."*
6. *"Once all three are gathered, they must be brought to a wound in the world, and there they will be unmade. Only there. Nowhere else. That is the task."*

The plinth of light remains. The Compass is on it. **A PC may take it at any time — it will not resist.**

> *One of you answers, "A few minutes."*

> *She says, "It has been three hours. I could not see you. I was not sure you were coming back. How do you feel?"*

## Phase 6 — Scene 5 Epilogue: Tajsh, Waiting (~3 min)

> **Budget:** 3 minutes. **No checks. No questions answered. Hook the next session.**

As the party climbs back out of the Warrens, Tajsh is on the steps of his Vault, lantern in hand. He has not slept. Sabel is on the rim above. The market is empty. Cira is gone.

Tajsh looks at them. Looks at the Compass. Looks back at them.

> *"In the morning, I'm asking you for something. Not tonight. Tonight you sleep. But in the morning, I'm asking — and I want you to come hear me out before you decide whether the god of light gets the next word."*

He doesn't elaborate. He doesn't push. He waits to see if anyone says anything.

**If a player asks what:** *"In the morning. Sleep first. Please."*

**If a player presses harder:** *"It's about a book Torvin saw. And a symbol. And whether the only voice you trust tonight is the one that knew your names before you walked in the room. That's all I'm saying tonight. Get some sleep."*

**If they refuse to wait:** he holds the line. *"Morning. I'm not negotiating this one. Be in the Vault at first light, or don't, and we'll see where we are."*

**The hook is set.** The Two-Sources Pitch and the Branching Choice play out in **Session 3 opening** — see the campaign master document, *The Morning After: Tajsh's Two-Sources Pitch* and *The Branching Choice.*

## Closing the Session

**Ask each player, in character, ONE sentence only:** *"How do you feel?"*

Go around the table. Each player, one sentence. **No discussion.** When the last has spoken:

> *"That's where we leave it for tonight. Good game, everyone."*
