# The Ulzammar Band

*First Age artifact. One of three Crown fragments. The Time piece.*

## Appearance

A circlet of **dark crystal, shot through with pale metallic veins like lightning frozen mid-strike.** Cool to the touch. Weighs almost nothing. Eyes want to slide off it. Looking at it feels like looking at something you are not supposed to see.

## Properties

### Passive — Short Range

Within Short range of the Band:
- Dropped objects fall roughly 1.5× slower than they should.
- Open flames flicker in slow motion.
- Heartbeats seem to lag half a beat behind breath.
- A character's own perception of time becomes unreliable — a five-minute conversation feels like fifteen.

### Passive — Long Range

Within Long range:
- Enchantments behave unpredictably. Aversion-glyphs waver at their edges. Ward-patterns stutter.
- Alchemy reactions delay, accelerate, or fail.
- Groundwater near the Band tastes metallic.
- Dogs refuse to come indoors.

### Pull

If left behind, the Band reappears in the nearest PC's pack by morning. It wants to be carried.

## Touching It — First Contact

First time a PC handles the Band: **Discipline (Willpower), Average ◆◆.**

| Result | Outcome |
|---|---|
| Success | Feels it as external. *Absolute certainty. Gears meshing. A single coherent thought: "Incomplete."* Can set it down. |
| Failure | Thought feels like their own. Wants to keep holding it. Second ◆◆ to voluntarily put it down. |
| Triumph | Success + glimpse of three interlocking crystal shapes for a heartbeat. |
| Despair | Failure + Staggered until end of next turn. |

## GM-Only — What It Actually Is

The Band is the **Time** piece of the Crown of the Ulzammar, and one of three fragments of [[The Architect]]'s own essence. Its temporal field is a spillover of its function — it was designed to *slow the entropy of everything in its field,* an instrument of preservation that can also be used as a weapon.

Slows aging of anything within its field (not known to the party in Session 1).

The three-ring symbol on the pedestal in the ruin is a **chord** — not a rune — and the Band sings one of the three notes.
