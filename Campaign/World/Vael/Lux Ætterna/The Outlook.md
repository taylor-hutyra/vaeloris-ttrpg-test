# The Outlook

**Theme Park Role:** Faction Hub (military authority)
**The One Thing:** The people maintaining this perimeter haven't slept properly in months, and they are the only thing standing between 400 lives and the Legio Null.

---

## Establishing Shot

The Outlook is not a place you go to; it's a place you earn access to. Warden Sabel's domain covers the canyon rim, the surrounding hills, and the three concealed observation posts positioned to give 270-degree coverage of the approach routes. From up here the canyon below looks like a wound in the rock — narrow, deep, and from the right angle you can see the faint amber glow of lantern-light from the carved dwellings far below. Beautiful and fragile.

The observation posts are cramped stone shelters barely large enough for two people, camouflaged with desert scrub and sand-colored canvas. Inside each one: a slit window facing the approach, a signal kit (colored glass panels for the flash system), water, two days' rations, and a blanket. Up on the canyon walls, you can see the ward-tenders at work — two mages at a time, roped to the cliff face, tracing the glyph-lines of the Stitching with chalk and blood and quiet concentration. Four-hour rotations, three shifts a day. The glyphs need constant refreshing; the desert wind and sun erode the chalk, and the mana that powers them bleeds away faster than anyone expected when the first wards were carved eleven years ago. The ward-tenders look like what they are: exhausted people doing critical work in discomfort, because nobody else can.

Sabel himself tends to be at whatever position needs him most. He doesn't have an office. He has patrol routes.

## How the Veil-Wards Work

The Stitching is not an illusion. It doesn't make the canyon invisible or project false images. It works on **perception and attention**.

The glyphs carved into the canyon walls radiate a subtle field that affects the minds of anyone who doesn't already know the settlement is here. The effect is layered:

- **At distance (1+ miles):** The canyon registers as unremarkable. Eyes slide over it. Scouts filing aerial reports don't mention it — not because they can't see it, but because their minds categorize it as "another dry wash, nothing interesting" and move on.
- **At approach (500 feet–1 mile):** Growing unease. A gut feeling that this is a bad route, that water won't be found this way, that the terrain ahead is more trouble than it's worth. Most people turn around here without consciously deciding to.
- **At close range (under 500 feet):** Active disorientation. Difficulty remembering why you came this direction. A low-grade headache. The feeling of being watched. Trained Legio Null scouts might push through this, which is why Sabel also maintains mundane camouflage and physical observation.

The wards don't work on people who already know the settlement's location. Once you've been inside and left, the aversion doesn't affect you anymore — which is why every person who leaves Lux Ætterna is a potential security risk, and why Sabel tracks every departure personally.

**Failure mode:** The wards don't drop all at once like a wall falling. They thin. The outer ring weakens first — scouts start noticing the canyon, filing it as "possible water source, worth checking." Then the middle ring — patrols start approaching. By the time the inner ring fails, someone is standing at the canyon mouth wondering why they feel so uneasy. The whole degradation process from "fully warded" to "discovered" takes about two weeks once the tenders stop refreshing the glyphs. Selin estimates the current erosion rate gives them six months before the outer ring can't be maintained at current staffing levels.

## Key NPCs

**[[Warden Sabel]]** — See [Campaign/People/Warden Sabel.md](../../../People/Warden%20Sabel.md) for full file.
Human male, late 30s, burn scar on his left forearm. Former Varnathi Legion sergeant who deserted rather than execute a family of Spark-touched farmers. Haunted by guilt, drinks too much, fiercely protective of every person under his roof — which is everyone in the canyon. Communicates affection through logistics. Calls no one by their first name until he trusts them. You know you've earned it when he does.

**The Ward-Tenders (named by the GM as needed)**
Six mages currently in rotation: three regular shifts. None are combat-specialized. They are:
- **Vera** (human, 30s) — the most experienced; can maintain the glyphs longest without Strain; has started coughing from the chalk dust and mana-burn.
- **Ral** (young Orkin male, 20s) — nervous, capable, hero-worships Sabel; would die for this settlement and knows it.
- **Tevris** (elderly Lothari, uses a staff) — slower rotation, longer endurance; Old Cira knew him before. Won't say from where.
- Three others: interchangeable for now, named when the players interact with them.

**Lothari Scout Relay (external)**
The Outlook communicates with a Lothari waystation network via the Signal Rock flash system. The scouts don't come to Lux Ætterna — they relay signals along a chain that eventually reaches Elder Soren. Current status: one relay station has gone dark.

## Social Network

The Outlook runs on **chain of command and loyalty**, which in Lux Ætterna means Sabel's personal authority rather than any formal military structure.

- Sabel's word is final in matters of external security. He and Tajsh cooperate by mutual necessity and genuine respect, occasionally fighting about resources.
- The ward-tenders report to both Sabel (scheduling, safety) and Selin (technique, glyph design). They are civilians, not soldiers, and Sabel treats them accordingly: with the care you give irreplaceable people.
- The Bolt Hole's full route is known only to Sabel and Tajsh. In an emergency, Sabel leads. Tajsh ensures the evacuation supplies are stocked.

**What players can learn here (Investigation or approaching Sabel directly):**
- The patrol schedule and where the Legio Null currently isn't
- The status of the Lothari signal relay (one station dark)
- The boot prints (Tension Hook #1) — if players earn Sabel's trust, he tells them before anyone else
- The Bolt Hole's existence — Sabel tells players this during the pre-adventure bonding if they take guard duty downtime
- How the wards work and their current degradation rate — Selin shares this if asked directly, more readily than Sabel would like

## Tension

**The Staffing Crisis:** The ward-tending rotation needs eight mages to run safely. There are six. Two of the six are showing signs of Strain accumulation — Vera's chalk-dust cough is getting worse, Ral is sleeping only four hours between shifts. If one mage breaks, they drop to five. If two break, the outer glyph ring starts to thin and scouts will begin noticing the canyon. This is not a future problem. It is the current problem.

**The Dark Relay:** One of the Lothari signal relay stations hasn't responded in six days. It could mean the station was abandoned, it could mean the scouts moved for safety, it could mean something found the station. Sabel is keeping this quiet. He cannot afford to send anyone to investigate without reducing the patrol perimeter.

**The Boot Prints:** Two canyons east, fresh prints in the sand near a known rock formation. Not from any patrol route Sabel has charted. Adult-sized, single person, not in a hurry. The prints were there one morning and gone by evening — swept. Someone knew they'd left a trace and came back to fix it.
