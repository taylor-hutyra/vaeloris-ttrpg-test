# The Warrens

**Theme Park Role:** Safe Zone (residential) with an Interest Node growing beneath it
**The One Thing:** 400 people live here because they have nowhere else, and they have built something real from that fact — which means losing it would be unbearable.

---

## Establishing Shot

Past the market the canyon narrows and the cave openings begin. The Warrens is not a neighborhood in any conventional sense — it's a maze of natural chambers connected by passages that the settlement's residents have widened, smoothed, and claimed over eleven years. Each chamber has a curtain or a door, usually both. Some chambers hold a single family. Some hold three. The children who grew up here know the tunnel routes the way city children know streets.

The smell here is different from the market: less smoke, more humanity. Cooking, unwashed laundry, the mineral cold of deep stone, and underneath it all the faint constant smell of the underground spring in the Sick-Bay. Sound carries strangely — you can hear a conversation two chambers away clearly and the chamber next door not at all.

The Warrens is where people sleep, grieve, argue about family things, and remember who they were before they became refugees. This is the part of Lux Ætterna that isn't performing survival. It's just living it.

## Key NPCs

**[[Aldric Greypen]]** — See [Campaign/People/Aldric Greypen.md](../../../People/Aldric%20Greypen.md) for full file.
Human, elderly, kind, terrible memory for names but perfect recall for spell theory. Studied under a student of Melek the Grey himself. Has the original demonstration notes memorized before the Decree's bonfires destroyed the copies. Doesn't talk about this. Teaches control before power, always, because he watched what happened when someone didn't.

**Brother Oram** (inline)
Human male, mid-40s, heavyset, soft-spoken. Former priest of the Ordained who lost his faith but kept every skill he was given. Runs the Sick-Bay with quiet competence — organizes supplies, trains volunteers, sits with the dying when there's nothing medical left to do. He doesn't minister. He just stays. People find this more comforting than the prayers ever were.
- **Want:** To be useful in a way that doesn't require him to believe anything.
- **Secret:** He still prays, privately, to nothing in particular. He thinks of it as talking to himself. He's not sure that's a meaningful distinction.

**The Deep Well Guard (d4 rotation)**
Sabel assigns two-person watches in four-hour shifts. The specific guard changes daily. This matters when players want to access the well quietly or when the Children's Tunnel becomes a problem.

## Social Network

The Warrens runs on **mutual favor and shared memory**, not authority.

- Disputes are settled by the people involved, usually with Marta as reluctant mediator if someone needs a neutral party.
- Aldric is the closest thing The Warrens has to a leader, in that people listen when he speaks. He has never asked for this. He manages it by pretending not to notice.
- Brother Oram is the second. The Sick-Bay is neutral ground — Sabel's authority does not extend inside it. This has only mattered twice, and both times Oram prevailed by simply not moving.
- Information that emerges in The Warrens: who is sick, who is sleeping badly, who is having nightmares, who is losing control of their magic, who has been crying. This is sensitive intelligence. Oram and Aldric both hold it tightly.

**What players can learn here (Investigation):**
- Who has been having bad dreams (relevant once the Band is in the settlement)
- Whether a specific NPC has been acting strangely (the stranger from Tension Hook #6 has been seen in the tunnels after midnight)
- The location and rough description of the Children's Tunnel

## Tension

**The Fire Scar:** A mage named Darro (human, 19, terrified of himself) accidentally blasted a two-chamber section of The Warrens during a nightmare two weeks ago. No one was hurt. He hasn't told Sabel. Three families displaced into the market. Darro has stopped sleeping rather than risk another incident. If players find him, he looks like death. If they help him, they earn a deeply loyal contact — and Sabel will thank them, not punish Darro, if they handle the report well.

**The Children's Tunnel:** Rima, Joss, and Kell (ages 9, 10, and 8 respectively) found the crack behind the Deep Well about a month ago. They've been exploring in shifts, bringing back small crystals that glow faintly with a light that isn't Mana-Crystal resonance. They have not told any adults. The tunnel smells like the Scar of Dissonance. They don't know what that means. They're having the time of their lives.

|----|-----------|
