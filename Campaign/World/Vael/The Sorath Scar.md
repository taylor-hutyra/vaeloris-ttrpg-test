# The Sorath Scar

*The specific [[Scars of Dissonance|Scar of Dissonance]] in the desert foothills east of [[Lux Æterna]]. Birthplace of this campaign's opening crisis.*

## Location

Beneath the low, dark hill that contains the **First Age ruin of Sorath**, a mile east of the [[Mining Camp]]. Three days' ride from [[Lux Æterna]] along the standard Lothari desert routes. The Lothari avoid this area — their dream-network flagged it centuries ago. The Silent Network has been quietly rerouting caravans around it for the last year.

## What It Is

A crack in reality left by Ulzammar harmonic work at Sorath in the First Age. When the Ulzammar scattered the Crown — burying the [[Ulzammar Band|Band]] in the stone here and fleeing — they did not fully close the wound their work opened. The ruin sits above. The Scar sits below. They are two faces of one injury.

For most of history, the Scar has been **slow** — widening by a handspan a century. Lesser Scar-spawn drift out occasionally. A Lothari caravan or a prospector vanishes every few decades. The Silent Network watches.

**The Band's excavation has accelerated this.** Since [[Torvin Dast|Torvin]]'s crew started cutting the aether-quartz seam, the Scar has been *breathing.* [[Old Cira]] feels it. [[Warden Sabel]] feels it but cannot name it. The [[Ulzammar Band|Band]] is not just near the Scar — it is *tethered* to it. What was buried is resonating with the wound it helped create.

## Physical Manifestation

From the outside, the Scar is not visible. It exists beneath the surface — a crack in the membrane of reality, not in the physical rock. A traveler could walk the foothills for a week and never see it.

Its signs are indirect:

- Aether-quartz seams nearby behave unusually — richer, warmer, occasionally humming.
- Compasses and mana-detection fail intermittently.
- Lantern flames near the thinnest points flicker out of rhythm.
- The air tastes faintly metallic.
- Lothari report bad dreams in the foothills.

## Thinned Seams (How Things Get In and Out)

The Scar has several places where the membrane is especially thin. [[Vorn]] found one on Day 12 of the expedition — his pick, striking the black crystalline stone beneath the aether-quartz vein, broke through to the Scar side. He fell in. The seam was physical from his end and immaterial from the Scar's end — which is why the hole in the stone was only waist-deep when his crew checked it.

[[Valeth]], on the Scar side, found this same thinned seam three days later. He had been wandering the connected Scar-network for years (his lost crew entered a different Scar; Scars are all one membrane). He was drawn west by the Band's song. When Vorn fell through, Valeth was close enough to hear him. He reached him, sang a protective field, and when they were surrounded, sang a new three-note pattern that **tore the seam open from the inside.** They fell out into the [[Mining Camp]].

The seam sealed behind them. It cannot be reopened by ordinary means. Whether [[Valeth]] can re-open it — or open a different one — is an open question.

## What Lives in the Scar

- **Lesser fiends** — nameless, small, numerous. They recoil from tonal dissonance (Valeth's Anchor).
- **[[Scorch-Wraiths]]** — drift out to feed on residual mana near the Sorath ruin above. The three the party killed in Chapter 1 were the most recent batch.
- **The Anchoring Fiend** — the greater entity that holds the Scar open from within. Valeth's original crew was tasked with finding and killing one of these (possibly not this one). Valeth has never seen the Anchoring Fiend here. He may not survive meeting it.

## GM Notes

- **The Band is the beacon.** Removing it from the area should eventually cause the Scar to narrow again. Bringing it closer accelerates the widening. The party does not know this yet.
- **Later Acts:** If the party learns to close Scars (via the Song-Ward tradition, or by returning [[Valeth]]'s Anchor-derived knowledge), this Scar is the one they will most plausibly be asked to close. Closing it is a possible Act III alternative to the Architect's plan.
- **Lothari dream-tradition:** [[Old Cira]] can provide intel on the Sorath Scar specifically if asked. She's been watching it since SA:1487.
