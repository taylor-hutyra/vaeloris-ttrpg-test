# The Dusty Lantern

*[[Lux Æterna]]'s only tavern. [[Marta Dustborn]]'s place. Warm light, thick stew, no questions asked.*

## Establishing Shot

The Lantern is a single long room cut into the east wall of the slot canyon, with a patched canvas awning covering the outdoor seating. Inside: a long bar along the far wall, six wooden tables of mismatched sizes, a fire-pit at the back that serves as both cooking hearth and heat source, and dozens of **lanterns** hanging from iron hooks in the rough stone ceiling. Hence the name.

The lanterns are the feature — some are oil, some are Mana-Crystal, some are candles in glass jars. On a normal evening, they flicker warmly in sync. Tonight, the night of [[01-scene-arrival|Chapter 2 Scene 1]], they are out of rhythm.

## Who's Here

**[[Marta Dustborn]]** — owner and cook. Human woman, 40s. Burn scars on her arms she does not hide. Warm and dry at the same time.

**The regulars** — [[Warden Sabel|Sabel]]'s off-duty militia, a handful of Lothari traders passing through, Hask on the nights he remembers to eat, and a small rotating cast of residents who came here to be alone in company.

## The Lantern's Role in Session 1

See [[02-scene-the-evening|Chapter 2 Scene 2]] — the Lantern is one of the vignette locations. Marta's *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why"* line lives here.

**[[Valeth]] in the Lantern:** Marta takes one long look at him, then serves him the same bowl she serves everyone else. She does not ask where he came from. She has been where he has been, in her own way.
