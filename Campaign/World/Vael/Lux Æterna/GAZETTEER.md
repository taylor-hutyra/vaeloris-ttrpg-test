# Lux Æterna — A GM's Gazetteer

> Everything you need to run the canyon without re-reading the story documents. Personalities, locations, prices, voices, stat blocks. Pulled clean of any "tonight specifically" hooks — those live in session guides.

**Currency:** Lumens (1 Spark = 1 Lumen, retained for legacy notes).
**Population:** ~400. Majority human; ~30 Halfling Wayfinders rotating through; ~20 Lothari mystics resident; a handful of Gnomes; a few Orkin laborers; one or two Thulkorûn dwarves.
**Founded:** SA:1479.
**Concealment:** the *Stitching* — a glyph-ward aversion field. See §3.

## 1. The City at a Glance

| | |
|--|--|
| **Type** | Hidden refugee settlement |
| **Location** | Box canyon, foothills between the Lothari Desert and Varnathi Highlands |
| **Population** | ~400 |
| **Governance** | Informal. Tajsh holds the economy; Sabel holds security. No formal council. |
| **Economy** | Relic salvage, Mana-Crystal trade, enchanted goods |
| **Prices** | 150% on common goods; 200% on magical. **Tomm at Iron Shelf is the only exception — standard prices, no markup.** |
| **Currency** | Lumens (Mana-Crystal chips); 1 Lumen ≈ 1 silver |
| **Languages** | Common (majority), Lothari (trade/mystic), Dwarven (merchants), Halfling, scraps of a dozen others |
| **External Threat** | Legio Null (Commander Thalric, Desert Division); Sand-Rider patrols |
| **Internal Threat** | Stitching degradation; Sorath Scar growing 1/4 mile east |

**The Name.** *Lux Æterna* — "Eternal Light." Given in defiance, not in description. The canyon is dark; the people make the light. Eleven years standing.

**The Joke Nobody Finds Funny.** The light is what the people make. Lanterns. Cookfires. Candles. The Grey Flame. The dark is the rock; the light is the people.

## 2. History

### Pre-Settlement (?? – SA:1479)

The canyon was empty desert. The **Deep Chamber** beneath was already there — a First Age ruin predating everything above it. The founders never knew.

### Founding (SA:1479)

A handful of mage-refugees fleeing the third decade of the Mage-Purge Wars discovered the canyon. They carved the earliest Veil-Wards into the rock by blood, chalk, and an old book none of them had a complete copy of.

### Expansion (SA:1480 – SA:1488)

- **SA:1480** — Marta Dustborn arrives, establishes the Dusty Lantern.
- **SA:1481** — Selin Voss arrives, formalizes the Stitching glyph system.
- **SA:1483** — Tajsh Thale arrives. Establishes the Vault and the Veiled Market. Transforms camp into functioning settlement.
- **SA:1484** — Hask arrives from the Vernath Orkin Quarter purges. Establishes the Crystal Counter.
- **SA:1485** — The Dusty Lantern is fully operational; becomes the social heart.
- **SA:1486** — Warden Sabel arrives, deserts the Varnathi Legion. Organizes the defensive perimeter.
- **SA:1487** — Old Cira "wanders in." Claims the sand told her to stop walking. (Silent Network operative; this is cover.)
- **SA:1488** — Population reaches ~400, the canyon's practical capacity.

### Current Era (TA:412 onward)

Active settlement. Stitching stuttering. Legio Null has narrowed its search to within twenty miles. Internal cohesion holding by mutual debt and shared memory.

## 3. How It Survives — The Stitching

Not an illusion. Works on **perception and attention.**

| Distance | Effect on outsider |
|----------|-------------------|
| 1+ mile | Canyon registers as unremarkable. Eyes slide past. |
| 500 ft–1 mi | Growing unease. Gut feeling: "bad route." |
| Under 500 ft | Active disorientation. Forgetting why one came. Low-grade headache. Sense of being watched. |

**Critical rule:** the wards do **not** affect anyone who has been inside before. Once in, always findable.

**Failure mode (gradual):** outer ring weakens first (scouts file the canyon as a "possible water source"), middle ring weakens (patrols start approaching), inner ring fails (someone stands at the canyon mouth wondering why they feel uneasy). Full degradation cycle once tenders stop refreshing: ~2 weeks.

**Maintenance:** six ward-tenders for an eight-tender weave (chronic understaffing). Four-hour rotations, three shifts a day. Chalk and enchanted ink.

## 4. Power Structure & Social Rules

### Authority (informal)

- **Tajsh** — economy, information, external contacts
- **Sabel** — security, ward-tending schedule, patrols
- **Aldric** — closest thing to a moral authority in the Warrens (he doesn't claim this)
- **Brother Oram** — second; the Sick-Bay is neutral ground
- **Old Cira** — no formal authority, considerable influence

### Social Rules

- The market runs on **reputation and debt**, not law.
- Information flows: rumors → Marta; relics → Tajsh; medical → Hask; wards → Selin; dreams → Cira; security → Sabel.
- The Lantern's one rule: **no faction business inside.** Enforced by Marta and the policy (a short-handled shovel; used twice in eleven years).
- The Sick-Bay is **neutral ground.** Sabel's authority does not extend inside.
- The phrase nobody asks: *"Where were you before?"*

### Cultural Notes

- New arrivals light the Grey Flame candle when it gutters.
- Children are quick, quiet, and unfrightened — they grew up here.
- Mages are the majority but the word "mage" is rarely used aloud.
- Dogs are trusted. Cats are trusted more.

## 5. Districts

| District | Role | Vibe |
|----------|------|------|
| **Veiled Market** | Commercial heart, social hub | Warm, loud, chaotic; the settlement's lung |
| **The Warrens** | Residential cave network | Labyrinthine, private, cramped, human |
| **The Outlook** | Defensive perimeter | Canyon rim, exhausted mages, controlled fear |
| **Deep Chamber** | Pre-settlement First Age ruin | Empty stone room; only reached via the Children's Tunnel |

### Geography

- Canyon runs north–south. ~300 ft long, 80–120 ft wide (widest at center), 60–80 ft tall walls leaning inward at the top.
- Entrance: a narrow ~10 ft crack at the south end, doglegging east then north. From outside, looks like any other dry wash.
- North end (deepest, narrowest): cliff face holds the cave openings into the Warrens.
- East rim has the Signal Rock; south rim has the Warden's Post and the Bolt Hole.

## 6. Locations

> Each entry: what it is, who runs it, what they sell or do, things a PC might learn here. **Sterile of session-specific tension.**

### 6.1 — Tajsh's Vault

**Where:** Veiled Market, wedged between a weaver's stall and a Lothari spice-merchant.
**Who:** Tajsh Thale, runner Korel.
**Frontage:** Heavy wooden door, iron banding, no signage. Brass bell wired to ring inside on entry.

**Front room:** catalogued First Age artifacts, prospecting maps, Tajsh's working inventory. Not for sale unless he says so. Long workbench, rolled Lothari rug pinned with notes, kettle on a brazier.

**Back room (locked):** **Items Tajsh shouldn't have but does.** Three sealed Shadewalker briefing cylinders, a Wandering Kin charter signed in blood, two First Age glyph-rubbings.

**Upstairs office:** desk, two chairs (only two), kettle, ledger. The window faces the Outlook — Tajsh can see Sabel from his chair.

**Goods (rare; only if asked):**
- Pry bars / chisels — 20 Lumens
- Glyph-rubbing kit — 30 Lumens
- Lothari travel maps — 40–80 Lumens

### 6.2 — The Dusty Lantern

**Where:** East wall of the slot canyon, patched canvas awning over outdoor seating.
**Who:** Marta Dustborn.
**Layout:** Single long room. Long bar on the far wall. Six tables, mismatched. Fire-pit at the back (cooking + heat). Dozens of lanterns on iron hooks. Three rooms upstairs for travelers.

**Menu:**

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Stew + bread | 2 Lumens |
| Flatbread + oil | 1 Lumen |
| House wine (bad; she's honest) | 2 Lumens |
| Dwarven dark ale (smuggled) | 5 Lumens |
| Room upstairs | 10 Lumens/night |
| A place to sit and say nothing | free (leave the chair clean) |
| Bone-Dice wager | variable |

**The Register:** small leather book behind the bar. Every name that has ever stayed. Date of arrival. Date of departure. Second date if known. **Empty lines are not crossed out.**

**Who's usually there:** off-duty militia (3–4), Lothari traders passing through, Hask if he forgets to eat, a small rotating cast of residents who came to be alone in company.

### 6.3 — The Crystal Counter (Hask's Apothecary)

**Where:** North side of the market, carved into the cliff face, three steps up.
**Who:** Hask.
**Frontage:** Heavy curtain instead of a door.
**Inside:** Low-ceilinged stone room, mana-crystal lamps in iron sconces. Floor-to-ceiling shelves of glass jars, ceramic vials, dried plants in linen bundles, labelled stoppered bottles. Air smells of dried herbs, distilled alcohol, and something sharp underneath. Copper still simmering at a back workbench.

**Barter:** aether-quartz dust = ~30 Lumens credit per thumb-sized shard.

### 6.4 — Cira's Corner

**Where:** Far north end of the market, under a jutting rock shelf, near the path up to the Warrens.
**Who:** Old Cira.
**What's there:** Faded woven mat. Two flat stones. Clay jar of olives (always replenished, somehow). A small dream-catcher of woven wire and bone, pinned above the mat with one iron nail. Not decorative — functional, in a way she has never explained.

**Hidden:** beneath the mat, arranged on the rock, seventeen olive pits in a Silent Network sigil. Knowledge (Lore) ◆◆◆◆ to recognize. **Do not volunteer.**

**Services:** readings. Payment is informal — a piece of dried fruit, a shawl when cold, sometimes nothing. She speaks in riddles that turn out, two days late, to be exactly correct.

### 6.5 — Iron Shelf

**Where:** Veiled Market. Half cliff-side dwelling, half open-air stall.
**Who:** Tomm Broadback.
**Layout:** Forge fire in a stone niche carved into the cliff, vented through a chimney crack so cleverly no smoke shows from the rim. Small anvil. Quenching trough. Wooden rack of finished work — knives, axe-heads, tools, three swords (only one good; he'll tell you which). Shelves and barrels of rope, lantern oil, dried rations, waterskins, prospecting kit, coiled climbing line.

### 6.6 — Needle & Thread (Selin's Shop)

**Where:** South side of the market, set deeper into the cliff face than most.
**Who:** Selin Voss.
**Layout:** Narrow, low ceiling, no windows. Three sewing tables, two with half-mended cloaks on dummies. A wall of spools — every color of thread the Lothari caravans bring. Workbench at back buried under chalk dust, iron-nibbed gravers, clay pots of enchanted ink, rolled vellum sheets covered in glyph diagrams.

**Inventory:**

**Service:** ward maintenance, glyph repair, arcane consultation. Often on a 20-ft ladder against the canyon wall retouching glyphs by chalk-lamp.

### 6.7 — The Grey Flame Shrine

**Where:** South end of the Veiled Market, near the path back to the slot.
**What:** A small niche carved at chest height in the canyon wall. Inside: a single candle behind smoky glass, burning with a thin steady flame. Above it, pinned with two iron nails, a charcoal sketch of a gaunt man with wild eyes — **Melek the Grey,** drawn from a description passed down through three generations.

**Significance:** Melek demonstrated the First Revelation (controlled mage-craft) in SA:1401. Died six years later, his mind burned through. The Decree of Prohibition outlawed his name and ordered every record of him burned.

**The shrine is defiance, not worship.** New arrivals light the candle when it gutters. It has not gone out in eleven years.

**Bench across from the niche:** for sitting. Has, on occasion, hosted visitors who sat there for hours without moving.

### 6.8 — The Schoolroom (Aldric's Domain)

**Where:** Largest natural chamber in the Warrens — roughly 30 ft across, high domed ceiling.
**Who:** Aldric Greypen.
**Inside:** Walls lined with shelves; shelves with books — not many, but well-cared-for. Long stone bench along one wall for students. Smaller table at the front for Aldric. Wooden practice circle in the center, marked in chalk, for mages working on Spark control.

**The library (~80 volumes):** Lothari grammar, basic mathematics, a children's compilation of stories from before the Decree, two technical manuals on metallurgy, one battered copy of pre-Decree mage-craft notes (incomplete; the rest was burned).

**The practice circle:** three concentric rings, broken in specific patterns. Aldric carved it from the memorized template Melek the Grey used for his First Revelation demonstrations.

**Aldric will lend:** the Lothari primer or the metallurgy manual. He will NOT lend the pre-Decree mage-craft notes.

### 6.9 — The Sick-Bay (Brother Oram's Grotto)

**Where:** Small natural grotto on the western side of the Warrens.
**Who:** Brother Oram.
**Inside:** Cold underground spring pools in a stone basin at the back, fed by a slow trickle from the rock above. Four pallets on raised stone platforms. Small workbench with bandages, dried herbs, clean basins. Three wooden stools. Air is colder here than anywhere else.

**Lothari blessing:** small carved sigil at the water's edge. Barely visible. The water is meant to settle the mind of a drinker.

**Services:** healing without charge. Spending ~30 min here, Brother Oram rolls Medicine ◆◆ to heal 2 Wounds beyond natural recovery. *"You can leave Lumens for the next person if you want. Not for me."*

**Neutral ground.** Sabel's authority does not extend inside. This has only mattered twice.

### 6.10 — The Deep Well

**Where:** Lowest point of the Warrens. Roughly circular chamber, 15 ft across.
**What:** Wide stone shaft sunk into the floor, dropping 20 ft to the dark water of the underground aquifer. Wooden frame supports a rope-and-bucket draw system. Walls smoothed by hands and chiseling. Eleven years of fetching water has worn paths in the rock.

**Guards:** Two on watch at all times. Sabel's people.

**Lothari blessing stone:** small flat stone at the chamber threshold with a carved sigil. Travelers sometimes touch it before drinking.

**The Children's Tunnel:** behind the well on the chamber's north wall. A crack in the rock, recently widened by smaller hands. Smells faintly of ozone. Leads to the Deep Chamber.

### 6.11 — The Outlook

**Where:** The canyon rim. Three observation posts.
**Who:** Warden Sabel, ward-tenders (rotation: Vera, Ral, Tevris, three others).
**Posts:** Cramped stone shelters, camouflaged with desert scrub and sand-colored canvas. Each has a slit window facing the approach, a signal kit (colored glass panels), water, two days' rations, a blanket.

**The Stitching:** hundreds of carved glyphs along the upper walls, weathered to look ancient. Six tenders for an eight-tender weave.

**The Signal Rock:** highest accessible point on the eastern rim. Colored-glass flash station relays with Lothari waystation scouts.

**The Bolt Hole:** concealed emergency exit. Entrance hidden behind a false rock face on the canyon's south side. Leads to a dry riverbed two miles south. Sabel calls the exit point **Lira's Run** — only he and Tajsh know the full route.

**Warden's Post:** rock outcropping at the canyon's south rim where Sabel conducts briefings. No shelter. (Apparently a feature.)

## 7. NPC Roster

> Each NPC: appearance, voice, what they know, what they share freely, what they withhold, sample lines, and a stat block where the GM might need one. **Stat blocks are Genesys-style.** Soak / Defense omitted for civilian NPCs who are unlikely to be in combat.

### 7.1 — Tajsh Thale ("The Broker")

> *Human male, mid-40s. Lean, weathered, brown skin worn dark by desert sun. Eyes that assess everything before the rest of his face catches up.*

**Where:** Tajsh's Vault. Office above the Vault.
**Role:** Employer, relic broker, de facto economic leader. Pragmatic skeptic counterweight to any divine voice the party hears.

**Voice:** Speaks fast. Finishes other people's sentences (sometimes incorrectly). Uses humor as armor. **Tell:** when nervous, polishes whatever he's holding — coin, cup, fragment, thumb-ring. Does not tell people he cares. Tells them the rations are better this week. Calls no one by their first name until late. He may, one day, use one.

**Shares freely:** general gossip, supply schedules, who's feuding, who's healthy. Stitching status. Lothari caravan timings.

**Under pressure / on revelation:** the three-ring symbol (two prior encounters — Ostmark Shadewalker archive at 22; Barren Hills sealed chamber at 27). Shadewalker contacts. The archivist **Ink.** That he maintains an emergency evacuation cache two miles south of the canyon stocked with supplies and route maps.

**Secret (GM only):** Skims 10% off every relic sale into the evacuation fund. Was kicked out of the Shadewalkers — took something they didn't want him to keep. Won't say what or where.

**Sample lines:**

> *"Stays here. In this room. In this wool. I don't want it on the road."*

> *"I have a guess. I don't want to commit to the guess until I've sent a message and slept on it. Don't make me commit yet."*

> *"You are not prisoners. You are not employees anymore, either. You are people carrying a problem through my home. Help me understand it, and I am your friend. Make me chase you for it, and I become very good at that."*

> *"I don't need you obedient. I need you honest enough that you don't accidentally kill everyone in this canyon."*

**Stat block:**

```
TAJSH THALE — Rival
Brawn 2 | Agility 2 | Intellect 4 | Cunning 4 | Willpower 3 | Presence 3
Wounds 14 | Soak 2 | Defense 0/0

Skills:
  Knowledge (Lore) 3, Knowledge (Education) 2, Negotiation 3,
  Vigilance 3, Cool 3, Streetwise 3, Deception 2, Perception 2,
  Skulduggery 2, Ranged (Light) 1

Equipment:
  Concealed hand-crossbow (Ranged Light; Damage 5; Crit 4; Pierce 1)
  Long-bladed knife (Melee; Damage 3; Crit 3)
  Thumb-ring (worth more than it looks; bribe leverage)

Talents / Notes:
  • Shadewalker Eye — Once per session, declare: "I have seen this
    before." Free Knowledge (Lore) check at ◆◆ to identify any
    First Age artifact, restricted symbol, or political figure.
  • Polish — Tell. If a PC is observing him and makes a
    Perception ◆◆ check, they read his nervousness or deception
    correctly.
  • Tajsh does not seek combat. If forced into a fight, he aims
    to escape and reach Sabel within two minutes.
```

**How to play him:** the most competent adult in any room. Be right about small things often, so when he's wrong about the big thing later, it lands harder. He is the party's reluctant father figure.

### 7.2 — Old Cira ("The Fortune-Teller")

> *Lothari female. Small, sun-bleached, wrapped in linen that was once white. Completely blind — milky-pale eyes that track sound rather than light. Age indeterminate.*

**Where:** Cira's Corner. Eats olives out of a clay jar.
**Role:** Truth-teller, riddler. Senses wrongness first. Trusted by neither Tajsh nor Sabel; listened to by both.

**Voice:** Speaks in riddles that turn out, two days later, to be exactly correct. Hums tunelessly. Doesn't explain after the fact. **Tell:** when she's serious, she goes still and her riddles stop.

**Shares freely:** Cryptic warnings. Riddles. Olives.

**What she actually is (GM only):** A ranking **Silent Network** operative placed in Lux Æterna 25 years ago to monitor the Sorath Scar. Communicates via Lothari dream-relay — three days out, three days back. Knows the Architect casts no shadow. Knows what Arden is and is choosing not to say so.

**Sample lines:**

> *"You walked in carrying weather. The canyon felt it."*

> *"Ask what heard you ask."*

> *"I cannot follow you where this goes. Listen carefully. Doubt generously. Come back."*

> *"It will not come to you if you stay up here. It will wait. It has waited fifteen hundred years."*

**Stat block:**

```
OLD CIRA — Rival
Brawn 1 | Agility 2 | Intellect 3 | Cunning 4 | Willpower 5 | Presence 4
Wounds 12 | Strain 14 | Soak 1 | Defense 0/0

Skills:
  Knowledge (Lore) 4, Perception 4, Vigilance 4, Coercion 1,
  Charm 2, Survival 3, Discipline 3

Equipment:
  Olives (jar; always replenished, somehow)
  Walking staff (Melee; Damage 3; Crit 4; Defensive 1)
  Dream-catcher of woven wire and bone

Talents / Notes:
  • Listener — Cira's blindness does not penalize her. She is
    immune to visual illusions; she "sees" via mana-currents.
    Once per session, declare she "heard" something a sighted
    character missed.
  • Dream-Riddle — Once per session, deliver a cryptic riddle to
    one PC. The riddle's truth becomes apparent 1d3 in-fiction
    days later. (GM authorial control.)
  • Cira will not fight. She will stand. She will not flinch.
```

**How to play her:** Use her **sparingly** so her appearances carry weight. She is not a tool. She is a person who has been watching this slide toward catastrophe for 25 years, alone, and has stopped expecting anyone to listen until they need to.

### 7.3 — Marta Dustborn ("The Lantern-Keeper")

> *Human female, late 40s. Stout, strong, the posture of someone who has been lifting things all her life and intends to keep doing it. Hair greying, worn practical. Burn scars on her arms she does not hide.*

**Where:** Behind the bar at the Dusty Lantern.
**Role:** Tavern-keeper, original settler, social glue. Keeps the register of every guest who has ever walked through her door.

**Voice:** Calls everyone "love" until they've earned a name. Names earned by coming back. Doesn't ask about your past, politics, or magic. Asks if you've eaten today. Sharp-tongued in the way of someone who loves people enough to be honest with them.

**Shares freely:** The settlement pulse. Who's feuding. Who's sleeping where. Who's sick. Rumors from the d8 table below.

**Holds back:** Her own past. The contents of the register. The names she's lost.

**Secret (GM only):** She loves this place more than she has told anyone. The register is how she shows it. If the settlement falls in Act I, she will turn back for the register at the worst possible moment.

**Sample lines:**

> *"You're back. Good. Sit. Stew first, gossip after."*

> *"You can sit with that, love. Just leave the chair clean."*

> *"They've been doing that for an hour. I'm not going to be the one who asks why."*

### 8.4 — Shopping Rule (table speed)

For standard gear, the party subtracts the coin and adds the item. No scene required. Reserve in-character shopping for:
- Anything magical or rare
- Any merchant who is also a plot hook
- Purchases that cost more than 50 Lumens

**Faction Discount:** PCs who complete a sidequest for a proprietor earn a **20% discount with that specific merchant permanently** — no haggling required.

### 8.6 — How to Decide What an NPC Knows

When in doubt:
- **Tajsh** knows the politics, the people, and the price of any object.
- **Marta** knows who's at the door, who's late, and who's hurt.
- **Hask** knows the medical truth of who's lying about their health.
- **Selin** knows the magic and only the magic.
- **Sabel** knows the perimeter, the patrols, and the routes.
- **Cira** knows what hasn't happened yet.
- **Aldric** knows the history and refuses to teach it.
- **Brother Oram** knows the wound and stays with it.
- **Tomm** knows the metal and the man who carried it.
