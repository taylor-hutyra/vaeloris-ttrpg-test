# Veiled Market

**Theme Park Role:** Safe Zone + Faction Hub
**The One Thing:** Every conversation here is also a transaction — information is currency as much as Mana-Crystals.

---

## Establishing Shot

The Veiled Market fills the canyon at its widest — roughly 120 feet across — where the cliffs step back and a broad natural overhang on the west wall shelters half the market floor. The sky is a white-hot slash far above, but down here the canyon walls cast deep permanent shade across the packed-earth floor. Stalls press against each other in no particular order: a spice seller wedged between an armorer and a fortune-teller, a bread-baker's smoke threading past a Lothari merchant's hanging carpets. The noise is constant and specific. You can hear at least four arguments happening simultaneously, none of them serious. The cobbles underfoot are salvaged stone and packed dirt, worn smooth by 400 people walking the same paths over eleven years.

The central well is the anchor — a wide stone ring worn to smoothness, perpetually surrounded by someone fetching water, someone sitting on its edge thinking, and at least one child using it as a dramatic stage. In the cliff faces, dwellings and shops at two levels glow amber with lantern-light from carved doorways. By full evening the canyon floor is lit by shaded oil lamps — kept low, angled inward, never bright enough to cast a glow visible from the rim — and it smells of cactus-root ale and goat stew from the Dusty Lantern.

This is the safest place in Lux Æterna. Not because nothing dangerous happens here — but because Marta Dustborn, Tajsh Thale, and 400 people all watching each other make it so.

## Key NPCs

**[[Tajsh Thale]]** — See [Campaign/People/Tajsh Thale.md](../../../People/Tajsh%20Thale.md) for full file.
The relic broker. Sharp, pragmatic, morally flexible. Runs the settlement's economy, maintains Shadewalker contacts, and privately funds an emergency evacuation cache nobody knows about. Speaks fast. Uses humor as a weapon and a shield. Has a tell when he's nervous: he polishes whatever he's holding.

**[[Marta Dustborn]]** — See [Campaign/People/Marta Dustborn.md](../../../People/Marta%20Dustborn.md) for full file.
Runs the Dusty Lantern. Stout, sharp-tongued, irreplaceable. She knows everyone's names, keeps track of everyone's moods, and enforces the Lantern's one rule — no faction business inside — with a combination of social pressure and the aforementioned shovel. The community's emotional center.

**[[Old Cira]]** — See [Campaign/People/Old Cira.md](../../../People/Old%20Cira.md) for full file.
Lothari dream-mystic. Blind. Indeterminate age. Speaks in riddles that are annoying until they're not. She is a ranking [[Silent Network]] operative who was placed here to monitor [[The Sorath Scar]] in the foothills. The first person in the settlement to sense something wrong about the Ulzammar Band — and later, about the Architect.

**[[Hask]]** — See [Campaign/People/Hask.md](../../../People/Hask.md) for full file.
Orkin alchemist. Scarred, one-eyed, privately furious at the world. Tests every draught on himself. Brews Scorpion-Sting Tinctures, Ash-Root Pastes, and Sand-Eye Drops from desert plants. Was a respected alchemist in Vernath's Orkin Quarter before the purges took it apart.

**[[Selin Voss]]** — See [Campaign/People/Selin Voss.md](../../../People/Selin%20Voss.md) for full file.
Gnomish seamstress and ward-scribe. Nervous, brilliant, chalk-dusted. She designed the interlocking glyph network that keeps the settlement hidden and is the only person who understands the full pattern. If she dies, the Stitching unravels within weeks. She knows this. It does not help with the nervousness.

**Tomm Broadback** (inline)
Halfling blacksmith. Big for his family, he says. Charges fair prices. Talks about metallurgy the way other people talk about religion. Not a plot hook — just a reliable, likeable presence who will quietly hand over a weapon when things go wrong.

## Social Network

The market is governed by **reputation and debt**, not authority.

- **Tajsh** holds economic leverage over almost every stall. He controls the relic-to-crystal exchange rate and the Lothari supply route contacts. Cross him and your supply dries up.
- **Marta** holds social leverage. She knows who's feuding, who's sleeping with whom, who owes a favor, who's about to break. Information flows to her tavern; she doesn't sell it, but she notices everything.
- **Old Cira** has no leverage and no desire for it. People come to her because she's been right too many times to ignore.
- **Hask** holds a quiet power nobody discusses: he's the only healer whose supplies work. Sabel makes sure Hask's supply runs are prioritized.

**Information flows:**
- **Rumors and settlement gossip** → Marta at the Lantern
- **Relic intelligence, outside contacts** → Tajsh at the Vault
- **Dream-warnings, unclassifiable wrongness** → Old Cira at her corner
- **Medical crises** → Hask, Crystal Counter (mornings) or Sick-Bay (emergencies)
- **Ward anomalies, Stitching degradation** → Selin at Needle & Thread

## Tension

**The Market's Running Sore:** Three stall-holders are quietly not paying their Mana-Crystal tithe to the informal communal fund that Tajsh administers. This is an open secret. Tajsh hasn't confronted them because two of them supply things the settlement needs, and doing so would force him to make a rule, which would require making more rules, which would require Lux Æterna to have a government. He is currently hoping it resolves itself.

**The Watcher:** The stranger who arrived two weeks ago and has spoken to no one has started sitting near the Grey Flame shrine each morning. They don't light the candle. They don't touch anything. They just sit and look at the face in the charcoal sketch. Marta has started leaving bread near them without comment. This has not answered any questions.
