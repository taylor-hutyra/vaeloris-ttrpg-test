# The Deep Chamber

*The oldest room in [[The Warrens|the Warrens]]. Pre-settlement. Possibly pre-Paroxysm. Nobody has had a reason to come here — until tonight.*

## How to Get There

From [[The Warrens]], past the Schoolroom, past the Sick-Bay, past the **Deep Well**, to the **Children's Tunnel** — the crack three settlement children recently widened enough to squeeze through. The children had been going quiet when asked about it. They had found the edge of this place. They did not go in.

Beyond the Children's Tunnel, a narrow passage descends into older stone. The walls become cut rather than natural. The cuts are First Age style — too-regular, geometric, precise beyond mortal patience.

## Physical Description (Before Anything Happens)

A roughly circular stone room, about twenty feet across. Cut stone walls. No exits other than the tunnel the party came down. Nothing else in the room. A faint circular indentation in the floor at the center — as if something once stood there and was removed a very long time ago.

The stone is cold. The air is still. Torches flicker less and less the deeper one descends. By the time a visitor reaches the chamber, the flame holds its shape like a held breath.

## What Actually Happens Here

**The chamber is *not* where [[The Architect]]'s scene happens.** The chamber is where the Architect's *influence* displaces ordinary reality and replaces it with a pocket-space. See [[05-scene-the-architect|Chapter 2 Scene 5]] for the full transition, but in summary:

- The moment the last PC crosses the chamber threshold, the space shifts.
- There is no door. No floor. No time. Their torch flame freezes mid-flicker.
- Rings of geometric light rotate in impossible planes around them.
- At the center: a point where geometry converges. The Architect.

### Pocket-Space Properties

- **No time.** 3 hours outside for every 5 minutes inside.
- **No exit.** Party waits to be released.
- **No weapons.** Drawn weapons don't affect anything.
- **Echoless voices.** Like speaking inside a glass sphere.

### After

When the Architect releases the party, they are standing in the empty stone chamber again. The torch is flickering normally. The tunnel is there. The [[Resonance Compass]] is in a PC's hand.

## GM Notes

- **Do not reveal the pocket-space exists before the moment the Architect creates it.** The chamber appears mundane on descent.
- **The pocket-space is not a fixed location.** It appeared because the Architect's attention was there. It will not appear again by returning — the chamber reverts to ordinary stone when the Architect withdraws.
- **The Children's Tunnel**, however, remains. Anyone can descend to the empty chamber from now on. It will be cold and still, and nothing will happen. [[Old Cira]] may visit it alone, rarely.
