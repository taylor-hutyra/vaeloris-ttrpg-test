# Lux Æterna

## Establishing Shot

You almost walked past it. That's the point.

The canyon entrance is a crack in the foothills — a narrow dogleg between two rock faces that looks like every other dry wash in this part of the desert. You'd have walked by it a hundred times and never thought to turn sideways and follow the shadows in. But you know the way, so you do, and the walls close to shoulder-width, and the light goes blue-grey, and then the dogleg opens and you stop.

It smells like cook-fires and alchemical reagents and sun-warmed stone and something faintly sweet, like incense burned down to ash. The canyon is deeper than it looked — the walls climb sixty feet and lean inward at the top, leaving a narrow strip of sky far above. Dwellings are carved into the cliff faces at two and three levels, connected by ladders and rope-and-plank walkways. The canyon floor is packed earth and salvaged stone, cluttered with market stalls, lean-tos, and carts — someone is arguing over the price of something in three languages. Children weave between the adults' legs.

Look up. The sky above is a hot white slash, but down here the canyon walls cast permanent shade across the floor. In the cliff faces, doorways glow amber with lantern-light from the rooms beyond. Smoke from cook-fires rises through natural chimney cracks in the rock that the settlers have widened with picks — you can see the thin threads of it dissipating long before they clear the rim. On the canyon walls, if you know where to look, you can see the glyphs: hundreds of small carved symbols, weathered to look ancient, running in interlocking patterns along the stone. The Stitching. The thing that keeps 400 people alive by making the world forget they're here.

A blind woman is reading someone's fortune in exchange for a piece of dried fruit. Somewhere a child is laughing. Somewhere above all of this, there are mages tracing glyph-lines with chalk and blood, refreshing the wards that make every Legio Null patrol fly past this canyon without a second glance — and the mages haven't slept enough in months.

## Tension Hooks

Pre-keyed incidents. Drop any of these when players need a reason to care.

**1. Fresh Prints**
Sabel found boot prints in the sand two canyons east. Not from any known patrol route. He's running the numbers on who might have left them and coming up short. He hasn't told Tajsh yet.

**2. The Late Supply Run**
Hask's medical resupply from the Lothari route is three weeks overdue. The usual caravan family stopped at a different waystation. A substitute seller is in the market now, asking twice the price and twice as many questions about where the settlement's other buyers are.

**3. The Scorched Rooms**
A mage in the Warrens lost control during a nightmare — a fire-blast scorched two chambers and singed the curtain partition. No one was hurt. No one has reported it to Sabel. Three families are now sleeping in the market.

**4. The Children's Tunnel**
Three kids found a natural passage behind the Deep Well leading deeper into the rock. They've been coming back smelling of ozone and going quiet when adults ask where they've been.

**5. The Symbol at the Bolt Hole**
Someone scratched a Legio Null identification mark — the gear-and-chain symbol — into the stone near the concealed emergency exit. It could be a warning from outside. It could be a threat from inside. Sabel is not sleeping.

**6. The Stranger Who Stayed Too Long**
A refugee arrived two weeks ago and has asked no questions, taken no work, and spoken to no one. In Lux Æterna, that's not caution — that's wrong. Marta has been watching them eat alone every night.

## Shopping

**The Rule:** For standard gear, players subtract the coin and add the item. No scene required. Reserve in-character shopping for:
- Anything magical or rare
- Any merchant who is also a plot hook
- Purchases that cost more than 50 Sparks

**Price Modifier:** ×1.5 standard for all goods (supply chain). ×2.0 for anything magical. Hask's Desert Remedies priced as listed in [[The Crystal Counter|Veiled Market]].

**Faction Discount:** Players who complete a sidequest for a proprietor earn a 20% discount with that specific merchant permanently — no haggling required.

## Rumor Table (d10)

Roll once per NPC the party befriends in the market. GM knows which are true.

| d10 | Rumor | Truth |
|-----|-------|-------|
| 1 | "Tajsh used to be a full Shadewalker archivist before he came here." | **True** — he left on bad terms but voluntarily |
| 2 | "Old Cira's been to this canyon before. Years before anyone else arrived." | **True** — she was monitoring the Scar for the Silent Network |
| 3 | "The Stitching is fraying faster than Selin tells us." | **True** — she's managing panic, not the repair timeline |
| 4 | "There's a Legio Null informant somewhere inside the settlement." | **Half-true** — there's no active informant, but one refugee accepted money in exchange for route information three years ago and has been terrified ever since |
| 5 | "The abandoned mine east of the canyon has enough Mana-Crystals to buy the whole settlement out of trouble." | **Half-true** — there ARE more crystals, but they're growing around something that shouldn't be disturbed |
| 6 | "Sabel used to outrank Commander Thalric before he deserted." | **Half-true** — different branches; they were equivalent rank, not superior/subordinate |
| 7 | "Tajsh sends reports on the settlement to the Azure Hand in exchange for the supply route." | **False** — he skims 10% off every sale into an evacuation fund that he's never told anyone about |
| 8 | "The settlement was founded by a mage who survived the original purges of SA:1425." | **False** — founded by a mixed group of refugees with no special history |
| 9 | "Old Cira can see the future perfectly. She just speaks in riddles to avoid responsibility." | **False** — she sees fragments, not futures, and she doesn't fully understand what she sees |
| 10 | "There's a second tunnel network under the Warrens that Sabel uses for his own purposes." | **False** — but the children's tunnel (Tension Hook #4) has made this rumor spread |
