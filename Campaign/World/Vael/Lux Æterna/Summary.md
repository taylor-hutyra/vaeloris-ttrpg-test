# Lux Æterna — Settlement Summary

> *"Eternal Light" — a name given in defiance, not description. The canyon is dark. The people in it refuse to be.*

---

## Overview

Lux Æterna is a hidden refugee settlement of approximately 400 souls concealed in a deep box canyon in the desert foothills between the [[Lothari Desert]] and the [[Varnathi Highlands]]. Founded in SA:1479, eleven years before the campaign begins, it shelters persecuted mages, deserters, refugees, and the desperate — anyone the [[Decree of Prohibition]] has marked for death.

The settlement survives through three overlapping systems: **geography** (a narrow, naturally concealed canyon with a dogleg entrance), **discipline** (strict light management, smoke venting through natural chimney cracks, no external signage), and **magic** (the Veil-Wards — a network of carved glyphs called "the Stitching" that radiates an aversion field, causing anyone approaching without prior knowledge to feel uneasy and look elsewhere).

---

## History

**SA:1479 — Founded.** A group of mage-refugees fleeing the [[Mage-Purge Wars]] discover the canyon while searching for water. The natural dogleg entrance and deep walls make it invisible from common approach routes. The first Veil-Ward glyphs are carved by three mages whose names are no longer remembered. The earliest camp is tents on the canyon floor and a single shared cook-fire.

**SA:1480–1482 — Survival years.** The camp grows slowly — word passes through the refugee networks, the [[Azure Hand]] begins routing passage, Lothari caravans start including the canyon on their supply circuits. Dwellings are carved into the cliff faces, first at ground level, then higher. The Stitching expands to cover the full canyon perimeter. Population reaches approximately 150.

**SA:1483 — Tajsh Thale arrives.** A former [[Shadewalkers|Shadewalker]] associate with relic-trade expertise, [[Tajsh Thale]] recognizes the economic potential of the surrounding First Age ruins. He establishes the [[Veiled Market]], creates the Mana-Crystal exchange system, and connects the settlement to Lothari trade intermediaries. Within two years, the camp becomes a functional settlement with a working economy. Population reaches 250.

**SA:1486 — Warden Sabel arrives.** [[Warden Sabel]] deserts the [[Varnathi Legion]] rather than execute a family of Spark-touched farmers. He reaches Lux Æterna through the underground refugee pipeline and immediately begins organizing defenses: observation posts on the canyon rim, the signal relay to Lothari waystation scouts, the concealed emergency exit he calls "Lira's Run." His militia of twenty volunteers becomes the settlement's first organized security.

**SA:1487 — Old Cira arrives.** An elderly [[Lothari]] dream-mystic wanders into the settlement and says "the sand told me to stop walking here." [[Old Cira]] sets up a mat in the market and begins reading fortunes. Unknown to anyone in the settlement, she is a ranking [[Silent Network]] operative placed to monitor [[The Sorath Scar]] in the nearby foothills.

**SA:1488–1489 — Growth and strain.** Population reaches 400 — the canyon's practical capacity. The Stitching now requires constant refreshing by rotating shifts of ward-tenders, and the mages who do this work are increasingly exhausted. The outer glyph ring shows signs of erosion. [[Selin Voss]], a Gnomish ward-scribe, redesigns the glyph-linkage system and doubles coverage, but the maintenance burden remains unsustainable at current staffing. Legio Null desert patrols under Commander Thalric begin operating closer to the foothills.

**SA:1490 — Campaign begins.** A relic-hunting crew working for Tajsh discovers the [[Ulzammar Band]] — a fragment of a First Age artifact — in ruins near the canyon. The artifact's presence destabilizes the Veil-Wards and attracts the attention of forces far more dangerous than the Legio Null.

## Geography & Concealment

The canyon runs roughly north-south, approximately 300 feet long and 80–120 feet wide, widest at the center. The walls are 60–80 feet of steep sandstone that lean inward at the top, leaving a narrow strip of sky visible from the floor. The canyon is deepest and narrowest at the north end, where a sheer cliff face holds the cave openings that lead to the Warrens.

**The entrance** is a narrow crack (~10 feet wide) in the south end that doglegs — turns east, then north — preventing any line of sight from outside. From the exterior, it is indistinguishable from dozens of similar dry washes in the foothills.

**The Stitching (Veil-Wards):** Hundreds of interlocking glyphs carved into the upper canyon walls, weathered to look ancient. They radiate a layered aversion field:
- At 1+ miles: the canyon registers as unremarkable. Eyes slide over it.
- At 500 feet–1 mile: growing unease, a gut feeling this is a bad route.
- Under 500 feet: active disorientation, difficulty remembering why you came this way.

The wards don't create illusions. They suppress interest. The canyon is still physically visible — it's just that nobody who doesn't already know it's here wants to look at it.

**Physical concealment:** Dwellings carved into cliff faces (not freestanding on the canyon floor). Cook-smoke vents through natural chimney cracks, dissipating before clearing the rim. Oil lamps kept low, angled inward, shaded — never bright enough to cast a glow from above. The Bolt Hole emergency exit leads south to a dry riverbed ("Lira's Run").

## Districts

### The Veiled Market
The commercial heart of the settlement, built into the widest section of the canyon. The canyon floor here holds market stalls, tent canopies, lean-tos, and carts clustered around a central stone well. Above the market floor, cliff-dwellings carved at two and three levels are connected by ladders and rope-and-plank walkways. A broad natural overhang on the west wall shelters half the market.

**Key locations:** Tajsh's Vault (relic brokerage), The Dusty Lantern (tavern, neutral ground), The Crystal Counter (apothecary), Needle & Thread (tailor / ward-scribe workshop), Iron Shelf (smithy), Cira's Corner (fortune-telling), The Grey Flame (shrine to Melek the Grey).

### The Warrens
The residential district — a maze of natural cave chambers and connecting tunnels behind the north cliff face, sloping 30 feet downward from the entrances to the Deep Well at the lowest point. Families claim chambers and hang curtains for privacy. The caves hold the Schoolroom (where [[Aldric Greypen]] teaches children), the Sick-Bay (a spring-fed grotto run by Brother Oram), and the Deep Well (the settlement's guarded water source).

Behind the Deep Well, three children have found a crack in the rock leading deeper. They've been coming back smelling of ozone. They haven't told anyone.

### The Outlook
The defensive perimeter — Warden Sabel's domain. Covers the canyon rim, the surrounding hills, and three camouflaged observation posts with 270-degree coverage. The Stitching glyphs are maintained from here, with ward-tenders roped to the cliff face in rotating shifts. Also hosts the Signal Rock (flash-communication with Lothari waystation scouts) and the Bolt Hole entrance.

## People

### Power Structure
No formal government. Authority is split between Tajsh (economy, information, external contacts) and Sabel (security, ward-tending schedule, patrols). They cooperate out of mutual respect and necessity. Old Cira holds no authority and considerable influence.

### Key Figures

**[[Tajsh Thale]]** — Relic broker, de facto economic leader. Human male, mid-40s. Former Shadewalker associate. Sharp, pragmatic, morally flexible. Secretly maintains an emergency evacuation fund skimmed from relic sales. The party's employer.

**[[Warden Sabel]]** — Head of defense. Human male, late 30s. Former Varnathi Legion sergeant, deserted rather than enforce the Decree. Haunted, protective, communicates through logistics. Named the Bolt Hole exit after a child he couldn't save.

**[[Old Cira]]** — Lothari dream-mystic. Blind, indeterminate age. Speaks in riddles that turn out to be accurate. Secretly a ranking Silent Network operative monitoring [[The Sorath Scar]]. The first person to sense something wrong about the Ulzammar Band and later the Architect.

**[[Marta Dustborn]]** — Tavern keeper, The Dusty Lantern. Human female, late 40s. The community's emotional anchor. Keeps a register of every person who has stayed in the Lantern. Enforces the tavern's one rule — no faction business inside — with a short-handled shovel she calls "the policy."

**[[Hask]]** — Alchemist, The Crystal Counter. Orkin male, early 40s. Scarred, one-eyed, quietly furious. Brews healing draughts from desert plants, tests everything on himself. His eyepatch hides a mage-touched eye that sees the Spark in living things.

**[[Selin Voss]]** — Ward-scribe, Needle & Thread. Gnomish female, late 20s. The only person who understands the full Stitching pattern. Has designed a self-sustaining glyph system that would eliminate the need for constant ward-tending — but implementing it requires a 48-hour shutdown of the aversion field.

**[[Aldric Greypen]]** — Schoolteacher. Human male, mid-to-late 60s. Teaches children to read and mages to control their Spark. Has the original demonstration notes of Melek the Grey memorized — the complete record of the First Revelation, including sections the Decree ordered burned. Terrified of dying before passing this knowledge on.

## Economy

Lux Æterna runs on relic salvage and Mana-Crystal trade. Prospecting crews (including the player party) recover First Age artifacts and raw Mana-Crystals from ruins in the surrounding foothills. Tajsh brokers sales through Lothari intermediaries. The settlement also produces enchanted goods and alchemical remedies (Hask's Desert Remedies) for external trade.

**Currency:** Mana-Crystal chips. 1 Spark ≈ 1 silver.
**Prices:** 150% standard for common goods, 200% for magical items. Supply chains are fragile and dependent on Lothari caravan schedules.

## Current Threats (SA:1490)

1. **The Stitching is eroding.** The ward-tending rotation needs eight mages; there are six. The outer glyph ring becomes unreliable in approximately six months at current staffing.
2. **Legio Null patrols are shifting closer.** Commander Thalric's Sand-Riders detected an anomaly and are converging on the settlement's approximate region.
3. **The Ulzammar Band.** The recently discovered First Age artifact is disrupting the Veil-Wards and attracting things from the [[Scars of Dissonance]].
4. **The Scar is growing.** [[The Sorath Scar]] in the nearby foothills has been showing increased anomalous activity for decades. The Band's discovery has accelerated it.
5. **Possible informant.** Someone scratched a Legio Null identification mark near the Bolt Hole exit. A stranger arrived two weeks ago and has spoken to no one.

## Nearby Points of Interest

**Ruin of Sorath** — A half-buried First Age complex approximately 1/4 mile east of the canyon. Pre-Paroxysm construction: too-regular angles, mathematically precise corridors, walls inscribed with notation systems predating all known languages. The Ulzammar Band was found in excavations near this site.

**[[The Sorath Scar]]** — A rift in the ambient mana field in the nearby foothills where corrupted essence from the Circles Below bleeds through into the mortal world. Monitored by the [[Silent Network]] for decades. Spawns Echo-Wisps and other minor aberrations. Growing.

**Lira's Run** — A dry riverbed two miles south of the canyon, the exit point of the Bolt Hole emergency tunnel. Named by Sabel after a child from the family he couldn't save.
