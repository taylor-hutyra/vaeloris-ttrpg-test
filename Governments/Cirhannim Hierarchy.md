# Cirhannim Hierarchy

The ancient, magically concealed forest of [[Hannan'r']] ("Spirit-Haven") is ruled by the [[Cirhannim]] ("The Wood-Spirit People"), the most traditionalist and isolationist of the [[Elves|Elven]] peoples. Their government seeks to perfectly preserve the "First Song" of creation, viewing change as a corruption of its sacred melody.

## Government: Theocratic Monarchy

- **The Monarch of the Verdant Throne** — A hereditary ruler whose bloodline is said to be the most direct echo of the [[Evendari'e]]. The current monarch, [[Queen Elara III]], holds immense ceremonial power and acts as the living symbol of [[Hannan'r']]'s unchanging grace.
- **The Talanvar'e Council** — The true power in the kingdom. Composed of the eldest and most powerful mystics from the three great houses. They interpret the will of the forest, divine the meaning of the seasons, and can veto a monarch's decree by declaring it a *Song-Wrong*. Their word is law, as they are the voice of the forest itself.

## Societal Structure: Caste of Harmony

1. **The Mystics** — The ruling council and their acolytes.
2. **The Silent Sentinels** — The warrior caste who guard the forest's borders.
3. **The Lore-Weavers** — Artisans and historians preserving ancient crafts and histories.
4. **The Green-Tenders** — Common folk who tend to the forest's needs.

## Major Houses

- **[[House Valerion]] (The Silent Weavers)** — Most powerful and ancient house, dominating the Talanvar'e Council. Staunch isolationists who believe any outside contact will taint their pure society.
- **[[House Silvanus]] (The Sunken-Leaf Faction)** — Younger house that secretly believes isolation has become slow decay. They maintain forbidden contact with scholars in [[M'ren'r']] and argue that new knowledge is needed to fight the [[Blight]].
- **[[House Lyrandor]] (The Wardens)** — Traditional house of the Silent Sentinels. Loyal to throne and council but pragmatic at heart, sometimes at odds with rigid magical doctrines.

## Philosophy

The Cirhannim's sacred duty is preservation — to act as guardians of the past and keep the original harmonies untainted. Their lives revolve around the *Talanvar'e* (the Seasonal Harmonies). They value memory and tradition above all else.
