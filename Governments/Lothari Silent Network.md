# Lothari Silent Network

The [[Lothari]] have no formal state. They are a semi-nomadic people bound by ancient rituals, a shared mystical tradition, and a network of hidden oases and secret routes across the [[Great Desert]].

## Government: Council of Dreamers

The Lothari are led by the Silent Network, a council of the most respected mystics and visionaries from each of the major clans. They do not rule by decree, but by interpreting dreams and celestial patterns to guide their people's movements and decisions. Their leadership is a matter of spiritual authority, not political power. They are the keepers of memories the rest of the world has forgotten.

## Culture and Philosophy

The Lothari speak of The Breathless Dream — a sleeping god whose thoughts became reality. They believe humans were the first waking thought, born from curiosity. The [[Paroxysm]] was a failed dream trying to reshape the world, but was forgotten.

Their view of the [[Four Makers]]:
- The Mason as **the Pillar**
- The Carpenter as **the Wandering Root**
- The Jeweler as **the Hidden Glint**
- The Painter as **the Flickering Breath**

These appear more often in dream-rituals than in doctrine.

## Society

Semi-nomadic clans traversing hidden routes between oases. Their culture values memory, secrecy, and vision. Speech is sparse and symbolic, mixing silence and pauses for spiritual effect.

## Political Role

Under the [[Lothari]] Mystics, the Desertborn Confederacy controls the overland Silk Road. Any trade moving between East and West by land pays tribute — the "Toll of Sand." This makes the confederacy a wealthy, neutral buffer state that preserves ancient history while others look to the future.

### Common Sayings

- "Wind writes where we forget." — The world remembers what we do not.
- "Drink the shade before the sun drinks you." — Take rest or pay for it.
- "Even silence has a pattern." — Listen deeper; there is always meaning.
