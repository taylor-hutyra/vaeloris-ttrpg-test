# Mirennian League

The Mirennian League is not a nation but a powerful maritime alliance of independent port-cities, bound together by mutual profit and a shared distrust of land-based empires.

## Government: Corporate Thalassocracy

- **The Admiralty Council** — Ruling body composed of the heads of the ten most powerful Merchant Houses. Each House's vote is weighted by the tonnage of their trade fleet, making politics a constant struggle for economic dominance.
- **The Master of Tides** — Elected head of the council, rotating annually. Acts as the League's chief diplomat and naval commander but has little direct power over individual member-cities.

## Societal Structure

Status is determined by wealth and influence. There are no hereditary nobles, only "Merchant Princes" whose power lasts only as long as their fortunes. This creates a society of ruthless ambition, opportunity, and spectacular ruin.

## Major Factions

- **The [[Golden Compass Syndicate]]** — The largest and most powerful house, focused on mainstream trade and maintaining the status quo. Views the [[Pirate Confederacy]] as a pest to be eradicated.
- **The [[Azure Hand]]** — A secretive house profiting from smuggling, espionage, and gray-market trade in magical artifacts. Maintains a complex, often collaborative relationship with the Pirate Lords to destabilize their [[Golden Compass Syndicate|Golden Compass]] rivals.
- **The [[Sea-Whisperers' Guild]]** — Controls the magical art of wind-shaping. Not a political faction per se, but no house can go to war or launch an expedition without their blessing, granting them immense indirect influence.

## Culture

The [[Mirennians]] believe the world began with a Celestial Flame that split into many fires out of loneliness. Humans are "flames that chose flesh." Their culture prizes freedom, commerce, and expression. Their speech is flowing and musical, full of idioms about wind, light, and luck.

### Common Sayings

- "Sails full or none at all." — Go all-in or not at all.
- "Coins jingle loudest in empty hands." — Boasts often hide desperation.
- "Every harbor forgets the last storm." — Time softens even hardship.
