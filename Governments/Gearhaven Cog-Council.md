# Gearhaven Cog-Council

[[Gearhaven]] is the antithesis of [[Kharnzarak Monarchy|Kharnzarak]]: a city-state driven by progress, logic, and the heretical belief in perfecting creation through magical technology. It is home to the [[Elm-korin]] ("The Flame-Struck"), [[Dwarves]] exiled during the Second Age for their belief that mortal magic was a tool to achieve perfect logical order.

## Government: Technocratic Meritocracy

- **The Cog-Council** — Ruling body composed of nine Guild-Masters elected based on their proven contributions to the city's progress. Seats are not permanent; a Guild-Master can be challenged and replaced if a subordinate presents a more innovative or efficient design.
- **The Arch-Artisan** — Head of the council, elected from among its members. Sets the city's "Grand Design" — its long-term goals for research and development.

## Societal Structure: Guild-Based Meritocracy

There are no clans in Gearhaven, only guilds. An individual's status is determined entirely by their skill, ingenuity, and the success of their projects. This creates a relentless, high-pressure environment where collaboration is a tool for advancement and failure can mean demotion to a menial role.

## Major Factions

- **The Mainframe Purity** — The dominant faction. Zealous believers in the Elm-korin philosophy who seek to create a world of perfect, sterile order. Architects of the Blight Obelisk project, viewing it as the ultimate tool to impose their vision of logical perfection.
- **The Symbiotic Weave** — A [[Gnomes|Gnomish]]-led and influenced faction including many Dwarven and Human bio-engineers. They argue that pure logic is a brittle foundation and that true perfection lies in the fusion of organic and mechanical. First to detect the dangerous "null-harmonic" of the Obelisk project.

## Philosophy

The Elm-korin believe mortal magic (*Elm-'ol*, the "Truth-Flame") is not chaos but a tool to achieve the perfect, logical order espoused by the [[Ulzammar]]. They see technomancy as the ultimate synthesis of [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] craft and magical law. Their philosophy aligns closely with the pursuit of completing the work the Ulzammar began.
