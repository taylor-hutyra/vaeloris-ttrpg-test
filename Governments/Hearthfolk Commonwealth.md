# Hearthfolk Commonwealth

The collection of [[Halflings|Halfling]] towns and villages, known as the Burrows, are loosely united in what the Tallfolk call the "Hearthfolk Commonwealth." To Halflings, it is simply "home."

## Government: Direct Democracy by Town Council

- **The Town Council** — Primary governing body of any given Burrow. Composed of elected elders, usually the heads of the most respected families or guild-masters (e.g., the Master of the Brewers' Guild, the Head of the Farmers' Collective).
- **The Mayor** — Elected official serving a one-year term. The Mayor's role is to mediate disputes, preside over council meetings, and organize the annual harvest festival. Being Mayor is a burdensome civic duty, not a position of power. A common Halfling phrase: "Never trust a man who wants to be Mayor."

## Societal Structure: The Unwritten Law of the Neighbors

The true law of the Burrows is an unspoken social contract. A Halfling's reputation — their generosity, the quality of their pies, their willingness to mend a fence — is their most valuable currency. Ostracism is far more severe than any official sanction.

## Major Families

- **The [[Thistlewicks]] (Traders)** — Sprawling, influential family known for shrewd business and extensive trade networks connecting the Burrows to the outside world. Seen as worldly and a bit too ambitious, but their imports of spices and news are indispensable.
- **The [[Appledowns]] (Farmers)** — Heart of the Commonwealth. Massive clan controlling the most fertile farmlands. Deeply traditional, isolationist, and suspicious of foreign ways.
- **The [[Rumblkins]] (Miners)** — Foothills clan mining stone and iron. Gruff and pragmatic, they feel underappreciated by the "dirt-scratchers" and "coin-counters."

## Political Philosophy

Halflings have no kings, no empires, and a deep distrust of anyone who calls themselves a "ruler." Their governance is woven from town charters, family ties, and the understanding that a happy neighbor is a quiet neighbor. A Halfling hero is not a king who conquers a nation, but the neighbor who organizes the bucket brigade that saves the town from a fire.

## The Treaty of the Hearth

The [[Varnathi Pentarchy|Varnathi Republic]] was forced to sign a humiliating treaty granting [[Greenmeadow]] permanent neutrality and forbidding military boots on Halfling soil. This established the Halflings as a "Sleeping Giant" of logistics whom no army dares anger.
