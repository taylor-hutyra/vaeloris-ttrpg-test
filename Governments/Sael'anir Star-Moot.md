# Sael'anir Star-Moot

The [[Sael'anir]] ("The Sky-Wanderers") are a nomadic and contemplative [[Elves|Elven]] people who rejected both the rigid traditions of the mainland and the ceaseless progress of [[Novaterra]]. They live in small, traveling bands on the pristine continent of [[Aerin'r']], serving as messengers and mediators for the various peaceful communities there.

## Government: Consensus-Based Democracy

The Sael'anir have no government in the traditional sense. Their society is a fluid network of families and individuals bound by a shared philosophy.

- **The Saelyn (The Bands)** — The core social unit is the traveling band. Each is autonomous and makes decisions through informal consensus.
- **The Star-Moot** — Once every five years, all bands gather at a sacred celestial alignment point. Major issues affecting all Sael'anir are discussed. No decision is binding unless unanimous consensus is reached among the elders of every band — a process that can take weeks of debate, storytelling, and silent contemplation.

## Philosophy: The Sael'mora (The Sky-Path)

The Sael'anir believe the world-song is not meant to be preserved or changed, but simply experienced. True wisdom comes not from staying still or pushing forward, but from constant movement, which allows one to hear the song from all possible angles.

## Society

Egalitarian, decentralized, and deeply connected to the natural world's rhythms, especially the wind and stars. They have no permanent cities, only seasonal camps and sacred meeting places. Their speech is fluid, simple, and rich with metaphors of travel, weather, and the sky.

## View of Others

They feel a gentle sorrow for the [[Cirhannim Hierarchy|Cirhannim]] and [[Aen'valar Council|Aen'valar]], believing both have become deaf to the quiet music of the present by focusing too much on the past or the future.

### Common Greeting

*"Sael lenenya"* — "May the wind be your rhythm."
