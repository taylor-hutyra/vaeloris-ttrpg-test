# Kharnzarak Monarchy

The great mountain kingdom of [[Kharnzarak]] is the heart of [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] tradition and the seat of the [[N'om-Khazal'on]] ("The Stone-Sworn"), the most orthodox of all Dwarven peoples.

## Government: Hereditary Monarchy

- **The High King** — Absolute monarch ruling from the Adamant Throne. The current ruler is [[High King Thorgar VII]] of the [[Barazkor Clan]]. His authority is absolute, but he is bound by the sacred laws carved into the D'om-G'ol, the deep halls where the first kings ruled.
- **The Stone-Lay Council** — Nine clan-lords who serve as advisors, high court, and legislative body. Their unanimous opposition can delay a royal decree, forcing the king to justify his decision before the ancestral tombs.

## Societal Structure: Caste of Craft and Clan

1. **High Clans** — Noble families with lineage tied to legendary First Age artisans and warriors.
2. **Guild Clans** — Families whose identity and standing are defined by hereditary craft.
3. **Gatestone Clans** — Perform the most menial and dangerous labor. Upward mobility is virtually nonexistent.

## Major Clans

- **[[Barazkor Clan]] (The Iron-Gavels)** — The Royal Clan. Fiercely traditionalist and militant, wardens of Kharnzarak's military and laws. [[Lord Durin Stone-Fist]] is the king's most trusted enforcer.
- **[[Farraz'ol Clan]] (The Gem-Cutters)** — Wealthiest High Clan, controlling gem and precious metal mines. Outwardly traditional but inwardly opportunistic rivals to the Barazkor.
- **[[Gulgrym Clan]] (The Deep-Miners)** — Most powerful Guild Clan. Proud miners forming the backbone of the economy, harboring resentment toward the High Clans.
- **[[Bronr'on Legacy]] (Secret Reformists)** — Clandestine society studying forbidden [[Gearhaven Cog-Council|Elm-korin]] schematics and [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] designs, believing stagnation will be Kharnzarak's doom.

## Philosophy

The N'om-Khazal'on believe that Dwarven craft reached its apex in the First Age. Their highest virtue is *Thral'ik* (Endurance) — maintaining cultural and structural integrity against time and change. They are deeply suspicious of mortal magic, viewing it as a chaotic force that defies the structured, rhythmic nature of true creation.
