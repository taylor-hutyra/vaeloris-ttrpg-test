# Aen'valar Council

The [[Aen'valar]] ("The Forward-Singers") reject monarchy and tradition, building their society around the pursuit of knowledge and the composition of the "Unwritten Song" (*Aen'valan*). Their capital, the great library-observatory of [[Aethelas]] ("Star-Sight") on [[Novaterra]], is a sovereign city-state governed by scholarship.

## Government: Scholastic Meritocracy

- **The Conclave of Masters** — The governing council, formed by the heads of the university's most prestigious schools of thought: Aetherial Physics, Arcane Theory, Historical Resonance, and more. Leadership is earned through groundbreaking research and peer-reviewed discovery.
- **The Loremaster Prime** — Elected head of the Conclave, serving a twenty-year term. Their role is to guide the institution's academic focus, not to rule.

## Notable Figures

- **[[Archmage Elaruniel]]** — "Melody of the Hidden Pattern." Renowned scholar who held the Loremaster Prime position for many years. She collaborated with the [[Grand University]] to publish *The Frayed Weave*, the foundational document for the Theory of Arcane Scarcity, arguing that the [[Magocracy]]'s wards caused magical instability. The Magocracy later denounced and suppressed her research.
- **[[Voronwe]]** — A hardline theorist and Elaruniel's chief rival, who now holds significant influence within the Conclave.

## Philosophy

The Aen'valar believe the "world-song" is not finished but is an ongoing composition. Their sacred duty is to seek new knowledge, discover new harmonies, and add new verses to creation's epic. They see true harmony in understanding and integrating new ideas, even those from other races or disciplines like technomancy.

## Society

Meritocratic, open, and centered around institutions of learning. Their speech is analytical and precise, readily adopting new terms for new discoveries. They view the [[Cirhannim Hierarchy|Cirhannim]] as tragic figures trapped by fear of change, and the [[Sael'anir Star-Moot|Sael'anir]] as contemplative but lacking the drive to move the world's song forward.

### Common Farewell

*"Thel an i'aen"* — "Seek in the future."
