# Aerin'r' Path of Consensus

The contemplative [[Waldkyn]] of [[Aerin'r']] reject formal government entirely, opting for a society based on empathy and consensus. The Aerin're Kith are descendants of Waldkyn who traveled to the pristine northern continent seeking a "purer" expression of the Synthesis.

## Government: Consensus-Based Anarchy

The Aerin're Kith have no rulers or councils. They live in small, nomadic kiths. When a decision affecting more than one kith is needed, the elders meet. There are no votes. They simply talk, listen, and wait — sometimes for weeks — until a single, unanimous path forward emerges that all can agree to walk.

This makes them profoundly peaceful but politically inert and extremely vulnerable to decisive, external threats.

## Philosophy: Stewardship

The Aerin're Kith believe their purpose is to protect what remains of the world's original, untainted harmony. Their society is decentralized, contemplative, and deeply empathetic. They form small, nomadic communities or live in quiet harmony within the towns of other peaceful peoples on [[Aerin'r']].

## View of Others

They feel sorrow for both of their other branches. They believe the [[Quiet Vales Great Council|Quiet Vales Kith]] are paralyzed by the past and the [[Chartered Kith Weaver's Synod|Chartered Kith]] are blinded by the future — both missing the balance of the present moment.

## Coexistence on Aerin'r'

The Aerin're Kith share [[Aerin'r']] with the [[Sael'anir Star-Moot|Sael'anir]] (Sky-Wanderer [[Elves]]) and the [[Vorok-Varr]] (peaceful [[Orkin]]). They live alongside these peoples in mutual respect, practicing non-interference and stewardship. Their integration with local communities has transformed their cultural drive from active synthesis into quiet guardianship.

## Vulnerability

While their consensus system ensures deep harmony within, it leaves them unable to respond quickly to crisis. A decisive external threat could overwhelm them before agreement is reached — a critical weakness in a world of growing dangers.
