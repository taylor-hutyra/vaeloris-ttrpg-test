# Mycohollow Mycelial Council

[[Mycohollow]] is the largest and most influential [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] settlement, a city woven into a gargantuan, semi-sentient fungal grove. Its government is as organic and interconnected as the city itself.

## Government: Symbiotic Council

- **The Mycelial Council** — Composed not of elected officials, but of Gnomes who have achieved the deepest symbiotic link with the grove. Membership is fluid and earned through deep communion, not votes. They do not issue commands, but "resonate intentions" through the city's network, guiding its growth and function.
- **The Twin Speakers** — The council is led by two co-equal figures representing the Gnomes' dual heritage from the [[Twin Roots]]:
  - **The Myco-Whisperer** — Spiritual leader responsible for the grove's health and its relationship with the natural world (representing [[Ceryn-Maia]]).
  - **The Geo-Weaver** — Lead artisan responsible for the city's physical structures and the integration of craft and nature (representing [[Ilys-Tekne]]).

## Societal Structure

Society is organized into communal Hollows (neighborhoods) and specialized Guilds (professions). A Gnome belongs to both, creating a cross-woven social fabric. Standing is determined by the health of one's Hollow and the quality of one's Guild work.

## Major Factions

- **The Balancers (Majority)** — Believe the *Syn-Koinos* (Shared Pattern) is a living pattern that must be gently guided, mended, and balanced. They favor diplomacy, patience, and subtle intervention.
- **The Purifiers (Scions of the Unbroken Pattern)** — Zealous faction believing the world's pattern is actively corrupted by the "Loud-Walkers." They see outsiders as dangerous anomalies that must be eliminated for the good of the world.
- **The Integrators (New-Growth School)** — Innovators who argue that the *Syn-Koinos* must evolve. They believe that new elements — magic, foreign technologies — can strengthen the pattern.

## Philosophy

The [[Forest Gnomes]] believe the work of the [[Twin Roots]] is a delicate dance between nature and artifice. Their sacred duty is the *Mori-Syna* (the Quiet Work) — tending to the world's subtle mechanics. Their most sacred proverb: "The deepest roots make no sound."
