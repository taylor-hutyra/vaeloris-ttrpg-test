Here is the comprehensive expansion of **Epoch VII of the Second Age**.

This epoch is the denouement of the Second Age and the prelude to the current era. It describes the rise of the **Magocracy**, a global government born of necessity that evolves into a regime of absolute control. It is an age defined by the "Great Lie"—that the world is healed, when in fact it is slowly rotting from the magical wounds of the Skyfall. It ends with the legal codification of the world as it exists at the start of the Third Age.

## Epoch VII: The Age of the Mage Ascendancy (2701 – 3280 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The world survives the Skyfall, but at a terrible cost. The **Grey Rot** (The Fraying) emerges as a direct consequence of the massive magical energies used to ground the Drakona. The Coalition of Mages rebrands as the **Magocracy**, positioning themselves as the only doctors capable of treating this wound. They trade security for liberty, enforcing a global peace that stifles innovation and enforces rigid social stratification for the "lesser" races (Orkin and Drakona).

### Phase I: The Rise of the Magocracy & The Grey Rot (2701 – 2866 S.A.)

### 2701 S.A. – The Corporeal Tax

**Summary:**  
In the immediate aftermath of the Skyfall, billions of tons of wreckage littered the continent. The Coalition of Mages, now the de facto world government, instituted the *Corporeal Tax*. Every able-bodied citizen was conscripted for three years of labor to clear the ruins of the Cloud Cities. These labor periods were on cycles where everyone would contribute their three years over the next 15\. This way, only 20% of the labor force would be used at a time, letting the world continue to progress.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Salvage Economy:** Entire cities sprang up around the crash sites to harvest "Sky-Stone" and "Draconic Steel."  
* **Mirennian Recovery:** The Mirennians, their sky-ports useless, became the masters of salvage logistics, rebuilding their wealth on the bones of their enemies.  
* **Resurgence of Naval Power:** having lost their air ships, the Mirennian League returns to their dominance of the seas. Without wind-shaping, their superiority is fully through skill, design, and action.

### 2705 S.A. – The Outbreak of the Grey Rot

**Summary:**  
In the craters where the largest Cloud Cities fell, the fabric of reality thinned. Kael'Zorai’s corrupted essence seeped through the cracks. It manifested as the **Grey Rot**: a magical plague that turned flesh to ash and made stone weep blood.  
**Consequences:**

* **Panic:** Conventional healing magic accelerated the Rot.  
* **The Quarantine:** The Mages cordoned off vast swaths of land, condemning millions to death to save the rest.

### 2710 S.A. – The Rise of the Plague-Wardens (Gnomish Intervention)

**Summary:**  
While Human mages faltered, the Gnomes of the Mycelial Collective discovered that the Rot was a fungal-magical hybrid. They developed "Spore-Masks" and alchemical sprays that could slow the spread.  
**Consequences:**

* **Gnomish Prestige:** The Gnomes became the frontline medics of the crisis.  
* **The Symbiotic Shift:** Many Gnomes began incorporating the Rot into their study of the "Shared Pattern," sowing the seeds for the "Integrator" faction later in history.

### 2715 S.A. – Publication of *The Frayed Weave*

**Summary:**  
Haunted by her role in the Skyfall, **Elaruniel** emerged from seclusion in Aethelas. She published *The Frayed Weave*, a dense theoretical text arguing that the Skyfall didn't just break the Drakona; it broke the immune system of the universe.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Heresy:** The Magocracy denounced the book as alarmist, banning it in human lands.  
* **The Academic Underground:** It became the bible of the Aen’valar and the secret societies of Novaterra.

### 2725 S.A. – The District of Scales (Drakona Segregation)

**Summary:**  
The surviving Drakona, stripped of flight and dignity, were relocated to the "District of Scales" in the ruins of their former capital (now grounded). The Magocracy classified them as "Wards of the State."  
**Consequences:**

* **Forced Labor:** Drakona were used as living batteries to power the anti-Rot wards, draining their life force.  
* **The Simmering Rage:** A resistance movement, the "Broken Wing," formed in the shadows.

### 2750 S.A. – The Technocratic Schism

**Summary:**  
Gearhaven faced a crisis. The Mages wanted to hoard all magical resources to fight the Rot, while the Engineers wanted to rebuild the economy. The **Cog-Council** split.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Binding Accord II:** A new constitution was written. Gearhaven formally recognized the Magocracy's authority over *magic*, but retained absolute autonomy over *machine*.

### 2780 S.A. – The Night of the weeping Stone

**Summary:**  
A massive outbreak of Grey Rot occurred in the Dwarven surface city of **Iron-Beach** (Novaterra). The Magocracy proposed "cleansing" the city (burning it). The Thulkorûn Dwarves refused, fighting off the Mages while Gnomish medics cured the outbreak.  
**Consequences:**

* Proven that the Magocracy’s "burn it all" tactic was not the only way.  
* Deepened the trust between the "Wandering Kin" (Dwarves/Gnomes) and the Free Peoples.

### 2815 S.A. – The Containment Declaration

**Summary:**  
After a century of struggle, the Magocracy declared the Grey Rot "Contained." They erected massive, permanent "Pylons of Warding" around the infected zones (The Scars).  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Lie:** The Rot was not gone; it was merely caged. The Magocracy took credit for saving the world, solidifying their political legitimacy.

### 2851 S.A. – The Fall of Vrak'Thul (The Final Resistance)

**Summary:**  
The fortress-city of Vrak'Thul, a haven for renegade mages and Drakona loyalists, refused to pay the Magocracy’s tithes. The Ruling Council decided to make an example.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Arcane Nullifier:** The Magocracy deployed a weapon that created a "Anti-Magic Vacuum." The city, held together by old magic, crumbled into dust instantly.  
* **Total Submission:** No nation dared defy the Magocracy openly after this.

### 2866 S.A. – The First Decree (The Pax Magica)

**Summary:**  
With their power absolute, the Magocracy issued the *First Decree*: "War is inefficient. Order is paramount."  
**Terms:**

* **Abolition of Standing Armies:** Nations could keep "City Guards," but large field armies were illegal.  
* **The Ban on Aeromancy:** The study or use of Flight Magic was punishable by "Stilling" (lobotomy of the magical center of the brain).  
* **The Magister’s Watch:** A global police force of mages was established to enforce these laws.

### Phase II: The Enforced Peace & The Return of the Skies (2867 – 3250 S.A.)

### 2879 S.A. – The Charter of Civic Unity (The Orkin Question)

**Summary:**  
To rebuild the workforce, the Magocracy pressured the successor states of the Varnathi Empire to integrate the Orkin. The *Charter of Civic Unity* granted Orkin "Subject Status."  
**Consequences:**

* **The Quarters:** Orkin were allowed to live in cities but confined to specific districts.  
* **The Criminal Underground:** Excluded from legal guilds, Orkin formed the "Shadow-Syndicates" to survive.

### 2900 S.A. – The War of Whispers

**Summary:**  
Since armies were illegal, conflict shifted to espionage. The **Shadewalkers** entered a golden age, acting as proxies for national grievances. The Magocracy’s **Inquisitors** fought a shadow war against them.  
**Consequences:**

* Assassination became the primary tool of diplomacy.

### 2950 S.A. – The Steam Revolution

**Summary:**  
Barred from researching powerful magic, the engineers of Novaterra turned to physics. High-pressure steam engines, rifled muskets, and clockwork automation began to replace magical equivalents.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Leveller:** A peasant with a rifle could kill a mage. The balance of power began to shift imperceptibly back to the common man.

### 2962 S.A. – The "Broken Wing" Insurgency

**Summary:**  
A radical faction of flightless Drakona, the **Broken Wing**, launched a decades-long campaign of magical terrorism against the Magocracy. They bombed Mana-Pylons and assassinated Magisters to protest their confinement in the District of Scales.  
**Consequences:**

* **Justified Brutality:** These attacks justified the Magocracy's harsh policing in the eyes of the public.

### **Moral Complexity:** It established the Drakona as an active internal threat, complicating the narrative of them being solely victims.2996 S.A. – The Founding of the Grand University

**Summary:**  
To control the narrative, the Magocracy centralized all higher education in Mórenórë. The **Grand University** was ostensibly open to all, but the curriculum was heavily curated to promote the Magocracy’s version of history.  
**Consequences:**

* **Indoctrination:** Generations of leaders were raised to view the Magocracy as benevolent guardians.

### 3010 S.A. – The Loremaster's Mandate (The Elven Schism)

**Summary:**  
At the Aen'valar Conclave in Aethelas, **Elaruniel** ran for Loremaster Prime against **Archmage Voronwe** (a Magocracy loyalist). Voronwe argued for isolation; Elaruniel argued for engagement to fix the Fraying.  
**Consequences:**

* **Elaruniel’s Victory:** She won by a landslide of academic support.  
* **The Cold Shoulder:** Aethelas began to drift away from the Magocracy’s influence, becoming a bastion of independent thought.

### 3050 S.A. – The Crack in the Door (Dwarven Trade)

**Summary:**  
The Nûm-Khazalûn of Kharnzarak, seeing the stability of the surface, allowed a single trade gate to open. They would sell steel and gold, but buy nothing (fearing surface taint).  
**Consequences:**

* The influx of high-quality Dwarven steel fueled the industrial revolution in Gearhaven and the Federation.

### 3100 S.A. – The Pirate Revival (The Glider Fleets)

**Summary:**  
The Pirate Confederacy of Serpent's Tooth, grounded for centuries, adapted. They developed "Wave-Skimmers"—extremely fast hydrofoil ships—and early non-magical gliders launched from cliffs to scout prey.  
**Consequences:**

* Smuggling returned to the high seas, challenging the Magocracy’s trade monopolies.

### 3134 S.A. – The Second Age of Flight (The Ironclad Era)

**Summary:**  
The pivotal moment of the late epoch. Engineers in Gearhaven, collaborating with Mirennian architects, unveiled the *Aether-Glider*. Using "Lift-Gas" (refined from alchemical processes) and steam propellers, they achieved flight without Aeromancy.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Legal Loophole:** The Magocracy tried to ban them, but the *First Decree* specifically forbade "Flight Magic." The machines used no magic. The Magocracy, bound by its own rigid laws, could not legally stop them without inciting a global revolt.  
* **The Sky-Rush:** The Mirennian League poured its fortune into building a massive fleet of commercial airships, reclaiming their title as masters of the sky.

### 3150 S.A. – The Sky-Tax Crisis

**Summary:**  
Humiliated by the loophole, the Magocracy imposed a heavy tax on "Atmospheric Displacement." The Merchant Houses of Mirennia refused to pay. The Federation of City-States mobilized its (illegal) reserves of battle-mages.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Brink of War:** The Magocracy backed down, agreeing to a reduced tax in exchange for the right to inspect all cargo. It was the first time the Magocracy blinked.

### 3217 S.A. – The Whisperwood Outbreak

**Summary:**  
A resurgence of the Grey Rot occurred in the Barony of Whisperwood. The Magocracy responded with terrifying efficiency, burning the entire barony to the ground in a night.  
**Consequences:**

* **Fear Renewed:** The public, growing rebellious, was reminded of *why* the Magocracy ruled. "Better the Mages than the Rot."

### 3220 S.A. – The "Red-Ink" Crisis

**Summary:**  
The Shadewalkers, realizing they could not defeat the Magocracy militarily, attacked the economy. They manipulated the exchange rate of Mana-Crystals (the global currency standard), causing hyperinflation that threatened to collapse the Magocracy's government.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Banker’s Guild:** The Magocracy was forced to hire the Shadewalkers to stabilize the banks. By the Third Age, the "Thieves' Guild" effectively runs the global banking system, making them untouchable by the law.

### 3250 S.A. – The Ashfall Compact

**Summary:**  
The Magocracy formally recognized the **Barony of Ashfall** (a nation of dark industrialism and necromantic pragmatism) as a sovereign state. They needed a source of "unethical" research that they could deny involvement with.  
**Consequences:**

* Ashfall became the dark laboratory of the world, supplying the Magocracy with forbidden weapons to fight the Rot.

### Phase III: The Convention of Accord (3280 S.A.)

### 3280 S.A. – The Convention of Accord

**Summary:**  
Sensing the shifting tides—the rise of technology, the restlessness of the nations, and the subtle return of the Rot—the Ruling Council of the Magocracy summoned all world leaders to Aurumport.  
**The Goal:**  
To codify the status quo into a permanent international law that would prevent the outbreak of a Third Great War.

**The Laws of Accord:**

1. **Sovereignty:** The borders of the Varnathi Democracy, Mirennian League, Federation, Technocracy, and Desertborn Confederacy were fixed in perpetuity.  
2. **The Neutrality of Aerinórë:** The northern continent was declared a "Sanctuary of Nature," forbidden to industrial development or military presence.  
3. **The Rights of the Arcane:** The Magocracy retained exclusive jurisdiction over all magical crimes and the regulation of magical education.  
4. **The Free Skies:** Non-magical air travel was guaranteed as a universal right, protected by the Magocracy (in exchange for the inspection rights).

**Consequences:**

* **End of the Second Age:** The signing of the Accord marked the official transition to the **Third Age**.  
* **The Stage is Set:** The world is stable, but rigid. Ancient grudges are suppressed by law, not resolved. The Rot festers in the dark. The "lesser" races chafe under their chains. And the Magocracy sits on a throne of lies, terrified that the Fraying they claimed to fix is about to unravel everything.
