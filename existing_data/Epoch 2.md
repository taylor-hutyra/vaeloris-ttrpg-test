## Epoch II: The Age of Ambition (381 – 800 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
With the monsters of the Paroxysm largely slain or driven into the shadows, and the minor deities withdrawing their avatars, the "Divine Leash" is removed. The Varnathi tribes consolidate into an Empire, the Mirennians turn their eyes to the ocean, and the Dwarves undergo a schism that will change the face of technology and magic. This era ends with the total withdrawal of divine influence, leaving mortals as the sole masters of Vaeloris.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Imperial Rise (381 – 580 S.A.)

### 381 S.A. – The Unification of the High Plateau

**Summary:**  
**Caelius I**, the great-grandson of Vardas (founder of Vernath), consolidated the seven major highland city-states through a mix of diplomacy and the "War of the Seven Spears." Standing atop the walls of Vernath, he declared the end of the tribal confederacy and the birth of the **Varnathi Empire**. He claimed a "Mandate of Order"—that since the gods had left, it was Man’s duty to impose structure upon the chaotic world.  
**Consequences:**

* Centralization of military power under the Emperor.  
* The "First Compact" was amended to include loyalty to the Throne alongside the State.

### 395 S.A. – The Standardization of Steel

**Summary:**  
To fuel the new Imperial Army, Varnathi smiths, aided by Thulkorûn (exiled Dwarf) consultants, perfected the "Blast-Furnace" technique. This allowed for the mass production of standardized steel armor and weapons, replacing the bronze and iron mix of the previous epoch.  
**Consequences:**

* The Varnathi Legions became the most heavily armed force on the continent.  
* Creation of the **Iron-Bound**, the Empire’s elite heavy infantry corps.

### 410 S.A. – The Southern Conquests (The Breaking of the Barrens)

**Summary:**  
Emperor Caelius I launched the "March of Order" into the southern wilds, territory held by scattered Orkin clans since their retreat in Epoch I. Using the new steel legions and rigid phalanx tactics, the Empire shattered the disorganized Orkin warbands.  
**Consequences:**

* The Orkin were pushed further into the inhospitable badlands or the western marshes.  
* **Founding of Castellum:** A massive fortress was built on the southern frontier to hold the conquered territory. It quickly evolved into a militaristic city-state, culturally distinct from the philosophical capital of Vernath.

### 425 S.A. – The Codex of the Road

**Summary:**  
With the empire expanding, the need for logistics grew. The Imperial Senate passed the *Codex of the Road*, a massive public works project that expanded the paved trade roads of Epoch I into a continental network.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the "Courier Corps," allowing messages to cross the empire in days.  
* The "Imperial Mile" became the standard unit of measurement for the continent.

### 440 S.A. – The Coastal Expansion & The First Clash

**Summary:**  
Seeking to control maritime trade, the Empire marched east to the coast. They founded **Port Farrick** at the mouth of the Great River. However, they encountered the Mirennians—now a loose confederation of wealthy city-states. When Imperial tax collectors arrived, the Mirennian captains threw them into the harbor.  
**Consequences:**

* Beginning of the "Cold War" between the Land (Empire) and the Sea (Mirennia).  
* Mirennians formalized their alliance into the **Mirennian League** for mutual defense.

### 455 S.A. – The Synod of the Ordained

**Summary:**  
To unify the disparate cultures within the expanding Empire, the Imperial Priesthood established the **Pantheon of the Ordained**. This state religion syncretized local beliefs into a structured hierarchy, emphasizing gods of Law, Harvest, and War, while suppressing "chaotic" nature worship.  
**Consequences:**

* Religious tension with the Mirennian League, who favored fluid, elemental deities.  
* Alienation of the Waldkyn, whose nature-worship was deemed "primitive."

### 485 S.A. – The Silver Boom

**Summary:**  
A Thulkorûn prospector named **Grogor the Eye** discovered the "Deep-Vein" in the Central Mountains—a massive deposit of nearly pure silver. The Empire annexed the region immediately.  
**Consequences:**

* **Founding of Argentum:** A mining camp that exploded into a metropolis of stone and coin.  
* Rise of the "Silver Lords," a new class of merchant-nobility who would eventually rival the Emperor in power.

### 520 S.A. – The Incident of the Burning Mast

**Summary:**  
An Imperial warship attempted to blockade a Mirennian port to enforce tariffs. In response, a Mirennian "Sea-Whisperer" (an early elementalist) summoned a localized gale that drove the ship onto the rocks.  
**Consequences:**

* First recorded combat use of Wind-Shaping.  
* The Empire realized it could not challenge the League on the open ocean.

### 550 S.A. – The Perfection of Wind-Shaping

**Summary:**  
Building on the Burning Mast incident, the Mirennian **Guild of Tides** codified the art of Wind-Shaping. It was not true magic in the later arcane sense, but a ritualistic communion with the elemental remnants of Gwynfaer (The Windweaver).  
**Consequences:**

* Mirennian ships could now sail against the wind, giving them absolute naval superiority.  
* Economic dominance of the League over all coastal trade.

### Phase II: The Republican Revolution (581 – 653 S.A.)

### 581 S.A. – The Bloodless Coup

**Summary:**  
Emperor **Titus II**, a decadent ruler who spent the treasury on pleasure palaces while ignoring the roads, attempted to seize the assets of the Silver Lords in Argentum. The Governors of Argentum, Castellum, and Port Farrick secretly allied. They cut off silver, soldiers, and trade goods to the capital.  
**Consequences:**

* Starved of resources and abandoned by his guard, Titus II abdicated.  
* **Declaration of the Republic:** The monarchy was dissolved. Power was transferred to a Senate composed of representatives from the major city-states.

### 600 S.A. – The Age of City-States

**Summary:**  
With central Imperial authority broken, the Republic functioned as a loose federation. The City-States developed distinct identities:

* **Vernath:** Philosophy and Law (The Old Guard).  
* **Argentum:** Finance and Industry (The New Money).  
* **Castellum:** Military and Discipline (The Iron Fist).  
* **Port Farrick:** Trade and Diplomacy (The Gateway).

### 615 S.A. – The Dwarven Isolation (The Closing of the Lower Deep)

**Summary:**  
Disturbed by the political chaos of the Humans and the rise of the "Silver Lords," the High King of Kharnzarak ordered the "Lower Deep" gates sealed. Trade continued on the surface, but non-Dwarves were no longer permitted into the cities proper.  
**Consequences:**

* Dwarven culture turned inward, becoming increasingly stagnant and traditionalist.

### 635 S.A. – The Sundering of Mount Cinder

**Summary:**  
The dormant volcano on the Mirennian outpost island of Vol erupted with cataclysmic force. The explosion was not natural; later scholars theorize a Mirennian Wind-Shaper attempted to tap into geothermal vents and failed.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Gloom Famine:** Ash choked the skies for three years.  
* **The Tsunami of Tears:** Massive waves destroyed 40% of the Mirennian fleet and harbor infrastructure.  
* Collapse of the Mirennian economy, leaving them vulnerable.

### Phase III: The Age of Rivalry & Discovery (654 – 800 S.A.)

### 654 S.A. – The Salt War Begins

**Summary:**  
With food scarce due to the Gloom Famine, salt (essential for preservation) became more valuable than silver. The Varnathi Republic, seeing the Mirennian League weakened by the tsunami, launched an invasion to seize the vast salt flats of the southern coast.  
**Consequences:**

* **Total War:** The first conflict involving large-scale armies of mortals against mortals.  
* **Mercenary Rise:** The League, lacking soldiers, used its remaining treasury to hire thousands of Thulkorûn Dwarves and Orkin exiles.

### 660 S.A. – The Siege of Solis

**Summary:**  
The Republican Legion of Castellum besieged the Mirennian city of Solis. The siege lasted two years. It was broken not by armies, but by the "Grey Death"—dycentery caused by the unsanitary conditions of the massive army camps.  
**Consequences:**

* Advancements in field medicine and sanitation.  
* **The Soldier’s Plague:** The disease spread back to the cities, killing more than the war itself.

### 665 S.A. – The Mórenórë Accords

**Summary:**  
The Aen’valar Elves, observing the carnage from their enclave, intervened. They invited Republican and League leaders to the neutral ground of Mórenórë. Elven diplomats mediated a ceasefire.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Salt Truce:** Borders were returned to pre-war status, but trade tariffs were lowered.  
* Mórenórë was recognized as a "Sanctuary City," immune to future conflicts.

### 690 S.A. – The Exile of the Elm-korin

**Summary:**  
In Kharnzarak, a sect of Dwarven smiths led by **Tharum Flame-Touched** claimed that *magic* was simply another ore to be worked. They performed the "Ritual of the Soul-Forge," infusing a hammer with arcane fire. The High King declared this a corruption of the Stone.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Banishment:** The Elm-korin faction was exiled. Unlike the Thulkorûn (criminals), these were visionaries. They wandered the human lands, looking for a place to build.

### 710 S.A. – The Rise of the Shadewalkers

**Summary:**  
Veterans of the Salt War, spies, and disgraced diplomats formed a secret society in the shadows of Port Farrick. They realized information was more profitable than war.  
**Consequences:**

* **New Organization:** The Shadewalkers (Espionage Guild).  
* They began selling state secrets to both the Republic and the League, ensuring neither side gained a decisive advantage.

### 752 S.A. – The Discovery of Novaterra

**Summary:**  
Mirennian Navigator **Tynna Swift-Tide**, seeking a route around the Republican blockades, sailed further east than any before. She discovered a massive, untamed continent.  
**Consequences:**

* **State Secret:** The League declared the existence of "Novaterra" a level-zero secret.  
* Establishment of the "Ghost Fleet"—ships officially listed as lost at sea, actually ferrying supplies to the new colony.

### 761 S.A. – The Pact of the Northern Sanctuary (Aerinórë)

**Summary:**  
A separate expedition discovered the smaller continent of Aerinórë. Finding it inhabited by peaceful Waldkyn and Orkin (Vorok-Varr), the Mirennian captains, weary of the corruption of the old world, swore a pact of non-interference.  
**Consequences:**

* Aerinórë became a myth to the general public, a "paradise" that sailors whispered about but never mapped.

### 780 S.A. – The Council of Divine Admonition

**Summary:**  
As tensions rose for a second Salt War, the avatars of the minor deities appeared simultaneously in the Senate of Vernath and the Council of Mirenn. They spoke in unison: "Your hunger devours the future. We will not watch you consume the world we saved."  
**Consequences:**

* The warning was largely ignored by the secular leaders, who believed the gods were powerless relics of the First Age.

### 800 S.A. – The Second Withdrawal (The Day of the Closed Sky)

**Summary:**  
On the winter solstice of the 800th year, all divine magic ceased for one hour. When it returned, it was fainter, more distant. The Minor Deities withdrew their consciousness from the mortal plane entirely. The sacred fires in the temples dimmed, and the winds no longer answered prayers, only physics.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of Epoch II:** The world was now truly secular.  
* **The Crisis of Faith:** The Church of the Ordained lost political power as their miracles failed.  
* **The Vacuum:** The moral authority of the gods was gone, clearing the path for the unrestricted ambition of the coming ages.

**New Cultural Element:**

* **Name:** The God-Grief  
* **Type:** Cultural Phenomenon  
* **Description:** A period of nihilism and artistic melancholy that swept through Human and Elven society following the withdrawal, influencing poetry and music for a generation.
