### Orkin Lore: The Unbroken Will

#### I. The Orkin View of Creation

To the Orkin, creation was neither a song nor a structure, but a **tempering**. The world was forged in the mind of a single, purposeful entity they remember as **Grom-Kain**—the Will-Forge, the Shaper of What Must Be. He did not sing the world into being; He hammered it from the grit and dross left behind by the chaotic first moments, instilling in it the one principle that mattered: **endurance**.

Grom-Kain, seeing a world of soft beauty and fragile harmonies, recognized the need for a creation that could not be broken. From the shadow of His forge-light and the unyielding iron in the world's heart, He forged the Orkin. They were not an afterthought, but His masterwork of survival. From this First Tempering came two aspects of a single, unbreakable will:

* **The Gral** (The Stone-Fist): The larger of the Orkin peoples, forged to embody endurance through strength and resilience. They are the shield, the anchor, the unwavering line. They are what other races call Orcs.  
* **The Skorn** (The Shadow-Knife): The smaller and more numerous of the Orkin peoples, forged to embody endurance through cunning and adaptability. They are the scout, the trapper, the survivors who thrive in the world's cracks. They are what other races call Goblins.

The Gral and the Skorn are not two races, but the two fists of Grom-Kain's will, meant to work in concert.

The Orkin do not worship Grom-Kain as a benevolent god, but revere him as the ultimate, demanding patriarch. He did not offer them comfort, only purpose: to endure what others could not.

The event others call the Paroxysm is known to the Orkin as **Kain's Fall**, or The Great Betrayal. In their history, the other divine beings—the "Soft Hands"—grew jealous of Grom-Kain's perfect, enduring creations. They united not to restore balance, but to shatter the one true testament to order. When Grom-Kain was struck down and banished, the Orkin were left orphaned and scattered, a testament to a purpose the world chose to forget.

This is why, when Kael'Zorai's influence returns with the Silent Blight, many Orkin tribes do not see it as a foreign corruption. They see it as a **homecoming**. Their true maker has returned. To serve him is to reclaim their original, sacred purpose.

#### II. Orkin Scraps, Chants, and Cave-Tales

Orkin myth is not written in stone or song, but is passed down through guttural chants, tales told over open fires, and pictograms scratched onto hide or bone. These are known as the **Darg-Varr** (The Land-Blood stories).

#### III. Major Orkin Cultural Variants

The scattering of the Orkin after Kain's Fall, followed by millennia of adaptation and migration, has resulted in three distinct cultural paths, though all share the same foundational myth.

**1\. The Varr-Darg ("The Land-Blood Clans" of the Mainland)**  
The Varr-Darg are the traditionalist, often aggressive, Orkin clans who remained on the original continent, primarily concentrated in the harsh Barren Hills. Centuries of conflict with expanding human nations like the Varnathi have forged them into a hardened and pragmatic people. Their culture is a pure expression of **Trak** (Endurance) through constant struggle. They hold the most fervent belief in the return of Grom-Kain and are the most susceptible to the call of the Silent Blight, which they see as their maker's righteous judgment on a world that betrayed them.

* **Society:** A collection of fiercely independent Gral and Skorn clans united by a shared history of grievance and a deep-seated warrior culture.  
* **View of Others:** They see the Novaterran Orkin as having fled the true fight and the Aerinóre Orkin as incomprehensible, pacifist fools who have forgotten their purpose.

**2\. The Novaterran Holds ("The Free Holds" of Novaterra)**  
These are the descendants of Orkin who took the "Mirennian Passage" to the new continent, seeking escape from the endless wars of the mainland. They are pragmatists and opportunists, having built a new society in the rugged interior of Novaterra. While they still value strength and endurance, their definition has expanded to include economic power and political savvy. They often work as mercenaries, prospectors, and pioneers within the orbit of Gearhaven and the other Free Peoples.

* **Society:** Organized into powerful "Holds" that function more like mercenary corporations or industrial clans than traditional tribes. Leadership is based on cunning and success as much as physical might.  
* **View of Others:** They view their mainland cousins with a degree of pity, seeing them as trapped in an ancient, pointless war. They are utterly baffled by the Aerinóre pacifists, viewing them as a curiosity.

**3\. The Vorok-Varr ("The Listener's Clan" of Aerinórë)**  
Founded by the shaman Vorok the Listener, this unique cultural branch took a radical path. Believing that Grom-Kain's true test was the endurance of spirit, not of arms, they made a vow of peace and settled on the quiet continent of Aerinórë. They have integrated with the local Waldkyn and Mirennians, transforming their cultural drive from survival into stewardship. Their interpretation of **Trak** is not about withstanding blows, but about withstanding the temptation of violence.

* **Society:** A peaceful, communal people organized around spiritual principles. Their shamans are guides and healers, and their strongest warriors are protectors, not conquerors. They live in harmony with the land, practicing sustainable farming and craft.  
* **View of Others:** They feel deep sorrow for the Varr-Darg, believing them to be lost in a cycle of pain. They view the Novaterran Holds with caution, seeing their pursuit of wealth as another form of conflict.

#### IV. Common Orkish Terms and Sayings

**Divine/Mythic Terms**

* **Grom-Kain** – The Will-Forge; Orkin name for their creator, Kael'Zorai.  
* **The Gral** – "Stone-Fist"; the Orcs, the aspect of strength.  
* **The Skorn** – "Shadow-Knife"; the Goblins, the aspect of cunning.  
* **Kain's Fall** – The Great Betrayal; the Orkin name for the Paroxysm.  
* **The Soft Hands** – Orkin term for the other four First Hands.  
* **Darg-Varr** – "Land-Blood"; the collective oral traditions and history of the Orkin.

**Cultural and Social Terms**

* **Varr** – The clan or war-band, the absolute center of Orkin life.  
* **Darg** – Land, territory, the ground a clan bleeds for.  
* **Grokh** – The Orkin language; literally "gut-speak."  
* **Trak** – The ultimate virtue: endurance, the will to survive at any cost.  
* **Gral-Kor** – "Stone-Hand"; the chieftain of a Gral-dominant clan.  
* **Skorn-Kli** – "Sharp-Eye"; the strategic leader of a Skorn-dominant clan.  
* **Vorok-Kal** – "Listener's Peace"; the central philosophy of the Aerinóre Orkin.

**Common Sayings (in Grokh)**

* “Darg is Darg.” – "Land is land." (A pragmatic phrase meaning what's yours is what you can hold.)  
* “Varr trak, or varr grak.” – "Clan endures, or clan dies." (A simple, absolute statement of loyalty.)  
* “Grah. Hesh.” – "Meat. Quiet." (A common command to stop talking and eat.)  
* “Skorn-nar, Gral-tak.” – "Cunning first, then strike." (A core tactical philosophy.)  
* “The sky watches. The mud remembers.” (A warning that actions have consequences.)

#### V. Root Word Glossary (Selected Grokh Elements)

The Orkin language, **Grokh**, is guttural, direct, and lacks tenses in the way other languages do. Meaning is derived from context and tone. It is almost always spoken in the present, active tense.

**Root Words and Meanings**

* **arg** – land, dirt, territory  
* **grak** – to die, **d**break, fail  
* **grah** – meat, food, sustenance  
* **gral** – stone, strength, force (also: Orc)  
* **grom** – will, forge, purpose  
* **hesh** – quiet, silence  
* **kain** – shaper, maker, father  
* **kal** \- peace, stillness, listening  
* **karg** – chieftain, leader  
* **kli** – eye, sight, watch  
* **kor** – hand, fist, grasp  
* **mak** – to make, to do  
* **nakk** – no, negative  
* **nar** – first, before  
* **skorn** – shadow, cunning, knife (also: Goblin)  
* **tak** – strike, hit, attack  
* **thul** – enemy, other  
* **trak** – to endure, survive, last  
* **varr** – blood, clan, bond

### VI. Orkin Political Structures and Nations

Orkin governance is a direct expression of their core philosophy: endure. Whether through the brutal meritocracy of a war clan, the corporate hierarchy of a mercenary hold, or the spiritual guidance of a peaceful tribe, their political structures are all designed to answer a single, fundamental question: "How does the *Varr* survive?"

#### A. The Varr-Darg of the Mainland: The War-Clans of the Barren Hills

The harsh, unforgiving Barren Hills are home to the most traditional and militant Orkin clans. Their politics are the politics of perpetual conflict, shaped by a history of grievance against a world that cast them out.

* **Government: A Diarchy of Strength and Cunning**  
  Most Varr-Darg clans are led by a diarchy that reflects the dual nature of Grom-Kain's creation:  
  * **The Gral-Kor (The Stone-Hand):** The warlord of the clan, always a Gral (Orc). They are the ultimate authority in matters of war and single combat. Their position is earned and maintained by physical might and proven success on the battlefield.  
  * **The Skorn-Kli (The Sharp-Eye):** The strategic leader, almost always a Skorn (Goblin). They are master tacticians, scouts, and spymasters, responsible for planning raids, managing supplies, and identifying the enemy's weaknesses. While the Gral-Kor commands the strike, the Skorn-Kli chooses the target.  
    A clan with a weak Gral-Kor may be strong but foolish. A clan without a clever Skorn-Kli may be strong but starves. The balance is essential.  
* **Societal Structure: The Varr (The Clan)**  
  The clan is everything. There are no other structures. An individual's worth is measured entirely by their contribution to the clan's survival. Loyalty is absolute. To be exiled is a death sentence.  
* **Major Factions (The Path to Reclamation):**  
  The central political question among the Varr-Darg is how to reclaim their lost honor and land.  
  * **The Bloodied Fist Clans:** The majority faction. They believe the only path to reclamation is through war. They are aggressive, expansionist, and hold deep-seated hatred for the Varnathi. They are the clans most susceptible to Kael'Zorai's call, seeing him as the returned Grom-Kain who will lead them to righteous victory.  
  * **The Whisper-Shamans:** A smaller but influential faction of shamans who commune with the spirits of the land. They argue that the Varr-Darg's suffering is a test of spiritual endurance, not just physical might. They are wary of the "new voice" (Kael'Zorai), sensing its "silent, unnatural order" is a perversion of Grom-Kain's true will. **Malgrimm's old tribe would belong to this faction**, making his eventual return to them a complex and dangerous event.

#### B. The Novaterran Holds: The Free Holds of Novaterra

The Orkin who migrated to Novaterra have adapted their concept of *Trak* to the new world. Here, endurance is measured not just in battle, but in wealth and influence.

* **Government: The Corporate Hold**  
  The Novaterran Orkin are organized into powerful "Holds" that function more like mercenary corporations or industrial syndicates than traditional clans.  
  * **The Hold-Thane:** The CEO-Chieftain of the Hold. Leadership is not hereditary but is won through a ruthless combination of business acumen, strategic alliances, and, when necessary, brutal corporate takeovers (which can be literal).  
  * **The Board of Captains:** The Thane's council, composed of their most successful lieutenants—the captain of the mining division, the master of the mercenary company, the chief of the prospecting teams.  
* **Major Holds:**  
  * **The Iron-Grit Syndicate:** The largest and most powerful Hold, controlling vast mining operations and supplying raw materials to Gearhaven. They are pragmatic capitalists who have largely abandoned the old traditions in favor of profit.  
  * **The Shattered-Spear Company:** A legendary mercenary Hold known for its discipline and effectiveness. They take contracts from any of the Free Peoples, their loyalty sold to the highest bidder.  
  * **The Dust-Diggers:** A loose confederation of smaller, independent prospecting Holds that roam the vast deserts of Novaterra, constantly seeking new veins of ore and ancient ruins to plunder.

#### C. The Urban Orkin: The Guilds and Quarters

Within the cities of other races, Orkin have formed their own internal structures for survival and governance, operating as nations-within-nations.

* **The Orkin Quarter of Luminas (Varnathi City)**  
  * **Government: The Neighborhood Council**  
    Forced into segregated districts by Varnathi law, the Orkin of Luminas govern themselves through a council of elders representing the various clans who settled there. This council mediates disputes, organizes community defense, and acts as the official liaison to the human city guard. It is a government born of necessity, constantly navigating the prejudices of the outside world.  
  * **The Butchers' Guild & The Masons' Guild:** Within the Quarter, powerful guilds hold immense sway. The Butchers' Guild (controlling the food supply) and the Masons' Guild (controlling construction and repair) are the true economic and political power brokers, their leaders often holding more influence than the elders on the council.

#### D. The Vorok-Varr of Aerinórë: The Listener's Peace

The most radical departure from traditional Orkin society, the Vorok-Varr have transformed their creator's mandate of endurance into a philosophy of peace.

* **Government: Shamanic Guidance**  
  * **The Council of Listeners:** A council of the tribe's wisest shamans and elders. They do not command, but guide the tribe through spiritual wisdom, meditation, and communion with the spirits of Aerinórë.  
  * **The Peace-Warden:** The tribe's designated protector. This is the strongest warrior, but their role is entirely defensive. They are tasked with protecting the tribe from external threats and, more importantly, protecting the tribe from its own lingering instincts for violence. To be chosen as Peace-Warden is a solemn duty, not a position of honor.
