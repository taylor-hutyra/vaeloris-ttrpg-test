Here is the comprehensive expansion of **Epoch VI of the Second Age**.

This epoch covers the devastating global conflict known as the **Drakona War**, often called the "War of the Winged Shadow." It is a period of desperate innovation, where the arcane and the mechanical are forced into a terrifying marriage to ensure survival. The era culminates in a cataclysmic decision that reshapes the world’s geography and politics forever.

## Epoch VI: The Dragonfall (2341 – 2700 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The Drakona Empire, ruling from floating cloud-cities hidden by ancient aeromancy, descends to subjugate the surface. The fragmented nations of Vaeloris are nearly annihilated in the first decade. Survival requires the unprecedented unification of Human, Elf, Dwarf, and Gnome under the banner of the **Allied Command**. The war drives rapid technological advancement—specifically the Airship—but ends with a magical atrocity that silences the skies.

### Phase I: The Unconquered Sky (2341 – 2480 S.A.)

### 2341 S.A. – The Scouring of the North

**Summary:**  
On the first day of spring, the sky over the northern Varnathi provinces darkened. Not with clouds, but with the hulls of Drakona cloud-ships and thousands of dragon-kin warriors. The invasion began with a systematic aerial bombardment of major fortifications using "Sun-Lances"—focused beams of solar magic.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Fall of the North:** Within three months, 30% of the Varnathi Empire was occupied.  
* **The Refugee Crisis:** Millions fled south, clogging the roads and causing famine in the unoccupied lands.

### 2345 S.A. – The Treaty of Survival

**Summary:**  
Facing extinction, the leaders of the **Varnathi Empire**, **Mirennian League**, **Federation of City-States**, and **Technocracy of Gearhaven** met at the neutral city of Luminas. Putting aside centuries of hatred, they signed the *Treaty of Survival*.  
**Consequences:**

* **Formation of the Allied Command:** A unified military structure.  
* **The Resource Pool:** All national treasuries were merged into a single war fund.

### 2350 S.A. – The Establishment of the Drakona Protectorates

**Summary:**  
The Drakona did not simply bomb the northern Varnathi provinces into submission. They offered an ultimatum: "Serve us, and live better than you did under the corrupt Senate." Many provinces accepted, forming the **Principality of the Scale**, a human nation loyal to the Dragons that fought against the Allied Command.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Permanent Divide:** After the war, these humans were branded traitors. This created a permanent "North-South" divide in Varnathi culture. Northerners are seen as "Dragon-Thralls," while Southerners are "Warmongers."  
* **Resistance Cells:** Conversely, this era birthed a fierce underground resistance in the north that persists as anti-government militias in the Third Age.

### 2355 S.A. – The Sacking of Mirenn

**Summary:**  
The Drakona realized the Allied supply lines relied on the sea. A massive aerial fleet descended on Mirenn, the capital of the League. The Mirennian navy, designed for surface combat, was helpless against attacks from directly above.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Burning Harbor:** The entire Mirennian fleet was destroyed at anchor.  
* The League ceased to exist as a naval power, forcing them to pivot their entire economy toward the new "Sky-Tech."

### 2360 S.A. – The Aethelas Contribution (Elaruniel’s Engine)

**Summary:**  
The Allied resistance was failing because they could not reach the enemy high altitude. The Technocracy had airship prototypes, but they lacked an engine powerful enough to lift heavy armor. **Elaruniel**, the controversial Elven scholar, was brought in. She applied her "Dissonance Theory" to the engines, creating a volatile but immensely powerful "Mana-Combustion Drive."  
**Consequences:**

* **The First Lift-Off:** The prototype heavy cruiser *The Iron Will* launched from Gearhaven.

### 2365 S.A. – The Great Re-Scaffolding

**Summary:**  
With their harbor destroyed, the Mirennians rebuilt their capital city *upwards*. Using Dwarven steel and Elven wood-singing, they constructed massive docking towers and gantries.  
**Consequences:**

* Mirenn transformed from a sea-port to a sky-port.  
* The Mirennians applied their centuries of naval tradition to the air, becoming the premier pilots of the Alliance.

### 2380 S.A. – The Launch of the Allied Air Fleet

**Summary:**  
After twenty years of frantic industry, the Allied Command unveiled the **First Air Fleet**. Composed of 500 ships—Gearhaven Ironclads, Mirennian Sky-Galleons, and Varnathi Assault Barges—it was the largest concentration of military power in history.  
**Consequences:**

* **The War Shifts:** The Allies could finally contest air superiority.

### 2410 S.A. – The Battle of the Grey Peaks

**Summary:**  
The first major fleet-on-fleet engagement. The Allied Fleet intercepted a Drakona armada attempting to cross the mountains into the central plains. The battle raged for three days in a thunderstorm.  
**Consequences:**

* **A Pyrrhic Victory:** The Allies stopped the invasion, but lost 60% of their ships.  
* Learned Lesson: Drakona individual warriors (dragons) could shred wooden hulls. Future ships were plated in "Star-Steel" alloy.

### 2450 S.A. – The Orkin Shield (The Northern Front)

**Summary:**  
While the air war raged, the Drakona launched a ground invasion through the Barren Hills to flank the Alliance. The **Varr-Darg Orkin**, defending their ancestral home, refused to retreat. Using tunnel warfare and suicide tactics, they held the pass for a decade against superior numbers.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Orkin Recognition:** The Allied Command was forced to acknowledge the Orkin contribution, sending them supplies and advisors. This laid the groundwork for future integration.

### 2480 S.A. – The Rise of the Cloudborn Corsairs

**Summary:**  
Captain **Kaelen "Skysplitter" Vance**, a Mirennian ace, grew disillusioned with the Allied Command’s willingness to sacrifice crews. He led a mutiny, taking his squadron and vanishing into the cloud banks.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Third Faction:** The Cloudborn Corsairs raided both sides for supplies.  
* They established hidden bases on floating sky-islands (chunks of land ripped up by raw magic during the Paroxysm).

### Phase II: The War of Innovation (2481 – 2680 S.A.)

### 2485 S.A. – The Skyreach Cascade (The Dissonance Event)

**Summary:**  
During an assault on the Drakona fortress of Skyreach, the Drakona unleashed a "Resonance Shriek"—a magical frequency designed to detonate the Mana-Drives of the Allied ships.  
**Consequences:**

* **Elaruniel’s Counter:** Present at the battle, Elaruniel broadcast a "Null-Harmony" that cancelled the shriek. It saved the fleet but caused a localized magical dead-zone that persisted for a century.  
* Validated Elaruniel’s darker theories about the cost of high-energy magic.

### 2500 S.A. – The Shadewalker Betrayal

**Summary:**  
The Shadewalkers, playing both sides, sold the blueprints of the new "Mk-IV Sky-Cruiser" to the Drakona. The Drakona began fielding "Mock-Ships"—vessels that looked Allied but were filled with dragon-kin boarders.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Paranoia Purge:** The Allied Command executed hundreds of suspected spies.  
* The Shadewalkers went deep underground, becoming enemies of the state.

### 2511 S.A. – The Floating Market of Zure

**Summary:**  
A massive chunk of land ripped up by Paroxysm magic became a neutral zone in the high atmosphere. Known as **Zure**, it became the "Casablanca" of the war—a place where Allied spies, Drakona defectors, Cloudborn Corsairs, and Shadewalkers met to trade intelligence and goods.  
**Consequences:**

* **Zure’s Grave:** The market was destroyed during the Skyfall. Its location is now a massive underwater ruin rumored to hold the lost treasury of the Shadewalkers and pre-Skyfall tech.

### 2550 S.A. – The Battle of Serpent’s Pass

**Summary:**  
A masterclass in aerial tactics. Mirennian Admiral **Lyra Wind-Caller** lured a superior Drakona fleet into a narrow mountain pass. She then detonated pre-planted magical charges on the cliffs, creating an avalanche of rock and snow that swatted the Drakona ships out of the sky.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Turning Point:** The Drakona realized they were losing the tactical war. They retreated to their high-altitude Cloud Cities to regroup.

### 2595 S.A. – The Privateer Accords

**Summary:**  
Desperate for more ships, the Allied Command offered a pardon to the **Pirate Confederacy** of Serpent's Tooth. If they fitted their sea-ships with flight-cores and fought for the Alliance, they would be granted legal status.  
**Consequences:**

* The "Sky-Privateers" wreaked havoc on Drakona supply lines using hit-and-run tactics.

### 2650 S.A. – The Long Stalemate

**Summary:**  
The war settled into a grind. The Drakona held the "High Sky" (above 20,000 feet), while the Allies held the "Low Sky" and the ground. Neither side could strike the other's heart. The world was exhausted.

### 2680 S.A. – The Reconnaissance Mission (Operation: Icarus)

**Summary:**  
The Coalition of Mages authorized a suicide mission. An elite team, disguised with high-level illusions, infiltrated the primary Drakona Cloud City, **Aethelgard**.  
**Consequences:**

* **Discovery of Aeromancy:** They learned the cities were held aloft by a continuous, localized ritual of Air Magic.  
* **The Moral Crisis:** They saw Drakona nurseries, schools, and civilians. They returned with the knowledge to win the war, but also the knowledge that winning meant genocide.

### Phase III: The Skyfall (2681 – 2700 S.A.)

### 2685 S.A. – The Conclave of Luminas

**Summary:**  
The Allied leadership debated the "Icarus Option." The military generals demanded the strike. The religious leaders of the Church pleaded for mercy.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Decision:** With the Drakona preparing a "Final Descent" weapon (a magically induced ice age), the Alliance voted to strike.  
* Elaruniel was tasked with designing the ritual.

### 2688 S.A. – The Earth-Anchor Ritual (The Day the Sky Fell)

**Summary:**  
On the winter solstice, thousands of Allied mages ascended the highest peaks of Vaeloris. At noon, they cast the *Earth-Anchor*. It was a metaphysical hook that grabbed the concept of "Flight" within the Drakona’s Aeromancy matrix and violently grounded it.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Cataclysm:** The great Cloud Cities plummeted. Millions of Drakona died in the impact.  
* **Collateral Damage:** The ritual was too broad. It destabilized *all* flight magic. Every Allied Airship, every Privateer vessel, every floating island also fell.  
* **The Silence:** In one hour, the Age of Flight ended. The skies were empty.

### 2690 S.A. – The Ground War (The Mop-Up)

**Summary:**  
The survivors of the Cloud Cities crawled from the wreckage. Broken, flightless, and surrounded, the Drakona fought a desperate last stand in the ruins.  
**Consequences:**

* Brutal, close-quarters combat in the wreckage zones.

### 2700 S.A. – The Treaty of the Broken Peaks

**Summary:**  
The Drakona Empress, **Valkiss**, surrendered to the Allied Command.  
**Terms:**

* **Unconditional Surrender:** The Drakona Empire was dissolved.  
* **The Binding:** The Drakona were magically bound to the earth; their ability to naturally fly was stripped as part of the surrender ritual (though some argue this was a side effect of the Skyfall).  
* **Integration:** Mankind, weary of death, chose not to exterminate them. The Drakona were granted a "reservation" in the ruins of their greatest city, beginning a long, painful era of second-class citizenship.

**New Geopolitical Reality:**

* **The Magocracy:** The Coalition of Mages, having wielded the power to pull down the sky, emerged as the only force capable of maintaining order. They began to consolidate power, transitioning from a military alliance to a global government.  
* **The Dark Age of the Skies:** Travel returned to the speed of a horse and a sailing ship. The world shrank once again.
