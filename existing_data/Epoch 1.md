

**Note on Naming:** *The name "Valerius" (associated with the later Empire and the Third Age General) has been flagged for replacement in future epochs to avoid pop-culture crossover. For this epoch, distinct Varnathi naming conventions (Vardas, Caelius, Aetius) are used.*

# The Second Age: The Age of Mortals

## Epoch I: Echoes of Dawn (1 – 380 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The First Epoch begins in the immediate, terrifying silence following the **Great Withdrawal**. The world is scarred by the **Paroxysm** (Divine War). The gods have retreated to the metaphysical margins, leaving mortals to fend for themselves against lingering horrors, elemental instability, and the remnants of Kael’Zorai’s twisted experiments. It is an era of survival, where the first great civilizations are forged not by ambition, but by the desperate need for safety.

### 1 S.A. – The Day of Silence (The Great Withdrawal)

**Summary:**  
The precise moment the twelve minor deities receded from the physical plane is recorded by all races, though interpreted differently. The localized avatars that had led armies against Kael’Zorai simply dissolved into light, stone, or wind. In the Human settlements of the Highlands, panic ensued as protective barriers faded. In the Dwarven halls, the forges briefly went cold. The psychological impact was devastating; for the first time, mortals felt truly alone.  
**Consequences:**

* Immediate collapse of the temporary military coalitions formed during the Divine War.  
* Mass migration of human tribes toward defensible high ground.  
* The onset of the "Years of Grey," a decade of magically induced twilight caused by the lingering debris of divine combat.

### 5 S.A. – The First Harvest of Ash

**Summary:**  
With the sun dimmed by the atmospheric debris of the Paroxysm, agriculture faced total collapse. The human tribes of the lowlands, unable to rely on the Minor Deity Belthorn (The Seasonturner) to bless their crops, faced starvation. Survival was achieved only through the discovery of "Ash-Root" (a tuber resistant to low light) by Waldkyn foragers, who shared the knowledge with starving human refugees on the border of the Whispering Vales.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the first uneasy truce between Human and Waldkyn populations.  
* Shift in human diet and agriculture toward hardy, subterranean crops.

### 12 S.A. – The Reclaiming of the Deep Halls

**Summary:**  
Dwarven clans, previously scattered fighting Kael’Zorai’s aberrations on the surface, began a concerted push back into the roots of the mountains. Led by the Stone-Sworn elders, they found their ancestral halls infested with "Skitter-Gloom," spider-like remnants of the Circles Below. The reclamation was a brutal, room-by-room tunnel war fought in total darkness.  
**Consequences:**

* Development of specialized tunnel-fighting tactics.  
* The rediscovery of the First Forge, though it remained cold.

**New Creature:**

* **Name:** Skitter-Gloom  
* **Classification:** Aberration / Insectoid  
* **Description:** Blind, chitinous predators that feed on heat and magical residue. They spin webs of "shadow-silk" that dampen sound, making them silent killers in the deep dark.

### 20 S.A. – The Root-Taking of the Vales

**Summary:**  
The Waldkyn, seeking to heal the land scarred by the war, formally settled the Whispering Vales. Rejecting stone cities, they engaged in the "Great Planting," using rapid-growth rituals to shape living trees into habitation. This marked the transition of the Waldkyn from nomadic war-bands (during the Paroxysm) to a sedentary, custodial society.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the Whispering Vales as a sovereign, protected territory.  
* Creation of the first "Song-Wards" to repel unnatural creatures.

### 28 S.A. – The Founding of Vernath

**Summary:**  
In the windswept northern highlands, the philosopher-warrior **Vardas the Elder** united seven warring tribes under a single banner. Rejecting the tribal superstition that the gods had abandoned them due to sin, Vardas preached that the gods had left because mortals were ready to stand on their own. He laid the foundation stone of Vernath on a high plateau, utilizing the ruins of a First Age fortification.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the "First Compact," a constitution emphasizing duty, order, and stoicism.  
* Birth of the Varnathi cultural identity, distinct from the scattering lowland tribes.

**New Law/Custom:**

* **Name:** The First Compact  
* **Type:** Legal/Social Code  
* **Summary:** "Stone breaks louder when ignored." A code mandating that all citizens must contribute to the defense and maintenance of the city-state; idleness was classified as a crime against the state.

### 35 S.A. – The Keel-Laying of the First Wave

**Summary:**  
On the eastern coast, the ancestors of the Mirennians, separated from the highlands by the treacherous Iron-Peak Range, turned to the sea for survival. Without the direct aid of Gwynfaer (The Windweaver), they were forced to innovate. The first "Deep-Hull" ships were constructed, capable of withstanding the rougher, unregulated post-divine oceans.  
**Consequences:**

* Transition from coastal subsistence fishing to deep-sea whaling and exploration.  
* The beginning of the distinct divergence between "Highlander" (Varnathi) and "Coaster" (Mirennian) human cultures.

### 42 S.A. – The Orkin Schism

**Summary:**  
Following the loss of Grom-Kain (Kael'Zorai), the Orkin tribes fell into a bitter theological war. The **Gral** (Orcs) argued for a crusade to find and free their creator, while the **Skorn** (Goblins) argued for survival and adaptation to the new world. The conflict resulted in the "Night of Severed Bonds," where the two sub-species nearly wiped each other out before agreeing to a tenuous, pragmatic hierarchy: Strength (Gral) would lead in war, Cunning (Skorn) would lead in peace.  
**Consequences:**

* Codification of the *Varr* (Clan) structure.  
* Migration of the Orkin toward the harsh Barren Hills, pushed by Human and Dwarven expansion.

### 55 S.A. – The Completion of the Great Weaving

**Summary:**  
After two decades of labor, Elven Queen **Lelyariel** and the Mages of the Sun-Leaf Conclave completed the *Great Weaving*. Fearing the chaotic ambition of the younger races, they engaged in a month-long ritual drawing magic from moonlight. This created a permanent, illusionary barrier around the forest of Hannanórë.  
**Consequences:**

* Hannanórë effectively vanished from mortal maps, appearing only as a bank of impenetrable mist.  
* Beginning of the "Long Silence" between Elves and Humans.

**New Technology/Artifact:**

* **Name:** The Mist-Lanterns  
* **Category:** Magical Infrastructure  
* **Description:** Crystalline beacons placed along the forest border that project and anchor the Great Weaving. Only those carrying a specific harmonic token can see the path through the mist.

### 62 S.A. – The Ore-Grain Exchange

**Summary:**  
A harsh winter forced the nascent city of Vernath to seek aid. Vardas the Elder sent emissaries to the lower foothills of the Dragon’s Tooth mountains, encountering the Iron-Hand Dwarven clan. A historic trade was struck: Dwarven iron (needed for Vernath’s walls) in exchange for Human grain (needed for the Dwarves' deep larders).  
**Consequences:**

* First formal economic treaty between Humans and Dwarves.  
* Establishment of the "Trade Road," the first paved inter-city highway of the Second Age.

### 75 S.A. – The First Sighting of Giants

**Summary:**  
A Dwarven mining expedition in the northern peaks broke into a high-altitude valley and recorded the first official contact with the Giants. These colossal beings were observed tending to cyclopean ruins and massive herds of woolly beasts. The Giants ignored the Dwarves until a prospector struck a rune-carved stone, prompting a Giant to casually crush the expedition’s supply cart with a single finger gesture.  
**Consequences:**

* High King’s decree of "Wide Berth," marking Giant territory as forbidden.  
* Theories arise that Giants are the "gardeners" of the First Hands’ abandoned mountain sanctuaries.

### 80 S.A. – The Sael'anir Separation

**Summary:**  
A philosophical rift grew within Elven society. While the Cirhannim (Wood Elves) sought to wall themselves off in Hannanórë, a faction known as the Sael’anir argued that the "Song of the World" could not be heard from behind a wall. Led by the Star-Gazer **Ilphara**, they left the forest to wander the open plains and coasts, becoming the first nomadic Elves.  
**Consequences:**

* Permanent cultural split of the Elven race.  
* The Sael’anir begin to map the stars and winds, laying the groundwork for future navigation.

### 89 S.A. – The Pact of Bark and Bronze

**Summary:**  
During the maintenance of the Great Weaving, Elven wards began to destabilize due to iron ore deposits in the forest floor. A reclusive Gnome artisan, **Tinbrel Quillroot**, revealed himself to the Elven high council. He offered a solution: weaving bronze lattice-work into the roots of the border trees to ground the magic. The work was done in secret.  
**Consequences:**

* Stabilization of Hannanórë’s defenses.  
* Establishment of the first "Hollows" (Gnomish homes) beneath the roots of Elven great-trees, creating a symbiotic relationship between the two races.

### 91 S.A. – The Unification of the Dwarven Clans

**Summary:**  
Amidst the clang of hammers, Thane **Korin Barazkor** of the Iron-Hand clan was declared the first High King of a unified Kharnzarak. He achieved this not just through strength, but by controlling the supply of "Star-Metal" (meteorite iron) found in the upper peaks.  
**Consequences:**

* End of the inter-clan "Tunnel Wars."  
* Establishment of the Caste System (High Clans, Guild Clans, Gatestone Clans).

**New Artifact:**

* **Name:** The Crown of the Mountain  
* **Category:** Regalia / Artifact  
* **Description:** A heavy geometric crown forged from seven distinct metals, representing the seven founding clans. It is said to heat up if the wearer speaks a lie while sitting on the Adamant Throne.

### 110 S.A. – The Stone-Road Projects

**Summary:**  
Vernath, now a bustling city-state, began a massive infrastructure project to pave the trade routes through the dangerous highlands. Using Dwarven consulting engineers and human labor, they built elevated roads with fortified waystations every day’s march to protect against beasts.  
**Consequences:**

* Drastic reduction in merchant mortality rates.  
* Rapid economic expansion of Vernath as the trade hub of the north.

### 125 S.A. – The Season of Breaking (Orkin Raids)

**Summary:**  
Pushed out of their ancestral hunting grounds by Human agricultural expansion, loose coalitions of Orkin clans launched a coordinated assault on the outlying settlements of Vernath and Dwarven surface outposts. Unlike previous raids, these were strategic, targeting granaries and water supplies.  
**Consequences:**

* Destruction of three major human frontier towns.  
* Realization among the "Civilized Races" that the Orkin were a strategic threat, not just a nuisance.

### 145 S.A. – The Charting of the Broken Coast

**Summary:**  
Mirennian pathfinders completed the first accurate maps of the treacherous eastern coastline. **Captain Hanno the Wave-Reader** discovered the "Safe-Harbor Currents," a series of reliable water flows that allowed ships to navigate the jagged reefs safely.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the first permanent Mirennian trade posts, which would later grow into the great Port Cities.  
* New Vocabulary: *Sárinth* (Elvish loan-word used by sailors for "Safe Harbor").

### 170 S.A. – The Whispering Plague

**Summary:**  
A minor magical echo from a sealed Scar of Dissonance leaked into the water table near the Human/Dwarven border. Those who drank the water fell into comas, muttering in the tongue of the Abyss. It was not a disease, but a magical contamination.  
**Consequences:**

* First collaboration between Dwarven Runesmiths and Human hedge-healers to purify a water source.  
* Establishment of the "Warding Corps" in Vernath, the precursors to professional monster hunters.

### 195 S.A. – The First Council of Races

**Summary:**  
Fearing the growing coordination of Orkin warbands (the "Season of Breaking" having become a yearly occurrence), the nature-attuned Waldkyn mediated a council. Delegates from Vernath (Human), Hannanórë (Elf), and Kharnzarak (Dwarf) met at the neutral Stone-Circle of Oth.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Pact of the Wardens:** Creation of an elite, multi-racial force to patrol borders.  
* **The Exclusion:** The Orkin were deliberately excluded. Elves cited their chaotic nature; Dwarves cited blood-feuds. This formally cast the Orkin as the "Common Enemy," cementing their role as outsiders for millennia.

**New Organization:**

* **Name:** The Wardens of the Wild  
* **Type:** Inter-racial Paramilitary Order  
* **Purpose:** To patrol the spaces between civilizations and hunt remnants of the Paroxysm.

### 210 S.A. – The Long March (Orkin Retreat)

**Summary:**  
Facing the combined might of the Wardens and the organized armies of Vernath and Kharnzarak, the major Orkin clans realized they could not hold the fertile valleys. The Gral-Kor (Warlords) ordered a mass retreat into the inhospitable Barren Hills.  
**Consequences:**

* The Barren Hills became the permanent, fortified homeland of the Varr-Darg.  
* Orkin culture shifted entirely to a "siege mentality," viewing all other races as existential threats.

### 230 S.A. – Discovery of the Silver Veins

**Summary:**  
Imperial prospectors (Human) discovered massive veins of silver in the Central Mountains, territory nominally claimed by Dwarves but unoccupied. A tense standoff ensued until the **Treaty of Coin** was signed: Humans could mine the silver, but Dwarves would refine it and take a percentage.  
**Consequences:**

* Precursor to the founding of the city of Argentum.  
* Introduction of a standardized silver currency, replacing the barter system in the region.

**New Law/Treaty:**

* **Name:** The Treaty of Coin  
* **Type:** Economic Accord  
* **Signatories:** The City-State of Vernath, High King of Kharnzarak.  
* **Summary:** Established mining rights and standardized the weight of the "Vernathi Silver Mark."

### 250 S.A. – The Warning of the Silent Canopy

**Summary:**  
As Human logging operations began cutting into the ancient woods bordering Hannanórë, the Cirhannim Elves sent a single, white-robed emissary to the court of Vernath. The emissary delivered a prophecy: "The forest breathes. The stone remembers. Should you deafen the world with the clamor of your ambition, you will one day find yourselves screaming into silence."  
**Consequences:**

* The warning was dismissed by the ruling Council of Vernath as arrogance.  
* Human expansion continued, but with a lingering superstition among the common woodcutters.

### 275 S.A. – Rise of the Merchant Houses

**Summary:**  
In the coastal settlements, the most successful ship captains began to consolidate power, forming the first Merchant Houses. They built fortified warehouses and private militias, effectively becoming the governing bodies of the coast.  
**Consequences:**

* Shift from tribal/village leadership to an oligarchy of wealth.  
* Founding of the **Golden Compass Syndicate**, the first major trade guild.

### 290 S.A. – The Rise of the "Thul" (The Broken)

**Summary:**  
In Kharnzarak, the rigid caste system began to fracture. Dwarves who could not meet the perfectionist standards of their guilds turned to crime or black-market smithing. Crime rates spiked in the deep halls, leading to harsh crackdowns by the King’s Guard.  
**Consequences:**

* Overcrowding of the prison-mines.  
* Growing resentment among the lower castes, planting seeds for the eventual Thulkorûn exile.

### 315 S.A. – The Southern Push

**Summary:**  
Vernath, its population booming due to trade and safe roads, began colonizing the southern lowlands. These lands were wild, filled with Primalkyn anomalies. The expansion was bloody, a war against the land itself.  
**Consequences:**

* Founding of military outposts that would eventually become the fortress-city of **Castellum**.  
* First systematic study and categorization of Primalkyn manifestations by Human scholars.

### 340 S.A. – The Emergence of Echo-Beasts

**Summary:**  
Strange, semi-translucent monsters began attacking travelers near the sealed Scars of Dissonance. These creatures mimicked the voices of their victims. They were the heralds of a growing power in the metaphysical undercurrents.  
**Consequences:**

* Panic on the trade roads.  
* The Wardens of the Wild suffered heavy casualties, realizing their conventional weapons were ineffective against these semi-corporeal threats.

### 365 S.A. – Formalization of the Thulkorûn

**Summary:**  
To deal with the "Thul" crisis (see 290 S.A.), the High King of Kharnzarak instituted the **Ritual of Shattering**. Rather than execution or imprisonment, criminals and oath-breakers were publicly stripped of their clan name, their hammer shattered, and exiled to the surface.  
**Consequences:**

* Creation of the **Thulkorûn** ("The Broken-Crafted") as a distinct demographic.  
* Influx of highly skilled Dwarven laborers into Human cities, accelerating Human technological development.

**New Custom:**

* **Name:** The Ritual of Shattering  
* **Type:** Judicial Punishment  
* **Summary:** The public breaking of a Dwarf's forging hammer. Without it, they are considered "spiritually dead" to the Stone.

### 375 S.A. – The Gathering of Heroes

**Summary:**  
The "Echo-Beasts" coalesced into a unified threat led by the **Echo-Lord**, a psychic entity occupying a massive Scar of Dissonance. A call was sent out across the continent. Heroes from all races—Human knights, Dwarven defenders, Elven archers, and Thulkorûn mercenaries—gathered at the Scar.  
**Consequences:**

* The first time the races fought side-by-side as equals, rather than just allies of convenience.  
* Appearance of the **Avatar of Bjalmund** (The Shieldbearer), a minor deity manifestation, to lead the charge.

### 380 S.A. – The Defeat of the Echo-Lord

**Summary:**  
In the final battle of the epoch, the coalition of heroes engaged the Echo-Lord. The battle raged for three days. Bjalmund the Shieldbearer sacrificed his physical avatar form to trap the Echo-Lord’s essence inside a shield of crystallized despair. The Scar was sealed by the combined magic of Elven weavers and Dwarven runesmiths.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of the Age of Dawn:** With the last great threat of the Paroxysm defeated, the world was deemed "safe."  
* **The Final Silence:** Following Bjalmund's sacrifice, the remaining minor deities withdrew their avatars completely, marking the true transition to the Age of Mortals.  
* **The Hero’s Mound:** Establishment of a great monument at the battle site, a pilgrimage point for the Wardens.
