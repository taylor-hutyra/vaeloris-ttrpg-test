### Dwarven Lore: The Deep Foundation

#### I. The Dwarven View of Creation

To the Dwarves of Vaeloris, creation was not a song but a strike, a structure, and a weight. The world was formed by the Pulse of **Aenûr-Khadaz**, the Deep Shaper, who awakened the bones of existence with a rhythm that rippled through the bedrock of the void. Yet even He, in His unmatched power, recognized the need for detail and stability. From the deep echoes of that First Rhythm came the **Four Pillars of Craft**, eternal forces who shaped the world into form.

* **Tharnûm** – Stone (known elsewhere as the Mason)  
* **Bronrûn** – Growth and Timber (known elsewhere as the Carpenter or Woodsman)  
* **Farrazul** – Depth and Gem (known elsewhere as the Jeweler)  
* **Elmura** – Firelight and Vision (known elsewhere as the Painter)

Dwarves hold that they were formed by the enduring alliance of **Tharnûm** and **Farrazul**, shaped from stone and soul-deep ore, carved beneath the world’s crust, and imbued with rhythm, purpose, and permanence. They do not worship these Pillars as gods, but honor them as **Fundamentals**—primal truths reflected in Dwarven life, architecture, clan, and law.

Of the Great Unbalancing—the event others call the Paroxysm—the Dwarves speak cautiously. They call it **Durâk Thul** (the Fracture of Law), a time when the pattern cracked. Ancient stone records speak of an additional force, the **Ulzammar**—not a being, but a concept that sought to refine creation into stillness. It is said to have attempted to seal the fires, stop the winds, and bind the stars in numerical lattice. It did not fail—but it was undone. The name Ulzammar has been chipped away in many Dwarven vaults, and elder runes are lost. To speak of it aloud is discouraged in the deep halls, for it is said that even its syllables draw echoes from the Circles Below.

#### III. Major Dwarven Cultural Variants

While all Dwarves share a common origin and revere the Four Pillars, millennia of ideological disputes, exiles, and migrations have forged three distinct cultural groups.

**1\. The Nûm-Khazalûn ("The Stone-Sworn" of the Mainland)**  
The Nûm-Khazalûn are the keepers of tradition, the deeply orthodox clans who reside in the great mountain kingdom of **Kharnzarak**. They believe that Dwarven craft reached its apex in the First Age and that their sacred duty is to preserve those ancient techniques and societal structures without deviation. They are deeply suspicious of mortal magic, which they view as a chaotic and unreliable force that defies the structured, rhythmic nature of true creation.

* **Core Philosophy:** Preservation. Their highest virtue is **Thralûk** (Endurance), the ability to maintain their cultural and structural integrity against the pressures of time and change.  
* **Society:** Rigidly hierarchical, governed by ancient laws carved into the Stone-Lays. Clan and craft are inextricably linked.  
* **View of Others:** They see the Thulkorûn as common criminals who broke their oaths, a source of shame. They view the Elm-korin with a mixture of pity and contempt, as dangerous idealists who abandoned the purity of the stone for the fleeting chaos of magic. They have a quiet, almost invisible respect for Gnomes, whom they call the **Nûm-kith** ("Stone-kin"), recognizing them as fellow children of a Stone-based Pillar.

**2\. The Thulkorûn ("The Broken-Crafted" of Novaterra)**  
The Thulkorûn are the descendants of Dwarves exiled from Kharnzarak for common crimes—theft, oath-breaking, violence. Banished from their clans and cast out from the deep halls, they sought a new beginning on the free continent of Novaterra. They are pragmatists and survivors, often working as mercenaries, laborers, and artisans in the burgeoning cities. They have largely abandoned the rigid honor codes of their ancestors, but retain their innate skill with stone and metal. Brenna is a Thulkorûn, though her exile was for philosophical, not criminal, reasons.

* **Core Philosophy:** Survival. Having had their clan identity stripped from them, their loyalty is to their contracts, their comrades, and their coin.  
* **Society:** A scattered diaspora with no central authority. They form loose-knit communities in the underbellies of Novaterran cities or serve in mercenary companies.  
* **View of Others:** They view the Nûm-Khazalûn with bitter resentment for casting them out. They often see the Elm-korin as naive idealists but will gladly work for them if the pay is right. They have little to no contact with or knowledge of Gnomes.

**3\. The Elm-korin ("The Flame-Struck" of Gearhaven)**  
The Elm-korin are the descendants of a reformist faction exiled during the Second Age for their heretical belief that mortal magic (**Elm-ûl**, the "Truth-Flame") was not chaos, but a tool to achieve the perfect, logical order espoused by the **Ulzammar**. They believe the other First Hands failed by stopping short of true perfection. They founded the city of **Gearhaven** alongside human mages, seeing technomancy as the ultimate synthesis of Dwarven craft and magical law. Brenna's philosophy aligns closely with theirs, making her a Thulkorûn by law but an Elm-korin in spirit.

* **Core Philosophy:** Perfection. They seek to complete the work the Ulzammar began, using logic, magic, and technology to create a world of flawless order and efficiency.  
* **Society:** Meritocratic, innovative, and highly organized. Their lives are dedicated to the Great Work of perfecting their city and its Clank servitors.  
* **View of Others:** They view the Nûm-Khazalûn as fearful traditionalists who cling to an incomplete design. They see the Thulkorûn as a reminder of the personal failings their ordered society seeks to eliminate. They are fascinated by the Gnomes' intricate work, seeing it as a primitive but promising form of their own philosophy.

#### IV. Common Dwarvish Terms and Sayings

**Divine Pillars & Concepts**

* **Tharnûm, Bronrûn, Farrazul, Elmura** – The Four Pillars of Craft.  
* **Ulzammar** – The Sealed Concept; the forbidden force of perfect order (Kael'Zorai).  
* **Durâk Thul** – The Fracture of Law; Dwarven name for the Paroxysm.

**Cultural and Mythic Terms**

* **Stone-Lays** – Mythic verses carved in sacred halls.  
* **Kharnazul** – Oath-Bond (sacred vow of unity).  
* **Rûz** – Clan or hearth-circle.  
* **Thralûk** – Endurance, perseverance, the quality of lasting.  
* **Nûm-Khazalûn** – "The Stone-Sworn"; traditionalist Dwarves.  
* **Thulkorûn** – "The Broken-Crafted"; exiled criminals and their descendants.  
* **Elm-korin** – "The Flame-Struck"; exiled magical purists of Gearhaven.  
* **Nûm-kith** – "Stone-kin"; a rare, respectful term for Gnomes.

**Common Sayings and Greetings (in Dwarvish)**

* “Nûm zalgûl.” – "Stone does not forget." (Said when recording events or delivering justice.)  
* “Kor zul elm.” – "Hammer before flame." (Said to prioritize structure before beauty or emotion.)  
* “Dûm baraz.” – "May your forge hold." (A blessing for stability, family, and strength.)  
* “Khazûn dur.” – "We chisel in silence." (A phrase signaling reverence, secrecy, or sacred duty.)  
* “Barak-nûm.” – "Stone be with you." (A formal greeting.)  
* “Zulgûl-dûm.” – "Until the echo returns." (A farewell expressing hope to meet again.)

#### V. Root Word Glossary (Selected Dwarvish Elements)

To ensure linguistic clarity and consistency, here is a compiled glossary of key Dwarvish roots and how they form complex terms:

**Root Words and Meanings**

* **barak** – be, remain, endure (used in blessings)  
* **baraz** – hold, strength, integrity  
* **bron** – growth, rootwork, shaping  
* **durâk** – law, pattern, structure  
* **elm** – flame, light, vision, forge (also magic, for the Elm-korin)  
* **farra / zarr** – gem, crystal, brilliance  
* **gul / zul** – deep, echo, abyss  
* **kharn** – clan, tribe, root  
* **khazûn** – secrecy, ritual work, sacred silence  
* **kith** \- kin, related folk  
* **kor** – chisel, strike, craft-mark  
* **nûm** – stone, foundation, memory  
* **rûz** – oath, union, hearth-circle  
* **thralûk** – endurance, persistence, legacy  
* **thul** – breaking, shatter, undoing  
* **ûl / ul** – concept, truth, essence  
* **zal** – memory, impression, mark  
* **zammar** – stillness, silence, perfect containment  
* **zarack** – hall, home, refuge  
* **zelgûl / zulgûl** – echo, return, rebounding call

These roots are foundational in Dwarvish word construction, reflecting their values of memory, structure, and endurance. Terms are often compounded for ritual use or poetic impact, and always spoken with intentional rhythm.

### VI. Dwarven Political Structures and Kingdoms

The Dwarven diaspora, forged by schism and exile, has resulted in political systems as varied and unyielding as the stone from which they were born. While all share a common ancestral memory of Kharnzarak, their paths have led to vastly different interpretations of law, leadership, and the very concept of a clan.

#### A. The Nûm-Khazalûn of the Mainland: The Stone-Sworn Hegemony

The great mountain kingdom of Kharnzarak is the heart of Dwarven tradition and the seat of its most ancient and powerful political body.

* **Government: Hereditary Monarchy under the Stone-Lays**  
  * **The High King:** The absolute monarch of Kharnzarak, ruling from the Adamant Throne. The current ruler is High King Thorgar VII of the Barazkor Clan. His authority is absolute, but he is bound by the sacred laws carved into the *Dûm-Gûl*, the deep halls where the first kings ruled.  
  * **The Stone-Lay Council:** A council of nine clan-lords who serve as the High King's advisors, high court, and legislative body. While they cannot override a royal decree, their unanimous opposition can delay it, forcing the king to justify his decision before the ancestral tombs—a grave political act. It was this council that ratified Brenna's exile.  
* **Societal Structure: The Rigid Caste of Craft and Clan**  
  Kharnzarak society is a pyramid. At the top are the **High Clans**, noble families whose lineage is tied directly to legendary First Age artisans and warriors. Below them are the numerous **Guild Clans**, families whose identity and social standing are defined by their hereditary craft. At the bottom are the **Gatestone Clans**, who perform the most menial and dangerous labor. Upward mobility is virtually nonexistent.  
* **Major Clans & Factions:**  
  * **The Barazkor Clan (The Iron-Gavels):** The Royal Clan. Fiercely traditionalist and militant, they are the wardens of Kharnzarak's military and its laws. They view any deviation from the Stone-Lays as a threat to their very existence. Brenna's primary rival, Lord Durin Stone-Fist, is a ranking member and the king's most trusted enforcer.  
  * **The Farrazûl Clan (The Gem-Cutters):** The wealthiest of the High Clans, their power derives not from armies but from their control over the kingdom's vast gem and precious metal mines. They are rivals to the Barazkor, advocating for more pragmatic policies like limited trade to increase their wealth. They are outwardly traditional but inwardly opportunistic.  
  * **The Gulgrym Clan (The Deep-Miners):** The most powerful of the Guild Clans, representing the miners who form the backbone of Kharnzarak's economy. They are a downtrodden but proud people, and their clan leaders often harbor deep resentments toward the High Clans. They are a potential source of populist unrest.  
  * **The Bronrûn Legacy (The Secret Reformists):** A clandestine society of artisans, engineers, and scholars who secretly revere the Pillar of Growth, Bronrûn. They believe Kharnzarak's stagnation will be its doom. They meet in hidden workshops, studying forbidden Elm-korin schematics and Gnomish designs. They are the only hope for radical change from within the mountain.

#### B. The Elm-korin of Novaterra: The Technocracy of Gearhaven

Gearhaven is the antithesis of Kharnzarak: a city-state driven by progress, logic, and the heretical belief in perfecting creation through magical technology.

* **Government: Technocratic Meritocracy**  
  * **The Cog-Council:** The ruling body of Gearhaven, composed of nine Guild-Masters elected based on their proven contributions to the city's progress. Seats are not permanent; a Guild-Master can be challenged and replaced if a subordinate presents a more innovative or efficient design.  
  * **The Arch-Artisan:** The head of the council, elected from among its members to serve as the city's leader. Their role is to set the city's "Grand Design"—its long-term goals for research and development.  
* **Societal Structure: Guild-Based Meritocracy**  
  There are no clans in Gearhaven, only guilds. An individual's status is determined entirely by their skill, ingenuity, and the success of their projects. This creates a relentless, high-pressure environment where collaboration is a tool for advancement and failure can mean demotion to a menial role.  
* **Major Factions (The Core Ideological Schism):**  
  * **The Mainframe Purity:** The dominant faction, led by the human Archmage Kaelen's former mentor, Elaruniel's rival. They are zealous believers in the Elm-korin philosophy and seek to create a world of perfect, sterile order. They are the architects of the Blight Obelisk project, seeing it as the ultimate tool to "bless" the world with their god's silent, perfect logic.  
  * **The Symbiotic Weave:** A Gnomish-led and influenced faction that includes many Dwarven and Human bio-engineers. They argue that pure logic is a brittle foundation and that true perfection lies in the fusion of the organic and the mechanical. Brenna's clank, Rivet, with his Gnomish-inspired core, is a product of their philosophy. They are the first to detect the dangerous "null-harmonic" of the Obelisk project and oppose it, framing the conflict as a struggle for the city's very soul.

#### C. The Thulkorûn: The Broken-Crafted Diaspora

The Thulkorûn have no single government. Stripped of their clans and heritage, their political structures are pragmatic, transient, and built around survival and profit.

* **Structure 1: The Mercenary Charter (e.g., The Grit-Shields)**  
  In the free cities of Novaterra, many Thulkorûn form mercenary companies. These are governed by a charter, a legal document that outlines pay, responsibilities, and the Captain's authority. Loyalty is to the contract, not a king or a god. Brenna's practical, no-nonsense attitude is a hallmark of this survivalist mindset.  
* **Structure 2: The Under-Guilds of Aurumport**  
  On the mainland, many Thulkorûn have found a niche in the criminal underworlds of port cities. They operate as enforcers, arms-smiths, and engineers for thieves' guilds, their loyalty bought and paid for. This is a potential source of conflict or contacts for Kaelan and Milo during their early adventures.  
* **Structure 3: The Freeholds**  
  In the untamed wilds of Novaterra, some Thulkorûn have established small, independent, and heavily fortified settlements called Freeholds. These are governed by a single, powerful leader—often the founder—who rules by strength and reputation. They are isolationist and deeply suspicious of all outsiders.

#### D. Other Notable Dwarven Enclaves

The world holds more than just the three great factions. Millennia of history have left other, smaller groups scattered across Vaeloris.

* **The Frost-Forge of Bjalmund's Spire (The Zelgûl-Kharn)**  
  High in the Dragon's Tooth mountains lies a fortress-monastery of Nûm-Khazalûn who were cut off from Kharnzarak centuries ago. Known as the Zelgûl-Kharn (The Echo-Clan), they have developed a unique culture blending traditional Dwarven ancestor-worship with a deep, pragmatic reverence for the fury of the mountain. They are led by a **Runespeaker**, a shamanic leader who interprets the will of the peaks through the cracking of ice and the groaning of stone. They could be vital allies or formidable obstacles for the party when they seek the Forge-Sanctuary in Book 3\.  
* **The Bronze-Crest Clans of the Southern Coasts**  
  A small but prosperous collection of coastal city-states founded by Nûm-Khazalûn who rejected the isolationism of the High King. They are master shipwrights and naval engineers, their "stone-ships" plated with enchanted bronze. They are governed by a **Council of Captain-Thanes**, where naval power and mercantile wealth determine political standing. They maintain cordial relations with the Mirennian League and are far more open-minded than their mountain-bound cousins.  
* **The Silent Weavers of the Deepwood**  
  Hidden in a secluded mountain range bordering the Gnomish forests is a small, secretive enclave where a single Dwarven clan and a guild of Rock Gnomes have lived in symbiosis for a thousand years. Here, they practice the **Art of the Quiet Weave**, a unique form of magic that stitches protective wards and runes directly into living stone and wood. They are governed by a dual council of a **Dwarven Oath-Keeper** and a **Gnomish Geo-Weaver**. Their existence is a closely guarded secret, known only to a few, and they are the creators of some of the most powerful defensive artifacts in the world.
