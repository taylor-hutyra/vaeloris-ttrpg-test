# The Second Age: The Age of Mortals

## Epoch I: Echoes of Dawn (1 – 380 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The First Epoch begins in the immediate, terrifying silence following the **Great Withdrawal**. The world is scarred by the **Paroxysm** (Divine War). The gods have retreated to the metaphysical margins, leaving mortals to fend for themselves against lingering horrors, elemental instability, and the remnants of Kael’Zorai’s twisted experiments. It is an era of survival, where the first great civilizations are forged not by ambition, but by the desperate need for safety.

|  |
| :---- |

### 1 S.A. – The Day of Silence (The Great Withdrawal)

**Summary:**  
The precise moment the twelve minor deities receded from the physical plane is recorded by all races, though interpreted differently. The localized avatars that had led armies against Kael’Zorai simply dissolved into light, stone, or wind. In the Human settlements of the Highlands, panic ensued as protective barriers faded. In the Dwarven halls, the forges briefly went cold. The psychological impact was devastating; for the first time, mortals felt truly alone.  
**Consequences:**

* Immediate collapse of the temporary military coalitions formed during the Divine War.  
* Mass migration of human tribes toward defensible high ground.  
* The onset of the "Years of Grey," a decade of magically induced twilight caused by the lingering debris of divine combat.

### 5 S.A. – The First Harvest of Ash

**Summary:**  
With the sun dimmed by the atmospheric debris of the Paroxysm, agriculture faced total collapse. The human tribes of the lowlands, unable to rely on the Minor Deity Belthorn (The Seasonturner) to bless their crops, faced starvation. Survival was achieved only through the discovery of "Ash-Root" (a tuber resistant to low light) by Waldkyn foragers, who shared the knowledge with starving human refugees on the border of the Whispering Vales.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the first uneasy truce between Human and Waldkyn populations.  
* Shift in human diet and agriculture toward hardy, subterranean crops.

### 12 S.A. – The Reclaiming of the Deep Halls

**Summary:**  
Dwarven clans, previously scattered fighting Kael’Zorai’s aberrations on the surface, began a concerted push back into the roots of the mountains. Led by the Stone-Sworn elders, they found their ancestral halls infested with "Skitter-Gloom," spider-like remnants of the Circles Below. The reclamation was a brutal, room-by-room tunnel war fought in total darkness.  
**Consequences:**

* Development of specialized tunnel-fighting tactics.  
* The rediscovery of the First Forge, though it remained cold.

**New Creature:**

* **Name:** Skitter-Gloom  
* **Classification:** Aberration / Insectoid  
* **Description:** Blind, chitinous predators that feed on heat and magical residue. They spin webs of "shadow-silk" that dampen sound, making them silent killers in the deep dark.

### 20 S.A. – The Root-Taking of the Vales

**Summary:**  
The Waldkyn, seeking to heal the land scarred by the war, formally settled the Whispering Vales. Rejecting stone cities, they engaged in the "Great Planting," using rapid-growth rituals to shape living trees into habitation. This marked the transition of the Waldkyn from nomadic war-bands (during the Paroxysm) to a sedentary, custodial society.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the Whispering Vales as a sovereign, protected territory.  
* Creation of the first "Song-Wards" to repel unnatural creatures.

### 28 S.A. – The Founding of Vernath

**Summary:**  
In the windswept northern highlands, the philosopher-warrior **Vardas the Elder** united seven warring tribes under a single banner. Rejecting the tribal superstition that the gods had abandoned them due to sin, Vardas preached that the gods had left because mortals were ready to stand on their own. He laid the foundation stone of Vernath on a high plateau, utilizing the ruins of a First Age fortification.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the "First Compact," a constitution emphasizing duty, order, and stoicism.  
* Birth of the Varnathi cultural identity, distinct from the scattering lowland tribes.

**New Law/Custom:**

* **Name:** The First Compact  
* **Type:** Legal/Social Code  
* **Summary:** "Stone breaks louder when ignored." A code mandating that all citizens must contribute to the defense and maintenance of the city-state; idleness was classified as a crime against the state.

### 35 S.A. – The Keel-Laying of the First Wave

**Summary:**  
On the eastern coast, the ancestors of the Mirennians, separated from the highlands by the treacherous Iron-Peak Range, turned to the sea for survival. Without the direct aid of Gwynfaer (The Windweaver), they were forced to innovate. The first "Deep-Hull" ships were constructed, capable of withstanding the rougher, unregulated post-divine oceans.  
**Consequences:**

* Transition from coastal subsistence fishing to deep-sea whaling and exploration.  
* The beginning of the distinct divergence between "Highlander" (Varnathi) and "Coaster" (Mirennian) human cultures.

### 42 S.A. – The Orkin Schism

**Summary:**  
Following the loss of Grom-Kain (Kael'Zorai), the Orkin tribes fell into a bitter theological war. The **Gral** (Orcs) argued for a crusade to find and free their creator, while the **Skorn** (Goblins) argued for survival and adaptation to the new world. The conflict resulted in the "Night of Severed Bonds," where the two sub-species nearly wiped each other out before agreeing to a tenuous, pragmatic hierarchy: Strength (Gral) would lead in war, Cunning (Skorn) would lead in peace.  
**Consequences:**

* Codification of the *Varr* (Clan) structure.  
* Migration of the Orkin toward the harsh Barren Hills, pushed by Human and Dwarven expansion.

### 55 S.A. – The Completion of the Great Weaving

**Summary:**  
After two decades of labor, Elven Queen **Lelyariel** and the Mages of the Sun-Leaf Conclave completed the *Great Weaving*. Fearing the chaotic ambition of the younger races, they engaged in a month-long ritual drawing magic from moonlight. This created a permanent, illusionary barrier around the forest of Hannanórë.  
**Consequences:**

* Hannanórë effectively vanished from mortal maps, appearing only as a bank of impenetrable mist.  
* Beginning of the "Long Silence" between Elves and Humans.

**New Technology/Artifact:**

* **Name:** The Mist-Lanterns  
* **Category:** Magical Infrastructure  
* **Description:** Crystalline beacons placed along the forest border that project and anchor the Great Weaving. Only those carrying a specific harmonic token can see the path through the mist.

### 62 S.A. – The Ore-Grain Exchange

**Summary:**  
A harsh winter forced the nascent city of Vernath to seek aid. Vardas the Elder sent emissaries to the lower foothills of the Dragon’s Tooth mountains, encountering the Iron-Hand Dwarven clan. A historic trade was struck: Dwarven iron (needed for Vernath’s walls) in exchange for Human grain (needed for the Dwarves' deep larders).  
**Consequences:**

* First formal economic treaty between Humans and Dwarves.  
* Establishment of the "Trade Road," the first paved inter-city highway of the Second Age.

### 75 S.A. – The First Sighting of Giants

**Summary:**  
A Dwarven mining expedition in the northern peaks broke into a high-altitude valley and recorded the first official contact with the Giants. These colossal beings were observed tending to cyclopean ruins and massive herds of woolly beasts. The Giants ignored the Dwarves until a prospector struck a rune-carved stone, prompting a Giant to casually crush the expedition’s supply cart with a single finger gesture.  
**Consequences:**

* High King’s decree of "Wide Berth," marking Giant territory as forbidden.  
* Theories arise that Giants are the "gardeners" of the First Hands’ abandoned mountain sanctuaries.

### 80 S.A. – The Sael'anir Separation

**Summary:**  
A philosophical rift grew within Elven society. While the Cirhannim (Wood Elves) sought to wall themselves off in Hannanórë, a faction known as the Sael’anir argued that the "Song of the World" could not be heard from behind a wall. Led by the Star-Gazer **Ilphara**, they left the forest to wander the open plains and coasts, becoming the first nomadic Elves.  
**Consequences:**

* Permanent cultural split of the Elven race.  
* The Sael’anir begin to map the stars and winds, laying the groundwork for future navigation.

### 89 S.A. – The Pact of Bark and Bronze

**Summary:**  
During the maintenance of the Great Weaving, Elven wards began to destabilize due to iron ore deposits in the forest floor. A reclusive Gnome artisan, **Tinbrel Quillroot**, revealed himself to the Elven high council. He offered a solution: weaving bronze lattice-work into the roots of the border trees to ground the magic. The work was done in secret.  
**Consequences:**

* Stabilization of Hannanórë’s defenses.  
* Establishment of the first "Hollows" (Gnomish homes) beneath the roots of Elven great-trees, creating a symbiotic relationship between the two races.

### 91 S.A. – The Unification of the Dwarven Clans

**Summary:**  
Amidst the clang of hammers, Thane **Korin Barazkor** of the Iron-Hand clan was declared the first High King of a unified Kharnzarak. He achieved this not just through strength, but by controlling the supply of "Star-Metal" (meteorite iron) found in the upper peaks.  
**Consequences:**

* End of the inter-clan "Tunnel Wars."  
* Establishment of the Caste System (High Clans, Guild Clans, Gatestone Clans).

**New Artifact:**

* **Name:** The Crown of the Mountain  
* **Category:** Regalia / Artifact  
* **Description:** A heavy geometric crown forged from seven distinct metals, representing the seven founding clans. It is said to heat up if the wearer speaks a lie while sitting on the Adamant Throne.

### 110 S.A. – The Stone-Road Projects

**Summary:**  
Vernath, now a bustling city-state, began a massive infrastructure project to pave the trade routes through the dangerous highlands. Using Dwarven consulting engineers and human labor, they built elevated roads with fortified waystations every day’s march to protect against beasts.  
**Consequences:**

* Drastic reduction in merchant mortality rates.  
* Rapid economic expansion of Vernath as the trade hub of the north.

### 125 S.A. – The Season of Breaking (Orkin Raids)

**Summary:**  
Pushed out of their ancestral hunting grounds by Human agricultural expansion, loose coalitions of Orkin clans launched a coordinated assault on the outlying settlements of Vernath and Dwarven surface outposts. Unlike previous raids, these were strategic, targeting granaries and water supplies.  
**Consequences:**

* Destruction of three major human frontier towns.  
* Realization among the "Civilized Races" that the Orkin were a strategic threat, not just a nuisance.

### 145 S.A. – The Charting of the Broken Coast

**Summary:**  
Mirennian pathfinders completed the first accurate maps of the treacherous eastern coastline. **Captain Hanno the Wave-Reader** discovered the "Safe-Harbor Currents," a series of reliable water flows that allowed ships to navigate the jagged reefs safely.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the first permanent Mirennian trade posts, which would later grow into the great Port Cities.  
* New Vocabulary: *Sárinth* (Elvish loan-word used by sailors for "Safe Harbor").

### 170 S.A. – The Whispering Plague

**Summary:**  
A minor magical echo from a sealed Scar of Dissonance leaked into the water table near the Human/Dwarven border. Those who drank the water fell into comas, muttering in the tongue of the Abyss. It was not a disease, but a magical contamination.  
**Consequences:**

* First collaboration between Dwarven Runesmiths and Human hedge-healers to purify a water source.  
* Establishment of the "Warding Corps" in Vernath, the precursors to professional monster hunters.

### 195 S.A. – The First Council of Races

**Summary:**  
Fearing the growing coordination of Orkin warbands (the "Season of Breaking" having become a yearly occurrence), the nature-attuned Waldkyn mediated a council. Delegates from Vernath (Human), Hannanórë (Elf), and Kharnzarak (Dwarf) met at the neutral Stone-Circle of Oth.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Pact of the Wardens:** Creation of an elite, multi-racial force to patrol borders.  
* **The Exclusion:** The Orkin were deliberately excluded. Elves cited their chaotic nature; Dwarves cited blood-feuds. This formally cast the Orkin as the "Common Enemy," cementing their role as outsiders for millennia.

**New Organization:**

* **Name:** The Wardens of the Wild  
* **Type:** Inter-racial Paramilitary Order  
* **Purpose:** To patrol the spaces between civilizations and hunt remnants of the Paroxysm.

### 210 S.A. – The Long March (Orkin Retreat)

**Summary:**  
Facing the combined might of the Wardens and the organized armies of Vernath and Kharnzarak, the major Orkin clans realized they could not hold the fertile valleys. The Gral-Kor (Warlords) ordered a mass retreat into the inhospitable Barren Hills.  
**Consequences:**

* The Barren Hills became the permanent, fortified homeland of the Varr-Darg.  
* Orkin culture shifted entirely to a "siege mentality," viewing all other races as existential threats.

### 230 S.A. – Discovery of the Silver Veins

**Summary:**  
Imperial prospectors (Human) discovered massive veins of silver in the Central Mountains, territory nominally claimed by Dwarves but unoccupied. A tense standoff ensued until the **Treaty of Coin** was signed: Humans could mine the silver, but Dwarves would refine it and take a percentage.  
**Consequences:**

* Precursor to the founding of the city of Argentum.  
* Introduction of a standardized silver currency, replacing the barter system in the region.

**New Law/Treaty:**

* **Name:** The Treaty of Coin  
* **Type:** Economic Accord  
* **Signatories:** The City-State of Vernath, High King of Kharnzarak.  
* **Summary:** Established mining rights and standardized the weight of the "Vernathi Silver Mark."

### 250 S.A. – The Warning of the Silent Canopy

**Summary:**  
As Human logging operations began cutting into the ancient woods bordering Hannanórë, the Cirhannim Elves sent a single, white-robed emissary to the court of Vernath. The emissary delivered a prophecy: "The forest breathes. The stone remembers. Should you deafen the world with the clamor of your ambition, you will one day find yourselves screaming into silence."  
**Consequences:**

* The warning was dismissed by the ruling Council of Vernath as arrogance.  
* Human expansion continued, but with a lingering superstition among the common woodcutters.

### 275 S.A. – Rise of the Merchant Houses

**Summary:**  
In the coastal settlements, the most successful ship captains began to consolidate power, forming the first Merchant Houses. They built fortified warehouses and private militias, effectively becoming the governing bodies of the coast.  
**Consequences:**

* Shift from tribal/village leadership to an oligarchy of wealth.  
* Founding of the **Golden Compass Syndicate**, the first major trade guild.

### 290 S.A. – The Rise of the "Thul" (The Broken)

**Summary:**  
In Kharnzarak, the rigid caste system began to fracture. Dwarves who could not meet the perfectionist standards of their guilds turned to crime or black-market smithing. Crime rates spiked in the deep halls, leading to harsh crackdowns by the King’s Guard.  
**Consequences:**

* Overcrowding of the prison-mines.  
* Growing resentment among the lower castes, planting seeds for the eventual Thulkorûn exile.

### 315 S.A. – The Southern Push

**Summary:**  
Vernath, its population booming due to trade and safe roads, began colonizing the southern lowlands. These lands were wild, filled with Primalkyn anomalies. The expansion was bloody, a war against the land itself.  
**Consequences:**

* Founding of military outposts that would eventually become the fortress-city of **Castellum**.  
* First systematic study and categorization of Primalkyn manifestations by Human scholars.

### 340 S.A. – The Emergence of Echo-Beasts

**Summary:**  
Strange, semi-translucent monsters began attacking travelers near the sealed Scars of Dissonance. These creatures mimicked the voices of their victims. They were the heralds of a growing power in the metaphysical undercurrents.  
**Consequences:**

* Panic on the trade roads.  
* The Wardens of the Wild suffered heavy casualties, realizing their conventional weapons were ineffective against these semi-corporeal threats.

### 365 S.A. – Formalization of the Thulkorûn

**Summary:**  
To deal with the "Thul" crisis (see 290 S.A.), the High King of Kharnzarak instituted the **Ritual of Shattering**. Rather than execution or imprisonment, criminals and oath-breakers were publicly stripped of their clan name, their hammer shattered, and exiled to the surface.  
**Consequences:**

* Creation of the **Thulkorûn** ("The Broken-Crafted") as a distinct demographic.  
* Influx of highly skilled Dwarven laborers into Human cities, accelerating Human technological development.

**New Custom:**

* **Name:** The Ritual of Shattering  
* **Type:** Judicial Punishment  
* **Summary:** The public breaking of a Dwarf's forging hammer. Without it, they are considered "spiritually dead" to the Stone.

### 375 S.A. – The Gathering of Heroes

**Summary:**  
The "Echo-Beasts" coalesced into a unified threat led by the **Echo-Lord**, a psychic entity occupying a massive Scar of Dissonance. A call was sent out across the continent. Heroes from all races—Human knights, Dwarven defenders, Elven archers, and Thulkorûn mercenaries—gathered at the Scar.  
**Consequences:**

* The first time the races fought side-by-side as equals, rather than just allies of convenience.  
* Appearance of the **Avatar of Bjalmund** (The Shieldbearer), a minor deity manifestation, to lead the charge.

### 380 S.A. – The Defeat of the Echo-Lord

**Summary:**  
In the final battle of the epoch, the coalition of heroes engaged the Echo-Lord. The battle raged for three days. Bjalmund the Shieldbearer sacrificed his physical avatar form to trap the Echo-Lord’s essence inside a shield of crystallized despair. The Scar was sealed by the combined magic of Elven weavers and Dwarven runesmiths.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of the Age of Dawn:** With the last great threat of the Paroxysm defeated, the world was deemed "safe."  
* **The Final Silence:** Following Bjalmund's sacrifice, the remaining minor deities withdrew their avatars completely, marking the true transition to the Age of Mortals.  
* **The Hero’s Mound:** Establishment of a great monument at the battle site, a pilgrimage point for the Wardens.

## Epoch II: The Age of Ambition (381 – 800 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
With the monsters of the Paroxysm largely slain or driven into the shadows, and the minor deities withdrawing their avatars, the "Divine Leash" is removed. The Varnathi tribes consolidate into an Empire, the Mirennians turn their eyes to the ocean, and the Dwarves undergo a schism that will change the face of technology and magic. This era ends with the total withdrawal of divine influence, leaving mortals as the sole masters of Vaeloris.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Imperial Rise (381 – 580 S.A.)

### 381 S.A. – The Unification of the High Plateau

**Summary:**  
**Caelius I**, the great-grandson of Vardas (founder of Vernath), consolidated the seven major highland city-states through a mix of diplomacy and the "War of the Seven Spears." Standing atop the walls of Vernath, he declared the end of the tribal confederacy and the birth of the **Varnathi Empire**. He claimed a "Mandate of Order"—that since the gods had left, it was Man’s duty to impose structure upon the chaotic world.  
**Consequences:**

* Centralization of military power under the Emperor.  
* The "First Compact" was amended to include loyalty to the Throne alongside the State.

### 395 S.A. – The Standardization of Steel

**Summary:**  
To fuel the new Imperial Army, Varnathi smiths, aided by Thulkorûn (exiled Dwarf) consultants, perfected the "Blast-Furnace" technique. This allowed for the mass production of standardized steel armor and weapons, replacing the bronze and iron mix of the previous epoch.  
**Consequences:**

* The Varnathi Legions became the most heavily armed force on the continent.  
* Creation of the **Iron-Bound**, the Empire’s elite heavy infantry corps.

### 410 S.A. – The Southern Conquests (The Breaking of the Barrens)

**Summary:**  
Emperor Caelius I launched the "March of Order" into the southern wilds, territory held by scattered Orkin clans since their retreat in Epoch I. Using the new steel legions and rigid phalanx tactics, the Empire shattered the disorganized Orkin warbands.  
**Consequences:**

* The Orkin were pushed further into the inhospitable badlands or the western marshes.  
* **Founding of Castellum:** A massive fortress was built on the southern frontier to hold the conquered territory. It quickly evolved into a militaristic city-state, culturally distinct from the philosophical capital of Vernath.

### 425 S.A. – The Codex of the Road

**Summary:**  
With the empire expanding, the need for logistics grew. The Imperial Senate passed the *Codex of the Road*, a massive public works project that expanded the paved trade roads of Epoch I into a continental network.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the "Courier Corps," allowing messages to cross the empire in days.  
* The "Imperial Mile" became the standard unit of measurement for the continent.

### 440 S.A. – The Coastal Expansion & The First Clash

**Summary:**  
Seeking to control maritime trade, the Empire marched east to the coast. They founded **Port Farrick** at the mouth of the Great River. However, they encountered the Mirennians—now a loose confederation of wealthy city-states. When Imperial tax collectors arrived, the Mirennian captains threw them into the harbor.  
**Consequences:**

* Beginning of the "Cold War" between the Land (Empire) and the Sea (Mirennia).  
* Mirennians formalized their alliance into the **Mirennian League** for mutual defense.

### 455 S.A. – The Synod of the Ordained

**Summary:**  
To unify the disparate cultures within the expanding Empire, the Imperial Priesthood established the **Pantheon of the Ordained**. This state religion syncretized local beliefs into a structured hierarchy, emphasizing gods of Law, Harvest, and War, while suppressing "chaotic" nature worship.  
**Consequences:**

* Religious tension with the Mirennian League, who favored fluid, elemental deities.  
* Alienation of the Waldkyn, whose nature-worship was deemed "primitive."

### 485 S.A. – The Silver Boom

**Summary:**  
A Thulkorûn prospector named **Grogor the Eye** discovered the "Deep-Vein" in the Central Mountains—a massive deposit of nearly pure silver. The Empire annexed the region immediately.  
**Consequences:**

* **Founding of Argentum:** A mining camp that exploded into a metropolis of stone and coin.  
* Rise of the "Silver Lords," a new class of merchant-nobility who would eventually rival the Emperor in power.

### 520 S.A. – The Incident of the Burning Mast

**Summary:**  
An Imperial warship attempted to blockade a Mirennian port to enforce tariffs. In response, a Mirennian "Sea-Whisperer" (an early elementalist) summoned a localized gale that drove the ship onto the rocks.  
**Consequences:**

* First recorded combat use of Wind-Shaping.  
* The Empire realized it could not challenge the League on the open ocean.

### 550 S.A. – The Perfection of Wind-Shaping

**Summary:**  
Building on the Burning Mast incident, the Mirennian **Guild of Tides** codified the art of Wind-Shaping. It was not true magic in the later arcane sense, but a ritualistic communion with the elemental remnants of Gwynfaer (The Windweaver).  
**Consequences:**

* Mirennian ships could now sail against the wind, giving them absolute naval superiority.  
* Economic dominance of the League over all coastal trade.

### Phase II: The Republican Revolution (581 – 653 S.A.)

### 581 S.A. – The Bloodless Coup

**Summary:**  
Emperor **Titus II**, a decadent ruler who spent the treasury on pleasure palaces while ignoring the roads, attempted to seize the assets of the Silver Lords in Argentum. The Governors of Argentum, Castellum, and Port Farrick secretly allied. They cut off silver, soldiers, and trade goods to the capital.  
**Consequences:**

* Starved of resources and abandoned by his guard, Titus II abdicated.  
* **Declaration of the Republic:** The monarchy was dissolved. Power was transferred to a Senate composed of representatives from the major city-states.

### 600 S.A. – The Age of City-States

**Summary:**  
With central Imperial authority broken, the Republic functioned as a loose federation. The City-States developed distinct identities:

* **Vernath:** Philosophy and Law (The Old Guard).  
* **Argentum:** Finance and Industry (The New Money).  
* **Castellum:** Military and Discipline (The Iron Fist).  
* **Port Farrick:** Trade and Diplomacy (The Gateway).

### 615 S.A. – The Dwarven Isolation (The Closing of the Lower Deep)

**Summary:**  
Disturbed by the political chaos of the Humans and the rise of the "Silver Lords," the High King of Kharnzarak ordered the "Lower Deep" gates sealed. Trade continued on the surface, but non-Dwarves were no longer permitted into the cities proper.  
**Consequences:**

* Dwarven culture turned inward, becoming increasingly stagnant and traditionalist.

### 635 S.A. – The Sundering of Mount Cinder

**Summary:**  
The dormant volcano on the Mirennian outpost island of Vol erupted with cataclysmic force. The explosion was not natural; later scholars theorize a Mirennian Wind-Shaper attempted to tap into geothermal vents and failed.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Gloom Famine:** Ash choked the skies for three years.  
* **The Tsunami of Tears:** Massive waves destroyed 40% of the Mirennian fleet and harbor infrastructure.  
* Collapse of the Mirennian economy, leaving them vulnerable.

### Phase III: The Age of Rivalry & Discovery (654 – 800 S.A.)

### 654 S.A. – The Salt War Begins

**Summary:**  
With food scarce due to the Gloom Famine, salt (essential for preservation) became more valuable than silver. The Varnathi Republic, seeing the Mirennian League weakened by the tsunami, launched an invasion to seize the vast salt flats of the southern coast.  
**Consequences:**

* **Total War:** The first conflict involving large-scale armies of mortals against mortals.  
* **Mercenary Rise:** The League, lacking soldiers, used its remaining treasury to hire thousands of Thulkorûn Dwarves and Orkin exiles.

### 660 S.A. – The Siege of Solis

**Summary:**  
The Republican Legion of Castellum besieged the Mirennian city of Solis. The siege lasted two years. It was broken not by armies, but by the "Grey Death"—dycentery caused by the unsanitary conditions of the massive army camps.  
**Consequences:**

* Advancements in field medicine and sanitation.  
* **The Soldier’s Plague:** The disease spread back to the cities, killing more than the war itself.

### 665 S.A. – The Mórenórë Accords

**Summary:**  
The Aen’valar Elves, observing the carnage from their enclave, intervened. They invited Republican and League leaders to the neutral ground of Mórenórë. Elven diplomats mediated a ceasefire.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Salt Truce:** Borders were returned to pre-war status, but trade tariffs were lowered.  
* Mórenórë was recognized as a "Sanctuary City," immune to future conflicts.

### 690 S.A. – The Exile of the Elm-korin

**Summary:**  
In Kharnzarak, a sect of Dwarven smiths led by **Tharum Flame-Touched** claimed that *magic* was simply another ore to be worked. They performed the "Ritual of the Soul-Forge," infusing a hammer with arcane fire. The High King declared this a corruption of the Stone.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Banishment:** The Elm-korin faction was exiled. Unlike the Thulkorûn (criminals), these were visionaries. They wandered the human lands, looking for a place to build.

### 710 S.A. – The Rise of the Shadewalkers

**Summary:**  
Veterans of the Salt War, spies, and disgraced diplomats formed a secret society in the shadows of Port Farrick. They realized information was more profitable than war.  
**Consequences:**

* **New Organization:** The Shadewalkers (Espionage Guild).  
* They began selling state secrets to both the Republic and the League, ensuring neither side gained a decisive advantage.

### 752 S.A. – The Discovery of Novaterra

**Summary:**  
Mirennian Navigator **Tynna Swift-Tide**, seeking a route around the Republican blockades, sailed further east than any before. She discovered a massive, untamed continent.  
**Consequences:**

* **State Secret:** The League declared the existence of "Novaterra" a level-zero secret.  
* Establishment of the "Ghost Fleet"—ships officially listed as lost at sea, actually ferrying supplies to the new colony.

### 761 S.A. – The Pact of the Northern Sanctuary (Aerinórë)

**Summary:**  
A separate expedition discovered the smaller continent of Aerinórë. Finding it inhabited by peaceful Waldkyn and Orkin (Vorok-Varr), the Mirennian captains, weary of the corruption of the old world, swore a pact of non-interference.  
**Consequences:**

* Aerinórë became a myth to the general public, a "paradise" that sailors whispered about but never mapped.

### 780 S.A. – The Council of Divine Admonition

**Summary:**  
As tensions rose for a second Salt War, the avatars of the minor deities appeared simultaneously in the Senate of Vernath and the Council of Mirenn. They spoke in unison: "Your hunger devours the future. We will not watch you consume the world we saved."  
**Consequences:**

* The warning was largely ignored by the secular leaders, who believed the gods were powerless relics of the First Age.

### 800 S.A. – The Second Withdrawal (The Day of the Closed Sky)

**Summary:**  
On the winter solstice of the 800th year, all divine magic ceased for one hour. When it returned, it was fainter, more distant. The Minor Deities withdrew their consciousness from the mortal plane entirely. The sacred fires in the temples dimmed, and the winds no longer answered prayers, only physics.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of Epoch II:** The world was now truly secular.  
* **The Crisis of Faith:** The Church of the Ordained lost political power as their miracles failed.  
* **The Vacuum:** The moral authority of the gods was gone, clearing the path for the unrestricted ambition of the coming ages.

**New Cultural Element:**

* **Name:** The God-Grief  
* **Type:** Cultural Phenomenon  
* **Description:** A period of nihilism and artistic melancholy that swept through Human and Elven society following the withdrawal, influencing poetry and music for a generation.

## Epoch III: The Age of Expansion (801 – 1400 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The silence of the gods creates a vacuum of moral authority. In its place, the "Law of Coin" and the "Law of Steel" take hold. The rivalry between the Varnathi Republic (Land) and the Mirennian League (Sea) goes global, turning the newly discovered continent of Novaterra into a proxy battlefield. Meanwhile, hidden societies—Gnomes, Halflings, and exiled Dwarves—begin to stitch together the underground networks that will survive the coming storms.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Secret Colonization & Rising Tensions (801 – 1100 S.A.)

### 805 S.A. – The Secular Decrees

**Summary:**  
In the wake of the Second Withdrawal, the Senate of the Varnathi Republic passed the *Secular Decrees*. These laws stripped the Priesthood of the Ordained of their tax-exempt status and political veto power. Temples were repurposed as civic halls or granaries.  
**Consequences:**

* Rapid secularization of human society.  
* The Church survives only by transforming into a charitable organization, planting the seeds for its eventual rebirth as the Church of the Celestial Word in later ages.

### 820 S.A. – The Castellum Consolidation

**Summary:**  
While the Republic was nominally a union of equals, the militaristic city-state of Castellum began to dominate the Senate. **Consul Marius the Iron-Hand** used the threat of Orkin raids to garrison Castellum troops in other cities, effectively turning the Republic into a military hegemony.  
**Consequences:**

* Erosion of democratic norms within the Republic.  
* Increased military funding led to the annexation of the independent "River Kingdoms" on the western border.

### 835 S.A. – The Founding of Windsail (Novaterra)

**Summary:**  
The Mirennian League established **Windsail**, the first permanent settlement on Novaterra’s western coast. To keep it secret from the Republic, they utilized the "Ghost Charts"—maps enchanted by Wind-Shapers that appeared blank to anyone who did not know the cipher key.  
**Consequences:**

* Novaterra became a secret garden for the League, providing exotic timber and spices that baffled Republican economists.  
* **Selective Colonization:** Only the most loyal House members were permitted to emigrate, creating a highly aristocratic colonial culture.

### 850 S.A. – The First Non-Human Settlers

**Summary:**  
A Mirennian supply fleet, blown off course, was forced to hire local mercenaries to repair their ships in a hidden cove. These mercenaries were **Thulkorûn Dwarves**. Seeing the potential of the new land, the Dwarves negotiated passage for their families in exchange for their silence and their steel.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the first Dwarven enclave on Novaterra, **Iron-Beach**.  
* The secret of Novaterra began to leak into the Dwarven underworld.

### 855 S.A. – The Network of the Wandering Kin

**Summary:**  
On the mainland, the exiled **Elm-korin** (Magic-Smiths) and the **Thulkorûn** (Broken-Crafted) realized they needed each other to survive. They formed the **Wandering Kin**, a loose confederation of caravans. The Thulkorûn provided protection, while the Elm-korin provided goods and repairs that no human smith could match.  
**Consequences:**

* Creation of a "State without Borders."  
* **New Custom:** *The Wagon-Vow* – A sacred oath of mutual aid sworn between caravaneers, superseding local laws.

### 880 S.A. – Expansion of the Shadewalkers

**Summary:**  
The espionage guild known as the Shadewalkers infiltrated the high courts of the Varnathi Republic. By blackmailing key Senators with proof of their illicit dealings with pirate captains, the Shadewalkers effectively became the shadow government of the Republic's trade policy.  
**Consequences:**

* Political corruption became systemic in Vernath.  
* The Guild established the "Whisper-Market" in Port Farrick, where secrets were bought and sold like grain.

### 900 S.A. – The Founding of Mórenórë (The City of Silver Tongues)

**Summary:**  
Rejecting the isolationism of Hannanórë, the progressive **Aen'valar Elves** joined with human architects and Waldkyn diplomats to found **Mórenórë** on a neutral peninsula. It was the first "Open City," governed by a Triumvirate representing the three founding races.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Intellectual Renaissance:** Mórenórë became the center of philosophy, art, and law.  
* **The Common Tongue:** Scholars in the city standardized the "Common" language, facilitating global trade and diplomacy.

### 925 S.A. – The Waldkyn Migration (The Chartered Kith)

**Summary:**  
Intrigued by the cosmopolitan success of Mórenórë, a faction of Waldkyn (primarily Simiah and Fungril) left the Whispering Vales. They believed the "Synthesis" could include civilization, not just nature. They became the **Chartered Kith**, settling in urban enclaves and becoming master architects and mediators.  
**Consequences:**

* First major cultural split within the Waldkyn people.  
* The Chartered Kith began experimenting with early bio-architecture (living buildings).

### 950 S.A. – The Rise of the Merchant Princes

**Summary:**  
In the Mirennian League, the "Council of Captains" evolved into a hereditary aristocracy. The **Golden Compass Syndicate** bought out its rivals, establishing a monopoly on the spice trade. The gap between the rich and poor in the League widened, leading to civil unrest in the dock districts.  
**Consequences:**

* Rise of organized crime syndicates in Mirennian cities to protect the lower classes.  
* Construction of the "Gilded Districts," walled neighborhoods for the elite.

### 955 S.A. – The Rise of the Vermillion Dynasties

**Summary:**  
While the north focused on the rise of the Merchant Princes, a line of Sorcerer-Kings arose in the deep southern oases. Using primitive "Blood-Binding" rituals found in First Age ruins, they united the scattered tribes into a terrifying military hegemony known as the Vermillion Dynasties. They rapidly conquered the southern tip of the Varnathi Republic and seized the salt-flats of the Mirennian League.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Trade Cut:** They severed the overland trade routes, forcing Mirennian merchants to sail further south into uncharted waters, directly accelerating the discovery of Novaterra.  
* **Legacy of Ruin:** The dynasties collapsed in 1150 S.A. due to magical drought and inbreeding, leaving behind red-stone ziggurats and a fierce culture of independence that would later birth the Federation of City-States.

### 980 S.A. – The Founding of Greenmeadow (Halfling Emergence)

**Summary:**  
Human expansion pushed into the fertile western river valleys, unknowingly encroaching on Gnomish territories. The **Halfling** clans, migrating away from the militarized Republic, settled the region they named Greenmeadow.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Gnomish Unveiling:** Rather than fight, the **Forest Gnomes** revealed themselves to the Halflings.  
* **The Declaration of Hollowkin Rights:** A historic treaty between Halflings and Gnomes, establishing a shared society of "Burrows and Hollows" that baffled human tax collectors with its lack of hierarchy.

### 1020 S.A. – The Scourge of Serpent’s Tooth

**Summary:**  
As the League and Republic grew richer, piracy exploded. **Captain Vane**, a renegade Mirennian admiral, united the disparate pirate crews of the southern archipelago. He established the "Code of the Coast" and fortified the island of **Serpent’s Tooth**.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Freebooter Nation:** Piracy transformed from a nuisance to a geopolitical power.  
* Varnathi and Mirennian navies were forced to convoy merchant ships, driving up prices and slowing trade.

### 1070 S.A. – The Blockade of Port Farrick

**Summary:**  
Captain Vane’s fleet grew bold enough to blockade the Republic’s primary port. For six months, no ship entered or left. The siege was broken only when the Shadewalkers assassinated Vane, fracturing the pirate coalition back into warring crews.  
**Consequences:**

* Establishment of the **Republic Naval Guard**, a dedicated pirate-hunting fleet.  
* The legend of Captain Vane inspired a "Golden Age" of romanticized piracy.

### 1071 S.A. – The "False Dawn" of Port Farrick (Altered Entry)

**Summary:**  
During the blockade by Captain Vane, the Governor of Port Farrick, feeling abandoned by the Republic, secretly petitioned the Mirennian League for annexation. In a stunning act of realpolitik, the League—fearing a total war on two fronts—refused the petition and leaked it to the Republic to buy goodwill.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Purge:** When the Republic broke the blockade, they did not liberate the city; they purged it. The nobility was stripped of titles and wealth.  
* **Separatist Roots:** This betrayal created a deep-seated "Separatist" culture in Port Farrick that persists into the Third Age, making the city a hotbed for anti-Imperial sentiment.

### Phase II: The Great Scramble & Internal Strife (1101 – 1300 S.A.)

### 1100 S.A. – The Mirennian Guild Wars

**Summary:**  
The monopoly of the Golden Compass Syndicate collapsed when the **Azure Hand** (a rival house) exposed evidence of embezzlement. The League descended into the "Guild Wars," a fifty-year period of assassinations, sabotage, and street battles between private house militias.  
**Consequences:**

* Mirennian naval power effectively ceased to exist as a unified force.  
* The secret supply lines to Novaterra were disrupted, leaving the colonists there to fend for themselves.

### 1115 S.A. – The Secret is Sold

**Summary:**  
A disgruntled Azure Hand bookkeeper, fleeing the Guild Wars, sold the "Ghost Charts" of Novaterra to the Varnathi Consul of Trade. The Republic immediately commissioned an expeditionary fleet.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Scramble:** The Republic launched a massive colonization effort to catch up to the League.  
* Panic in Windsail (Novaterra) as they prepared for invasion.

### 1150 S.A. – The Skirmish of Red Clay (First Colonial War)

**Summary:**  
Republican colonists made landfall on Novaterra’s eastern coast, clashing with Mirennian outposts. The "Skirmish of Red Clay" was the first battle fought between the two powers on the new continent.  
**Consequences:**

* Stalemate: The continent was too vast for either side to dominate.  
* Division of Novaterra: The West Coast remained culturally Mirennian, while the East Coast became heavily Varnathi/Imperial.

### 1200 S.A. – The Earthwake of Kharnzarak

**Summary:**  
A catastrophic seismic event struck the Dwarven capital. It was not natural; it was a structural failure of the "Deep Law" architecture caused by centuries of stagnation and the sealing of the lower levels. Several major clan halls collapsed into the abyss.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Bronrûn Exodus:** The **Bronrûn Clan** (The Growth/Reformist faction), blamed for the disaster by the paranoid High King, fled the mountain.  
* Using the Wandering Kin network, thousands of Dwarves migrated to the surface, eventually seeking passage to Novaterra where they hoped to build anew.

### 1210 S.A. – The War of the Barren Hills

**Summary:**  
The Varnathi Republic, needing resources to fuel the colonial race, launched a war of extermination against the **Varr-Darg Orkin** in the Barren Hills to seize iron and coal deposits. The legions used "Scorched Earth" tactics, burning the limited food sources of the Orkin.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Mirennian Passage:** Desperate and starving, thousands of Orkin paid Mirennian smugglers (who cared only for coin) to ferry them to Novaterra.  
* The Orkin population on the mainland was reduced to scattered guerrilla bands.

### 1250 S.A. – The Rise of the Free Holds (Novaterra)

**Summary:**  
The displaced Orkin and Bronrûn Dwarves on Novaterra, rejecting the authority of both Human colonies, moved inland to the rugged central highlands. They established the **Free Holds**—fortified, independent city-states based on meritocracy and survival.  
**Consequences:**

* Novaterra became a tri-polar political landscape: The Varnathi East, The Mirennian West, and the Independent Central Highlands.

### 1280 S.A. – The "Green-Thumb" Strike

**Summary:**  
During the height of the "War of the Barren Hills," Varnathi legions trampled the Halfling harvest fields of Greenmeadow to create supply roads. The Halflings did not fight back with weapons; they simply stopped exporting grain and ale. Within three months, the Imperial Army faced mutiny due to starvation and plummeting morale.  
**Consequences:**

* **Treaty of the Hearth:** The Republic was forced to sign a humiliating treaty granting Greenmeadow permanent neutrality and forbidding military boots on their soil.  
* **Soft Power:** Established the Halflings as a "Sleeping Giant" of logistics whom no army dares anger.

### 1300 S.A. – The Desertborn Confederacy

**Summary:**  
On the mainland, the chaos of the Guild Wars and the distraction of the Republic allowed the nomadic tribes of the Southern Sands to unite. Under the **Lothari Mystics**, they seized control of the overland Silk Road.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Toll of Sand:** Any trade moving between East and West by land now paid tribute to the Desertborn.  
* The Confederacy became a wealthy, neutral buffer state, preserving ancient history while the north focused on the future.

### Phase III: The New World Order (1301 – 1400 S.A.)

### 1320 S.A. – The Halfling Hearthsong Era

**Summary:**  
While the "Tallfolk" fought over colonies, Halfling culture entered a golden age. Their agricultural surpluses from Greenmeadow fed the warring armies of the Republic, making them indispensable. Traveling Halfling minstrels spread "Hearthsongs"—ballads of simple life and peace—that became popular even among the soldiers.  
**Consequences:**

* Halflings gained a "diplomatic immunity" of sorts; harming a Halfling village became taboo even for ruthless generals.  
* Cultural unification of the lower classes across national borders.

### 1350 S.A. – The Silent Network

**Summary:**  
Fearing that the frantic pace of expansion was erasing the past, the Lothari mystics formally organized the **Silent Network**. They began archiving histories, artifacts, and myths in hidden desert vaults. They believed the "Age of Noise" (Expansion) would eventually collapse, and the world would need its memory back.  
**Consequences:**

* Preservation of knowledge regarding the First Age and the Paroxysm, which would otherwise have been lost to secular revisionism.

### 1360 S.A. – The invention of the Printing Press (Gnomish)

**Summary:**  
In the mixed burrow-city of **Gear-Hollow** (a Halfling/Gnome settlement), a Gnome tinkerer named **Bimble Pockets** invented the movable type printing press.  
**Consequences:**

* Explosion of literacy and pamphlets.  
* The "Secrets of the Guilds" began to leak to the common public, threatening the monopoly of the Shadewalkers and artisan guilds.

### 1380 S.A. – The Paranoia of the High King

**Summary:**  
In Kharnzarak, High King **Thorgar VI** grew increasingly erratic. He claimed the "surface taint" caused the Earthwake. He began a purge of any Dwarf who had contact with the surface world or the Wandering Kin.  
**Consequences:**

* Civil war within the mountain.  
* The "Pure-Blood" doctrine became law.

### 1400 S.A. – The Sealing of Kharnzarak

**Summary:**  
In the final year of the epoch, High King Thorgar VI ordered the Great Adamant Gates of Kharnzarak sealed. The runes were hammered shut. For the next several centuries, the Dwarven Kingdom would be a tomb of silence to the outside world.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of Epoch III.**  
* The Wandering Kin and the Novaterran Free Holds became the *only* visible face of Dwarvenkind to the rest of the world.  
* The stage was set for the discovery of magic, as the old, stable powers (Dwarves) retreated, leaving the ambitious, unstable young races (Humans) to stumble upon forces they did not understand.

## Epoch IV: The Arcane Revolution (1401 – 1750 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
For over a thousand years, magic was the exclusive domain of gods, spirits, and the elder races. When humanity stumbles upon the keys to the arcane, it triggers a societal immune response. The Varnathi Republic attempts to purge it, the Mirennians attempt to sell it, and the outcasts of the world gather in Novaterra to master it. By the end of this epoch, the world has fundamentally changed: armies are no longer defined by the number of spears, but by the potency of their spells.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Spark & The Panic (1401 – 1480 S.A.)

### 1401 S.A. – The Excavation of the Whispering Tomb

**Summary:**  
A Varnathi archeological team, funded by the Shadewalkers, excavated a pre-Paroxysm ruin near the city of Argentum. They breached a sealed vault containing texts from the First Age "Divine Workshop."  
**Consequences:**

* **The Patient Zero:** The lead scholar, **Melek the Grey**, was exposed to raw arcane residue. He did not die; instead, his mind was forcibly opened to the "ambient field" left behind by the departed gods.

### 1407 S.A. – The First Revelation of Melek

**Summary:**  
After six years of seclusion, Melek appeared in the central market of Argentum. Before a crowd of thousands, he levitated the city’s massive stone obelisk and transmuted a merchant’s lead weights into pure silver. He proclaimed, "The gods have left the keys in the lock."  
**Consequences:**

* **The Spark:** Melek distributed copies of his translated notes. Within months, hundreds of intellectuals, artists, and desperate commoners began unlocking latent abilities.  
* **Melek’s Fate:** He was found dead three days later, his brain burned out by channeling too much energy, foreshadowing the dangers of the Fraying.

### 1410 S.A. – The Arcane Hysteria (The Wild Years)

**Summary:**  
Without training or discipline, the "New Mages" caused chaos. In Port Farrick, a dockworker accidentally boiled the harbor water. In Vernath, a student set the Great Library on fire with a sneeze. The public terrified.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Mob Justice:** Vigilante mobs began lynching anyone suspected of "witchcraft."  
* Charlatans sold "anti-magic amulets" (painted rocks), draining the savings of the fearful peasantry.

### 1425 S.A. – The Decree of Prohibition

**Summary:**  
The Senate of the Varnathi Republic, pressured by the military generals of Castellum, passed the *Decree of Prohibition*. The practice, study, or possession of arcane materials was punishable by death.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Burning of Books:** Great bonfires were lit in every city square to destroy Melek’s texts.  
* The Shadewalkers quietly hoarded the surviving texts, seeing their immense future value.

### 1450 S.A. – The Mage-Purge Wars Begin

**Summary:**  
The Republic formed the **Legio Null** (The Inquisitors). Clad in "Cold-Iron" armor (thought to dampen magic) and led by zealot-captains, they began a systematic hunt. Villages were razed if they harbored a single "Spark-User."  
**Consequences:**

* **The Diaspora:** Mages fled the Republic in droves.  
* **Radicalization:** Persecuted mages began to fight back, using fire and lightning to defend their families, confirming the Republic’s propaganda that they were monsters.

### 1454 S.A. – The Mirennian Exodus (The Profiteers)

**Summary:**  
The Mirennian League, ever pragmatic, saw an opportunity. They publicly condemned magic to appease the Republic but privately sent agents to offer passage to fleeing mages.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Ticket of Gold:** The price for passage to Novaterra was exorbitant—a lifetime of servitude, family heirlooms, or magical secrets.  
* Mirennian coffers overflowed, funding the restoration of their fleets.

### 1460 S.A. – The Star-Veiled Passage

**Summary:**  
The Aen’valar Elves of Mórenórë, disgusted by the brutality of the Purge, intervened covertly. They established the **Star-Veiled Passage**, an underground railroad. Using Waldkyn guides and sympathetic Halfling smugglers, they moved refugees to neutral ports.  
**Consequences:**

* Saved the lives of over 4,000 "Spark-Users."  
* Deepened the rift between the Elves and the Human Republic.

### 1467 S.A. – The Kingdom of the Silver Hand

**Summary:**  
**General Aric the Silver**, a hero of the Republic, discovered his own latent magical ability. Rather than submit to the Mage-Purge, he turned his legion against the Senate. He carved out a sanctuary kingdom on the borderlands, briefly allying with local Orkin tribes to hold the line. For fifty years, it was a utopia for the persecuted.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Betrayal:** As Aric succumbed to the Fraying (madness from raw magic), the Orkin leaders betrayed him to the Republic in exchange for amnesty. The Republic accepted the help, crushed the Kingdom, and then slaughtered the Orkin anyway.  
* **Stigma:** Created the cultural stigma of the "Mage-Tyrant" in human lands and cemented Orkin cynicism toward human alliances.

### 1475 S.A. – The Landing at Rust-Water

**Summary:**  
A massive flotilla of refugee ships, carrying a mix of human mages, exiled Dwarves (Elm-korin), and opportunistic Gnomes, landed in a desolate bay on Novaterra. They found the land rich in geothermal vents—a natural source of power.  
**Consequences:**

* The decision was made to stop running and start building.

### Phase II: The Age of Foundings (1481 – 1600 S.A.)

### 1485 S.A. – The Founding of Gearhaven

**Summary:**  
The "Great Collaboration" began. The **Bronrûn Dwarves** (master builders) laid the foundations. The **Elm-korin Dwarves** (technomancers) designed the power grid. The **Human Mages** provided the arcane spark. The **Gnomes** designed the intricate clockwork control systems.  
**Consequences:**

* **Birth of Technomancy:** The fusion of magic and machine.  
* **The Binding Accord:** A constitution ensuring no single race ruled the city. It was governed by the *Cog-Council*, representing the Guilds.

**New City:**

* **Name:** Gearhaven  
* **Location:** The Rust-Water Bay, Novaterra.  
* **Description:** A city of brass, steam, and glowing runes, built vertically into the sea cliffs.

### 1500 S.A. – The Masked Rebellion

**Summary:**  
Tension arose in Gearhaven. The "Mundane" workers felt treated as second-class citizens by the Mages. A group called **The Masked** seized the geothermal plants, threatening to blow up the city unless they were granted seats on the Council.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Compromise:** The Mages capitulated. The *Guild of Labor* was granted two seats.  
* Established Gearhaven as a true meritocracy, where ability (magical or mechanical) determined status.

### 1510 S.A. – The Founding of Nexus

**Summary:**  
The **Chartered Kith** (Waldkyn migrants), observing Gearhaven, found it "loud and soulless." They moved inland to the Great Forests of Novaterra. Fusing their nature-magic with the new technomancy, they grew the city of **Nexus**.  
**Consequences:**

* **Bio-Architecture:** Buildings were grown from genetically modified trees, reinforced with living steel.  
* Nexus became the agricultural and biological research hub of the new world.

### 1530 S.A. – The Wandering Kin Integration

**Summary:**  
On the mainland, the Wandering Kin (Dwarven Caravans) began acting as intermediaries for the hidden mages. They developed "Rune-Boxes"—lead-lined containers that could hide magical artifacts from Republican Inquisitors.  
**Consequences:**

* The Kin became the primary smugglers of magical contraband on the mainland.

### 1547 S.A. – The Rise of the Arcane Black Market

**Summary:**  
Despite the Purge, demand for magic grew. Wealthy Varnathi Senators secretly hired "Hedge-Wizards" to cure illnesses or spy on rivals, creating a massive hypocrisy in the Republic.  
**Consequences:**

* Magic didn't disappear; it just went underground and became expensive.

### 1550 S.A. – The Mirennian Civil War (Salt vs. Silk)

**Summary:**  
As the black market for magic grew, the Mirennian League fractured. The "Old Money" (Salt/Timber merchants) wanted to uphold the ban on magic to appease the Republic. The "New Money" (Smugglers/Artifact Traders) wanted to embrace it. The conflict escalated into open naval warfare.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Southern Shift:** The Southern Cities, hiring Thulkorûn mercenaries and battle-mages, defeated the Northern traditionalists.  
* **Moral Flexibility:** The capital and moral center of the League shifted south. The League became a nation that valued profit over tradition or law, laying the groundwork for their technological dominance later.

### 1580 S.A. – The Orkin Awakening (The Free Marches)

**Summary:**  
In the eastern provinces of the Republic, slave labor (mostly captured Orkin and indebted humans) fueled the economy. An Orkin gladiator named **Gruk the Breaker** discovered he was a "Spark-User" (an Earth-Mage). He didn't use spells; he infused his skin with stone.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Stone-Fist Revolt:** Gruk shattered the walls of the slave pens.  
* Thousands of Orkin and slaves rallied to him, seizing a swath of territory known as the **Free Marches**.

| :---- |

### Phase III: The Wars of Definition (1601 – 1750 S.A.)

### 1610 S.A. – The Siege of the Free Marches

**Summary:**  
The Republic sent three legions to crush Gruk’s revolt. However, the Shadewalkers, seeing profit in chaos, supplied the rebels with stolen Mirennian "Fire-Pots" (early grenades). The Legions were bogged down in a decade-long guerrilla war.  
**Consequences:**

* The Republic’s military strength was drained.  
* The myth of Varnathi invincibility was shattered.

### 1650 S.A. – The Silent Coup of the Free Marches

**Summary:**  
Gruk died (assassinated by a republican agent), but his lieutenants established a Council. They formally declared the **Republic of the Free Marches**. Though short-lived, it was the first nation founded on anti-slavery principles.  
**Consequences:**

* Inspired slave revolts across the continent.  
* Forced the Varnathi Republic to keep its armies at home, halting their colonial expansion.

### 1680 S.A. – The Return of the Mages (The Gearhaven Armada)

**Summary:**  
Gearhaven, now a superpower, decided to intervene on the mainland to stop the persecution of mages. They launched the **First Fleet of Iron**, steam-powered ironclad ships equipped with "Arcane Cannons."  
**Consequences:**

* They broke the blockade of Novaterra.  
* They landed troops on the mainland coast, rallying the hidden mages.

### 1695 S.A. – The Battle of Crimson Field

**Summary:**  
The defining battle of the epoch. The **Grand Army of the Republic** (50,000 steel-clad soldiers) met the **Coalition of the Arcane** (3,000 Battle-Mages and Gearhaven Riflemen).  
**The Battle:**

* The Republican cavalry charged.  
* The Mages of Gearhaven combined their power to cast *Tectonic Rupture*, creating a mile-long canyon that swallowed the cavalry charge.  
* Arcane Cannons decimated the infantry lines from a mile away.  
  **Consequences:**  
* **Total Defeat:** The Republican army surrendered within hours.  
* **The End of the Age of Steel:** It was proven that magic was superior to conventional arms.

### 1700 S.A. – The Treaty of the Silver Quill

**Summary:**  
The humiliated Republic was forced to sign a treaty with the Free Peoples of Novaterra.  
**Terms:**

* Immediate end to the Mage-Purge.  
* Legalization of magic (with regulation).  
* Recognition of Gearhaven as a sovereign power.

### 1715 S.A. – The Great Arcane Integration

**Summary:**  
Nations scrambled to adapt.

* **Varnath:** Established the **Magister’s Council** to oversee state-sanctioned mages. They turned magic into a bureaucracy.  
* **Mirennia:** Integrated "Tide-Mages" into every ship crew, regaining their naval edge.  
* **Kharnzarak:** The Dwarves remained sealed, but the Wandering Kin began openly trading runic items.

### 1730 S.A. – The Rise of the Federation

**Summary:**  
The southern city-states, fearful of both the weakened Republic and the powerful Technocracy, united. They formed the **Federation of City-States**. Their military doctrine was "Sword and Spell"—integrating mages into infantry squads rather than keeping them separate.  
**Consequences:**

* A new superpower emerged on the mainland.

### 1750 S.A. – The Founding of the Grand University

**Summary:**  
To standardize magical education and prevent another "Wild Years" scenario, the Magister’s Council and the Aen'valar Elves co-founded the **Grand University** in Mórenórë.  
**Consequences:**

* Magic became an academic science rather than a wild talent.  
* **End of Epoch IV.** The world has stabilized into a "High Magic" society, but the rapid use of arcane energy begins to draw the attention of ancient, sleeping things in the sky.

## Epoch V: The Silent Skies (1751 – 2340 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The "Pax Magica" enforces a cessation of large-scale ground wars, but peace does not bring prosperity to all. The Varnathi state fractures into civil war, the Mirennian League turns to decadence, and the Technocracy of Novaterra isolates itself. It is an era of silence—the silence of the Giants who vanish, the silence of the gods who remain distant, and the deceptive silence of the skies before the storm.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Unraveling of the Old Order (1751 – 1950 S.A.)

### 1755 S.A. – The Gilded Peace

**Summary:**  
With magic legalized and integrated, a global economic boom occurred. The **Federation of City-States** and the **Technocracy** established the "Trans-Oceanic Trade Route," bypassing the tariffs of the Varnathi and Mirennians.  
**Consequences:**

* Rapid industrialization of Novaterra.  
* The Varnathi/Mirennian economies began to stagnate, leading to social unrest.

### 1775 S.A. – The Passing of the Giants (The Empty Peak Expedition)

**Summary:**  
A Gearhaven survey team ascended the highest peak of the Dragon’s Tooth range to install a weather station. They found the massive cyclopean fortress there completely abandoned. Meals were left on tables; forges were cold but unweathered.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Mystery:** No bodies were found. The Giants simply ceased to exist on the material plane.  
* **The Scavenger Rush:** Adventurers and nations scrambled to loot the Giant strongholds for "Titan-Steel" and ancient artifacts.

### 1805 S.A. – The Beast of Black Rock (First Drakona Probe)

**Summary:**  
A remote Varnathi border fort was annihilated overnight. Investigators found the stone walls melted into glass. The official report blamed a rogue fire-elemental, but secret Shadewalker reports noted "claw marks the size of siege engines" and "scales harder than diamond."  
**Consequences:**

* The first confirmed, though classified, contact with the organized military might of the Drakona.  
* Ignored by the Senate as a localized anomaly.

### 1820 S.A. – The Rise of the Oligarchs

**Summary:**  
In the Varnathi Republic, the **Senate** became a playground for the ultra-wealthy. Seats were openly bought and sold. The "Citizen" class was effectively disenfranchised, creating a massive population of angry, impoverished commoners.  
**Consequences:**

* Rise of populist demagogues.  
* The Republic ceased to function as a representative government.

### 1845 S.A. – The Silent Repulsion (Aerinórë)

**Summary:**  
A Varnathi timber syndicate, backed by mercenaries, attempted to clear-cut the coastal forests of Aerinórë. They were not met by armies. Sael’anir wind-shapers redirected the fog to trap them in a maze, while Waldkyn druids caused rapid overgrowth that dismantled their logging camps overnight.  
**Consequences:**

* The expedition went bankrupt without a single sword being drawn.  
* Cemented Aerinórë’s reputation as a "haunted" land that defends itself.

### 1860 S.A. – The Technocratic Isolation

**Summary:**  
Disgusted by the corruption of the mainland, the **Cog-Council** of Gearhaven passed the *Isolation Edict*. They restricted the export of advanced magitech to the Old World.  
**Consequences:**

* Technological divergence: Novaterra advanced rapidly (early computation, steam-turbines), while the Mainland stagnated in the medieval-arcane era.

### 1880 S.A. – The Technocratic Blockade & The Iron Pact

**Summary:**  
The industrializing Technocracy of Gearhaven (Novaterra) ran short of "Brimstone" (high-grade fuel). They blockaded the ports of the Federation of City-States (Mainland) to force a favorable trade deal. In a shocking twist, the Varnathi Empire—historically the enemy of the Federation—formed the **Iron Pact**, a temporary military alliance with the Federation to break the blockade.  
**Consequences:**

* **Military Modernization:** For 30 years, Imperial discipline mixed with Federalist innovation. The Federation emerged from the war with a modernized military doctrine that would eventually allow them to eclipse the Empire.  
* **Resource Realism:** Proved that resource needs superseded ideology.

### 1890 S.A. – The Star-Tide Festival

**Summary:**  
To celebrate their continued independence, the diverse peoples of Aerinórë (Elves, Waldkyn, Orkin, Human) held the first **Star-Tide Festival** in Lanthir Haven.  
**Consequences:**

* Became a cultural beacon. The music and art produced here began to spread via Mirennian traders, subtly influencing the youth of the mainland to question their rigid, segregated societies.

### 1920 S.A. – The Shadewalker Hegemony

**Summary:**  
By this year, the Grandmaster of the Shadewalkers, **Varia the Whisper**, effectively ruled the Varnathi Republic from the shadows. She manipulated grain prices, incited border skirmishes to sell weapons, and assassinated any honest politician who tried to reform the system.  
**Consequences:**

* Total erosion of public trust in government.  
* The Republic was a hollow shell, ready to crack.

### 1950 S.A. – The Great Betrayal (The Fall of the Republic)

**Summary:**  
Seeking to install a puppet dictator, the Shadewalkers leaked forged documents implicating the five most powerful City-States in a plot to enslave the others.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Succession Wars:** The Republic shattered. Civil war erupted instantly.  
* Castellum declared independence.  
* Argentum hired mercenaries to besiege Vernath.  
* The "Pax Magica" on the mainland was broken.

### Phase II: The Interregnum & The Rise of Faith (1951 – 2150 S.A.)

### 1960 S.A. – The Bleeding Years

**Summary:**  
For forty years, the mainland was a slaughterhouse. Warlords rose and fell. Magic was used indiscriminately, scarring the land. Famine was widespread. The secular promises of the previous epochs—that men didn't need gods—rang hollow.

### 2020 S.A. – The Founding of the Church

**Summary:**  
In the neutral city of Luminas, a former soldier turned pacifist named **Iovan** began preaching. He did not call for the return of the old gods, but for the "Celestial Word"—the idea that the universe responds to moral resonance.  
**Consequences:**

* Founding of the **Church of the Celestial Word**.  
* It offered soup kitchens, healing, and hope, filling the void left by the state.

### 2032 S.A. – The Herald in Flame (Appearance of the Aetheris)

**Summary:**  
During a massive refugee crisis outside Luminas, a bandit army attacked the helpless camp. As Iovan and the refugees prayed—not for salvation, but for the strength to endure—the sky ignited. A winged figure of pure light descended, placing itself between the bandits and the refugees. It did not attack; it simply *stood*. The bandits, overcome by a wave of unnatural dread and awe, fled.  
**Consequences:**

* **First Aetheris Sighting:** The entity dissolved after the danger passed.  
* Validated the Church's doctrine: The cosmos *listens*.  
* Conversion rates skyrocketed.

### 2085 S.A. – The Treaty of Ash and Iron

**Summary:**  
The Succession Wars ended in mutual exhaustion. The Warlord **Aetius IV** of Castellum conquered Vernath but realized he couldn't hold it without the support of the merchant guilds.  
**Consequences:**

* **Formation of the Varnathi Imperial Democracy:** A bizarre hybrid government. A hereditary Emperor (controlled by the military) presided over an elected Senate (controlled by the merchants).  
* It was a stable, if corrupt, compromise.

### 2100 S.A. – The Gnomish "Root-Hack"

**Summary:**  
As global tensions rose, the Forest Gnomes of the mainland felt threatened by the expanding human armies. Instead of building walls, they modified the *mycelial network* of the forests to act as a continental vibration-sensor.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Info-Brokers:** The **Myco-Whisperers** became the world's premier intelligence agency. They could track troop movements anywhere there were roots, selling this data to the highest bidder (often the Lothari or Elves) to maintain the balance of power.

### 2110 S.A. – The Declaration of Sovereignty

**Summary:**  
Seeing the new Varnathi state emerging, the Technocracy of Gearhaven and the Federation of City-States formally declared the **Free Peoples Alliance**. They warned the Empire that any aggression would be met with overwhelming magitech force.  
**Consequences:**

* The world settled into a tri-polar deadlock: The Empire (North), The Federation (South), and The Free Peoples (Novaterra).

### 2130 S.A. – The Orkin Integration (The Quarters)

**Summary:**  
To rebuild the shattered infrastructure of the Empire, Emperor Aetius passed the *Labor Integration Act*. Orkin were granted citizenship (of a sort) in exchange for labor.  
**Consequences:**

* Formation of the **Orkin Quarters** in human cities.  
* While legally citizens, they were socially ostracized, leading to the formation of tight-knit, mafia-like Orkin Guilds for protection.

### Phase III: The Shadow from the Skies (2151 – 2340 S.A.)

### 2175 S.A. – The Founding of Aethelas

**Summary:**  
On Novaterra, the Aen'valar Elves founded **Aethelas**, a city-sized library and observatory. It was dedicated to the "Aen'valan" (The Unwritten Song)—the pursuit of future knowledge.  
**Consequences:**

* Became the global center for magical research.  
* Attracted the most brilliant minds from all races, draining the mainland of its intellectual capital.

### 2188 S.A. – The Hymn of the Hidden Star

**Summary:**  
In Mórenórë, a blind musician named **Sylla** gained fame for her haunting compositions. Upon her deathbed, she revealed herself as an Aetheris who had lived a mortal life to understand human suffering.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Velo Aspectus:** The revelation that Aetheris could walk among mortals in disguise caused a religious fervor.  
* "Sylla's Hymn" became the anthem of the Church.

### 2200 S.A. – The Cloud-Shadow Incidents

**Summary:**  
Mirennian ships crossing the deep ocean began disappearing. Not sinking—vanishing. No wreckage.  
**Consequences:**

* Mirennian "Wind-Listeners" reported strange, high-altitude disturbances, like "mountains moving through the air."  
* Dismissed as weather anomalies by the Magister's Council.

### 2250 S.A. – The Dragon’s Tithe

**Summary:**  
A single Drakona wing descended upon the remote mining town of Oakhaven. They did not burn it. The commander demanded 50% of the mine’s output in gold and gems, to be left on a high peak every month.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Extortion:** Oakhaven paid. The Drakona left.  
* This pattern repeated across the remote frontiers. The world realized these were not mindless monsters, but a disciplined empire gathering resources.

### 2280 S.A. – The Scaled Pact (Pirate Betrayal)

**Summary:**  
The Drakona High Command approached the Pirate Lords of Serpent’s Tooth. In exchange for "Air Cover" (immunity from navies) and a share of the spoils, the Pirates agreed to act as the Drakona’s eyes and ears on the surface.  
**Consequences:**

* Piracy spiked uncontrollably.  
* The Drakona gained detailed maps of human defenses without risking their own scouts.

### 2305 S.A. – The Luminas Conclave

**Summary:**  
Realizing the national governments were too paralyzed by internal politics to act, the Archmages of the Grand University, Gearhaven, and the Federation met in secret.  
**Consequences:**

* **Formation of the Coalition of Mages:** A "Deep State" organization dedicated to preparing for the inevitable war.  
* They began stockpiling mana crystals and researching anti-aerial combat.

### 2315 S.A. – The Kyn-Clan Scourge

**Summary:**  
In the Whispering Vales, a radical sect of Waldkyn (The Kyn-Clans) claimed the "Dragon Shadow" was punishment for the "Single-Forms" (Humans/Elves) abandoning nature. They launched a terrorist campaign against Varnathi border towns.  
**Consequences:**

* Destabilized the northern border of the Empire right before the invasion.  
* Forced the Empire to move its legions north, away from the key southern ports.

### 2319 S.A. – The Dissident of Aethelas (Elaruniel’s Rise)

**Summary:**  
A young Elven scholar, **Elaruniel**, published "On the Efficacy of Dissonance." She argued that harmonic magic was too slow for warfare and that "breaking" reality was more efficient than "bending" it.  
**Consequences:**

* She was censured by the academics but secretly recruited by the Coalition of Mages, who knew they would need ugly weapons to fight dragons.

### 2335 S.A. – The Warning of the Wind

**Summary:**  
Every Wind-Shaper in the Mirennian League simultaneously felt a massive displacement of air in the far north. A pressure wave that signaled the movement of millions of tons of mass.  
**Consequences:**

* The League mobilized its fleet, but the Empire refused to believe the reports, calling it "Mirennian hysteria."

### 2340 S.A. – The Eve of War

**Summary:**  
Communications with the northernmost observation posts ceased. The skies turned a strange, bruise-colored purple. The birds stopped singing.  
**Consequences:**

* **End of Epoch V.**  
* The Coalition of Mages activated their emergency protocols.  
* The Drakona Invasion Fleet crossed the cloud barrier.

## Epoch VI: The Dragonfall (2341 – 2700 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The Drakona Empire, ruling from floating cloud-cities hidden by ancient aeromancy, descends to subjugate the surface. The fragmented nations of Vaeloris are nearly annihilated in the first decade. Survival requires the unprecedented unification of Human, Elf, Dwarf, and Gnome under the banner of the **Allied Command**. The war drives rapid technological advancement—specifically the Airship—but ends with a magical atrocity that silences the skies.

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Unconquered Sky (2341 – 2480 S.A.)

### 2341 S.A. – The Scouring of the North

**Summary:**  
On the first day of spring, the sky over the northern Varnathi provinces darkened. Not with clouds, but with the hulls of Drakona cloud-ships and thousands of dragon-kin warriors. The invasion began with a systematic aerial bombardment of major fortifications using "Sun-Lances"—focused beams of solar magic.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Fall of the North:** Within three months, 30% of the Varnathi Empire was occupied.  
* **The Refugee Crisis:** Millions fled south, clogging the roads and causing famine in the unoccupied lands.

### 2345 S.A. – The Treaty of Survival

**Summary:**  
Facing extinction, the leaders of the **Varnathi Empire**, **Mirennian League**, **Federation of City-States**, and **Technocracy of Gearhaven** met at the neutral city of Luminas. Putting aside centuries of hatred, they signed the *Treaty of Survival*.  
**Consequences:**

* **Formation of the Allied Command:** A unified military structure.  
* **The Resource Pool:** All national treasuries were merged into a single war fund.

### 2350 S.A. – The Establishment of the Drakona Protectorates

**Summary:**  
The Drakona did not simply bomb the northern Varnathi provinces into submission. They offered an ultimatum: "Serve us, and live better than you did under the corrupt Senate." Many provinces accepted, forming the **Principality of the Scale**, a human nation loyal to the Dragons that fought against the Allied Command.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Permanent Divide:** After the war, these humans were branded traitors. This created a permanent "North-South" divide in Varnathi culture. Northerners are seen as "Dragon-Thralls," while Southerners are "Warmongers."  
* **Resistance Cells:** Conversely, this era birthed a fierce underground resistance in the north that persists as anti-government militias in the Third Age.

### 2355 S.A. – The Sacking of Mirenn

**Summary:**  
The Drakona realized the Allied supply lines relied on the sea. A massive aerial fleet descended on Mirenn, the capital of the League. The Mirennian navy, designed for surface combat, was helpless against attacks from directly above.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Burning Harbor:** The entire Mirennian fleet was destroyed at anchor.  
* The League ceased to exist as a naval power, forcing them to pivot their entire economy toward the new "Sky-Tech."

### 2360 S.A. – The Aethelas Contribution (Elaruniel’s Engine)

**Summary:**  
The Allied resistance was failing because they could not reach the enemy high altitude. The Technocracy had airship prototypes, but they lacked an engine powerful enough to lift heavy armor. **Elaruniel**, the controversial Elven scholar, was brought in. She applied her "Dissonance Theory" to the engines, creating a volatile but immensely powerful "Mana-Combustion Drive."  
**Consequences:**

* **The First Lift-Off:** The prototype heavy cruiser *The Iron Will* launched from Gearhaven.

### 2365 S.A. – The Great Re-Scaffolding

**Summary:**  
With their harbor destroyed, the Mirennians rebuilt their capital city *upwards*. Using Dwarven steel and Elven wood-singing, they constructed massive docking towers and gantries.  
**Consequences:**

* Mirenn transformed from a sea-port to a sky-port.  
* The Mirennians applied their centuries of naval tradition to the air, becoming the premier pilots of the Alliance.

### 2380 S.A. – The Launch of the Allied Air Fleet

**Summary:**  
After twenty years of frantic industry, the Allied Command unveiled the **First Air Fleet**. Composed of 500 ships—Gearhaven Ironclads, Mirennian Sky-Galleons, and Varnathi Assault Barges—it was the largest concentration of military power in history.  
**Consequences:**

* **The War Shifts:** The Allies could finally contest air superiority.

### 2410 S.A. – The Battle of the Grey Peaks

**Summary:**  
The first major fleet-on-fleet engagement. The Allied Fleet intercepted a Drakona armada attempting to cross the mountains into the central plains. The battle raged for three days in a thunderstorm.  
**Consequences:**

* **A Pyrrhic Victory:** The Allies stopped the invasion, but lost 60% of their ships.  
* Learned Lesson: Drakona individual warriors (dragons) could shred wooden hulls. Future ships were plated in "Star-Steel" alloy.

### 2450 S.A. – The Orkin Shield (The Northern Front)

**Summary:**  
While the air war raged, the Drakona launched a ground invasion through the Barren Hills to flank the Alliance. The **Varr-Darg Orkin**, defending their ancestral home, refused to retreat. Using tunnel warfare and suicide tactics, they held the pass for a decade against superior numbers.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Orkin Recognition:** The Allied Command was forced to acknowledge the Orkin contribution, sending them supplies and advisors. This laid the groundwork for future integration.

### 2480 S.A. – The Rise of the Cloudborn Corsairs

**Summary:**  
Captain **Kaelen "Skysplitter" Vance**, a Mirennian ace, grew disillusioned with the Allied Command’s willingness to sacrifice crews. He led a mutiny, taking his squadron and vanishing into the cloud banks.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Third Faction:** The Cloudborn Corsairs raided both sides for supplies.  
* They established hidden bases on floating sky-islands (chunks of land ripped up by raw magic during the Paroxysm).

### Phase II: The War of Innovation (2481 – 2680 S.A.)

### 2485 S.A. – The Skyreach Cascade (The Dissonance Event)

**Summary:**  
During an assault on the Drakona fortress of Skyreach, the Drakona unleashed a "Resonance Shriek"—a magical frequency designed to detonate the Mana-Drives of the Allied ships.  
**Consequences:**

* **Elaruniel’s Counter:** Present at the battle, Elaruniel broadcast a "Null-Harmony" that cancelled the shriek. It saved the fleet but caused a localized magical dead-zone that persisted for a century.  
* Validated Elaruniel’s darker theories about the cost of high-energy magic.

### 2500 S.A. – The Shadewalker Betrayal

**Summary:**  
The Shadewalkers, playing both sides, sold the blueprints of the new "Mk-IV Sky-Cruiser" to the Drakona. The Drakona began fielding "Mock-Ships"—vessels that looked Allied but were filled with dragon-kin boarders.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Paranoia Purge:** The Allied Command executed hundreds of suspected spies.  
* The Shadewalkers went deep underground, becoming enemies of the state.

### 2511 S.A. – The Floating Market of Zure

**Summary:**  
A massive chunk of land ripped up by Paroxysm magic became a neutral zone in the high atmosphere. Known as **Zure**, it became the "Casablanca" of the war—a place where Allied spies, Drakona defectors, Cloudborn Corsairs, and Shadewalkers met to trade intelligence and goods.  
**Consequences:**

* **Zure’s Grave:** The market was destroyed during the Skyfall. Its location is now a massive underwater ruin rumored to hold the lost treasury of the Shadewalkers and pre-Skyfall tech.

### 2550 S.A. – The Battle of Serpent’s Pass

**Summary:**  
A masterclass in aerial tactics. Mirennian Admiral **Lyra Wind-Caller** lured a superior Drakona fleet into a narrow mountain pass. She then detonated pre-planted magical charges on the cliffs, creating an avalanche of rock and snow that swatted the Drakona ships out of the sky.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Turning Point:** The Drakona realized they were losing the tactical war. They retreated to their high-altitude Cloud Cities to regroup.

### 2595 S.A. – The Privateer Accords

**Summary:**  
Desperate for more ships, the Allied Command offered a pardon to the **Pirate Confederacy** of Serpent's Tooth. If they fitted their sea-ships with flight-cores and fought for the Alliance, they would be granted legal status.  
**Consequences:**

* The "Sky-Privateers" wreaked havoc on Drakona supply lines using hit-and-run tactics.

### 2650 S.A. – The Long Stalemate

**Summary:**  
The war settled into a grind. The Drakona held the "High Sky" (above 20,000 feet), while the Allies held the "Low Sky" and the ground. Neither side could strike the other's heart. The world was exhausted.

### 2680 S.A. – The Reconnaissance Mission (Operation: Icarus)

**Summary:**  
The Coalition of Mages authorized a suicide mission. An elite team, disguised with high-level illusions, infiltrated the primary Drakona Cloud City, **Aethelgard**.  
**Consequences:**

* **Discovery of Aeromancy:** They learned the cities were held aloft by a continuous, localized ritual of Air Magic.  
* **The Moral Crisis:** They saw Drakona nurseries, schools, and civilians. They returned with the knowledge to win the war, but also the knowledge that winning meant genocide.

### Phase III: The Skyfall (2681 – 2700 S.A.)

### 2685 S.A. – The Conclave of Luminas

**Summary:**  
The Allied leadership debated the "Icarus Option." The military generals demanded the strike. The religious leaders of the Church pleaded for mercy.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Decision:** With the Drakona preparing a "Final Descent" weapon (a magically induced ice age), the Alliance voted to strike.  
* Elaruniel was tasked with designing the ritual.

### 2688 S.A. – The Earth-Anchor Ritual (The Day the Sky Fell)

**Summary:**  
On the winter solstice, thousands of Allied mages ascended the highest peaks of Vaeloris. At noon, they cast the *Earth-Anchor*. It was a metaphysical hook that grabbed the concept of "Flight" within the Drakona’s Aeromancy matrix and violently grounded it.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Cataclysm:** The great Cloud Cities plummeted. Millions of Drakona died in the impact.  
* **Collateral Damage:** The ritual was too broad. It destabilized *all* flight magic. Every Allied Airship, every Privateer vessel, every floating island also fell.  
* **The Silence:** In one hour, the Age of Flight ended. The skies were empty.

### 2690 S.A. – The Ground War (The Mop-Up)

**Summary:**  
The survivors of the Cloud Cities crawled from the wreckage. Broken, flightless, and surrounded, the Drakona fought a desperate last stand in the ruins.  
**Consequences:**

* Brutal, close-quarters combat in the wreckage zones.

### 2700 S.A. – The Treaty of the Broken Peaks

**Summary:**  
The Drakona Empress, **Valkiss**, surrendered to the Allied Command.  
**Terms:**

* **Unconditional Surrender:** The Drakona Empire was dissolved.  
* **The Binding:** The Drakona were magically bound to the earth; their ability to naturally fly was stripped as part of the surrender ritual (though some argue this was a side effect of the Skyfall).  
* **Integration:** Mankind, weary of death, chose not to exterminate them. The Drakona were granted a "reservation" in the ruins of their greatest city, beginning a long, painful era of second-class citizenship.

**New Geopolitical Reality:**

* **The Magocracy:** The Coalition of Mages, having wielded the power to pull down the sky, emerged as the only force capable of maintaining order. They began to consolidate power, transitioning from a military alliance to a global government.  
* **The Dark Age of the Skies:** Travel returned to the speed of a horse and a sailing ship. The world shrank once again.

## Epoch VII: The Age of the Mage Ascendancy (2701 – 3280 S.A.)

**Overview:**  
The world survives the Skyfall, but at a terrible cost. The **Grey Rot** (The Fraying) emerges as a direct consequence of the massive magical energies used to ground the Drakona. The Coalition of Mages rebrands as the **Magocracy**, positioning themselves as the only doctors capable of treating this wound. They trade security for liberty, enforcing a global peace that stifles innovation and enforces rigid social stratification for the "lesser" races (Orkin and Drakona).

|  |
| :---- |

### Phase I: The Rise of the Magocracy & The Grey Rot (2701 – 2866 S.A.)

### 2701 S.A. – The Corporeal Tax

**Summary:**  
In the immediate aftermath of the Skyfall, billions of tons of wreckage littered the continent. The Coalition of Mages, now the de facto world government, instituted the *Corporeal Tax*. Every able-bodied citizen was conscripted for three years of labor to clear the ruins of the Cloud Cities.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Salvage Economy:** Entire cities sprang up around the crash sites to harvest "Sky-Stone" and "Draconic Steel."  
* **Mirennian Recovery:** The Mirennians, their sky-ports useless, became the masters of salvage logistics, rebuilding their wealth on the bones of their enemies.

### 2705 S.A. – The Outbreak of the Grey Rot

**Summary:**  
In the craters where the largest Cloud Cities fell, the fabric of reality thinned. Kael'Zorai’s corrupted essence seeped through the cracks. It manifested as the **Grey Rot**: a magical plague that turned flesh to ash and made stone weep blood.  
**Consequences:**

* **Panic:** Conventional healing magic accelerated the Rot.  
* **The Quarantine:** The Mages cordoned off vast swaths of land, condemning millions to death to save the rest.

### 2710 S.A. – The Rise of the Plague-Wardens (Gnomish Intervention)

**Summary:**  
While Human mages faltered, the Gnomes of the Mycelial Collective discovered that the Rot was a fungal-magical hybrid. They developed "Spore-Masks" and alchemical sprays that could slow the spread.  
**Consequences:**

* **Gnomish Prestige:** The Gnomes became the frontline medics of the crisis.  
* **The Symbiotic Shift:** Many Gnomes began incorporating the Rot into their study of the "Shared Pattern," sowing the seeds for the "Integrator" faction later in history.

### 2715 S.A. – Publication of *The Frayed Weave*

**Summary:**  
Haunted by her role in the Skyfall, **Elaruniel** emerged from seclusion in Aethelas. She published *The Frayed Weave*, a dense theoretical text arguing that the Skyfall didn't just break the Drakona; it broke the immune system of the universe.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Heresy:** The Magocracy denounced the book as alarmist, banning it in human lands.  
* **The Academic Underground:** It became the bible of the Aen’valar and the secret societies of Novaterra.

### 2725 S.A. – The District of Scales (Drakona Segregation)

**Summary:**  
The surviving Drakona, stripped of flight and dignity, were relocated to the "District of Scales" in the ruins of their former capital (now grounded). The Magocracy classified them as "Wards of the State."  
**Consequences:**

* **Forced Labor:** Drakona were used as living batteries to power the anti-Rot wards, draining their life force.  
* **The Simmering Rage:** A resistance movement, the "Broken Wing," formed in the shadows.

### 2750 S.A. – The Technocratic Schism

**Summary:**  
Gearhaven faced a crisis. The Mages wanted to hoard all magical resources to fight the Rot, while the Engineers wanted to rebuild the economy. The **Cog-Council** split.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Binding Accord II:** A new constitution was written. Gearhaven formally recognized the Magocracy's authority over *magic*, but retained absolute autonomy over *machine*.

### 2780 S.A. – The Night of the weeping Stone

**Summary:**  
A massive outbreak of Grey Rot occurred in the Dwarven surface city of **Iron-Beach** (Novaterra). The Magocracy proposed "cleansing" the city (burning it). The Thulkorûn Dwarves refused, fighting off the Mages while Gnomish medics cured the outbreak.  
**Consequences:**

* Proven that the Magocracy’s "burn it all" tactic was not the only way.  
* Deepened the trust between the "Wandering Kin" (Dwarves/Gnomes) and the Free Peoples.

### 2815 S.A. – The Containment Declaration

**Summary:**  
After a century of struggle, the Magocracy declared the Grey Rot "Contained." They erected massive, permanent "Pylons of Warding" around the infected zones (The Scars).  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Lie:** The Rot was not gone; it was merely caged. The Magocracy took credit for saving the world, solidifying their political legitimacy.

### 2851 S.A. – The Fall of Vrak'Thul (The Final Resistance)

**Summary:**  
The fortress-city of Vrak'Thul, a haven for renegade mages and Drakona loyalists, refused to pay the Magocracy’s tithes. The Ruling Council decided to make an example.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Arcane Nullifier:** The Magocracy deployed a weapon that created a "Anti-Magic Vacuum." The city, held together by old magic, crumbled into dust instantly.  
* **Total Submission:** No nation dared defy the Magocracy openly after this.

### 2866 S.A. – The First Decree (The Pax Magica)

**Summary:**  
With their power absolute, the Magocracy issued the *First Decree*: "War is inefficient. Order is paramount."  
**Terms:**

* **Abolition of Standing Armies:** Nations could keep "City Guards," but large field armies were illegal.  
* **The Ban on Aeromancy:** The study or use of Flight Magic was punishable by "Stilling" (lobotomy of the magical center of the brain).  
* **The Magister’s Watch:** A global police force of mages was established to enforce these laws.

### Phase II: The Enforced Peace & The Return of the Skies (2867 – 3250 S.A.)

### 2879 S.A. – The Charter of Civic Unity (The Orkin Question)

**Summary:**  
To rebuild the workforce, the Magocracy pressured the successor states of the Varnathi Empire to integrate the Orkin. The *Charter of Civic Unity* granted Orkin "Subject Status."  
**Consequences:**

* **The Quarters:** Orkin were allowed to live in cities but confined to specific districts.  
* **The Criminal Underground:** Excluded from legal guilds, Orkin formed the "Shadow-Syndicates" to survive.

### 2900 S.A. – The War of Whispers

**Summary:**  
Since armies were illegal, conflict shifted to espionage. The **Shadewalkers** entered a golden age, acting as proxies for national grievances. The Magocracy’s **Inquisitors** fought a shadow war against them.  
**Consequences:**

* Assassination became the primary tool of diplomacy.

### 2950 S.A. – The Steam Revolution

**Summary:**  
Barred from researching powerful magic, the engineers of Novaterra turned to physics. High-pressure steam engines, rifled muskets, and clockwork automation began to replace magical equivalents.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Leveller:** A peasant with a rifle could kill a mage. The balance of power began to shift imperceptibly back to the common man.

### 2962 S.A. – The "Broken Wing" Insurgency

**Summary:**  
A radical faction of flightless Drakona, the **Broken Wing**, launched a decades-long campaign of magical terrorism against the Magocracy. They bombed Mana-Pylons and assassinated Magisters to protest their confinement in the District of Scales.  
**Consequences:**

* **Justified Brutality:** These attacks justified the Magocracy's harsh policing in the eyes of the public.  
* **Moral Complexity:** It established the Drakona as an active internal threat, complicating the narrative of them being solely victims.

### 2996 S.A. – The Founding of the Grand University

**Summary:**  
To control the narrative, the Magocracy centralized all higher education in Mórenórë. The **Grand University** was ostensibly open to all, but the curriculum was heavily curated to promote the Magocracy’s version of history.  
**Consequences:**

* **Indoctrination:** Generations of leaders were raised to view the Magocracy as benevolent guardians.

### 3010 S.A. – The Loremaster's Mandate (The Elven Schism)

**Summary:**  
At the Aen'valar Conclave in Aethelas, **Elaruniel** ran for Loremaster Prime against **Archmage Voronwe** (a Magocracy loyalist). Voronwe argued for isolation; Elaruniel argued for engagement to fix the Fraying.  
**Consequences:**

* **Elaruniel’s Victory:** She won by a landslide of academic support.  
* **The Cold Shoulder:** Aethelas began to drift away from the Magocracy’s influence, becoming a bastion of independent thought.

### 3050 S.A. – The Crack in the Door (Dwarven Trade)

**Summary:**  
The Nûm-Khazalûn of Kharnzarak, seeing the stability of the surface, allowed a single trade gate to open. They would sell steel and gold, but buy nothing (fearing surface taint).  
**Consequences:**

* The influx of high-quality Dwarven steel fueled the industrial revolution in Gearhaven and the Federation.

### 3100 S.A. – The Pirate Revival (The Glider Fleets)

**Summary:**  
The Pirate Confederacy of Serpent's Tooth, grounded for centuries, adapted. They developed "Wave-Skimmers"—extremely fast hydrofoil ships—and early non-magical gliders launched from cliffs to scout prey.  
**Consequences:**

* Smuggling returned to the high seas, challenging the Magocracy’s trade monopolies.

### 3134 S.A. – The Second Age of Flight (The Ironclad Era)

**Summary:**  
The pivotal moment of the late epoch. Engineers in Gearhaven, collaborating with Mirennian architects, unveiled the *Aether-Glider*. Using "Lift-Gas" (refined from alchemical processes) and steam propellers, they achieved flight without Aeromancy.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Legal Loophole:** The Magocracy tried to ban them, but the *First Decree* specifically forbade "Flight Magic." The machines used no magic. The Magocracy, bound by its own rigid laws, could not legally stop them without inciting a global revolt.  
* **The Sky-Rush:** The Mirennian League poured its fortune into building a massive fleet of commercial airships, reclaiming their title as masters of the sky.

### 3150 S.A. – The Sky-Tax Crisis

**Summary:**  
Humiliated by the loophole, the Magocracy imposed a heavy tax on "Atmospheric Displacement." The Merchant Houses of Mirennia refused to pay. The Federation of City-States mobilized its (illegal) reserves of battle-mages.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Brink of War:** The Magocracy backed down, agreeing to a reduced tax in exchange for the right to inspect all cargo. It was the first time the Magocracy blinked.

### 3217 S.A. – The Whisperwood Outbreak

**Summary:**  
A resurgence of the Grey Rot occurred in the Barony of Whisperwood. The Magocracy responded with terrifying efficiency, burning the entire barony to the ground in a night.  
**Consequences:**

* **Fear Renewed:** The public, growing rebellious, was reminded of *why* the Magocracy ruled. "Better the Mages than the Rot."

### 3220 S.A. – The "Red-Ink" Crisis

**Summary:**  
The Shadewalkers, realizing they could not defeat the Magocracy militarily, attacked the economy. They manipulated the exchange rate of Mana-Crystals (the global currency standard), causing hyperinflation that threatened to collapse the Magocracy's government.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Banker’s Guild:** The Magocracy was forced to hire the Shadewalkers to stabilize the banks. By the Third Age, the "Thieves' Guild" effectively runs the global banking system, making them untouchable by the law.

### 3250 S.A. – The Ashfall Compact

**Summary:**  
The Magocracy formally recognized the **Barony of Ashfall** (a nation of dark industrialism and necromantic pragmatism) as a sovereign state. They needed a source of "unethical" research that they could deny involvement with.  
**Consequences:**

* Ashfall became the dark laboratory of the world, supplying the Magocracy with forbidden weapons to fight the Rot.

### Phase III: The Convention of Accord (3280 S.A.)

### 3280 S.A. – The Convention of Accord

**Summary:**  
Sensing the shifting tides—the rise of technology, the restlessness of the nations, and the subtle return of the Rot—the Ruling Council of the Magocracy summoned all world leaders to Aurumport.  
**The Goal:**  
To codify the status quo into a permanent international law that would prevent the outbreak of a Third Great War.

**The Laws of Accord:**

1. **Sovereignty:** The borders of the Varnathi Democracy, Mirennian League, Federation, Technocracy, and Desertborn Confederacy were fixed in perpetuity.  
2. **The Neutrality of Aerinórë:** The northern continent was declared a "Sanctuary of Nature," forbidden to industrial development or military presence.  
3. **The Rights of the Arcane:** The Magocracy retained exclusive jurisdiction over all magical crimes and the regulation of magical education.  
4. **The Free Skies:** Non-magical air travel was guaranteed as a universal right, protected by the Magocracy (in exchange for the inspection rights).

**Consequences:**

* **End of the Second Age:** The signing of the Accord marked the official transition to the **Third Age**.  
* **The Stage is Set:** The world is stable, but rigid. Ancient grudges are suppressed by law, not resolved. The Rot festers in the dark. The "lesser" races chafe under their chains. And the Magocracy sits on a throne of lies, terrified that the Fraying they claimed to fix is about to unravel everything.

## Epoch III Additions: The Age of Expansion

### 950 S.A. – The Rise of the Vermillion Dynasties

**Summary:**  
While the north focused on the rise of the Merchant Princes, a line of Sorcerer-Kings arose in the deep southern oases. Using primitive "Blood-Binding" rituals found in First Age ruins, they united the scattered tribes into a terrifying military hegemony known as the Vermillion Dynasties. They rapidly conquered the southern tip of the Varnathi Republic and seized the salt-flats of the Mirennian League.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Trade Cut:** They severed the overland trade routes, forcing Mirennian merchants to sail further south into uncharted waters, directly accelerating the discovery of Novaterra.  
* **Legacy of Ruin:** The dynasties collapsed in 1150 S.A. due to magical drought and inbreeding, leaving behind red-stone ziggurats and a fierce culture of independence that would later birth the Federation of City-States.

### 1070 S.A. – The "False Dawn" of Port Farrick (Altered Entry)

**Summary:**  
During the blockade by Captain Vane, the Governor of Port Farrick, feeling abandoned by the Republic, secretly petitioned the Mirennian League for annexation. In a stunning act of realpolitik, the League—fearing a total war on two fronts—refused the petition and leaked it to the Republic to buy goodwill.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Purge:** When the Republic broke the blockade, they did not liberate the city; they purged it. The nobility was stripped of titles and wealth.  
* **Separatist Roots:** This betrayal created a deep-seated "Separatist" culture in Port Farrick that persists into the Third Age, making the city a hotbed for anti-Imperial sentiment.

### 1280 S.A. – The "Green-Thumb" Strike

**Summary:**  
During the height of the "War of the Barren Hills," Varnathi legions trampled the Halfling harvest fields of Greenmeadow to create supply roads. The Halflings did not fight back with weapons; they simply stopped exporting grain and ale. Within three months, the Imperial Army faced mutiny due to starvation and plummeting morale.  
**Consequences:**

* **Treaty of the Hearth:** The Republic was forced to sign a humiliating treaty granting Greenmeadow permanent neutrality and forbidding military boots on their soil.  
* **Soft Power:** Established the Halflings as a "Sleeping Giant" of logistics whom no army dares anger.

## Epoch IV Additions: The Arcane Revolution

### 1460 S.A. – The Kingdom of the Silver Hand

**Summary:**  
**General Aric the Silver**, a hero of the Republic, discovered his own latent magical ability. Rather than submit to the Mage-Purge, he turned his legion against the Senate. He carved out a sanctuary kingdom on the borderlands, briefly allying with local Orkin tribes to hold the line. For fifty years, it was a utopia for the persecuted.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Great Betrayal:** As Aric succumbed to the Fraying (madness from raw magic), the Orkin leaders betrayed him to the Republic in exchange for amnesty. The Republic accepted the help, crushed the Kingdom, and then slaughtered the Orkin anyway.  
* **Stigma:** Created the cultural stigma of the "Mage-Tyrant" in human lands and cemented Orkin cynicism toward human alliances.

### 1550 S.A. – The Mirennian Civil War (Salt vs. Silk)

**Summary:**  
As the black market for magic grew, the Mirennian League fractured. The "Old Money" (Salt/Timber merchants) wanted to uphold the ban on magic to appease the Republic. The "New Money" (Smugglers/Artifact Traders) wanted to embrace it. The conflict escalated into open naval warfare.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Southern Shift:** The Southern Cities, hiring Thulkorûn mercenaries and battle-mages, defeated the Northern traditionalists.  
* **Moral Flexibility:** The capital and moral center of the League shifted south. The League became a nation that valued profit over tradition or law, laying the groundwork for their technological dominance later.

## Epoch V Additions: The Silent Skies

### 1880 S.A. – The Technocratic Blockade & The Iron Pact

**Summary:**  
The industrializing Technocracy of Gearhaven (Novaterra) ran short of "Brimstone" (high-grade fuel). They blockaded the ports of the Federation of City-States (Mainland) to force a favorable trade deal. In a shocking twist, the Varnathi Empire—historically the enemy of the Federation—formed the **Iron Pact**, a temporary military alliance with the Federation to break the blockade.  
**Consequences:**

* **Military Modernization:** For 30 years, Imperial discipline mixed with Federalist innovation. The Federation emerged from the war with a modernized military doctrine that would eventually allow them to eclipse the Empire.  
* **Resource Realism:** Proved that resource needs superseded ideology.

### 2100 S.A. – The Gnomish "Root-Hack"

**Summary:**  
As global tensions rose, the Forest Gnomes of the mainland felt threatened by the expanding human armies. Instead of building walls, they modified the *mycelial network* of the forests to act as a continental vibration-sensor.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Info-Brokers:** The **Myco-Whisperers** became the world's premier intelligence agency. They could track troop movements anywhere there were roots, selling this data to the highest bidder (often the Lothari or Elves) to maintain the balance of power.

## Epoch VI Additions: The Dragonfall

### 2350 S.A. – The Establishment of the Drakona Protectorates

**Summary:**  
The Drakona did not simply bomb the northern Varnathi provinces into submission. They offered an ultimatum: "Serve us, and live better than you did under the corrupt Senate." Many provinces accepted, forming the **Principality of the Scale**, a human nation loyal to the Dragons that fought against the Allied Command.  
**Consequences:**

* **The Permanent Divide:** After the war, these humans were branded traitors. This created a permanent "North-South" divide in Varnathi culture. Northerners are seen as "Dragon-Thralls," while Southerners are "Warmongers."  
* **Resistance Cells:** Conversely, this era birthed a fierce underground resistance in the north that persists as anti-government militias in the Third Age.

### 2500 S.A. – The Floating Market of Zure

**Summary:**  
A massive chunk of land ripped up by Paroxysm magic became a neutral zone in the high atmosphere. Known as **Zure**, it became the "Casablanca" of the war—a place where Allied spies, Drakona defectors, Cloudborn Corsairs, and Shadewalkers met to trade intelligence and goods.  
**Consequences:**

* **Zure’s Grave:** The market was destroyed during the Skyfall. Its location is now a massive underwater ruin rumored to hold the lost treasury of the Shadewalkers and pre-Skyfall tech.
