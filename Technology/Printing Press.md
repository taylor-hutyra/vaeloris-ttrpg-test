# Printing Press

The Printing Press is a mechanical device for mass-producing written text using movable type, invented in SA:1360 by the Gnome tinkerer [[Bimble Pockets]] in the mixed Halfling/Gnome burrow-city of [[Gear-Hollow]]. Its invention triggered an explosion of literacy and information-sharing that fundamentally altered the power structures of [[Vaeloris]].

## Invention

[[Bimble Pockets]] was a [[Forest Gnomes|Forest Gnome]] artisan known for her obsessive attention to precision. Working in [[Gear-Hollow]] -- a settlement where Gnomish craft and Halfling communal culture merged into a uniquely collaborative environment -- she developed the movable type system: individual metal letter blocks that could be arranged, inked, and pressed onto paper to produce identical copies of any text.

The key innovation was the type itself. Using techniques borrowed from [[Rock Gnomes|Rock Gnome]] gem-cutting and metallurgy, Pockets cast each letter from a durable tin-antimony alloy with tolerances measured in fractions of a hair. The letters could be rearranged endlessly, making the press adaptable to any language or content.

## Impact

Before the press, written knowledge was the province of the wealthy, the religious, and the powerful. Scribes copied texts by hand, a process so slow and expensive that a single book could cost more than a year's wages. The [[Shadewalkers]] -- the espionage guild that had made information their currency -- thrived in this environment of controlled scarcity.

The press shattered that monopoly. Within a decade of its invention:

- **Literacy rates doubled** in regions where the press was adopted
- **Pamphlets** became a tool of political discourse, allowing common people to debate ideas that had previously circulated only in elite councils
- **Guild secrets** began to leak to the public, threatening the monopoly of artisan guilds and the [[Shadewalkers]]
- **Academic texts** became affordable, accelerating the spread of knowledge from the [[Grand University]] and [[Mórenórë]]

## Cultural Significance

The press embodied the Gnomish philosophy of "Kelos on mori" -- "Growth in silence." It was a quiet invention that changed the world not through force but through the steady, patient spread of knowledge. The Halflings of [[Gear-Hollow]] embraced it with characteristic enthusiasm, establishing printing cooperatives that produced everything from agricultural almanacs to collections of [[Hearthsongs]].

## Resistance

The press was not universally welcomed. The [[Shadewalkers]] attempted to suppress it, recognizing that freely available information undermined their business model. The [[Magocracy]] later sought to regulate and license presses, fearing the spread of banned texts like [[The Frayed Weave]]. In the sealed halls of [[Kharnzarak]], the press was unknown; the Stone-Sworn continued to carve their Stone-Lays by hand, as they had for millennia.

## Legacy

The Printing Press is widely regarded as one of the most consequential inventions of the Second Age. It democratized knowledge, empowered the common citizen, and laid the groundwork for the intellectual revolutions of the Third Age. Bimble Pockets herself is honored in [[Gear-Hollow]] with an annual festival -- the Day of the Open Page -- where free pamphlets are distributed to all who attend.
