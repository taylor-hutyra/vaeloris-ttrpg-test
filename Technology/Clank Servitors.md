# Clank Servitors

Clank Servitors -- commonly called "Clanks" -- are autonomous mechanical constructs created through [[Technomancy]], designed to perform dangerous, repetitive, or precision tasks that would be impractical or hazardous for mortal workers. They are the signature creation of [[Gearhaven]] and a defining symbol of the [[Elm-korin]] philosophy of perfection through craft.

## Design

A Clank Servitor is a mechanical humanoid (or sometimes multi-limbed construct) built from brass, steel, and rune-inscribed alloys. Its internal structure combines Gnomish [[Clockwork Mechanisms]] for physical articulation with an [[Arcane Conduits|arcane conduit]] core that provides motive energy and basic decision-making capability.

The "brain" of a Clank is a crystalline logic matrix -- a lattice of focusing crystals inscribed with operational instructions. This matrix allows the Clank to interpret simple commands, navigate familiar environments, and adapt its behavior within narrow parameters. Clanks are not sentient; they operate on a level comparable to a well-trained animal, following routines and responding to stimuli without self-awareness.

Key variants include:

- **Labor Clanks**: Heavy-framed units designed for mining, construction, and foundry work
- **Service Clanks**: Lighter units for domestic tasks, courier duties, and warehouse management
- **Precision Clanks**: Delicate units with multiple articulated appendages for assembly work and fine crafting
- **Guardian Clanks**: Armored units equipped with defensive capabilities for security and patrol

## History

The first Clank Servitors were assembled in [[Gearhaven]] during the city's founding era (SA:1485 onward). The [[Elm-korin]] engineers, driven by their philosophy of perfection, sought to create machines that could perform the imperfect, dangerous tasks of building a city so that mortal hands could focus on higher work.

The early models were crude -- lurching, noisy contraptions that frequently seized up or wandered into walls. But decades of refinement, drawing on [[Rock Gnomes|Gnomish]] clockwork mastery and human arcane innovation, produced increasingly sophisticated machines. By the late Second Age, Clanks were a common sight in Gearhaven's streets, workshops, and docks.

## The Masked Rebellion

In SA:1500, the proliferation of Clanks triggered a social crisis. The "Mundane" workers of Gearhaven -- those without magical ability who performed manual labor -- saw the Clanks as a direct threat to their livelihoods. A group called [[The Masked]] seized the city's geothermal plants and threatened to destroy them unless non-magical workers were granted political representation.

The resulting compromise established the Guild of Labor with seats on the Cog-Council, ensuring that automation would supplement rather than replace mortal workers. This principle -- that Clanks serve, not supplant -- became a core tenet of Gearhaven's governance.

## Ethical Considerations

The question of Clank sentience is a recurring philosophical debate. Are the more advanced models truly incapable of awareness, or have they simply not been given the means to express it? The [[Elm-korin]] maintain that their logic matrices are fundamentally different from consciousness, but dissenting scholars at the [[Grand University]] have noted that the most sophisticated Clanks exhibit behaviors -- hesitation, preference, apparent curiosity -- that resist easy mechanical explanation.

The existence of [[Necro-Industrial Machinery]] in [[Ashfall]] has further complicated the debate. If corpses can be animated to perform labor, what is the moral distinction between a dead body following instructions and a metal body doing the same?
