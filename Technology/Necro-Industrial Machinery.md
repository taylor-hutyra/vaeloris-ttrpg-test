# Necro-Industrial Machinery

Necro-Industrial Machinery encompasses the death-powered factories, forges, and automated systems of the sovereign barony of [[Ashfall]]. First deployed during Ashfall's secession in SA:3100, these machines represent the technological application of [[Necro-Industrialism]] -- the fusion of necromantic magic with industrial processes. They are the most controversial and reviled technology in [[Vaeloris]].

## Principles

Where [[Technomancy]] powers its machines with ambient mana channeled through [[Arcane Conduits]], Necro-Industrial Machinery draws its energy from death itself. The decomposition of organic matter releases a form of magical energy that necromancers call *mortis flux* -- a cold, entropic force that is the antithesis of the creative mana used by conventional mages.

Ashfall's engineers learned to harvest, concentrate, and channel this energy through specially treated conduits made from bone-ite (a composite material of calcite, processed bone ash, and iron filings). These bone-ite conduits conduct mortis flux with high efficiency, enabling the construction of machines that operate continuously without conventional fuel.

## Key Systems

### Death-Forges
Industrial smelters that use the thermal energy released during magical decomposition to melt ore and shape metal. Death-Forges burn hotter and more consistently than conventional furnaces, producing steel of exceptional quality. The fuel is a slurry of alchemically preserved organic matter fed into combustion chambers inscribed with necromantic runes.

### Bone-Looms
Automated textile and manufacturing facilities operated by undead laborers. These reanimated workers -- stripped of consciousness and bound to repetitive task-loops -- operate looms, presses, and assembly lines around the clock. They require no food, no rest, and no wages. They do require periodic re-animation as the bodies degrade.

### Risen Carriages
Transport vehicles powered by necromantic engines. Unlike steam engines, which require water and fuel stations, a Risen Carriage draws energy from a sealed mortis flux reservoir, giving it a range limited only by the degradation of its bone-ite conduits.

### The Animation Mills
The foundational infrastructure of Ashfall's economy. In these facilities, corpses are prepared for industrial reuse: preserved through alchemical treatment, inscribed with binding runes, and infused with sustained mortis flux to create compliant undead laborers.

## Military Applications

Ashfall's military advantage lies in attrition. The Risen Legions -- formations of undead soldiers commanded by living necromantic officers -- replenish their losses from the battlefield dead, including enemy casualties. The [[Magocracy]] invaded Ashfall three times; each time, the invading army's own fallen soldiers rose to fight against them. The mathematical impossibility of winning such a war of attrition led to the [[Ashfall Compact]] (SA:3250), in which the Magocracy recognized Ashfall's sovereignty.

## Human Cost

Living workers in Ashfall's factories suffer from chronic exposure to mortis flux. Symptoms include pallid skin, premature aging, chronic fatigue, and "Gray-Lung" -- a respiratory condition caused by inhaling bone-ite particulate saturated with death energy. Life expectancy in Ashfall's industrial districts is roughly half the continental average.

The barony sustains its living population through a constant influx of the desperate, the criminal, and the exiled -- those with no other options. This grim reality is Ashfall's darkest open secret.

## Global Hypocrisy

Every major nation in Vaeloris officially condemns Necro-Industrial Machinery. The [[Grand University]] classifies it as an ethical violation. Religious orders call it desecration. The [[Waldkyn]] call it the ultimate "Song-Wrong."

Yet Ashfall's products -- cheap steel, tireless labor, and forbidden weapons -- flow through the [[Shadewalkers|Gray Market]] into every nation on the continent. The world publicly reviles what it privately consumes, and Ashfall thrives on the contradiction.
