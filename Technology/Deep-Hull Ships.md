# Deep-Hull Ships

Deep-Hull Ships are the signature maritime innovation of the [[Mirennian League]], first constructed around SA:35 on the eastern coast of the mainland. Designed to withstand the rougher, unregulated oceans of the post-divine world, they transformed the Mirennians from coastal subsistence fishers into the dominant naval power of [[Vaeloris]].

## Origins

When the gods withdrew at the start of the Second Age, the oceans changed. The divine influence of [[Gwynfaer]] (The Windweaver) had kept currents predictable and storms manageable. Without that oversight, the seas became treacherous -- erratic tides, sudden squalls, and rogue waves that shattered conventional shallow-draft vessels.

The ancestors of the Mirennians, separated from the highland [[Varnathi Empire|Varnathi]] by the Iron-Peak Range, had no choice but to adapt. They turned to the sea for survival, and necessity drove innovation. The first Deep-Hull ships featured reinforced keels that sat lower in the water, broader beams for stability, and compartmentalized holds that could survive partial flooding without capsizing.

## Design

The Deep-Hull design prioritized resilience over speed. Key features included:

- **Reinforced keel**: A single massive timber, often ironwood, running the full length of the ship
- **Compartmentalized holds**: Watertight bulkheads that prevented a single breach from sinking the vessel
- **Deeper draft**: The hull sat lower in the water, reducing the risk of capsizing in heavy seas
- **Outrigger stabilizers**: Removable lateral supports for open-ocean crossings

These ships were heavier and slower than their predecessors, but they survived where lighter craft did not. Mirennian sailors developed the saying "Sails full or none at all" -- a reflection of the all-or-nothing commitment required for deep-sea voyaging.

## Impact

The Deep-Hull enabled the transition from coastal fishing to deep-sea whaling and exploration. By SA:145, Mirennian pathfinders had charted the entire eastern coastline, discovering the Safe-Harbor Currents that allowed navigation through treacherous reefs. By SA:275, the most successful ship captains had consolidated into Merchant Houses, and Deep-Hull trading vessels linked every port on the continent.

The ultimate achievement of Deep-Hull technology came in SA:752, when Navigator [[Tynna Swift-Tide]] sailed further east than any before and discovered [[Novaterra]]. Without the Deep-Hull's ability to endure weeks of open ocean, the discovery would have been impossible.

## Evolution

When [[Wind-Shaping]] was codified by the [[Guild of Tides]] in SA:550, the Deep-Hull design was adapted to take full advantage. Wind-Shaper platforms were added to the foredeck, and mast-mounted "wind-harps" became standard equipment. A Deep-Hull with a Wind-Shaper aboard could sail against the wind, giving the [[Mirennian League]] absolute naval superiority that even the [[Varnathi Empire]]'s legions could not challenge.

Later generations saw the introduction of arcane enhancements: rune-reinforced hulls, mana-lantern navigation arrays, and eventually steam-hybrid propulsion from [[Gearhaven]] engineers. But the fundamental Deep-Hull architecture -- deep keel, compartmentalized holds, rugged construction -- remained the standard for ocean-going vessels well into the Third Age.
