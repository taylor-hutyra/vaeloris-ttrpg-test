# Bronr'n Legacy

The **Bronr'n Legacy** is a clandestine society of artisans, engineers, and scholars hidden within the halls of [[Kharnzarak]]. They secretly revere the Pillar of Growth, [[Bronr'n]], and believe that the kingdom's stagnation will be its doom. They are the only hope for radical change from within the mountain.

## The Pillar of Growth

While orthodox [[N'om-Khazal'on]] doctrine venerates all Four Pillars of Craft, it places overwhelming emphasis on [[Tharn'om]] (Stone) and [[Farrazul]] (Gem). The Legacy remembers that [[Bronr'n]] -- the Pillar of Growth, Rootwork, and Shaping -- represents change, adaptation, and the living world. They believe [[Kharnzarak]]'s rejection of Bronr'n's teaching has left the kingdom brittle and dying.

## History

The Legacy traces its spiritual roots to the [[Bronr'n Clan]], the reformist faction blamed for the catastrophic [[Earthwake of Kharnzarak]] in SA:1200. When [[High King Thorgar VI]] scapegoated the clan and drove them into exile, some reformists remained behind, going underground. They preserved forbidden knowledge and waited for the day the mountain would need them.

## Operations

Members of the Legacy meet in hidden workshops deep in abandoned tunnels, studying forbidden [[Elm-korin]] schematics and [[Gnomish]] designs. They experiment with techniques that blend traditional Dwarven craftsmanship with innovations from the outside world -- heresy punishable by the [[Ritual of Shattering]] and exile. Communication between cells uses the old Dwarvish phrase "Khaz'on dur" -- "We chisel in silence."

## Recruitment

The Legacy quietly courts sympathetic members of the [[Gulgrym Clan]], recognizing that the Deep-Miners represent the indispensable workforce for any reform movement. They also seek out disillusioned younger members of the Guild Clans who chafe under the rigid caste system. Recruitment is slow and cautious -- a single informer could doom the entire network.

## Philosophy

The Legacy believes that [[Kharnzarak]]'s isolation and orthodoxy, championed by the [[Barazkor Clan]], will lead to the kingdom's eventual collapse. They point to the Earthwake as proof that the mountain itself is failing under the weight of stagnation. Their motto, drawn from the forbidden Stone-Lay of Bronr'n, is: "What does not grow, crumbles."

## Enemies

Discovery by the [[Barazkor Clan]] would mean death or exile for every member. [[Lord Durin Stone-Fist]], the king's enforcer, actively hunts for signs of reformist activity within the mountain.
