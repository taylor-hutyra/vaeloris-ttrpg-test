# Silent Network

The **Silent Network** is the spiritual and political leadership of the [[Lothari]] people, a council of the most respected mystics and visionaries from each of the major desert clans. They are the keepers of memories the rest of the world has forgotten.

## Structure

The Silent Network is led by a body known as the **Council of Dreamers**. They do not rule by decree but by interpreting dreams and celestial patterns to guide their people's movements and decisions. Their leadership is a matter of spiritual authority, not political power. Each major clan sends its most gifted dreamer to sit on the Council.

## Purpose

Formally organized in SA:1350, the Silent Network was born from a conviction that the frantic pace of the [[Age of Expansion]] was erasing the past. The Lothari mystics began archiving histories, artifacts, and myths in hidden desert vaults scattered across the [[Great Desert]]. They believed the "Age of Noise" would eventually collapse, and the world would need its memory back.

## Methods

The Network operates through secrecy and subtlety, reflecting the Lothari cultural values of memory, silence, and vision. Their agents move as travelers, storytellers, and merchants along the ancient trade routes of the [[Desertborn Confederacy]]. The Lothari saying "Even silence has a pattern" captures their belief that truth hides in what others overlook.

## Cultural Role

Among the Lothari, the Silent Network is intertwined with the spiritual practice of dream-interpretation rooted in the myth of [[The Breathless Dream]] -- the sleeping god whose thoughts became reality. The Network's mystics conduct fire-circle rituals and read the patterns of sand and starlight to preserve the deep history of [[Vaeloris]].

## Archives

The hidden desert vaults of the Silent Network contain preserved knowledge of the [[First Age]], [[The Paroxysm]], and forgotten histories that secular revisionism would have erased. These archives make the Network an invaluable -- and dangerous -- repository of truths that powerful nations would prefer to remain buried.
