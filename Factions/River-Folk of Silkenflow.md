# River-Folk of Silkenflow

The River-Folk are a semi-nomadic culture of [[Halflings]] who live on flotillas of interconnected river barges along the [[Silkenflow River]]. They travel the great rivers of the mainland, trading goods, telling stories, and fiercely avoiding land-based politics. They are masters of river navigation and renowned smugglers.

## Purpose & Goals

The River-Folk prize independence above all else. They answer to no town council or family patriarch---only to the river's current and the Flotilla-Council's consensus. Their purpose is trade, storytelling, and the preservation of a way of life that predates the settled [[Burrows]]. They carry goods and news between riverside towns, acting as a vital (if unofficial) artery of mainland commerce.

## Structure

The River-Folk are governed by the **Flotilla-Council**, where the captain of each barge has an equal vote. The flotilla is led by a single **Commodore**, elected for their knowledge of the river's currents and their skill in navigating tricky trade negotiations with riverside towns. Authority is practical and earned, not inherited.

## History

Some [[Halflings]] never settled. While the [[Hearthfolk]] built their [[Burrows]] and tended their fields, the River-Folk took to the waterways, discovering that a barge could be as cozy as any hill-hole and considerably more interesting. Over generations, they built a flotilla culture of interconnected families and independent captains, developing an expertise in river trade---and the occasional smuggling run---that made them indispensable to riverside communities. Their fierce independence has kept them free from the political entanglements of both the [[Hearthfolk Commonwealth]] and the Tallfolk nations.

## Notable Members
