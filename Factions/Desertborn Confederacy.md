# Desertborn Confederacy

The **Desertborn Confederacy** is the political alliance of the [[Lothari]] nomadic tribes, founded in SA:1300 when the chaos of the [[Guild Wars]] and the distraction of the [[Varnathi]] Republic allowed the desert peoples to seize control of the overland Silk Road.

## Formation

During the [[Age of Expansion]], the northern powers were consumed by colonial rivalries and internal strife. The [[Lothari]] mystics, guided by the [[Silent Network]], recognized the opportunity. Under their spiritual leadership, the scattered nomadic tribes united into a confederacy that claimed dominion over the ancient trade routes crossing the [[Great Desert]].

## The Toll of Sand

The Confederacy's power rests on a simple truth: any trade moving between East and West by land must pay tribute to the Desertborn. This "Toll of Sand" transformed the Lothari from impoverished nomads into a wealthy, neutral buffer state. Caravans that refuse to pay find their guides vanishing, their water sources drying up, and the desert itself turning hostile.

## Government

The Confederacy has no centralized government in the traditional sense. Authority flows through the clan structure, with each major tribe governing its own territory and trade route. The [[Silent Network]]'s Council of Dreamers provides spiritual guidance and resolves disputes between clans through dream-interpretation and the reading of celestial patterns.

## Culture

The Desertborn preserve the ancient Lothari traditions of memory, secrecy, and vision while the rest of the world races toward the future. Their semi-nomadic way of life, rooted in the myth of [[The Breathless Dream]], gives them a perspective no settled nation can match. The Lothari saying "Wind writes where we forget" reflects their role as custodians of history.

## Strategic Position

As a neutral buffer state, the Confederacy occupies a unique position in the geopolitics of [[Vaeloris]]. They trade with all sides and ally with none, preserving ancient history while the northern powers focus on conquest and commerce. Their control of the overland routes makes them indispensable to every nation's economy.
