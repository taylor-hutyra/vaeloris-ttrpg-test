# Admiralty Council

The **Admiralty Council** is the ruling body of the [[Mirennian League]], a corporate thalassocracy composed of the heads of the ten most powerful Merchant Houses. Each House's vote is weighted by the tonnage of their trade fleet, making politics a constant struggle for economic dominance. The Council is led by the **Master of Tides**, an elected position that rotates annually, serving as the League's chief diplomat and naval commander but holding little direct power over member-cities' internal affairs.

The Council's power has waxed and waned across the centuries. At its height, the codification of Wind-Shaping in SA:550 gave the Mirennian fleet absolute naval superiority, and the Council ruled the seas unchallenged. The devastating Guild Wars of SA:1100 shattered this unity when the [[Golden Compass Syndicate]] and [[Azure Hand]] turned on each other, reducing the League to a collection of warring merchant fiefdoms. Recovery was slow, and the Sacking of Mirenn in SA:2355 during the [[Drakona War]] destroyed the entire fleet at anchor.

The Mirennians adapted with characteristic pragmatism, rebuilding [[Mirenn]] as a sky-port and applying their centuries of naval tradition to airship warfare. After the Skyfall ended the Age of Flight, the League returned to its roots as masters of the sea, their superiority now earned through skill, design, and action rather than magical wind-shaping.

In the current era (TA:412), the Admiralty Council oversees a society where status is determined by wealth and influence. There are no hereditary nobles, only "Merchant Princes" whose power lasts only as long as their fortunes -- creating a culture of ruthless ambition, opportunity, and spectacular ruin.
