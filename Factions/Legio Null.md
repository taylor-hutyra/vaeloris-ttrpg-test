# Legio Null

The **Legio Null**, known as the Cold-Iron Inquisitors, was the Varnathi Republic's anti-mage enforcement corps, formed in SA:1450 to carry out the systematic persecution known as the Mage-Purge Wars. Clad in "Cold-Iron" armor -- believed to dampen magic -- and led by zealot-captains from the militaristic city-state of [[Castellum]], they were empowered to hunt, capture, and execute anyone who practiced, studied, or possessed arcane materials.

The Inquisitors' methods were brutal and indiscriminate. Entire villages were razed if they harbored a single "Spark-User." Their campaign triggered a massive diaspora as mages fled the Republic in droves -- some to the [[Mirennian League]], which publicly condemned magic while privately profiting from refugee passage, and others along the [[Star-Veiled Passage]], an underground railroad established by the [[Aen'valar]] Elves. The [[Wandering Kin]] developed lead-lined "Rune-Boxes" specifically to hide magical artifacts from the Inquisitors' searches.

The Legio Null's persecution had the perverse effect of radicalizing the very mages they hunted. Persecuted practitioners fought back with fire and lightning to defend their families, confirming the Republic's propaganda that they were monsters. This cycle of violence only ended when [[Gearhaven]], now a magitech superpower, intervened militarily. At the Battle of Crimson Field in SA:1695, 3,000 battle-mages annihilated a 50,000-strong Republican army -- proving conclusively that the Age of Steel was over.

The Legio Null was formally dissolved in SA:1700 after the humiliated Republic signed the Treaty of the Silver Quill, which legalized magic. Their legacy lives on in the lingering cultural fear of unregulated magic and in the cautionary tale of what happens when a state wages war on knowledge itself.
