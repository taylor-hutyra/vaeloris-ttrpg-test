# Whisper-Shamans

A smaller but influential faction of shamans among the [[Varr-Darg]] [[Orkin]] of the [[Barren Hills]]. The Whisper-Shamans commune with the spirits of the land and argue that the [[Varr-Darg]]'s suffering is a test of spiritual endurance, not merely physical might. They are wary of the "new voice" of [[Kael'Zorai]], sensing its "silent, unnatural order" as a perversion of [[Grom-Kain]]'s true will.

## Purpose & Goals

The Whisper-Shamans seek to guide their people through spiritual wisdom rather than war. They interpret [[Trak]] (endurance) as the strength to resist the easy path of violence and instead listen to what the land itself demands. They are the first among the Varr-Darg to suspect that the [[Silent Blight]] is not the homecoming it appears to be, but something fundamentally wrong.

## Structure

The Whisper-Shamans are a loose network of spiritual leaders embedded within the various Varr-Darg clans. They lack the military organization of the [[Bloodied Fist Clans]], relying instead on their spiritual authority and the respect traditionally afforded to those who speak with the land's spirits. Their influence waxes and wanes with the spiritual health of the clans.

## History

The shamanic tradition among the [[Orkin]] predates the faction divisions of the Varr-Darg. As the [[Bloodied Fist Clans]] pushed toward open war, the shamans who counseled patience and spiritual reflection were gradually marginalized into their own faction. [[Malgrimm]]'s old tribe belonged to this faction, making his eventual return a complex and dangerous event.
