# Mainframe Purity

**Mainframe Purity** is the dominant political faction within the [[Technocracy of Gearhaven]], composed of zealous believers in the [[Elm-korin]] philosophy who seek to create a world of perfect, sterile order through the fusion of magic and technology.

## Philosophy

The faction takes the [[Elm-korin]] core belief -- that mortal magic is a tool to achieve the perfect, logical order espoused by the [[Ulzammar]] -- to its most extreme conclusion. Where the original Elm-korin exiles sought to complete the work of perfection, Mainframe Purity seeks to impose it. They view the natural world's chaos, organic imperfection, and emotional irrationality as flaws to be engineered out of existence.

## The Blight Obelisk

Mainframe Purity's most ambitious and controversial project is the [[Blight Obelisk]], a technomantic device designed to "bless" the world with the [[Ulzammar]]'s silent, perfect logic. They see it as the ultimate tool of order -- a mechanism that would impose mathematical harmony on reality itself. The project has drawn fierce opposition from the [[Symbiotic Weave]], who detected a dangerous "null-harmonic" emanating from the device.

## Structure

As the dominant faction on the [[Cog-Council]], Mainframe Purity controls the direction of [[Gearhaven]]'s research and development. Their Guild-Masters hold the majority of seats, and their [[Arch-Artisan]] sets the city's "Grand Design" -- the long-term goals that define [[Gearhaven]]'s purpose. Membership is earned through demonstrated innovation in the field of pure technomancy.

## Culture

Mainframe Purity operates with the relentless, high-pressure intensity that defines [[Gearhaven]]'s meritocratic society. Collaboration is a tool for advancement, and failure means demotion. Members speak in the precise, structured cadence of the [[Elm-korin]], valuing logic over emotion and efficiency over sentiment.

## Rivals

The [[Symbiotic Weave]] is their primary opposition, arguing that pure logic is a brittle foundation and that true perfection requires the fusion of organic and mechanical. The conflict between these two factions is a struggle for the very soul of [[Gearhaven]].
