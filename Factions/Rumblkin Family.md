# Rumblkin Family

The Rumblkins are an offshoot [[Halflings|Halfling]] clan that lives in the foothills near the [[Burrows]], mining for stone and iron. They are gruff, pragmatic, and perpetually frustrated that the "dirt-scratchers" ([[Appledown Family|Appledowns]]) and "coin-counters" ([[Thistlewick Family|Thistlewicks]]) do not give them enough respect for providing the very foundations of Halfling homes.

## Purpose & Goals

The Rumblkins supply the raw materials---stone, iron, and occasionally rarer minerals---that make Halfling construction and tool-making possible. They believe their contribution is chronically undervalued. Their ongoing struggle is for fair trade prices and the status they feel their essential work deserves. Without Rumblkin stone, there would be no Burrows at all.

## Structure

The family is organized around mining operations, with experienced miners and foremen holding authority. Their lifestyle is harder and less comfortable than typical [[Hearthfolk]] living, which has bred a culture of toughness and plain-speaking. Family elders negotiate trade terms with the other major families and occasionally serve on town councils, though they feel perpetually outvoted by farming and merchant interests.

## History

While most [[Halflings]] were content to farm the lowlands, the Rumblkins were drawn to the foothills where stone and ore lay close to the surface. They built a reputation for hard work and reliability, but their physical distance from the main [[Burrows]] and their rougher manners have always placed them at the margins of Halfling social life. The constant, low-grade friction over trade prices and perceived status is the quiet engine of Commonwealth politics.
