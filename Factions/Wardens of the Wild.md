# Wardens of the Wild

The **Wardens of the Wild** were the first inter-racial paramilitary order in [[Vaeloris]], created in SA:195 through the Pact of the Wardens at the First Council of Races. The [[Waldkyn]], nature-attuned mediators, convened delegates from [[Vernath]] (Human), [[Hannanórë]] (Elf), and [[Kharnzarak]] (Dwarf) at the neutral [[Stone-Circle of Oth]] to address the growing threat of coordinated [[Orkin]] raids.

The Wardens' mandate was to patrol the dangerous spaces between civilizations and hunt the remnants of the [[The Paroxysm]] -- twisted creatures and magical anomalies that still plagued the land. Human knights, Dwarven defenders, and Elven archers served side by side, marking one of the first sustained cooperations between the mortal races. Notably, the Orkin were deliberately excluded from the Council. Elves cited their chaotic nature and Dwarves cited blood-feuds, formally casting the Orkin as the "Common Enemy" -- a stigma that would endure for millennia.

The Wardens proved their worth during the crises of the late First Epoch. They helped drive the Orkin into the [[Barren Hills]] during the Long March of SA:210, though they suffered devastating casualties against the semi-corporeal Echo-Beasts that emerged near the Scars of Dissonance around SA:340. Their finest hour came during the Gathering of Heroes in SA:375, when Wardens fought alongside warriors of all races and the Avatar of [[Bjalmund]] to defeat the Echo-Lord and seal the last great Scar.

The Wardens' legacy endures as the model for every multi-racial alliance that followed -- from the [[Allied Command]] of the Drakona War to the peacekeeping ambitions of the [[Magocracy]]. The Hero's Mound at the battle site remains a pilgrimage point.
