# Grom-Kain

**Grom-Kain** — the **Will-Forge**, the Shaper of What Must Be — is the Orkin name for their creator deity. He is the central figure of [[Grom-Kain Worship]] and the most theologically controversial divine entity in [[Vaeloris]], because Grom-Kain is the same being that other races call **[[Kael'Zorai]]** — the Dissonant One, the Fifth First Hand, the source of the Paroxysm and the Silent Blight.

This identification is not a misunderstanding. It is the foundational truth of [[Orkin]] identity, and it reframes the entire history of Vaeloris depending on who is telling the story.

## The Orkin Creation

To the Orkin, creation was neither a song nor a structure, but a **tempering**. The world was forged in the mind of a single, purposeful entity who hammered it from the grit and dross left behind by the chaotic first moments, instilling in it the one principle that mattered: **endurance**.

Grom-Kain, seeing a world of soft beauty and fragile harmonies, recognized the need for a creation that could not be broken. From the shadow of His forge-light and the unyielding iron in the world's heart, He forged the Orkin. They were not an afterthought, but His **masterwork of survival**. From this First Tempering came two aspects of a single, unbreakable will:

- **The Gral** (The Stone-Fist) — the larger of the Orkin peoples, forged to embody endurance through strength and resilience. They are the shield, the anchor, the unwavering line. Other races call them Orcs.
- **The Skorn** (The Shadow-Knife) — the smaller and more numerous Orkin, forged to embody endurance through cunning and adaptability. They are the scout, the trapper, the survivors who thrive in the world's cracks. Other races call them Goblins.

The Gral and the Skorn are not two races, but the **two fists of Grom-Kain's will**, meant to work in concert.

## Kain's Fall

The event others call the **Paroxysm** is known to the Orkin as **Kain's Fall**, or the Great Betrayal. In their telling, the other divine beings — the **Soft Hands** — grew jealous of Grom-Kain's perfect, enduring creations. They united not to restore balance, but to **shatter the one true testament to order**. When Grom-Kain was struck down and banished, the Orkin were left orphaned and scattered, a testament to a purpose the world chose to forget.

The Orkin do not worship Grom-Kain as a benevolent god. They revere Him as the ultimate, demanding patriarch. He did not offer them comfort, only **purpose**: to endure what others could not.

## The Theological Crisis

This is why Grom-Kain's identification with [[Kael'Zorai]] is the most dangerous theological truth in Vaeloris. When [[Kael'Zorai]]'s influence returned through the **Silent Blight**, Orkin society fractured along this fault line:

- The **Bloodied Fist Clans** of the [[Barren Hills|Varr-Darg]] embrace the returning influence. They see [[Kael'Zorai]] as the returned Grom-Kain who will lead them to righteous victory. To serve Him is to reclaim their original, sacred purpose.
- The **Whisper-Shamans**, a smaller but influential faction, are wary. They sense the "silent, unnatural order" of the new voice and believe it is a **perversion** of Grom-Kain's true will — endurance through strength, not through the annihilation of all life.
- The **[[Vorok-Varr]]** of [[Aerin'r']], who transformed Grom-Kain's mandate into a philosophy of peace, view the Blight with deep suspicion. They believe Grom-Kain's true test was the endurance of **spirit**, not of arms.

## The Question That Haunts Vaeloris

Every race must eventually confront the question the Orkin have always known: **was Kael'Zorai truly evil, or was the Fifth First Hand a creator whose methods the other gods could not tolerate?** The [[Elves]] grieve the Dissonance but do not hate it. The [[Dwarves]] sealed the name Ulzammar but the [[Elm-korin]] seek to complete His work. The [[Gnomes]] see a flawed gear, not a malicious one.

Only the Orkin have the courage — or the blindness — to look directly at this question and answer: **He was our father. Whatever He was to you, He made us, and we will not be ashamed of that.**

## Legacy

Grom-Kain is not a separate deity from [[Kael'Zorai]]; He is Kael'Zorai seen through the eyes of His children. The difference between the Will-Forge and the Dissonant One is not a matter of fact but of **perspective** — and that perspective may determine the fate of Vaeloris when the Fifth stirs again.
