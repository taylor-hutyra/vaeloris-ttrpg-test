# Thane Korin Barazkor

**Thane Korin Barazkor** was the first High King of a unified [[Kharnzarak]], a [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] warlord of the Iron-Hand clan who, in **SA 91**, brought an end to the brutal inter-clan Tunnel Wars and forged the scattered [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] clans into a single kingdom beneath the mountains.

## Rise to Power

In the aftermath of the [[Great Withdrawal]], the [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] clans had spent decades reclaiming their ancestral halls from the horrors of the [[Paroxysm]]. The Reclaiming of the Deep Halls was a room-by-room tunnel war fought in total darkness against [[Skitter-Gloom]] and worse. When the external threat was finally subdued, the clans turned their fury inward. The **Tunnel Wars** -- a series of bitter territorial disputes over mine shafts, forge-rights, and ancestral vaults -- threatened to destroy the very civilization the [[Dwarves]] had fought so hard to rebuild.

Korin Barazkor, thane of the Iron-Hand clan, understood that military strength alone would not unify the mountain. His genius lay in economics.

## The Star-Metal Gambit

High in the upper peaks of the [[Dragon's Tooth Mountains]], Korin's clan controlled access to deposits of **Star-Metal** -- meteorite iron of extraordinary purity and strength. No other clan possessed this resource, and no smith could match the weapons and tools forged from it. Korin leveraged this monopoly with ruthless precision, offering Star-Metal to clans who submitted to his authority and withholding it from those who refused.

It was not merely strength that won the crown -- it was the mastery of supply.

## The Crown of the Mountain

Upon his declaration as High King, Korin commissioned the **Crown of the Mountain**, a heavy geometric crown forged from seven distinct metals, each representing one of the seven founding clans. It is said the Crown heats up if the wearer speaks a lie while seated upon the **Adamant Throne** -- a claim that has never been publicly disproven, and which has kept every High King since measurably honest in their official pronouncements.

## The Caste System

Korin's most enduring and controversial legacy was the establishment of the rigid Dwarven caste system. Under his decree, [[Kharnzarak]] society was divided into three tiers:

- **High Clans**: Noble families with direct lineage to the seven founding clans, granted dominion over governance and military command
- **Guild Clans**: Families whose identity and standing were defined by their hereditary craft
- **Gatestone Clans**: Those who performed the most menial and dangerous labor, with virtually no avenue for upward mobility

This system brought immediate stability to [[Kharnzarak]], ending the chaos of the Tunnel Wars by giving every clan a fixed place in the social architecture. But it also planted the seeds for future resentment -- the slow-burning grievances of the Gatestone Clans and the eventual rise of the [[Thulkor'n]] exile tradition.

## Legacy

Korin Barazkor's unification endured for millennia. The [[Barazkor Clan]] -- later known as the Iron-Gavels -- remained the Royal Clan into the Third Age, with [[High King Thorgar VII]] carrying on the dynasty's tradition. The First High King is remembered as the ultimate pragmatist: a leader who understood that in the deep dark, the clan that controls the metal controls the mountain. His sayings are still carved into forge-hall walls: *"N'nm zalg'l"* -- Stone does not forget.
