# General Aric the Silver

**General Aric the Silver** was a hero of the [[Varnathi Republic]] who discovered his own latent magical ability during the [[Mage-Purge Wars]]. Rather than submit to execution under the [[Decree of Prohibition]], he turned his legion against the Senate and founded the [[Kingdom of the Silver Hand]] -- a sanctuary for persecuted mages on the Republic's borderlands.

## The Kingdom of the Silver Hand

In **SA 1467**, Aric carved out a territory on the borderlands and briefly allied with local [[Orkin]] tribes to hold the line against Republican forces. For fifty years, the Kingdom served as a utopia for those persecuted under the Mage-Purge -- a rare beacon of tolerance in an era of fear and violence.

## The Great Betrayal

As time passed, Aric succumbed to the Fraying -- the madness that afflicted early magic users who channeled raw arcane energy without proper training or understanding. His deteriorating mental state weakened the Kingdom's leadership. Seeing an opportunity, the Orkin leaders betrayed Aric to the [[Varnathi Republic]] in exchange for amnesty. The Republic accepted the help and crushed the Kingdom. In a bitter twist, the Republic then turned on the Orkin and slaughtered them despite the amnesty agreement.

## Legacy

Aric's story created two enduring cultural legacies. In human lands, the tale of a powerful mage driven mad by his own power cemented the stigma of the "Mage-Tyrant" -- the fear that any mage given authority would inevitably descend into tyranny. Among the [[Orkin]], the Republic's treachery after the betrayal deepened their cynicism toward human alliances, a distrust that persists into the Third Age.
