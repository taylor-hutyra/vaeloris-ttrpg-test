# Tharum Flame-Touched

Tharum Flame-Touched was a [[Dwarves|Dwarven]] smith of [[Kharnzarak]] who led a sect of radical artisans in the belief that magic was simply another ore to be worked — not a chaotic force to be feared, but a tool for achieving greater craft. His philosophy, which would become the foundation of the [[Elm-korin]] ("The Flame-Struck"), challenged the rigid orthodoxy of the [[Nûm-Khazalûn]] and their reverence for tradition above all else.

In SA:690, Tharum performed the **Ritual of the Soul-Forge** — infusing a forging hammer with arcane fire, creating a tool that could sense the flaws in any material it struck. This act of magical craftsmanship was unprecedented in the deep halls of Kharnzarak, where mortal magic was viewed as unreliable chaos that defied the structured, rhythmic nature of true creation. The High King of Kharnzarak declared the ritual a corruption of the Stone and ordered the Great Banishment.

Unlike the [[Thulkorûn]], who were exiled for common crimes, Tharum and his followers were visionaries cast out for daring to evolve. They wandered the human lands as part of the Wandering Kin before eventually founding [[Gearhaven]] alongside human mages in SA:1485. Tharum's legacy endures in the Technocracy he helped inspire — a city-state dedicated to the heretical belief that mortal magic and Dwarven craft together could achieve the perfect, logical order that creation itself had left incomplete.
