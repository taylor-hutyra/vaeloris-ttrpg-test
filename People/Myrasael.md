# Myrasael

**Myrasael** is the First Hand of Refinement — one of the four divine beings brought forth by [[A'rastheon]] in the Second Creation. Myrasael embodies beauty, value, and perfection of form. Of all the First Hands, Myrasael is that which elevates raw creation into meaningful expression.

## Domain and Essence

Myrasael's domain encompasses beauty in all its forms: precious metals and gems, artistry, the refinement of the raw into the sublime. Where [[Tham'dran]] provides structure and [[Virandros]] brings life, Myrasael reveals the hidden beauty within both.

## First Great Work

Myrasael inlaid the earth of [[Vaeloris]] with veins of precious metals and gems — adorning the foundations [[Tham'dran]] had laid with concentrated beauty.

## Mortal Creations

### The Dwarves

Through collaborative creation with [[Tham'dran]], Myrasael brought forth the [[Dwarves]] — beings of methodical endurance who seek perfection in their craft. The Dwarves embody the combined essences of Stability and Refinement: masterful craftspeople who create objects of enduring beauty.

### The Waldkyn

After the Paroxysm, Myrasael joined with the other three First Hands to create the [[Waldkyn]].

## Minor Deity Aspects

Myrasael created three aspects to serve as focused guides:

- **[[Illysia]]** the Craftmother — patron of artisans and refined creation
- **[[Vaelynn]]** the Songkeeper — muse of music, poetry, and harmonic expression
- **[[Asteron]]** the Gemheart — master of precious materials and concentrated beauty

## Cultural Names

- **Farrazul** (Depth/Gem) — the Dwarven Pillar of hidden beauty refined through labor
- **Mirelaris** (Gem shimmer) — the Elven Harmony of hidden beauty and inner worth
- **The Jeweler** — the Human Maker who adorned the depths with beauty
- **Myrasael** (Water/Refinement) — the Waldkyn Voice, true name preserved

## The Great Withdrawal

After the creation of the Waldkyn, Myrasael withdrew into the realm of perfection. Myrasael's presence is felt in beauty, refinement, and the pursuit of excellence. The legacy: the drive to create meaning and value.
