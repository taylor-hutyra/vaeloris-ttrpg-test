# Bimble Pockets

**Bimble Pockets** was a [[Gnomes|Gnome]] tinkerer from the mixed [[Halflings|Halfling]]/[[Gnomes|Gnome]] burrow-city of **Gear-Hollow** who, in **SA 1360**, invented the **movable type printing press** -- a device that would democratize knowledge, shatter information monopolies, and quietly reshape the political landscape of [[Vaeloris]] more profoundly than any army or empire.

## Gear-Hollow

Gear-Hollow was one of the settlements born from the historic **Declaration of Hollowkin Rights** (**SA 980**), the treaty between [[Halflings]] and [[Gnomes]] that established shared communities of "Burrows and Hollows." It was a place that baffled [[Humans|Human]] tax collectors with its cheerful lack of hierarchy -- a warren of workshops, kitchens, and fungal gardens where [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] engineering and [[Halflings|Halfling]] hospitality existed in comfortable symbiosis.

Bimble himself was a quintessential [[Forest Gnomes|Forest Gnome]] artisan: quiet, meticulous, and utterly disinterested in fame. His workshop was said to smell perpetually of ink, mushroom broth, and the particular kind of obsession that forgets to eat lunch.

## The Printing Press

The movable type press was not a single flash of inspiration but the culmination of decades of Gnomish refinement. [[Gnomes]] had long possessed intricate knowledge of metallurgy, precision casting, and mechanical reproduction -- skills honed in service of the *Mori-Syna*, the Quiet Work. Bimble applied these principles to the problem of text reproduction, creating individual letter molds that could be arranged, inked, and pressed onto paper in any combination.

The result was a machine that could produce in hours what a scribe required months to copy by hand. It was, in Gnomish terms, simply a more efficient gear in the world's machine. Bimble reportedly failed to understand why anyone found it remarkable.

## The Information Revolution

The consequences of the press were immediate and explosive. Literacy rates surged across [[Vaeloris]]. Pamphlets proliferated. The closely guarded "Secrets of the Guilds" -- trade techniques, alchemical formulas, engineering principles that artisan guilds had hoarded for generations -- began leaking to the common public through cheaply printed manuals and broadsheets.

This threatened two powerful institutions simultaneously: the artisan guilds, whose monopoly on specialized knowledge was the foundation of their economic power, and the [[Shadewalkers]], the espionage guild whose entire business model depended on information being scarce and expensive. A secret that could be printed on a thousand pamphlets was a secret that was worthless.

## Legacy

Bimble Pockets never sought recognition for his invention, and his name is largely unknown outside of [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] and [[Halflings|Halfling]] communities. This would not have surprised him. As the [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] saying goes: *"Kelos on mori"* -- growth in silence. The press-maker's legacy is not a statue or a title, but the simple, revolutionary fact that in [[Vaeloris]], knowledge stopped being a privilege and became a commodity. Every pamphlet, every broadsheet, every printed book in the world traces its lineage back to a small, ink-stained Gnome in a mushroom-scented workshop who thought he was just building a slightly better tool.
