# Tobin Willow-snap

**Tobin Willow-snap** -- known in fireside tales as **Old Man Willow-snap** -- was the [[Halflings|Halfling]] who led the first families to settle the fertile western river valleys and founded **[[Greenmeadow]]** in **SA 980**, establishing the community that would become the heart and soul of [[Halflings|Halfling]] civilization in [[Vaeloris]].

## The Migration

By the late tenth century of the Second Age, the expanding militarism of the [[Varnathi|Varnathi Republic]] had made the eastern lowlands an increasingly uncomfortable place for a people whose highest ambition was a full pantry and a quiet evening. [[Halflings|Halfling]] clans, pushed out by army recruiters, tax collectors, and the general noise of "The Big Scramble," migrated westward toward land that the Tallfolk had overlooked -- a network of lush river valleys too far from the trade roads to interest imperial expansionists and too peaceful to attract military attention.

Tobin Willow-snap, by all accounts a practical and unpretentious farmer with a talent for reading soil, led the first group of families to what he reportedly called *"the most sensible bit of dirt I've ever seen."*

## The Founding of Greenmeadow

The settlement that Tobin established was not grand. There were no walls, no fortifications, no proclamation of sovereignty. There was a communal garden, a shared well, and a row of neat burrows dug into a sunny hillside. The place was named [[Greenmeadow]] because that is precisely what it was, and [[Halflings]] see no reason to name things more dramatically than they deserve.

What made the founding historically significant was not the settlement itself but what Tobin's people found when they arrived: the [[Gnomes]].

## The Gnomish Unveiling

The [[Forest Gnomes]] had lived hidden in the western valleys for centuries, tending to the land's mycelial networks and root systems in perfect obscurity. When the [[Halflings]] arrived and began -- quite naturally -- planting gardens, building pantries, and generally making themselves comfortable, the Gnomes faced a dilemma. These new arrivals were not the "Loud-Walkers" they had feared. These were small, quiet, garden-loving folk who might actually be *compatible*.

Rather than retreat further into hiding, the Gnomes revealed themselves. Tobin's reported response -- *"Well, that explains the uncommonly good mushrooms"* -- set the tone for the entire relationship.

## The Declaration of Hollowkin Rights

The meeting between Tobin's settlers and the local [[Gnomes]] led to the historic **Declaration of Hollowkin Rights**, a treaty establishing a shared society of "Burrows and Hollows" that baffled [[Humans|Human]] tax collectors with its cheerful absence of hierarchy, centralized authority, or any mechanism for declaring war. It was, in effect, a formalized agreement to be good neighbors -- and it has endured for over two thousand years.

## Legacy

Tobin Willow-snap is remembered in [[Halflings|Halfling]] culture not as a hero or a conqueror but as the patron saint of good sense. *"The Tale of Old Man Willow-snap"* is the closest thing [[Halflings]] have to a founding epic, and it is told not in marble halls but over hearth-fires, with ale, to children who are almost but not quite asleep. His greatest accomplishment, in [[Halflings|Halfling]] eyes, was not founding a civilization but finding a good spot with excellent soil and having the decency to share it with the neighbors who were already there.
