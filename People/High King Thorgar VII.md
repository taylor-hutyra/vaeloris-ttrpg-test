# High King Thorgar VII

**High King Thorgar VII** is the current ruler of [[Kharnzarak]], seated upon the **Adamant Throne** and governing the [[N'nm-Khazal'n]] -- the Stone-Sworn [[Dwarves]] of the mainland mountains. He is the monarch who cracked open the Great Adamant Gates after centuries of isolation, allowing a single trade gate to resume contact with the surface world.

## The Cracking of the Gates

In **SA 3050**, after centuries of self-imposed silence ordered by his distant predecessor [[High King Thorgar VI]], Thorgar VII made the momentous decision to allow a single gate of [[Kharnzarak]] to open. It was not a grand gesture of reunion but a pragmatic concession: the kingdom's internal resources, long strained by isolation, needed the economic stimulus that trade could provide.

The terms were deliberately asymmetric. [[Kharnzarak]] would *sell* -- high-quality Dwarven steel and gold, products no surface forge could match -- but would *buy* nothing. Thorgar VII maintained his predecessor's fear of "surface taint," even as he acknowledged the practical impossibility of total self-sufficiency. The result was a one-way economic flow that flooded the surface markets with superior Dwarven metallurgy and fueled the industrial revolution in [[Gearhaven]] and the [[Federation of City-States]].

## Governing the Mountain

Thorgar VII rules with the absolute authority granted by the sacred **Stone-Lays** -- the ancient laws carved into the deepest halls of [[Kharnzarak]]. He is advised by the **Stone-Lay Council**, nine clan-lords who serve as high court and legislative body. While the council cannot override a royal decree, their unanimous opposition can delay it, forcing the king to justify his decision before the ancestral tombs.

The kingdom's internal politics remain fraught. The [[Barazkor Clan]], Thorgar's own Iron-Gavels, are fiercely traditionalist and militant. The **Farraz'l Clan** (Gem-Cutters), the wealthiest of the High Clans, push for more pragmatic trade policies that would increase their fortunes. The **Gulgrym Clan** (Deep-Miners), the most powerful of the Guild Clans, harbor deep resentments about the rigid caste system that keeps them laboring in the dark while the High Clans feast in the light.

And in hidden workshops, the **[[Bronr'n Legacy]]** -- a clandestine society of reformist artisans and engineers -- studies forbidden [[Elm-korin]] schematics and [[Gnomes|Gnomish]] designs, believing that [[Kharnzarak]]'s stagnation will be its doom.

## The Third Age Dilemma

Thorgar VII is caught between the weight of tradition and the pressure of a world that has changed beyond recognition. The surface civilizations now possess technologies -- steam engines, firearms, arcane science -- that the sealed kingdom never developed. The [[Dwarves]] who remained behind the gates are master craftsmen of ancient techniques, but they are centuries behind in innovation.

The High King's challenge is existential: open the gates wider and risk the cultural contamination his predecessors feared, or keep them shut and watch the mountain become a beautifully carved tomb. Every decision he makes is scrutinized by traditionalists who see change as betrayal and reformists who see stagnation as death.

## Legacy

Thorgar VII's reign is still unfolding, and history's judgment remains unwritten. He is neither the paranoid isolationist his predecessor was nor the radical reformer the [[Bronr'n Legacy]] hopes for. He is a cautious king on an ancient throne, listening to the grinding of the mountain and trying to determine whether the sound he hears is the engine of renewal or the prelude to collapse.
