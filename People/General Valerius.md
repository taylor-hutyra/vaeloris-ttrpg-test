# General Valerius

**General Valerius** is a celebrated [[Varnathi]] military commander and the current **Consul of Blades** in the Pentarchy of [[Vernath]], active as of **TA 412**. Immensely popular with the public and fiercely ambitious, he represents the sharp edge of a political faction that believes the [[Laws of Accord]] have made the [[Varnathi]] weak -- and that only military strength can secure humanity's future in an increasingly dangerous world.

## Rise to Fame

Valerius earned his reputation through a series of celebrated campaigns along [[Vernath]]'s northern and western borders, defending against [[Kyn-Clan]] incursions from the [[Whispering Vales]] and pirate raids along the coast. His victories were not merely military but *theatrical* -- he understood the value of spectacle, ensuring that news of each triumph reached the capital before the dust had settled. The common people of [[Vernath]] speak his name with the reverence once reserved for the philosopher-warriors of [[Vardas the Elder]]'s era.

## The Crimson Banner

Valerius is the de facto leader of the **[[Crimson Banner]]** -- the political arm of the military and the most hawkish faction in the Pentarchy. Composed of ambitious young nobles, military veterans, and industrialists who profit from defense spending, the Crimson Banner argues that the [[Laws of Accord]], imposed by the [[Magocracy]] at the Convention of Accord (**SA 3280**), have kept [[Vernath]] in a state of enforced weakness. They champion a strong, proactive military that can project power without waiting for permission from the mages of [[Luminas]].

## Political Deadlock

Valerius constantly pushes for increased military funding and more aggressive "border security" measures -- proposals that his critics see as thinly veiled preparations for territorial expansion. He is consistently blocked by the **[[Marble Hand]]** faction, the traditionalist old guard who control the Consulships of Pillars and Scrolls and who fear provoking the [[Magocracy]] into retaliatory action.

This has created a bitter political deadlock within the Pentarchy, with the Consuls of Coin and the People caught in the middle. The Consul of Coin sees profit in military contracts; the Consul of the People fears that the common folk will bear the burden of any escalation. [[Vernath]]'s government, once a model of structured debate, has ground to near-paralysis.

## The Danger

What makes Valerius dangerous is not his ambition but his sincerity. He genuinely believes that the [[Varnathi]] are destined to lead -- that the [[Mandate of Order]] first proclaimed by [[Emperor Caelius I]] remains humanity's sacred charge. In a world where the [[Silent Blight]] creeps outward, where the [[Magocracy]]'s wards show signs of strain, and where ancient threats stir in forgotten places, his argument that strength is the only reliable currency carries a persuasive weight that his opponents struggle to counter.

Whether Valerius is the leader [[Vernath]] needs or the demagogue who will lead it to ruin remains the defining political question of the Third Age.

## Legacy

As of **TA 412**, Valerius's story is unwritten. He stands at the fulcrum of Varnathi politics, a man whose next move could tip the balance toward war or reform. The [[Open Palm Movement]], the small reformist faction, watches him with particular alarm -- not because they doubt his patriotism, but because they know that a patriot with a sword is the most dangerous kind of leader.
